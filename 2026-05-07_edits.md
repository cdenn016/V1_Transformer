## Participatory_it_from_bit.tex — rectangular multi-head fix for $W_Q W_K^\top$ factorization

**Defect.** The transformer-reduction subsection (Section "Connection to Standard Transformers") set $K = d_k$ throughout and identified $M = W_Q W_K^\top \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ and $W_Q W_K^\top = \sigma^{-2} M \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ at multiple sites (lines 2403, 2415, 2455–2457). This implicitly forces $W_Q, W_K$ to be square. In standard multi-head attention, however, the projections are rectangular, $W_Q, W_K \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{head}}}$ with $d_{\text{head}} < d_{\text{model}}$, so the lift $W_Q W_K^\top \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{model}}}$ has rank at most $d_{\text{head}}$ and is not in $\mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{model}})$. The manuscript therefore needed to make explicit (i) that the working dimension $d_k$ in this section corresponds to the per-head dimension $d_{\text{head}}$, and (ii) the thin-SVD lift that recovers an invertible head-space kernel and the block-diagonal multi-head gauge group $\mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{head}})^H \subset \mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{model}})$.

**Fix.** Surgical edits at four sites in `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex`:

Restated the bilinear $M \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ as the head's invertible kernel and added explicit prose that $W_Q W_K^\top \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{model}}}$ is rank-deficient under rectangular projections; the thin-SVD recovery of $M \in \mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{head}})$ is referenced to the companion paper.
3. **Score-equation caption** (line ~2415). Replaced the unconditional $M = \sigma^2 W_Q W_K^\top \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ identification with a conditional one valid for square per-head projections, with the rectangular case recovered by thin SVD.
4. **Factorization paragraph** (lines ~2455–2457). Kept the equation $W_Q W_K^\top = \sigma^{-2} M \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ as the per-head/square statement; added a follow-on paragraph that, for $W_Q, W_K \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{model}} \times d_{\text{head}}}$, the thin SVD $M = A_Q A_K^\top$ yields full-column-rank factors with $A_Q^\top A_K \in \mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{head}})$ realising the head-space kernel, and that the gauge group of multi-head attention is the block-diagonal subgroup $\mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{head}})^H \subset \mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{model}})$.

## Participatory_it_from_bit.tex + GL(K)_attention.tex — formalize non-trivial holonomy as discrete Regime II

**Question.** How can non-trivial holonomy be incorporated into the framework, given that the cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ forces $\Omega_{ik}\Omega_{kj}\Omega_{ji} = I$ on every 3-cycle?

**Resolution.** The framework already partially realises a non-flat extension in code (`non_flat_transport`, `cocycle_relaxation`, the `holonomy.py` 3-cycle diagnostic, and the `holonomy_penalty` Wilson-style regulariser), but the manuscripts had not formalised the underlying mathematics. The resolution is to recognise that the existing edge-relaxed parameterisation
$\Omega_{ij} = U_i \exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G) U_j^{-1}$
is already a valid Regime II structure — standard lattice gauge theory written in a vertex-trivialised parameterisation — with the correct gauge-covariance law $\Omega_{ij} \to g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$ under vertex-local gauge actions $U_i \to g_i U_i$.

**Manuscript edits (Participatory_it_from_bit.tex).**

1. **New subsubsection `\subsubsection{Discrete Regime II via an Edge-Relaxed Cocycle}` (label `sec:discrete_regime_ii`)**, inserted after the existing `sec:field_strength` subsection and before `\subsection{The Hierarchy of Transport Operators}`. Six paragraphs:
   - Edge-relaxed parameterisation $\Omega_{ij} = U_i \exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G) U_j^{-1}$, with $\delta_{ij}=0$ recovering Regime I.
   - Gauge covariance proof: $\Omega_{ij} \to g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$, with $\delta_{ij}$ gauge-invariant in the internal frame.
   - Holonomy $H_{ijk}$ around a 3-cycle and the Wilson observable $W_{ijk} = \mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}(H_{ijk})$.
   - Wilson-style regulariser $S_{\mathrm{YM}} = \beta\sum_{(i,j,k)}(1 - W_{ijk}/K)$, with $\beta\to\infty$ recovering Regime I.
   - Cocycle relaxation as a homotopy parameter $\alpha\in[0,1]$ (matching the existing `cocycle_relaxation` config).
   - DAG-vs-cycle resolution: the autoregressive (causal) attention mask defines a DAG on tokens, so the holonomy is a property of the connection field on the index space rather than of the data flow; bidirectional/encoder attention recovers genuine dynamical holonomy. This dichotomy is the clearest experimental discriminator and was previously absent from both manuscripts.
