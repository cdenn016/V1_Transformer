## Participatory_it_from_bit.tex — defend dual role of φ_i and downgrade global bundle claim to local-trivialization scope

**Two structural concerns raised by review:**

1. *Role A / Role B conflation.* The "two roles for the gauge frame" paragraph distinguished $\phi_i$ as gauge-redundant (Role A) from $\phi_i$ as cognitive state (Role B), but did not justify that the dual role is mathematically rigorous. Quantities like $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu^{(i)} A_\nu^{(i)})$ used in the Lorentzian-signature mechanism and the pulled-back metric are not invariant under the full local gauge — yet the manuscript invoked them as if physical. The reviewer's challenge: pick one role, or prove both are simultaneously consistent.

2. *Principal-bundle overreach.* The framework defined a principal $G$-bundle $\pi: \mathcal{N} \to \mathcal{C}$, then introduced global frame fields $\phi_i: \mathcal{C} \to \mathfrak{g}$ and $U_i = \exp(\phi_i)$ — but a general principal bundle does not admit a global section. Even the exponential map fails to be globally surjective for non-compact groups like $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$. The bundle formalism overreached what the construction actually supports.

**Resolution.** Two new labelled paragraphs and one targeted line edit, with three new bibliography entries:

A. **`\paragraph{Why the dual role is mathematically rigorous.}`** (label `sec:dual_role_rigor`), inserted after the existing Role A/B paragraph. Defends the dual role through three established mechanisms in modern gauge theory and quantum reference frames:
   - Edge modes (Donnelly & Freidel 2016): "would-be pure-gauge" boundary data become genuine physical d.o.f. when a region's interior is treated as a subsystem.
   - Quantum reference frames (Bartlett, Rudolph, Spekkens 2007; Vanrietvelde, Höhn, Giacomini, Castro-Ruiz 2020): a frame is gauge from a global perspective-neutral viewpoint, but is a physical system from its own perspective and from any other frame relative to which it is described.
   - Rovelli's relational interpretation (already cited): properties have meaning only relative to other systems.

   The paragraph then commits the framework to a specific reading: the **global diagonal** action $U_i \to U_i g$ (single $g$ shared across agents) is the bulk gauge under which $\phi_i$ is purely redundant; the **agent-local** frame data is the edge-mode/QRF content carrying physical relational stance. Quantities $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu^{(i)} A_\nu^{(i)})$ are explicitly **invariant under the constant-per-agent residual subgroup** (where $g_i$ is constant in $c$, so trace cyclicity absorbs the conjugation) but **not under full local gauge** with $g_i = g_i(c)$. The Lorentzian-signature mechanism, the pulled-back metric, and the gauge-curvature linguistic conjecture are therefore restricted to this residual subgroup.

B. **`\paragraph{Local trivializations and the scope of the bundle formalism.}`** (label `sec:local_trivialization`), inserted immediately after the Principal Bundle Definition and before the intuition prose. States that:
   - A general principal $G$-bundle does not admit a global section; a globally defined Lie-algebra-valued frame field exists iff the bundle is trivializable.
   - The framework operates exclusively in **local trivializing patches** $\{\mathcal{U}_i\}$, with each $\phi_i: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathfrak{g}$ a local section.
   - The transition functions $\Omega_{ij}$ on overlaps satisfy the Čech cocycle condition; the cocycle is the natural mathematical object, the global bundle is the formal Čech limit of these patches.
   - Two technical caveats: (i) the exponential parameterisation $U_i = \exp(\phi_i)$ is only locally bijective — surjective for connected compact $G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$ used in the simulations, but not for non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$, where the polar/Cartan decomposition $U = \exp(X)\exp(Y)$ is needed; (ii) the Maurer–Cartan one-form $\theta = U^{-1}dU$ is well-defined patch-wise regardless of global trivializability.

C. **Targeted line edit.** The previous text "the agent's section $U_i: \mathcal{C} \to G$" at the Maurer–Cartan one-form passage was changed to "the agent's local section $U_i: \mathcal{U}_i \to G$ on the trivializing patch $\mathcal{U}_i$ (Section~\ref{sec:local_trivialization})". This is the single clearest instance of the global-section overreach that the review flagged.
