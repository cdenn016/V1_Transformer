## Holonomy diagnostic numerics fix

## Symptom

User reported a busted holonomy log line during training:

```
mean ‖C-I‖=1537664.000000 | frac>0.1=0.726 | spectral_gap=nan | nan_frac=0.274
delta_spec[max/p95/mean]=431.608/227.817/65.134
[HOLONOMY] L0H0 variant=scaled mean ‖C-I‖=inf | max=inf | std=nan
median=3138009956352.000000 | frac>0.1=0.974 | spectral_gap=nan | nan_frac=0.026
delta_spec[max/p95/mean]=215.804/113.908/32.567
```

Pathologies on display:

- `‖C-I‖` mean either ~1.5e6 (raw) or `inf` (scaled) — should be O(1) for a
  well-conditioned connection, zero for a flat connection.
- `spectral_gap=nan` despite individual snapshots showing only 2.6–27.4% NaN —
  inconsistent if the reductions are masking properly.
- The "scaled" variant produced `inf` while having a *lower* `nan_fraction`
  (2.6%) than the raw variant (27.4%) — an inversion that's not credible
  unless `inf` is silently passing the NaN mask.

## Investigation

Three parallel investigators (`Explore` agents) plus one verifier audited:

1. The holonomy metric pipeline (`publication_metrics.py:_extract_exp_delta`,
   `holonomy_metrics.py:compute_holonomy_snapshot`, `holonomy.py:75-84`).
2. The upstream `Connection` / Ω / φ data path
   (`core/connection.py`, `vfe/e_step.py`, `core/transport_ops.py`).
3. Where NaN/inf enter the snapshot reductions.

The verifier's primary-source read of `publication_metrics.py:2196-2197`
identified the proximate bug all three investigators had missed:

```python
_orig_dtype = delta_matrix.dtype                                  # float32 in user run
exp_delta = torch.linalg.matrix_exp(delta_matrix.double()).to(_orig_dtype)
```

The `.to(_orig_dtype)` cast immediately undoes the float64 upgrade. The
in-line comment claimed "stable to spectral radius >1000", which is true
*in float64* — but the result was downcast back to float32 before any
consumer saw it. float32 max ≈ exp(88.7), so per-edge spectral norms above
~88 produce `+inf` after the cast even though the float64 matrix exponential
was finite. With `delta_max_spec=215-431` from the user's run, this happens
on a substantial fraction of edges.

The downstream snapshot reductions checked `torch.isnan` only and did not
catch `+inf`. So `inf · 0`-style cancellations in the triangle product
`C = exp(δ_ij) · exp(δ_jk) · exp(δ_ki)` produced NaN entries (counted),
while pure-`inf` entries were silently averaged into `mean_norm=inf` and
counted as "well-conditioned" (i.e. a low `nan_fraction`). This explains
the inversion — the scaled variant's `inf` median (3.1e12) and `inf` mean
were the same numerical breakdown, just below the NaN cancellation
threshold rather than at it.

The upstream cause (deferred — see TODO below) is unbounded growth of the
connection weight matrix `W` in `core/connection.py`: there is no weight
decay, no spectral clamp, and no penalty wired up. `transport_ops.py:383-386`
clamps `‖scaled_delta‖ ≤ 5.0` at the training forward pass, but does not
bound `W` itself. The diagnostic in `_extract_exp_delta` recomputes
δ = µᵀ W µ from scratch *without* the training-path clamp, which is why
the diagnostic surfaces the W-drift that training quietly suppresses.
