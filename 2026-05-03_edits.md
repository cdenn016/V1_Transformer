### Time-dilation sign verification

R2 claimed the "Proper Time Dilation from Information Geometry"
section had its sign reversed relative to special relativity. Direct
verification: the paper computes $\tau = \int \sqrt{d\mu^T \Sigma_p^{-1}
d\mu}$, a positive-definite Riemannian arc length on the Fisher
manifold. For any non-trivial trajectory on a Riemannian manifold,
arc length exceeds straight-line distance, so $\tau \geq t$ is
geometrically necessary, not "relativistic." Special-relativistic
proper time satisfies $d\tau^2 = dt^2 - dx^2/c^2$, giving $\tau \leq t$
with equality only at rest in the chosen frame. Furthermore, in the
paper's results, "heavier" agents accumulate more $\tau$; in GR, an
observer in a deeper gravitational well accumulates less proper time.
The two quantities have opposite character on both axes. R2's
diagnosis is correct.
