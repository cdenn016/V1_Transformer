## Disentangling priors (p) and models (s)

### Why

Throughout the draft the author conflated priors $p_i$ (per-agent /
per-token regulariser of beliefs) with generative models $s_i$
(per-agent slow distribution over generative-model parameters). The
canonical hierarchy per CLAUDE.md and per the GL(K) companion papers
(`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex`, `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex`)
is `r → s → p → q → observations`. The draft was using $p$ for what
the canonical formalism calls $s$ in (a) the model-coupling term
$\gamma_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}(\cdot \| \tilde{\Omega}_{ij}\cdot)$, (b) the
slow-subsystem free energy, (c) the discussion of scientific theories
and Kuhnian revolutions, and (d) several pullback-metric passages.

### Investigators run

Two parallel Explore agents:
1. Extracted the canonical $p/s/r$ treatment from
   `GL(K)_attention.tex` (lines 389-391, 661, 857-866, 1252-1256,
   1847-1854) and `GL(K)_supplementary.tex` (lines 1065-1086).
   Confirmed: GL(K) main paper operates with $\gamma_{ij}=0$ and $s$
   frozen; supplementary introduces the full four-level hierarchy.
   GL(K) uses $r$ (not $h$) for the model hyper-prior.
2. Audited `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` for every conflation site,
   producing line-numbered findings across symbol misuse, verbal
   conflation, missing levels, and equation-level bugs.

User decisions taken: Option 2 (architectural alignment with GL(K));
$r$ for hyper-prior; abstract keeps $(q, p, \phi)$; the existing
$\gamma_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}(p\|p)$ terms are renamed to
$\gamma_{ij}\,\mathrm{KL}(s\|s)$ (same coupling, correct variable).
