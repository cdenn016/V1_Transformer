## Issue 1 — Envelope-theorem / reduced free-energy clarification

### Why

The boxed pointwise free energy (Eq. 32 at `eq:pointwise_free_energy`, line ~802) and the field free energy (Eq. 39 at `eq:free_energy_functional_final`, line ~828) display $\sum_{ij} \beta^*_{ij} \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}q_j)$, which is the autograd-convention form $\langle E\rangle_{\beta^*}$, not the reduced free energy $-\tau \log Z_i$. Section 3.4 ("The Envelope Theorem and the Reduced Free Energy") at lines 867-905 already disambiguates the two conventions; but a reader stopping at the boxed Eq. 39 reads it as the reduced free energy, which it is not. The two objectives share critical points but differ off-equilibrium by $\tau \sum_j \beta^*_{ij}\log(\beta^*_{ij}/\pi_j)$.

### Approach

Inserted clarifying notes after Eq. 32 and after the boxed Eq. 39. Each note explicitly labels the displayed object as the joint (un-minimized, autograd-convention) free energy with $\beta_{ij}, \gamma_{ij}$ as free variational parameters, and points the reader to `eq:free_energy_reduced` in `\ref{sec:envelope_theorem}` for the reduced form. The notes also state the off-equilibrium difference explicitly. No equation was altered. Verified algebraically by sympy that $F_{\mathrm{align}}(\beta^*) = -\tau\log Z$ and $\langle E\rangle_{\beta^*} = -\tau\log Z - \tau\,\mathrm{KL}(\beta^* \| \pi)$.

## Issue 2 — Regime I / Regime II distinction for connection and curvature

### Why

The connection $A^{(i)}_\mu = U_i^{-1}\partial_\mu U_i$ derived from the smooth gauge frame $\phi_i$ together with the cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = U_iU_j^{-1}$ forces $F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu} = 0$ identically by the Maurer–Cartan identity (Theorem `thm:vanishing_holonomy`). Yet roughly twelve passages elsewhere in the manuscript treated $F_{\mu\nu}$, frame-twist holonomy, Yang-Mills kinetic terms, and gauge-curvature linguistic conjectures as if they had nontrivial dynamical content under the same parameterization. This is a real internal inconsistency that the verifier rated high severity.

### Approach

Adopted explicit two-regime distinction. Inserted an opening paragraph in Section `\ref{sec:connection_forms}` defining **Regime I** (pure-gauge connection, $F = 0$ identically; what the implementation realizes) and **Regime II** (independent connection variable; transport becomes path-ordered, $F$ becomes dynamical). Annotated downstream passages as Regime II content where they appear:

- "Gauge Field Strength: Path-Dependent Information Transport" subsubsection — opening sentence states that nonzero $F$ scenarios are Regime II, and Regime I forces $F = 0$.
- "Curvature Structure" gauge-field-curvature paragraph — paragraph rewritten to flag the Regime II conditional.
- Yang-Mills curvature-penalty regularizer in the boxed free energy at line ~865 — relocated into Regime II since the integrand is structurally zero in Regime I.
- The "Gauge Curvature Conjecture" linguistic prediction (line 2625) — explicitly conditional on Regime II, with the $F = \partial \phi - \partial \phi + [\phi,\phi]$ typo at line 2628 corrected to use $A$.
- Section `\ref{sec:signature_resolution}` regime-status paragraph at the head, noting that the signature mechanism stays in Regime I (uses $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$, not $F$) but relies on the frame-as-state reading of $\phi$.

## Issue 3 — Two roles for the gauge frame (gauge redundancy vs cognitive state)

### Why

The same symbol $\phi_i$ was used in two incompatible roles. In its **transport role** (gauge redundancy) the manuscript said frames are "arbitrary choices with no physical content" (line 235); in its **state role** (cognitive ontology) the manuscript said "there is something it is like to occupy gauge frame $\phi_i$" (line 2747) and treated frame-dependent pulled-back metrics $G_i^{(q)}$ as load-bearing in the qualia and signature constructions. A referee will object that the framework cannot have both readings simultaneously without disambiguation.

### Approach

Inserted "Two roles for the gauge frame" paragraph in Section `\ref{sec:agents_as_sections}` (the Principal Bundles section) explicitly distinguishing **Role A** (transport, gauge-covariant, $\phi_i$ redundant) from **Role B** (cognitive state, $\phi_i$ contributes to ontology, gauge-equivalent frames yield distinct phenomenologies). Established convention that "gauge-invariant" and "gauge-covariant" carry their standard mathematical meanings, and "frame-dependent" is used for Role-B quantities to signal dependence on cognitive state rather than on labelling. Added cross-reference labels (`sec:agents_as_sections`, `sec:qualia`, `sec:consensus_metric`) and a Role-B reminder at the top of the qualia subsection. The 1546 escape hatch — which had appealed to the gauge-invariant Yang-Mills action density $\mathrm{tr}(F F)$ as the gauge-invariant content — was rewritten: $\mathrm{tr}(F F) = 0$ identically under Regime I cocycle, so it supplies no escape hatch. Gauge-invariant ontology is instead routed through the consensus / Haar-averaged metric of Section `\ref{sec:consensus_metric}` (which now carries an explicit label).

## Issue 4 — Yang-Mills naming and sign-convention conflicts

### Why

The bundle pullback's horizontal block $g_{\mathcal{C},\mu\nu} = \mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ was labelled the "Yang-Mills kinetic metric" throughout (`g^{\mathrm{YM}}` in equations). It is not Yang-Mills: Yang-Mills is the curvature invariant $\mathrm{tr}(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$, which presupposes a base metric and is gauge-invariant; the manuscript's $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ is connection-dependent and gauge-noninvariant. Separately, sign convention: for compact $\mathfrak{so}(n)$ the standard positive-definite inner product is $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$, with $\mathrm{tr}(A^2) \le 0$ on skew-symmetric $A$. The Lorentzian worked example uses $T = \mathrm{diag}(1,-1)$, which is symmetric (in $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})$, not $\mathfrak{so}(2)$) and gives $\mathrm{tr}(T^2) = +2 > 0$. Under the standard compact-form sign convention the construction's signs would reverse. These convention choices were unstated.

### Approach

Renamed `g^{\mathrm{YM}}` to `g^{\mathrm{tw}}` ("frame-twist") at the bundle metric definition (lines 1530, 1535, 1538, 1546, 1617, 1672) and added a sentence at the definition specifying the bilinear form: $\langle A, B\rangle := \mathrm{tr}(AB)$ on $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$, indefinite, distinct from the standard positive-definite $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ on compact forms. In the worked example added a sentence stating that $T = \mathrm{diag}(1,-1) \in \mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R}) \setminus \mathfrak{so}(2)$ and that this non-compact choice is what makes $\mathrm{tr}(T^2) > 0$ available. Updated all "Yang-Mills kinetic" descriptive prose in the signature-resolution subsection and downstream cross-references (Lahav–Neemeh discussion, Conclusion's open-problems summary) to use "frame-twist quadratic form".

## Issue 5 — Drop the unsupported "complex KL" claim

### Why

The Concrete-Pathway bullet at line 1623 stated: "Extend gauge frames to $\phi \in \mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$ and distribution parameters to complex values. The KL divergence generalizes naturally to complex exponential families." This is mathematically unjustified: KL is defined for nonnegative probability densities, and complex-valued "densities" require one of (i) quantum relative entropy on density matrices, (ii) Born rule with $|\psi|^2$, (iii) real $2K$-realification, or (iv) signed/complex measures (under which KL is not standard). Grep confirmed the manuscript does not specify which is intended. Worse, the line directly contradicts line 1631 (which states the Fisher-Rao fiber metric remains positive semi-definite). The worked example only complexifies the gauge frame $\phi$, never the distributions $q_i$.

### Approach

Replaced the offending bullet with: "Complexified gauge frames acting on real Gaussian beliefs", explicitly noting that $q_i$ remain real Gaussians on real $\mathbb{R}^K$, the KL divergence on the fiber stays in standard real form, and a genuinely complexified probability theory is a stricter step that this manuscript does not adopt (parked as separate future work). This also fixes the Yang-Mills-pathway sentence's "non-compact structure" overclaim by noting in surrounding text that real $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ is non-compact yet still produces positive-definite transport on real Gaussian fibers (Sylvester).

## Issue 7 — Mass-from-precision: stiffness vs inertia, and Hessian symmetry

### Why

The Hessian $M_{\mu\mu} = \partial^2 \mathcal{F}/\partial\mu\partial\mu$ derived in Section `\ref{sec:mass}` is, in the harmonic-oscillator analogy, a *stiffness* $k$ on belief configuration space, not a mass $m$. For $L = \tfrac12 m \dot{x}^2 - \tfrac12 k x^2$ the dispersion is $\omega^2 = k/m$, so the Hessian-of-potential gives stiffness; the precision plays the mass role only after a kinetic-metric postulate is in place. The manuscript supplied that postulate at lines 1283-1290 ("Velocity-Quadratic Metric Form") but did not flag it as the load-bearing assumption. Separately, the off-diagonal-block caveat at lines 1240-1242 said $M_{\mu\mu}$ is "symmetric only when $\beta_{ik} = \beta_{ki}$ and $\Omega_{ik} = \Omega_{ki}^\top$". By Schwarz's theorem any Hessian of a scalar functional automatically satisfies block-symmetry $M_{ik} = M_{ki}^\top$; the displayed formula at line 1232 already does (sympy-verified). What reciprocity actually buys is the stronger condition for $M_{\mu\mu}$ to act as a *symmetric bilinear form* on stacked $(\mu_i)_i$, which is what licenses the conservative-Hamiltonian reading.

### Approach

Inserted "What the Hessian gives, and what is added" paragraph at the top of Section `\ref{sec:mass}` distinguishing stiffness from mass and stating that the Hessian-as-mass identification is contingent on the kinetic-metric postulate of the Velocity-Quadratic subsection. Added a label `sec:velocity_quadratic` to that subsection and rewrote its opening paragraph to make explicit that the kinetic form is a postulate, not a consequence of $\mathcal{F}$. Rewrote the "Off-diagonal block caveats" paragraph at lines 1240-1242 to separate (i) automatic Hessian block-symmetry $M_{ik} = M_{ki}^\top$ (which always holds by Schwarz's theorem) from (ii) the symmetric-bilinear-form condition $M_{ik} = M_{ki}$ (which requires reciprocity and licenses the conservative-Hamiltonian reading). Replaced lingering `sec:empirical_mass` references with the existing `sec:mass` label.

## Issue 8 — Meta-agent emergence: variational principle + threshold-based implementation

### Why

Three rounds of investigation (reports 15-18 in `Attention/peer_review_2026-05-06/`) confirmed that the manuscript contained a real and pervasive overclaiming defect on meta-agent emergence. The "Consensus Detection and Meta-Agent Formation" subsection at line 1313 honestly disclosed that meta-agent formation is implemented via discrete threshold-based detection (ad-hoc thresholds $\Gamma_{\min} = 0.5$, $N_{\min} = 2$), and the next paragraph stated explicitly that "in a more principled implementation, meta-agent formation would be governed directly by free energy gradients..." But thirteen other passages elsewhere in the manuscript treated the threshold-based demonstration as a derivation of emergence: "spontaneously generate," "first direct evidence," "No hierarchical structure was imposed," subsection title "Participatory Structure Validated," figure caption claiming the structure "emerged spontaneously... without imposed architectural priors," and so on. A referee reading the abstract or the figure caption first would form an expectation about derivation that the line-1313 disclosure then contradicts.

The math investigation also showed that ~70% of the user's proposed variational-RG / information-bottleneck reformulation was already implicit in the manuscript: line 1328 already stated the free-energy-improvement criterion as future work; the existing aggregation formulas for $\bar{\mu}_I, \bar{\Sigma}_I$ at line 1340 were the leading-order forward-KL barycenter with the dispersion term dropped; and the additive frame formula $\phi_I = \sum w_i \phi_i / \sum w_i$ was the first-order BCH approximation to the group-valued Karcher barycenter. Promoting this content from future-work footnotes to the lead variational principle of the section therefore earned a substantial fraction of the strong-emergence claims without rerunning simulations or adding new derivation. The remaining defect in the variational-RG proposal — the user's prose said "precision-weighted consensus" while the equation as written gave forward-KL moment-matching with a dispersion term — was resolved by sympy verification: the existing manuscript formulas correspond to forward-KL with the dispersion term dropped as $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ in the high-coherence regime, and the rewrite documents this approximation explicitly.

### Approach

Two coordinated phases:

**Option B-lite (substantive reorganisation).** Renamed and restructured the "Consensus Detection and Meta-Agent Formation" subsection. The new subsection has two subsubsections:

1. `sec:meta_agent_variational` — the variational principle, with three equations: the free-energy improvement criterion ($\mathcal{F}_{\text{parent}} + C(I) < \mathcal{F}_{\text{sep}}$, eq:meta_agent_FE_criterion), the parent state as gauge-covariant variational barycenter (eq:meta_agent_barycenter, with closed Gaussian forms eq:meta_agent_mu_barycenter and eq:meta_agent_sigma_barycenter), and the parent frame as group-valued Karcher barycenter (eq:meta_agent_frame_barycenter). The savings analysis shows the free-energy gain from collapsing $\mathcal{O}(|I|^2)$ pairwise alignment KLs into $|I|$ child-parent KL terms scales as $\sim |I|(|I|-1)\varepsilon$ in the high-coherence limit, giving the threshold detector a principled scaling-law interpretation.
2. `sec:meta_agent_threshold` — the threshold-based detector, now framed as a practical surrogate for the variational criterion, motivated by the observation that in the high-coherence regime $\Gamma$ tracks the same coherence factor that controls the savings. The thresholds $\Gamma_{\min} = 0.5$, $N_{\min} = 2$ are demoted from "ad-hoc" to "implementation details, not theoretical requirements."

The "Gauge-Covariant Belief Construction" subsection now opens by referencing eq:meta_agent_barycenter and explains that the implementation drops the dispersion term as a leading-order approximation valid in the high-coherence regime. The frame construction is reframed as the first-order BCH approximation to the Karcher mean, exact for abelian or commuting frames and valid to $\mathcal{O}(\|\phi_i\|^2)$ on the convexity ball for compact $G$; the GL$^+$($K$) obstruction (no bi-invariant Riemannian metric, signature (2,1,1) on $\mathfrak{gl}(2)$ verified directly) is documented.

**Option A (textual sweep).** All thirteen overclaiming passages were rewritten to either reference the variational principle and its threshold-based surrogate, or to qualify emergence claims with the post-detection conditioning. Notable changes:

- Opening of `\subsection{The Participatory Universe...}` (was line 1305): "agents spontaneously organize... arise naturally from variational free energy minimization" → "when coherent agent clusters satisfy the variational free-energy improvement criterion of `sec:meta_agent_variational`, they aggregate... The simulations of this paper trigger the aggregation through the threshold-based surrogate of `sec:meta_agent_threshold` rather than through a continuous-time evaluation of the variational criterion."
- Line 121 `\textbf{Validated.}` header split into `\textbf{Validated.}` (transformer + WikiText-103) and `\textbf{Demonstrated in working code (single illustrative run).}` (Ouroboros Tower).
- Line 2066 ("We report spontaneous emergence...") → "We report detector-mediated emergence..."
- Figure caption at 2174 ("emerged spontaneously... without imposed architectural priors") → "produced by the threshold-based consensus detector... acting on agent state variables driven by the gauge-theoretic dynamics; the energy functional and initialization do not hard-code a hierarchical depth, scale assignments, or a tree topology, but the detector that nucleates meta-agents is imposed."
- Line 2195 ("first direct evidence... can spontaneously generate") → "Conditional on the threshold-based consensus detector..., these results show that gauge-theoretic active inference with softmax coupling weights produces a stable thirteen-scale hierarchical organization... We do not claim free-energy-driven spontaneous emergence at this resolution, since the detector is imposed rather than derived."
- Line 2204 ("No hierarchical structure was imposed... emerged purely from") → "The energy functional and the initialization do not encode a hierarchical depth, scale assignments, or a tree topology; in that restricted sense, no hierarchical \emph{architecture} is hard-coded. The hierarchy in Fig.~\ref{fig:hierarchy} is nucleated, however, by the threshold-based consensus detector."
