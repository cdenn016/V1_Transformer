## Edit — new Theorem after Proposition 1

Inserted a new `\begin{theorem}[Explicit dispersion and anharmonic
constants]` block at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` after
the schematic Proposition~\ref{thm:rg_residual}. The theorem states
the upgraded claim under three explicit specifications:

1. The closure norm $\|\cdot\|_\mathcal{B}$ is the parent-neighborhood
   $L^\infty$ norm modulo additive constants of
   Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_closure} — the manuscript's own existing definition,
   carried over unchanged.
2. The parent ansatz lies in the $C^4$ multi-agent Gaussian KL
   functional class on $\mathcal{N}_I$, with bounded Fisher operator
   norm $\|F(q_I)\|_{\mathrm{op}}$ and bounded third and fourth
   derivative tensors $\|T_3\|_{\mathrm{op}}, \|T_4\|_{\mathrm{op}}$
   at the parent saddle in the standard spectral operator norm of the
   matricization `[HornJohnson2013]`.
3. The integrated post-Laplace correction is read in Wick-contraction
   normalization rather than in Edgeworth density-correction
   normalization.

Under these specifications the theorem provides

$$ C_1 = \frac{\sqrt 2}{12} \cdot \frac{\|F(q_I)\|_{\mathrm{op}}^{3/2}}{m_I^{1/2}} $$

via the Pinsker–Edgeworth chain `[CoverThomas2006]` with canonical
Pinsker constant $\tfrac{1}{\sqrt 2}$ composed with the third-cumulant
Edgeworth coefficient $\tfrac{1}{6}$. The equivalent Taylor-route
presentation is $C_1 = (\sqrt 2 / 3) \|T_3\|_{\mathrm{op}}/m_I^{3/2}$.

The anharmonic constant is

$$ C_5 = \frac{1}{8} \cdot \frac{\|T_4\|_{\mathrm{op}}}{m_I^2} + \frac{5}{24} \cdot \frac{\|T_3\|_{\mathrm{op}}^2}{m_I^3} $$

from the integrated post-Laplace Wick coefficients per
`[BenderOrszag1999 §6.5 Eq. 6.5.4]` and `[wong2001asymptotic §IX.5]`.
The $\tfrac{5}{24}$ on $T_3^2/m_I^3$ resolves in multidimensional
internal-mode space into the sunset topology $\tfrac{1}{12}$ and the
double-bubble topology $\tfrac{1}{8}$ of the two-loop saddle-point
correction; both are summed under the spectral operator-norm bound.

The remaining constants $C_2, C_3, C_4, C_6$ stay proposition-grade
for now; analogous Laplace-derived values for them are deferred to
future work and are scoped out of the present theorem.

## Density-vs-integrated reconciliation paragraph

A short follow-up paragraph after the theorem records the relation
to the Edgeworth density-correction coefficients $\tfrac{1}{24},
\tfrac{1}{72}$ that the original debate claim proposed. Those are
the prefactors on Hermite polynomials $H_4, H_6$ in the perturbed
Gaussian density expansion, and they reduce to the integrated Wick
coefficients $\tfrac{1}{8}, \tfrac{5}{24}$ only at the saddle
centerpoint via the central Hermite values $|H_4(0)| = 3, |H_6(0)| = 15$.
For neighborhoods of Fisher-normal radius $R > 1.126$, the sup-norm
$\|H_4\|_{L^\infty([-R, R])}$ exceeds the central value and grows
like $R^4$, so the centerpoint identification fails as an $L^\infty$
bound at large radius. This is the load-bearing reason the integrated
Wick coefficients are the correct ones under the manuscript's
existing closure norm at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:4372`.

The reconciliation paragraph is written in academic prose and gives
the reader the bridge from a potential Edgeworth-density reading to
the manuscript's integrated-free-energy reading, without re-litigating
the debate.
