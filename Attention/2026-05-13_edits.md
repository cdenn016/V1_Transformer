### Findings

**Both manuscripts contain the β-entropy term `β log(β/π)` structurally in F (not as a Lagrangian-only auxiliary).** The user's hypothesis that `GL(K)_attention.tex` was missing the entropy term is incorrect.

- `GL(K)_attention.tex:715` writes F_align as the joint KL `D_KL[Q(k,z) || P(k,z)] = Σ β q log(qβ/(Pπ))`, decomposing at line 730 to `Σ_j β_ij(E_ij + log β_ij − log π_j)` and labeling this as "energy minus entropy form" at line 734. The entropy is structural.
- `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1216-1238` writes the canonical functional with `τ Σ β log(β/π)` explicitly, with τ on the entropy term from the outset.

**Notation difference**: GL(K) introduces τ via the rescaling `E → E/τ` (line 764). This is equivalent to multiplying F_align by τ to obtain the canonical form `Σ β E + τ Σ β log(β/π)`, but the two-step equivalence was not stated.

**Envelope theorem treatment**:
- Both manuscripts invoke the envelope theorem when computing gradients of F_red.
- Both note that standard autograd through the softmax computes `∇⟨E⟩_β*`, not `∇F_red`.
- `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1338` (and 1836 second-order form) quantifies the gradient gap as `−τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∇E)`. This is the sophisticated treatment.
- `GL(K)_attention.tex` originally stated only that the two objectives "share the same critical points but have different gradient fields", which is mathematically incorrect: critical points coincide only where the same covariance vanishes (i.e., at the joint stationary point of F_red, or in the τ → ∞ limit).

### Sympy verification (verifier agent)
- Minimizer of `F(β) = Σ β_j (E_j + log β_j − log π_j)` over the simplex confirmed symbolically: `β*_j = π_j exp(−E_j)/Σ π exp(−E)`.
- Substituted value confirmed: `F* = −log Z` (sympy reduces `F* + log Z` to 0).
- Envelope identity verified numerically with `E_1(θ)=θ²`, `E_2(θ)=sin θ + 1`: `dF_red/dθ − ∂F/∂θ|_β* = 0`; cross-term `Σ (∂F/∂β)(dβ*/dθ) ≈ 2×10⁻¹⁶`.

### Verdict
`Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` has the more complete and rigorous attention derivation. It retains τ explicitly on the entropy term throughout, quantifies the autograd-vs-envelope gap in covariance form, includes the meta-attention (γ_ij) channel with its own entropy/prior, and provides both first- and second-variation forms of the surrogate-gap correction. `GL(K)_attention.tex` is cleaner pedagogically (separate subsubsections for F_align, the Lagrangian, the softmax, and τ rescaling) but loses the τ on F_align before rescaling, defers the model channel to an appendix, and previously made an incorrect critical-points claim.
