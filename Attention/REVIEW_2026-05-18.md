## 1. Convergence findings (ranked by load-bearingness)

These are the issues at least two independent reviewers raised, that the verifier confirmed against the actual text. They are the most likely real defects.

### C1. Vanishing-Holonomy "Theorem" is a one-line algebraic identity
Lines 1184–1200. Both math and physics reviewers, plus the manuscript's own line 802. The proof is literally `g_i g_j^{-1} g_j g_k^{-1} g_k g_i^{-1} = I`. In Regime I the connection is pure-gauge ($F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu} \equiv 0$), so "holonomy vanishes" is the definition of "flat," not a deduction.
**Fix:** downgrade `\begin{theorem}` to `\begin{lemma}` and rename to "Cocycle Identity" or "Flatness of the Pure-Gauge Connection."

### C2. Lorentzian-signature mechanism rests on a stack of postulates, not derivation
Lines 2230–2361. Math M-MATH-19, physics M-PHYS-3,4,7, philosophy M-PHIL-7. Verified quotes:
- Line 2277: "the construction below continues $\phi_\tau \to i\phi_\tau$ in the Lie algebra" (postulate, not Wick rotation in standard sense).
- Line 2279: single generator $T = \mathrm{diag}(1,-1)$ used for *both* temporal and spatial directions — 4D extension structurally blocked.
- Manuscript self-disclaimer at line 3536: "the four-dimensional, fully nonlinear extension is left open."
**Fix:** drop "Lorentzian-signature mechanism" from the abstract's contribution list; reframe as "existence demonstration under a stack of explicit postulates."

### C3. Mass analogy reuses the same matrix for stiffness and inertia
Physics M-PHYS-8 and the manuscript's own admission at line 1560: "the precision $\Sigma_p^{-1}$ plays both roles." The empirical $\omega^2 \cdot m_\text{eff} = \mathrm{const}$ scaling is built in by the postulate, not a prediction. Math reviewer's M-MAJOR-17 separately flags that the velocity-quadratic form (Eq. 1742) is written as if it were a conservative kinetic-energy form even though the Hessian is non-symmetric under asymmetric attention.
**Fix:** rename §"Statistical Precision as Configuration-Space Stiffness: A Mass Analogy" to drop "Mass Analogy"; state the kinetic-metric postulate at Eq. 1742 (not in a separate caveat paragraph at 1669).

### C4. Wheeler invocation rhetorically exceeds the framework's reach
Physics M-PHYS-11, philosophy M-PHIL-15, organization (Wheeler comparison table currently in Results §2882–2899). The manuscript's *own* caveat at line 2896 disclaims any derivation of delayed choice, law without law, or measurement collapse. But the title, abstract, introduction, and Conclusion (line 3683: "transform vague ideas into testable hypotheses") use Wheeler-language as if the framework realizes his program.
**Fix:** retitle to drop "Participatory It From Bit"; move the Wheeler comparison table from Results to Discussion §"Realization of Wheeler's Program"; soften Conclusion to clarify that only the transformer recovery yields concrete testable content.

### C5. "Implementation" and "Results" sections contain mostly theory
Organization review, verified line-by-line:
- "Implementation" §1753–2633 = 880 lines. Less than 30 lines (§1897–1927) describe actual implementation. Bulk is either theory (§1771–1896 meta-agent variational + RG construction) or speculative physics (§2031–2633 — 600 lines on pullback, signature, observer-dependent reality, dimensional structure, physical-quantity philosophy).
- "Results" §2634–3212 = 578 lines. §2915–3189 (274 lines) is the transformer-recovery derivation — pure theory. §2844–2914 (70 lines) is Wheeler-bootstrap philosophy. Only §2700–2843 (meta-agent simulation, single seed honestly disclosed) and §3191–3212 (WikiText scaling) are empirical.
**Fix:** see the atomic-move table in §4.

### C6. Lahav-Neemeh treatment: derived in the wrong section, and conflates diagonal invariance with per-agent covariance
Organization M-ORG-5 and philosophy M-PHIL-4. The Alice/Bob composition formula is derived inside Discussion §3503–3543 — 41 lines of theory inside Discussion. Separately, the GL(K) invariance the manuscript invokes is for the *diagonal subgroup* (same gauge on every agent, lines 543, 771, 3525), but the special-relativity analogy at line 3525 requires invariance under the *full per-agent group action* (independent Lorentz boosts per observer). The manuscript flags the diagonal restriction in two places (lines 543, 771) but does not bring that disclaimer into the Lahav-Neemeh subsection where it changes the force of the analogy.
**Fix:** move §3514–3522 (the derivation) to Theory §2.7 next to the transport definition at line 761; rewrite the SR analogy in §3503–3543 to acknowledge the diagonal-vs-full distinction.

## 2. Additional MAJOR findings (single reviewer, verified)

### Math / information geometry

**MR-1. Symbol overloads (verified).** Four distinct overloads, each used live in multiple equations:
- `s` = model field section AND scale index. Lines 524, 600, 608, 622 use both meanings in the same expression `s_i^{(s)}`.
- `κ` = Killing form (2148–2154), attention temperature `κ_β/κ_γ` (3732 etc.), inter-cluster conductance `κ_{IJ}` (1868).
- `τ` = attention/entropy temperature (1217, 1237), dimensionless information-update clock (2035), coupling strengths `τ^{(q)}_{ij}/τ^{(s)}_{ij}` (4283).
- `α_i` = per-agent precision parameter (1267–1311) AND cluster-aggregation weight (1781, 1794).
**Fix:** add a Notation subsection with global symbol table; rename collisions (`s` → `m` or `g^{\mathrm{mdl}}` for the model field, `α_i` → `w_i^I` for the cluster weight, etc.).

**MR-2. `τ = κ√K` is never written in the manuscript.** CLAUDE.md states this as the canonical attention temperature, but `\sqrt{K}` does not appear near τ anywhere. The closest formulation (line 1091) only says "rescaling the alignment energy $E_{ik} \to E_{ik}/\tau$."
**Fix:** either add the identity at line 1217 (first canonical use), or justify its omission.

**MR-3. `λ_h` (hyper-prior weight) used before defined.** First appears at line 1943 inside the meta-agent RG free energy with no introduction. The body's canonical free energy at Eq. 1237 uses unit weight on the model-prior term.
**Fix:** introduce `λ_h` explicitly in Eq. 1237.

**MR-4. `thm:rg_exact_closure` (line 4586) overstates closure.** The "augmentation" $V(Y) = (\tau/2)\log\det' A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ generally lies outside the Gaussian-KL class $\mathfrak{M}$. The manuscript itself admits this at line 4596 ("**augmented by a smooth scalar potential**").
**Fix:** restructure as two claims — (i) strict closure iff $A_{\xi\xi}$ is $Y$-independent, (ii) controlled deviation otherwise — and rename to "Schur-Laplace reduction with closure obstruction."

**MR-5. Conditional Representation Theorem `thm:uniqueness_app` (line 4423) hides real-analyticity in the proof.** The Richness Lemma at line 4482 explicitly invokes real-analyticity to extend pointwise identity to all of $(0,\infty)$. Theorem statement does not list analyticity as a hypothesis.
**Fix:** move analyticity into the theorem statement.

**MR-6. Homogeneous limit at line 4159 silently drops $\Lambda_o$.** Listed assumptions (i)–(iii) at lines 4148–4154 omit "$\Lambda_o = 0$" but the conclusion $\Sigma_\infty = \Sigma_0$ requires it.
**Fix:** add assumption (iv) or write $\Sigma_\infty = (\Sigma_0^{-1} + \Lambda_o)^{-1}$.

**MR-7. Self-coupling $i=j$ in the attention sum is admitted by Eq. 1217 entropy term but never declared.** The KL term vanishes at $i=j$, but the entropy term $\tau\beta_{ii}\log(\beta_{ii}/\pi_{ii})$ is nonzero and the row constraint $\sum_j\beta_{ij}=1$ assigns positive mass to the self-edge.
**Fix:** state explicitly whether self-attention is admitted (it is in transformers); if not, restrict sums to $i \neq j$ everywhere.

**MR-8. Off-diagonal mass-block transpose check (Eq. 1665) relies on the cocycle identity $\Omega_{ik}^{-1} = \Omega_{ki}$, which holds in Regime I but fails in Regime II.**
**Fix:** annotate Eqs. 1665, 1697 with the Regime I restriction.

### Physics / gauge theory

**PH-1. `Ω_{ij} = U_iU_j^{-1}` is identified as both a transition function (line 557, "Čech cocycle") and an inter-agent transport operator (line 759).** The principal-bundle vocabulary is loaned in to dignify what is in fact a globally trivial structure with one chart up to gauge equivalence. The lattice gauge content lives in the separate Regime II edge variables `δ_{ij}` (lines 806–859) which the simulations do not use.
**Fix:** state up front in §2.3 that the bundle is being treated as globally trivial via the smooth field $\phi_i$, and that the Čech-cocycle apparatus is invoked only because Regime II promotes the edges to independent variables.

**PH-2. Theorem `thm:glk_invariance` (line 917) overstates GL(K) as "the natural" gauge group.** The proof (lines 922–963) actually shows that any invertible push-forward preserves KL — which is true for any invertible map, not specifically GL(K). What is special is that GL(K) is the maximal *linear* group preserving the Gaussian family.
**Fix:** rename theorem to "GL(K) Preserves Gaussian KL Under Common Pushforward"; drop "natural symmetry" rhetoric.

**PH-3. The "Kinetic Term Missed" framing (line 3220) misrepresents the active-inference literature.** Friston and collaborators use first-order natural-gradient flow because synaptic time constants dominate over any putative inertial term in cortical inference — not because they "missed" the kinetic term. The gap the manuscript claims to identify is created by its own decision to read the variational principle as fundamental physics.
**Fix:** rewrite the section title to "Why the Kinetic Term Is Discarded in Biological Active Inference," explicitly state the present framework's reintroduction is contingent on the physics-fundamental reading.

**PH-4. "Pullback-Curvature Reading of Gravity" (lines 3230–3267) does not produce a stress-energy tensor or Einstein equations.** The manuscript admits at line 3255: "no analog of the Einstein field equations has been derived." Eq. 3243 (curvature–precision relation) is "an ansatz, not a derived consequence."
**Fix:** retitle to "Information-Geometric Curvature: A Structural Analogy to Gravity"; cut "potential resolution" language.

**PH-5. "Macroscopic Objects as Consensus Enforcers" (3268–3286) and "Inferential-Consensus Measurement" (3287–3309) are stipulations, not predictions.** Manuscript admits at 3270 ("a stipulation extending the multi-scale meta-agent construction by analogy") and at 3308 ("requires extending the framework to include quantum superposition states, which remains future work"). Despite both admissions, the surrounding prose uses "framework suggests a potential resolution to the measurement problem."
**Fix:** cut measurement-problem language until a quantum extension exists.

**PH-6. The §"Gauge Field Strength: Path-Dependent Information Transport" (lines 794–805) is structurally inactive in the simulations** — $F_{\mu\nu}^{(i)} = 0$ identically in Regime I (manuscript's own line 802).
**Fix:** fold into the Regime II discussion at line 806; drop as standalone subsection.

### Philosophy

**PL-1. Kantian framing is incoherent.** Introduction §91 disavows the constitutive commitment that does the work in Kant ("framework does not commit to the substrate being structurally inaccessible in Kant's sense") but §6.7.3 line 2553 then claims to "**strengthen** this claim." Strip the constitutive a priori and the position is neo-Kantian structural realism (Cassirer, Worrall, Ladyman & Ross) — not Kant.
**Fix:** either commit to a Friedman-style "relativized constitutive a priori" (Friedman 2001) and engage the secondary literature, or relabel as structural realism and demote Kant to historical analogy.

**PL-2. The gauge-invariance-as-consensus argument (§3402–3450) ignores Kretschmann's century-old objection.** Kretschmann (1917) and Norton (1993) establish that general covariance / coordinate invariance is empirically empty content and is distinct from gauge invariance proper (which carries specific empirical consequences — Higgs, confinement, chiral structure). Grep returns zero matches for "Kretschmann" or "Norton" in the file. Cognitive shareability would at most motivate the empty version; it leaves the substantive structure entirely unexplained.
**Fix:** address Kretschmann directly. This is the philosophical bug.

**PL-3. Hard Problem "relocation" is structurally identical to Russellian neutral monism / panpsychism / IIT** and is not credited as such. The relocation move — locating phenomenal character in the intrinsic structure of some non-physical layer — is shared by Russell (1927), Strawson (2006), Goff (2017), Tononi & Koch (2015).
**Fix:** explicitly state the relocation strategy is shared with these positions; articulate what additional explanatory work the gauge-theoretic vocabulary does (if any).

**PL-4. QBism is mischaracterized.** QBism's central commitment is decision-theoretic personalism — *one* agent's degrees of belief, not a shared state. Fuchs has been explicit that QBism *resists* multi-agent consensus extensions. The closer relative is Rovelli's RQM. (Lines 162, 3320–3323.)
**Fix:** state the disagreement plainly; reposition as a contrast case.

**PL-5. The pan-agentic ontology is buried in §1.7 Scope (line 142) but carries the heaviest explanatory load** in the framework. It enables the cross-scale Alice/Bob factorisation (§3.5), underwrites the qualia route (§7.4), carries the meta-agent emergence (§4).
**Fix:** promote to §1.4 with explicit defence; address: what makes an electron's "gauge frame" different from a description of its state in GL(K)?

**PL-6. "Testable predictions" in §7.4.4 (lines 3493–3502) are not predictions of *this* framework.** Two of the four overlap with predictive-processing predictions; one overlaps with IIT's $\Phi$; one (altered states = gauge-frame shifts) has no operational definition in neural terms.
**Fix:** supply an experimental protocol that would confirm a prediction here while disconfirming IIT or active inference simpliciter, or remove the "testable predictions" header.

**PL-7. Three-column falsifiability ledger.** Verified by the philosophy reviewer with quoted line numbers:
- *Falsifiable (and in some cases tested):* transformer recovery (§5), scaling exponent $b \approx -1$ (§5.2.1), meta-agent threshold-detector behaviour, attention–dependency-parse correlation (§7.3.2 conjecture), $M_\text{eff} \propto \Sigma_p^{-1}$ (§4).

- *Presented as testable, on closer reading not:* dimensionless-constants conjecture (line 2541, "failure would not falsify the framework"); gauge-curvature conjecture (3367, "strongest testable prediction" — but vacuous in implemented Regime I); observable-sector (3+1) hypothesis (2475); altered-states gauge-frame shift (3501); qualia inversion under gauge-equivalent assignments (3475).
