# Cover letter to Dr. Nir Lahav

Dear Dr. Lahav,

I read your 2022 Frontiers paper and the 2025 arXiv shortened version of *A Relativistic Theory of Consciousness* with a particular shock of recognition. I have been working independently on a gauge-theoretic variational free energy framework for transformer-style attention, and the resulting machinery has, on its own internal logic, arrived at the same conclusion you and Neemeh argue for: that phenomenal experience is observer-relative in the way velocity is observer-relative, and that what plays the role of an inertial frame in physics is what you call a cognitive frame of reference. I had no idea your paper existed when the relevant sections of my draft were written. I would not have used the phrase "cognitive reference frame" had I encountered it earlier; I converged on it because the gauge-theoretic structure forces the language.

The attached manuscript, *A Theoretical and Computational Implementation of a Participatory "It From Bit" Universe*, is the long-form write-up of that framework. The Lahav-relevant content is concentrated in three places; the rest of the manuscript provides the technical scaffolding but is not required for the philosophical engagement:

1. **Section 2.7, "Cognitive Reference Frames as Gauge Frames"** introduces the construction. Each agent carries a smooth gauge frame field $\phi_i: \mathcal{C} \to \mathfrak{gl}(K)$, and the transport operator $\Omega_{ij} = e^{\phi_i} e^{-\phi_j}$ provides an explicit transformation between cognitive frames, acting on belief means by left multiplication and on belief covariances by the sandwich product $\Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top$. KL divergences are invariant under simultaneous push-forward by any element of $\mathrm{GL}(K)$. This is the group action and the invariant structure that an explicit relativistic formalisation of cognitive frames would require; it is what made me read your paper as soon as I learned of it.

2. **Section 9.10, "Independent Convergence with Lahav and Neemeh's Relativistic Theory of Consciousness"** is the section I would most like your reaction to. It articulates three points of mathematical contact between your framework and mine: the gauge group as your transformation between cognitive frames, the gauge-equivariant free energy as the invariant scalar analogous to the spacetime interval, and your "identical neural dynamics imply identical experience" postulate as a consequence of section identity in our setting, conditional on a functionalist bridging assumption that I would value your reaction to. I also flag two honest divergences: my framework commits to information-geometric structuralism — different from your relational/measurement-theoretic physicalism in detail, but in the same broad neighbourhood — and neither of us has a second postulate analogous to the constancy of the speed of light to fix the transformation group uniquely.

3. **Section 9.9, "Consciousness and Hierarchical Information Integration"**, immediately preceding it, sets up the gauge-frame interpretation of qualia and the relocation of the hard problem. I should note that the manuscript's discussion of the hard problem was dissolutionist in places in earlier drafts and relocationist in others; the relocationist framing is the one the formalism actually supports, and the current version is consistent on this point — the question is moved from "why does neural computation have felt character?" to "why does information-geometric structure have felt character?", with the explanandum shifted, not eliminated.

I want to be candid about what this manuscript does and does not do, since you will see this stated up front in the abstract and in Section 1.7 (Scope and Limitations). The 0-dimensional gauge theory rigorously recovers standard transformer attention and I have a working language model trained without learned attention projections, MLPs, or pointwise activation functions; that part is empirical, and there is a separate companion paper with the full statistical treatment (multi-seed scaling on WikiText-103 with bootstrap confidence intervals, scaling exponent $b = -1.05$ [-1.10, -1.00]). The Lorentzian-signature construction holds in a 2D linearised worked example and is open in four nonlinear dimensions; the imaginary temporal component is imposed by hand in the construction, and whether it would emerge dynamically from free-energy minimisation remains the central open question. There are no quantitative physics predictions and no rigorous quantum extension; an earlier draft contained a speculative C₆₀ mass-deficit prediction which I have removed, since it was conditional on a quantum extension that does not yet exist and therefore was not in fact testable in the Popperian sense. I am not asking you to endorse the speculative parts.

What I would value most is your judgement on whether the gauge-theoretic structure is in fact the formal machinery your relativistic-consciousness proposal calls for, or whether you see the cognitive frame of reference as a different kind of object altogether. If the former, I think there is a productive collaboration possible; if the latter, I would learn a great deal from being told why. Either way, the intellectual debt to your reframing of phenomenal consciousness as a frame-relative quantity is real, the manuscript now cites it, and I wanted you to see the connection from me directly rather than discover it later.

Thank you for the time. The manuscript is messy in the way independent first drafts are messy; I would rather you see it now than wait for it to be polished out of recognition.
