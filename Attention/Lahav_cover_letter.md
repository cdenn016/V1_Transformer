# Cover letter to Professor Zohar Lahav and Dr. Zakaria Neemeh

Dear Professor Lahav and Dr. Neemeh,

I am writing to share a manuscript, *Participatory It-from-Bit: A Gauge-Theoretic Variational Free Energy Framework*, that arrives independently at a structural overlap with your Relativistic Theory of Consciousness from a stronger ontological starting point I would rather state up front than bury. The framework treats every system at every scale as an agent carrying its own gauge frame and Gaussian belief tuple $(\mu, \Sigma, \phi)$, with transport between agents given by $\Omega_{ab} = \exp(\phi_a) \exp(-\phi_b)$ and a gauge-equivariant variational free energy in the role your account assigns to a frame-independent invariant. The substantive contribution I would value your judgement on is that, under a pan-agentic, multi-scale ontology, the structural map your Alice/Bob thought experiment asserts but does not write down is recovered as a derived composition of two transports of the same form, and your cognitive frames of reference fall out as the human-cognitive slice of a hierarchy whose constituent--meta-agent and inter-agent transports are unified under a single group action.

The pan-agentic commitment is the substantive divergence and belongs first. Your account is single-scale: the carriers of cognitive frames are cognitive systems in roughly the human-cognitive sense, and the relativistic move denies those frames any privileged status. The framework here makes the stronger claim that there are no non-agent physical things at all. Scale-0 electrons, molecules, cells, brains, and ecosystems each carry $(\mu, \Sigma, \phi)$ and a section of the associated bundle, and the constituent--meta-agent transport $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)}) \exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$ is a cross-scale instance of the same group action that relates two agents at a single scale. This is a pan-agentic structuralist position, not the relational physicalist one I read your papers as defending; §\ref{sec:scope_limitations} owns it as a philosophical premise rather than slipping it in tacitly.

A direct consequence is that your Alice/Bob isomorphism becomes a derived special case rather than a posited structural map. Treating Alice and Bob as scale-$N$ meta-agents and Alice's neurons as her scale-$(N-k)$ constituents, the relation between Alice's first-person section and Bob's third-person measurement of Alice factors as $\Omega_{\mathrm{Bob}, i} = \exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Bob}}^{(N)}) \exp(-\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)}) \cdot \exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)}) \exp(-\phi_i^{(N-k)})$: an inter-agent factor at scale $N$ composed with a cross-scale factor internal to Alice. Both factors are instances of the unified transport law and neither is assumed. Your single-scale isomorphism is the case in which the cross-scale factor reduces to the identity.

Three points of departure are labelled candidly in §\ref{sec:lahav_convergence}. The first is substance and ontology: relational physicalism in your case versus pan-agentic structuralism here, with agentic structure constituting physical reality rather than perspectivally accessing a substrate beneath it. The second is the multi-scale extension, which licenses the Alice/Bob derivation and has no place in a single-scale account. The third concerns the second postulate: your relativistic analogy proceeds without an analogue of the speed of light, and the manuscript does not supply one either. What it offers instead is a Yang--Mills kinetic term on $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{C})$ frames under which a worked two-dimensional example produces Lorentzian signature dynamically rather than by stipulation, with the four-dimensional nonlinear extension left open.

I want to pre-empt the natural rebuttal that the relational reading already dissolves the qualia-indeterminacy problem and that the structuralist commitment merely re-introduces it. On your account the absence of an observer-independent fact about which gauge frame produces redness is constitutive: the demand for one presupposes the absolutist framing the relational move rejects, and the indeterminacy is the dissolution rather than a residual difficulty. The structuralist route accepts the same group-theoretic indeterminacy at a single scale but conjectures that the assignment is fixed one level down, by the agent's specific multi-scale constituent composition; the cross-scale piece $\Omega_{i,I}$ is determined once the constituent frames and consensus weights are. I do not claim the conjecture is established. Both routes are flagged as open, and I would value your reading of which, if either, you find live.

The pan-agentic commitment incurs an explanatory debt your single-scale account does not, and I want to own it. The consensus-aggregation construction in §\ref{sec:participatory} supplies the mathematical aggregation of constituent frames into a meta-agent frame and is gauge-covariant by construction; it does not supply a phenomenal-aggregation argument. Nothing in the formal construction settles whether the meta-agent's pullback metric inherits anything experiential from the constituent metrics, whether the inheritance fails at certain scale jumps, or whether scale-0 frames carry phenomenal character at all. Why electrons should carry $(q, p, \phi)$ in the same sense humans do is the central open piece of work the position raises, and it is what would have to be done to convert the structuralist route on qualia indeterminacy from a relocation into a derivation.

The manuscript does not deliver quantitative physics predictions, a rigorous quantum extension, or a solution to the hard problem; on the structuralist reading the hard problem is relocated rather than solved, and on the relational reading the residue is constitutive. What I would defend is the mathematical content: an explicit non-compact transformation group, a gauge-equivariant invariant, a covariance transport law via the sandwich product $\Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top$, and an explicit account of why first-person and third-person measurements relate as you describe.

I would be grateful for your judgement on the structural identification, on whether the pan-agentic extension reads as a genuine continuation of your project or as a different project sharing vocabulary, and on whether the structuralist reframing of qualia indeterminacy is intelligible from the relational standpoint. The manuscript and supporting code are available in the project repository, and I am happy to share an arXiv link once it is posted.
