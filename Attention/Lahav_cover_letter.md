# Cover letter to Dr. Nir Lahav

Dear Dr. Lahav,

I read your 2022 Frontiers paper and the 2025 arXiv shortened version of *A Relativistic Theory of Consciousness* with a particular shock of recognition. I have been working independently on a gauge-theoretic variational free energy framework for transformer-style attention, and the resulting machinery has, on its own internal logic, arrived at the same conclusion you and Neemeh argue for: that phenomenal experience is observer-relative in the way velocity is observer-relative, and that what plays the role of an inertial frame in physics is what you call a cognitive frame of reference. I had no idea your paper existed when the relevant sections of my draft were written. I would not have used the phrase "cognitive reference frame" had I encountered it earlier; I converged on it because the gauge-theoretic structure forces the language.

The attached manuscript, *A Theoretical and Computational Implementation of a Participatory "It From Bit" Universe*, is the long-form write-up of that framework. The Lahav-relevant content is concentrated in three places, and I would not blame you for reading only those before deciding whether the rest is of interest:

1. **Section 2.7, "Cognitive Reference Frames as Gauge Frames"** introduces the construction. Each agent carries a smooth gauge frame field $\phi_i: \mathcal{C} \to \mathfrak{gl}(K)$, and the transport operator $\Omega_{ij} = e^{\phi_i} e^{-\phi_j}$ provides an explicit transformation between cognitive frames, acting on belief means by left multiplication and on belief covariances by the sandwich product $\Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top$. KL divergences are invariant under simultaneous push-forward by any element of $\mathrm{GL}(K)$. This is the group action and the invariant structure your account requires; it is what made me read your paper as soon as I learned of it.

2. **Section 9.10, "Independent Convergence with Lahav and Neemeh's Relativistic Theory of Consciousness"** is the section I would most like your reaction to. It articulates three points of mathematical contact between your framework and mine: the gauge group as your transformation between cognitive frames, the gauge-equivariant free energy as the invariant scalar analogous to the spacetime interval, and your "identical neural dynamics imply identical experience" postulate as a consequence of section identity in our setting. I also flag two honest divergences --- my framework commits to neutral monism rather than relational physicalism, and neither of us has a second postulate analogous to the constancy of the speed of light to fix the transformation group uniquely.

3. **Section 9.9, "Consciousness and Hierarchical Information Integration"**, immediately preceding it, sets up the gauge-frame interpretation of qualia and the "dissolution rather than solution" reading of the hard problem. The dissolution, here as in your account, comes at the cost of accepting that whether a system is conscious has no observer-independent fact of the matter.

I want to be candid about what this manuscript does and does not do, since you will see this stated up front in the abstract and in Section 1.7 (Scope and Limitations). The 0-dimensional gauge theory rigorously recovers standard transformer attention and I have a working language model trained without learned attention projections, MLPs, or pointwise activation functions; that part is empirical. The Lorentzian-signature construction holds in a 2D linearized worked example and is open in four nonlinear dimensions. There are no quantitative physics predictions, no rigorous quantum extension, and the consciousness section reframes the hard problem rather than solving it. I am not asking you to endorse the speculative parts.

What I would value most is your judgement on whether the gauge-theoretic structure is in fact the formal machinery your relativistic-consciousness proposal calls for, or whether you see the cognitive frame of reference as a different kind of object altogether. If the former, I think there is a productive collaboration possible; if the latter, I would learn a great deal from being told why. Either way, the intellectual debt to your reframing of phenomenal consciousness as a frame-relative quantity is real, the manuscript now cites it, and I wanted you to see the connection from me directly rather than discover it later.

Thank you for the time. The manuscript is messy in the way independent first drafts are messy; I would rather you see it now than wait for it to be polished out of recognition.

With respect,
