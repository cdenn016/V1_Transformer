## Round 16 — Calibration Pass on Five Reviewer Criticisms (commit 354d9613)


Round of surgical corrections to `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` addressing five
calibration criticisms (overclaim relative to evidence). Each edit is targeted;
existing hedged language in the manuscript was retained where already adequate.

## Criticism 1 — Title and abstract overclaim

Status: no edit.

The current title is `A Gauge-Theoretic Framework Toward a Participatory "It From Bit"
Program: Mathematical Foundations and Computational Implementation`. The leading
"Toward" already matches the reviewer's safer suggestion ("Toward Participatory
It-from-Bit via Gauge-Covariant Variational Inference"), and the abstract already
hedges with "We prove that, under a zero-dimensional isotropic-Gaussian limit,
the KL-consensus weights reduce to scaled dot-product attention", "speculative
extensions", and "We state these limitations explicitly in Section ...". No further
title or abstract change required.

## Criticism 2 — Transformer derivation overstated

Two edits in `sec:transformers`:

- Lead paragraph rewritten to state that standard scaled dot-product attention
  is "recovered as a gauge-fixed and isotropic-Gaussian limit, not derived
  uniquely from the gauge-theoretic framework". Added explicit acknowledgement
  that, once a learned bilinear $M$ is introduced, factorisation $M = W_Q W_K^\top$
  is essentially algebraic, so the result is a consistency statement rather than
  a uniqueness claim.
- `\subsubsection{Derivation of Dot-Product Attention}` renamed to
  `\subsubsection{Recovery of Dot-Product Attention as a Gauge-Fixed Limit}`.

The body of `sec:dot_product_derivation` was already careful (it states the three
limits explicitly, treats $M$ as a separate object, and flags the rank-deficient
multi-head case via thin SVD); no further body changes were needed.

## Criticism 3 — Observation/environmental-agent proposition overstated

Edit at `sec:formal_equivalence` (line ~1376). The proposition statement previously
asserted full functional gradient equivalence,
$\delta\mathcal{F}_{\text{obs}}/\delta q_i = \delta\mathcal{F}_{\text{agent}}/\delta q_i$,
which contradicted the explicit derivation below (mean-gradient equivalence only;
covariance gradients differ by $-\tfrac{1}{2}\Sigma_i^{-1}$).

Rewrote the proposition itself to state mean-gradient equivalence as the primary
claim, and full variational equivalence as conditional on either a cross-entropy
substitution or a fixed-covariance restriction. The prose below the proposition
already developed this distinction correctly; no body changes were needed.

## Criticism 4 — Mass section overclaim

Two edits in `sec:mass`:

- Section title `Mass from Statistical Precision` renamed to
  `Statistical Precision as Configuration-Space Stiffness: A Mass Analogy`.
  Lead paragraph rewritten to call the Hessian a "stiffness tensor" rather than
  a "configuration-space metric", and explicitly states that the construction is
  not a derivation of physical inertial mass.
- Subsection title `Physical Interpretation: Mass as Precision (within the framework)`
  renamed to `Within-Framework Interpretation: Stiffness as Precision (Mass Analogy)`.
- Deleted the sentence "Conversely, a quantum particle in spatial superposition
  has larger position uncertainty, lower precision, and a smaller effective mass."
  Replaced with an explicit non-extension: spatial delocalization does not imply
  lower inertial mass in standard quantum mechanics, and the framework's effective
  mass is a configuration-space stiffness not equated with physical inertial mass.

The leading "What the Hessian gives, and what is added" paragraph was already
strong on the kinetic-postulate distinction and is retained verbatim.

## Criticism 5 — Conditional uniqueness theorem mislabelled

Three edits in `app:conditional_uniqueness`:

- Appendix title renamed: `Conditional Uniqueness of the Forward KL Divergence`
  → `Conditional Representation Theorem for the Forward KL Divergence`.
- Theorem environment annotated as `[Conditional representation]`; the heading
  `Conditional Uniqueness Theorem` renamed to `Conditional Representation Theorem`.
- Intro paragraph rewritten to call the result "a representation theorem rather
  than an unconditional uniqueness theorem", and to state explicitly that without
  the richness assumption the term-by-term comparison does not extend to a
  functional identity.
- The "Dual Cost Identification" lead-in updated from "Having established
  $D_{\mathrm{KL}}$ is the unique divergence" to "Conditional on the richness
  assumption above, $D_{\mathrm{KL}}$ is the unique $f$-divergence in the

The richness-lemma argument itself (varying $p_i(c)$ and $q_j(c)$ to span
$(0,\infty)$) is retained; the theorem label `thm:uniqueness_app` is preserved
to avoid breaking any cross-references.
