# Derivation: `W_Q W_K^T` as a Factorization of the Gauge-Induced Attention Kernel

This note gives a step-by-step derivation of the statement:

$$
W_Q W_K^T
\quad\text{is a particular factorization of the score-relevant gauge kernel, not usually of raw }\Omega_{ij}.
$$

The precise object being factorized is the bilinear form appearing in the Gaussian KL attention logit. In the isotropic constant-transport limit this kernel is

$$
B = \sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
$$

Thus the correct identification is

$$
W_Q W_K^T = B = \sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T},
$$

or, equivalently,

$$
\Omega = \sigma^{-2}(W_Q W_K^T)^{-T}
$$

when the head-space product `W_Q W_K^T` is square and invertible.

---

## 1. Conventions

All vectors are column vectors.

A Gaussian belief is written

$$
q_i = \mathcal N(\mu_i, \Sigma_i).
$$

A gauge transport from token/agent `j` into token/agent `i`'s frame is

$$
\Omega_{ij}: E_j \to E_i.
$$

The transported Gaussian is

$$
\Omega_{ij\#}q_j
=
\mathcal N(\Omega_{ij}\mu_j,\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T).
$$

Transformer-style query and key projections are taken as

$$
Q_i = W_Q^T\mu_i,
\qquad
K_j = W_K^T\mu_j.
$$

Therefore the standard bilinear score is

$$
Q_i^T K_j
=
\mu_i^T W_Q W_K^T\mu_j.
$$

So the product

$$
M := W_QW_K^T
$$

is the ambient bilinear kernel that determines the attention score.

---

## 2. Gaussian KL with transport

For two Gaussians

$$
q_i = \mathcal N(\mu_i,\Sigma_i),
\qquad
\Omega_{ij\#}q_j
=
\mathcal N(\Omega_{ij}\mu_j,\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T),
$$

the Gaussian KL divergence is

$$
\begin{aligned}
D_{ij}
&:=
D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i\|\Omega_{ij\#}q_j)
\\
&=
\frac12
\Bigg[
\log\frac{\det(\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T)}{\det\Sigma_i}
-K
+
\operatorname{tr}\left((\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T)^{-1}\Sigma_i\right)
\\
&\qquad\qquad
+
(\mu_i-\Omega_{ij}\mu_j)^T
(\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T)^{-1}
(\mu_i-\Omega_{ij}\mu_j)
\Bigg].
\end{aligned}
$$

The attention weights are generated by the entropy-regularized KL source-selection rule

$$
\beta_{ij}
=
\operatorname{softmax}_j\left(-\frac{D_{ij}}{\tau}\right).
$$

---

## 3. Isotropic covariance limit

Assume a common isotropic covariance

$$
\Sigma_i=\Sigma_j=\sigma^2 I.
$$

Then

$$
\Omega_{ij}\Sigma_j\Omega_{ij}^T
=
\sigma^2\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{ij}^T.
$$

The KL becomes

$$
D_{ij}
=
S(\Omega_{ij})
+
\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}
\|\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j\|^2,
$$

where the transport-distortion term is

$$
S(\Omega_{ij})
=
\frac12
\left[
\log\det(\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{ij}^T)
+
\operatorname{tr}\left((\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{ij}^T)^{-1}\right)
-K
\right].
$$

The identity used above is

$$
\begin{aligned}
& (\mu_i-\Omega_{ij}\mu_j)^T
(\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{ij}^T)^{-1}
(\mu_i-\Omega_{ij}\mu_j)
\\
&\qquad=
(\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j)^T
(\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j)
\\
&\qquad=
\|\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j\|^2.
\end{aligned}
$$

---

## 4. Expanding the quadratic term

Expand the mean-mismatch term:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\|\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j\|^2
&=
(\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j)^T
(\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i-\mu_j)
\\
&=
\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}^{-T}\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i
-2\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}^{-T}\mu_j
+
\mu_j^T\mu_j.
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
D_{ij}
&=
S(\Omega_{ij})
+
\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}^{-T}\Omega_{ij}^{-1}\mu_i
-
\frac{1}{\sigma^2}\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}^{-T}\mu_j
+
\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_j^T\mu_j.
\end{aligned}
$$

The score-relevant cross term is

$$
\frac{1}{\sigma^2}\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}^{-T}\mu_j.
$$

Thus the score kernel is

$$
B_{ij} := \sigma^{-2}\Omega_{ij}^{-T}.
$$

This is the object that transformer-style factors `W_Q` and `W_K` split.

---

## 5. Constant transport specialization

Standard transformer projections are layer/head parameters and do not usually vary with the token pair `(i,j)`. Therefore the closest degenerate transformer limit assumes

$$
\Omega_{ij}=\Omega
\qquad\text{for all }i,j.
$$

Then

$$
S(\Omega)
$$

is constant in `j` and cancels inside the row-wise softmax. The query-side quadratic term

$$
\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_i^T\Omega^{-T}\Omega^{-1}\mu_i
$$

is also constant in `j`, so it cancels inside the row-wise softmax.

The remaining logit is

$$
\ell_{ij}
=
\frac{1}{\sigma^2}\mu_i^T\Omega^{-T}\mu_j
-
\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_j^T\mu_j.
$$

The key-norm term

$$
-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\mu_j^T\mu_j
$$

is not automatically zero. The exact result at this stage is Gaussian/RBF-style attention with a key-norm bias.

If key norms are constant, normalized, concentrated, or absorbed into a key bias, then the effective dot-product logit is

$$
\ell_{ij}
\simeq
\mu_i^T B\mu_j,
\qquad
B=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
$$

---

## 6. Factorizing the gauge-induced score kernel

Assume now that we work inside a square head fiber

$$
E_h\cong\mathbb R^{d_h},
$$

and that

$$
\Omega\in\mathrm{GL}(d_h).
$$

Then

$$
B = \sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}
$$

is also invertible:

$$
B\in\mathrm{GL}(d_h).
$$

Any invertible matrix admits a factorization

$$
B=W_QW_K^T.
$$

For example, take an SVD

$$
B=U\Lambda V^T,
$$

where all singular values are positive because `B` is invertible. Then define

$$
W_Q=U\Lambda^{1/2},
\qquad
W_K=V\Lambda^{1/2}.
$$

Then

$$
W_QW_K^T
=
U\Lambda^{1/2}(V\Lambda^{1/2})^T
=
U\Lambda V^T
=
B.
$$

Therefore

$$
W_QW_K^T
=
\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\Omega
=
\sigma^{-2}(W_QW_K^T)^{-T}.
$$

So, in a square full-rank head fiber,

$$
\boxed{
W_QW_K^T\text{ is a factorization of the gauge-induced score kernel }B=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
}
$$

It is more precise to say this than to say that `W_Q W_K^T` directly factorizes raw `Omega`.

---

## 7. Nonuniqueness of the query-key split

The factorization

$$
B=W_QW_K^T
$$

is not unique. For any invertible matrix

$$
R\in\mathrm{GL}(d_h),
$$

we have

$$
B
=
(W_QR)(W_KR^{-T})^T.
$$

Indeed,

$$
(W_QR)(W_KR^{-T})^T
=
W_QR(R^{-T})^TW_K^T
=
W_QRR^{-1}W_K^T
=
W_QW_K^T.
$$

Thus `W_Q` and `W_K` individually are not unique geometric objects. The invariant object is the product

$$
M=W_QW_K^T,
$$

which represents the score kernel

$$
B=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
$$

---

## 8. Why this is not the same as a full ambient `GL(d_model)` transport

In a standard multi-head transformer, per-head projections usually have the form

$$
W_Q^h,W_K^h\in\mathbb R^{d_{\mathrm{model}}\times d_h},
\qquad
 d_h < d_{\mathrm{model}}.
$$

The ambient product is

$$
M_{\mathrm{amb}}^h
=
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T
\in
\mathbb R^{d_{\mathrm{model}}\times d_{\mathrm{model}}}.
$$

Its rank satisfies

$$
\operatorname{rank}(M_{\mathrm{amb}}^h)
\le d_h.
$$

Therefore, if

$$
d_h<d_{\mathrm{model}},
$$

then `M_amb^h` is generally singular and cannot be an element of

$$
\mathrm{GL}(d_{\mathrm{model}}).
$$

So the correct statement is not

$$
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T\in\mathrm{GL}(d_{\mathrm{model}}).
$$

The correct statement is that the ambient matrix is a low-rank lift of an invertible head-space kernel.

---

## 9. Head-space rank factorization

Assume full column rank:

$$
\operatorname{rank}(W_Q^h)
=
\operatorname{rank}(W_K^h)
=d_h.
$$

Then each rectangular projection admits a thin factorization

$$
W_Q^h=U_Q^hA_Q^h,
\qquad
W_K^h=U_K^hA_K^h,
$$

where

$$
U_Q^h,U_K^h\in\mathbb R^{d_{\mathrm{model}}\times d_h}
$$

have orthonormal columns, and

$$
A_Q^h,A_K^h\in\mathrm{GL}(d_h).
$$

Then

$$
\begin{aligned}
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T
&=
U_Q^hA_Q^h(A_K^h)^T(U_K^h)^T
\\
&=
U_Q^hM_h(U_K^h)^T,
\end{aligned}
$$

where

$$
M_h:=A_Q^h(A_K^h)^T\in\mathrm{GL}(d_h).
$$

The gauge identification applies to the head-space kernel:

$$
M_h
=
\sigma^{-2}\Omega_h^{-T}.
$$

Thus

$$
\Omega_h
=
\sigma^{-2}M_h^{-T}.
$$

The ambient product is not itself the gauge transport. It is the low-rank lift

$$
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T
=
U_Q^h\left(\sigma^{-2}\Omega_h^{-T}\right)(U_K^h)^T.
$$

This resolves the rank objection.

---

## 10. Rank-deficient heads

If either projection has rank

$$
r_h<d_h,
$$

then the effective head dimension is only `r_h`. In that case the invertible factorization exists only on the image subspace:

$$
\operatorname{im}(W_Q^h),
\qquad
\operatorname{im}(W_K^h).
$$

One should either replace the head fiber by the effective rank-`r_h` image fiber or use a regularized/pseudoinverse covariance formulation.

The clean theorem requires

$$
\operatorname{rank}(W_Q^h)
=
\operatorname{rank}(W_K^h)
=d_h.
$$

A bilinear matrix

$$
M_{\mathrm{amb}}\in\mathbb R^{d_{\mathrm{model}}\times d_{\mathrm{model}}}
$$

can be represented by a `d_h`-dimensional head iff

$$
\operatorname{rank}(M_{\mathrm{amb}})\le d_h.
$$

---

## 11. Pair-dependent transports

The full gauge framework uses pair-dependent transports

$$
\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1}.
$$

Then the score kernel is pair-dependent:

$$
B_{ij}=\sigma^{-2}\Omega_{ij}^{-T}.
$$

Using

$$
\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1},
$$

we get

$$
\Omega_{ij}^{-T}
=
U_i^{-T}U_j^T.
$$

Therefore

$$
B_{ij}
=
\sigma^{-2}U_i^{-T}U_j^T.
$$

The cross term becomes

$$
\mu_i^T B_{ij}\mu_j
=
\sigma^{-2}\mu_i^T U_i^{-T}U_j^T\mu_j.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\mu_i^T B_{ij}\mu_j
=
\sigma^{-2}(U_i^{-1}\mu_i)^T(U_j^T\mu_j).
$$

Thus the pairwise score kernel is carved into a query-side factor and a key-side factor by the local frames `U_i` and `U_j`.

For orthogonal frames,

$$
U_i^{-1}=U_i^T,
\qquad
U_j^T=U_j^{-1},
$$

so the factors become symmetric frame-normalized coordinates:

$$
Q_i=U_i^T\mu_i,
\qquad
K_j=U_j^T\mu_j.
$$

Then

$$
\mu_i^T\Omega_{ij}\mu_j
=
Q_i^TK_j
$$

for the orthogonal score convention, and the pairwise transport is represented directly by relative rotations.

---

## 12. Why fixed `W_Q,W_K` cannot represent arbitrary pairwise `Omega_ij`

A standard transformer head uses fixed learned matrices

$$
W_Q^h,
\qquad
W_K^h.
$$

Therefore the kernel

$$
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T
$$

is fixed for the layer/head. It can represent a constant score kernel

$$
B=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T},
$$

but it cannot represent arbitrary pair-dependent kernels

$$
B_{ij}=\sigma^{-2}\Omega_{ij}^{-T}
$$

unless the pair-dependence is separately factorized into token-dependent or position-dependent operations.

This is exactly the role of mechanisms like RoPE.

---

## 13. RoPE as a concrete factorized relative transport

Let position-dependent rotations be

$$
R_i\in \mathrm{SO}(d_h).
$$

Rotary position embedding applies these rotations to projected queries and keys:

$$
Q_i^{\mathrm{rope}}=R_iW_Q^T h_i,
\qquad
K_j^{\mathrm{rope}}=R_jW_K^T h_j.
$$

Then the attention logit is

$$
\begin{aligned}
(Q_i^{\mathrm{rope}})^T K_j^{\mathrm{rope}}
&=
(R_iW_Q^T h_i)^T(R_jW_K^T h_j)
\\
&=
h_i^T W_Q R_i^T R_j W_K^T h_j.
\end{aligned}
$$

The relative rotation is

$$
R_i^TR_j.
$$

This is a concrete architectural instance of a pairwise gauge factorization:

$$
\text{relative transport} = R_i^TR_j.
$$

In score-kernel language,

$$
B_{ij}^{\mathrm{rope}}
=
W_QR_i^TR_jW_K^T.
$$

Thus RoPE does not merely add a positional bias. It inserts a position-dependent orthogonal gauge factor into the query-key score kernel.

---

## 14. The frame-covariant covariance closure gives a different, narrower theorem

The previous derivation used local isotropic covariance

$$
\Sigma_i=\sigma^2I.
$$

For full `GL(K)` this is not gauge-covariant, because

$$
U_i(\sigma^2I)U_i^T=\sigma^2U_iU_i^T,
$$

which equals `sigma^2 I` only when

$$
U_i\in O(K).
$$

The gauge-covariant closure is instead

$$
\Sigma_i=U_iCU_i^T,
\qquad
C\succ0.
$$

Write

$$
\mu_i=U_ix_i,
\qquad
\mu_j=U_jx_j,
\qquad
\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1}.
$$

Then

$$
\Omega_{ij\#}q_j
=
\mathcal N(U_ix_j,U_iCU_i^T),
$$

so the covariances match exactly and

$$
D_{ij}
=
\frac12(x_i-x_j)^TC^{-1}(x_i-x_j).
$$

Expanding,

$$
D_{ij}
=
\frac12x_i^TC^{-1}x_i
-
x_i^TC^{-1}x_j
+
\frac12x_j^TC^{-1}x_j.
$$

After removing row constants, the exact logit is

$$
\ell_{ij}
=
\frac{1}{\tau}x_i^TC^{-1}x_j
-
\frac{1}{2\tau}x_j^TC^{-1}x_j.
$$

If the key Mahalanobis norm is constant, normalized, concentrated, or absorbed into a key bias, then

$$
\ell_{ij}\simeq \frac{1}{\tau}x_i^TC^{-1}x_j.
$$

Define

$$
Q_i=C^{-1/2}x_i,
\qquad
K_j=C^{-1/2}x_j.
$$

Then

$$
\ell_{ij}\simeq \frac{1}{\tau}Q_i^TK_j.
$$

This is tied-projection dot-product attention, because the same map

$$
C^{-1/2}
$$

is used for both queries and keys.

Therefore:

$$
\boxed{
\text{frame-covariant covariance closure gives Gaussian/RBF attention and, after normalization, tied-projection dot-product attention.}
}
$$

It does not by itself produce fully untied standard transformer attention.

---

## 15. Recovering untied attention with query/key morphisms

To recover fully untied transformer attention, introduce separate query and key morphisms

$$
P_Q:E_{\mathrm{model}}\to E_Q,
\qquad
P_K:E_{\mathrm{model}}\to E_K.
$$

Given an underlying hidden state

$$
h_i\in E_{\mathrm{model}},
$$

define

$$
z_i^Q=P_Qh_i,
\qquad
z_j^K=P_Kh_j.
$$

Let the query-key transport be

$$
\Omega_{ij}^{Q\leftarrow K}:E_K\to E_Q.
$$

In the isotropic transported-KL score, the cross term is

$$
(z_i^Q)^T\left(\sigma^{-2}(\Omega_{ij}^{Q\leftarrow K})^{-T}\right)z_j^K.
$$

Substituting `z_i^Q=P_Qh_i` and `z_j^K=P_Kh_j` gives

$$
\ell_{ij}^{\mathrm{cross}}
=
h_i^T
P_Q^T
\left(\sigma^{-2}(\Omega_{ij}^{Q\leftarrow K})^{-T}\right)
P_K
h_j.
$$

Thus the effective ambient bilinear is

$$
M_{ij}
=
P_Q^T
\left(\sigma^{-2}(\Omega_{ij}^{Q\leftarrow K})^{-T}\right)
P_K.
$$

In the constant-transport limit,

$$
M
=
P_Q^T
\left(\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}\right)
P_K.
$$

This can be nonsymmetric and indefinite even when the fiber metric is positive definite, because

$$
P_Q\ne P_K.
$$

So full untied transformer attention is recovered by allowing separate query and key morphisms. These morphisms are not themselves gauge transports on the full model space. They are bundle morphisms or head readouts, while the gauge transport acts inside or between the projected fibers.

---

## 16. Relationship to standard transformer notation

With column-vector convention, standard attention uses

$$
Q_i=W_Q^Th_i,
\qquad
K_j=W_K^Th_j.
$$

The score is

$$
Q_i^TK_j
=
h_i^TW_QW_K^Th_j.
$$

The gauge interpretation is

$$
W_QW_K^T
=
P_Q^T
\left(\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}\right)
P_K.
$$

Special cases:

1. **Square head-space case**:

$$
P_Q=P_K=I,
\qquad
W_QW_K^T=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
$$

2. **Rectangular multi-head case**:

$$
W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T
=
U_Q^h\left(\sigma^{-2}\Omega_h^{-T}\right)(U_K^h)^T.
$$

3. **Frame-covariant tied case**:

$$
W_Q=W_K=C^{-1/2}
$$

in the gauge-normalized coordinates `x_i=U_i^{-1}mu_i`.

4. **RoPE-like relative-position case**:

$$
W_QR_i^TR_jW_K^T
$$

is the score kernel, with relative orthogonal transport `R_i^T R_j` inserted between query and key factors.

---

## 17. The honest theorem statement

A manuscript-safe theorem is:

> **Theorem.** In the isotropic Gaussian constant-transport limit, KL source selection with gauge transport `Omega` produces the score kernel
>
> $$
> B=\sigma^{-2}\Omega^{-T}.
> $$
>
> If `B` is represented in a square full-rank head fiber, then any transformer factorization
>
> $$
> B=W_QW_K^T
> $$
>
> is a nonunique query-key splitting of the gauge-induced score kernel. Conversely, any invertible head-space transformer kernel `W_Q W_K^T` determines a gauge transport
>
> $$
> \Omega=\sigma^{-2}(W_QW_K^T)^{-T}.
> $$
>
> In ordinary multi-head transformers, the ambient matrix `W_Q^h(W_K^h)^T` is a low-rank lift of this head-space kernel, so the gauge identification applies only after projection to the rank-`d_h` head fiber. For pair-dependent transports, fixed transformer matrices recover only the constant or separately factorized cases, while mechanisms such as RoPE implement a concrete factorized relative transport.

---

## 18. Recommended manuscript wording

Use this wording instead of saying that `W_Q` and `W_K` individually are gauge transformations:

> The standard transformer product `W_Q W_K^T` should be understood as a nonunique factorization of the gauge-induced score kernel, not as the raw transport itself. In the isotropic constant-transport limit, the KL logit contains the bilinear form `B = sigma^{-2} Omega^{-T}`. A transformer head represents this bilinear as `B = W_Q W_K^T`. For rectangular multi-head projections, the ambient product is a low-rank lift of an invertible head-space kernel; the identification with `GL(d_h)` transport applies only on the head fiber under full-rank assumptions. Pair-dependent transports `Omega_ij` require either explicit pairwise gauge factors or architectures such as RoPE, where the relative frame `R_i^T R_j` is inserted between query and key projections.

---

## 19. Main conclusion

The statement

$$
W_QW_K^T\text{ is a way to carve up }\Omega_{ij}
$$

is directionally right but should be made precise as

$$
\boxed{
W_QW_K^T\text{ is a way to carve up the gauge-induced score kernel }\sigma^{-2}\Omega_{ij}^{-T}.
}
$$

For square full-rank head-space kernels, this identification is exact.

For ordinary rectangular transformer heads, the identification holds only after separating the low-rank ambient projection from the invertible head-space factor.

For fully pair-dependent gauge transports, fixed `W_Q,W_K` are insufficient unless the pair-dependence is introduced by additional factored frame terms, such as RoPE-style relative rotations or learned token/edge-dependent gauge factors.
