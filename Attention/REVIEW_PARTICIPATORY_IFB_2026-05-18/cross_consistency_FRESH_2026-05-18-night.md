## Fresh-eyes findings (new this pass)

### N11 (Major). Bare `\kappa` in Participatory's own notation paragraph contradicts the manuscript's own body usage.

Participatory:185 declares: *"$\kappa$ without subscript denotes the Killing form on the gauge Lie algebra"*. The very next line (Participatory:187) writes *"$\tau$ without subscript is the attention/entropy temperature ... in the working implementation it is factorised as $\tau = \kappa \sqrt{K}$ with $\kappa$ a learnable scalar"*. The bare `\kappa` in L187 is a learnable scalar, not the Killing form. The same bare `\kappa` reading (learnable temperature scalar) appears in Participatory:54 (abstract), L1244 (canonical F), L1803/L1805/L1834 (transformer recovery box equations). This is internal-to-Participatory; the pairwise body-vs-body relation between Participatory and `GL(K)_attention.tex` is consistent because the main paper uses the same convention (bare κ = temperature scalar, L409). The fix is either to (a) rewrite L185 to say "the Killing form is denoted $\kappa_{\mathrm{Kill}}$ or $B(\cdot,\cdot)$, distinct from the bare $\kappa$ used in $\tau = \kappa\sqrt{K}$", or (b) rename the Killing form usages (lines 2581, 2602, 2642, 2645-2647, 2666, 2669-2680, 2684, 2730, 3148, 3153, 3163) to a different symbol. Option (a) is the minimum-touch fix.

`GL(K)_attention.tex:400` declares `$\Phi_i, \tilde{\Phi}_i$` in the symbol table as "Cross-fiber morphisms (belief ↔ model)" with explicit dimensions `R^{K_p × K_q}` and `R^{K_q × K_p}`. The main paper has no analogous disclosure. A casual reader of the main paper's symbol table will infer that Φ enters somewhere downstream; it does not.

### E9 (Minor). The "Rényi α-divergence" extension paragraph in both papers re-uses `\alpha` for the Rényi order parameter, in collision with `\alpha_i` (prior precision) from the same paper.

`GL(K)_attention.tex:1055-1069` and `Participatory:1630-1641` both contain a "Generalisation to Rényi α-divergence" paragraph in which the bare `\alpha` is the Rényi order parameter. Both papers use `\alpha_i` (subscripted) for the per-agent prior precision earlier in the same section. The collision is contextual rather than literal (because the Rényi `\alpha` is bare while the precision is subscripted `\alpha_i`), but is unannounced — neither paper's notation paragraph mentions the dual usage. The MR-1 disambiguation campaign (per commit `89e7982d`) made `α_i` the canonical prior-precision symbol; the Rényi-order reuse partially re-introduces the collision the campaign was designed to remove. Recommend a parenthetical at the first Rényi α use, e.g., "(distinct from the per-agent prior precision $\alpha_i$ of Section~\ref{sec:state_dependent_precision})".

### NC6 (Minor). The canonical-F vs reduced-F display asymmetry between the two manuscripts.

Main paper's main "Full Variational Free Energy" `eq:free_energy_final` (L843-852) is the **reduced/envelope** form `Σ KL(q_i ‖ p_i) - τ Σ log Z_i - E_q[log p(o|·)]`. The canonical form with explicit `τ β log(β/π)` entropy appears at L766 only as the row-Lagrangian `F_align^{(τ)}`, not as a full assembly. Participatory's main F `eq:free_energy_functional_final` (L1248-1262) is the **canonical form** with explicit `τ β log(β/π)` and `τ γ log(γ/π^{(s)})` entropy terms displayed. Both manuscripts state (and prove) the envelope-gap identity `-τ^{-1} Cov_β(KL, ∇KL)` between the two forms (main L869, Participatory L1244/L1281), so the math is consistent. The visual asymmetry — Participatory displays the canonical form, main displays the envelope — is a presentation choice, but a reader bundling both papers and reading the headline F equations side-by-side will see two equations that disagree on their face. Recommend a one-line bridge in the main paper near `eq:free_energy_final` saying explicitly "the canonical form with the attention-entropy term displayed is given in the companion paper~\cite{Dennis2025it} Eq.~[eq:free_energy_functional_final]; the envelope above is its specialisation after the inner β-minimisation."
