## Issue 1: Partially Minimized Free Energy Inconsistency (MOST SERIOUS)

**Problem:** Eq. `eq:free_energy_final` wrote the alignment term as $\sum_j \beta_{ij}^* K_{ij}$ and called it the "partially minimized free energy," but the actual reduced free energy after minimizing over $\beta$ is $-\tau\log Z_i$ (which includes the entropy term). The envelope theorem argument at lines 843-855 was self-contradictory: it stated that $\partial\beta/\partial x$ cross-terms vanish, then claimed this validated gradient expressions containing exactly those terms.

**Fix (hybrid approach):**
- Rewrote Eq. `eq:free_energy_final` to show the correct reduced form $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}} = \sum_i D_{\mathrm{KL}} - \tau\sum_i\log Z_i - E_q[\log p]$
- Stated the envelope theorem correctly: gradient of $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ has NO $\partial\beta/\partial x$ terms
- Added "Autograd versus reduced-free-energy gradients" paragraph explaining that autograd differentiates $\sum_j \beta^* K$ (producing $\partial\beta/\partial x$ terms), which is a different but related objective
- Kept all $\partial\beta/\partial x$ terms in gradient equations (matching the code) but annotated each as "autograd gradient"
- Updated vacuum free energy equation to use $-\tau\log Z_i$ form
- Annotated algorithm pseudocode as "autograd gradient"
- Updated softmax-gradient paragraph to identify the terms as arising from the autograd objective

**Affected locations in main text:** Lines ~827-855 (equation + discussion), ~1344 (isotropic gradient), ~1379 (general mean gradient intro), ~1401 (general mean gradient explanation), ~1434 (covariance gradient intro), ~1456 (covariance gradient explanation), ~1253-1256 (vacuum free energy), ~1825 (softmax-gradient paragraph), ~1926 (algorithm pseudocode)

**Affected locations in supplement:** Lines ~249-271 (covariance gradient), ~394 (gauge frame gradient intro)

## Issue 2a: False Statement About Prefactor (Supplement)

## Issue 2b: Factor 1/2 Dropped Incorrectly (Supplement)

**Problem:** Lines 333-343 claimed $\tau \to 0$ gives $\Sigma_i^{-1} \approx \sum_j\beta_{ij}(\Omega\Sigma\Omega^\top)^{-1}$ without the factor 1/2. From the fixed-point equation (with $\alpha_i=1$), dropping the prior still gives $(1/2)\sum_j\beta(\cdots)^{-1}$. The 1/2 removal requires $\alpha_i \ll 1$ (main text), not small $\tau$.

**Fix:** Rewrote to correctly attribute the coefficient change to $\alpha_i \ll 1$ (state-dependent prior coupling) rather than small $\tau$ (attention sharpness). Noted the complementary roles: small $\alpha_i$ changes the coefficient, small $\tau$ concentrates $\beta$.

## Issue 3: Dirac KL Not Literally Valid (Main Text)

**Problem:** Lines 1292-1299 wrote $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\delta(\mu_i) \| \mathcal{N}(\mu_p, \sigma_p^2 I)) = \frac{1}{2\sigma_p^2}\|\mu_i - \mu_p\|^2$, but KL between a Dirac and a Gaussian is literally $+\infty$.

**Fix:** Replaced with small-variance limit $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \epsilon I)$ with $\epsilon \to 0^+$. Explicitly decomposed into $\mu_i$-dependent part (finite, gives the quadratic) and $C(\epsilon)$ (divergent, $\mu_i$-independent, drops from gradients).

## Issue 4: "Exactly Recovering" Overclaim (Main Text)

**Fix:** Changed to "recovering the standard transformer attention weighting rule, up to the key-norm bias term that cancels under the layer normalization or high-dimensional concentration conditions established above."

## Issue 5: GL(K) vs SO(K) Conflation (Main Text)

**Problem:** Line 1047 wrote $M_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j) \in \mathrm{SO}(K)$, but $\exp(\phi)\exp(-\phi) \in \mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ unless $\phi \in \mathfrak{so}(K)$ or orthogonal projection is applied.

**Fix:** Changed $\in \mathrm{SO}(K)$ to $\in \mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ in the equation. Added clarifying sentence that $M_{ij} \in \mathrm{SO}(K)$ either by restricting $\phi_i$ to $\mathfrak{so}(K)$ or via orthogonal projection.

## Issue 6: Matrix Exponential Derivative Errors (Supplement)

**Problem A:** Line 435 used $\mathrm{dexp}_{\phi_j}$ where it should be $\mathrm{dexp}_{-\phi_j}$ (the derivative of $\exp(-\phi_j)$ involves the differential at $-\phi_j$).

**Problem B:** Lines 441 and 468 gave inconsistent definitions of $\mathrm{dexp}$: the ad-series (right-trivialized, differs from Frechet by factor $\exp(\phi)$) vs the integral (Frechet derivative itself).

**Fix A:** Changed $\mathrm{dexp}_{\phi_j}$ to $\mathrm{dexp}_{-\phi_j}$.

**Fix B:** Added clarifying sentence explaining Eq. (dexp_series) is the right-trivialized differential, related to the Frechet derivative by $D_\phi(\exp)[\xi] = \mathrm{dexp}_\phi(\xi) \cdot e^\phi$, and annotated the GL(K) integral as the Frechet derivative.

## Issue 7: Observation-Term Scaling (Supplement + Main Text)

**Problem:** Lines 249 (supplement) and 1434 (main text) stated the observation term contributes an "$O(\Sigma_i^{-1})$ correction." For Gaussian observations, the derivative is $\frac{1}{2}R^{-1} = O(1)$, not $O(\Sigma_i^{-1})$.

**Fix:** Corrected to state the observation term contributes an $O(1)$ correction (e.g., $\frac{1}{2}R^{-1}$ for Gaussian observations), which is negligible compared to $O(\Sigma_i^{-1})$ alignment terms in the high-precision regime.

## "Standard Transformers Implement Gauge Structure" Overclaim

**Problem:** The manuscript repeatedly states that "standard transformers already implement gauge structure" or "$W_Q, W_K \in GL(d_k)$." Both are wrong as stated. Standard per-head projections $W_Q^a, W_K^a \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{model} \times d_\text{head}}$ are rectangular and not invertible. The manuscript itself acknowledges this at lines 1150-1152 (the identification is within the $d_\text{head}$-dimensional projected subspace) and at line 1622 (rectangular shape = projection + GL(d_head) map). But 6 other locations make the unqualified claim.

Moreover, the identification only holds in the constant-gauge, isotropic, flat-bundle, deterministic limit. Standard transformers do not have transported covariances, explicit $\Omega_{ij}$, non-flat connections, or belief-state dynamics.

**Fix (6 locations):**
- Abstract: "suggesting...already implement gauge-theoretic attention" → "suggesting...can be interpreted as a degenerate case of gauge-theoretic attention operating in this limit"
- Line 566: "$W_Q, W_K \in GL(d_k)$" → corrected to reference the bilinear form, notes rectangular shape, cites Section on multihead
- Line 1607: "reveals this as implementing" → "Under the gauge-theoretic identification, this corresponds to"
- Line 1611: "standard transformers implement $GL(d_k)$ gauge-theoretic attention" → "in the isotropic flat-bundle limit, standard transformer attention can be interpreted as"
- Line 2183: "already implement gauge symmetry" → "can be interpreted as a degenerate case...operating in the constant-gauge, isotropic, flat-bundle limit"
- Conclusion (line 2214): "reveals that standard transformers already implement this gauge structure" → "permits interpreting standard transformer attention as a degenerate case of this gauge structure"

## GL+(K) Surjectivity Error

**Problem:** Two locations (main text line ~1952, supplement line ~552) claimed $\exp: \mathfrak{gl}(K) \to GL^+(K)$ is surjective. This is false: matrices in $GL^+(K)$ with negative real eigenvalues of odd Jordan-block multiplicity have no real logarithm (Culver 1966). The code already correctly documents this (in `generators.py`, `transport.py`, `gauge_utils.py`).

**Fix:** Both locations now state that a single $\exp$ is NOT surjective, but the pairwise transport $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ covers all of $GL^+(K)$ via polar decomposition: any $A \in GL^+(K)$ factors as $A = PO$ where $P = \exp(\log P)$ (symmetric positive-definite) and $O = \exp(S)$ with $S \in \mathfrak{so}(K)$. Added Culver 1966 citation.
