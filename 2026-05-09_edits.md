## Untied Q/K Carving from Nontrivial Gauge Transport

### Context

Earlier rounds set up the transformer reduction in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` along the trivial-frame route: collapse to a single shared frame $U_i = U$ so that $\Omega_{ij} = I$, then introduce a learned shared bilinear $M = \sigma^2 W_Q W_K^\top$ as a separate object filling the cross-coupling slot in the dot-product attention formula. A draft note `Attention/nontrivial_omega_transformer_derivation.md` proposed an alternative route that retains $\Omega_{ij} = U_iU_j^{-1}$ throughout and recovers attention from the transported Gaussian KL directly. Initial review of the note flagged the apparent result as tied query-key attention ($Q_i$ and $K_j$ as the same function of $\mu$) under the frame-covariant Gaussian closure $\Sigma_i = U_i C U_i^\top$. Pushback established that the closure is not the only available specialisation: with arbitrary SPD covariances $\Sigma_j$ independent of the frames, the cross term in the KL factors as untied $Q_i^\top K_j$ with $Q_i = U_i^{-1}\mu_i$ and $K_j = U_j^\top\Sigma_j^{-1}\mu_j$, and the implicit pair-dependent bilinear $\Omega_{ij}^{-\top}\Sigma_j^{-1}$ ranges over all of $\mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ as the per-token data vary.

### SymPy verification

Two parallel symbolic-numeric verifications were run before any manuscript edits.

The first script (`C:\tmp\verify_untied_carving.py`, $K=4,5$, twenty trials each) established five claims to machine precision. The cross-term identity $\mu_i^\top\Omega_{ij}^{-\top}\Sigma_j^{-1}\mu_j = Q_i^\top K_j$ holds with maximum residual $2\times 10^{-12}$. The query and key projections at the same token are not inverse-transposes of each other generically, with median Frobenius distance 25 to 40 between $W_Q^{(i)}$ and $(W_K^{(i)})^{-\top}$. Surjectivity of the implicit bilinear onto $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ holds with maximum residual $1.6\times 10^{-14}$ when reconstructing arbitrary target matrices via the choice $U_j = I, \Sigma_j = I, U_i = T^{-\top}$. The closure $\Sigma_j = U_j C U_j^\top$ collapses the carving to the symmetric tied form to maximum residual $2.3\times 10^{-12}$. Without the closure, the covariance-coupling terms $x_i^\top H_j x_i$ and $\mathrm{tr}(H_j P_i)$ have variance across $i$ for fixed $j$ that is two to four orders of magnitude larger than the cross-term variance, so they do not cancel under the row-softmax.

The second script (`C:\tmp\verify_full_logit.py`, $K=4$) verified the full §5 logit against the direct Gaussian KL to maximum residual $5.2\times 10^{-8}$, confirmed gauge invariance under simultaneous global frame change to maximum residual $8.2\times 10^{-8}$, established the four-way equality of the cross term across the equivalent expressions in $x_i, k_j, \Omega^{-\top}, \Lambda_j$, and Q/K to maximum residual $3.4\times 10^{-14}$, and verified the value-aggregation factorisation $U_i^{-1}m_i = \sum_j\beta_{ij}V_j$ with $V_j = U_j^{-1}\nu_j$ to maximum residual $8\times 10^{-15}$. The orthogonal-isotropic specialisation reproduced the rotary positional embedding form $Q_i = U_i^\top\mu_i$, $K_j = U_j^\top\mu_j$ exactly.

### Context

### Investigation and integration

Three agents were deployed in parallel: one extracted the verbatim source content from `GL(K)_attention.tex` (full subsection plus the deterministic-limit treatment plus the general mean and covariance gradients), one confirmed `GL(K)_supplementary.tex` defers the construction to the main GL(K) text, and one located the natural insertion point in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` as the position immediately preceding `\subsubsection{The Envelope Theorem and the Reduced Free Energy}` (line 1243), inside the `\subsection{The Complete Free Energy Functional}` block. A new `\subsubsection{State-Dependent Prior Precision}` was inserted there with label `sec:state_dependent_precision`. The subsubsection contains three paragraphs ("Precision as a Variational Parameter", "Optimal Precision", "Adaptive Weight Decay and Recovery of the Uniform Limit") and three new equation labels (`eq:free_energy_adaptive`, `eq:precision_regularizer`, `eq:state_dependent_alpha`).

The first paragraph motivates the construction by promoting the self-coupling weight from a fixed unit constant to a per-agent variational parameter $\alpha_i(c) > 0$, and writes the augmented free energy with a log-barrier regulariser $R(\alpha_i) = b_0\alpha_i - c_0\log\alpha_i$. The second paragraph derives the closed-form optimum $\alpha_i^*(c) = c_0/(b_0 + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i(c)\|p_i(c)))$ from the stationarity condition, and gives the prior-trust interpretation: agents close to their prior have $\alpha_i^* \approx c_0/b_0$ at maximum, agents far from their prior have $\alpha_i^* \to 0$ and defer to alignment and observation. The third paragraph derives the deterministic-limit reduction to an adaptive weight decay $\lambda_{p,i} = \alpha_i/\sigma_p^2$, identifies the constant-$\alpha = 1$ specialisation as the recovery of the canonical functional and uniform L2 regularisation, and states explicitly that the simulations of this paper run in the constant-$\alpha = 1$ regime.

A closing paragraph notes that the envelope reduction below applies to the adaptive functional in the same way, with the inner optimum over $\alpha_i$ contributing an additional $\alpha_i$-dependence inside the reduced free energy that the envelope theorem absorbs. Backward references go to `eq:free_energy_functional_final`; forward reference goes to `\ref{sec:mass}` for the precision-as-mass interpretation; the GL(K) companion paper is cited via `\cite{Dennis2025trans}`, the same key already used elsewhere in the manuscript.
