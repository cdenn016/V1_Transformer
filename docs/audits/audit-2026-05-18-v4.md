- `core/gauge_preconditioner.py` (active under `phi_preconditioner='killing'`) implements `2K·gram − 2·tr⊗tr` with the *wrong* `K` (uses ambient `K=90` instead of per-block `d_h=15`) AND introduces off-block coupling that the direct-sum Killing form does not have.

## Confirmed Punch List (critical → high → medium)

### Critical
1. **[critical] F4.1** — `_update_phi` rebuilds full attention every E-iter on the flat path. `vfe/e_step.py:1151-1172`. The `phi_for_grad = phi.detach().requires_grad_(True)` leaf forces `compute_kl_attention` to recompute `block_exp_pairs` from scratch even though the outer loop just built them at `:653`. Fix is architectural — thread a fresh `block_exp_pairs` built from `phi_for_grad` into the call. Estimated 40–60% of flat-path forward time.

## Notes for the user (open decisions)

1. **F6.1 (Killing form)** — Two valid interpretations of the natural-gradient metric on a block-diagonal `gl(d_h)^H` Lie algebra. The active path currently uses interpretation (b) `gl(K_full)` Killing form restricted to the subalgebra. Interpretation (a) (strict direct-sum per-block Killing form, zero off-block coupling, `2·d_h` instead of `2·K_full`) is the textbook choice and aligns with the per-block construction in `omega_direct.py`. Switching would change the natural-gradient direction under `phi_preconditioner='killing'`. Marked as `CAVEAT` in source; awaiting user sign-off.
2. **F4.1 (per-iter Omega rebuild)** — Estimated 40-60% of flat-path forward time. Requires threading a `phi_for_grad`-built cache into `compute_kl_attention`. Worth a dedicated PR.
3. **F4.5 (per-call accumulator allocation)** — ~672 MB allocator churn per forward at default. Requires shape-keyed instance buffers on `VFEEStep`. Worth a dedicated PR.
