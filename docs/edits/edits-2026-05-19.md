### New appendix — Augmented Hierarchical Generative Model and Mean-Field Derivation of the Cross-Scale Shadow

Inserted as a new appendix `app:augmented_joint`, placed just before the RG construction appendix. Contains:

- **Definition** of the augmented joint as a product of tree-edge Gibbs factors $\chi_{i,\pi(i)}(k_i, k_{\pi(i)}) \propto \exp[-\|k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)} k_{\pi(i)}\|^2 / (2\sigma^2)]$ on the hierarchical agent tree, with $\sigma^2$ a link-stiffness parameter and $\Omega_{i,\pi(i)}$ the inter-scale gauge transport.
- **Lemma 1 (well-definedness):** the augmented joint is a proper probability density on the product space of all agent-scale latents, via the standard tree-Markov decomposition (Lauritzen 1996 §3.2) and exponential-family integrability (Brown 1986 §1.5).
- **Lemma 2 (mean-field fixed-point equations):** the variational free energy is stationary under mean-field factorization when each component satisfies the standard coordinate-update equation (Wainwright-Jordan 2008 Prop. 4.1; Bishop 2006 §10.1).
- **Lemma 3 (cross-scale shadow as rigid-link mean-field optimum):** under zero within-scale couplings, the mean-field marginal $q_i^{(s)}$ is Gaussian with mean $\Omega_{i,\pi(i)}\mu_{\pi(i)}^{(s+1)}$ and covariance $\Omega_{i,\pi(i)}\Sigma_{\pi(i)}^{(s+1)}\Omega_{i,\pi(i)}^\top + \sigma^2 I$; in the rigid-link limit $\sigma^2 \to 0$ this collapses to the gauge pushforward $\Omega_{i,\pi(i)}[q_{\pi(i)}^{(s+1)}]$, which is exactly the cross-scale shadow.
- **Discussion** of the relationship to ladder VAEs (Sønderby et al. 2016): the augmented joint is the deterministic-decoder limit of a ladder-VAE-style hierarchical prior with gauge-transport instead of learned neural-network mean function. The finite-$\sigma^2$ form is a natural relaxation that regularizes degenerate-prior pathology.
