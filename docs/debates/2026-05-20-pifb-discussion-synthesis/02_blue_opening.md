## Falsification conditions

This claim of correct calibration fails if:

1. The participial "providing mathematical realization" at 3454 is shown by a reader-comprehension argument to scope outside the "might emerge" conditional and therefore to make a declarative metaphysical claim. English participial subordination defaults the participle's mood to the matrix clause; the falsification would require evidence that this default fails for this construction.
2. The 3458 phrase "rigorous derivations (transformers from gauge theory)" is shown to refer to the *interpretation* rather than the *derivation*. If the only reasonable reading is "transformers are rigorously derived to *be* gauge theories" rather than "the transformer recovery is rigorously derived from gauge-theoretic primitives," then 3458 contradicts 3190's "one interpretive lens." The parenthetical grammar "(X from Y)" reads more naturally as "deriving X from Y" than as "X is a kind of Y," so this falsification is unlikely but not impossible.
3. The empirical validation claim at 3458 is shown to assert validation of the gauge-theoretic *interpretation* rather than of the transformer-recovery experiments. The manuscript's transformer experiments validate the holonomy diagnostics, the scaling behaviour, and the recovery itself; they do not validate the broader interpretive thesis that "transformers are gauge theories" in any deep sense. If the natural reading of 3458 conflates these two scopes, calibration fails.
4. The 3454 ML clause "are revealed as" is shown to be itself an interpretive claim disguised as mathematical reduction — that is, if the zero-dimensional limit does not actually reproduce standard transformer attention as a theorem but only via interpretive choices. This would falsify both this subsection and `\ref{sec:transformer_zero_dim}`.
5. A reader unfamiliar with the manuscript's internal distinctions (between rigorous math and interpretive lens) reads 3458 as endorsing the gauge-theoretic interpretation as the privileged one. Calibration must survive reading by a reasonable skeptical reader, not only by one with the cross-reference key. If the subsection cannot stand alone without the 3190 cross-reference, then the cross-reference must be made explicit at 3458, and the claim of in-place calibration fails.
