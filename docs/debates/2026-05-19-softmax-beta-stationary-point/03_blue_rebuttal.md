## Core attack

Red's opening argues that because step (b) at line 715 (`F_align = KL[Q ‖ P]`) is a definition rather than a derivation — i.e., the prior `P(k | z=j) = Ω_{ij} q_j` is *posited* rather than derived from an independently-justified joint generative model — the headline fails the "every intermediate step mathematically valid" clause. Red names sub-claim A as the falsification target and concedes sub-claims B, C, D, E (`02_red_opening.md` lines 9, 34: "Sub-claims C, D, E are clean; the falsification target is sub-claim A").

This collapses two questions that the claim keeps separate:

**Question 1.** Is `F_align^(τ)` the correct upstream Bayesian functional, derived from a joint generative model whose components do not depend on the variational quantities?

**Question 2.** Given `F_align^(τ)` as written at line 766, is `β = softmax(-E/τ)` its exact stationary point under the row-Lagrangian with the simplex constraint?

The headline of `00_claim.md` is about Question 2: "softmax ... is the **exact** stationary point of the canonical alignment free energy `F_align^(τ)` ... **derived via the row-Lagrangian** shown in §4.6." The load-bearing words are "stationary point of `F_align^(τ)`" and "derived via the row-Lagrangian." Both of these statements are about what happens *after* the functional is written down. The row-Lagrangian is `L = Σ_j β_{ij}(E_{ij} + log β_{ij} - log π_j) - λ(Σ_j β_{ij} - 1)` at line 741; its stationarity equations at line 747 yield the softmax form at line 753 by elementary algebra (red's own sympy run at `02_red_opening.md:28–33` confirms this). Sub-claims C, D, E are exactly the row-Lagrangian / softmax / temperature steps, and red has conceded all three.

Red's attack therefore lands on Question 1 (the justification of `P`), not Question 2 (the stationarity of β under `F_align^(τ)` with the given `P`). The claim's "every intermediate step mathematically valid" clause is bounded by what is being derived — the stationary point of `F_align^(τ)`. The line-697 verbal resolution is upstream input to that derivation, not an intermediate step *of* it.

Red attempts to bridge this gap via line 725's "with no approximation." Red reads this as certifying "the entire `F_align = KL[Q ‖ P]` construction as a Bayesian KL bound" (`02_red_opening.md:22`). The text does not support that reading. Line 725 reads:

> "The identification of the integral with `D_KL[q_i ‖ Ω_{ij} q_j]` follows directly from the definition of KL divergence and the generative model `P(k | z=j) = (Ω_{ij} q_j)(k)`, with no approximation."

The "no approximation" qualifier modifies "the identification of the integral with `D_KL`" — an algebraic statement about evaluating `∫ q_i(k) log[q_i(k)/P(k|z=j)] dk` exactly given `P(k|z=j) = (Ω_{ij}q_j)(k)`. The Gaussian integral evaluates without approximation, yielding `D_KL[q_i ‖ Ω_{ij}q_j]` (line 720–725; `01_evidence.md:31–34`). Red's own falsification analysis at `02_red_opening.md:22–24` already concedes the calculus reading: "If line 725's 'no approximation' is meant as a calculus statement (the Gaussian integral evaluates exactly), it is correct." The calculus reading is the natural one given the sentence's structure ("identification of the integral with `D_KL`"). The stronger reading — that line 725 certifies upstream Bayesian validity of the joint model — is a reading red imposes to manufacture tension with line 697. The two statements are about different things: line 697 is about the *origin* of the component `P(k|z=j)`; line 725 is about the *algebraic identification* of the inner integral once `P` is given. They do not conflict.

## Defense

The standard variational-inference literature [BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §3, Wainwright & Jordan 2008 §3.6] derives mean-field updates for each `q_i` (or each variational factor) with the other factors `q_{-i}` held fixed. Coordinate-ascent variational inference (CAVI) is exactly this pattern: at each step, one variational factor is optimized while the others are held at their current values; the joint generative model whose mean-field decomposition produces this update is *implicit in the factorization assumption*. [BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §3.2] writes the CAVI update for factor `q_j(z_j)` as a function of `E_{q_{-j}}[log p(z, x)]` — the joint generative model `p(z, x)` appears inside an expectation under the *other* factors, with `q_j` itself the optimization variable. The structural pattern the manuscript instantiates is exactly this: when minimizing `F_align` over `β` (the variational distribution over the source index `z`), the components `P(k|z=j) = Ω_{ij}q_j` are held fixed at the current values of the other agents' beliefs `q_j`. Mutual consistency at the joint fixed point is the standard CAVI fixed-point condition.

This is what line 697 means by "at the fixed point of the joint optimization (over all `{q_i}` simultaneously), the pairwise objectives are mutually consistent." The verbal invocation does not require the full joint model to be written down for the per-row update to be a valid coordinate-ascent step. The per-row update is well-defined whenever the per-row functional is well-defined and the optimization variable is `β_{i·}` with `q_j` held fixed. Both conditions hold at line 715: `F_align` is well-defined as a function of `(β_{i·}, q_i, {q_j})` (the integral evaluates; the components `Ω_{ij}q_j` are well-defined Gaussians); the row-Lagrangian fixes `q_{-i}` and optimizes `β_{i·}` subject to `Σ_j β_{ij} = 1`.

The claim's headline is "exact stationary point of `F_align^(τ)`," not "derived from first principles as the unique correct multi-agent FEP coupling." The first is mathematically tight given the row-Lagrangian construction (sub-claims B, C, D, E; red concedes); the second would require the joint generative model to be written down explicitly. The claim's wording — "stationary point of the canonical alignment free energy `F_align^(τ)`" — names the functional and the optimization problem; the row-Lagrangian operates on this functional and produces the softmax. The "every intermediate step mathematically valid" clause refers to the chain (energy-entropy decomposition → KKT → softmax → temperature), which red has confirmed clean by sympy and which the evidence pack (`01_evidence.md:114–135`) confirms by independent KKT and convexity analysis.

Red's reading-(i) vs reading-(ii) framing makes this concrete. Under reading (i) ("posit `F_align` as an engineered functional"), the claim that softmax is its stationary point holds trivially-but-correctly via the row-Lagrangian. Under reading (ii) ("derive `F_align` from a joint ELBO via mean-field decomposition"), the algebra of the row-Lagrangian is unchanged — what changes is whether the prior `P(k|z=j)` is justified by an externally-fixed joint model or by a self-consistent fixed-point coupling. The headline does not assert reading (ii) over reading (i); it asserts the stationarity result under the canonical functional, which holds under either reading. Red's falsification conditions F1–F2 demand the joint ELBO be written down or cited; these would *strengthen* the manuscript at line 697 but are not necessary for sub-claims B–E, which red concedes.

The judge can read the headline strictly: "exact stationary point of `F_align^(τ)`, derived via the row-Lagrangian, with every intermediate step mathematically valid." The intermediate steps of the row-Lagrangian (from line 730 to line 766) are: energy-entropy decomposition (line 730); first-order Lagrangian stationarity (line 747, with `∂(β log β)/∂β = log β + 1`); algebraic solution `β_k = π_k exp(λ-1) exp(-E_k)` (line 752); normalization absorbing `exp(λ-1) = 1/Σ_m π_m exp(-E_m)` (line 753); uniform-π specialization (line 760); temperature rescaling `τ · [β(E/τ) + β log β - β log π] = βE + τβ log(β/π)` (line 766). Each of these is verified by `01_evidence.md:114–151` and by red's own sympy at `02_red_opening.md:28–33`. The intermediate-step chain of the row-Lagrangian is clean.

Red's strongest residual point is that the manuscript's *self-description* at line 697 uses the word "engineered" for framing (b), which is honest disclosure that framing (b) is a posit rather than a derivation. Granted. The same line offers framing (a) as the alternative, derivation-shaped reading; the manuscript states "in either framing, the resulting update equations are the same." This is mathematically true: in framing (a), `F_align` is what mean-field decomposition of a joint ELBO produces (with `q_{-i}` held fixed, exactly as CAVI prescribes); in framing (b), `F_align` is posited directly. Either way, the row-Lagrangian and its softmax stationary point are unchanged. The claim's "every intermediate step mathematically valid" is about the row-Lagrangian, not about which of the two upstream framings the reader prefers. The truth status of the headline is independent of the choice of framing.
