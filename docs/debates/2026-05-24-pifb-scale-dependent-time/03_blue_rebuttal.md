# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-scale-dependent-time

## Concession

I grant two points from red's opening; both are real manuscript defects, and defending them would be performative.

**Concession 1 — Eq. `Ilow_eq_dispersion` (2651) overclaims an equality.** The quantity $\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1}$ is *defined* at line 2233 (Eq. `cross_scale_information_flow`) as a sum of full transported Gaussian KLs, $\sum_i \mathrm{KL}(q_i^{(s)} \| \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}])$, with explicit gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_I)$ (transport rule, canon_math.md:107–112) and Gaussian beliefs carrying covariance. Line 2651 then writes this **equal** (not $\approx$) to the pure-mean Mahalanobis dispersion $\sum_i \tfrac12(\mu_i-\mu_I)^\top\Lambda(\mu_i-\mu_I)$, and the prose at 2649 reinforces it as "*exactly* the energy these modes hold." By the closed-form Gaussian KL (canon_math.md:42),

$$\mathrm{KL}(q\|p) = \tfrac12\big[\mathrm{tr}(\Sigma_p^{-1}\Sigma_q) + (\mu_p-\mu_q)^\top\Sigma_p^{-1}(\mu_p-\mu_q) - K + \log(|\Sigma_p|/|\Sigma_q|)\big],$$

the transported meta-belief $\Omega_{i,I}[q_I] = \mathcal{N}(\Omega\mu_I, \Omega\Sigma_I\Omega^\top)$ collapses to the pure-mean form only when **both** $\Sigma_i=\Sigma_I$ **and** $\Omega_{i,I}=I$. The common-$\Sigma$ stipulation at 2628 buys the first; nothing in the framework buys the second, and I searched for a gauge-fixing convention that would force $\Omega_{i,I}=I$ at the scale transition — line 1609 ("The Zero-Dimensional Limit and Gauge Fixing") concerns a different construction, not the meta-agent clock. Red's falsification condition 1 is therefore not met. My own executed check confirms red's numbers (common $\Sigma$, $K=3$, generic $\Omega=\exp(B)\in\mathrm{GL}^+(3)$):

```
full transported Gaussian KL  = 2.98268
pure-mean dispersion (Eq.2651)= 0.70189
difference                    = 2.28079
Omega=I, common Sigma: full KL= 0.70189   dispersion= 0.70189   -> equal: True
```

The upstream Laplacian reduction at 2642 honestly carries the qualifier "to leading order in the post-transport mean difference"; that qualifier must be inherited at 2651. The fix is mechanical and does not touch the physics: the `=` becomes `≈`, conditioned on the leading-order/post-transport-aligned regime, and "exactly" at 2649 softens to "to leading order." This is a defect of overclaimed exactness, not of substance.

**Concession 2 — the first sentence of the renormalization verdict (2656) is a non-sequitur.** The opening sentence reasons "Because the bit is reparametrization-invariant, it is a scale-invariant unit, and the slowdown cannot reside in a rescaling of the unit." The reparametrization-invariance grounded at line 2596 is invariance under relabeling the belief *coordinates* $\theta\to f(\theta)$ at a fixed scale — the Cencov property (canon_math.md:24, [Cencov1972]; canon_math.md:60), because the underlying distributions are unchanged. That is coordinate invariance on the belief manifold. RG invariance across scales $s\to s+1$ — integrating out fast constituent modes — is a different transformation, and coordinate-invariance of an object at scale $s$ places no constraint on how it maps to scale $s+1$ under the blocking map. Red is right that this sentence smuggles in the conclusion from a premise that does not entail it.

## Core attack

Red's Vector 2 lands on the wrong sentence and reports the result as if it sank the whole verdict. The Position states the verdict "rest[s] on a non-sequitur." It does not. The verdict paragraph at 2656 advances **two logically independent arguments**, and red engaged only the first.

The second argument is the load-bearing one: "We resist calling $Z^{(s)}$ a dynamical critical exponent: the $1/N$ suppression is the law of large numbers acting on the barycenter, whereas a dynamical exponent is defined at a critical point through the scaling of the relaxation time with the correlation length $\xi$ as $\xi^z$, and its identification would require the analytic renormalization-group fixed point... that Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_rg} flags as open." This sentence does not invoke reparametrization-invariance at all. It rests the conclusion on (i) the *definition* of the dynamical critical exponent $z$ via $\tau\sim\xi^z$ at a critical point ([Hohenberg & Halperin, Rev. Mod. Phys. 49, 435 (1977)], the dynamic-scaling hypothesis red itself cites), and (ii) the manuscript's own admission at line 2227 that no analytic fixed point has been identified — "an analytic identification of fixed points from the variational principle and an associated critical-exponent computation remain open." With no critical point and no diverging correlation length in evidence, there is no $\xi$ for $Z^{(s)}$ to scale against, so $Z^{(s)}$ cannot *be* a $z$ by the definition red supplied. The conclusion follows from the definition plus the open-fixed-point flag, not from Cencov invariance. Red's Vector 2, by isolating the first sentence and labeling the verdict a non-sequitur, mischaracterizes which sentence carries the logical weight.

## Defense

The claim's component (C) survives the concession, on the correct ground. Restate it cleanly: $Z^{(s)} = N^{-1}\sum_i \xi_i$-variance-suppression is a law-of-large-numbers ratio (component A), and a *dynamical critical exponent* is a sharply different object — it is the exponent $z$ in $\tau_{\mathrm{relax}}\sim\xi^z$ governing critical slowing down as $\xi\to\infty$ at a continuous phase transition [Hohenberg & Halperin 1977]. These coincide only if the $1/N$ slowdown were the relaxation-time scaling of an order parameter at a critical point. The manuscript identifies no such point; it explicitly defers fixed-point identification, relevant-operator analysis, and critical-exponent computation to the open problem at 2227 and `sec:meta_agent_rg`. So the verdict's *content* — "$Z^{(s)}$ is a scale-adapted comoving-units choice, $\hat\tau^{(s+1)}=\hat\tau^{(s)}/Z^{(s)}$, and promoting it to $z$ is the open RG problem, not a result" — is exactly the calibrated, falsifiability-respecting statement the philosophy-of-science lens rewards. The conclusion is sound; only its first-sentence justification is mis-stated, and concession 2 isolates that surgically.

Two further points strengthen the surviving claim. First, the piece red's own opening conceded — the $Z_I$ trace-ratio and its decomposition at 2633–2636 — is exact, not leading-order, and I verified the algebra: with $\Delta\mu_i = c+\xi_i$ and $\xi_i$ independent of covariance $v_\xi$, $\mathrm{Cov}(\Delta\mu_I) = \mathrm{Cov}(N^{-1}\sum_i(c+\xi_i)) = \mathrm{Cov}(c) + N^{-2}\sum_i v_\xi = \mathrm{Cov}(c) + v_\xi/N$, with no isotropy or leading-order assumption needed in the $K$-dimensional belief space. Component (A) is a theorem under stated independence, and it is the spectral origin of the $1/N$ that component (C) reads off. Second, component (B) is uncontested by both sides: Eq. `kl_arclength` (2593), $\mathrm{KL}=\tfrac12\delta\mu^\top\Lambda\delta\mu=\tfrac12 ds^2$, is the correct pure-mean Gaussian specialization of the second-order Fisher expansion (canon_math.md:35, [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]), explicitly conditioned at 2591 on "a pure mean step at fixed covariance," and the $ds=1$-is-$\tfrac12$-bit conclusion at 2596 follows directly.

Red's Vector 3 (Laplacian symmetrization) is openly hedged by the manuscript at 2642 — the symmetrized weights $(\beta_{ij}+\beta_{ji})/2$ are stated, not concealed — and red itself labeled it "not load-bearing." It does not move the verdict.

Net position after rebuttal: the claim's word "rigorous formalization" is too strong for the subsection *as written*, on the strength of concession 1 — Eq. `Ilow_eq_dispersion` states a leading-order approximation as an exact identity, false in the framework's own non-flat regime. That is a citable, fixable manuscript defect, and I do not defend it. But the substance the claim asserts survives: component (A) is a theorem, component (B) matches canon, and component (C)'s *conclusion* (Z is not a critical exponent) is correct on the LLN-plus-no-critical-point ground, with only its first-sentence justification needing the repair concession 2 names. The claim is defensible on its conclusions and on two of three components; it is not defensible on the unqualified exactness of 2651 or the first-sentence reasoning of 2656.

## Falsification conditions (carried forward)

My defense of component (C) is wrong if the manuscript, contrary to 2227, has elsewhere identified an analytic RG fixed point and a diverging correlation length against which $Z^{(s)}$ scales as $\xi^z$ — in which case $Z^{(s)}$ would be a genuine dynamical critical exponent and the verdict's conclusion would itself be false, not merely mis-justified. I found no such identification; 2227 and `sec:open_problems` flag it open. My concession of Vector 1 is wrong only if a gauge-fixing convention forcing $\Omega_{i,I}=I$ at the scale transition exists in the framework; I searched and found none.
