# Blue Rebuttal — cross-layer-phi-handoff

## Concession

I grant Red's textual reading of PIFB §1547-1549. The verb at PIFB:1549 ("frames φ_i evolve while averaging over both belief and model fluctuations") is indicative, not modal, and the differential equation at PIFB:1545 (`\dot U_i = -η_φ U_i \tilde{\nabla}_{\phi_i}\mathcal{F}_{\text{frame}}`) is a non-trivial dynamical law for φ that the manuscript explicitly endorses. Reading "may evolve" at PIFB:1537 as license-to-freeze across the slow τ_φ timescale would invert §1547-1549. The previous verdict (`docs/debates/2026-05-20-vfe-module-purity-for-pifb/04_verdict.md`) under-weighted the surrounding paragraph; on the timescale-separation question Red is correct that PIFB prescribes φ to be a non-static slow field, not a frozen constant.

I also grant that the module docstring at `transformer/vfe/stack.py:18-34` self-admits a documentation gap: it states "Set `prior_handoff_sigma=1.0` (and a matching mechanism for phi if desired) to recover the canonical scheme." The phrase "if desired" implies an absent feature. Either that mechanism should be implemented as a `prior_handoff_phi` toggle, or the docstring should be rewritten to explain why no such toggle is needed. The current text invites the cascade-gap reading.

## Core attack

Red conflates the slow-timescale evolution τ_φ of PIFB §1547-1549 with the L-block architectural depth index ℓ. PIFB:1545 writes `\dot U_i = -η_φ U_i \tilde{\nabla}_{\phi_i}\mathcal{F}_{\text{frame}}` — a *time* derivative on a single agent i, parameterized by τ. PIFB:1571 ("Transformer Architectures as the Zero-Dimensional Limit") introduces transformer stacking only as a "zero-dimensional limit" reduction of the *spatial* base manifold; nowhere in §1571-1582 is the layer index ℓ identified with the slow temporal coordinate τ_φ. Red's first piece of evidence at §1547-1549 says φ evolves under τ; Red then asserts this implies a cross-layer cascade `priors.phi^{(ℓ+1)} = beliefs.phi^{(ℓ)*}` without exhibiting any PIFB passage that maps ℓ to τ. Red's falsification condition (2) explicitly anticipates this gap: "PIFB:1571-1574 is at the boundary of this, but does not say it explicitly." Red admits the manuscript does not equate L-block depth with the τ_φ slow flow. The architectural mapping is the user's interpretive choice, not the manuscript's prescription [Friston2017Graphical; ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 9 — both discuss hierarchical models, neither prescribes that a finite-depth network's layer index ℓ corresponds to the τ_φ slow time of a Lie-group natural-gradient flow].

Red's structural argument is also empirically wrong on this codebase. Red writes (in the "Code — no toggle exists" block): "the cleaner refutation is structural: `Ω_ij = exp(φ_i) exp(-φ_j)` and the KL functional `KL(q_i || Ω_ij q_j)` both reference the φ that defines the *transport between agents in the prior's orbit*." This is false. `transformer/vfe/attention.py:124-198` (`compute_kl_attention`) takes a single `phi` argument — the *belief* φ — and builds Ω from it. Searched across the entire `transformer/` tree, the symbol `priors.phi` has exactly three appearances: `transformer/vfe/stack.py:14` (docstring), `:142` (comment), and `:145` (assignment). No call site reads it. The omega-direct path at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1981-2026` likewise constructs Ω from `beliefs.omega` (the posterior gauge state), not from a prior φ. Red's "the prior distribution π_v is gauge-transported by Ω, not free of it" applies to the *manuscript's* generative model `π_v = A_v π_0`, but the implementation at `compute_kl_attention` does not transport the prior by Ω in the KL — it transports the *posterior* q_j by Ω_ij where both Ω endpoints are taken from `beliefs.phi`. The structural premise of Red's attack on the dead-slot defense does not survive the code.

## Defense

The posterior gauge frame cascades correctly across L-block depth in the current implementation, just via `beliefs.phi` rather than via the named `priors.phi` slot. The chain is direct and traceable. `transformer/vfe/block.py:172` (`beliefs = self.e_step(beliefs, priors, mask)`) returns a BeliefState whose `phi` field is the post-E-step φ — the converged output of the inner iteration loop's `_update_phi` retractions at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1474-1498` (each iteration constructs `phi_for_grad = phi.detach().requires_grad_(True)`, computes the autograd gradient through `compute_kl_attention`, and retracts). `transformer/vfe/stack.py:94-95` then loops `beliefs = block(beliefs, priors, mask)` so the *next* block's `beliefs.phi` is the previous block's converged posterior φ. The next block's E-step at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:779` (`phi = beliefs.phi`) reads exactly that posterior, and feeds it into `compute_kl_attention` and `_update_phi` in the next inner loop. The slow τ_φ flow of PIFB:1545 is implemented as the iterated `_update_phi` retractions chained across (a) the inner E-step iterations within each block and (b) the outer block-to-block `beliefs.phi` handoff. The `priors.phi` slot is dead and only retained because `BeliefState` is a fixed namedtuple; deleting it would be a refactor with no semantic effect.

Red's third falsification condition asks for the user's generative model to make freezing `priors.phi` the canonical choice. The structural answer follows directly from the call graph above and from the canon: the E-step's KL functional is `KL(q_i || Ω_ij q_j)` with Ω built from the *belief's* φ, not from a separate prior gauge orbit. There is no distinct "prior φ orbit" object that the prior π_v sits on independently of the posterior. The prior is `π = N(μ_p, Σ_p)` in the same coordinate chart as q; the gauge state φ is a single field on the agent index i, not a tuple `(φ_prior, φ_posterior)`. The hierarchical-VI canon Red invokes [Friston2017Graphical, Bishop2006 §10.1.3, BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017] passes the full posterior q(s_ℓ) to inform the prior at level ℓ+1 — and that handoff happens in this code via `beliefs.phi`. The named slot `priors.phi` is not "one component of the prior distribution" in the Hall2015/Nakahara2003 sense — it is an unused field carried for API symmetry with `priors.mu` and `priors.sigma`, which *do* enter the KL `KL(q_i || p_i)` term explicitly.

The asymmetry between σ (which has a `prior_handoff_sigma` toggle) and φ (which does not) is therefore principled, not arbitrary. The prior σ enters `compute_vfe_gradients_gpu` through the self-KL term `α · KL(q_i || p_i)` and acts as a regularizer pulling the posterior σ toward the prior σ; freezing it at the embedding ("σ does not cascade") is a meaningfully different generative model from cascading it. The prior φ, in contrast, has no analogous consumer in the E-step — the KL self-term operates on (μ, Σ) only, and the cross-token transport term uses belief φ on both endpoints [`transformer/vfe/attention.py:165-181`, the call into `compute_attention_weights` passes a single `phi`]. A `prior_handoff_phi` toggle would have nothing to toggle: there is no code path that reads `priors.phi`. The honest fix is either to delete the slot or to introduce a *new* term in F (e.g., a `KL(q_i || Ω(φ_p_i) q_j)` variant that transports the prior orbit by a prior-φ-dependent Ω) — but that would be a manuscript change, not a `stack.py` toggle.

The claim as written ("`stack.py:145` hard-wires `new_prior_phi = initial_priors.phi` ... with no toggle to recover the posterior-φ cascade prescribed by the manuscript, and this is a falsifying deviation from the canonical hierarchical-VI structure") fails the second clause of its own falsification condition (b): "`stack.py` (or its callers / `e_step.py`) does in fact route posterior φ into the next layer's prior φ under some toggle, even if the variable name `priors.phi` stays at the embedding value." The cascade exists, structurally, via `beliefs.phi` under every config; the named slot is cosmetic. The deviation from canonical hierarchical VI is at most a documentation/clarity gap, not a falsifying mathematical deviation.

## Falsification conditions (restated for the judge)

The defense fails if Red shows either: (i) a PIFB passage that explicitly identifies the L-block depth index ℓ with the slow temporal coordinate τ_φ of Eq. at PIFB:1545 (Red has not produced one, and Red's own falsification condition 2 concedes PIFB:1571-1574 stops short); or (ii) a call site in `transformer/vfe/` that reads `priors.phi` and uses it as a distinct gauge frame from `beliefs.phi` in `compute_kl_attention` or any other E-step computation (grep of the codebase returns three docstring/comment/assignment references and zero reads).
