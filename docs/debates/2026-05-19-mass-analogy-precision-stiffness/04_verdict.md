## Reasoning

The compound claim requires both C1 (algebraic correctness of the boxed Hessian and mass-matrix equations) and C2 (appropriate motivation under the section's caveats) to hold under standard external canon. C1 was conceded by Red on sympy and finite-difference grounds and is correct: the boxed identities at lines 1858–1863, 1937, 1947, 1953–1956, 1975–1991, and 1995–2010 verify symbolically, including the GL(d) precision-transport law and the at-consensus cross-block vanishing. The Fisher–Rao decomposition $g_F = \Sigma^{-1} \oplus \tfrac12(\Sigma^{-1}\otimes\Sigma^{-1})$ [Amari & Nagaoka 2000 §3.5; Calvo & Oller 1990] is the canonical form and matches the isolated-agent limit of the boxed equations.

C2 fails on the evidence under Blue's own falsification condition F3, which specifies that the defense breaks if load-bearing prose elsewhere in the manuscript claims a derivation or uses the precision-mass identification outside the symmetric/isolated regime the section restricts it to. Red located that prose at line 2983 and at lines 1933, 1981. The $R^2 = 0.9998$ value cited at line 2983 is transported from the perplexity-vs-$K$ fit at line 2483 ([sec:scaling_validation]) onto a mass-precision identification the cited section does not measure. Blue conceded this directly: "Red's claim that the cited experiment is absent from the location it is cited at is correct" and "Red's conflation finding is correct."

Red's circularity attack on the kinetic-metric postulate is also sustained on direct analysis. Blue's rebuttal works through the Euler–Lagrange equation when both kinetic and potential matrices equal $M_{\mu\mu}$ and obtains $\omega^2 = 1$ per direction, independent of $M_{\mu\mu}$. The harmonic scaling $\omega^2 \propto 1/M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ promoted at lines 1846, 1848, 2014, 2018, and 2983 is therefore structurally trivialized by the postulate that licenses it, not just contingent on it. External canon backs this finding: [Arnold 1989 §22] requires the inertia tensor and the potential Hessian to be operationally independent (0,2)-tensors at the equilibrium configuration for $\omega^2 = k/m$ to be non-degenerate, and [Marsden & Ratiu 1999 §1.4] specifies that identifying them by postulate requires checking against an independent measurement of either one. The manuscript supplies no such independent measurement. The line-2028 hedge ("contingent on the kinetic postulate, and would be vacated by any other choice of kinetic metric") removes the harmonic match from the set of falsifiable predictions of the framework rather than supplying physical content; it labels the relation as a definitional consequence of the postulate.

The rock illustration at line 2023 is the one place where Blue's partial defense holds. Read with the section's established meaning of "dynamics" as the natural-gradient flow on $\mathcal{F}$ [§sec:framework line 181; Amari 1998], the sentence "Rocks are certain, thus exhibit large second-variation rigidity, thus are hard to move under the dynamics" is a within-framework claim about belief-update resistance, and the dimensional caveat one sentence earlier covers it. This partial finding does not rescue C2 because the defect at lines 1933, 1981, 2983 and the circularity defect at line 2028 are independent of it.

The compound claim therefore fails on C2. The algebra-only sub-claim (C1 in isolation) is correct; if the user separately asks "is §sec:mass's derivation mathematically correct?" the answer under this evidence is yes. The question asked was "correctness AND motivation"; under that compound framing, the motivation portion is overclaimed at three specific lines that Blue's own falsification condition names, and at the kinetic-postulate licensing that external canon (Arnold 1989 §22; Marsden & Ratiu 1999 §1.4) does not bridge.

## Action

Three remediations follow directly from the decisive evidence.

First, the cross-reference at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2983` must be repaired. The $R^2 = 0.9998$ number sourced from the perplexity-vs-$K$ fit at line 2483 cannot be transported onto the precision-mass identification. The repair is either (a) delete the cross-reference and the "computationally validated" framing, or (b) display an actual mass-precision experiment in §sec:mass that measures $\omega^2$, $m_{\mathrm{eff}}$, and $\Sigma_p^{-1}$ as operationally independent quantities, fit $\log \omega^2 = a \log m_{\mathrm{eff}} + b$ (or equivalent), and report a goodness-of-fit value computed from that fit rather than from the WikiText-103 scaling regression.

Second, the within-section self-references at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1933` and `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1981` to "the empirical mass-precision validation of Section~\ref{sec:mass}" must either be removed or be backed by the displayed experiment described above. The integrator description at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3547` ("velocity-Verlet symplectic integrator with energy drift $<0.001\%$ over $25$ time units") describes machinery for a presumed experiment but does not display its results; if the experiment was run, its outputs (figure, R², ω², m_eff measurements) need to appear in §sec:mass; if it was not run, the references at 1933 and 1981 are stating something the manuscript does not have.

Third, the kinetic-postulate hedge at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2028` should be sharpened to acknowledge that the $\omega^2 \propto 1/M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ relation is a definitional consequence of the postulate rather than a non-trivial empirical scaling, and that under [Arnold 1989 §22] and [Marsden & Ratiu 1999 §1.4] a non-degenerate harmonic identification requires operational independence of $M$ and $V''$ that the section does not supply. The current hedge ("contingent on the kinetic postulate, and would be vacated by any other choice of kinetic metric") is true but understates the structural issue.

After these three repairs, the residual content of §sec:mass — the correct C1 algebra, the analogy framing at lines 1846, 1848, 2014 under the section's existing disclaimers, and the rock illustration at line 2023 under its within-framework reading — stands. The section title "A Mass Analogy" remains a labeled novel construction in the sense of `external_canon_math.md` §4, and that label is appropriate provided the empirical-anchor overclaim is repaired and the kinetic-postulate disclosure is sharpened.
