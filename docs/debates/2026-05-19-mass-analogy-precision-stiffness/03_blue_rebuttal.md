## Core attack

Red's strongest argument is attack 3 (circularity of $\omega^2 = k/m$ under coincident kinetic and stiffness matrices). My pre-investigation hypothesis was to defend this on the information-geometric ground that the Fisher metric is the canonically unique kinetic form on a statistical manifold by Cencov's theorem [Cencov 1972, AmariNagaoka 2000 §2.4], with the natural-gradient flow $\dot\theta = -F^{-1}\nabla L$ admitting a Lagrangian reading $T = \tfrac12 \dot\theta^\top F \dot\theta$. On direct analysis this defense collapses, and the collapse direction strengthens red's attack rather than rebutting it.

Take the manuscript's own kinetic form at line 2030, $\mathcal{T} = \tfrac12 \dot\mu^\top M_{\mu\mu}\dot\mu$, with the potential Hessian also equal to $M_{\mu\mu}$. The Euler–Lagrange equation $\mathcal{T} - V$ gives $M_{\mu\mu}\ddot\mu = -M_{\mu\mu}(\mu - \mu^*)$, which after left-multiplication by $M_{\mu\mu}^{-1}$ on the invertible regime reduces to $\ddot\mu = -(\mu - \mu^*)$. The natural mode frequency is $\omega^2 = 1$ per direction, independent of $M_{\mu\mu}$ entirely. The claim "$\omega^2 \propto 1/M_{\mathrm{eff}}$" at lines 1846, 1848, 2014, 2018, and 2983 is therefore not just contingent on the kinetic postulate — it is structurally trivialized by it. Whichever value $M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ takes, the harmonic frequency reads as unity in framework-internal units. The Fisher-metric defense, far from rescuing the harmonic identification, sharpens red's circularity charge: the kinetic identification of $M_{\mu\mu}$ with the Fisher matrix uniquely fixes $\omega^2 = 1$ in the isolated-agent quadratic limit, contradicting rather than confirming a $1/M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ scaling.

The only defense remaining is the explicit hedge at line 2028: "The match is consistent but contingent on the kinetic postulate, and would be vacated by any other choice of kinetic metric." Read straightforwardly, this disclosure removes the harmonic match from the set of falsifiable predictions of the framework: it does not assert that $\omega^2 \propto 1/M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ is a constraint on belief dynamics derivable from $\mathcal{F}$, only that under one particular kinetic-metric choice the relation can be made to hold. Red is correct that this is not a non-trivial empirical check on the precision-mass identification. The methodology rewards honest disclosure, but the C2 claim — that the analogy is "appropriately motivated as a within-framework precision-induced stiffness/mass analogy under explicit caveats" — requires the disclosure at 2028 to bear more weight than it can. The hedge labels the harmonic match as a definitional consequence of the postulate, not as a scaling relation.

External canon backs red here. [Arnold 1989 §22] specifies that for a multi-particle Lagrangian $T = \tfrac12 \dot q^\top M(q)\dot q$ with potential Hessian $V''(q^*)$, the small-oscillation frequencies $\omega_n^2$ are the eigenvalues of $M^{-1}V''$, and the relation $\omega^2 = k/m$ is non-degenerate precisely when $M$ and $V''$ are operationally independent objects on the configuration manifold. [Marsden & Ratiu 1999 §1.4] makes the same point: the inertia tensor and the potential Hessian are separate (0,2)-tensors at the equilibrium configuration, and identifying them by postulate requires the identification to be checked against an independent measurement of either one. The manuscript supplies no independent measurement.

## Defense

What survives of the C2 claim is the part that does not depend on the empirical-anchor references. §sec:mass at line 1846 announces "an empirically observable mass-like scaling $\omega^2 \propto m_{\text{eff}}^{-1}$ … once the kinetic-metric postulate of Section sec:velocity_quadratic is in place" — under attack 3 this announcement is on weak ground for the reasons just given. But the section then makes three structurally distinct moves that should be evaluated separately.

First, the second-variation algebra (Equations eq:precision_transport through eq:cross_block, lines 1860–2010) is correct as derived. Red concedes this under C1 with sympy and finite-difference verification at 7-digit precision (red opening, evidence items 1–5). Independent of the harmonic-scaling question, the Hessian block structure of $\mathcal{F}$ on the joint $(\mu, \Sigma)$ sector at consensus is computed correctly, including the GL(d) precision-transport law (Eq. eq:precision_transport), the boxed sender-side identity at line 1863 ($\Omega_{ji}^\top \tilde\Lambda_{q_i}^{(j)}\Omega_{ji} = \Lambda_{q_i}$), the mean-sector diagonal and off-diagonal blocks (Eqs. eq:mass_mu_diagonal and eq:mass_mu_offdiagonal), the covariance-sector blocks (Eqs. eq:mass_sigma_diagonal and eq:mass_sigma_offdiagonal), and the cross-block vanishing at consensus (Eq. eq:cross_block). The naming of this Hessian as a "mass matrix" is interpretive; the algebraic content is not.

Second, the line-1846 paragraph and the line-1848 paragraph explicitly disclose that the Hessian of $\mathcal{F}$ is "in the first instance, a *stiffness* on belief configuration space, not a mass", that "identification of $\Sigma_p^{-1}$ with an effective mass requires a separate kinetic-metric postulate", and that "no explicit Lagrangian $L = T - V$ is constructed from first principles, no Euler–Lagrange equations are derived". The line-2014 paragraph adds that "the identification of the Hessian sector $[M_{\mu\mu}]$ with an 'effective mass' is interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass" and that "the effective mass we obtain has dimensions inherited from $\Sigma^{-1}$ (dimensionless on the framework's representational space) rather than the kilogram dimension of physical mass." The line-2028 paragraph adds the kinetic-postulate hedge in plain terms. These disclosures are present and load-bearing. They do not cover the within-section references at 1933 and 1981 to an in-section experiment that the section does not display, but they do cover the analogy framing itself: a reader who reads §sec:mass without those two pointers receives an explicit second-variation derivation plus a postulated kinetic metric plus an explicit announcement that the resulting mass terminology is shorthand for the dual stiffness/inertia reading under that postulate.

Third, attack 4 (rock illustration as cross-framework overclaim) does not hold under the natural reading. Line 2023 reads, in full: "A macroscopic object maintains extraordinarily precise self-localization: $\bar\Sigma_{\mathrm{rock}} \approx \epsilon I$ with $\epsilon \ll 1$ a small dimensionless precision (we make no claim that $\epsilon$ corresponds to specific physical units; cf. the dimensional caveat in Section sec:scope_limitations) yields a large effective stiffness $M_{\mathrm{rock}} \sim \epsilon^{-1}$ in the Newtonian-analogy reading of Section sec:mass. Rocks are certain, thus exhibit large second-variation rigidity, thus are hard to move under the dynamics." The "dynamics" of the section is the natural-gradient flow on $\mathcal{F}$ defined in §sec:framework (line 181); the section's word "dynamics" is established at line 1846 ("variational free energy $\mathcal{F}$ that serves as the potential for agent dynamics") and is consistently used for belief-update dynamics throughout. The sentence "Rocks are certain, thus exhibit large second-variation rigidity, thus are hard to move under the dynamics" therefore reads as: a high-precision belief configuration is slow to update under natural-gradient descent on $\mathcal{F}$. Under that reading the dimensional caveat one sentence earlier suffices — the configuration-space stiffness $M_{\mathrm{rock}} \sim \epsilon^{-1}$ is a within-framework belief-update-resistance quantity, and the claim that high precision implies slow belief updates follows directly from the natural-gradient form $\dot\theta = -F(\theta)^{-1}\nabla\mathcal{F}$ [Amari 1998]: when $F$ is large, the inverse is small, and the update velocity is suppressed. Red's reading of "hard to move under the dynamics" as a physical-mechanics claim about inertial resistance to kinematic acceleration requires importing a physical-mechanics meaning of "the dynamics" that the section has not invoked. The sentence immediately following 2023 then explicitly disclaims the extension: "We do not extend this analogy to quantum-mechanical scenarios … the framework's effective mass is a configuration-space stiffness that we are not equating with physical inertial mass here." Read together, lines 2023 and the following sentence make the within-framework reading explicit and disclaim the cross-framework reading. Falsification condition F3 from red's own opening is satisfied: the rock illustration is a within-framework observation about belief-update difficulty rather than a cross-framework claim about physical mechanical resistance, with the disclaimers covering the bridge.

The aggregate position: the C2 claim does not survive intact, because the within-section empirical-anchor references at 1933 and 1981 and the cross-section conflation at 2983 are not covered by the existing caveats and the kinetic-postulate disclosure at 2028 cannot bear the falsifiability weight red places on it. What does survive is the C1 derivation (already conceded by red), the analogy framing at lines 1846, 1848, 2014 read on its own (with the disclosures functioning as designed), and the rock illustration at line 2023 read within-framework. The right action under this debate is not "BLUE_WINS" — it is a partial finding that the C1 algebra is correct, the C2 framing is overclaimed at three specific lines (1933, 1981, 2983) that require either deletion or substantiation by a displayed mass-precision experiment with R², ω², and m_eff measured as operationally independent quantities, and the residual analogy under explicit caveats stands once those overclaims are repaired.
