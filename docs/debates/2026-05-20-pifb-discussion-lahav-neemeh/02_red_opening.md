# Red Opening — pifb-discussion-lahav-neemeh

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Lahav-Neemeh subsection (Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3356-3396) is correctly calibrated: it accurately reports that Lahav-Neemeh 2022 indicate but do not supply an explicit cognitive-frame transformation law, it supplies the missing map as a derived composition with the cross-scale factor canceling to recover the single-scale special case, it differentiates pan-agentic structuralism from relational physicalism in three named ways, it honestly flags the diagonal-vs-per-agent invariance tension as an open question at 3378, and it registers the phenomenal-aggregation problem as the principal explanatory debt at 3395-3396.

## Position

Two of the manuscript's six sub-claims fail under primary-source verification and internal-consistency review: sub-claim 1 (Lahav-Neemeh characterization) is wrong in a load-bearing way because the manuscript at 3359 inverts the priority of the published paper's two thought experiments — it treats Alice/Bob as the primary case carrying the consciousness argument and Alice/ALICE as a "variant", when the published paper does the opposite — and sub-claim 6 (mathematical-content offer at 3381) is internally inconsistent with the 3378 open-question acknowledgement because 3381 lists "an explicit invariant scalar" without the diagonal-vs-per-agent qualification that 3378 just installed.

## Evidence

- **Lahav-Neemeh 2022 §"Equivalence Principle of Consciousness: Conceptual Argument"** [LahavNeemeh2022, Frontiers in Psychology 12:704270]: The primary thought experiment carrying the consciousness argument is Alice/ALICE (human vs zombie AI named "Artificial Learning Intelligent Conscious Entity"). The Alice/Bob train-platform setup is a brief Galilean-relativity setup analogy that appears in the introductory subsection "The Principle of Relativity and the Equivalence Principle" before consciousness is introduced. The Alice/ALICE comparison is what carries the equivalence-principle derivation; Alice/Bob is a physics analogy that establishes the relativistic mode of argument and does not itself involve a third-person measurement of a phenomenal state. Primary-source verification: Alice/Bob does not appear as a thought experiment in §4-5 — only in the relativity-setup material.

- **Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3359**: "In their Alice/Bob thought experiment (presented in the published paper alongside an Alice/ALICE human and zombie-AI variant), each observer measures the other as 'mere brain activity' from their own cognitive frame; both measurements are taken to be correct on their account, and the two perspectives are described as corresponding to one another." This parenthetical inverts the priority: the published paper's primary case is Alice/ALICE (consciousness-equivalence), and the Alice/Bob "each observer measures the other as mere brain activity" framing is not the framing the published paper uses for Alice/Bob — that framing is closer to the Alice/ALICE conceptual argument. The subsection then builds the entire eq:alice_bob_composition derivation (3367-3376) on top of an inverted summary of the source.

- **Lahav-Neemeh 2022, primary-source check on the transformation law** [LahavNeemeh2022 §4-5]: The transformation between first-person and third-person frames is referenced ("we will describe the transformations between first-person... and third-person... cognitive frames of reference") and conceptually analogized to a Lorentz transformation, but no explicit mathematical transformation formula is presented. Equations 1-10 are cognitive-subsystem processing equations (sensation, perception, cognition, memory, attention, emotion, affordance, self, language), not inter-frame transformations. Sub-claim 1's narrower claim that the transformation is "indicated rather than supplied" is therefore accurate; what fails is the Alice/Bob priority inversion in the same sentence.

- **Lahav-Neemeh 2022, primary-source check on second postulate** [LahavNeemeh2022 §3-5]: No constancy postulate analogous to the speed of light is proposed; no group-theoretic transformation structure is developed. Sub-claim 4's Divergence III at 3389-3390 is therefore accurately stated, modulo R5 below.

- **Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3378**: "Whether a fully frame-independent scalar (analogous to $ds^2$) survives the full per-agent action of the gauge group is itself an open question; the candidate constructions in Section sec:consensus_metric are gauge-orbit-averaged and conditional on a regulator not yet supplied." This is the manuscript's own honest acknowledgement that the per-agent invariant scalar is open.

- **Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3381**: "Where we can offer mathematical content beyond what their published formalization provides (an explicit non-compact transformation group, an explicit invariant scalar, an explicit covariance transport law, and an explicit account of why first-person and third-person measurements relate the way they do) we view this as complementary to rather than competitive with their proposal." The "explicit invariant scalar" entry is unqualified. Read in isolation, it claims the framework supplies an invariant scalar of the kind sub-claim 6's parallel construction requires (the special-relativistic analogue is ds²); read against 3378 three paragraphs back, it can only refer to the diagonal-invariant F, which is invariant under one-common-transformation but not under the per-agent action that the special-relativistic analogy requires. The manuscript does not flag the gap at 3381 itself. A reader who arrives at the mathematical-content sentence after the diagonal-vs-per-agent paragraph at 3378 has to back-reference to disambiguate; a reader who quotes 3381 without 3378 is misled. This is an internal-consistency failure of the mathematical-content sub-claim at the level of presentation.

- **Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3367-3376, eq:alice_bob_composition**: The factored map is $\Omega_{\mathrm{Bob}, i} = [\exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Bob}}^{(N)}) \exp(-\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)})] \cdot [\exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)}) \exp(-\phi_{i}^{(N-k)})]$. The two cross-scale exponentials $\exp(-\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)})\exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Alice}}^{(N)})$ are mutual inverses and cancel to the identity, leaving the direct transport $\Omega_{\mathrm{Bob}, i} = \exp(\phi_{\mathrm{Bob}}^{(N)})\exp(-\phi_i^{(N-k)})$. This is the trivial structural-identity decomposition of a single transport into an inserted pair of inverse factors. The manuscript's claim at 3365 that the Alice/Bob isomorphism is "supplied here as a composition of two transports, neither of which is assumed" overstates what is being done: the framework supplies one transport ($\Omega_{\mathrm{Bob},i}$) and a name for inserting a chosen intermediate frame. The decomposition is informative as commentary on which intermediate frame the two factors correspond to, but the structural map is the single direct transport, not a non-trivial composition that the two-exponential parameterization derives. This weakens sub-claim 2's claim of a "derived special case" — the cross-scale-cancels-to-identity case is the structural-identity case, which is also what Lahav-Neemeh's single-scale setup would correspond to. The derivation is the identity, not new.

## Falsification conditions

R6 (the priority-inversion attack): I concede this attack if Lahav-Neemeh 2022 in fact has Alice/Bob as a developed thought experiment in §4-5 (not merely as an introductory Galilean-relativity analogy) and if "each observer measures the other as mere brain activity" is the published framing of Alice/Bob in those sections.

R2 (the invariant-scalar internal-tension attack): I concede this attack if either (a) the diagonal-vs-per-agent distinction at 3378 is read by a competent reader as a binding qualifier on the "explicit invariant scalar" listed three paragraphs later at 3381, or (b) the manuscript at 3381 in fact qualifies "invariant scalar" with "(under diagonal action of $\mathrm{GL}(K)$)" or equivalent and I have misread the line.

R-trivial (the structural-identity decomposition attack on sub-claim 2): I concede this attack if blue can produce a non-trivial transport composition derived from $\Omega_{ab} = \exp(\phi_a)\exp(-\phi_b)$ in which the cross-scale factor does not collapse to the identity — i.e., if there is a regime within the framework where eq:alice_bob_composition is not the algebraic insertion of $I$ into a single direct transport.

The decisive falsification of the overall claim is R6: if the Alice/Bob thought experiment is the published paper's primary framing of the consciousness argument and the manuscript's summary at 3359 is accurate to the source, then sub-claim 1 stands and the principal red attack collapses to the secondary R2 internal-consistency tightening, which would put red into RED_WINS_NARROW territory at best. Primary-source check via WebFetch of the Frontiers PMC article and the §-headings indicates the opposite: Alice/ALICE is the consciousness-argument case, Alice/Bob is the relativity-setup case, and the manuscript's parenthetical at 3359 has them inverted.
