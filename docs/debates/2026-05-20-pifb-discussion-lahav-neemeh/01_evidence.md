## What this evidence does NOT settle

1. Whether the Lahav-Neemeh 2022 paper genuinely "leaves as a placeholder" the transformation law (manuscript at 3359). Primary-source verification needed. If LahavNeemeh 2022 §4-5 does provide a transformation law that the manuscript missed, the characterization needs revision.
2. Whether the Lahav-Neemeh 2025 follow-up paper provides additional formalization that the manuscript's characterization should account for.
3. Whether the Alice/Bob composition at eq:alice_bob_composition (3371-3375) is mathematically correct. The two-exponential parameterization $\Omega_{ab} = \exp(\phi_a)\exp(-\phi_b)$ has the composition rule $\Omega_{ab}\Omega_{bc} = \exp(\phi_a)\exp(-\phi_b)\exp(\phi_b)\exp(-\phi_c) = \exp(\phi_a)\exp(-\phi_c) = \Omega_{ac}$, which gives the cancellation. So the composition is the trivial identity decomposition; the manuscript correctly notes this is a "structural" decomposition rather than substantive composition. The presentation is correct.
4. Whether the "invariant scalar" claim at 3381 is in tension with the diagonal-only invariance at 3378. The 3378 paragraph explicitly admits "Whether a fully frame-independent scalar (analogous to $ds^2$) survives the full per-agent action of the gauge group is itself an open question." The 3381 "invariant scalar" claim then refers to the diagonal-invariant F. These two are consistent if the reader carries forward the diagonal-vs-per-agent distinction. The manuscript could tighten by adding "(under diagonal action)" at 3381's "invariant scalar" mention.
5. Whether the "single-scale" characterization of Lahav-Neemeh at 3361 is accurate. Their published work focuses on human-cognitive-scale observers, but whether they explicitly rule out multi-scale extensions or merely don't develop them is a primary-source check.

Teams should verify points 1-2 and 5 against primary sources; point 4 is a textual-tightening question.
