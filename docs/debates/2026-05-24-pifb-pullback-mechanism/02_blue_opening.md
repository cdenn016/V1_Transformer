# Blue Opening — pifb-pullback-mechanism

Synthesized from five expert memos (geometer, gauge-theorist, info-geometer, variational, philosophy-of-science). Each is cited or discounted below.

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest attack: the canonical fiber-bundle metric built from a connection is the Kaluza–Klein form $\hat g = \pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}} + k(\omega,\omega)$, in which the connection one-form $\omega$ enters the **vertical/fiber** block as an Ad-invariant fiber metric and the **horizontal** block is the pulled-back base metric $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ [Kaluza–Klein line element; the bundle-metric recipe]. The manuscript's $g_{E_q}$ inverts this: it places a connection-built quadratic form $\kappa(A,A)$ in the **horizontal** block and Fisher-Rao in the vertical. So the manuscript does **not** match the textbook bundle-metric form, and worse, the horizontal block it substitutes is gauge-noninvariant — a base-space scalar quadratic in $A$ alone, which standard gauge theory says cannot be a tensor on the total space because only curvature invariants are gauge-invariant [Nakahara2003 §10.4]. On this reading, calling $G^{(q)}$ "the pullback of a bona-fide bundle metric" rather than "an $L^2$ score pullback" is relabeling, and the "perceived space $=G^{(s)}$" identification is an untestable metaphysical preference.

## Position

I concede the textbook-match outright: the manuscript's $g_{E_q}$ is **not** the canonical Kaluza–Klein form, and any defense resting on "this is the standard bundle metric" is malformed. The defensible claim is narrower and survives the canon.

A "metric on the total space $E$" means *any* Riemannian metric on $E$ — not specifically the Kaluza–Klein recipe. In the body regime ($\mathfrak{so}(3)$, compact, $\kappa=-\mathrm{tr}$), $g_{E_q}$ is a block-diagonal sum of two positive-definite forms over a well-defined horizontal/vertical split coming from a legitimate pure-gauge connection $A=U^{-1}dU$. That is, by definition, a Riemannian metric on $E_q$ — hence a bundle metric. The pullback $G^{(q)}=(\sigma^{(q)})^* g_{E_q}$ is then the ordinary pullback of a (0,2)-tensor by a smooth section, and the formula $G^{(q)}_{\mu\nu}=\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)+\mathbb{E}_q[(\nabla_\mu\log q)(\nabla_\nu\log q)]$ (:2763) follows from decomposing the section's tangent map through the block-diagonal $g_{E_q}$ [geometer memo; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III, vertical part of a section's differential = covariant derivative].

The horizontal block being $\kappa(A,A)$ rather than $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ is a **principled novel substitution forced by the setting**, not the textbook recipe and not a defect. The "it from bit" program (:2706) precludes a pre-existing base metric $g_{\mathcal{C}}$ on the noumenal substrate $\mathcal{C}$ — the entire point is to *induce* geometry on $\mathcal{C}$ from information, so the $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ horizontal block of Kaluza–Klein is unavailable by hypothesis. The manuscript substitutes a connection-built horizontal form and **labels it as such**: "tw" not "YM" (:2747), connection-dependent, generally not gauge-invariant, with the non-invariance computed explicitly (:2768). Under the external-canon rule for distinguishing standard from novel claims [external_canon_math.md §4], a construction that differs from the standard form and is *labeled and justified as novel* is admissible; the error to flag is asserting standard-equivalence without proof, which the manuscript does not do. The four-tier family $G^{(q,p,s,r)}$ (:2771–2782) is well-defined: four genuine pullbacks differing only in which fiber section is pulled back.

On sub-claim (3), "perceived space $=G^{(s)}$": the mathematical tier-decomposition is well-defined and the slow-tier assignment is architecturally motivated, but the *identification* is an interpretive proposal without a discriminant, and I defend it only as such — the manuscript proposes it ("We propose… most naturally identified," :2795) and explicitly declines to adjudicate the metaphysics (:2797).

## Evidence

- **Vertical block is forced, not chosen [info-geometer].** The Fisher-Rao metric is the unique reparameterization-invariant Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold up to scale [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]. The manuscript's fiber metric (:2716) is the textbook Gaussian Fisher-Rao form $\delta\mu^\top\Sigma^{-1}\delta\mu+\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1}\delta\Sigma\,\Sigma^{-1}\delta\Sigma)$, and the score-form identity at :2759 is the definitional Fisher identity $g_{ij}=\mathbb{E}[\partial_i\log p\,\partial_j\log p]$ [AmariNagaoka2000; Nielsen2020 *Entropy* 22(10):1100]. The vertical block — the load-bearing information-geometric content — is canonically mandated and unassailable.
- **Horizontal-block non-invariance is expected and disclosed, not a defect [gauge-theorist].** Under change of trivialization the connection one-form transforms inhomogeneously, $A\to g^{-1}Ag+g^{-1}dg$ [Nakahara2003 §10.4; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.1]; any quadratic form in $A$ alone is therefore gauge-noninvariant. This is the generic behavior of a connection-built object — only curvature invariants escape it, and in Regime I the pure-gauge connection has $F=dA+\tfrac12[A,A]\equiv 0$, so $\mathrm{tr}(FF)$ vanishes and supplies no invariant. The manuscript states exactly this (:2768) and routes genuine invariance to the consensus metric rather than faking a Yang–Mills escape. Disclosed non-invariance of a connection-built block, in the Kaluza–Klein tradition where the base-tensor representative is itself section-dependent [Bleecker1981; Frankel2011 Ch. 20], is the correct treatment.
- **Tier semantics rest on the active-inference fast/slow split [variational].** Hierarchical active inference separates fast Bayesian state inference (posterior $q$, updated every observation) from slow parameter learning (generative-model concentration parameters, updated at trial boundaries) [FristonEtAl2017 *Neural Computation* 29:1–49; Da Costa et al. 2020 *J. Math. Psych.* 99:102447]. This grounds the manuscript's slow-tier $s$ vs fast-tier $q$ split in canon and validates the [Friston2017] attribution at :2797. The manuscript also handles [Hoffman2019] honestly, explicitly confining it to the species-evolutionary timescale and declining to attribute the within-lifetime mechanism to it (:2797).
- **The defense is non-circular [philosophy-of-science].** The mathematical claims derive from external authority — Cencov forces the vertical block, Nakahara/Kobayashi–Nomizu supply the connection transformation law — not from the manuscript asserting them. The manuscript is cited only to identify *what is claimed*; canon establishes *that it holds*.

The geometer memo's framing that $g_{E_q}$ is "structurally identical to Sasaki/Kaluza–Klein" is **discounted**: the red panel's canon (Kaluza–Klein line element; Albuquerque on Sasaki) correctly shows the textbook recipe puts the connection in the *vertical* block and $\pi^*g$ in the horizontal, which the manuscript does not match. The geometer's valid retained contribution is the narrower point that the pullback-of-a-(0,2)-tensor mechanics and the H/V orthogonality-by-definition are standard; the Sasaki/KK *match* claim is not defended.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold.

1. **The horizontal block fails positive-definiteness in the body regime.** It does not: for compact $\mathfrak{so}(N)$ with $\kappa=-\mathrm{tr}$, $\kappa(A,A)\ge 0$, and the block sum of two positive-definite forms is positive-definite, so $g_{E_q}$ is Riemannian on $E_q$ [geometer memo]. (In the non-compact $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})$ worked example the horizontal block is indefinite by design — the manuscript labels that an indefinite bilinear form, not a Riemannian metric, :2745, so the claim is not made there.)
2. **The pullback formula does not follow from the tangent-map decomposition through a block-diagonal $g_{E_q}$.** It does: the vertical part of a section's differential is the covariant derivative, and a block-diagonal metric kills the cross term by definition [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III]. The cross-term vanishing at :2755 is definitional (the metric is *built* block-diagonal), not a theorem — legitimate, but to be read as a definition.
3. **The vertical block is not Fisher-Rao.** It is, and Cencov uniqueness forbids any other information metric [Cencov1972].

The position is **NOT** falsified by the horizontal block failing to equal $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ — the manuscript never claims it does, the "it from bit" setting precludes a prior $g_{\mathcal{C}}$, and the substitution is labeled novel and disclosed.

Two sub-claims I concede rather than defend.

- **The "$L^2$ vs bundle-metric pullback" distinction (:2726) is a category upgrade, near-vacuous as a formula.** Specifying $g_{E_q}$ on $E$ genuinely repairs a category error (the score pullback metrizes only the fiber; now a total-space metric exists). But its only *numerical* content over the $L^2$ score form is the additive horizontal block, which vanishes when $A=0$ (:2771), collapsing $G^{(q)}$ to the bare score expression. The manuscript's "does not earn the name" rhetoric should not be granted weight beyond this: real as a definition, additive content exactly $\kappa(A,A)$ and nothing more [info-geometer, philosophy-of-science].
- **"Perceived space $=G^{(s)}$" is interpretive preference, not a well-posed empirical claim, as stated.** Active inference locates *structure* in slow parameters generically, but supplies no discriminant distinguishing a $G^{(s)}$-identification of phenomenal perceived space from a $G^{(p)}$- or $G^{(q)}$-identification; and in the reported simulations the slow channel is frozen (:2799), so $G^{(s)}$ is constant and the assignment is never exercised against the fast tiers [variational, philosophy-of-science]. The identification is well-posed only if a discriminant is named (e.g., a frozen-slow-channel agent reporting stable perceived geometry under fluctuating $G^{(q)}$; a $G^{(s)}$-ablated agent failing structural-perception while passing attention tasks). Absent that, I defend only the weaker, well-posed claim: the tier-decomposition is mathematically well-defined and the slow-tier assignment is architecturally motivated. The stronger reading — that the framework *establishes* perceived space is $G^{(s)}$ — is not defensible, but the manuscript's hedging forecloses that reading.
