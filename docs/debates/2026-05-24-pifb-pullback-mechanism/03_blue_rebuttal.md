# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-pullback-mechanism

## Concession

Three of Red's points are granted, and the concessions are not cosmetic.

**The canonical-form inversion is real (Red point 1; geometer, gauge-theorist).** The standard Kaluza-Klein / Sasaki bundle metric puts the *pulled-back base metric* in the horizontal block and routes the connection one-form into the vertical/fiber shift. The canonical 5D line element `ds² = g_{μν}dx^μ dx^ν + φ²(A_ν dx^ν + dx^5)²` carries the base metric `g_{μν}` horizontally and the gauge potential `A` only inside the fiber term [Nakahara2003 §11.1; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II–III; KK line element verified by canon-cop, 02b_canon_cop.md:16–26]. The manuscript's `g_{E_q}` puts `κ(A_μ,A_ν)` in the horizontal slot. That is an inversion of the canonical layout, and canon-cop verified it against the primary source. Blue does not contest this. Any defense that calls `κ(A,A)` "the canonical horizontal block" is wrong and is abandoned here.

**The "bona-fide / earns the name" framing is unearned against the canonical object (Red point 5; philosophy-of-science).** The manuscript's :2726 contrast — score-only is "merely L²" whereas `g_{E_q}` is a "pullback of a bona-fide fiber-bundle metric" — reads, against Nakahara / Kobayashi-Nomizu, as inflated. "Bona-fide" connotes the canonical KK/Sasaki construction, and the construction is not that. Blue concedes the *label*. What survives is a narrower object, defended below; the rhetorical upgrade at :2726 does not survive and should be rewritten.

**"Perceived space = G^{(s)}" is not Popper-falsifiable (Red point 4; variational, philosophy-of-science).** The manuscript itself states the framework "does not adjudicate" among the coexistent tiers (:2797) and offers `G^{(s)}` as the "most natural" identification (:2795). "Most natural" is not a discriminator; no measurement separates the localizations. Red is right that this is not falsifiable in Popper's sense [Popper 1959]. Blue concedes the falsifiability point. The defense below narrows the claim to *well-posed proposal*, which is all the claim asserts ("well-posed (if interpretive)", 00_claim.md:12) — not to *empirically testable*.

## Core attack

**Red's point 3 — the Cencov attack — is a category error, and it is the load-bearing conjunct of Red's case (info-geometer).** Red argues that because `G^{(q)} = κ(A,A) + Fisher` fails sufficient-statistic invariance, it "is not an information metric," and that the only information-bearing part is the vertical block, "identical in both readings." This misplaces the domain of Chentsov's theorem.

Chentsov/Cencov uniqueness states that the Fisher metric is the unique Riemannian metric — up to scale — *on a statistical manifold*, i.e. a manifold whose points are probability distributions, invariant under sufficient statistics [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 §2; external_canon_math.md §1; Fisher-information-metric scope confirmed against primary source — the theorem makes no claim about metrics on manifolds other than statistical manifolds, nor about pullbacks to a base]. The statistical manifold in this construction is the Gaussian fiber `B`. The vertical block of `g_{E_q}` is exactly the Fisher-Rao metric on `B` (:2716–2720, :2759), so Cencov uniqueness *is satisfied where it applies*. `G^{(q)}` is a pullback of `g_{E_q}` to the base `C` (:2749–2755). `C` is the noumenal substrate, not a manifold of probability distributions. Nothing in the manuscript claims `G^{(q)}` is an information metric on `C`; it claims `G^{(q)}` is the pullback of a bundle metric on `E` whose vertical block is Fisher-Rao (:2734, :2771).

Demanding Cencov-invariance of `G^{(q)}` on `C` is demanding uniqueness in a category the theorem does not govern. The Fisher metric is not even *defined* as "the unique metric on every manifold a statistical object gets pulled back to" — it is unique on the statistical manifold. A pullback by a section can carry any additional horizontal structure the total-space metric was given without violating Cencov, exactly as the Sasaki metric on `TM` carries base-metric horizontal structure on top of a fiber metric and no one accuses it of "failing to be an information metric." Red's point 3 therefore proves nothing about the validity of the construction; it proves only that `G^{(q)}` is not itself a Fisher metric on `C` — which the manuscript never asserts and which is true of every bundle-metric pullback ever written.

Strip the category error and Red's point 3 collapses to the true-but-harmless observation that `κ(A,A)` is statistically inert (depends on the frame, not the family). Blue agrees `κ(A,A)` is statistically inert. That is the premise of the non-vacuity defense below, not an objection to it.

## Defense

**`g_{E_q}` is a genuine Riemannian metric on `E_q` within the disclosed fixed gauge, and `G^{(q)}` is a genuine pullback — independent of the canonical-form question (geometer).** A Riemannian metric on a smooth manifold is, by definition, any smooth, symmetric, positive-definite (0,2)-tensor field — a smoothly varying inner product on each tangent space [Lee2013 Ch. 13; definition confirmed against primary source]. The block-diagonal form `g_{E_q} = κ(A,A) ⊕ g_B` is symmetric, smooth in `(c,q)`, and positive-definite on `H ⊕ V` whenever `κ = −tr` on compact `so(N)` (the body regime, :2744, :2766) makes the horizontal block positive-definite and Fisher-Rao makes the vertical block positive-definite. A direct sum of two positive-definite forms on complementary subspaces is positive-definite on the whole tangent space. Therefore `g_{E_q}` is a Riemannian metric on `E_q` within the gauge fixing the manuscript explicitly restricts to (:2732, :2747). This holds whether or not it equals the canonical KK horizontal layout: the definition of "Riemannian metric" is not "the KK form," it is "smooth positive-definite (0,2)-tensor." The pullback `G^{(q)} = (σ^{(q)})^* g_{E_q}` by the smooth section `σ^{(q)}` is the standard pullback operation `(σ^*g)(v,w) = g(dσ·v, dσ·w)` [Nakahara2003 §5.4; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §I.5; pullback-of-metric operation confirmed against primary source], positive-definite precisely when `dσ` is injective (the section is an immersion) — a generic and checkable condition, not a defect. So the construction is a valid pullback of a valid metric. Red never disputes this; Red disputes the *canonical-status label*, which Blue has already conceded.

**The bundle framing is not vacuous: the A=0 collapse at :2771 is the diagnostic that proves it (gauge-theorist).** Red's deepest charge is that the "L²-only vs bundle-metric" distinction at :2726 is near-vacuous because the cross term vanishes by fiat and the score term is unchanged. The cross term does vanish by definition — `g_{E_q}` is declared block-diagonal in `H ⊕ V` (:2734) — and Blue grants that the orthogonality is definitional, not a theorem. But block-diagonality being definitional does not make the *horizontal block* vanish. The substantive content the bundle framing adds is exactly the horizontal `κ(A_μ,A_ν)` term. The manuscript itself supplies the diagnostic: when the gauge frame is locally constant, `A^{(i)} = 0`, and `G^{(q)}` collapses to the bare score expression (:2771). The contrapositive is the whole point — when `A^{(i)} ≠ 0`, i.e. in the *generic non-gauge-fixed regime* where the agent's representational frame `U_i(c)` twists across `C`, the pullback carries a nonzero frame-twist length `κ(A_μ,A_ν)` that the score-only `L²` pullback does not have. Score-only is the `A=0` special case; the bundle pullback is the general case. Two constructions that agree only on a measure-zero slice (constant frame) and differ everywhere else are not the same object relabeled. The connection `A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1}dU_i` is a legitimate `g`-valued connection one-form [external_canon_math.md §2, "connection on a principal bundle"], and `κ(A,A)` is a smooth quadratic form in it; assembling it into the horizontal block of a total-space metric is non-standard in *layout* (conceded) but well-defined as a (0,2)-tensor within the fixed gauge. Red's own falsification condition — "κ(A,A) is shown to depend on the statistical family, restoring Cencov" — is irrelevant: the bundle framing's content is geometric (frame twist), not statistical, and it does not need to restore Cencov to be non-vacuous, because it lives on `C`, where Cencov does not apply (Core attack).

**The tier-identification is a well-posed proposal, which is all the claim asserts (variational).** "Perceived space = G^{(s)}" is grounded in a real architectural distinction the framework commits to independently: slow-timescale generative-model parameters (`s`) versus fast-timescale state beliefs (`q`), and the hierarchical active-inference literature does locate structural perceptual organization in *parameter learning* of the generative model rather than in state-level inference [Friston2017, on the parameter/state separation in hierarchical models]. Identifying the quasi-static structural background with the parameter tier is therefore a principled mapping onto an existing timescale separation, not an arbitrary pick among indistinguishable tensors — `G^{(s)}` is quasi-static, `G^{(q)}` fluctuates on the perceptual timescale (:2789, :2793), and that difference is a real property of the construction. The claim asserts only that this is "well-posed (if interpretive)" (00_claim.md:12); it does not assert falsifiability. Blue concedes (above) that it is not Popper-falsifiable and that the manuscript's own Hoffman/Friston caveats (:2797) correctly fence the attribution. A well-posed interpretive proposal tied to a real architectural distinction is exactly what the claim says it is.

## Falsification conditions

Blue's defense is wrong, and the claim should fall, if any of the following holds:

- **The body regime does not actually use compact `so(N)` with `κ = −tr`**, so the horizontal block is not positive-definite and `g_{E_q}` is not Riemannian on `E_q`. (The manuscript states `so(3)` and `κ = −tr` for the body, :2714, :2744, :2766; the worked signature example at :2745 is explicitly indefinite and Blue does *not* defend it as Riemannian — there `g_{E_q}` is a pseudo-Riemannian metric, and the claim's "bona-fide *metric*" must be read in that weaker sense for the `sl(2,R)` example.)
- **The section `σ^{(q)}` fails to be an immersion** on a set of positive measure, so the pullback `G^{(q)}` is degenerate rather than a genuine metric on `C`. (Pullback positive-definiteness requires `dσ` injective [Lee2013]; if the agent's belief field is locally constant in `μ`, `Σ`, *and* frame over an open region, `G^{(q)}` degenerates there.)
- **Cencov's theorem is shown to govern pullback metrics on a base manifold**, not only metrics on a statistical manifold — which would revive Red's point 3. (Primary source confirms it does not.)
- **The connection contribution `κ(A,A)` is identically zero in the framework's operative regime** — i.e. agents' frames `U_i(c)` are constant across `C` in every realized configuration — collapsing the bundle pullback to score-only everywhere and making the :2726 distinction genuinely vacuous in practice. (This is an empirical question about the realized frame fields; if the simulations only ever run with `A=0`, the non-vacuity defense holds in principle but is inert in the manuscript's own experiments.)
