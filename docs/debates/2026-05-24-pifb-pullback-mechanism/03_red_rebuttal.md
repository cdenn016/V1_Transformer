# Red Rebuttal — pifb-pullback-mechanism

Synthesized from the five red expert memos (geometer, gauge-theorist, info-geometer, variational, philosophy-of-science). Each is cited or discounted below. This rebuttal targets the blue opening (`02_blue_opening.md`); the red opening was not consulted.

## Concession

Three points in the blue opening are correct and Red grants them without reservation.

First, the vertical block is Cencov-canonical and unassailable. Blue is right that the fiber metric at :2716 is the textbook Gaussian Fisher-Rao form and that the score-outer-product identity at :2759 is the definitional Fisher identity $g_{jk} = \mathbb{E}[\partial_j\log p\,\partial_k\log p]$, fixed uniquely up to scale by invariance under sufficient statistics [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2; Fisher information metric (Chentsov's theorem), https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fisher_information_metric]. The red info-geometer memo concurs that the vertical content is "a legitimate information-geometric object." This block is not where the claim fails.

Second, the gauge-noninvariance of the horizontal block is disclosed and behaves exactly as the canon predicts. Under a change of trivialization the connection one-form transforms inhomogeneously, $A \to g^{-1}Ag + g^{-1}dg$ [Nakahara2003 §10.4; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.1], so any quadratic form in $A$ alone is non-invariant; the manuscript computes the Maurer-Cartan cross terms explicitly at :2768 rather than hiding them. Red does not convict on "the block is non-invariant and that was concealed" — it was not concealed, and disclosed frame-dependence of a connection-built object is generic, as the red gauge-theorist memo's own steelman grants.

Third, the tangent-map decomposition and the vanishing cross term are legitimate as a definitional move. Blue's reading at :33 that the cross term vanishes because $g_{E_q}$ is *built* block-diagonal (not because of a derived gauge-orthogonality theorem) is the correct reading, and the red geometer memo independently calls the cross-term vanishing "tautological — $g_{E_q}$ is defined block-diagonal in $H\oplus V$ (:2734), so $g_{E_q}(X_H,Y_V)=0$ by fiat." Sub-point 3's pullback mechanics are sound. Red and Blue agree here.

## Core attack

Blue's defense is internally inconsistent, and the inconsistency is exactly where the claim lives. At :15 Blue defends the "bona-fide bundle metric" label by calling $\kappa(A,A)$ "a principled novel substitution forced by the setting" — a positive claim that the substitution earns the bundle-metric framing. At :40 Blue concedes the opposite: the "$L^2$ vs bundle-metric pullback" distinction is "near-vacuous as a formula," with "additive content exactly $\kappa(A,A)$ and nothing more." These two statements cannot both carry the claim. If the distinction is near-vacuous as a formula (Blue :40), then the manuscript's :2726 rhetoric ("does not earn the name") and the :2784 assertion that the bundle-pullback "gives 'it from bit' a precise sense: a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback of a fiber-respecting metric" are doing work the mathematics does not support. If the distinction is substantive enough to ground "bona-fide bundle-metric pullback" — the claim's load-bearing wording — then Blue's :40 concession gave away the case.

The retreat that papers over this is Blue's :13 redefinition: "a metric on the total space $E$ means *any* Riemannian metric on $E$ — not specifically the Kaluza-Klein recipe." Grant the redefinition arithmetic and it still fails to save the claim as written. The canon-cop verified, against the primary Kaluza-Klein line element, that in the standard fiber-bundle metric the connection enters the *vertical* block and the horizontal block is the pulled-back base metric $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ (`02b_canon_cop.md`:16-22); the manuscript inverts this, placing a standalone $\kappa(A,A)$ horizontally [red geometer memo; HandWiki "Bundle metric," $\hat g = \pi^* g + k\omega$, https://handwiki.org/wiki/Bundle_metric; Albuquerque, "Notes on the Sasaki metric," 2018]. Under the *only non-trivial* reading of "bundle metric" — the one that distinguishes a connection-coupled total-space metric from an arbitrary block-diagonal form — the manuscript does not match. Under Blue's *trivial* reading, every block-diagonal positive form on $E$ is "a bundle metric," which is true and empty: it cannot underwrite :2726's claim that the score-only pullback "does not earn the name pullback of a fiber-bundle metric" while $G^{(q)}$ does. Both pullbacks are then pullbacks of "a metric on $E$" in the trivial sense (the score-only metric is the $A=0$ block-diagonal form, :2771), so the manuscript's own contrast collapses on Blue's definition. The claim asserts the L²-vs-bundle distinction is "substantive"; Blue's surviving defense establishes only that it is trivial.

The claim's fourth conjunct is conceded outright by Blue and Red banks it. The claim asserts that "perceived space $= G^{(s)}$" is "well-posed (if interpretive) rather than an arbitrary choice." Well-posed requires a discriminator. Blue concedes at :41 there is none: "active inference ... supplies no discriminant distinguishing a $G^{(s)}$-identification ... from a $G^{(p)}$- or $G^{(q)}$-identification; and in the reported simulations the slow channel is frozen (:2799), so $G^{(s)}$ is constant and the assignment is never exercised." The red variational and philosophy-of-science memos reach the same finding: the identification is "a preference among coexistent tensors (:2782), not a derived result," with "no behavioral signature, no consequence" distinguishing the tiers, and the [Friston2017] attribution at :2797 is loaded past what the source supports — active inference distributes structural organization across hierarchical depth, not in a single slow-parameter tier [FristonEtAl2017, *Neural Computation* 29:1-49; ParrPezzuloFriston2022, *Active Inference*, MIT Press]. A choice among coexistent objects defended by "most naturally" (:2795) with no discriminating consequence is an interpretive preference, which is the negation of "well-posed rather than arbitrary." Sub-point 4 of the claim is not earned, and Blue does not contest this.

## Defense

Blue's central rescue — "the 'it from bit' program precludes a pre-existing base metric $g_{\mathcal{C}}$, so the $\pi^* g_{\mathcal{C}}$ horizontal block of Kaluza-Klein is unavailable by hypothesis" (:15) — does not excuse the substitution; it diagnoses why the construction cannot deliver what the claim promises. The red geometer memo identifies the circularity precisely: the canonical Sasaki/Kaluza-Klein horizontal block requires a base metric as *input*, and the manuscript wants the base metric as *output* (:2784, "a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback"). Conceding that no base metric exists to pull up is conceding that the canonical horizontal block — the part of a bundle metric that carries base-manifold geometry — is absent. What remains is the vertical Fisher block (which is identical to the score-only $L^2$ pullback) plus an additive $\kappa(A,A)$. So the construction does not metrize $\mathcal{C}$ "by pullback of a bona-fide bundle metric" in any sense stronger than the $L^2$ pullback already did; it adds a frame-rotation form to the same Fisher object. Blue's "forced by the setting" is correct as to the force and wrong as to the conclusion: the setting forces the construction *out of* the canonical bundle-metric form, into a block-diagonal sum whose only base-coupled term is a non-tensorial quadratic in the connection.

That non-tensoriality is the second leg, and Blue does not neutralize it. Blue at :22 reads the non-invariance as "the generic behavior of a connection-built object ... in the Kaluza-Klein tradition where the base-tensor representative is itself section-dependent." This conflates two distinct properties that the red gauge-theorist memo separates: the canonical Kaluza-Klein total-space metric is connection-*dependent* yet invariant under the principal action — "a metric on the total space invariant under the principal action reduces to a metric together with a gauge field on the base" [Wikipedia, Kaluza-Klein theory, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaluza-Klein_theory; Nakahara2003 §10.4]. The transformation $U_i \to U_i g(c)$ at :2768 is a vertical bundle automorphism — a genuine gauge transformation of the same agent's frame, not a change of agent — under which $\kappa(A,A)$ changes value at a point. A quantity whose value at a point changes under a passive change of trivialization is not a tensor on the total space; a bona-fide metric on $E$ is a tensor on $E$. The manuscript's own admissions confirm this: $F_{\mu\nu}=0$ in Regime I supplies no Yang-Mills invariant, and "the genuinely gauge-invariant ontological content" is routed to a *different* construction (the consensus metric, :2769). Both routings concede that the object defined in this section is not a tensor on $E$. Blue's appeal to Kaluza-Klein section-dependence does not transfer, because the Kaluza-Klein object remains a tensor under the principal action and $\kappa(A,A)$ does not.

The red philosophy-of-science memo supplies the frame that ties this together: under this debate's binding source-of-truth rule [debate_methodology.md, "The source-of-truth rule": cite external standards, not the manuscript], "bona-fide" must be earned against [Nakahara2003 Ch. 10-11] / [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I], not asserted by stipulation at :2726/:2747. The canonical form, verified against the primary source, does not match. Red's opening falsification therefore stands against Blue's rebuttal: the claim is well-framed enough to adjudicate, its load-bearing first conjunct ("bona-fide bundle-metric pullback ... not merely an $L^2$ pullback") fails the canonical-form comparison, and its fourth conjunct (perceived space $= G^{(s)}$ "well-posed") is conceded by Blue to lack a discriminator. The narrowest blue position that survives — that the pullback-of-a-(0,2)-tensor mechanics and the Fisher block are textbook — is true but too narrow to earn the claim's wording. The red variational memo is cited above for sub-point 4; no red memo is discounted.
