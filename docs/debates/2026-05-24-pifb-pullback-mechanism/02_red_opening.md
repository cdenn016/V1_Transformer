# Red Opening — pifb-pullback-mechanism

## Steelman (opposing position)

A Riemannian metric on a manifold is any smooth positive-definite (0,2)-tensor; declaring the connection-induced horizontal and vertical subspaces orthogonal and assigning the vertical block the Fisher-Rao metric and the horizontal block $\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)$ (positive-definite for compact $\mathfrak{so}(N)$ under $\kappa=-\mathrm{tr}$) yields a well-defined positive-definite form on $E_q$, whose section pullback $G^{(q)}$ is then a genuine bundle-metric pullback carrying both an intended, disclosed agent-frame-dependence (:2768–2769) and the standard Fisher information of the fiber (:2759), with the four coexistent tiers and the $G^{(s)}$ identification offered honestly as an interpretive proposal.

## Position

The claim fails on its load-bearing conjunct. The horizontal block $g^{\mathrm{tw}}_{\mu\nu}=\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)$ (:2739) is not the horizontal block of any canonical fiber-bundle metric, so $g_{E_q}$ is not the "bona-fide bundle metric" the manuscript names it (:2726); the "$L^2$-only vs bundle-metric pullback" distinction collapses to "the same Fisher score-outer-product plus a gauge-frame-dependent additive term that is neither a tensor on the total space nor an information metric." The four formulas $G^{(q,p,s,r)}$ are well-defined as symbolic objects, but that is the cheap conjunct; the expensive one — that they are pullbacks of a bona-fide bundle metric in any sense stronger than the score $L^2$ pullback — is false. The tier-identification "perceived space $=G^{(s)}$" (:2795) is an interpretive preference with no falsification condition, supported by a citation read more strongly than its source allows; it is not "well-posed" in the sense the claim asserts.

## Evidence

**1. The horizontal block is non-canonical (geometer, gauge-theorist).** The canonical Kaluza–Klein / Sasaki bundle metric on the total space is $\hat g = \pi^* g + k\,\omega$: the horizontal block is the **pullback of a base metric** $g_{ab}(x)$, and the connection one-form enters **only the vertical/fiber block** as a covariantizing shift. The 5D KK line element is $ds^2 = g_{\mu\nu}dx^\mu dx^\nu + \phi^2(A_\nu dx^\nu + dx^5)^2$ — base metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ horizontal, connection $A$ inside the fiber shift, never a standalone horizontal block [Bundle metric, HandWiki; Kaluza–Klein theory, Wikipedia; Sasaki metric, Albuquerque 2018; standard treatments Nakahara2003 Ch. 10–11, KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II–III]. The manuscript substitutes the connection-norm-squared $\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)$ into the horizontal slot because it has no base metric on $\mathcal C$ to put there — the construction's stated purpose is to *manufacture* a base metric by pullback (:2784). That is circular: the canonical horizontal block requires a base metric as **input**; the manuscript wants it as **output**, and bridges the gap with an object that appears in no standard bundle metric. The cross-term vanishing (sub-point 3) is tautological, not a theorem: $g_{E_q}$ is *defined* block-diagonal in $H\oplus V$ (:2734), so orthogonality holds by fiat.

**2. $\kappa(A,A)$ is not a tensor on the total space (gauge-theorist).** "Connection-dependent" is not "gauge-noninvariant." The canonical KK metric is connection-dependent yet invariant under the principal action: "a metric on the total space invariant under the principal action reduces to a metric together with a gauge field on the base" [Kaluza–Klein theory, Wikipedia]. The manuscript's $\kappa(A,A)$ is admitted gauge-noninvariant (:2768): under the gauge transformation $U_i\to U_i g(c)$ — a vertical bundle automorphism of the *same* agent's frame — the connection transforms $A\to g^{-1}Ag+g^{-1}dg$ and $\kappa(A,A)$ acquires Maurer–Cartan cross terms. A quantity whose pointwise value changes under a passive re-trivialization is not a tensor on the total space; the curvature $F=dA+\tfrac12[A,A]$ is the tensorial object built from $A$, not $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ [Nakahara2003 Ch. 10–11; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II–III]. The manuscript concedes this twice over: $F_{\mu\nu}=0$ in Regime I gives no Yang–Mills invariant, and it routes "the genuinely gauge-invariant ontological content" to a *different* (consensus/Haar) construction (:2768). Both admissions concede the object in this section is not gauge-invariant, hence not a metric on $E$. The disclosed-frame-dependence steelman defends "different agents, different geometry"; it does not cover "same agent, arbitrary internal frame, different horizontal block."

**3. The sum is not an information metric (info-geometer).** Cencov's theorem: the Fisher metric is the unique Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold invariant under sufficient statistics, up to scale [Fisher information metric, Wikipedia, quoting Chentsov; Cencov1972; external_canon_math.md §1]. The vertical Fisher block earns this; the additive $\kappa(A,A)$ depends only on the frame $A^{(i)}=U_i^{-1}dU_i$, not on the statistical family $\{q_i(c)\}$ (the manuscript recovers the score-only metric by setting $A=0$, :2771, confirming $\kappa(A,A)$ is statistically inert). A "Fisher + frame-dependent term" sum fails sufficient-statistic invariance and so is not an information metric. The only information-bearing part of $G^{(q)}$ is the vertical block — identical in the "$L^2$-only" and "bundle-metric" readings. The upgrade adds no information geometry, only a frame-rotation form.

**4. The tier-identification is interpretive, not well-posed (variational, philosophy-of-science).** "Perceived space is most naturally identified with $G^{(s)}$ rather than $G^{(p)}$ or $G^{(q)}$" (:2795) is a preference among "coexistent" tensors (:2782) with no discriminator — no measurement distinguishes the three localizations, and the manuscript states the framework "does not adjudicate" (:2797). "Most naturally" is aesthetic, not testable [Popper 1959, demarcation by falsifiability]. The supporting [FristonEtAl2017] citation is over-read: the active-inference literature distributes structural organization across deep hierarchical generative models and treats parameter learning and state inference as complementary processes; it does not localize "perceived space" in the parameter-learning tier [FristonEtAl2017; ParrPezzuloFriston2022; Da Costa et al. 2020]. The honest Hoffman caveat (:2797) is credited but concerns attribution, not the falsifiability of the proposal.

**5. The naming is the claim, not evidence for it (philosophy-of-science).** The conjunct "bona-fide bundle metric ... not merely $L^2$" is settled in the manuscript by *stipulation* (:2726, :2747 call $g_{E_q}$ "bona-fide" and "fiber-respecting"), not by matching the canonical KK/Sasaki form. Under this debate's source-of-truth rule [debate_methodology.md], canonical status must be earned against Nakahara / Kobayashi–Nomizu, not asserted in the manuscript — and against that canon (point 1) it fails.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds:

- A standard reference exhibits a bundle metric whose **horizontal block is the trace form $\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)$** of the connection components rather than the pullback of a base metric, and calls that the canonical horizontal block. (Every canonical construction found — KK, Sasaki, Nakahara Ch. 11 — uses a pulled-back base metric horizontally and puts the connection in the fiber shift.)
- $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ for a non-tensorial connection $A$ is shown to be a **tensor on the total space** (gauge-invariant under $U_i\to U_i g(c)$), or that transformation is shown not to be a genuine gauge transformation. (The Maurer–Cartan term is the textbook signature of non-tensoriality.)
- $\kappa(A,A)$ is shown to **depend on the statistical family** $\{q_i(c)\}$, restoring Cencov-invariance of the sum. (It depends only on the frame $\phi_i$; the $A=0$ collapse at :2771 confirms statistical inertness.)
- The manuscript supplies a **discriminator** that makes "perceived space $=G^{(s)}$" falsifiable, or [FristonEtAl2017] is shown to localize perceptual spatial structure specifically in parameter learning.

All five experts are used: geometer and gauge-theorist carry the load-bearing canonical-form and non-tensoriality attacks (points 1–2); info-geometer establishes the Cencov failure (point 3); variational and philosophy-of-science carry the tier-identification and naming-circularity attacks (points 4–5). No memo is discounted.
