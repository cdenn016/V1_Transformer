# Expert Memo — Red / Gauge-Theorist — pifb-pullback-mechanism (opening)

## Steelman

The manuscript does not hide the gauge-noninvariance; it discloses it at :2768 and argues at :2769 that distinct gauge frames are *meant* to generate distinct pulled-back geometries — agent-frame-dependence is the intended physics, not a defect. On this reading the construction is "within an agent's gauge fixing" (:2732, :2769), invariance is deferred to a consensus/Haar-averaged metric in a later section, and the horizontal block $\kappa(A,A)$ is the legitimate frame-relative content of "how rapidly the agent's representational basis rotates" (:2784). A connection-dependent metric is unremarkable; the canonical Kaluza–Klein metric is also connection-dependent. So disclosed frame-dependence is not by itself disqualifying.

## Strongest falsification

"Connection-dependent" and "gauge-noninvariant" are not the same property, and the manuscript conflates them. The canonical Kaluza–Klein metric on the total space $P$ is connection-dependent (it uses $A$ to define the horizontal distribution) yet, as a tensor on $P$ built from an Ad-invariant fiber metric and the horizontal lift of a base metric, it is **invariant under the principal (gauge) action**: a change of local section/trivialization is a passive re-coordinatization that leaves the total-space tensor's intrinsic value unchanged. That is exactly the statement "a metric on the total space invariant under the principal action reduces to a metric plus a gauge field on the base."

The manuscript's $\kappa(A_\mu,A_\nu)$ fails this test, and it admits so (:2768): under $U_i\to U_i g(c)$ the connection transforms $A\to g^{-1}Ag + g^{-1}dg$, and $\kappa(A,A)$ picks up Maurer–Cartan cross terms $\kappa(A_\mu,\partial_\nu g\,g^{-1})$, $\kappa(g^{-1}\partial_\mu g, g^{-1}\partial_\nu g)$. The transformation $U_i\to U_i g(c)$ is a *vertical bundle automorphism* — a genuine gauge transformation of the same agent's frame, not a change of agent. So the non-invariance is not "different agents see different geometry" (which the steelman defends); it is "the same agent, re-gauging an arbitrary internal frame choice, gets a different horizontal block." A quantity whose value at a point changes under a passive change of trivialization is **not a tensor on the total space**. A bona-fide metric on $E$ is a tensor on $E$. Therefore $\kappa(A,A)$ is not part of a metric on $E$; it is a quadratic form attached to one chosen trivialization.

The manuscript's own escape hatches confirm this. It notes $F_{\mu\nu}=0$ in Regime I (pure gauge), so the Yang–Mills invariant $\mathrm{tr}(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$ vanishes and supplies no invariant content (:2768); and it routes "the genuinely gauge-invariant ontological content" to a *different* construction (consensus/Haar averaging) in another section. Both admissions concede that the object defined in *this* section is not gauge-invariant — i.e., not a tensor on $E$. The label "bona-fide bundle metric" (:2726) therefore overstates: it is a frame-fixed quadratic form, not a metric on the total space, and the contrast with "merely $L^2$" is not earned by the horizontal block.

## External citation

[Nakahara2003 Ch. 10–11]; [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.1, §III.1]: a connection one-form on a principal $G$-bundle satisfies $R_g^* A = \mathrm{Ad}(g^{-1})A$ and transforms between local sections as $A' = g^{-1}Ag + g^{-1}dg$ (the inhomogeneous Maurer–Cartan term). The curvature $F=dA+\tfrac12[A,A]$ is the tensorial (gauge-covariant) object; $A$ itself is *not* tensorial, and any scalar built quadratically from $A$ alone (such as $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$) inherits $A$'s inhomogeneous transformation and is therefore not gauge-invariant. [Wikipedia, Kaluza–Klein theory]: "a metric on the total space invariant under the principal action reduces to a metric together with a gauge field on the base" — the canonical KK total-space metric *is* invariant under the principal action, unlike $\kappa(A,A)$.

## Falsification condition

My falsification is wrong if (i) a standard reference shows that $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ for a non-tensorial connection $A$ is a tensor on the total space, or (ii) the transformation $U_i\to U_i g(c)$ is not a genuine gauge transformation (vertical automorphism) but only a relabeling that should leave intrinsic values fixed. Both fail: $A$'s Maurer–Cartan term is the textbook signature of non-tensoriality, and $U_i\to U_i g(c)$ is the defining form of a local gauge transformation [Nakahara2003 Ch. 10].

- Wikipedia, "Kaluza–Klein theory": the total-space metric is built so that "metrics $\hat g$ on the bundle $P$ ... consistent with both the fiber metric and the metric on $M$"; "an invariant metric is one invariant under [the principal action]"; the invariant total-space metric reduces to base metric + gauge field. The connection enters the fiber shift, not a horizontal trace form. URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaluza%E2%80%93Klein_theory
- HandWiki, "Bundle metric": the principal-bundle metric is $\pi^* g + k\omega$ with Ad($G$)-invariant fiber metric $k$; the construction's invariance rests on Ad-invariance of $k$, not on a quadratic form in $A$. URL: https://handwiki.org/wiki/Bundle_metric
