## False dichotomies / equivocations detected

1. **"bona-fide fiber-bundle metric" equivocates between two propositions.** Strong reading: matches canonical KK/Sasaki (connection in vertical block, base metric in horizontal). Canon-cop verified against the primary KK line element (`02b_canon_cop.md:16-22`) that the manuscript INVERTS this. Weak reading: any smooth positive-definite (0,2)-tensor on E (Lee2013 definition, `03_blue_rebuttal.md:25`). Red wins the strong reading; Blue wins the weak reading. The claim's single word "bona-fide" rides both. Blue's explicit redefinition at `02_blue_opening.md:13` ("means *any* Riemannian metric on E — not specifically the Kaluza-Klein recipe") is the diagnostic that the term is unanchored.

2. **Blue's internal inconsistency = answering two sub-claims under one umbrella.** `02_blue_opening.md:15` calls `κ(A,A)` "a principled novel substitution forced by the setting" (positive content, defending "bona-fide"); `02_blue_opening.md:40` calls the distinction "near-vacuous as a formula ... additive content exactly κ(A,A) and nothing more" (conceding triviality). Red's rebuttal (`03_red_rebuttal.md:17`) correctly identifies these cannot both carry the claim. They are answers to two DIFFERENT propositions — "is g_{E_q} a valid metric?" (yes) and "is the L²-vs-bundle distinction substantive?" (contested) — packed into one conjunct.

3. **Red's "manuscript-CLAIMS vs construction-delivers" equivocation is what Red is correctly attacking, not committing.** Red's point 5 (`02_red_opening.md:21`) targets the gap between `:2726`'s rhetorical naming ("does not earn the name," "bona-fide") and what the math delivers. This is a frame-level finding, not a Red equivocation. Canon-cop confirmed (`02b_canon_cop.md:68`) both sides use `:NNNN` for claim-identification, not as authority. Clean.

4. **"well-posed" equivocates on conjunct (4).** Blue reads it as "well-posed interpretive proposal" (defensible, `03_blue_rebuttal.md:29`); the claim phrases it as "well-posed (if interpretive) rather than an arbitrary choice," which Red reads as requiring a discriminator (`03_red_rebuttal.md:21`). Both sides agree there is no discriminator. The disagreement is purely about what "well-posed" means — definitional, not empirical.

## My verdict reasoning

The claim is a conjunction of four propositions, and the two sides win different conjuncts on frame-correct evidence, with two conjuncts conceded by both. Conjunct (1) strong reading (canonical KK/Sasaki match): Red wins, canon-cop verified, Blue conceded (`02_blue_opening.md:11`). Conjunct (1)/(3) weak reading (`g_{E_q}` is a valid Riemannian metric, `G^{(q)}` a valid pullback): Blue wins on the Lee2013 definition, Red does not dispute. Conjunct (4) (perceived space = `G^{(s)}` well-posed): both sides agree it is non-falsifiable and interpretive. The decisive frame fact is that the single load-bearing word — "bona-fide ... not merely L²" — is unanchored: it means one thing (canonical form) when Red attacks and a different thing (any Riemannian metric) when Blue defends, and Blue had to explicitly redefine it at `02_blue_opening.md:13` to mount its defense. A claim whose verdict flips on which of two definitions of its central term is in force is not a single answerable proposition; it is two crisp propositions with opposite verdicts welded to a third (contested, empirical) and a fourth (non-falsifiable, metaphysical). This is the canonical REMAND pattern: the most useful output is to decompose the sloppy conjunction into the sub-claims that each have a clean verdict, not to pick a winner of a debate where the two sides never disputed the same proposition. This is not OUT_OF_SCOPE — every sub-claim below IS answerable; it is the welding that is malformed, not the constituents. The manuscript itself has already disclosed every defect Red names (the "(in a Fixed Gauge)" subsection title at `:2724`, the gauge-noninvariance computation at `:2768`, the "tw not YM" label at `:2747`, the "does not adjudicate" caveat at `:2797`, the frozen-slow-channel admission at `:2799`); the live dispute is therefore not about the math, which is sound and honestly hedged, but about the rhetorical upgrade words at `:2726` ("does not earn the name," "bona-fide") and `:2784` ("a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback"), which assert more than the disclosed construction delivers. Blue conceded exactly this (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:9`: "the rhetorical upgrade at :2726 does not survive and should be rewritten").

## Decisive evidence

The frame-breaking observation: Blue's explicit redefinition at `02_blue_opening.md:13` — "A 'metric on the total space E' means *any* Riemannian metric on E — not specifically the Kaluza-Klein recipe" — set against canon-cop's primary-source verification (`02b_canon_cop.md:16-22`) that the canonical bundle metric puts the connection in the vertical block and a pulled-back base metric in the horizontal, which the manuscript inverts.
