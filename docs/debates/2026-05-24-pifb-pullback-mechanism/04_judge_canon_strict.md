## Reasoning

The compound claim is `A ∧ B ∧ C` and fails because two of three conjuncts fail against external canon. Conjunct A asserts a "bona-fide fiber-bundle metric" pullback that is "not merely an $L²$ pullback of scores"; the primary-source-verified Kaluza–Klein line element (`02b_canon_cop.md:18-22`) puts the connection in the vertical block and the pulled-back base metric horizontally, while the manuscript inverts this with a standalone `κ(A,A)` horizontal block — and Blue concedes both the inversion (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:7`) and that the "bona-fide / earns the name" framing is unearned and should be rewritten (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:9`), with the only additive content being `κ(A,A)` itself (`02_blue_opening.md:40`). Blue's Lee2013 defense — that any smooth positive-definite (0,2)-tensor is a Riemannian metric, so `g_{E_q}` is *a* metric and `G^{(q)}` *a* pullback — is verified canon and correct, but it defends the narrow true proposition ("$g_{E_q}$ is some Riemannian metric") rather than the claim's inflated proposition ("the genuine pullback of a *bona-fide bundle* metric … *not merely* $L²$"); under the source-of-truth rule the manuscript's "bona-fide / does not earn the name" stipulation (:2726, :2747) must be earned against Nakahara/Kobayashi–Nomizu and is not. Conjunct C asserts the tier-identification is "well-posed (if interpretive) rather than an arbitrary choice"; Blue concedes there is no discriminator and the slow channel is frozen so the identification is never exercised (`02_blue_opening.md:41`), which is the negation of "rather than arbitrary." Conjunct B (four tiers well-defined as symbolic objects) is uncontested but cannot rescue a conjunction whose other two parts fail. Red's own Cencov attack (point 3) is canon-refuted — the primary source confirms Chentsov's theorem governs only the statistical manifold (the fiber), not a pullback to the base — but this is non-load-bearing: Red's canonical-form contradiction and the conceded falsifiability gap carry the verdict without it.
