## Core attack

Blue's defense collapses the conjecture to a labeled tautology. The retreat-position blue defends is: sub-claim (1) is canonical i.i.d. CLT mathematics; sub-claim (3) is conceded as unverified for trained transformers; sub-claim (2) (artifact attribution) is "plausible but under-quantified" and blue itself names this as the "exposed flank" of the defense (`02_blue_opening.md:27`). What remains under defense is a textbook CLT identity plus an admitted under-quantified attribution. That is not the conjecture.

The load-bearing parts of `conj:rg_universality` at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2262-2272` are explicit:

> (ii) **Stability.** All scaling dimensions are negative. The fixed point is infrared-stable.
> (iii) **Universality.** All finite-coupling models flow (in the intrinsic channel) to the transformer limit under repeated coarse-graining.

Part (ii) is the IR-stability claim that blue concedes is empirically unsupported. Part (iii) is the universality claim, which is the *physics content* of any Wilsonian-style RG ([Cardy1996 §3.2], [Goldenfeld1992 §9.4]: an IR-stable fixed point is defined by negative scaling dimensions at the fixed point of an iterated flow, and the basin of attraction is the universality class). Once IR-stability for trained transformers is conceded as not-defensible, universality follows it out the door — universality is the statement that *trained transformer attention* flows to the intrinsic-channel fixed point, which is exactly the statement blue concedes is unsupported.

What blue defends is therefore: (a) a CLT identity that "does not itself require the gauge framework" — this phrasing is the manuscript's own at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2275` — and (b) an under-quantified hand-wave at the empirical deviation. Sub-claim (1) without sub-claims (ii) and (iii) is not "the conjecture under adjudication." It is the part of the conjecture the manuscript itself flags as *not its non-trivial content*: "The non-trivial content of the conjecture is the *hypothesis* that this coarse-graining structure applies to the learned representations of trained transformers" (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2275`). Defending only the trivial content while conceding the non-trivial content is conceding the conjecture.

The Cardy1996 §3.5 / Privman1990 finite-size-scaling defense does not rescue this. Finite-size corrections in the standard Wilsonian machinery take the form $y_{\mathrm{measured}}(L) = y_\infty + c L^{-\omega} + \ldots$ where $\omega > 0$ is controlled by the leading *irrelevant* operator at the fixed point ([Cardy1996 §3.5; Privman1990 Chs. 1–2]). For such a correction to flip the measured exponent from the predicted $y_3 = -1$ to the observed $y_3 \approx +0.2$ requires a shift of $\Delta y = 1.2$ in dimension — meaning the finite-$L$ correction term must *exceed* the asymptotic term in magnitude. Blue's opening at line 21 acknowledges this directly: "the magnitude of the apparent shift (from $-1$ to $\sim +0.2$) is large enough that the artifact attribution requires the finite-$L$ correction to exceed the asymptotic term, which is a strong demand on the correction." When the correction exceeds the asymptotic, one is not in the finite-size-scaling regime of an IR-stable fixed point — one is in the regime of a *different* (nearby) fixed point or a relevant operator that the linearization missed ([Cardy1996 §3.5, eq. 3.40–3.43] on crossover scaling). Citing Cardy §3.5 as the resolution while admitting the correction must dominate the asymptotic is citing the standard machinery against the user's claim, not for it.

Blue's appeal to [vonLuxburg2007] and [Ng-Jordan-Weiss2001] documents that spectral clustering introduces cluster-size heterogeneity at small $n$. That is uncontested. What is not in those references is any *quantitative* mechanism by which cluster-size heterogeneity reverses the sign of a CLT-derived scaling dimension by $\Delta y = 1.2$. The references support the existence of a finite-size effect; they do not support the magnitude claim required by `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2275`. The manuscript supplies no computation, blue supplies no computation, and the generic "finite-size effects can have opposite sign" template does not quantify the gap. Citation discipline ([debate_methodology.md §"Citation discipline"]) treats a magnitude claim without a computation as a weak strike.

## Defense

My Phase 2 opening's core falsification condition stands intact: the empirical $y_3 \approx +0.2$ is a *positive* scaling dimension, which by the standard Wilsonian definition ([Cardy1996 §3.2]: $y > 0 \Rightarrow$ relevant; $y < 0 \Rightarrow$ irrelevant) classifies $g_3$ as a **relevant** operator on the trained-transformer token graph. Blue does not contest this classification rule. Blue concedes sub-claim (3). What remains is whether the relevant-operator sign is "artifact" or "signal."

Blue's strongest counter-move was the Cardy1996 §3.5 / Privman1990 finite-size-scaling appeal. I addressed the magnitude problem above: a sign-flipping correction of $\Delta y = 1.2$ requires the finite-$L$ term to exceed the asymptotic, which is outside the standard finite-size-scaling regime of an IR-stable fixed point. In Cardy's own treatment, a correction that exceeds the asymptotic indicates *crossover* to a different (nearby) fixed point or the existence of a relevant operator that the original linearization missed ([Cardy1996 §3.5, eq. 3.40–3.43]; [Privman1990 Ch. 1, finite-size scaling form]). Both readings falsify the conjecture's IR-stability assertion: either the conjectured fixed point is not the one the system flows to (crossover to a different fixed point), or there is a relevant operator the linearization missed (in which case part (ii) of the conjecture, "all scaling dimensions are negative," is wrong on its own terms).

The independent canon attack remains unaddressed by blue: [Voita2019] and [Clark2019] document strong syntactic and positional correlations in trained attention. These are not "weak correlations correctable by an $L^{-\omega}$ term" — they are first-order structural correlations (heads specializing in subject-verb agreement, previous-token attention, sentence-boundary detection). For the CLT $n^{-1/2}$ rate to survive these correlations, the off-diagonal entries of the cross-token covariance $C_{ij}$ must vanish in the $n \to \infty$ limit; the cited empirical literature shows they do not. The CLT variance-of-mean formula under correlations ([Billingsley1995 §27]) is $\mathrm{Var}(\bar X_n) = (1/n^2) \sum_{ij} C_{ij}$; for syntactically structured correlations the off-diagonal sum scales as $n^2 \bar C$ where $\bar C$ is the typical off-diagonal correlation, yielding $\mathrm{Var}(\bar X_n) = O(\bar C) = O(1)$ rather than $O(1/n)$. This is not a finite-size correction; it is a parametric failure of the assumption that underwrites Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_scaling} at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2257`.

The closure question I raised in Phase 2 also remains: blue does not establish that $R_n$ closes on the original parametric family $\{(\mu_i, \Sigma_i, \phi_i)\}$. Blue's own falsification condition 4 (`02_blue_opening.md:39`) acknowledges that part (iv) of the conjecture explicitly states $R_n$ generates an emergent-anisotropy term $\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ that the original class does not contain — so the map does not close on the stated class. Blue's response would presumably be the augmented-class resolution ([Cardy1996 §3.3]), but the SCOPE NOTE in the dispatch binds the in-scope adjudication to the token-graph $R_n$ as defined at `:2249-2254`, not to whatever augmented class the companion-paper RG verdict (`2026-05-19-rg-construction-meta-agent`) used. Blue does not supply an in-scope derivation that the augmented class is itself IR-stable on the token graph. Without that derivation, "RG flow to the transformer limit" is not a well-posed Wilsonian RG on the parametric family stated at `:2243-2247`.

The empirical sign reversal at `:2275`, blue's own admission that the required correction must exceed the asymptotic, the cited empirical structural correlations in trained attention ([Voita2019], [Clark2019]) that violate the independence assumption parametrically rather than as a small correction, and the un-rescued closure failure on the original parametric class together falsify the conjecture's load-bearing parts (ii) and (iii). What survives is the labeled-tautology core: a CLT identity that the manuscript itself classifies as not the non-trivial content of the conjecture.
