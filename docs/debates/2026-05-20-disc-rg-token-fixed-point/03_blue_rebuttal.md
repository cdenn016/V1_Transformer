# Blue Rebuttal — disc-rg-token-fixed-point

## Concession

I concede two points outright; both are required by the evidence and the manuscript's own text.

First, red's argument in section 2 against sub-claim 3 lands. The CLT scaling at `GL(K)_attention.tex:2255-2260` is conditioned on independence of $\Delta_i$ and $\delta\Omega_{ij}$ across tokens, and the standard correlated-mean identity $\mathrm{Var}(\bar{X}_n) = n^{-2}\sum_{ij} C_{ij}$ [Billingsley1995 §27] makes this conditioning load-bearing: under a uniform off-diagonal correlation the variance does not contract at the $n^{-1/2}$ rate. The trained-transformer evidence red invokes — Voita et al. (2019) on head specialization and Clark et al. (2019) on BERT attention structure — documents exactly the structured, cross-token-correlated attention statistics that defeat the i.i.d. premise. Sub-claim 3 as written ("part (ii) holds for trained transformers") is therefore not licensed by §8.4 alone. The manuscript itself acknowledges this at `:2237` ("we conjecture---but do not claim to have validated on trained models") and at `:2255` ("an assumption that holds by construction but requires verification in trained models"). The defensible form of sub-claim 3 is **conditional**: part (ii) holds at the intrinsic-channel origin *under the stated independence assumption* (Sub-claim 1), and the empirical question for trained transformers is open. Red is correct that the unconditional form is unsupported.

Second, I concede red's section 1 attack on the empirical attribution as stated. The manuscript at `:2275` calls the $y_3 \approx +0.2$ deviation a clustering artifact without a quantitative finite-size correction of the form $y_3^{\mathrm{meas}}(n) = -1 + c(n) + \dots$ that the standard practice in finite-size scaling requires [Cardy1996 §3.5, Ch. 4]. A sign reversal is not a typical $1/n$ correction. Sub-claim 2 — the **predominant-artifact attribution** — is not derivable from §8.4 in its current form. The honest reading of `:2275` is "the deviation is unexplained on the current evidence and trained-model validation is required," which the closing clause of `:2275` essentially says. The manuscript should be edited to drop the unsupported "artifact rather than failure" attribution and replace it with a flag that the deviation is unexplained and that the artifact hypothesis is one of two open candidates.

## Core attack

Red's section 3 attack — that $R_n$ fails Wilsonian closure because $\Sigma_A = (1/|A|)\sum_i \Sigma_i + \mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ generates a structurally new moment — overstates what the manuscript hides. The closure-breaking term is **named in the conjecture itself**: `GL(K)_attention.tex:2247` defines $g_1^{(\mathrm{emer})} = \|\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)\|/\sigma^2$ and splits the anisotropy into intrinsic and emergent channels, and part (iv) of `conj:rg_universality` at `:2269` states verbatim that "$R_n$ generates anisotropy via $\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ even at $g_1^{(\mathrm{orig})} = 0$, so the total $g_1^{(\mathrm{tot})}$ does not flow to zero." The construction is not silent about the extra moment; it elevates that moment to a distinct coupling and confines the IR-stability claim of part (ii) to the **intrinsic channel** (part (i) is stated for the intrinsic-channel fixed point at `:2266`: "$g_1^{(\mathrm{orig}),*} = g_2^* = g_3^* = 0$ is a fixed point of $R_n$ in the intrinsic-channel coordinates"). This is precisely the augmented-class pattern that the canon authorizes: effective actions under block-spin RG generically acquire new operators, and the standard remedy is to enlarge the parametric family to track them [Cardy1996 §3.3 on effective-action generation of new operators under RG; Goldenfeld1992 §9.3 on the parametric family closure step]. The augmented family $(\mu_i, \Sigma_i, \phi_i; g_1^{(\mathrm{emer})})$ — with the emergent component as an additional coordinate — is therefore the natural closure of $R_n$, and the conjecture is explicit that part (ii) does not extend to $g_1^{(\mathrm{emer})}$. Red's framing that closure is "undemonstrated" elides that closure is **declared open in part (iv)** and that the IR-stability claim is restricted to the intrinsic submanifold.

The CLT-versus-fixed-point-linearization complaint in section 3 is a separate issue, and on that point red is partially correct: §8.4 computes single-step coarse-graining rates, not eigenvalues of a linearized iterated map at a fixed point [Wilson1971 §III]. But the intrinsic-channel origin $g_1^{(\mathrm{orig})} = g_2 = g_3 = 0$ is by construction a fixed point of $R_n$ (zero in, zero out under arithmetic averaging and the cocycle structure), and linearization at the origin of a map that is a sample-mean operation reproduces the CLT rate on the perturbation moments. For the **intrinsic channel under independence**, the CLT rate **is** the linearized-flow rate; the two coincide because the map is linear in the moments at leading order. Red's distinction lands as a critique of the prose framing, not of the mathematics of sub-claim 1.

## Defense

The defensible content of the conjecture survives the concessions, in a strictly narrower form than the manuscript states.

**Sub-claim 1 (mathematical CLT result) stands as cited.** The exponents $y_1 = -1/2$, $y_2 = -1$, $y_3 = -1$ follow from the variance of the sample mean of i.i.d. zero-mean perturbations [Billingsley1995 §27] applied to the moments at `:2243-2245`. The manuscript itself at `:2275` reports "the CLT exponents are exact (to three significant figures) under direct averaging of synthetic i.i.d. perturbations." Red grants sub-claim 1 explicitly in section 2 of the opening. No further defense is needed.

**Part (ii) of the conjecture, restricted to the intrinsic submanifold and under the stated independence assumption, is the textbook IR-stable fixed point.** Three negative scaling dimensions classify three irrelevant operators; an IR-stable fixed point is defined as one at which all couplings in a neighborhood have $y_g < 0$ [Cardy1996 §3.2; Goldenfeld1992 §9.4]. The intrinsic-channel origin meets this definition by Sub-claim 1, which red grants. The conjecture's load-bearing claim is the **conditional**: *if* the independence assumption holds, *then* the intrinsic-channel origin is IR-stable. Red has not contested the conditional; red has contested only its application to trained transformers, which the manuscript at `:2237` and `:2255` already flags as unverified.

**Closure via augmented class is in-scope canon support, not external import.** The Cardy1996 §3.3 treatment of effective-action generation of new operators is part of the standard Wilsonian-RG playbook for any block-spin construction; the parametric family is enlarged whenever the coarse-graining generates moments the original family did not track [Goldenfeld1992 §9.3]. Part (iv) of `conj:rg_universality` at `:2269` performs exactly this enlargement by introducing $g_1^{(\mathrm{emer})}$ and confining the IR-stability claim of part (ii) to the intrinsic channel at `:2266`. Red's section 3 attack reads as "the manuscript has not closed $R_n$ on the original family," which is correct but uninteresting — the manuscript has not closed $R_n$ on the original family because it has elevated the obstruction to a named coupling. This is the correct move under canon. The remaining defensible reformulation: "$R_n$ is RG-on-the-augmented-class, with the intrinsic-channel submanifold IR-stable under independence; the emergent-channel growth is the standard effective-action correction that LayerNorm projects back toward the isotropic submanifold (`:2277`)." That is a narrower claim than the manuscript writes, and it survives.

**The empirical $y_3 \approx +0.2$ measurement does not falsify part (ii) of the conjecture in its conditional form; it falsifies the unconditional-trained-transformer reading.** Red is right that the manuscript's "artifact" attribution is undefended; I conceded that above. But the alternative — that $y_3 > 0$ on trained transformers is a genuine relevant-operator signature — is also undefended by §8.4. Two hypotheses remain open: (H1) finite-size clustering bias [von Luxburg2007 §5 on graph-Laplacian eigenvalue bias documents systematic spectral-statistic biases that survive at moderate cluster counts; this is the candidate the manuscript invokes without quantitative analysis], and (H2) genuine cross-token correlation in trained attention statistics generating an $O(1)$ floor [Billingsley1995 §27 correlated-mean identity, Voita2019, Clark2019]. The empirical evidence does not yet discriminate. The honest manuscript edit is to flag both hypotheses and call the question open, not to commit to (H1) without finite-size analysis. The conditional theoretical content of parts (i)–(iii) is unaffected.

## Falsification reaffirmed

The conditional claim ("under independence of $\Delta_i, \delta\Omega_{ij}$ across tokens, the intrinsic-channel origin is IR-stable in the augmented-class submanifold sense") is falsifiable on the standard canon: a single positive scaling dimension at the origin in the linearization of $R_n$ on $g_1^{(\mathrm{orig})}, g_2, g_3$ under i.i.d. averaging would break it [Cardy1996 §3.2]. No such positive dimension is derivable from CLT arithmetic. The unconditional claim about trained transformers is the empirically open question; on this I have conceded that the available evidence — including the manuscript's own $y_3 \approx +0.2$ at `:2275` — does not support an unconditional answer in either direction. Sub-claim 2 (the predominant-artifact attribution) I concede is not supported as stated and recommend a manuscript edit.
