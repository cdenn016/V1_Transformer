# Verdict — disc-rg-token-fixed-point

## Outcome

RED_WINS

## Decisive evidence

The standard Wilsonian classification rule, applied to the manuscript's own admission. [Cardy1996 §3.2; Goldenfeld1992 §9.4] define an IR-stable fixed point as one at which every coupling in a neighborhood has scaling dimension $y_g < 0$; a single $y_g > 0$ classifies that coupling as relevant and defeats IR-stability along that direction. The manuscript's own measurement at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2275` reports $y_3 \approx +0.2$ on the spectral-clustering test, a positive scaling dimension. The manuscript at that same line states verbatim that "the non-trivial content of the conjecture is the hypothesis that this coarse-graining structure applies to the learned representations of trained transformers" — which is exactly the content blue concedes is not defensible on the available evidence (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:7`: "Sub-claim 3 as written... is therefore not licensed by §8.4 alone"). Blue's finite-size-scaling defense via [Cardy1996 §3.5] is foreclosed by blue's own admission at `02_blue_opening.md:21` that closing the $-1 \to +0.2$ gap "requires the finite-$L$ correction to exceed the asymptotic term," which by [Cardy1996 §3.5, eq. 3.40–3.43] places the data in the crossover-to-a-different-fixed-point regime, not in the finite-size correction regime of an IR-stable fixed point.

## Reasoning

The claim under adjudication is `conj:rg_universality` parts (i)–(v) together with the spectral-clustering attribution at `:2275`. Three sub-claims partition the load. Sub-claim 1 (CLT exponents under i.i.d. averaging) is canonical mathematics ([Billingsley1995 §27]) and uncontested by either side. Sub-claim 3 (IR-stability for trained transformers) is the manuscript-declared "non-trivial content" of the conjecture at `:2275`; blue concedes this in its unconditional form (`02_blue_opening.md:11`; `03_blue_rebuttal.md:7`), citing the manuscript's own hedges at `:2237` and `:2255` and the trained-attention literature ([Voita2019], [Clark2019]) documenting structural cross-token correlations that break the independence premise underwriting Eq. (rg_scaling). Sub-claim 2 (the artifact attribution at `:2275`) is also conceded: blue states at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:9` that "Sub-claim 2 — the predominant-artifact attribution — is not derivable from §8.4 in its current form" and recommends an edit dropping the "artifact rather than failure" attribution.

What blue defends is the conditional form: if the independence assumption at `:2255` holds, then the intrinsic-channel origin has three negative scaling dimensions and meets the textbook IR-stable-fixed-point definition. That conditional is canonically true but it is the CLT identity itself — sub-claim 1 — under a new label. The manuscript's framing at `:2275` is binding here: the trivial content of the conjecture is the CLT mathematics; the non-trivial content is the application to trained transformers. Blue's calibration defends the trivial content and concedes the non-trivial content. Per the dispatch's decisive question, this is not "enough substantive content to count as defending the conjecture"; it is the labeled-tautology reduction red anticipated.

Red's [Cardy1996 §3.2] application to the $y_3 \approx +0.2$ measurement is uncontested by blue on the classification rule itself. Blue's only line of defense against the sign-flip is the finite-size-scaling appeal to [Cardy1996 §3.5], and blue itself admits the correction would need to exceed the asymptotic — which in Cardy's own treatment indicates crossover or a missed relevant operator, both of which falsify part (ii) on its own terms. Red's closure attack also lands in narrower form: blue's reply correctly notes that part (iv) of the conjecture at `:2269` names the closure-breaking $\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ moment and invokes the [Cardy1996 §3.3] augmented-class pattern. The SCOPE NOTE allows the §3.3 citation as in-scope and the dispatch instructs the judge that part (iv) treats this as emergent-channel rather than as a strict closure violation. The closure question is therefore not where this debate is decided; the decision falls on parts (ii) and (iii) where blue has conceded.

The verdict is not "the conjecture is wrong as i.i.d. mathematics." It is "the conjecture's substantive trained-transformer content is not defended on the evidence, by blue's own concession, and the canon directly applied to the manuscript's own measurement classifies $g_3$ as a relevant operator on the empirical probe the manuscript actually ran." Blue has stated the honest, narrower form that survives — the conditional IR-stable claim on the intrinsic-channel submanifold under independence — and red has explicitly acknowledged that this narrower form is mathematically correct. The dispute is over whether that narrower form *is* the conjecture, and the manuscript's own scope language at `:2275` answers no.

## Action

The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2234–2277` should be revised in three coordinated edits, all of which blue concedes are warranted by the debate record.

First, the artifact attribution at `:2275` ("These deviations arise from clustering artifacts... rather than a failure of the underlying scaling argument") should be replaced with the two-hypothesis open statement blue lays out at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:27` (H1 finite-size clustering bias; H2 genuine cross-token correlation generating an $O(1)$ floor under the [Billingsley1995 §27] correlated-mean identity), with the explicit note that the manuscript does not currently discriminate between them and that a quantitative finite-size correction $y_3^{\mathrm{meas}}(n) = -1 + cL^{-\omega} + \ldots$ would be required to support H1.

Second, the conjecture statement at `:2262-2272` should be split into the conditional theorem (parts i and ii under the independence assumption, which is canonically true) and the empirical conjecture (the application to trained transformers, which is the open question). The hedge at `:2237` and `:2255` should be promoted into the conjecture statement itself rather than left as section-level commentary.

Third, the closing paragraph at `:2277` claiming that "the ordering of the three limits in Section~\ref{sec:transformer_limit} is dictated by the RG hierarchy" should be tempered to acknowledge that §5's ordering was algebraically determined upstream of §8.4 and is at best consistent with — not derived from — the RG hierarchy.

No follow-up debate is needed on the in-scope claim. The closure question on the augmented family and the empirical question of whether $y_3 \approx +0.2$ is signal or artifact are both legitimate open questions but require empirical work (trained-transformer measurements with controlled independence proxies; quantitative finite-size analysis of the spectral-clustering map) rather than further adversarial adjudication of §8.4 as currently written.
