# Blue Opening — disc-rg-token-fixed-point

## Steelman (opposing position)

Red's strongest attack: the empirical $y_3 \approx +0.2$ reported at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2275` is a *positive* scaling dimension — the operator $g_3$ is **relevant**, not irrelevant — and the manuscript's blanket attribution to "clustering artifacts" supplies no quantitative finite-size correction, while [Voita2019] and [Clark2019] independently document strong cross-token correlations in trained attention that *directly violate* the independence assumption at `:2255` on which the CLT scaling argument depends; therefore sub-claim (3) (IR-stability for trained transformers) is not merely unsupported but actively contradicted by the only empirical probe the manuscript actually ran, and the predicted IR-stability is at best i.i.d. mathematics with no demonstrated bearing on real transformers.

## Position

The defensible reading of Conjecture~\ref{conj:rg_universality} is **conditional and labeled**: sub-claim (1) (CLT exponents $y_1=-1/2$, $y_2=y_3=-1$) is exact canonical mathematics under the independence assumption that the manuscript names explicitly at `:2255`; sub-claim (2) (artifact attribution) is plausible but under-quantified, supported by the established direction-of-sign behavior of finite-size corrections under correlated coarse-graining variables [Cardy1996 §3.5; Privman1990]; sub-claim (3) (IR-stability for trained transformers) is *not* defensible as a present empirical fact and the manuscript itself does not claim it as one — the line at `:2237` reads "we conjecture---but do not claim to have validated on trained models---that the standard transformer occupies a stable infrared fixed point of this flow." The claim under adjudication is a conjecture-with-disclosed-hedges, not a theorem, and the defense is that the conjecture is *internally consistent under its stated independence assumption* and that the empirical deviation is *flagged as requiring future validation by the manuscript itself*, which is the appropriate scholarly posture.

I concede sub-claim (3) as an *unconditional* statement about trained transformers — that is not a defensible reading of the manuscript and red is right that on the empirical evidence available the independence assumption does not hold. I defend sub-claims (1) and (2) in their hedged form.

## Evidence

- **Sub-claim 1 is canonical CLT and unobjectionable.** For i.i.d. zero-mean $X_i$ with finite variance $\sigma^2$, $\mathrm{Var}(\bar X_n) = \sigma^2/n$ and $\sqrt{n}(\bar X_n)\Rightarrow\mathcal N(0,\sigma^2)$ [Billingsley1995 §27]. The map $\Sigma_A = (1/|A|)\sum_i \Sigma_i + \mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2252` and the resulting $n^{-1/2}$ contraction of $\|\Sigma_i-\sigma^2 I\|$ at `:2257` is the operator norm of a sample-mean deviation, which contracts at the CLT rate when the summands are independent — this is textbook (Durrett2010 §7.1). For $g_2 = \|\Omega_{ij}-\Omega\|/\|\Omega\|$ and $g_3 = \|H_{ijk}-I\|$ the averaging is over $n^2$ and $n^3$ pairs/triples; the corresponding variance reduction $n^{-2}$ on the variance, hence $n^{-1}$ on the linear norm, is the standard CLT scaling. The $y_3^{(\mathrm{action})}=-2$ remark at `:2260` is the squared-norm version, which is what the action-norm interpretation requires.

- **The independence assumption is named explicitly.** `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2255`: "Under the assumption that perturbations $\Delta_i$ and transport variations $\delta\Omega_{ij}$ are approximately independent across tokens---an assumption that holds by construction but requires verification in trained models---the central limit theorem yields...". The CLT exponents are derived *conditionally on* this assumption; attacking the unconditional statement attacks a stronger claim than the manuscript makes.

- **The conjecture is hedged at the section level.** `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2237`: "we conjecture---but do not claim to have validated on trained models---that the standard transformer occupies a stable infrared fixed point of this flow." The disclaimer is *part of* the claim under adjudication; it does not have to be defended as a verified theorem.

- **Finite-size scaling corrections can carry opposite sign to the asymptotic scaling dimension.** [Cardy1996 §3.5] develops the standard machinery of finite-size scaling in Wilsonian RG: at finite block size $L$, the apparent scaling exponent extracted from data is the asymptotic $y$ plus correction terms $L^{-\omega}$ controlled by the leading irrelevant operator. When the correction term is large and correlations between block degrees of freedom are non-negligible — exactly the regime of spectral clustering on small attention graphs ($n\sim 10$–$20$) with unequal cluster sizes — the leading finite-$L$ correction can dominate the asymptotic $y$, and in the presence of a competing relevant operator near a nearby fixed point the apparent exponent can flip sign. This makes the manuscript's qualitative attribution at `:2275` (clustering artifacts dominate at finite $n$) consistent in direction with standard finite-size-scaling phenomenology, even though the manuscript does not supply the quantitative correction or identify which competing operator the correction would couple to — and the magnitude of the apparent shift (from $-1$ to $\sim +0.2$) is large enough that the artifact attribution requires the finite-$L$ correction to exceed the asymptotic term, which is a strong demand on the correction.

- **Spectral clustering on small graphs is documented to bias eigenvalue-derived statistics.** [vonLuxburg2007] (tutorial on spectral clustering) and [Ng-Jordan-Weiss2001] both document that cluster sizes are *not* equalized by Laplacian-eigenvector clustering, that the resulting block-summed statistics depend on cluster-size distribution, and that for $n\lesssim 20$ tokens per graph the variance of within-cluster averages is dominated by cluster-size heterogeneity rather than the asymptotic CLT rate. This is canon for why naïve sample-mean exponents extracted from spectral clustering at small $n$ should not be taken as the asymptotic scaling dimension.

- **The synthetic-i.i.d. verification at `:2274` is a genuine consistency check on sub-claim (1).** The manuscript reports that "the CLT exponents are exact (to three significant figures) under direct averaging of synthetic i.i.d. perturbations." This is verification that the *mathematical content* of sub-claim (1) is correct: when the assumption holds, the exponents are recovered to numerical precision. The disagreement on the spectral-clustering test is then attributable to *violation of the assumption*, not to error in the derivation.

- **Sub-claim (2) is plausible but under-quantified — and I name this as the defense's exposed flank.** The manuscript asserts the artifact attribution but does not compute the finite-size correction $L^{-\omega}$ for the spectral-clustering map, so the artifact claim rests on direction-of-sign plausibility plus the synthetic-i.i.d. consistency check. A quantitative finite-size analysis (compute the leading correction from the cluster-size distribution; refit the exponent against $L^{-\omega}$ corrections) would close this gap. Until that analysis exists, sub-claim (2) is honestly labeled as a plausible-but-unverified attribution, not as an established quantitative result.

## Falsification conditions

The defense above is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **Sub-claim (1) fails** — the CLT exponents in Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_scaling} at `:2257` are *not* recovered to three significant figures under direct synthetic-i.i.d. averaging. If a reproduction of the synthetic test at `:2274` yielded materially different exponents from $y_1=-1/2$, $y_2=y_3=-1$, the mathematical basis of the conjecture would be broken regardless of artifact arguments. (The manuscript reports the contrary; falsification requires reproducing the test and finding a different result.)

2. **The independence assumption can be empirically *enforced* on a trained transformer attention graph and the measured exponents still disagree from the predictions.** If one computed coarse-graining on attention-graph partitions chosen so that within-block tokens have empirically uncorrelated $\Delta_i$ and $\delta\Omega_{ij}$ (e.g., randomly assigned blocks, or blocks chosen by an independence proxy), and the measured $y_3$ still came out positive, then the artifact attribution would fail and the conjecture would be falsified for trained transformers within its own stated scope.

3. **A quantitative finite-size analysis gives a correction of the wrong sign or wrong magnitude.** If one computes the leading $L^{-\omega}$ correction from the spectral-clustering cluster-size distribution at the reported $n$ and finds that the correction is small or has the same sign as $y_3$, then the artifact attribution at `:2275` is quantitatively wrong, and the measured $y_3\approx +0.2$ is signal not noise — a relevant operator, and the IR-stability claim fails along the holonomy direction.

4. **The closure question forces a parametric-class expansion that itself fails to be IR-stable.** Sub-claim (iv) of the conjecture at `:2269` admits that $\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ is generated by $R_n$ even when $g_1^{(\mathrm{orig})}=0$, so the original parametric class $\{(\mu_i, \Sigma_i, \phi_i)\}$ is not strictly preserved. If the natural augmented class (including the emergent-anisotropy term) is itself not closed or has additional relevant operators, then "RG flow to the transformer limit" is not a well-posed Wilsonian RG and the conjecture's labeling as an RG flow [Cardy1996 §3.3] fails.

5. **Sub-claim (3) is intended unconditionally.** If the judge reads the manuscript at `:2237` and `:2267` as claiming IR-stability for trained transformers *unconditionally* (not as a hedged conjecture pending validation), then sub-claim (3) is not defensible on the empirical evidence and I concede. The defense above presupposes the conditional reading of the conjecture; the empirical attacks of [Voita2019] (head specialization) and [Clark2019] (BERT attention patterns) document strong cross-token correlations that violate the independence assumption at `:2255` and therefore the unconditional reading is empirically unsupported.

The honest scope of this defense: sub-claim (1) is exact canon math, sub-claim (2) is plausible but under-quantified and stands or falls on whether the manuscript or a future analysis supplies the finite-size correction, and sub-claim (3) is defensible only in its conditional ("if independence holds...") form, not as a present empirical fact about trained transformers.
