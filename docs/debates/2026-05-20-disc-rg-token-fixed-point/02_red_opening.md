# Red Opening — disc-rg-token-fixed-point

## Steelman (opposing position)

Under the explicit i.i.d.-perturbation assumption stated at `GL(K)_attention.tex:2255`, the coarse-graining map $R_n$ defined at `:2249-2254` acts as a sample mean over $n$ tokens, so elementary CLT arithmetic [Billingsley1995 §27] gives $g_1^{(\mathrm{orig})}, g_2, g_3$ the scaling exponents $-1/2, -1, -1$; every operator is negative-dimension and so irrelevant by the textbook definition [Cardy1996 §3.2], the intrinsic-channel fixed point at the origin is IR-stable by construction, and the spectral-clustering deviation $y_3 \approx +0.2$ at `:2275` is a finite-cluster sampling artifact whose sign reversal traces to unequal $|A|$ and correlated assignments rather than to any failure of the underlying averaging law.

## Position

The conjecture's part (ii) (IR-stability of the intrinsic-channel fixed point as a claim about *trained transformers* — sub-claim 3 in `00_claim.md`) is not supported by the evidence in §8.4. Two independent canon-grounded faults are load-bearing: (a) the manuscript's own sign-flipped empirical measurement $y_3 \approx +0.2$ at `:2275` is, by the textbook definition, evidence that $g_3$ is **relevant**, not irrelevant, and the manuscript's blanket attribution to "clustering artifacts" is undefended by any quantitative finite-size analysis; (b) the independence premise underwriting the CLT exponents at `:2255` is empirically false for trained-transformer attention statistics, so the $-1/2, -1, -1$ exponents do not predict behavior on the very objects the conjecture claims to describe. The construction is also missing the Wilsonian closure step that would license calling $R_n$ an RG flow at all.

## Evidence

**1. Sign-flip is the manuscript's own admission, and the canon makes the sign decisive.**

`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2274-2275` states verbatim that spectral-clustering measurement gives $y_3 \approx +0.2$ against a prediction of $y_3 = -1$ for the linear holonomy norm. The classification of scaling dimensions is unambiguous in the canon: a coupling $g$ near a fixed point transforms as $g' = b^{y_g} g + O(g^2)$, and $y_g > 0$ makes the operator **relevant** — it grows under coarse-graining and the flow drives the system *away* from the candidate fixed point along that direction [Cardy1996 §3.2; Goldenfeld1992 §9.4; Wilson1971 §III]. An IR-stable fixed point is defined as one at which all couplings in a neighborhood have $y_g < 0$ [Cardy1996 §3.2]. A single positive scaling dimension at the candidate point is sufficient to defeat IR-stability along that coupling direction. The measured $y_3 \approx +0.2$ therefore directly contradicts part (ii) of `conj:rg_universality` as a claim about the objects on which the measurement was performed.

The manuscript's defense is the parenthetical at `:2275`: "These deviations arise from clustering artifacts (unequal cluster sizes, correlated assignments) rather than a failure of the underlying scaling argument." This is an assertion, not a derivation. The standard practice for handling such deviations is finite-size scaling: compute the leading finite-$n$ correction analytically and check whether the residual after correction is consistent with the asymptotic exponent [Cardy1996 §3.5, Ch. 4]. The manuscript supplies no such correction. A sign reversal — not merely a magnitude shift — is a stronger anomaly than finite-size theory typically absorbs: under leading $1/n$ corrections one expects $y_3^{\mathrm{meas}} = y_3 + c/n + \dots$, not $y_3^{\mathrm{meas}} = -y_3 \cdot 0.2$. Naming the deviation "artifact" without quantitative correction is unsupported attribution.

**2. The independence premise fails for trained-transformer attention.**

The CLT scaling at `:2255-2260` is conditioned at `:2255` on "perturbations $\Delta_i$ and transport variations $\delta\Omega_{ij}$ are approximately independent across tokens." For correlated variables, the variance of the sample mean is $\mathrm{Var}(\bar{X}_n) = n^{-2} \sum_{ij} C_{ij}$; under a uniform off-diagonal correlation $C_{ij} \to C_0$ for $i \neq j$ the variance approaches $C_0 + O(n^{-1})$, an $O(1)$ floor that defeats the $n^{-1/2}$ CLT rate [Billingsley1995 §27 — the standard correlated-mean variance identity is a direct corollary of the variance-bilinearity formula]. The whole apparatus of `Eq. (rg_scaling)` is the i.i.d. averaging rate; correlated tokens do not give it.

Trained-transformer attention statistics are not i.i.d. across tokens. Voita et al. (2019, *Analyzing Multi-Head Self-Attention*) document that BERT and machine-translation transformer heads partition into specialized roles — positional heads (previous-token, next-token), syntactic heads (subject-verb, dependency-arc), and rare-token heads — each producing strong, structured cross-token correlations in attention patterns. Clark et al. (2019, *What Does BERT Look At?*) report similar specialization, including coreference-resolution heads where attention weights at token $i$ are deterministically tied to syntactic structure at distant tokens. Both papers report attention-weight statistics that are extreme departures from independent across positions. These are not exotic special cases; they are the modal behavior of every trained transformer the field has measured. The conjecture's stated domain of application — trained transformers — is therefore precisely the regime where its independence premise fails.

Sub-claim 1 ("CLT exponents are correct under independence") is granted; sub-claim 3 ("part (ii) holds for trained transformers") is the load-bearing claim and it does not survive the premise failure. The manuscript's own hedge at `:2237` ("we conjecture---but do not claim to have validated on trained models") and at `:2255` ("requires verification in trained models") acknowledges the gap; my position is that the available evidence — Voita 2019, Clark 2019, and the manuscript's own $y_3 \approx +0.2$ measurement at `:2275` — already points the wrong way for trained transformers and is sufficient to reject sub-claim 3.

**3. CLT dimensional analysis is not a Wilsonian RG; closure is undemonstrated.**

A Wilsonian RG construction in the standard sense requires (i) a coarse-graining map combining local degrees of freedom into a renormalized degree of freedom on a coarser lattice, (ii) a rescaling of fields, and (iii) demonstration that the renormalized Hamiltonian (or action) lies in the same parametric class as the original, so that the map can be iterated and a fixed-point linearization makes sense [Wilson1971 §III; Wilson1974 §V; Kadanoff1966; Cardy1996 §3.3; Goldenfeld1992 §9.3]. The scaling dimensions are extracted from the linearization of the iterated map at the fixed point, not from naïve dimensional analysis of a single-step averaging operation.

The construction at `:2249-2260` supplies (i) — the meta-agent map $\mu_A, \Sigma_A$ — and computes single-step variance scaling under the CLT. It does not supply (iii). The meta-agent covariance $\Sigma_A = (1/|A|) \sum_i \Sigma_i + \mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$ at Eq. (`meta_agent_beliefs`) acquires the within-cluster mean variance $\mathrm{Var}_A(\mu)$, which is a structurally new term — the manuscript itself flags this as the "emergent anisotropy" channel in part (iv) of the conjecture at `:2269`. The renormalized object therefore does *not* lie in the same parametric class as the original $(\mu_i, \Sigma_i, \phi_i)$ family unless that family is augmented to track the additional within-cluster mean-variance moment. No augmentation is performed in §8.4. Iterating $R_n$ a second time is therefore not defined inside the stated parametric family.

The exponents at `:2257` are then computed not as eigenvalues of the linearized iterated map at a fixed point — the actual Wilsonian construction — but as the trivial CLT averaging rate for a single application of the map. Calling the result an "RG fixed point" is interpretive, not derived. The sample mean $\bar{X}_n \to \mu$ at rate $n^{-1/2}$ is a statement about i.i.d. random variables [Billingsley1995 §27]; promoting it to a Wilsonian fixed-point claim requires the additional structure that §8.4 does not provide.

**4. The post-hoc ordering claim at `:2277` reverses the logical dependence.**

The closing paragraph at `:2277` asserts that "the ordering of the three limits in Section~\ref{sec:transformer_limit} is dictated by the RG hierarchy." But the three limits are introduced at `:995-1352` (§5), upstream of the RG analysis. Inside §5 the ordering is dictated by the algebraic structure of the Q-K-V reduction, not by an RG argument that did not yet exist in the manuscript flow. Treating the §8.4 RG hierarchy as causally responsible for an ordering that was already fixed by §5's derivation is a post-hoc rationalization. This does not by itself falsify the conjecture, but it does show that part of the manuscript's rhetorical support for the conjecture is illusory: the §5 ordering is not independent corroboration of §8.4; §5 was already in place.

## Falsification conditions

My position is wrong if any of the following holds.

A defensible quantitative finite-size analysis recovers $y_3 \approx -1$ from the spectral-clustering measurement after standard finite-size corrections. The relevant standard is a leading-order correction with controlled remainder — e.g., a derivation of the form $y_3^{\mathrm{meas}}(n) = -1 + c(n)/\log n + \dots$ with an explicit $c(n)$ from the clustering geometry, matched to the measurement and yielding $y_3 \approx -1$ in the $n \to \infty$ extrapolation [Cardy1996 §3.5; Cardy1996 Ch. 4]. The manuscript at `:2275` does not perform this analysis.

A trained-transformer empirical study demonstrates that despite the head-specialization patterns reported in Voita 2019 and Clark 2019, the effective cross-token correlation of the relevant perturbations $\Delta_i, \delta\Omega_{ij}$ averages out at the level of $g_3$ so that the CLT $n^{-1/2}$ rate is approximately recovered. No such study is cited in §8.4 or in the supplementary appendices referenced from §8.4.

A closure argument is supplied showing that $R_n$ acting on the augmented parametric family (extended to track the within-cluster mean-variance moment, in the spirit of [Cardy1996 §3.3 effective-action treatment]) lies in that augmented family and that the linearized iterated map at the candidate fixed point has eigenvalues matching the claimed exponents. The companion paper's RG construction (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` §4) adopted an augmented-class form per its own debate; the §8.4 construction has not, and the scope rule of `00_claim.md` forbids importing that resolution here.
