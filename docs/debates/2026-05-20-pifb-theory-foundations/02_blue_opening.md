# Blue Opening — pifb-theory-foundations

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §Theory subsections trade ordinary differential-geometry vocabulary (principal bundle, associated bundle, section, Fisher-Rao) for an unusual cargo — same-symbol dual role for $\phi_i$, a "cross-scale shadow" identity $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ that is not the standard hierarchical-AIF prior conditional, and a Cencov uniqueness statement at line 510 whose scope (finite category vs. general parametric Gaussian family) is left loose — so the surface rigor is doing more rhetorical work than the underlying construction supports.

## Position

The §Theory foundations at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:483-911` are rigorous in the operational sense required for a working framework: every primitive object is a textbook construction with a primary-source citation, the two genuinely framework-specific moves (dual role for $\phi_i$, cross-scale shadow) are explicitly flagged as such with antecedents and a reduction proof, the alternative associated-bundle convention is registered, and the exponential-map non-compactness is admitted as a regime boundary. Red can land citation-tightening hits on §Statistical Manifolds (Cencov scope) and on the rhetorical economy of the same-symbol dual role, but cannot land a foundation-breaking strike on the evidence.

## Evidence

**B1 — All definitions are textbook constructions with primary-source citations.**

The base manifold $\mathcal{C}$ is a smooth $n$-dimensional manifold (line 488), the principal $G$-bundle $\pi:\mathcal{N}\to\mathcal{C}$ with free right action and fiber-preserving projection (lines 567-575), and the associated bundle $\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{N}\times_\rho \mathcal{B}$ via the equivalence relation $(n\cdot g, b) \sim (n,\rho(g)b)$ (line 601) are the standard Kobayashi-Nomizu / Nakahara / Frankel constructions in canonical form — see `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:591`, which cites `\cite{nakahara2003geometry, frankel2011geometry}` at the associated-bundle introduction. The Fisher-Rao metric $g_\mathcal{B}(\partial_a,\partial_b) = \mathbb{E}[(\partial_a \log\rho)(\partial_b \log\rho)]$ at line 503 is the textbook form of `[Amari-Nagaoka 2000, Eq. 2.1]`. The closed-form Gaussian KL at line 530 is `[Bishop2006 §10.1]`. An agent as a smooth section $q_i: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathcal{E}_\mathrm{state}|_{\mathcal{U}_i}$ at line 620 is the bundle-theoretic version of a field over a base manifold, with $\Gamma(\mathcal{E})$ the section module — `[Frankel2011 §1.6]`, `[Nakahara2003 §10.1]`.

**B2 — The Two Roles / dual-role paragraph at 557-565 is a pre-emption, not a hand-wave.**

At line 557 the manuscript splits $\phi_i$ into Role A (transport, gauge-redundant: only $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ enters dynamics, invariant under global right-translation $U_i \mapsto U_i g$) and Role B (state, frame-dependent ontology that survives in pulled-back geometries $G_i^{(q)}$ and signature-mechanism quantities). At line 561 the dual-role rigor paragraph cites three independent literatures that have already deployed exactly this construction: edge modes in gauge theory `\cite{DonnellyFreidel2016}` where boundary data is pure gauge from the global view but physical from the subsystem view; quantum reference frames `\cite{BartlettRudolphSpekkens2007, Vanrietvelde2020}` where a frame is gauge from the perspective-neutral viewpoint but a state-carrying physical system from its own perspective; Rovelli's relational interpretation `\cite{Rovelli1996}`. The decisive technical move sits at line 565: $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu^{(i)} A_\nu^{(i)})$ is invariant under the residual constant-per-agent subgroup $\{(g_i): g_i \in G \text{ constant in } c\}$ because $\mathrm{tr}(g^{-1}A_\mu g \cdot g^{-1}A_\nu g) = \mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ by trace cyclicity — that is a one-line proof, not an analogy. The pre-emption is rigorous: a quantity is invariant under the residual subgroup, the manuscript names that subgroup explicitly, the invariance follows from a textbook identity.

**B3 — The Cross-Scale Shadow construction is admitted as a structural commitment and discharged by Appendix A.**

Line 546 reads "This is a structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference," followed by an explicit reduction sketch and a forward reference to Appendix A. Appendix A delivers the proof. `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:4436-4438` gives Lemma `aug_joint_welldefined`: the augmented hierarchical joint with Gibbs cross-scale factor $\chi_{i,\pi(i)} \propto \exp[-\|k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)} k_{\pi(i)}\|^2 / (2\sigma^2)]$ is a proper probability density on the product space of all agent-scale latents, proved by the tree-decomposition argument of `[Lauritzen1996 §3.2]` and the exponential-family integrability conditions of `[Brown1986 §1.5]`. Lines 4449-4471 give Lemma `mf_fixedpoint`: the standard tree-structured-factor-graph coordinate-update mean-field equation, `[Wainwright2008 Prop. 4.1]`, `[Bishop2006 §10.1]`. Lines 4481-4502 give Lemma `shadow_mf_optimum`: the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is the rigid-link limit $\sigma^2 \to 0$ of the mean-field optimum, with the explicit closed form at line 4485 $q_i^{(s)} = \mathcal{N}(\Omega \mu_{\pi(i)}, \Omega \Sigma_{\pi(i)} \Omega^\top + \sigma^2 I)$ recovering the gauge pushforward $(\Omega_{i,\pi(i)})_* q_{\pi(i)}^{(s+1)}$ at line 4499 in the $\sigma^2 \to 0$ limit. The construction is exhibited as the deterministic-decoder limit of a ladder-VAE-style hierarchical prior at line 4508, with the standard scheme's $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ recovered as the finite-$\sigma^2$ Gibbs factor. The "structural commitment" framing at 546 is honest about which step is non-standard (the rigid-link limit), and the Appendix A reduction is the rigorous backbone.

**B4 — The associated-bundle convention statement at 604 explicitly handles the alternative.**

Line 604 names the chosen convention $(n\cdot g, b) \sim (n, \rho(g)b)$ (Nakahara), states the alternative $(n\cdot g, b) \sim (n, \rho(g^{-1})b)$ found in some texts, gives the relation under $g \mapsto g^{-1}$ via the homomorphism property $\rho(g^{-1}) = \rho(g)^{-1}$, and instructs the reader to substitute $\rho(g) \to \rho(g^{-1})$ when importing identities from a text with the inverse convention. This is the level of bundle-convention bookkeeping that `[Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 §I.5]` performs.

**B5 — The exponential-map non-compactness caveat at 581 is registered correctly for $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$.**

The line says the exponential parameterization $U_i = \exp(\phi_i)$ is "only locally bijective: surjective for connected compact groups (such as the $G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$ used in the proof-of-concept simulations), but for the non-compact group $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ relevant to the natural variational-inference setting, a single $\phi$ does not parameterize every group element." This is the standard Lie-theory fact (`[Hall2015 §3.7]`) — $\exp:\mathfrak{gl}(K) \to \mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ is surjective but not injective on a single chart of $\mathfrak{g}$ — and the manuscript names the two correct remedies (restrict to a neighborhood of zero where exp is bijective, or pass to a polar/Cartan decomposition $U = \exp(X)\exp(Y)$). The local-trivialization caveat at line 577 makes the same move at the bundle level: agents operate on local trivializing patches $\{\mathcal{U}_i\}$ joined by the Čech cocycle $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk} = \Omega_{ik}$, so the patch-wise statement is what the framework requires; no global section is claimed.

**B6 — The Multi-Agent / Meta-Agent / Culture subsections distinguish strong and weak consensus correctly.**

The Perfect Consensus definition at line 672 is stated as the pointwise-equality idealization $\Omega_{ij}[q_j] = q_i$. Line 682 immediately tells the reader that pointwise consensus is "the epistemic-death limit of Section~\ref{sec:epistemic_collapse}, not a healthy coarse-graining target." The Meta-Agent definition at line 684 then specifies three conditions: coherence threshold $C_\mathrm{belief} \cdot C_\mathrm{model} > \Gamma_\mathrm{min}$, non-empty multi-child support $U_I = \mathrm{ConnComp}\{c : \sum_i \chi_i(c) \ge 2\}$, minimum cluster size $|I| \ge N_\mathrm{min}$. The Culture subsection at line 697 supplies the realistic criterion: the graph-weighted slow-channel inequality at Eq. `culture_internal_coherence`, line 704, which says members share a common slow generative model up to gauge transport. The distinction is explicit: pointwise $s_i = s_j$ is "a strict identity that no nontrivial culture exhibits" (line 707) and the operational form is $s_i \approx \tilde\Omega_{ij} s_j$, with the gauge transport "doing the cultural work, distinguishing intra-cultural disagreement (translatable across frames) from radical incommensurability (not translatable)". The closure-relative-to-the-outside condition at line 710 is the standard RG cluster-isolation criterion.

## Pre-empted attacks

**Attack R1: Cencov 1982 uniqueness at line 510 has scope caveats — the original theorem is stated for finite-dimensional category-theoretic invariance.**

Granted as a citation-tightening point but not as a foundation strike. Cencov's original 1982 theorem is for invariance under Markov morphisms on finite probability spaces; the extension to general parametric families (including the Gaussian families that the manuscript actually uses) is in `[Ay-Jost-Le-Schwachhöfer 2017 §V]` and `[Bauer-Jost-Tumpach 2016]`. The manuscript's line 510 says the metric is "unique up to scaling as the only Riemannian metric on probability spaces invariant under sufficient statistics" — the statement is correct for the Gaussian families at lines 513-518, which is what the rest of the manuscript uses, but the citation could be tightened by adding Ay-Jost-Le-Schwachhöfer 2017 alongside Cencov 1982 to capture the parametric-family extension. This is a one-line citation patch, not a load-bearing failure. The Fisher-Rao metric is the working metric throughout, and the uniqueness claim is empirically a footnote relative to the bundle construction.

**Attack R2: The dual role for $\phi_i$ uses one symbol for two distinct objects — a notational sin.**

The two-roles paragraph at line 557 is the manuscript's explicit answer: the dual role is named, the two roles are described separately, line 559 reserves "gauge-invariant" / "gauge-covariant" for their standard mathematical meanings and reserves "frame-dependent" for Role-B quantities. The line-565 residual-subgroup analysis makes the Role-B invariance group precise. Demanding two distinct symbols would be a major notational overhaul that the edge-mode and quantum-reference-frame literatures `\cite{DonnellyFreidel2016, BartlettRudolphSpekkens2007, Vanrietvelde2020}` themselves do not perform — in those literatures, the same boundary / frame data does double duty as gauge-redundant from the global view and as physical from the subsystem view. The manuscript inherits the convention from its primary-source antecedents and signals it explicitly. The notational complaint is a style preference, not a rigor failure.

**Attack R3: Cross-Scale Shadow is non-standard hierarchical inference.**

The manuscript says exactly this at line 546 — "a structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference" — and then supplies the Appendix A reduction proving it is the rigid-link mean-field limit of a well-defined hierarchical generative model. The reduction was the subject of an earlier debate (commit `b6c7b71d`: "new appendix discharges cross-scale shadow reduction obligation"). The honest scoping at line 546 plus the Appendix A backbone is exactly the level of rigor the claim asserts.

**Attack R4: The connection-form / curvature material at 797-816 is not verified to be the standard Yang-Mills setup.**

This is in the evidence pack as "verify the framework's use is consistent," not "shown to be inconsistent." The §Connection Forms section at 797-816 defines $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1}dU_i$ (the Maurer-Cartan form, `[Frankel2011 §2.6]`) and the field strength $F = dA + A\wedge A$ (`[Nakahara2003 §10.5]`). The gauge transformation $A \mapsto g^{-1}Ag + g^{-1}dg$ is the standard inhomogeneous transformation, and the residual-subgroup observation at line 565 governs which quantities of these are invariant in the manuscript's working sense.

## Falsification conditions

This blue position is wrong if any of the following holds.

**F1.** The Lemma `aug_joint_welldefined` proof at lines 4441-4443 contains a load-bearing error (e.g., the tree-decomposition integrability claim fails for the manuscript's actual hierarchy because the breadth is not finite, or the Gibbs factor at Eq. `gibbs_factor` is not the conditional density it is asserted to be). The manuscript would then have claimed a reduction it does not have, and the line-546 disclaimer would not be discharged.

**F2.** The Lemma `shadow_mf_optimum` rigid-link limit at lines 4497-4499 is computed incorrectly (e.g., the trace term that is dropped at line 4495 actually depends on $k_i$ through a mis-identified Gaussian normalization), so the closed form at line 4485 is wrong and the gauge pushforward at line 4499 is not recovered. The cross-scale shadow would then not be the mean-field optimum of the augmented joint.

**F3.** The residual-subgroup invariance claim at line 565 is incorrect: $\mathrm{tr}(g^{-1}A_\mu g \cdot g^{-1} A_\nu g) \ne \mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ for $g$ constant in $c$. This is the one-line computation $\mathrm{tr}(g^{-1}A g g^{-1}B g) = \mathrm{tr}(g^{-1}ABg) = \mathrm{tr}(AB)$ by cyclicity, so failing this would require $g$ to be non-invertible — but $g \in G$ is invertible by definition. The claim should hold.

**F4.** The dual-role analogy fails because the framework's frame-as-state usage is structurally distinct from edge modes and quantum reference frames in a way the manuscript does not register. Specifically, if the residual-subgroup invariance group identified at line 565 does not in fact contain the cited literatures' working invariance groups, the antecedents would be misappropriated and the line-561 citations would be load-bearing rhetoric rather than load-bearing math.

**F5.** Cencov 1982's uniqueness theorem genuinely does not apply to the parametric Gaussian families at lines 513-518 (i.e., the Ay-Jost-Le-Schwachhöfer 2017 extension is itself wrong, or does not cover the Gaussian case). This would be falsified by exhibiting a second Riemannian metric on the Gaussian family that is invariant under sufficient statistics and is not a constant scaling of Fisher-Rao.

**F6.** The associated-bundle convention switch at line 604 introduces an inconsistency downstream — i.e., some transport law in §Transport Operators (line 778) or the field-strength definition at 816 actually uses the alternative convention and the manuscript fails to track the substitution.

Falsification conditions F3 and F5 are the ones where blue is on near-zero footing if red lands the strike — a counter-example would be decisive. F1 and F2 are where the manuscript's claimed rigor lives or dies in the Appendix A computation. F4 is the soft spot of the dual-role argument: blue grants that the analogy carries less weight than a theorem, and rests on the residual-subgroup move at line 565 being the actual math.

## Closing

The §Theory foundations are not flawless — the Cencov scope citation could be tightened to Ay-Jost-Le-Schwachhöfer 2017 alongside Cencov 1982, and the single-symbol dual role is a style choice that some readers will find cluttered. They are rigorous in the sense that the load-bearing claim asserts: every primitive object is textbook, every non-textbook move is named as such with a reduction, the two technical caveats (local trivialization, exp-map non-compactness) are registered at the right level of detail, and the multi-agent / culture material correctly disambiguates the strong and weak consensus criteria. Red wins on citation tightening or notational preference; red does not win on the foundations.
