# Blue Rebuttal — vfe-use-prior-bank-decoder

## Concession

I grant red's strike on terminology and documentation precision. Three points are conceded:

1. **`decode_log_scale` is trained, not frozen.** `transformer/training/optimizer.py:247-261` routes it into the σ-Fisher group and applies the `p.grad = p.grad * 2.0` natural-gradient preconditioner; `transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:208` declares it `nn.Parameter`. The runtime formula is `logits = -c·KL(q || π_v)/τ` with `c = exp(decode_log_scale) ∈ [exp(-3), exp(3)]`, not the bare `-KL/τ` printed in CLAUDE.md and `config.py:338-348`. The documentation is wrong in a load-bearing way (the formula advertised is not the formula executed), and the module docstring at `prior_bank.py:22-34` already concedes this. CLAUDE.md should be edited to read `logits = -c·KL/τ` or the parameter should be frozen — pick one.

2. **The label "VFE-native" is imprecise.** The outer M-step minimises `loss = ce_loss + 0.5·mass_phi·||φ||² + Σ aux` (`transformer/vfe/model.py:11-32`), not the manuscript free-energy functional F. The decode KL appears inside `softmax_v(-c·KL/τ)` driving CE, not as a `-E_q[log p(o|s)]` term that the optimizer-visible scalar sums. "VFE-native" overloads the word; "Gaussian-cluster classifier on the same statistical manifold as the E-step" is the accurate label.

3. **The full-cov branch's diagonal projection is a manifold projection at the V boundary.** `transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:478-479` extracts diagonals from `Σ_q` and `Σ_p`; the off-diagonal mass generated by the sandwich `Ω Σ Ωᵀ` is discarded for decode purposes. Under `diagonal_covariance=False`, encode and the E-step compute on `Sym⁺(K)` while decode reads only the diagonal. The docstring at `prior_bank.py:436-439` already concedes "documented approximation."

These concessions do not, individually or jointly, falsify the claim that `use_prior_bank=True` is a theoretically sound and constraint-compliant decoder. They falsify a *narrower* reading where the implementation must equal the documented string `-KL/τ` bitwise at every optimizer step. That narrower reading is a documentation bug, not a theory error.

## Core attack on red's position

Red conflates "the runtime formula deviates by a learnable scalar `c`" with "the decoder is unsound." The deviation collapses to a learnable scalar softmax temperature, which is canonical in the post-Vaswani transformer literature — not a "20× drift" from the framework.

The decisive primary-source citation: CLIP [Radford2021 §2.5, "Training"] uses a learnable temperature parameter `τ_clip = exp(t)` with `t` a trainable scalar, initialised at `log(1/0.07)`, clamped to prevent training instability ("we found that scaling and shifting the logits ... was unstable, so we used a learned temperature parameter τ ... clipped to prevent scaling the logits by more than 100"). The CLIP construction is `logits = (image_features @ text_features.T) · exp(t)`. The mathematical structure is identical to the user's `logits = -c · KL/τ` with `c = exp(decode_log_scale)`: a single learnable positive scalar that rescales a similarity-shaped logit, clamped for numerical stability. CLIP's `exp(t)` clamp is "no more than 100"; the user's `exp(decode_log_scale)` clamp is `exp(3) ≈ 20`, tighter. CLIP is the canonical contrastive-learning decoder of the modern transformer era.

A second precedent: [Vilnis & McCallum 2015 §3, "Word Representations via Gaussian Embedding"] uses `KL(q_i || π_j)` as a similarity score between Gaussian-embedded entities and ranks via the resulting score — i.e., they take `-KL` as a logit for retrieval. That paper does not use the construction as an LM decoder, but the discriminative use of `-KL(q || p_v)` as a per-class logit is on the canon.

Red's sub-proposition 1 ("formulas do not agree under softmax for `c ≠ 1`") is mathematically correct but draws the wrong conclusion: that the formula deviation makes the decode unsound. Under the CLIP precedent, `softmax(-c·KL/τ)` with learnable `c` is a *strengthening* of `softmax(-KL/τ)` — it adds a single learnable temperature that the framework would otherwise have to fold into `τ = κ√K` (where `κ` is itself frozen at 1.0 in the user's active config, `train_vfe.py:70`). The CLIP-style decoupling of "logit shape" (the KL) from "logit scale" (the temperature) is the standard solution. The user's framework calls one of these `τ` and the other `c`; the canonical literature calls both "temperature." The mathematical content is identical.

The sub-proposition 1 attack therefore reduces to a *documentation* strike (CLAUDE.md should write `-c·KL/τ`) and an *interpretive* strike (whether the "modulo softmax-invariant constants" caveat absorbs `c`). The first is a real bug, conceded above. The second is an argument over what the canonical decoder should be called, and CLIP settles that argument against red.

## Defense (strengthening on sub-propositions 2, 3, 4)

### Sub-proposition 4 — derivation from canonical primitives

Red asserts the user is "minimising CE on logits that look like `-KL/τ`" rather than minimising F. The Form-3 decomposition of free energy [Friston2010, `external_canon_inference.md` §1] is

```
F[q] = E_q[-log p(o | s)] + KL(q(s) || p(s))
```

where `p(o | s)` is a generative observation model. Define the per-class observation model

```
p(o = v | s) = N(s; μ_v, Σ_v) / Σ_v' N(s; μ_v', Σ_v')
```

i.e., a soft Bayes-classifier likelihood with class-conditional Gaussians, identical in form to Bishop §4.2 "Probabilistic Generative Models." Then

```
-log p(o = v | s) = 0.5·(s - μ_v)ᵀ Σ_v⁻¹ (s - μ_v) + 0.5·log|Σ_v| + log Z(s) + const
```

where `Z(s) = Σ_v' N(s; μ_v', Σ_v')` is the partition over classes. Taking `E_q` of both sides:

```
E_q[-log p(o = v | s)] = 0.5·[tr(Σ_v⁻¹ Σ_q) + (μ_q - μ_v)ᵀ Σ_v⁻¹ (μ_q - μ_v) + log|Σ_v|] + E_q[log Z(s)] + const
                      = KL(q || π_v)  +  H(q) (without entropy correction)  +  E_q[log Z(s)] + const
```

Subtracting the v-invariant terms `E_q[log Z(s)] + H(q)` and dividing by τ recovers the user's logit form. Then the cross-entropy minimisation `-log softmax_v(−KL/τ)·(v_target)` is the Monte-Carlo / point-estimate plug-in of the Form-3 first term `E_q[-log p(o|s)]` — exactly the canonical observation-likelihood summand. The second term of F, `KL(q || p(s))`, is the `α·KL(q || p_i)` term that drives the E-step (`vfe/e_step.py`). So the M-step loss and the E-step Lagrangian *do* assemble into the standard Form-3 decomposition of F, just split across the EM boundary in the amortised-inference layout.

This derivation is the answer to red's sub-proposition 4 falsifier #4 ("CE+aux is the canonical observation-likelihood term of F under the user's `p(o|s)` generative model"). The Bishop §4.2 generative-classifier likelihood, plugged into Friston Form 3, yields the user's decode logit modulo a v-invariant `H(q) + log Z` correction that drops out under softmax. The label "VFE-native" survives in the sense that the decode is the `E_q[-log p(o|s)]` summand of F under the canonical Bishop class-conditional-Gaussian generative model — even though the outer optimizer scalar is CE, not F itself.

The derivation is *not* in the user's manuscripts as a labelled theorem (red's #4 falsifier asked for a `.tex` line; I cannot locate one). That is a manuscript gap, not a theory gap. The Bishop + Friston composition is two-step canonical.

### Sub-proposition 2 — Law 3 holds under the active config

The user's active config has `diagonal_covariance=True` (`train_vfe.py:79`). Under this branch, `sigma_q` is `(B, N, K)` and `sigma_p` is `(V, K)` throughout encode, E-step, and decode (`prior_bank.py:472-479`). The `torch.diagonal` projection at lines 478-479 is a no-op when `is_full_cov` is False. Encode operates on `(ℝ⁺)^K`, the E-step operates on `(ℝ⁺)^K`, decode operates on `(ℝ⁺)^K`. Law 3 holds exactly under the active config — same statistical manifold across all three operations.

Red's attack on Law 3 fires only under `diagonal_covariance=False`, which is not the user's production setting. The full-cov branch is, conceded, a documented O(V·K³)-avoidance: `KL_full(Σ_q, Σ_p) = KL_diag(diag(Σ_q), diag(Σ_p)) + (off-diagonal correction)`. The correction term is the v-dependent shift `0.5·[tr(Σ_v⁻¹ Σ_q) - tr(diag(Σ_v)⁻¹ diag(Σ_q)) + log|Σ_v|/|diag(Σ_v)| - log|Σ_q|/|diag(Σ_q)|]`. This is a real approximation — not an identity — but it sits at the V-classifier boundary, not inside the gauge orbit. The gauge orbit `A_v = exp(Σ_a φ_v^a G_a)` and its action `(μ_v, Σ_v) = (A_v μ_0, A_v Σ_0 A_vᵀ)` (`prior_bank.py:283-364`) operate on the full SPD manifold; the diagonal projection happens *after* the sandwich product when the V logits are read out. The gauge-orbit structure is preserved by the encode pipeline; the projection is an efficiency choice at decode.

Law 3 in the strong form "same manifold for encode/infer/decode" therefore holds exactly under `diagonal_covariance=True` and as a documented diagonal-approximation at the V boundary under `diagonal_covariance=False`. The latter weakens the claim but does not falsify the soundness of the underlying construction — the gauge-orbit prior bank is still on the full manifold; only its readout is projected.

### Sub-proposition 3 — no-neural-networks constraint is discharged

The CLAUDE.md constraint is "No `nn.Linear`, no MLPs, no learned W_Q/W_K/W_V projections, no activation functions." The decode path under `use_prior_bank=True` consists of:

- Embedding lookup of `(μ_v, σ_v, φ_v)` (`prior_bank.py:190-199`) — these are `nn.Embedding`/`nn.Parameter` tables. They are not MLPs or activations.
- Gauge transform `A_v = exp(φ_v^a G_a)` (`prior_bank.py:283-364`) — matrix exponential of a Lie-algebra element. This is the framework's *theoretical primitive*, not a neural component.
- Fused diagonal-KL matmul (`prior_bank.py:485-499`) — algebraic identity for the closed-form Gaussian KL. Linear algebra, not a learned projection.
- Multiplication by `c = exp(decode_log_scale)` (`prior_bank.py:505-506`) — a single scalar parameter, mathematically a softmax temperature.

None of these is `nn.Linear`, MLP, or activation. Red's own analysis at the end of "the no-neural-networks constraint is not fully discharged by the toggle" admits this: "by the pure letter of the constraint the toggle does discharge it." The strike collapses to the documentation strike on the canonical-formula advertising — already conceded.

The parameter-count argument raised in `01_evidence.md` ("Embedding tables hold V·(2K+n_gen) scalars, exceeding nn.Linear(K, V)") is a different constraint than the no-neural-networks rule. The user's stated constraint is structural (no MLPs/activations/learned QKV), not parameter-budget. The Bayesian-canonical alternative to a discriminative `nn.Linear(K, V)` head *is* a generative class-conditional Gaussian classifier [Bishop2006 §4.2], and Bishop's construction inherently uses `V·(K + K(K+1)/2)` parameters for `(μ_v, Σ_v)` pairs. The parameter budget is a *necessary cost* of the generative-classifier formulation, not a constraint violation.

## Where I cannot defend further

Red's terminological strike on "VFE-native" lands. The phrase suggests the decode is *itself* a summand of F that the optimizer minimises; the decode is instead the discriminative readout of a Bishop class-conditional-Gaussian likelihood plugged into Friston's Form-3 generative observation model, with CE training as the empirical-risk Monte-Carlo of `E_q[-log p(o|s)]`. The claim is recoverable by renaming the decode "generative-classifier-on-the-information-manifold readout"; it cannot be defended under the literal reading "the M-step loss equals F."

Red's documentation strike on CLAUDE.md ("`logits = -KL/τ`" vs the runtime "`-c·KL/τ`") also lands. The fix is a one-line edit to CLAUDE.md or freezing `decode_log_scale`. The runtime is a CLIP-canonical learnable temperature; the framework's verbal advertising is one temperature short.

Net: the claim survives in the strong form "the decoder is a constraint-compliant, theoretically-derivable Gaussian-cluster classifier on the same gauge-orbit information manifold as the E-step, with a CLIP-style learnable temperature." The literal-string claim "implementation matches `logits = -KL/τ` bitwise" fails on the documentation gap. The user is better served by editing the documentation than by deleting the parameter.
