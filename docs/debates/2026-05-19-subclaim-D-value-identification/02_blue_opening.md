# Blue Opening — subclaim-D-value-identification

## Steelman (opposing position)

The reduction `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_{ij} Ω_{ij} μ_j → Σ_j β_{ij} V_j` is only exact under a constant-gauge specialization that contradicts the framework's own claim (manuscript `:1867`) that attention-gauge and value-gauge need not coincide; furthermore, the absorbed `Ω` is identified with a square `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` (manuscript `:1310`), whereas Vaswani §3.2.1 specifies a rectangular `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_v}` ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.1], `external_canon_transformers.md §1`), so what is recovered is at most a head-space sub-factor and not Vaswani's value projection.

## Position

Under the stated constant-gauge specialization `Ω_{ij} = Ω` (manuscript `:1325`, "constant gauge $\Omega_{ij} = \Omega$ for all agent pairs"), the value-aggregation reduction at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1296 → :1316` is **exact at two distinct algebraic levels**: (i) the pullout `Σ_j β_{ij} Ω μ_j = Ω · Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j` is an identity by linearity, with the only assumption being the very pair-independence that *defines* the constant-gauge limit (so it is not a hidden assumption — it is the specialization itself); and (ii) the absorption `V_j ≡ W_V^T μ_j` with `W_V ≡ Ω^T` (manuscript `:1311`, "we absorb the gauge transport $\Omega$ into the learned matrix $W_V$") is a definitional identification of one invertible matrix with another, parallel to the Q–K identification `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}` at `:1240`. The square notation `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` is the per-head invertible head-space factor that lifts to Vaswani's rectangular `W_V` via the same thin-SVD construction the manuscript already gives for Q and K at `:1245–:1252` (Eq. `eq:head_space_kernel`). The attention-gauge / value-gauge coincidence is a strict specialization of the more general framework noted at `:1867`, and it is the specialization standard transformers in fact occupy.

## Evidence

- **Pullout is linearity, not a hidden step.** Manuscript `:1296` writes `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_{ij} Ω_{ij} μ_j`. Setting `Ω_{ij} = Ω` (the explicit specialization at `:1325`) and using the distributivity of matrix multiplication over a scalar-weighted sum gives `Σ_j β_{ij} Ω μ_j = Ω Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j`. The factor `Ω` is constant in `j`; the `β_{ij}` are scalars. No analytic limit, no approximation, no truncation — this is the same operation that pulls a constant out of a finite sum. Red's framing of "constant gauge is circular" misreads what the manuscript is doing: `:1325` declares the specialization as one of three preconditions; under that precondition, the identity holds; the manuscript is not deriving the precondition.

- **W_V ≡ Ω^T is a definition that makes the identification hold by construction.** Manuscript `:1308–:1311`: `V_j ≡ W_V^T μ_j` with the absorption `Ω → W_V`. The chain is `Ω Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j = Σ_j β_{ij} (Ω μ_j) = Σ_j β_{ij} (W_V^T μ_j) = Σ_j β_{ij} V_j` once `W_V^T ≡ Ω`. This is the value-side analog of the Q–K identification at `:1240`: a particular learned matrix is identified with a particular gauge element. Both identifications are *definitions* under the constant-gauge limit, not derived consequences. Sub-claim C accepts this move for `W_Q W_K^T`; the same logic applies to `W_V`.

- **Square `W_V` matches Vaswani's per-head dimension `d_v` and lifts to `d_model × d_v` via thin SVD already documented in the manuscript.** Vaswani writes `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_v}` with `d_v = d_model / h` per head ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] via `external_canon_transformers.md §1`). The manuscript's `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` at `:1310` is the per-head invertible head-space factor; `d_k` here is the head dimension (the manuscript uses `d_k` and `d_{\text{head}}` interchangeably in this section — compare the boxed reduction at `:1336` which writes `softmax(QK^T/√d_k)V` and the immediately following text at `:1339` which restates the same kernel as `GL(d_{\text{head}})`). The lift to the ambient rectangular form is the *same thin-SVD argument the manuscript already gives for Q and K* at `:1245–:1252` and Eq. `eq:head_space_kernel`: `W_V^a = U_V^a A_V^a` with `U_V^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` an isometric subspace embedding (orthonormal columns) and `A_V^a ∈ GL(d_head)` the invertible head-space factor. The gauge identification `Ω^T ↔ A_V^a` operates on the invertible head-space factor; the ambient `W_V^a = U_V^a A_V^a` is its low-rank lift through the isometry `U_V^a`. This is structurally identical to the `M_h^a` construction at `:1248` that sub-claim C's evidence pack treats as the resolution to the rectangular-Q/K issue.

- **Attention-gauge / value-gauge collapse is a strict specialization, not a contradiction.** Manuscript `:1867`: "The asymmetry in RoPE with position-dependent attention but position-independent values corresponds to factoring the gauge transport into an attention gauge and a value gauge that need not coincide. This decomposition is supported (but not required) by the full framework." The full framework permits attention-gauge ≠ value-gauge; the §5.2.3 reduction at `:1325` invokes the specialization where they coincide because that is what standard scaled dot-product attention does. Standard transformers do not have separate attention and value gauges — they have a single, learned `W_V` applied to every position uniformly, which is exactly the constant-gauge specialization on the value side. The framework's "factor into separate gauges" is a *generalization*, not a contradiction; the reduction direction (general → specialized) gives standard attention as the most restrictive sub-case.

- **Standard `W_Q, W_K, W_V` independence is not a contradiction.** Red could push that standard transformers have three independently learned matrices, whereas the gauge framework collapses everything to one `Ω`. This misreads the identification: under the gauge framework, `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}` at `:1240` and `W_V^T = Ω` at `:1311` are *two distinct learned matrix-valued specializations* of the same `Ω`. Standard transformers learn `W_Q, W_K, W_V` independently; the gauge framework's identification says those independent learnings *jointly* parameterize the same underlying `Ω` modulo the bilinear `W_Q W_K^T` factorization freedom noted at `:1243` and the thin-SVD freedom at `:1245`. The reduction does not over-constrain; it under-determines, because many `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` triples realize the same `Ω`. This is a feature of the identification, not a defect.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **The manuscript's `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` at `:1310` is the FULL aggregation kernel (including the output projection `W_O`), not the per-head value projection.** If `W_V` here is the `d_model × d_model` aggregation including `W_O`, the dimension matches (square in `d_model`) but the identification with Vaswani's per-head `W_V` is broken: it would correspond to `W_V W_O` jointly, which is a different object. The manuscript's notation is ambiguous on this point — `d_k` is used both for the head dimension (e.g., `:1336`) and for the ambient model dimension in other equations. If the orchestrator reads `d_k = d_model` at `:1310`, the per-head reduction fails. **However**: the immediately following boxed reduction at `:1336` writes `softmax(QK^T/√d_k)V` with the same `d_k`, and Vaswani's `√d_k` denotes the head dimension, so the per-head reading is the consistent one.

2. **The thin-SVD lift used for Q/K at `:1245–:1252` cannot be applied to V because V's structural role differs.** Q and K appear bilinearly inside the softmax; their thin-SVD lift preserves the bilinear form `μ_i^T (U_Q M_h U_K^T) μ_j` because `U_Q, U_K` enter symmetrically. V appears linearly via `Σ_j β_{ij} W_V^T μ_j`. If `W_V^a = U_V^a A_V^a` introduces a leftover isometry `U_V^a` on the OUTPUT side, the per-head aggregation lands in a `d_head`-dimensional subspace of `ℝ^{d_model}` and an extra `W_O`-like step is needed to lift back. **However**: standard multi-head attention does exactly this — each head produces a `d_head`-dim output, the heads concatenate, and `W_O` mixes them back to `d_model`. The isometry `U_V^a` is naturally absorbed into the per-head slice of `W_O`. The lift is exact under the standard concat-then-`W_O` multi-head structure ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] multi-head form, `external_canon_transformers.md §1`).

3. **Red produces a primary-source citation showing that standard transformers' independence of `W_Q, W_K, W_V` is incompatible with their being three independent specializations of a single gauge element `Ω`.** The identification at `:1311` and `:1240` requires that learned `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` triples be drawn from a parameter set that admits the joint factorization `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}` and `W_V^T = Ω` for some shared `Ω`. If a standard transformer's training dynamics can produce `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` outside this joint set — i.e., a triple where no single `Ω` satisfies both — the identification fails and the reduction is at best partial. No such impossibility result exists in the evidence pack; the joint set is large (the bilinear `W_Q W_K^T` has factorization freedom by `:1243`; `W_V^T` is unconstrained), so any standard transformer's learned weights admit such an `Ω` by construction (pick any invertible `W_V^T` and call it `Ω`, then back out `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2} (W_V^{-1})^T` up to scale).

4. **The orchestrator rules that `:1867`'s "decomposition is supported (but not required)" admits an interpretation under which the constant-gauge collapse used at `:1325` is not in the support set.** Manuscript `:1867` explicitly says the factored attention/value gauge is *supported* by the framework; the converse (the collapsed common-`Ω` case used at `:1325`) is the more restrictive specialization, and the support relation is `full ⊃ factored ⊃ collapsed` (see the explicit subset chain at `:1860–:1862`). If the orchestrator reads `:1867` as forbidding the collapsed case, the reduction at `:1325` is internally inconsistent. The chain at `:1860–:1862` makes the support relation explicit, so this falsification path is closed by the manuscript itself.

If none of (1)–(4) hold, the value-aggregation reduction at `:1296 → :1316` is exact under the constant-gauge specialization, and the square-`W_V` notation reconciles with Vaswani's rectangular `W_V` through the same thin-SVD lift the manuscript already provides for Q and K.
