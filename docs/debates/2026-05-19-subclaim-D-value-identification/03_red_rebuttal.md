# Red Rebuttal — subclaim-D-value-identification

## Concession

I grant blue's first move in full. Under the explicit constant-gauge specialization `Ω_{ij} = Ω` declared at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1325`, the pullout `Σ_j β_{ij} Ω μ_j = Ω · Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j` is an algebraic identity by linearity of the finite weighted sum. There is no hidden assumption beyond the specialization itself — the constant gauge is *declared* as a precondition, not smuggled in mid-derivation, and the orchestrator's question (1) at `00_claim.md:19` resolves to "trivially correct under the stated precondition." The pullout step is exact. My opening's framing of the pullout as load-bearing was wrong; the load is borne entirely by the absorption step, not the pullout.

## Core attack

Blue's defense rests on three claims that fail under audit.

**(a) The thin-SVD lift inherits sub-claim C's open issue rather than resolving it.** Blue argues at `02_blue_opening.md:17` that the square `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1310` lifts to Vaswani's rectangular `W_V ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_v}` via `W_V^a = U_V^a A_V^a` with `U_V^a` an isometric subspace embedding, "structurally identical to the `M_h^a` construction at `:1248` that sub-claim C's evidence pack treats as the resolution to the rectangular-Q/K issue." Sub-claim C's evidence pack does *not* "treat the M_h^a construction as the resolution" — it treats it as one of two open readings and explicitly flags the rectangular issue as unresolved (`01_evidence.md:64`, red reading). Sub-claim D's "exact" claim cannot be more settled than the construction it inherits from. If sub-claim C's identification lands at the level of the invertible head-space factor `M_h^a` rather than at Vaswani's ambient rectangular `W_Q W_K^T`, then sub-claim D's identification `W_V^T ≡ Ω` lands at the corresponding invertible head-space factor `A_V^a`, not at Vaswani's ambient rectangular `W_V`. The "exact reduction to standard transformers" therefore reduces to a *head-space sub-factor* of standard value aggregation. The evidence pack states this explicitly at `01_evidence.md:64`: "the reduction is therefore to a head-space sub-factor of standard value aggregation, not to the standard's rectangular per-head `W_V`."

**(b) "Absorption into W_V" is renaming, not derivation.** Blue's chain at `02_blue_opening.md:15` — "`Ω Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j = Σ_j β_{ij} (Ω μ_j) = Σ_j β_{ij} (W_V^T μ_j) = Σ_j β_{ij} V_j` once `W_V^T ≡ Ω`" — relabels the same matrix twice. The standard transformer learns `W_V` by SGD on a downstream task loss; the gauge framework's `Ω` is constructed from `exp(φ_i) exp(-φ_j)` at `CLAUDE.md` (Mathematical Reference, "Transport") and updated by E-step φ retractions. These are different parameterizations operating on different optimization landscapes. The identification "pick any invertible `W_V^T` and call it `Ω`" that blue offers at `02_blue_opening.md:31` (falsification path 3) confirms the renaming character of the move: any invertible matrix can be called `Ω` at one operating point, but a one-point relabeling of a learned matrix is not a structural reduction between architectures. Vaswani's standard form `[Vaswani2017 §3.2.1]` specifies that `W_V` is *learned by gradient descent on the downstream loss*; the gauge framework specifies that `Ω` is *constructed from learned φ via the exponential map* and *updated in an E-step inner loop*. These produce different gradient flows on different parameters. The reduction `Ω → W_V` is exact as a relabeling at one configuration; it is not exact as a statement that the two architectures are the same.

**(c) The attention-gauge / value-gauge collapse is a coupling the standard does not impose.** Blue concedes at `02_blue_opening.md:19` that "the full framework permits attention-gauge ≠ value-gauge; the §5.2.3 reduction at `:1325` invokes the specialization where they coincide because that is what standard scaled dot-product attention does." This inverts the support relation. Standard transformers learn `W_Q, W_K, W_V` *independently* — three separate parameter tensors, three separate gradient signals (`external_canon_transformers.md §1`, "Multi-head" entry, each head has its own `W_Q^i, W_K^i, W_V^i`). The gauge framework's `:1325` specialization ties them to *one* `Ω` via `W_Q W_K^T = σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` (`:1240`) and `W_V^T = Ω` (`:1311`). This is a *joint constraint* on the standard-transformer parameter triple `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)`: it requires the triple to satisfy `(W_Q W_K^T)^T = σ⁻² W_V⁻¹`, i.e., `W_K W_Q^T W_V = σ⁻² I`. Standard transformers have no training-time mechanism that enforces this coupling. The gauge reduction therefore lands not on the *class* of standard transformers but on the *sub-class* of standard transformers whose learned `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` happen to satisfy this `Ω`-tying constraint. Blue's claim at `02_blue_opening.md:21` that "many `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` triples realize the same `Ω`" is the right form of statement but the wrong direction: yes, the gauge framework underdetermines `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` given `Ω`, but the *standard transformer's parameter space includes triples that no `Ω` satisfies* (any triple with `W_K W_Q^T W_V ≠ σ⁻² I` up to scale). Those triples are inaccessible to the gauge framework's constant-gauge reduction. The "exact reduction" therefore exhibits a strict sub-class structure, not the equivalence the claim asserts.

## Defense

My opening's load-bearing point is the rectangular-projection mismatch inherited from sub-claim C, sharpened by the parameter-space sub-class observation in (c) above.

Blue's strongest counter is the thin-SVD lift at `:1245–:1252`. That construction is real and the manuscript does provide it. The defense gap is that the lift introduces *additional learnable structure* (`U_V^a`, the isometric column subspace) that the gauge identification `W_V^T ≡ Ω` does not pin down. Under the constant-gauge specialization, `Ω` is a single `GL(d_head)` element; lifting to ambient `W_V^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` requires choosing `U_V^a`. The manuscript does not specify how `U_V^a` is learned, and the gauge framework's variational free energy at `CLAUDE.md` ("Free energy" canonical form) has no term that depends on `U_V^a` independently of `Ω`. The thin-SVD lift therefore introduces a parameter (`U_V^a`) for which the framework provides no learning signal. Either (i) `U_V^a` is fixed at init (a constraint the standard does not have, since standard `W_V` is unconstrained rectangular), or (ii) `U_V^a` is rolled into a downstream `W_O`-like step as blue suggests at `02_blue_opening.md:29`, in which case the "exact reduction at the value aggregation step" is incomplete — the equivalence holds only after composition with `W_O`, which is a different identification at a different point in the architecture.

The relabeling character of the absorption matters because the claim under debate is whether `Ω` reduces to `W_V` *exactly*. Exact reduction between two architectures requires a bijection between their parameter spaces that commutes with their training dynamics (a standard reduction notion, applied throughout `[Vaswani2017 §3.2.1]`-style equivalence arguments). The gauge reduction provides a map at a single configuration (`W_V^T ≡ Ω`); it does not provide the bijection required for "exact reduction." This is the same gap sub-claim C's evidence pack flags at `01_evidence.md:6` of the parent debate.

The attention-gauge / value-gauge collapse closes the case. Manuscript `:1863` (cited at `01_evidence.md:32`): "factoring the gauge transport into an attention gauge and a value gauge that need not coincide ... This decomposition is supported (but not required) by the full framework." The constant-gauge §5.2.3 reduction picks the most-collapsed sub-case (single `Ω` for both). Standard transformers do not occupy this sub-case by construction — they occupy a *strict superset* in parameter space (three independent matrices, no `Ω`-tying constraint). The reduction therefore points from gauge-framework-with-constant-gauge to a *strict sub-class* of standard transformers, not to the class of standard transformers itself. Blue's framing of "the full framework permits ≠, the reduction picks =, standard transformers occupy =" inverts this: standard transformers occupy ⊇, the gauge reduction lands in ⊆, and the asymmetry is what the claim suppresses.

The reduction is exact as an *injection* from the constant-gauge gauge framework into the parameter space of standard transformers. It is not exact as the *equivalence* the claim asserts.
