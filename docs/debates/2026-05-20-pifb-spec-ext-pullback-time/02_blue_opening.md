# Blue Opening — pifb-spec-ext-pullback-time

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest red attack is that registering speculation in a section-opening Outlook frame plus four in-place disclaimers does not, on its own, make every interior identification correctly calibrated; specifically, the "consistent with relational and emergent approaches" sentence at line 2536 risks being read as substantive (claiming Page-Wootters / Rovelli / Connes-Rovelli share the framework's opposite-direction-of-motion behavior), the line-2611 bundle-metric construction is presented with subsection emphasis but then immediately disclosed at 2645 to be gauge-non-invariant (so the "fiber-respecting metric on $E_q$" label at 2624 is technically a within-gauge metric, not a metric on the bundle in the gauge-invariant sense), and the three-tier identification of phenomenal spatial structure with $G^{(s)}$ at 2672 is a substantive metaphysical commitment that the manuscript's "We propose" register does not by itself discharge.

## Position

The first three subsections of §Speculative Extensions (lines 2504-2695) are correctly calibrated as speculative-but-mathematically-explicit extensions when read against the standard canon of bundle geometry [Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 Vol. I, Ch. II §1, §5; Nakahara 2003 §10.3], information geometry [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §2.1-§2.2; Rao 1945], and Killing-form theory [Helgason 1978 Ch. II §6, §8]; the Outlook bracket at line 2507 is operative, every load-bearing identification carries a paragraph-local disclaimer that an external-canon reader can verify, the bundle-metric construction is mathematically standard as a connection-dependent horizontal-vertical decomposition on an associated bundle, and the three-tier reading at 2663-2676 uses interpretive register ("We propose", "is most naturally identified with", "within the manuscript's interpretive frame") rather than derivation register. Two cosmetic editorial defects survive: the inline `\subsection` at line 2536 jammed onto the end of the preceding paragraph, and the self-reference at line 2534 to `Section~\ref{sec:fisher_arc_length}` while sitting inside that section (label at 2526). These are presentation defects that do not affect the calibration claim.

## Evidence

**E1. The Outlook bracket at 2507 is explicit and binding.** Line 2507 reads "the framework no longer makes empirically falsifiable predictions in the sense set out in Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations}" and "Readers seeking the empirically anchored content of the framework should read this section as Outlook rather than as Results." The forward-reference label `sec:scope_limitations` exists at line 134 of the same manuscript (verified). This is the structural bracket every interior claim operates under, and it is registered with the language [Friston2010] uses for non-empirical extensions of variational free-energy formalism — programmatic rather than testable.

**E2. The Dimensional disclaimer at 2513 carries the load.** "$\tau_i$ is a *dimensionless* count parameter labeling positions on belief-update trajectories; it has no SI units of seconds. The assignment $\Delta\tau_i = \Delta I_i / (1\text{ bit})$ makes $\tau_i$ dimensionless by construction since KL divergence is already dimensionless and we normalize by the reference unit '1 bit'." This is correct dimensional analysis: $\mathrm{KL}(q\|p)$ is a dimensionless number on the natural-log scale [Cover-Thomas 2006 §2.3]; dividing by the unit "1 bit" produces a dimensionless count. The disclaimer further says "Where later passages invoke a Planck time $t_P$ or speak of connections to physical time, those references involve a $t$ with SI units; the connection is speculative and readers should not infer that $\tau$ and $t$ have the same dimensionality." This pre-empts the dimensional-conflation reading of the 2521-2523 bit-Planck paragraph.

**E3. The bit-Planck identification at 2523 is explicitly registered as speculative.** "This is highly speculative and requires substantial development to make rigorous. But it suggests an avenue for connecting our information-geometric framework to quantum gravity." The 2521 setup correctly states the Planck-time definition $t_P = \sqrt{\hbar G/c^5} \sim 10^{-43}$ s [Misner-Thorne-Wheeler 1973 §43.4]. The identification $t_P \sim \hbar/E_P \sim 1\text{ bit}/(\text{max info rate})$ is heuristic but is presented as an "avenue", not a derivation; this is the same epistemic register Lloyd 2002 uses when bounding the computational capacity of the universe by Planck-scale arguments, and Lloyd is cited at 2517.

**E4. The Fisher arc length disclaimer at 2534 is mathematically substantive, not rhetorical.** The Fisher-Rao metric on a smoothly parameterized family of probability distributions is positive-definite Riemannian [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 Thm 2.1, §2.2]; relativistic proper time is the line element of an indefinite Lorentzian metric on spacetime [Misner-Thorne-Wheeler 1973 §1.5]. These are mathematically distinct objects with different signatures, and the framework's "opposite directions with motion" observation follows from the elementary fact that an agent doing more belief updating accumulates *more* Fisher arc length, whereas a fast-moving observer accumulates *less* proper time (time dilation, $d\tau = dt\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}$). The 2534 disclaimer registers this mismatch explicitly: "We do not identify this quantity with relativistic proper time." This is calibrated by external standard.

**E5. The "consistent with relational and emergent approaches" framing at 2536 is narrow, not substantive.** The sentence reads "consistent with relational and emergent approaches to time in quantum gravity and foundations of physics. Time is not absolute background but rather emerges from the accumulated information updates each agent performs." Read in context, the consistency claim is precisely the no-external-background-time property — the *relational character* shared with Page-Wootters 1983 (time recovered from clock-rest entanglement), Rovelli 2004 (time as inter-system correlation), Connes-Rovelli 1994 (modular flow as state-intrinsic time). The opposite-direction-of-motion property at 2534 is registered at the immediately preceding paragraph as a *difference* from relativistic proper time, and the 2517 passage explicitly differentiates the construction from each of the cited proposals ("The construction here differs from each of these in placing the temporal parameter on the agent's own belief trajectory"). The "consistent" claim is therefore relational-sense consistency, not dilation-sense consistency.

**E6. The bundle-metric construction at 2611 is textbook.** [Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 Vol. I, Ch. II §1, §5] develops the horizontal-vertical decomposition $T_{(c,q)} E = H \oplus V$ on an associated bundle equipped with a connection on the underlying principal bundle, and [Nakahara 2003 §10.3, §10.4] gives the standard construction of a fiber-bundle metric as a connection-dependent sum of a fiber metric (vertical block) and a base-metric component (horizontal block) pulled through the connection. The framework's Eq. eq:bundle_metric at 2611, $g_{E_q} = g_\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{tw}}(\pi_* X_H, \pi_* Y_H) + g_\mathcal{B}(X_V, Y_V)$, is exactly this construction with $g_\mathcal{B}$ the Fisher-Rao fiber metric and $g_\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{tw}}(c) = \kappa(A^{(i)}_\mu(c), A^{(i)}_\nu(c))$ the horizontal block built from the connection one-form. That such a bundle metric depends on the choice of connection is standard [Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 Vol. I, Ch. II §6] — connection-dependent geometric objects on bundles are the norm, not a defect. The manuscript labels the construction "tw" rather than "YM" specifically to keep it distinct from the gauge-invariant Yang-Mills curvature invariant (2624), and the 2645 disclosure routes gauge-invariant content through the Haar-averaged consensus metric of sec:consensus_metric (label verified at 2843).

**E7. The piecewise $\kappa$ convention at 2620-2623 matches Killing-form canon.** [Helgason 1978 Ch. II §6, Prop. 6.6] establishes that the Killing form $B(X,Y) = \mathrm{tr}(\mathrm{ad}_X \, \mathrm{ad}_Y)$ is negative-definite on the compact real form of a semisimple Lie algebra and indefinite on non-compact forms. For $\mathfrak{so}(K)$ with $K > 2$, the Killing form evaluates to $B(X,Y) = (K-2)\mathrm{tr}(XY)$ restricted to skew-symmetric matrices, which is proportional to $-\mathrm{tr}(XY)$ and positive-definite [Helgason 1978 Ch. III §8]; this is exactly the manuscript's compact-case convention at 2621. For non-compact $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$, the bilinear form $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ is indefinite [Helgason 1978 Ch. III §8]; this is exactly the manuscript's non-compact-case convention at 2622. The 2643 footnote justifying the sign choice — "for skew-symmetric $A^{(i)} \in \mathfrak{so}(N)$ one has $\mathrm{tr}(A^2) \le 0$, so a bare $+\mathrm{tr}(A^{(i)}_\mu A^{(i)}_\nu)$ would produce a negative-semidefinite horizontal contribution" — is sign-correct linear algebra.

**E8. The 2645-2646 gauge-invariance disclosure is mathematically tight.** Under $U_i \to U_i g(c)$, the connection $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1} dU_i$ transforms as $A^{(i)} \to g^{-1} A^{(i)} g + g^{-1} dg$ [Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 Vol. I, Ch. II §1, Prop. 1.4]. The Maurer-Cartan piece $g^{-1} dg$ is the source of the cross-terms the manuscript lists. The disclosure is then routed to consensus_metric (label at 2843) and explicitly says "We do not attempt to absorb this non-invariance by appealing to the Yang-Mills invariant $\mathrm{tr}(F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})$" — the manuscript names its own escape hatches and refuses to use them, which is the strict register.

**E9. The three-tier reading at 2663-2676 uses interpretive register, not derivation register.** Line 2672: "We propose that what an agent phenomenally experiences as 'spatial structure' or 'ambient geometry' is most naturally identified with the structural tier $G_i^{(s)}$" — followed by "within the manuscript's interpretive frame and conditional on the regulator caveat of Section~\ref{sec:consensus_metric}, there is no agent-independent metric on $\mathcal{C}$, only the family of agent-dependent induced metrics arising from their slow-timescale model parameters." The "We propose" / "most naturally identified" / "interpretive frame" language explicitly marks an interpretive identification, not a derivation. The connection to Kant (form of intuition), Wheeler (participatory), Clark-Seth (controlled hallucination / predictive processing), Hoffman (Interface Theory), and hierarchical active inference [Friston et al. 2017] at 2674 is appropriate hedging by citation: each is an interpretive framework the identification draws on, none is offered as a derivation source.

**E10. The toy-model disclaimer at 2585 is the strongest in the entire section.** "We emphasize this is a toy model demonstration that spacetime-like geometry can emerge from information, not a claim that this is how physical spacetime actually arises... The signature problem is not a minor technical detail but the central unsolved challenge preventing this framework from genuinely explaining relativistic spacetime rather than merely producing toy models with spacetime-like features... We acknowledge that the foundation remains incomplete." This is unambiguous epistemic register and self-falsifies any reading of the subsequent pullback construction as a substantive claim about physical spacetime.

**E11. The Gaussian $\mathbb{R}^2$ example at 2680-2694 is canonical Fisher-Rao on Gaussian families.** [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §2.4] gives the Fisher information matrix for a Gaussian as the manuscript's Eq. at 2683. The isotropic-constant-$\Sigma$ reduction $G^{(q)}_{\mu\nu}(c) = (1/\sigma^2)(\partial_\mu \mu_i)\cdot(\partial_\nu\mu_i)$ at 2691 is the standard score-form Fisher-Rao pullback on a location family. The phenomenological "high certainty appears larger" framing at 2694 is the standard reading of Fisher-Rao on a Gaussian location family: small $\sigma$ makes nearby means distinguishable, hence the conformal factor magnification — this is Amari's "statistical distinguishability magnifies with precision" reading [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §3.1], not framework-specific interpretation.

**E12. Concessions registered up front.** The line-2536 inline `\subsection{Natural Gradient Dynamics on Statistical Manifolds}` jammed onto the end of the preceding paragraph is a real presentation defect (verified by grep on `\subsection\{Natural Gradient Dynamics`). The line-2534 forward-reference `Section~\ref{sec:fisher_arc_length}` is a self-reference to its own enclosing section (label at 2526) and should point to sec:signature_resolution or sec:worked_signature, both of which exist (labels at 2697 and 2741 verified). Both are cosmetic editorial defects: a missing `\par` or blank line before the subsection at 2536, and a target-correction for the ref at 2534. Neither affects substantive calibration of the claim being defended; both should be fixed in a presentation pass.

## Falsification conditions

This blue position is wrong if any of the following holds:

1. **Disclaimer absence or contradiction.** If any of the four named in-place disclaimers — Dimensional 2513, bit-Planck 2523, Fisher arc length 2534, toy-model 2585 — is absent on its line, or contradicted by adjacent material in the same paragraph. (Verified present and self-consistent.)

2. **Bundle-metric construction is non-standard.** If [Kobayashi-Nomizu 1963 Vol. I, Ch. II §5] or [Nakahara 2003 §10.3] do not support connection-dependent bundle metrics as a standard construction on associated bundles — i.e., if the gauge-non-invariance of the horizontal block alone disqualifies the object from being called a bundle metric in the textbook sense. (Canon supports the construction; gauge non-invariance is standard for connection-dependent geometric objects.)

3. **Killing-form sign convention is incorrect.** If [Helgason 1978 Ch. II §6 / Ch. III §8] does not support the compact-form / non-compact-form sign-of-$\kappa$ split at 2621-2622, or if the manuscript's sign at 2643 is linear-algebraically wrong. (Verified: compact form gives positive-definite $-\mathrm{tr}(XY)$ on skew-symmetric; non-compact form gives indefinite $+\mathrm{tr}(XY)$; the 2643 footnote argument is sign-correct.)

4. **"Consistent with relational and emergent" at 2536 is substantive overreach.** If the sentence is read as claiming Page-Wootters / Rovelli / Connes-Rovelli share the framework's *opposite-direction-of-motion* behavior, rather than the *relational* (no external background) character. The 2515-2517 passage explicitly differentiates the construction; the 2534 sentence explicitly registers the direction-of-motion difference; the 2536 sentence is bound by these adjacent paragraphs. If a fair reading nonetheless attributes the dilation behavior, this is a calibration miss and the claim fails on this sub-point.

5. **Three-tier identification is derivation-register, not interpretive-register.** If the 2672 paragraph is found to use derivation language ("therefore", "implies", "follows from") rather than interpretive language ("We propose", "most naturally identified", "interpretive frame"). The phrase "within the manuscript's interpretive frame" is the explicit register marker; if it is removed, the claim fails. (Verified present.)

6. **Forward-reference graph is broken.** If `sec:scope_limitations` (134), `sec:consensus_metric` (2843), `sec:signature_resolution` (2697), or `sec:worked_signature` (2741) do not exist in the manuscript, the Outlook bracket and gauge-invariance routing fail. (All four labels verified present.)

7. **A material defect beyond the two conceded cosmetic ones.** If red identifies a substantive miscalibration — a derivation-register claim presented without disclaimer, a dimensional error in the bit-Planck setup, a mathematical error in the bundle-metric construction, a signature error in $\kappa$, an interpretive identification not registered as interpretive — this position fails for that defect. The two cosmetic defects already conceded (inline subsection at 2536; self-reference at 2534) do not, individually or jointly, falsify the section-level calibration claim.

8. **The Outlook bracket is too distant to operate over the interior subsections.** If a fair reading holds that the 2507 framing does not reach the line-2611 bundle-metric construction or the line-2672 three-tier identification — i.e., that "Outlook" applies only to the Time subsection and not to the Pullback Mechanism subsections — this position fails. The 2507 wording "the subsections that follow" is plural and explicitly enumerates "time, geometric signature, observer-relativity, dimensional structure, and the status of physical quantities" as the section's targets, which textually covers all three subsections under debate.

The judge's verdict pivots on (4), (5), (7), and (8). On (4) and (5), the manuscript's interior disclaimers are textually present and self-consistent; on (7), only cosmetic defects survive on careful reading; on (8), the section opener is grammatically and substantively a plural bracket. If the judge accepts all four, BLUE_WINS. If the judge finds (4), (5), or (7) breached on a non-cosmetic point, RED_WINS_NARROW with a specific tightening required. Either outcome is decided by external canon, not by manuscript self-citation.
