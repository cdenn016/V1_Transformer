# Red Opening — pifb-spec-ext-pullback-time

## Steelman (opposing position)

The first three subsections of §Speculative Extensions are correctly calibrated: each substantive claim carries an in-place disclaimer (dimensional disclaimer at 2513, "highly speculative" label at 2523, "We do not identify this quantity with relativistic proper time" at 2534, toy-model disclaimer at 2585, gauge-invariance disclosure at 2645-2646, "We propose" register at 2672), the section opens with an explicit Outlook framing at 2507, and the bundle-metric construction is mathematically rigorous as a within-agent-gauge-fixing pullback with the gauge-invariant content explicitly routed through `sec:consensus_metric`.

## Position

The first three subsections are *substantively* well-hedged at the load-bearing claim sites, but the calibration breaks at three specific seams: (i) a presentation defect at line 2536 where `\subsection{Natural Gradient Dynamics on Statistical Manifolds}` is jammed onto the same line as the preceding paragraph; (ii) the second sentence of 2534-2536 ("consistent with relational and emergent approaches to time in quantum gravity and foundations of physics") understates a genuine divergence between Fisher arc length and the cited Page-Wootters / Rovelli / Connes-Rovelli proposals; and (iii) the bare phrase "A Bundle Metric on the Associated Bundle" in the 2601 subsection heading plus the unqualified Eq.~`eq:bundle_metric` label at 2612 misalign with the 2645-2646 disclosure that the horizontal block is not gauge-invariant. These are calibration leaks, not falsifications of the framework's mathematical core.

## Evidence

- **Presentation defect at 2536.** `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2536` — the line reads `...emerge from more fundamental information-theoretic dynamics.\subsection{Natural Gradient Dynamics on Statistical Manifolds}`. Every other subsection break in §Speculative Extensions (2509, 2519, 2525, 2580, 2587, 2601, 2663, 2678) is preceded by a blank line. LaTeX will render this because `\subsection` implicitly starts a new section, but the source is inconsistent with the surrounding pattern, and the absence of a blank line is a documented code-smell for spacing artifacts in compiled PDFs. The evidence pack at `01_evidence.md:19` already flags this defect.

- **Opposite-direction-with-motion is a divergence, not a shared property, of the cited emergent-time canon.** The manuscript at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2534` states correctly that "Fisher arc length and special-relativistic proper time have opposite character (positive-definite Riemannian versus indefinite Lorentzian) and run in opposite directions with motion." A fast-moving relativistic clock accumulates LESS proper time (time dilation: $d\tau = dt\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}$, the standard textbook form). A fast-updating agent in the bit-time framework accumulates MORE Fisher arc length $\Delta\tau = \int\sqrt{g_\mathcal{B}(\dot q, \dot q)}\,d\tau$ (2528-2533). The next sentence at 2536 reads "The formalism thus provides a natural notion of observer-dependent time arising from information geometry, consistent with relational and emergent approaches to time in quantum gravity and foundations of physics." The cited relational/emergent-time canon — Page-Wootters 1983 (clock-system entanglement recovers a Schrödinger-type parameter from a globally stationary state), Rovelli 2004 (relational time between subsystems, no inversion under motion), Connes-Rovelli 1994 (modular flow of a state's von Neumann algebra, thermodynamically forward) [`01_evidence.md:53-55`] — does not share the opposite-direction-with-motion feature. Page-Wootters' recovered clock parameter maps to external Schrödinger time in the limit where the clock subsystem decouples, so a clock moving relativistically slowly still shows the textbook time-dilation direction, not the opposite. The 2534 disclaimer covers the Fisher-vs-proper-time direct identification but the 2536 sentence still claims broad compatibility with a canon whose members do *not* have the inversion-under-motion feature. Calibration leak: "consistent with" elides a genuine disanalogy.

- **The bundle-metric framing at 2601/2612 versus the disclosure at 2645-2646.** The subsection title at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2601` is "A Bundle Metric on the Associated Bundle." Equation~`eq:bundle_metric` at 2611-2612 is presented unqualified. In the canonical Kobayashi-Nomizu sense [`01_evidence.md:65-66`; *Foundations of Differential Geometry* Vol. 1, Ch. III on bundle connections], a metric on a fiber bundle is a section of $T^*E \otimes T^*E$ that does not depend on a choice of trivialization or gauge fixing. The horizontal block $g_{\mathcal{C},\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{tw}}(c) = \kappa(A_\mu^{(i)}, A_\nu^{(i)})$ at 2615-2616 depends on the agent-specific connection $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1} dU_i$ — and the 2645-2646 paragraph admits this is *not* gauge-invariant. The 2624 sentence does include the parenthetical "(see the gauge-invariance disclosure below)" and "agent-frame-dependent in the same sense as the section itself," which partially defuses the tension. The remaining leak is the bare subsection title at 2601 and the bare equation label "eq:bundle_metric": a reader skimming the heading and the boxed equation will register a "bundle metric" in the differential-geometry sense, then encounter the disclosure four paragraphs later. The construction is internally consistent under the within-an-agent's-gauge-fixing reading made explicit at 2646; the calibration leak is in the unqualified title and label.
