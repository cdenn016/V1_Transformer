## Core attack

Blue treats lines 590, 599, and 606 as three independent formula errors patchable by three independent rewrites. They are not independent. The line-599 formula defines the differential `d exp_φ(T_a)` that the line-606 pullback metric is constructed from. The line-606 formula reads `G_{ab}(φ) = ⟨Ψ(ad_φ)(T_a), Ψ(ad_φ)(T_b)⟩_G`, justified in the chapter only by the line-599 identity `d exp_φ(T_a) = exp(φ) · Ψ(ad_φ)(T_a)` with `Ψ(z) = (e^z - 1)/z`. Blue's own finite-difference table at K=3 / ‖φ‖ ≈ 0.7 establishes that this identity has absolute error 1.46 against the true Fréchet derivative, while the canonical right- and left-trivialized forms each match FD to ~1e-10. Therefore the construction at line 606 inherits the line-599 error: even if a reader corrects only the trivialization symbol (Concession A's notational unification), the pullback formula is still wrong by the position-dependent factor `exp(φ)^⊤ exp(φ)` that I flagged in Issue 4 and that blue conceded at the third bullet of the ζ refutation. This is cascading mathematical error, not five scoped editorial fixes.

The consequence for §C.5 mode 4 (pullback natural gradient preconditioner) is that the natural-gradient preconditioner derived from the chapter formulas does not implement the Frobenius-pulled-back metric the chapter says it implements. Per blue's own derivation at the ζ refutation (line 88-92 of `02_blue_opening.md`),

```
G^{pullback}_{ab}(φ) = tr(Ψ(ad_φ)(T_a)^⊤ · exp(φ)^⊤ exp(φ) · Ψ(ad_φ)(T_b))
```

The line-606 formula drops `exp(φ)^⊤ exp(φ)`. For φ ∈ so(K), this drop is exact because `exp(so(K)) ⊂ O(K)` and `exp(φ)^⊤ exp(φ) = I`. For φ ∈ gl(K) — which is the stated scope of §C.5 per its title "Gauge Frame Preconditioning for **GL(K)**" — the drop is wrong by an amount that grows with the symmetric part of φ. The chapter never restricts the formula to the skew direction. Either the formula is wrong in its stated scope, or the chapter silently restricts to so(K) and then mode 4 is no longer a "GL(K) preconditioner."

The Killing-form normalization at line 590 is a separate defect, not editorial in blue's "single-equation fix" sense. Blue's sympy verification establishes `g(X, Y) = K tr(X Y^⊤) - tr(X) tr(Y)` from the line-587 definition `g(X, Y) = -(1/2) B(X, θ(Y))`. The line-590 formula `g̃_{ab} = 2K tr(T_a^⊤ T_b) - 2 tr(T_a) tr(T_b)` is exactly `2 · g`, i.e., off by a factor of 2 from the chapter's own preceding definition. A natural-gradient preconditioner is the *inverse* metric — `G^{-1}` — so a metric off by a factor of 2 produces a preconditioner step off by a factor of 1/2. Per [Amari1998 §4] the natural-gradient update is `Δφ = -η G^{-1} ∂F/∂φ`, so this is a 2× error in the effective learning rate of the Killing-metric mode (§C.5 mode 3). Blue calls this "editorial / single-equation fix"; I call it a mathematical mistake that propagates into the numerical preconditioner output by a finite, non-vanishing constant. The "fix" of choosing one normalization is a fix, but the *current* text is wrong, not ambiguous.

Counting the cascading line-599 → line-606 error as a single defect of §C.5 mode 4 (because mode 4 is one mode), the Killing-form normalization at line 590 as a separate defect of mode 3, the notation collision on `dexp_φ` between line 446 and lines 473 / 480 (Issue 1), and the cross-reference drift at line 556 (Issue 3), §C.5 alone carries three substantive defects across two of its four preconditioner modes plus an inherited notational drift from §C.2 / §C.2.2. This is larger in scope than the supplementary §B verdict and includes derivation-incorrect content (line 599 FD-falsified at 1.46 absolute), not only editorial gaps.
