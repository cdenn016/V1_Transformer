# Blue Rebuttal — single-config-mutual-exclusion

## Concession

I grant the load-bearing point. Red's evidence forces concession on the literal single-configuration reading the claim demands.

The two `__post_init__` guards in `transformer/vfe/config.py` intersect at the empty set on the `diagonal_covariance` axis. At `config.py:566-573`, `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'` raises `ValueError` unless `diagonal_covariance=True`. At `config.py:484-507`, the only path that reaches the exact full-covariance prior-bank sandwich at `transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:326-329` (gated on `not self.diagonal_covariance` at `prior_bank.py:310`) is `exact_full_cov_decode=True`, which raises unless `diagonal_covariance=False`. Both validations execute in dataclass `__post_init__`, so the rejection is structural: a single `VFEConfig` keyword set cannot simultaneously satisfy both clauses.

The defensive runtime guard at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:2042-2045` confirms that the omega-direct dispatch raises `RuntimeError` if `diagonal_covariance=False` reaches it, and the pairwise-Omega KL kernel at `transformer/vfe/non_flat.py:500-505` raises `NotImplementedError` on full-cov input (`sigma.dim() != 3`). The kernel itself, at `non_flat.py:539-541`, evaluates `sigma_target = einsum('bijkl,bijkl,bjl->bijk', Omega, Omega, sigma)` — the diagonal of `Ω diag(σ) Ω^T`, with the off-diagonal entries discarded. Per `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`, the canonical (0,2)-tensor transport under structure-group action `ρ(g)` is the two-sided sandwich `T → ρ(g) T ρ(g)^T`; the diagonal-truncation is not that. Under the single-configuration reading the claim demands, this path is not the sandwich transport, and no alternate configuration combines omega-direct with the exact sandwich.

The claim's blue-side falsification condition reads: "Red exhibits config.py validation code that rejects any single configuration combining `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'` with the exact (0,2)-tensor sandwich transport on Σ at all sites where Σ is transported." Red exhibited exactly that. The claim is not defensible on the evidence under its own falsification clause.

## Core attack

Red's universal statement that "the (0,2)-tensor sandwich `Ω Σ Ω^T` does not preserve the diagonal subspace under non-orthogonal Ω ∈ GL+(K)" is correct, but red overstates it as a blanket refutation of every degenerate sub-case. Two narrow counter-cases survive red's geometry:

First, the isotropic case Σ = σ²I_K is a fixed point of the SO(K) action: `Ω σ²I Ω^T = σ² Ω Ω^T = σ² I = Σ` for any orthogonal Ω. The diagonal subspace contains σ²I and the orthogonal-conjugation orbit through σ²I is the singleton {σ²I}. See `[Hall2015 §1.2.4]` and `[Frankel2011 Ch. 17]` on orthogonal conjugation of multiples of the identity. Red's "diagonal subspace is not invariant under non-orthogonal Ω" reading does not exclude this slice on its face.

Second, on the diagonal Cartan subalgebra of `gl(K)`, the abelian diagonal subgroup acts on diagonal Σ by entrywise rescaling, which stays diagonal. For Ω = diag(d_1, ..., d_K), `Ω diag(σ) Ω^T = diag(d_i² σ_i)`, exactly the diagonal kernel output.

These are narrow strikes. They do not rescue the claim — see Defense below — but red's argument is sharper if framed as "the diagonal subspace is not invariant under generic Ω", not as a universal impossibility. Per `[Absil2008 §3.6.2]`, the right-invariant retraction `Ω ← Ω · exp(η X)` on GL+(K) does not in general preserve the orthogonal submanifold, and the diagonal Cartan subgroup is a measure-zero slice in the omega-direct state space, so red's substantive claim survives once narrowed.

## Defense

The strongest available defense of the original claim is the isotropic + orthogonal slice: a `VFEConfig` with `isotropic_covariance=True`, `enforce_orthogonal=True`, `diagonal_covariance=True`, `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'`. On this slice, the algebraic identity `Ω σ²I Ω^T = σ²I` for orthogonal Ω means the diagonal kernel's numerical output coincides with the true sandwich. This defense fails for three reasons, each cited:

First, `enforce_orthogonal` is not threaded into the omega-direct dispatch. `_forward_omega_direct` at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1981` calls `compute_pairwise_omega_from_endpoints` at `e_step.py:2078, 2086` and `compute_kl_attention_pairwise` at `e_step.py:2093, 2143`. Neither receives `enforce_orthogonal`. The right-invariant Riemannian retraction `Ω ← Ω · exp(-η X_proj)` at `omega_direct.py` is performed on GL+(K), and per `[Absil2008 §3.6.2]` retractions onto the GL+(K) ambient manifold do not preserve the SO(K) submanifold without explicit Newton-Schulz projection. Setting the flag in `VFEConfig` is a no-op for the omega-direct kernel, so Ω drifts off SO(K) after the first E-step iteration and the algebraic identity that made the diagonal kernel coincide with the sandwich no longer applies.

Second, the prior-bank decode at `transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:333` computes `(exp_h_f32 ** 2 @ base_sigma_h)` — the diagonal-truncation algorithm `diag(A diag(s) A^T) = Σ_j A_{ij}² s_j`. The claim's text requires the exact (0,2)-tensor sandwich `Σ → Ω Σ Ω^T` "without diagonal approximation, anywhere in the forward path (E-step, encode, decode)." Even on the slice where the numerical output coincides with the sandwich for orthogonal Ω and isotropic σ, the algorithm at `prior_bank.py:333` is the diagonal-truncation `(A**2) @ s`, not the sandwich `A @ diag(s) @ A^T`. The claim is a universal quantifier over forward-path sites, asking for the sandwich algorithm at every site. The encode prior-bank evaluation is the diagonal-truncation algorithm on the omega-direct config and that does not change on any sub-slice of inputs.

Third, even granting numerical coincidence as the criterion (a weakened reading), the omega-direct path does not expose `exact_diagonal_transport`. At `transformer/vfe/non_flat.py:533-536` the comment is explicit: "The non-flat track does not expose an `exact_diagonal_transport` toggle — `compute_kl_attention_pairwise` rejects full-cov input at the top." So the route by which the φ-mode path can lift diagonal σ to a full (K,K) sandwich is closed off for omega-direct. The omega-direct kernel evaluates the diagonal-of-sandwich at every E-step pairwise alignment step (`e_step.py:2143`), and there is no toggle to switch it to the sandwich algorithm.

The claim asks for a single `VFEConfig` that simultaneously realizes the group-level Lie-group retraction `Ω^{t+1} = Ω^t · exp(-η ∇̃F)` from `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2566-2570` and the exact `Σ → Ω Σ Ω^T` from `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1619-1626` at every Σ-transport site without diagonal approximation. The `__post_init__` validation, the kernel `NotImplementedError`, and the runtime `RuntimeError` form a three-layer rejection of any such configuration. I cannot defend the claim under the current evidence. The strongest defense available is the isotropic + orthogonal slice, but it does not survive because (a) `enforce_orthogonal` is not threaded into the omega-direct kernel call sites, (b) the prior-bank diagonal kernel at `prior_bank.py:333` is the diagonal-truncation algorithm regardless of numerical coincidence on any input slice, and (c) no `exact_diagonal_transport` toggle exists on the omega-direct path.

The honest action that follows: either the single-configuration reading of the claim is wrong (the per-construction reading from the prior debate verdict at `docs/debates/2026-05-20-vfe-module-purity-for-pifb/04_verdict.md` is the correct reading), or the codebase needs a fourth configuration path that combines omega-direct with full-covariance Σ via a per-pair logdet-aware KL kernel — implementing the open `NotImplementedError` at `transformer/vfe/non_flat.py:500-505`.
