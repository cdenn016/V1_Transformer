# Red Rebuttal — pifb-discussion-kinetic-term

## Concession

Blue's third evidentiary block is granted: the body of `sec:mass` does register the stiffness-as-inertia postulate four times (lines 1849, 2013-2015, 2026, 2029-2030), and a reader who works through the body in order is not misled. The opening's challenge that the postulate is unannounced is conceded — it is announced, and at length. Blue's pre-concession on line 3093 (the "traditional approaches predictive models are considered fixed" wording) is also accepted as a corroborating point: the manuscript will need a one-line fix there regardless of the calibration verdict on 3095, and that edit can be enacted independently.

The surviving live disagreement is narrower: whether the Discussion subsection's compressed restatement at 3095 is itself precise enough to do its job for a reader who does not first read 200 lines of body, and whether the load-bearing literature anchor blue invokes ("Friston's own action-principle line of work") is the same construction as the manuscript's "kinetic-metric postulate."

## Core attack

Blue's load-bearing literature anchor — block (2) of the opening, citing Friston 2008 DEM, Friston et al. 2022 path-integrals, and Friston-Da Costa-Sajid et al. 2023 Phys Reports — does not support the manuscript's specific construction. Blue presents these three sources as the "principal author of FEP" reading "the variational principle as a candidate fundamental scaffolding," and treats that as endorsement of the kinetic-metric postulate at 3095. The three sources do not endorse the kinetic-metric postulate. They construct second-order or action-principle dynamics by routes that differ from the manuscript's identification of $\Sigma_p^{-1}$ as inertia tensor.

First, Friston 2008 DEM builds second-order behavior by *embedding* the state into generalized coordinates of motion $\tilde{\mu} = (\mu, \dot{\mu}, \ddot{\mu}, \ldots)$ and running a first-order variational filter on the embedded state [Friston, Trujillo-Barreto, Daunizeau 2008, *NeuroImage* 41(3) §"Generalized coordinates of motion"; canonical summary at Friston 2008 abstract: "variational filtering rests on a formulation in generalised coordinates of motion"]. The "action" in DEM is the path-integral of free energy over the trajectory $\int F\, dt$; the inertia of the dynamics enters through the empirical prior on serial derivatives of $\mu$ (the "generalized precision" $\tilde{\Pi}$ on the augmented state), not through identifying the Hessian of $F$ with a kinetic metric. DEM does not contain a velocity-quadratic Lagrangian term $\frac{1}{2}\dot{\mu}^T M \dot{\mu}$ with $M$ = potential Hessian.

Second, Friston-Da Costa-Sajid-Heins-Ueltzhöffer-Pavliotis-Parr 2023 *Physics Reports* "The free energy principle made simpler but not too simple" decomposes the flow via a generalized Helmholtz decomposition into solenoidal and gradient parts of a **first-order** stochastic vector field on state space [confirmed via Royal Society / PMC summaries 2026-05-20; see also Ramstead-Sakthivadivel et al. 2023 *Interface Focus* 13(3) "On Bayesian mechanics"]. The canonical form is $\dot{x} = (Q(x) - \Gamma(x))\,\nabla \log p(x) + \nabla \cdot (Q(x) - \Gamma(x))$ with $Q$ antisymmetric (solenoidal, conservative) and $\Gamma$ symmetric positive semidefinite (dissipative, gradient). The solenoidal part is a divergence-free vector field on state space; it is the source of stationary-state circulation. It is not a velocity-quadratic kinetic energy $\frac{1}{2}\dot{x}^T M \dot{x}$, and $M$ is nowhere identified with the Hessian of free energy. Calling this decomposition "the standard mechanical-action signature," as blue's opening does, conflates two different constructions: a first-order stochastic flow whose drift splits into rotational and dissipative parts is not a Lagrangian system with a kinetic-metric tensor.

Third, the manuscript at line 2031 writes a manifestly Lagrangian velocity-quadratic kinetic energy

$$\mathcal{M}_{\text{geom}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\dot{\mu}^T M_{\mu\mu} \dot{\mu} + \tfrac{1}{2}\operatorname{tr}[M_{\Sigma\Sigma}[\dot{\Sigma},\dot{\Sigma}]] + \tfrac{1}{2}\langle\dot{\phi},\dot{\phi}\rangle_{\mathfrak{g}}$$

with $M_{\mu\mu} = \Sigma_p^{-1}$ (the potential Hessian, identified with the kinetic metric by the postulate at 2029-2030). That is the standard Riemannian-mechanics form [Arnold 1989, *Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics*, 2nd ed., §22.2] in which $T = \tfrac{1}{2}g_{ij}(q)\dot{q}^i\dot{q}^j$ and the Euler-Lagrange equations produce $\ddot{q}$ at leading order in the inertia tensor. Friston 2008 DEM and Friston 2023 Phys Reports do not write this object. The manuscript's construction is not "the same reading the principal author of FEP has been advancing since 2008" — it is a separate construction that the FEP canon does not endorse.

The load-bearing weakness in blue's defense is therefore: the anchoring literature does the work of softening the postulate-status of 3095 only if blue can show the three FEP sources construct the same kinetic-metric identification. They do not. The three sources establish that FEP-as-action-principle is a real research program, which is true and not in dispute; they do not establish that *equating the kinetic metric with the Hessian of free energy* is part of that program.

This is the calibration gap. The Discussion at 3095 says the kinetic reintroduction "is contingent on the fundamental-physics reading" — but the "fundamental-physics reading" in the cited canon (DEM generalized motion; Helmholtz-decomposed stochastic flow) does not contain this kinetic-metric identification. The reader is left to assume the postulate is canonical FEP. It is not. A correctly calibrated Discussion hedge would name the postulate not as one downstream consequence of "the fundamental-physics reading" but as an *additional* postulate on top of that reading — analogous to the equivalence of inertial and gravitational mass that the manuscript itself raises at 3114-3116 in the very next subsection but does not connect to 3095.

## Defense

The Phase 2 opening identified the load-bearing assumption as: *the calibration of 3095 is acceptable because the body has already done the registering work*. The defense against blue's argument is that the body's registrations are about a *different* axis than the one 3095 needs to register.

The four body registrations blue cites (1849, 2013-2015, 2026, 2029-2030) all flag the *intra-framework* version of the postulate: "we are reusing the matrix $M_{\mu\mu}$ for both roles, this is a postulate not a derivation, the harmonic scaling is therefore structural rather than empirical." The manuscript line 1849 explicitly cites Arnold 1989: "Under standard Lagrangian mechanics a non-degenerate harmonic identification requires the inertia tensor and the potential Hessian to be operationally independent (0,2)-tensors at the equilibrium configuration; the present construction reuses the same matrix $M_{\mu\mu}$ for both roles." That registration is correct on its own terms and the body does its job.

What the body does *not* register, and what 3095 also does not register, is that this kinetic-metric identification is **not** the construction Friston has been advancing in the action-principle line of work. The body registers the postulate against the Arnold 1989 canon; the Discussion at 3095 invokes "the variational principle as a candidate fundamental scaffolding" as if the FEP-as-action-principle canon already contained the postulate, when in fact it constructs second-order or action-like dynamics by different mechanisms (generalized coordinates [Friston2008] and Helmholtz-decomposed first-order flow [Friston2023]). The Discussion hedge therefore subtly imports the legitimacy of one canon (FEP-as-action) to underwrite a postulate that canon does not contain.

Blue's anticipation of the triple-identification attack (block 5 of the opening) does not address this. Blue is correct that natural-gradient flow has no separate damping coefficient $\gamma$ as a distinct object from its Fisher metric — that is the Amari 1998 §2 form — but this strengthens rather than weakens the present attack. The standard active-inference dynamics is $\dot{\mu} = -F^{-1}\nabla \mathcal{F}$, a first-order Riemannian gradient flow with Fisher metric as preconditioner. The manuscript's Lagrangian form at 2031 inserts a $\frac{1}{2}\dot{\mu}^T M_{\mu\mu} \dot{\mu}$ kinetic term where $M_{\mu\mu} = \Sigma_p^{-1}$, producing second-order Euler-Lagrange equations $M_{\mu\mu}\ddot{\mu} = -\nabla \mathcal{F} - \text{Christoffel}$. The Fisher metric in Amari 1998 is a Riemannian metric on the statistical manifold whose role is to define steepest descent; reading the same object as a kinetic-metric in a Lagrangian $T = \tfrac{1}{2}\dot{q}^T g \dot{q}$ is a second postulate not contained in Amari's construction either. [Amari 1998, *Neural Computation* 10(2) §2, eq. (12): $\dot{\theta} = -G(\theta)^{-1} \nabla L(\theta)$ — a first-order Riemannian gradient flow, no kinetic term.]

The hedge at 3095 is therefore too weak on this specific axis. The correctly calibrated form would either (a) name the postulate as additional to the FEP-as-action canon rather than implicit in it, or (b) move the postulate-status flag out of the parenthetical inheritance phrase and into the main clause. A one-line edit that would close the calibration gap reads roughly: "this kinetic-metric identification is an additional postulate not contained in the standard FEP-as-action-principle constructions of Friston 2008 / Friston et al. 2023, which build second-order or action-like dynamics via generalized coordinates and Helmholtz-decomposed first-order flow respectively."

Blue's editorial-parsimony argument (the Discussion compresses a four-times-registered body caveat) does not apply, because the body never registers *this* particular caveat — the body registers the postulate against Arnold 1989 Lagrangian mechanics, not against the FEP-as-action canon. The Discussion is the only place in the manuscript where the FEP-as-action canon is invoked at all in connection with the kinetic-metric postulate, and it is invoked in a way that suggests the postulate is downstream of that canon rather than additional to it. Parsimony cannot compress a registration that was never made.
