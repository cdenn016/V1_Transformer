# Verdict — subclaim-A-flat-bundle

## Outcome

BLUE_WINS

## Decisive evidence

Theorem `thm:glk_invariance` at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:520-528` is universally quantified over `Ω ∈ GL(K)`, and the proof at `:531-555` uses only the algebraic identities `Ω^{-1}Ω = I` and `det(Ω^T) = det(Ω)`, both of which hold for any single invertible matrix including the identity and any non-identity constant. The `(det Ω)^2` cancellation at `:552`, the `Ω^{-T} Σ_Q^{-1} Ω^{-1} · Ω Σ_P Ω^T` trace cancellation at `:541`, and the `Ω^T Ω^{-T} = I` quadratic cancellation at `:547` all carry through under `Ω_{ij} = Ω`. Red concedes this explicitly at `03_red_rebuttal.md:7`: "the proof is valid for any Ω ∈ GL(K), including the identity ... The proof never uses pair-dependence." Combined with the supplementary admission at `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex:53` that the working framework already lives in the trivial-bundle subclass (so the constant-Ω specialization is a degeneration within the framework's own subclass, not an exotic boundary case), the flat-bundle limit is a well-defined specialization of the upstream theorem, and the upstream theorem itself is intact at that point in its domain.

## Reasoning

Sub-claim A as stated in `00_claim.md:11` and elaborated in `00_claim.md:17-21` asserts the **weak reading**, explicitly named in `01_evidence.md:66-68`: the upstream framework before specialization remains gauge-equivariant, and selecting one orbit representative does not retroactively invalidate the theorem at the other orbit points. Blue defends precisely this reading. Red attempts to relocate the dispute to the **operational** content of how the manuscript uses gauge vocabulary downstream — specifically the §5.7 summary at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1974` ("the learned projections W_Q, W_K are valid gauge transformations") and `:1962` ("variational natural-gradient descent on the gauge-equivariant free energy"). Red's stabilizer computation under the connection transformation law `Ω_{ij} → g_i Ω_{ij} g_j^{-1}` correctly shows that fixing `Ω_{ij} = Ω` leaves only the diagonal `g_i = g` subgroup as residual symmetry, not the full per-agent `G^N`. Both sides agree this proves the **strong reading** fails. Blue's `02_blue_opening.md:13` falsification condition (3) and red's `03_red_rebuttal.md:11` concession (3) align on this point.

The dispositive question is whether sub-claim A asserts the strong reading. It does not. The claim text and the evidence pack jointly define the weak reading as the load-bearing one. Red's strongest remaining attack — that the §5.7 summary at `:1974` and `:1978` invokes a per-agent equivariance the theorem does not deliver — is a real manuscript defect (blue concedes it at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:5`) but it is a defect of one paragraph's rhetoric, not of the limit's well-definedness or of the upstream theorem's continued validity at constant Ω. The §5.7 paragraph cites `thm:glk_invariance` to justify a stronger statement than the theorem proves; that conflation is separable from whether the limit `Ω_{ij} = Ω` is itself well-defined and whether the universally quantified theorem still holds at that point. The first question is the sub-claim under debate, and on it blue wins on the cited text of the theorem and the cited proof. The second question is a real but distinct issue.

Red's appeal to the canon (`external_canon_math.md` §2 on gauge invariance vs equivariance, the Nakahara/Frankel lineage on gauge-fixed Lagrangians) cuts against the strong reading — which blue does not defend — and not against the weak reading. Blue's Levi-Civita analogy at `02_blue_opening.md:33` is formally tight: a universal theorem instantiated at a particular point remains the theorem at that point. Red's response that this makes the manuscript's downstream "gauge-equivariant" language "operationally vacuous" is an attack on the manuscript's downstream rhetoric, not on the claim. The verdict on the claim follows the evidence on the claim.

## Action

Accept sub-claim A: the flat-bundle / constant-gauge limit `Ω_{ij} = Ω` is a well-defined specialization of the GL(K) framework, and the upstream theorem `thm:glk_invariance` remains valid at this point in its domain.

Open a separate debate or audit item targeting the §5.7 summary at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1974-1978` and the gauge-equivariance language at `:1962`. Both sides agree that paragraph commits a conflation: it invokes `thm:glk_invariance` (a single-Ω simultaneous push-forward statement, a generic f-divergence change-of-variables property per `external_canon_math.md` §1) to license a per-agent equivariance reading (`W_Q, W_K` as independent gauge transformations), and it describes the post-limit training dynamics as "gauge-equivariant" when the gauge-fixed object has only the diagonal residual symmetry under the manuscript's own connection transformation law at `:510`. The fix is editorial: either restate `:1974-1978` to claim only that `W_Q W_K^T = σ^{-2} Ω^{-T} ∈ GL(d_k)` (a codomain statement) without invoking per-agent equivariance of the post-limit object, or supply an independent per-agent equivariance argument with its own proof. The natural-gradient descriptor at `:1962` should likewise be qualified to identify which metric and which group action it refers to in the post-limit regime.
