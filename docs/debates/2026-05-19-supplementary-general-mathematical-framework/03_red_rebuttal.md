## Defense

My opening's load-bearing finding stands: the §General Mathematical Framework chapter, taken at the strict reading the title invites ("the entire section... is complete and mathematically/theoretically pure"), is not self-contained. It introduces statistical manifolds without supplying the Fisher-Rao metric, beliefs on Gaussian manifolds without supplying the Gaussian KL, gauge frames in a Lie algebra without supplying the differential of the matrix exponential, and a free-energy functional that is only named once (line 122, as "Eq.~7 of the main paper") and never written in the chapter that allegedly serves as its mathematical foundation. The deferral apparatus exists in the surrounding supplementary chapters but is invisible from inside lines 46–177, because the chapter contains no internal forward references to where its own deferred material lives.

Under blue's charitable reading — "the entire section" = the supplementary as a whole — the architecture works, sub-claims δ and ε land at §B/§C/§D, and the deferral is on the main-paper record at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1453`. Under the strict reading the chapter title invites — "§General Mathematical Framework is *itself* the complete foundational chapter" — sub-claims δ and ε fail to land inside lines 46–177 and there is no forward reference to where they do land.

The remedy is editorial and clear: (i) add forward references at the end of §General Mathematical Framework to "Appendix~\ref{app:covariance_dynamics} for the Gaussian KL and covariance dynamics, Appendix~\ref{app:phi_gradients} for the gauge-frame gradient, and Appendix~D for natural-gradient descent and manifold retraction," (ii) reconcile the $p_i \in \mathcal{B}_p$ ("model") line-76 definition with the main paper's $p_i(k_i)$ ("belief prior") at line 602 — either by renaming the supplementary's $\sigma^i_p$ as $\sigma^i_s$ (the model section) so that line 76 reads "$s_i(c) := \sigma^i_s(c) \in \mathcal{B}_p(c)$," or by relocating $p_i$ to $\mathcal{B}_q$ to match the main paper, (iii) qualify the §A.2.1 hierarchical meta-agent claim at lines 96–130 so that the introduction of $s_i$ at line 103 does not precede its definition, (iv) add a Čencov uniqueness citation at line 55 or line 615 where the Fisher-Rao metric is invoked. None of these is a derivational repair; each is editorial scope on a load-bearing primitive.

The compound claim therefore fails on the strict reading and survives on the charitable reading. The judge's task is to pick the reading. My residual position: under either reading, the $p_i$ / $\mathcal{B}_p$ / "model"-label inconsistency at line 76 against `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:602` is a real load-bearing defect that is not curable by deferral, because both manuscripts define their primitives in their own §3.1-equivalent sections and the primitives disagree.
