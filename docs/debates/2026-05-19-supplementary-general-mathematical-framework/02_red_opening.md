# Red Opening — supplementary-general-mathematical-framework

## Steelman (opposing position)

The supplementary's §General Mathematical Framework (lines 46–177) is a self-contained bundle-theoretic scaffold that is internally consistent on the four subsections it claims to cover (principal bundle, agents, transport operators, gauge frames and connections); the information-geometric and variational-EM material is developed in later supplementary sections (§B at line 178+, §C at line 388+, §D at line 611+), and the supplementary as a whole, read with charitable forward-referencing, supplies what §3–§5 of the main paper need.

## Position

The claim is false on its compound reading because sub-claims δ (natural-gradient information geometry) and ε (variational EM machinery) are not established within §General Mathematical Framework, and the section provides zero forward references to the supplementary sections where that material actually appears; the compound verdict therefore breaks on the worst sub-claim, as the user's own claim file instructs.

- **Direct textual absence (sub-claim ε).** The variational free energy functional `F = KL(q ‖ p) − E_q[log p(o|s)]` (the canonical form per `external_canon_inference.md §1, Form 3` and `[Friston2010]`) is not stated in §General Mathematical Framework. No E-step / M-step decomposition appears in lines 46–177, no softmax-β attention rule, no mixture-of-sources generative model, no recognition / generative split.

The user's own claim file at line 23 and line 25 allows the charitable reading "deferred elsewhere with adequate forward references"; the supplementary as written does not satisfy the "adequate forward references" condition the user explicitly attached.

- **Primary-source notational contradiction with the main paper (corroborating, sub-claim β).** Supplementary line 55 declares `B_p` the "model/prior fiber" and line 76 places `p_i(c) := σ^i_p(c) ∈ B_p(c)` — `p_i` on the model fiber. The supplementary's "model/prior fiber" labelling at line 55, combined with `p_i ∈ B_p` at line 76, places `p_i` on the wrong fiber relative to both the main paper and the Participatory paper. This is a primary-source contradiction between the supplementary and the main paper it is supposed to support, not an interpretation.

- **Corroborating absence (sub-claim ε implementation).** The cross-scale shadow construction `p_i^{(s)}(c) = Ω_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}](c)` that derives priors from the meta-agent's posterior is given in the Participatory paper at lines 539–548 as a "structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference." The supplementary's §General Mathematical Framework introduces `p_i` at line 76 as an independent primitive section with no derivation, no cross-scale shadow construction, and no forward reference to where such a derivation might live. The supplementary's §A.2.1 (lines 96–130) discusses meta-agents and renormalized coupling constants without specifying how priors are passed across scales.
