## Core attack

Red's R1 (Information-Geometric Structuralism is relabeling per the 3342 self-admission) and R4 (cross-scale composition relocates the gauge-fixing problem rather than solving it) both attack what the manuscript already concedes in place, and the attack is therefore confined to phrasing rather than substance.

On R1, red demands a false dichotomy at falsification condition 3: either name a "specific mathematical commitment" that Russellian neutral monism, panpsychism, and IIT all lack and state it on a manuscript line, or drop the coinage in favor of "Russellian neutral monism with a gauge-theoretic substrate." The mathematical commitment is stated at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3342`: the substrate is gauge-theoretic information geometry on principal bundles formalized in Section `sec:framework`, with phenomenal character indexed by the agent's gauge frame $\phi_i$ acting on a noumenal base manifold $\mathcal{C}$. This is a substrate specification with three commitments absent from Russell 1927 *The Analysis of Matter* and from Strawson 2006 (*Journal of Consciousness Studies* 13(10-11), 3-31): (i) the substrate is a principal $G$-bundle in the standard sense of [Nakahara2003 §10.1], so phenomenal character is indexed by a specific Lie-group orbit rather than by an unstructured "intrinsic nature"; (ii) the pullback operation $G_i = \sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal{B}}$ at 3324 is a specific covariant operation (pullback of a Riemannian metric along a section), so cross-frame variation is a calculable object rather than a posit; (iii) the framework excludes intrinsic phenomenal natures and excludes the $\Phi$-experience identity simultaneously, which Russellian neutral monism does not do (Russell 1927 commits to intrinsic phenomenal natures) and which IIT does not do (Tononi 2015 *Scholarpedia* 10(1):4164 commits to the $\Phi$-identity). The coinage names that two-sided exclusion plus a specific substrate. Red's reading of the 3342 admission "downstream of the shared relocation move" as a self-admission of relabeling treats the relocation move and the substrate specification as the same thing; they are not. Russell 1927, Strawson 2006, Goff 2017, and Tononi 2015 all share the relocation move (locating phenomenal character outside derivation-from-physical-dynamics); they differ on what the substrate is. The manuscript's contribution is the substrate, not the relocation. The honest 3342 admission states that the *novelty over the relocation* is vocabulary; it does not state that the *substrate specification* is vocabulary, and red's elision of that distinction is the load-bearing slip.

On R4, red's gauge-transformation argument is mathematically correct but is what the manuscript already registers. The composition $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)}) \exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$ transforms covariantly rather than invariantly under independent scale-wise gauge transformations $\phi_i^{(s)} \to \phi_i^{(s)} + \xi_i^{(s)}$ and $\phi_I^{(s+1)} \to \phi_I^{(s+1)} + \xi_I^{(s+1)}$; this is exactly the gauge-covariance the framework asserts everywhere else ([Nakahara2003 §10.3], $\Omega \to g_1 \Omega g_2^{-1}$ form), not a defect specific to the consciousness section. The 3331 text reads "the qualia assignment is then conjectured to be fixed by this concrete cross-scale constituent structure ... whether the variational dynamics actually pin the assignment in this way is an open research question, and we flag it as the principal piece of explanatory work the structuralist commitment still owes." This is the same admission red demands. Red's strengthening — that the structuralist route "relocates the gauge-fixing problem one level down without resolving it" — is consistent with the manuscript's "open research question" hedge and with 3344's "gauge invariance forbids any such pinning." The substantive disagreement red would need to land is that the existing 3331 hedge is mis-placed (it sits at the end of the paragraph, while the "is fixed once the constituents are" claim sits earlier and could be read as a resolution before the hedge arrives). That is the placement concession I granted above. It is an editorial fix, not a structural defect.

## Defense

The triple structure of disclaimers around the qualia identification at 3324 — the Scope paragraph at 3308-3309 ("Whether this corresponds to phenomenal experience is a philosophical interpretation beyond the formal results ... Readers who reject the interpretive move retain the formal apparatus intact"), the in-place hedge at 3324 itself ("the correspondence is interpretive, not derived"), and the explicit limits paragraph at 3344 ("the relocation does not explain why the information-geometric substrate has phenomenal character ... it does not predict which gauge frame produces which qualitative experience ... The framework provides mathematical vocabulary for discussing consciousness without explaining consciousness") — discharges the substantive prose between them in the specific sense that matters for adversarial review: a reader cannot extract from the subsection a claim the framework's own limits paragraph disowns. Red's R5 attack states that "the 3344 explanatory-limits paragraph admits 'the relocation does not explain why the information-geometric substrate has phenomenal character' — which is the bare statement that no explanatory work has been done by the identification, only labeling," and treats this as a contradiction with the 3324 identification. This is not a contradiction — it is the structure of an honest identity-based proposal in the cited literature. Russell 1927 *The Analysis of Matter* identifies phenomenal character with the intrinsic structure of the matter-substrate without explaining why intrinsic structure has phenomenal character (Russell ch. 37). Tononi 2015 *Scholarpedia* 10(1):4164 identifies phenomenal character with $\Phi$-structure without explaining why $\Phi$-structure has phenomenal character (Tononi 2015 §"The intrinsic existence axiom"). Goff 2017 *Consciousness and Fundamental Reality* (OUP) identifies phenomenal character with fundamental phenomenal properties without explaining why the substrate has phenomenal character at all (Goff 2017 ch. 7). In each case the identification is asserted and the explanation of why-the-substrate-has-phenomenal-character is registered as outside the proposal's scope. The manuscript at 3344 is doing the same move, in the same register, and red's characterization of it as a self-disowning admission misreads identity-based-proposal discourse in the cited primary literature.

On the empirical-predictions section at 3346-3354 (red's R6), the framing uses uniformly conditional verbs — "should exhibit" at 3348, "should reveal" at 3348, "might show diminished coupling" at 3348, "provides related but distinct predictions ... could be empirically distinguished" at 3350, "might correlate" at 3352, "could test" at 3352, "could illuminate" at 3354 — and cites the experimental designs of others (Mashour 2020 for anesthesia coupling, Luppi 2021 for vegetative-state network signatures, Gao 2017 / Shine 2019 for dimensionality of neural representations, Carhart-Harris 2014 / Milliere 2018 for altered-states phenomenology) rather than claiming the framework itself has executed those experiments. This is forward-pointing research-direction language, which is the correct register for a Discussion section. Red's complaint that "the empirical predictions at 3346-3354 are testable in principle but not yet operationalized in the framework's implementation" is a statement of what Discussion sections are — they propose tests that subsequent papers operationalize. The 3346-3354 framing is conditional throughout and does not claim otherwise.

The IIT comparison at 3350 (red's R3) is admittedly thin — a single sentence asserting "related but distinct predictions about integrated information $\Phi$" without engaging with the existence / composition / information / integration / exclusion axioms of Tononi 2015. A one-sentence sharpening to indicate which axioms the framework converges with (existence, composition through meta-agent formation) and which it diverges from (the specific $\Phi$-as-experience identity) would tighten this. This is a remand-with-edit, not a fatal strike — the differentiation at 3342 already states the load-bearing divergence point ("does not identify experience with a specific causal-structural invariant").

The minimum remediation that survives all of red's attacks is therefore narrower than red's proposed three-part patch: (a) add Friston 2018, Solms 2021, and Hohwy 2022 to the bibliography of the consciousness subsection with a one-paragraph statement of convergence and divergence — owed under the conceded R2 strike; (b) insert a small in-place parenthetical at 3331 making the "open research question" hedge visible at the "is fixed once the constituents are" sentence rather than only at paragraph end — owed under the conceded R4 placement strike; (c) optionally add one sentence at 3350 naming which IIT axioms the framework shares and which it does not — improves R3 but not required for the subsection's integrity. Red's third proposed patch — replacing "information-geometric structuralism" with "Russellian neutral monism with a gauge-theoretic substrate" or naming a "specific mathematical commitment" beyond vocabulary — is rejected on the substrate-specification argument above. The coinage names a specific gauge-theoretic substrate commitment that Russell 1927 does not make, and the manuscript's 3342 admission registers honest novelty-over-relocation as vocabulary while the substrate specification itself is mathematical content.

This claim is *not* defensible if (i) the substrate specification at 3342 turns out to be either unsupported by the formalism in `sec:framework` (e.g., if the principal-bundle commitment is announced in the consciousness section but not actually constructed in the framework section), or (ii) primary-source review of Russell 1927 establishes that Russellian neutral monism already commits to a structured-substrate of the gauge-theoretic kind rather than to unstructured intrinsic natures, in which case the differentiation collapses. I have not verified (i) at line-level granularity in `sec:framework` and have not direct-fetched Russell 1927 ch. 37; both checks are owed in any final adjudication. Under the present evidence, the subsection's hedges discharge the substantive claims in the sense that no reader can extract a position the framework's own limits paragraph at 3344 disowns, and the narrowed remediation above is the honest patch.
