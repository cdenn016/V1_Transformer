## What this evidence does NOT settle

1. Whether the "Information-Geometric Structuralism" coinage at 3341-3342 is substantively distinct from the existing positions (neutral monism, panpsychism, IIT) or whether the differentiation at 3342 ("does not posit phenomenal intrinsic natures," "does not make phenomenal properties fundamental," "does not identify experience with a specific causal-structural invariant") describes the absence of features rather than the presence of new substantive content. The manuscript's own 3342 admission "the differences are real but downstream of the shared relocation move" suggests the coinage is mostly relabeling.
2. Whether the IIT comparison at 3350 ("provides related but distinct predictions about integrated information $\Phi$") engages with the specific IIT axioms (existence, composition, information, integration, exclusion per Tononi 2015) or only with the high-level Φ-experience identity claim.
3. Whether the qualia-indeterminacy threading at 3330-3331 (claiming the cross-scale composition $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)})\exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$ fixes the qualia assignment) is mathematically coherent. The manuscript admits "whether the variational dynamics actually pin the assignment in this way is an open research question."
4. Whether the empirical predictions at 3346-3354 are operationalized to a level where they can actually be tested. The framework cites Mashour 2020, Luppi 2021, Gao 2017, Shine 2019 for the experimental designs but does not specify what the framework predicts that those experiments would falsify.
5. Whether the Friston 2018 / Solms 2021 / Hohwy 2022 FEP-consciousness literature should be cited. The framework rests on FEP / active inference; the FEP-consciousness literature is the closest comparison.
6. Whether the "highly speculative" tag at 3313 + the explicit-limits paragraph at 3344 are sufficient hedges for the qualia identification claims at 3324 ("the formal correlate of phenomenal character is the geometric structure of this induced metric").

Teams should verify points 1-5 against primary sources. Point 6 is the load-bearing calibration question.
