# Blue Opening — pifb-discussion-consciousness

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Consciousness subsection's hedges are decorative rather than load-bearing: a triple-disclaimer Scope paragraph + "highly speculative" + "interpretive reading" cannot discharge the substantive identity-style sentences sandwiched between them (e.g. line 3324, "the formal correlate of phenomenal character is the geometric structure of this induced metric"; line 3339, "There is, in this reading, something it is like to occupy gauge frame $\phi_i$ because that frame induces a specific metric $G_i$"; line 3342, the "Information-Geometric Structuralism" coinage that the author's own next clause admits is a "specific mathematical vocabulary... not a new mechanism"). On this reading, the subsection's calibration is performative — it hedges around the edges while still asserting a positive metaphysical thesis the framework cannot earn from its mathematics, and the IIT axiomatic literature (Tononi 2015 §3 axioms; Oizumi-Albantakis-Tononi 2014 IIT 3.0) is engaged at the level of slogan rather than substance.

## Position

The Consciousness subsection (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3305-3354`) is correctly calibrated. It is correctly calibrated in the precise sense that every substantive interpretive sentence is subordinated, at the same scale of prose, to an in-place qualifier (Scope paragraph 3308-3309; "interpretive, not derived" 3324; "We do not assert identity" 3326; "We are deliberate about that word" 3337; "We do not claim this resolves" 3339; "the differences are real but downstream of the shared relocation move" 3342; "must be stated plainly" 3344; conditional verbs throughout 3346-3354). The hedges are not perimeter disclaimers; they are interleaved with the claims they qualify. The novelty claimed is correctly limited to "mathematical vocabulary," not explanatory mechanism. The framework does not assert a Hard-Problem solution, an axiomatic engagement with IIT 3.0, or a derivation of phenomenal content — and it states each of those non-claims plainly in the prose.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong — i.e., the subsection is *not* correctly calibrated — if any of the following hold:

1. **The Scope bracket is not actually load-bearing.** If a reader can show that any sentence in `3311-3354` makes a substantive metaphysical claim that survives bracketing the entire interpretive layer (i.e., a claim about phenomenal experience that is not framed as "interpretive commentary on the formal apparatus"), the Scope paragraph fails its job. I have not found such a sentence; every candidate (3324, 3326, 3339, 3342, 3344) is paired with an in-place qualifier at the same prose scale.

2. **The Hard-Problem relocation collapses into a covert Hard-Problem solution.** If 3337-3339's "we offer a relocation, not a solution" is contradicted elsewhere in the subsection by a sentence that does claim derivation or solution, the relocation framing is dishonest. The candidate is 3339's "There is, in this reading, something it is like to occupy gauge frame $\phi_i$ because that frame induces a specific metric $G_i$" — but the "in this reading" clause and the immediately following "We do not claim this resolves the explanatory gap; it shifts it" closure pre-empt that reading.

3. **The Information-Geometric Structuralism coinage adds substantive metaphysical content beyond the relabeling the manuscript itself admits at 3342.** If the framework's claimed differences from neutral monism, panpsychism, and IIT (3342 lines 4-6) do explanatory work that the predecessor positions do not, then the manuscript is under-claiming. The opposite failure — that the coinage claims more than the framework earns — is the calibration-too-loud failure. The 3342 admission "the differences are real but downstream of the shared relocation move" matches the latter; the manuscript correctly limits its claim to vocabulary.

4. **The qualia-indeterminacy threading at 3330-3331 makes a mathematical claim that is provably false on the cited transport composition** $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)}) \exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$. If red can show, by direct computation on the framework's own definitions, that the cross-scale composition cannot fix the qualia assignment (e.g., because gauge invariance of the variational dynamics propagates through the composition and the cross-scale structure is itself gauge-equivalent to its transformed version), then "conjectured to be fixed" is mathematically untenable, and the paragraph would need to be downgraded from "open research question" to "negative result." This is a non-trivial mathematical check; I have not run it. The manuscript flags this as the open research question 3330-3331 closes on.

5. **The IIT comparison at 3350 misrepresents what Tononi 2015 / Oizumi-Albantakis-Tononi 2014 actually claim.** If "provides related but distinct predictions about integrated information $\Phi$" + "complementary perspectives that could be empirically distinguished" is contradicted by the IIT axiomatic literature — for example, if Tononi 2015 §2 explicitly precludes a complementary information-geometric perspective rather than tolerating one — then 3350 is over-claiming complementarity. Tononi 2015 *Scholarpedia* §4 frames IIT as an identity claim about the integrated cause-effect structure, not a closure of the field against gauge-theoretic substrate proposals; I read the manuscript's "complementary" as correctly scoped against this, but red could press it.

6. **The Solms 2021 / Friston 2018 / Hohwy 2022 FEP-consciousness literature is the closer comparison than Russell-Strawson-Goff-Chalmers-Tononi for the framework's consciousness claims.** If primary-source review of *The Hidden Spring* (Solms 2021) establishes that an FEP-substrate account of phenomenal experience is structurally closer to "information-geometric structuralism" than Russellian neutral monism is, the FEP-consciousness citation patch at 3341-3342 is required, not optional. This is the most plausible RED_WINS_NARROW outcome. I would concede the citation patch on that primary-source showing.

7. **The empirical predictions at 3346-3354 are operationalizable within the framework's own implementation in a way that the prose does not actually claim.** This is the opposite failure from red's typical attack: the predictions are *under*-stated, not over-stated, if the framework's `vfe/` or `aif/` module could compute $\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1}$ or the effective dimensionality of induced metrics on real neural data and the prose does not say so. The prose is correctly conservative here; this falsification condition is unlikely to fire, but I name it for completeness.

The single most likely falsifier is #6. The other six describe failures the manuscript's in-place hedging has already pre-empted, and red would need to break the hedge before reaching the underlying claim.
