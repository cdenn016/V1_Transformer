# Red Memo — info-geometer (Opening)

Mode: theory. Round: opening. Side: red. Claim: §Theory of `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` is rock solid and publication ready.

## Steelman of the claim (from this lens)

The strongest information-geometric reading is that §Theory uses the canonical apparatus correctly at every primitive: closed-form KL of Gaussians at Eq.~(\ref{eq:gaussian_kl}) on line 536 matches the textbook form ([Bishop 2006 §10.2, KingmaWelling2014 App. B](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6114)); Fisher–Rao metric on the Gaussian fiber is invoked with the correct block-diagonal $g_{\mu\mu}=\Sigma^{-1}$, $g_{\Sigma\Sigma}[V,W]=\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}(\Sigma^{-1}V\Sigma^{-1}W)$ at line 1555 with `[Amari2016]` cited; Chentsov / Cencov uniqueness is correctly attributed at line 516 with both the original 1982 finite-sample-space theorem and the continuous-parametric extension [Ay, Jost, Lê, Schwachhöfer 2017]; the natural-gradient flow $\tilde\nabla_q f = g_{\mathcal{B}}^{-1}\nabla_q f$ is named without claiming Adam-as-natural-gradient (the standard sloppiness, `external_canon_math.md:135`); the sandwich product $\Sigma \to \Omega\Sigma\Omega^\top$ matches [Nakahara 2003 §10.3] for the (2,0)-tensor reading of covariance under GL(K); the precision-as-(0,2) reading is given dually as $\Lambda\to\Omega^{-T}\Lambda\Omega^{-1}$ at Eq.~(\ref{eq:precision_transport}) on line 1894 with the explicit warning at line 1897 that "substituting the orthogonal form into the GL setting is a frequent source of error." This is exactly the level of care a publication-ready information-geometric construction requires at the primitive level.

## Strongest weakness from this lens

The "geometric necessity" claim at the heart of §1.16 (Figure caption at line 1411: "attention mechanisms are geometric necessities rather than arbitrary architectural choices") rests on a conflation of two distinct uniqueness results, and §Theory does not make either explicit.

**Uniqueness 1 — Cencov / Chentsov:** the Fisher metric is the *unique* (up to a positive scalar) Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold that is invariant under sufficient statistics ([Chentsov 1982; Ay-Jost-Lê-Schwachhöfer 2017; Bauer-Bruveris-Michor 2016 for non-parametric extension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chentsov%27s_theorem)). This is a uniqueness result for *the metric*; it does not select any particular *divergence* on the manifold (KL, Hellinger, Rényi, Bregman family — all are consistent with Fisher), and it certainly does not select any particular multi-agent *functional* built on those divergences. The Cencov reading licenses calling $g_{\mathcal{B}}$ "the natural geometry on probability spaces" — it does not license calling the full multi-agent canonical free energy "a geometric necessity."

**Uniqueness 2 — the f-divergence appendix theorem:** the manuscript at line 1252 ("Within the f-divergence class satisfying assumptions (i)–(iii) of Appendix~\ref{app:conditional_uniqueness}, the forward KL divergence $D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}q_j)$ is the unique f-divergence that preserves exponential-family closure under linear coupling and yields a consistent dual interpretation for the attention weights") and at line 1118 ("the forward KL divergence is the unique f-divergence that yields a consistent dual interpretation for the attention weights; the conditional uniqueness theorem and full proof are reproduced there"). This is a *conditional* uniqueness result — unique *within* a class defined by three assumptions: locality, linear coupling, exponential-family closure. The conditional theorem is in the appendix, not in §Theory; assumptions (i)–(iii) are not even displayed in §Theory; whether assumptions (i)–(iii) are independently motivated, or whether they are simply the assumptions that force KL to come out unique, is opaque from §Theory alone.

These two uniqueness results are not the same, and the figure caption at line 1411 does not distinguish them. The "geometric necessity" reading of attention is therefore underdetermined by §Theory: either (a) it means Cencov-uniqueness of Fisher → KL → softmax, which is too weak (Hellinger and Rényi also give Fisher-compatible divergences, and Sinkhorn-style entropy-regularized soft assignment yields softmax from many other starting energies — see for instance [Cuturi 2013, "Sinkhorn distances"](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2013/hash/af21d0c97db2e27e13572cbf59eb343d-Abstract.html) for the entropy-regularized optimal-transport derivation of the same softmax); or (b) it means the conditional-uniqueness theorem of Appendix C, which is *not* a derivation of attention as a necessity but as a unique stationary point under three structural assumptions. §Theory equivocates between the two readings.

A second info-geometric strike: the "natural-gradient flow" appeal in the structural-analogy table at line 1494 is overstated. The table identifies "Our Framework" with the variational principle "$\delta\mathcal{F}=0$ (natural-gradient flow; Fisher–Rao on $(\mu,\Sigma)$; Lie-group natural gradient on $G$)." Two problems:

1. Natural gradient on $(\mu,\Sigma)$ for Gaussian beliefs requires the *correct Fisher metric on the (μ,Σ) chart*. The block-diagonal form quoted at line 1555 is standard for the natural-parameter chart $(\Sigma^{-1}\mu, -\tfrac12\Sigma^{-1})$ of the exponential family, *not* for the mean-parameter chart $(\mu, \Sigma)$ used in the manuscript. In the mean-parameter chart the Fisher has cross-terms that are not zero, and the block-diagonal form is the (e-)natural-gradient form on the (e-)natural parameters. The distinction matters for whether the displayed gradient updates are natural gradients in the canonical sense. See [Amari & Nagaoka 2000, *Methods of Information Geometry*, Ch. 3] and [Amari 1998, "Natural gradient works efficiently in learning"] for the two dual affine charts and which Fisher form applies in each. §Theory does not flag the chart choice.
2. "Lie-group natural gradient on $G$" requires a bi-invariant Riemannian metric on $G$. The manuscript invokes the Killing form at line 2069 with the correct caveat that "the Killing form on $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$ is indefinite," then defers to "the position-dependent right-invariant form of Eq.~(\ref{eq:pullback_metric})" — which is in §1.4 of the §Pullback section, *not* in §Theory. The table at line 1494 asserts "Lie-group natural gradient" as if it were a delivered structural feature of the framework; what §Theory actually displays is the chart-coordinate first-order form $\partial\phi_i/\partial\tau = -\eta_\phi\tilde\nabla_{\phi_i}\mathcal{F}_{\text{frame}}$ at line 1578, with the Riemannian metric supplied off-stage.

## Primary-source citations

- Chentsov / Cencov 1982, *Statistical Decision Rules and Optimal Inference*, AMS Translations of Math Monographs vol. 53 — original uniqueness theorem on finite sample spaces.
- Ay, Jost, Lê, Schwachhöfer 2017, *Information Geometry*, Ergebnisse der Mathematik 64, Springer — continuous-parametric extension of Chentsov; §3 develops the canonical Fisher.
- Bauer, Bruveris, Michor 2016, "Uniqueness of the Fisher–Rao metric on the space of smooth densities," *Bull. London Math. Soc.* 48: 499–506 — non-parametric extension. Establishes that the uniqueness is for the metric.
- Amari & Nagaoka 2000, *Methods of Information Geometry*, AMS — dual affine connections, exponential and mixture flatness, the (e-) and (m-)natural parameter charts and their Fisher matrices.
- Amari 1998, "Natural gradient works efficiently in learning," *Neural Computation* 10: 251–276 — the canonical natural-gradient construction.
- Bishop 2006, *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning*, Springer, §10.2 — closed-form KL of Gaussians; mean-field Gamma-Normal stationary equations.
- Kingma & Welling 2014, "Auto-encoding variational Bayes," ICLR ([arXiv:1312.6114](https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6114)), App. B — closed-form Gaussian KL for VAE.
- Nakahara 2003, *Geometry, Topology and Physics*, 2nd ed., §10.3 — sandwich product for parallel transport of (0,2) and (2,0) tensors.
- Cuturi 2013, "Sinkhorn distances: lightspeed computation of optimal transport," NeurIPS — softmax from entropy-regularized optimal transport, showing softmax is *not* unique to a KL-of-Gaussians starting energy.

## Falsification condition

The position above is falsified if blue exhibits, in §Theory (not appendix, not companion paper): (i) an explicit disambiguation between Cencov-uniqueness-of-Fisher and conditional-uniqueness-of-KL-within-f-divergences, so the "geometric necessity" framing at line 1411 stops trading on the conflation; (ii) the assumptions (i)–(iii) of Appendix~C displayed inside §Theory, with a one-line argument for why each is independently motivated rather than reverse-engineered to deliver KL uniqueness; or (iii) a chart-specific identification of the Fisher metric the natural-gradient flow at line 1555 is running on, with the cross-terms between $(\mu,\Sigma)$ that the mean-parameter chart requires.

## Newly-discovered canon

- Amari 2016, *Information Geometry and Its Applications*, Applied Mathematical Sciences 194, Springer — modern integrated treatment. The dually-flat structure on exponential families is Ch. 2; the natural-gradient construction on Gaussian families with the precise chart-dependent Fisher matrix is in §4.3. The manuscript cites "[Amari2016]" at line 1555 without page or chapter; this is the chapter that matters.
- Pacheco & Sasai 2024, "f-divergences and their relations to functional inequalities and information geometry" ([arXiv:2402.05014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05014)) — modern review of the f-divergence class. Confirms the manuscript's class-of-divergences framing is a recognized but technical setting, and that uniqueness results within the class always carry assumption-list caveats.
- Cuturi 2013, "Sinkhorn distances," NeurIPS — the canonical reference establishing that entropy-regularized soft assignment yields softmax from many starting energies. Cited because if the manuscript's "geometric necessity" reading rested on softmax-from-energy-minimization with entropy, the same softmax is recovered from optimal-transport / Sinkhorn formulations with no information-geometric input at all.
