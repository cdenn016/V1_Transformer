## (ii) Strongest defense against red's core attack

Red's overall position rests on a conflation between "labeled within-framework interpretation" and "claim drift." These are different categories.

The textbook standard for what makes an analogy publication-acceptable rather than pseudoscientific is whether the author explicitly demarcates the empirical claim from the structural interpretation [Popper 1959, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §6 — demarcation by falsifiability of empirical content, not by absence of analogical or interpretive content]. A paragraph titled "Within-Framework Interpretation: Stiffness as Precision (Mass Analogy)" at line 2046 of the manuscript, opening with the sentence "the identification of the Hessian sector $[M_{\mu\mu}]$ with an 'effective mass' is interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass," is exactly the Popperian discipline. The TODO at line 1880 deferring the empirical $\omega^2 \propto m_{\text{eff}}^{-1}$ test to future work is *also* the Popperian discipline: it states explicitly that the construction *as given* does not supply an operationally independent test of its own scaling. That is the opposite of an unfalsifiable claim presented as derivation — it is a falsifiable claim that admits no test has been run.

A reviewer encountering "future work" markers in a 53-page theory section is reading a manuscript that admits its scope. This is the routine pattern across published theoretical papers in *J. Stat. Mech.*, *Information Geometry*, NeurIPS theory track. Sub-claim 6 of the operationalization reads "No TODO, no 'future work', no 'this requires further treatment' inside §Theory that would block reviewer acceptance" — the operative qualifier is "block reviewer acceptance," not "exist at all." Theory sections in published variational-inference papers routinely flag empirical discriminators for future work without blocking acceptance; the bar is whether the deferral is honest (it is here — the manuscript admits the construction does not supply the test) or evasive (it is not — the manuscript identifies exactly what operationally independent measurement would constitute the test).

Red did not cite a venue policy document, an editorial board statement, or a published methodological note establishing this exact threshold as binding. The threshold is conjunctive on three specific predicates (count ≥10, "load-bearing reduction step" classification, literal `\textbf{TODO}` token) chosen to be jointly difficult to match. A bar that no precedent can meet is not a bar the methodology of philosophy-of-science recognizes; it is an argument from absence dressed as a falsification condition.

The honest position is that the verifiable count by *strict load-bearing reduction* criterion is approximately 4–5 (lines 1209, 1607, 1615, 1818, 1875 carry the strongest delegations of derivational content), not 14. The remaining citations are provenance / convention / "adopts the form" / literal "see also" pointers — see the variational and gauge-theorist memos for the per-citation reclassification. Red's strike is real but the *quantitative* characterization is overstated.
