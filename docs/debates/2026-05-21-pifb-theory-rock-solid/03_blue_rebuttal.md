Under the Arnold-1989 generalized eigenvalue standard ([Arnold 1989, *Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics*, Springer GTM 60, Ch. 5 §22–25] — the generalized eigenvalue problem $Vv = \omega^2 Tv$ requires the inertia tensor $T$ and the potential Hessian $V$ to be independent positive-definite quadratic forms) this is a unit relation, not a Newtonian dispersion. The TODO at line 1880 deferring the empirical test of operationally independent $\omega$ and $m_{\text{eff}}$ to future work is a real deferral. This is conceded.

Broad point contested. Red's overall claim that these admissions falsify §Theory's "publication ready" status assumes that *any* TODO inside §Theory blocks reviewer acceptance and that *labeled* within-framework interpretation is a structural flaw rather than the publication-appropriate epistemic discipline. Both assumptions are disputed below.

## Defense

The Popperian standard for distinguishing publication-acceptable from publication-unacceptable analogies is whether the author explicitly demarcates empirical claims from structural interpretations [Popper 1959, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §6]. The manuscript's §1.19 satisfies this demarcation in three places. The subsection title at line 2046 reads "Within-Framework Interpretation: Stiffness as Precision (Mass Analogy)." The opening paragraph at lines 2047–2048 states: "the identification of the Hessian sector $[M_{\mu\mu}]$ with an 'effective mass' is interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass." Line 2064 states: "Under this identification the harmonic-oscillator scaling $\omega^2 \propto k/m$ is a definitional consequence of the postulate rather than an independent dynamical scaling." The TODO at line 1880 names the specific operationally independent measurement that would constitute a test ("fitting $\omega$ from the autocorrelation of a natural-gradient trajectory under a stiffness $M_{\mu\mu}$ that does not coincide with the kinetic metric"). A subsection whose title is "Within-Framework Interpretation," whose opening sentence calls the construction interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass, and whose deferred test specifies the operationally independent measurement that would falsify the scaling is not equivocating; it is doing scholarly demarcation.

The differential-geometric status of the Hessian as a stiffness on belief configuration space is independent of any kinetic-metric postulate [Marsden-Ratiu 2002, *Introduction to Mechanics and Symmetry*, 2nd ed., Springer TAM 17, §3]. The manuscript's second-variation computation at Eq. eq:effective_mass — $M_i = \bar\Lambda_{p_i} + \sum_k \beta_{ik}\tilde\Lambda_{q_k} + \sum_j \beta_{ji}\Lambda_{q_i} + \Lambda_{o_i}$ — is the standard Hessian of a sum of Gaussian KL terms [Kingma-Welling 2014, App. B; Bishop 2006 §10.1.3 for the per-pair pieces]. Read as a stiffness computation it is correct as stated; the mass labeling is the postulated overlay flagged at line 2046. This is publication-ready under any reviewer standard that distinguishes a derived stiffness Hessian from its interpretive labeling.

The appendix referenced from the body text at line 1252 — `app:conditional_uniqueness`, beginning at line 4258 — has now been read directly and yields a partial vindication and a partial refinement of the body-text claim. The appendix is **explicitly titled** "Conditional **Representation** Theorem for the Forward KL Divergence via Variational Duality" and opens at line 4261: "The result is a representation theorem rather than an unconditional uniqueness theorem." Line 4268 states: "We are not claiming that forward KL is uniquely required by 'well-behaved coordination' in any broader sense: weakening the target form (allowing other Boltzmann-type stationary solutions, or allowing the minimizer to leave the exponential family) reopens the divergence choice." The three assumptions at line 4272 are displayed in the form the body text at line 1252 claims: (i) locality / standard f-divergence form; (ii) linear coupling; (iii) exponential-family closure of the minimizer. Theorem 1 at line 4351 then proves the geometric-mean Boltzmann form is the unique f-divergence minimizer under these three assumptions, with the proof at lines 4369–4407 having the standard Csiszár-Liese-Vajda shape [Csiszár-Shields 2004, *Information Theory and Statistics: A Tutorial*, Foundations and Trends in Communications and Information Theory 1(4): 417–528, §4 — uniqueness within the f-divergence class under closure-class constraints]. This is *conditional* uniqueness within a divergence class under displayed assumptions, not Cencov-style metric uniqueness [Chentsov 1982; Bauer-Bruveris-Michor 2016, *Bull. London Math. Soc.* 48: 499–506]. Red's Cuturi 2013 [NeurIPS, "Sinkhorn distances"] correctly establishes that softmax-from-energy is also recoverable from entropy-regularized optimal transport; the two uniqueness statements live on different closure classes (f-divergences under linear-coupling closure vs OT under entropy regularization) and the manuscript does not contradict Cuturi.

Two refinements emerge from the appendix reading that strengthen rather than weaken the blue position. First, the proof at line 4357 introduces a **real-analyticity hypothesis on the generator** $f$ used in Step 3 to extend pointwise identification of $f'(t)$ to all of $(0, \infty)$ by analytic continuation — this is a fourth structural condition the body text at line 1252 does not enumerate among "(i)–(iii)." Second, the proof's richness lemma at line 4409 carries an acknowledged "global-normalizer caveat" that the appendix itself flags. The body-text count of three assumptions is therefore technically imprecise — the operative count for the full proof is four (the three displayed + the real-analyticity hypothesis), and the richness argument carries an admitted caveat. This is a minor revision (replace "(i)–(iii)" with "(i)–(iv)" in the body text and flag the real-analyticity hypothesis), not a structural collapse. The appendix is *honest about its own scope*: it labels itself a representation theorem rather than a uniqueness theorem, it acknowledges the real-analyticity strengthening, and it flags the global-normalizer caveat. Read together, body and appendix are consistent up to the (i)–(iii) vs (i)–(iv) enumeration discrepancy. Figure 1's caption remains the only place where the body's correct conditional-representation language is overstated to "geometric necessities."

Red's strongest version of Strike 3 would be: the body text says "three assumptions" but the appendix proof needs four, so the body text undercounts the assumption list and the conditional-uniqueness theorem is presented in §Theory as cleaner than it actually is. This is a real strike, narrower than the original Strike 3 caption issue, and it converges with red's "uniqueness-trade" concern from a different direction. The honest blue response is: **the strike has bite at the assumption-count level** (it requires editing the body text at line 1252 to enumerate four conditions and flag the real-analyticity hypothesis); **it does not falsify the conditional representation theorem itself** (the proof at lines 4357–4407 is correct as stated under the real-analyticity hypothesis); and **the appendix's own labeling — "representation theorem rather than... uniqueness theorem" at line 4261 — preempts the broader interpretive overreach** that the figure caption at line 1411 commits.

Sub-claim 6 of the operationalization reads "No TODO, no 'future work', no 'this requires further treatment' inside §Theory that would block reviewer acceptance." The operative qualifier is "block reviewer acceptance," not "exist at all." Theory sections in published variational-inference and information-geometry papers routinely defer empirical discriminators to future work without blocking acceptance; the standard reviewer test is whether the deferral is honest (states what would constitute the test) or evasive (defers without specifying the test). The manuscript's TODO at line 1880 specifies the test — "operationally independent measurement of $\omega$, for example fitting $\omega$ from the autocorrelation of a natural-gradient trajectory under a stiffness $M_{\mu\mu}$ that does not coincide with the kinetic metric." This is honest deferral. Whether reviewers in particular venues (NeurIPS, ICML, JMLR, *Information Geometry*, *J. Stat. Mech.*) would accept a 53-page theory section with one explicit TODO of this type is a matter of venue editorial practice, not of methodological principle.

Red's falsification condition (ii) — requiring a single peer-reviewed precedent in a comparable venue with this exact citation-and-TODO pattern — was not answerable by web search at the literal-token level. I report this candidly: I cannot produce such a precedent. The honest blue position is that the threshold itself is red's construction, not a community-recognized binding criterion, and that the operative reviewer test is whether the manuscript's organizational choices (companion-paper outsourcing of multi-head/RoPE/FFN extensions; appendix backstop for cross-scale shadow; TODO for the operationally-independent mass-analogy test) are honest organizational choices or evasive deferrals. Under the per-citation reclassification above, the strict load-bearing delegation count is 5 against red's threshold of ≥10. Under the demarcation analysis above, the labeled within-framework interpretation is the Popperian standard rather than its violation. Under the conditional-uniqueness analysis above, the body text states the correct conditional theorem and the only equivocation is at the figure-caption level. The three strikes do not jointly carry the section-level rejection red asserts; what they carry is **a list of three minor revisions**: figure-caption tightening, an explicit "Limitations" paragraph promoting the §1.19 TODO out of §Theory and into a discussion section, and an explicit per-citation reading of the companion-paper dependencies clarifying which are organizational choices.
