## Defense

**Strike C is misaddressed at trigger (b).** Trigger (b) is at the *gradient* level: "silently substitutes $\nabla\langle E\rangle_\beta$ for $\nabla\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ at the gradient level." The covariance correction $-\tau^{-1}\mathrm{Cov}_{\beta^*}(E, \nabla_x E)$ at manuscript line 1404 is the gradient-level identity per `[Milgrom Segal 2002]` and is a property of first variations. Eq. \eqref{eq:effective_mass} at line 2052 is a second-variation (Hessian) display, not a gradient identity. Line 1967 of the manuscript states explicitly *before* the boxed expressions: "the second-variation expressions below treat the attention weights $\beta_{ij}$ as fixed at their softmax-equilibrium values, equivalent to the envelope-theorem convention for analyzing the local geometry of equilibria." Under this convention switch, by `[Milgrom Segal 2002]`'s envelope-theorem licensing of the first-variation reduction, the second variation of $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ at fixed $\beta^* $ is precisely the second variation of $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{align}}|_{\beta^*}$; the covariance correction is a first-variation phenomenon and does not appear in the fixed-$\beta$ Hessian at second order. Red's reading conflates the gradient identity with the Hessian display; trigger (b) is not fired. Sub-claim 1 stands against Strike C.

**Strike B passes sub-claim 4's literal labeling bar.** Sub-claim 4 of the operationalization reads: "Empirical/analogy claims … are labeled as analogy where they are analogy and as derivation where derivation is supplied." The manuscript labels the mass identification as analogy three times in the §1.19 block: line 2048 ("the identification of the Hessian sector $[M_{\mu\mu}]$ with an 'effective mass' is interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass"), line 2064 ("the analogy is structural, not empirical … the present construction does not supply such an independent test"), and line 1882 ("we do *not* claim to derive the kinetic structure from $\mathcal{F}$ alone … not as a derivation of mechanics from information geometry"). Red's `[Popper1959 Ch. 4 §15]` invocation reads "definitional consequence" as a falsifiability failure, but the operationalization's sub-claim 4 is a labeling requirement, not an external falsifiability requirement; the manuscript meets the labeling requirement by stating "definitional consequence" inline at line 2064 and committing the section to the analogy-not-derivation reading at line 1882. The TODO at line 1880 names what is *not* claimed (an operationally-independent measurement of $\omega$). Sub-claim 4 is met under literal labeling.

**Strike A's finite-$\sigma^2$ closure via Appendix A.** Setting the precedent-map concession aside, the manuscript's own internal closure for the cross-scale shadow is the augmented-joint generative model at Appendix A (referenced at line 552 as Lemma~\ref{lem:aug_joint_welldefined}, Lemma~\ref{lem:shadow_mf_optimum}) with Gibbs cross-scale factors $\chi_{i,\pi(i)}(k_i, k_{\pi(i)}) \propto \exp[-\|k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)}k_{\pi(i)}\|^2/(2\sigma^2)]$.
