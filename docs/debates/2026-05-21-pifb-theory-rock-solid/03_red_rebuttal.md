## Core attack

The conjunctive operationalization at `00_claim.md` line 20 is binding text: "Both sides should treat the claim as carrying these conjunctive sub-claims (**any one failing falsifies the whole**)." Blue's "honest concession" at lines 41–43 of `02_blue_opening.md` admits that under literal reading sub-claim 5 (self-containedness) and sub-claim 6 (no unresolved gaps) both fail — sub-claim 5 because the load-bearing transformer-limit reduction at manuscript line 1615 is deferred to the companion paper ("The remainder of this subsection follows the development in the gauge-theoretic transformer companion paper~\cite{Dennis2025trans}"), the multi-head lift at line 1623 is deferred ("the lift to the block-diagonal multi-head gauge group $\mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{head}})^H \subset \mathrm{GL}(d_{\text{model}})$ is treated in the companion paper~\cite{Dennis2025trans}"), and the multi-head / RoPE / FFN derivations at line 1875 are deferred; sub-claim 6 because the `\textbf{TODO:}` at line 1880 inside §Theory defers the only empirical test of the load-bearing scaling $\omega^2 \propto m_{\text{eff}}^{-1}$ to future work.

Blue then invites the chief judge to choose between "strict literal" and "intent-faithful" readings, conceding that under strict literal reading blue loses regardless of how sub-claims 1–4 fare. This is not a defense of the claim — it is a request to amend the operationalization mid-debate. Per `[Popper1959 Ch. 4 §15]` *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (Routledge), a scientific claim is falsifiable iff its falsification conditions are enumerated in advance and not revised under pressure (`03_red_memo_philosophy-of-science.md` "Strongest defense" item 1). The user enumerated the conjunctive sub-claims before the debate. A post-hoc reading change is a frame-check failure of the defense.

Multi-head attention is not an "extension" of standard transformer attention. `[Vaswani2017 §3.2.2]` introduces multi-head attention as integral to the canonical architecture, not as an optional refinement. The trivial-frame reduction route to scaled dot-product attention at manuscript line 1615 is deferred to the companion paper as a "sequence of explicit limits ... isotropic covariances ... shared learned bilinear compatibility $M = W_QW_K^\top$" — and the canonical transformer reduction *requires* this trivial-frame route. The §Theory section therefore does not close its own canonical-form reduction without the companion paper. Sub-claim 5 fails as stated, by manuscript-line construction, not by interpretation (`03_red_memo_philosophy-of-science.md` "Strongest attack").

## Defense

Even granting blue the relaxed "intent-faithful" reading, sub-claims 1 (mathematical correctness) and 4 (falsifiability and scope) fail under two independent primary-source-backed strikes.

**Strike A — the cross-scale shadow's $\sigma^2 \to 0$ Dirac limit contradicts manuscript line 1458.** Blue's evidence item 4 puts decisive weight on the claim that the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ at line 547 is "closed by explicit reduction at line 552, not papered over" via the rigid-link $\sigma^2 \to 0$ limit of a Gibbs cross-scale factor $\chi_{i,\pi(i)}(k_i, k_{\pi(i)}) \propto \exp[-\|k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)}k_{\pi(i)}\|^2/(2\sigma^2)]$, identified with the "deterministic-decoder limit of a ladder-VAE-style hierarchical prior~\cite{Sonderby2016}." As $\sigma^2 \to 0$, the Gibbs factor converges in the distributional sense to $\delta(k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)}k_{\pi(i)})$ and the resulting "prior" $p_i^{(s)}$ becomes a Dirac measure concentrated at the transported parent posterior. The manuscript itself states at line 1458, inside §Theory: "A Dirac choice $q_{e_k}(c) = \delta(c - c_k)$ ... gives an infinite KL whenever $q_i$ does not coincide pointwise with $\delta(c - c_k)$, since $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \delta(c - c_k)) = +\infty$ for any non-degenerate $q_i$." The $\mathrm{KL}(q_i^{(s)} \| p_i^{(s)})$ term in the canonical free energy at Eq.~\eqref{eq:free_energy_functional_final} therefore evaluates to $+\infty$ in the very limit blue's defense uses to close the standard-FEP gap. This is the manuscript's own line-1458 self-contradiction with the load-bearing reduction at line 552 (`03_red_memo_variational.md` "Strongest attack" item 1). Sub-claim 1 (mathematical correctness) fails by manuscript-line construction.

The Sønderby Ladder VAE citation is a wrong-domain cite per the canon-cop rubric: Sønderby et al. *Ladder Variational Autoencoders* (NeurIPS 2016, [arXiv:1602.02282](https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.02282)) constructs the top-down generative model as a learned-mean, learned-finite-variance Gaussian at each layer — not a deterministic point-passing scheme. The standard hierarchical-VI corpus (`[Friston2017Graphical]`, `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 9]`, `[RanganathTranBlei2016]` arXiv:1511.02386) likewise uses finite-variance conditional densities for $p(s_\ell | s_{\ell+1})$. The rigid-link limit is not the deterministic-decoder limit of any standard hierarchical-VI construction; it is a singular limit that none of the cited references take. The standard-FEP gap `external_canon_inference.md:60` records is therefore *not* closed by the ladder-VAE reduction (`03_red_memo_variational.md` "Strongest attack" item 2).

**Strike B — the mass-analogy section deploys a scaling claim its own line 2064 admits is definitional, not dynamical.** Manuscript line 2064 states: "Under this identification the harmonic-oscillator scaling $\omega^2 \propto k/m$ is a definitional consequence of the postulate rather than an independent dynamical scaling: when $k$ and $m$ are both equal to $M_{\mu\mu}$ by construction, $\omega^2$ reduces to a per-direction unit relation and the analogy is structural, not empirical." The manuscript cites `[Arnold1989]` GTM 60 Ch. 5 §22–25 at line 1882 for the standard requirement that the inertia tensor $T$ and the potential Hessian $V$ be operationally independent positive-definite quadratic forms at the equilibrium configuration in the generalized eigenvalue problem $V v = \omega^2 T v$, *and then explicitly fails to supply that independence* (line 1882: "the present construction reuses the same matrix $M_{\mu\mu}$ for both roles and therefore does not supply such an independent test"). Per `[GoldsteinPooleSafko2002 Ch. 6]` *Classical Mechanics* (3rd ed., Addison-Wesley), the same independence requirement appears in every standard mechanics text. A scaling that is "a definitional consequence of the postulate" is unfalsifiable as stated per `[Popper1959 Ch. 4 §15]` (`03_red_memo_geometer.md` "Strongest attack" + `03_red_memo_philosophy-of-science.md` "Strongest defense" item 2). Sub-claim 4 (falsifiability and scope) fails.

The line-1880 `\textbf{TODO:}` is the manuscript's own admission that the only empirical test of this scaling is not in this manuscript. Sub-claim 6 (no unresolved gaps) fails by manuscript-line construction even under the most generous "publication-realistic" reading, because the TODO defers not "what is not claimed" but precisely the central scaling that is claimed.

**Strike C (reinforcement) — surrogate-substitution past line 1404 fires blue's own falsification trigger (b).** Blue's falsification trigger (b) at line 33 of `02_blue_opening.md` states: "If any subsequent §Theory derivation silently substitutes $\nabla\langle E\rangle_\beta$ for $\nabla\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ at the gradient level without re-flagging, sub-claim 1 fails." Manuscript line 1967 names the distinction and immediately states the boxed mass-matrix forms apply *only* in the $\beta_{ij} = 0$ isolated-agent limit. The "effective mass" identification at Eq.~\eqref{eq:effective_mass} (line 2052) then deploys non-zero $\beta_{ik}$ and $\beta_{ji}$ contributions ("incoming relational" and "outgoing recoil" terms) without re-flagging the covariance correction $-\tau^{-1}\mathrm{Cov}_{\beta^*}(E, \nabla_x E)$ named at line 1404. The surrogate-vs-reduced distinction is collapsed (`03_red_memo_info-geometer.md` "Strongest attack"). The asymmetric-attention "Lyapunov / dissipative" relabel at line 1994 abandons the conservative-Hamiltonian reading entirely, yet the mass-analogy identification at line 2052 requires that reading; the manuscript wants both and admits at line 1994 that the asymmetric case is "open and is not addressed here." Sub-claim 1 fails on blue's own falsification trigger.

**Strike D (reinforcement) — residual-subgroup discipline fires blue's own falsification trigger (d).** Blue's falsification trigger (d) at line 37 of `02_blue_opening.md` states: "A single later §Theory equation that asserts gauge-invariance without naming the residual restriction would falsify the gauge-theory defense at sub-claim 4." The lattice gauge transformation $\Omega_{ij} \mapsto g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$ at manuscript line 610 is the *full* per-vertex local gauge with different $g_i, g_j$ at different vertices, not the constant-per-agent residual subgroup of line 571. The manuscript does not state at line 610 whether the $g_i$ are constant-in-$c$ (in which case the line-610 cocycle is the same as the line-571 global subgroup, and the lattice-gauge structure is degenerate) or per-vertex local (in which case Role-B quantities like $\mathrm{tr}(A^{(i)}_\mu A^{(i)}_\nu)$ are *not* invariant under it, and the line-571 commitment is broken). Blue's combined-license cite for edge-modes `[DonnellyFreidel2016]`, `[BartlettRudolphSpekkens2007]`, `[Vanrietvelde2020]`, `[Rovelli1996]` carries the *language* of edge-mode physical-vs-gauge splitting without the *machinery* (symplectic form, conjugate variables, boundary action) that makes the edge-mode treatment rigorous in `[DonnellyFreidel2016 §3]`, `[Witten2018]` (arXiv:1803.04993), and `[CarrozzaHoehn2022]` (arXiv:2109.06184) (`03_red_memo_gauge-theorist.md` "Strongest attack"). Sub-claim 4 fails on blue's own falsification trigger (d).
