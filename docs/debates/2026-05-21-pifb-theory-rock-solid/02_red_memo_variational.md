# Red Memo — variational (Opening)

Mode: theory. Round: opening. Side: red. Claim: §Theory of `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` is rock solid and publication ready.

## Steelman of the claim (from this lens)

The strongest variational-inference reading is that §Theory cleanly extends single-agent FEP to a multi-agent setting in three structurally honest moves: (i) §1.13 (line 1029) labels the multi-agent coupling terms $\sum_{ij}\beta_{ij}\mathrm{KL}(q_i\|\Omega_{ij}q_j)$ and the attention-entropy term as a "novel multi-agent extension" rather than a consequence of FEP alone; (ii) §1.14 (lines 1041–1118) derives the softmax attention weights from a mixture-of-sources alignment energy with explicit "status of the construction" caveat acknowledging it is a consensus-energy ansatz; (iii) §1.16 (line 1278 ff.) recovers the entropy-regularized softmax as the row-Lagrangian stationary point of the canonical free energy, with the envelope-theorem / reduced-free-energy distinction at §1.16 (lines 1370–1404) cleanly separating $\nabla\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ from $\nabla\langle E\rangle_\beta$. Under this reading, the section is exactly the kind of careful variational treatment a venue should accept.

## Strongest weakness from this lens

The cross-scale shadow construction at Eq.~(\ref{eq:cross_scale_shadow}) on line 547 is the load-bearing structural commitment of the entire framework — it is what licenses the variational hierarchy $r\to s\to p\to q\to o$ at line 634, the prior coupling in the canonical functional at line 1262, the meta-agent feedback at line 547, and the participatory dynamics referenced from §Theory at line 552. But the construction is **not standard hierarchical variational inference**, and §Theory itself admits this in plain words at line 552: "This is a structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference: in the standard scheme [Friston 2017, Parr-Pezzulo-Friston 2022] the level-$\ell$ prior is derived from a generative-model conditional $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$, not posited as a transported posterior."

The canon (`external_canon_inference.md:60`) is explicit on the same point: "Cross-level couplings come from the generative model $p(s_\ell | s_{\ell+1})$, not from a deterministic point-passing scheme. The user's multi-layer cascade… is **not standard mean-field across the hierarchy**. It is a deterministic point-passing scheme that loses the variational uncertainty about $s_\ell$ when passed to ℓ+1." The manuscript's defense is the appendix backstop cited at line 552: "the reduction is supplied in Appendix~\ref{app:augmented_joint}: an augmented hierarchical generative model with Gibbs cross-scale factors… and the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is the rigid-link limit $\sigma^2 \to 0$ of the joint marginal of this joint over the parent." Three problems with this backstop, each fatal for sub-claim 5 (self-containedness):

1. **The backstop is in an appendix, not in §Theory.** The operationalization of "publication-ready" requires §Theory to be self-contained at the level of its load-bearing reductions. The cross-scale shadow is load-bearing — it appears in 8 separate equations and definitions inside §Theory (lines 547, 552, 632, 634, 683, 1233, 1419, 1582). The justification of its variational status is delegated.
2. **The rigid-link limit $\sigma^2\to 0$ is the Dirac-delta singularity that §1.15 itself flags as ill-defined.** Lines 1458–1459 acknowledge: "A Dirac choice $q_{e_k}(c) = \delta(c - c_k)$ is the singular limit of a finite-precision Gaussian sensory agent and gives an infinite KL whenever $q_i$ does not coincide pointwise with $\delta(c - c_k)$, since $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \delta(c - c_k)) = +\infty$ for any non-degenerate $q_i$." The same singular limit is then invoked at line 552 as the licensing reduction for cross-scale shadows. Either the rigid-link limit is well-defined (in which case lines 1458–1459 are wrong) or it is not (in which case the cross-scale shadow's standard-FEP credentials are not established by the appendix). §Theory does not resolve the tension.
3. **The mean-field rigid-link limit is not what Sønderby et al. 2016 do.** The Ladder VAE — cited at line 552 as the precedent — passes a *full Gaussian* through the top-down generative branch with a finite-variance conditional $p(z_\ell \mid z_{\ell+1}) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta(z_{\ell+1}), \Sigma_\theta(z_{\ell+1}))$, and the top-down inference path *shares parameters* with the generative path but does not collapse the variance. The manuscript's $\sigma^2 \to 0$ collapse is a degenerate special case, and the standard ladder-VAE framing does not endorse it. ([Sønderby et al. 2016, arXiv:1602.02282](https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.02282))

A second variational strike, on the mixture-of-sources construction (§1.14):

4. **Source-independence factorization is load-bearing for the softmax derivation but acknowledged as a "substantive structural assumption."** Line 1064 reads: "The factorization $Q(k \mid z) = q_i(k)$ for all $z$ is a substantive structural assumption, not a tractability simplification: it imposes source-independence on the consensus belief, requiring that the same $q_i$ explain the data conditional on every candidate source $j$. The softmax form of the attention weights derived below follows from this source-independence constraint together with the entropy-regularized KL energy, not from the variational principle alone." This caveat sits inside the very subsection that the figure caption at line 1411 invokes for the "attention is a geometric necessity" claim. Either the source-independence assumption is innocuous and the necessity reading holds, or the assumption is structural and the necessity reading drops to "necessity given a substantive structural assumption" — and §Theory does not pick a lane.

## Primary-source citations

- Friston 2010, "The free energy principle: a unified brain theory?" *Nat. Rev. Neuro.* 11: 127–138 — single-agent variational free energy. Multi-agent KL-coupling with gauge transport is not present (`external_canon_inference.md:29`).
- Parr, Pezzulo, Friston 2022, *Active Inference*, MIT Press, Ch. 9 — hierarchical generative model with conditional $p(s_\ell\mid s_{\ell+1})$. (`external_canon_inference.md:60`).
- Sønderby, Raiko, Maaløe, Sønderby, Winther 2016, "Ladder Variational Autoencoders," arXiv:1602.02282 — top-down inference shares parameters with the generative model but passes a full Gaussian, not a delta. The $\sigma^2\to 0$ limit is not invoked.
- Blei, Kucukelbir, McAuliffe 2017, "Variational inference: a review for statisticians," *J. Am. Stat. Assoc.* 112: 859–877 — mean-field factorization $q(s_1,\ldots,s_N) = \prod q_i(s_i)$ loses correlations across factors; a hierarchical scheme that passes only a posterior *mean* between levels is a point-estimate approximation, not the full variational hierarchical scheme.
- Bishop 2006, *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning*, Springer, §10.2 — the Gamma-Normal conjugacy that the manuscript invokes at line 1336 for the per-coordinate precision construction. Bishop's mean-field derivation places an independent Gamma prior on the precision of each coordinate factor and computes the closed-form ELBO; the manuscript's per-coordinate $\alpha_i^{(k)*}$ form is consistent with this, but §Theory does not display the Gamma-Normal joint or its mean-field stationary equations — it cites Bishop and asserts the result. Sub-claim 5 is again in question.

## Falsification condition

The position above is falsified if blue exhibits, in §Theory itself (not appendix, not companion paper): (i) a finite-$\sigma^2$ derivation in which the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is the mean-field stationary point of an explicitly displayed hierarchical generative model, with both $\mu$ and $\Sigma$ of $p_i^{(s)}$ inheriting non-degenerately from $q_I^{(s+1)}$, or (ii) a derivation of the mixture-of-sources softmax that does not assume source-independence as a separate structural axiom, or (iii) a canonical FEP / active-inference reference (Friston, Parr, Ramstead, Bogacz) that endorses the rigid-link $\sigma^2\to 0$ identification as a standard variational-hierarchical move.

## Newly-discovered canon

- Ranganath, Tran, Blei 2016, "Hierarchical Variational Models," arXiv:1511.02386 — the canonical hierarchical-VI treatment in modern variational inference. The hierarchical $q$ is constructed by placing a prior on variational parameters, *not* by passing transported posteriors between levels. Worth flagging because it is the closest published precedent for what a "principled hierarchical variational scheme" looks like, and it does not match the manuscript's cross-scale shadow. ([arxiv.org/abs/1511.02386](https://arxiv.org/abs/1511.02386))
- Friston 2017, "Active inference, curiosity and insight," *Neural Computation* 29: 2633–2683 — the manuscript's [Friston2017] citation for the hierarchical generative-model conditional $p(s_\ell\mid s_{\ell+1})$. The conditional is finite-variance, not a delta; the manuscript's $\sigma^2\to 0$ collapse is not endorsed.
- Beal 2003 thesis, *Variational Algorithms for Approximate Bayesian Inference* — the conjugate-prior mean-field treatment for Gaussian families. The Gamma-Normal joint stationary equations Bishop §10.2 builds on are derived in full here; the manuscript could have followed Beal's display rather than gesturing at Bishop §10.2 in passing.
