# Blue Memo — gauge-theorist

Lens: gauge theory — Lie groups, representations, holonomy, equivariance, gauge-fixing, spontaneous vs explicit symmetry breaking.

## Steelman (opposing position)

§Theory is not publication ready because the dual-role gauge frame (Role A pure-gauge / Role B physical, lines 555–565) is suspect: standard gauge theory does not allow a single field to be both pure-gauge (redundant) and physical (ontologically loaded) simultaneously, since gauge invariance of observables would otherwise be violated. Furthermore, the Goldstone analogy at line 1518 carries enough connotational weight — "Goldstone parallel … suggestive" — that a reviewer reading the §Theory section without the caveat (which is buried in a single sentence) would naturally infer a spontaneous-symmetry-breaking story.

## Defense from the gauge-theory lens

(1) **The dual role is given precise residual-gauge-subgroup separation, not a contradiction.** Lines 567–571 isolate the subgroup under which each role is invariant. Role A is invariance under the *global diagonal right-translation* $U_i \to U_i g$ for a single $g\in G$ shared across all agents: under this action $\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1}$ is invariant and $\phi_i$ alone is redundant. Role B treats the *per-agent* frame data $\phi_i:\mathcal{U}_i\to\mathfrak{g}$ as physical state — invariant only under the residual constant-per-agent subgroup $\{(g_i)_{i\in I}: g_i\in G\text{ constant in }c\}$. Quantities like $\mathrm{tr}(A^{(i)}_\mu A^{(i)}_\nu)$ are invariant under this residual subgroup by trace cyclicity, *not* under the full local gauge with a Maurer–Cartan derivative. This is the standard edge-mode treatment of `[DonnellyFreidel2016]` and the quantum-reference-frame split of `[BartlettRudolphSpekkens2007, Vanrietvelde2020]`, all three cited at line 569: the same data is gauge or physical depending on which subgroup of the symmetry group is acting. This is rigorous, not ad hoc; the manuscript names the subgroup at every step.

(2) **The Goldstone "analogy not derivation" caveat is load-bearing, explicit, and stated in §Theory.** Line 1518 reads: *"The breaking here is \emph{explicit} rather than spontaneous: environmental agents enter the free energy with fixed gauge frames, and these fixed frames play the role of an external source field analogous to a Zeeman term in a ferromagnet, lifting the degeneracy of the vacuum orbit by hand rather than through internal vacuum selection. A genuine spontaneous-symmetry-breaking story would require an internal mechanism by which the rest of the system selects a gauge-orbit representative without an externally fixed frame, together with a derived Goldstone spectrum in linearized fluctuations around the vacuum, neither of which we establish here. The Goldstone parallel is suggestive but is taken here as analogy rather than derivation."* This matches canonical Goldstone theory `[Weinberg1995 Vol. II §19, Peskin Schroeder Ch. 11]` exactly: spontaneous breaking of a continuous global symmetry yields massless bosons per broken generator; explicit symmetry breaking (e.g., Zeeman term) does *not* give Goldstone modes. The manuscript correctly identifies its observation-induced breaking as Zeeman-type explicit breaking and rules out the Goldstone reading by name. This is publication-grade analogy discipline; a reviewer who insists on "no analogies" is asking for a different paper, not this one.

(3) **The associated-bundle action at line 614 is the canonical $\rho_{\mathrm{state}}(g)\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)=\mathcal{N}(g\mu,g\Sigma g^\top)$.** Line 610 makes the convention explicit and matches `[Nakahara2003]`: "$\rho:G\to\mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B})$ as a left action paired with the right principal action $n\mapsto n\cdot g$ on $\mathcal{N}$." The lattice gauge transformation $\Omega_{ij}\mapsto g_i\Omega_{ij}g_j^{-1}$ at line 610 is the standard cocycle transformation under per-vertex gauge — correct for a $G$-valued connection on a graph. The structural-action-table at line 1491 explicitly labels the analogy "structural, not dimensional equivalence" with units called out (action units kg·m²·s⁻¹ vs nats), pre-empting the standard "free energy = action" overclaim.

## Falsification condition for the claim

The gauge-theory defense breaks if (a) any §Theory equation labeled "gauge-invariant observable" turns out to be invariant only under the residual constant-per-agent subgroup rather than under the full local gauge with Maurer–Cartan derivative, and the manuscript fails to state this restriction at that equation. The blanket statement at line 571 ("we attach this restriction to each occurrence below rather than appealing to a uniformly stated invariance") *commits* to this discipline, so a single later equation that violates it would be a falsification. (b) If the Lorentzian-signature complex $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{C})$ extension at line 593 acts on the belief fiber anywhere (it should act only on the gauge connection per the manuscript's own restriction), the dual-action structure collapses. (c) If the connection one-form $A_\mu^{(i)} = U_i^{-1}\partial_\mu U_i$ (line 1289) is treated as the connection of the structure group while the holonomy claim later treats it as the connection of an associated bundle without naming the representation, the holonomy class is ambiguous.

## Newly-discovered canon

The constant-per-agent residual subgroup treatment at line 571 is the lattice-gauge-theory analog of the *Wilson line* construction — `[Wilson1974]` ("Confinement of quarks") or the standard textbook treatment of lattice gauge under per-vertex transformations. The trace-cyclicity invariance $\mathrm{tr}(g^{-1}A_\mu g \cdot g^{-1}A_\nu g) = \mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ for $c$-constant $g$ is standard lattice gauge.
