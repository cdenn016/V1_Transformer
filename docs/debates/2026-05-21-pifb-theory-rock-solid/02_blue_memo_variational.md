# Blue Memo — variational

Lens: variational inference — ELBO, EM separation, mean-field, mixture models, FEP forms.

## Steelman (opposing position)

§Theory is not publication ready because (i) the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ at line 550 promotes a meta-agent's *posterior* to the constituent's *prior*, which is not the standard hierarchical mean-field scheme of `external_canon_inference.md:60` ("Standard variational hierarchical inference passes the full posterior $q(s_\ell)$ to inform $q(s_{\ell+1})$ … not a deterministic point-passing scheme"), and (ii) the mixture-of-sources construction at line 1038 admits its components $P(k|z=j) = \Omega_{ij}q_j$ depend on other agents' variational posteriors, meaning the "generative model" is itself a function of variational quantities and is not held fixed in the standard variational sense (the manuscript's own admission).

## Defense from the variational lens

(1) **The cross-scale shadow is given a proper hierarchical generative-model construction in Appendix A, not posited as an ad-hoc identification.** Line 552 explicitly addresses the standard-FEP gap raised by the steelman: *"This is a structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference … the reduction is supplied in Appendix~\ref{app:augmented_joint}: an augmented hierarchical generative model with Gibbs cross-scale factors $\chi_{i,\pi(i)}(k_i, k_{\pi(i)}) \propto \exp[-\|k_i - \Omega_{i,\pi(i)}k_{\pi(i)}\|^2/(2\sigma^2)]$ … and the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is the rigid-link limit $\sigma^2 \to 0$ of the joint marginal of this joint over the parent."* This is exactly the ladder-VAE construction `[Sonderby2016]`, which the manuscript also cites at line 552. Under the deterministic-decoder limit, the user's identification matches the standard form `p(s_\ell|s_{\ell+1})` of `[Friston2017Graphical, ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 9]` with the Gibbs factor playing the role of the conditional. The standard-FEP gap is *closed* by an explicit reduction, not papered over.

(2) **The mixture-of-sources construction is flagged as an ansatz, not promoted to a derivation.** Line 1038 reads: *"The mixture-of-sources construction below is best read as a consensus-energy ansatz rather than as a generative-model derivation … The alignment free energy $\sum_j \beta_{ij} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}q_j)$ is therefore an engineered energy functional that penalizes belief disagreement after gauge transport."* Line 1064 separately flags the source-independence factorization $Q(k|z)=q_i(k)$ as a "substantive structural assumption, not a tractability simplification." The choice of forward KL is then closed under the conditional uniqueness theorem in Appendix A which the manuscript invokes at line 1044 as the post-hoc justification within the f-divergence class. This is publication-grade epistemic discipline per `external_canon_inference.md:29` ("multi-agent coupling … should be labeled as a novel construction requiring its own justification") — the user labels and justifies; the steelman's "not in standard FEP" critique is met head-on.

(3) **The softmax-from-Lagrangian derivation at lines 1272–1286 is the canonical entropy-regularized soft-assignment derivation.** Differentiating $L_i = \sum_j[\beta_{ij}E_{ij} + \tau\beta_{ij}\log(\beta_{ij}/\tilde\pi_{ij})] - \lambda_i(\sum_j\beta_{ij} - 1)$ with respect to $\beta_{ik}$ and setting to zero gives $E_{ik} + \tau(\log\beta_{ik} + 1) - \tau\log\tilde\pi_{ik} = \lambda_i$, whence $\beta_{ik} = \tilde\pi_{ik}\exp(-E_{ik}/\tau)/Z_i$. This is the textbook entropy-regularized OT / Sinkhorn-divergence soft-assignment, and Eq. (1287) is the exact canonical form. `external_canon_inference.md:30` records this as "standard if presented as a Lagrangian for the soft-assignment problem; novel if claimed to follow from FEP alone" — the manuscript presents it as a Lagrangian (line 1272), staying within the safe category.

## Falsification condition for the claim

The variational defense breaks if (a) Appendix A's conditional uniqueness theorem (cited at lines 1038, 1044, 1112) does not actually establish closure under the assumptions claimed, since the soft-max-from-Lagrangian derivation is a *theorem* under the source-independence factorization but the *choice* of forward KL is justified only via Appendix A — a load-bearing dependency on §Appendix, not §Theory. I have not verified Appendix A. (b) The cross-scale shadow's rigid-link limit at $\sigma^2\to 0$ requires the joint Gibbs measure to converge to a well-defined marginal; if Lemma `lem:shadow_mf_optimum` does not establish this, the cross-scale identification fails. (c) The autograd-vs-reduced-free-energy distinction at line 1404 must hold uniformly: if any subsequent §Theory derivation silently substitutes the autograd surrogate for $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{red}}$ at the gradient level, the envelope discipline collapses.
