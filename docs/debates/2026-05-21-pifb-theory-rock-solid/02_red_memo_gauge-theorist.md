# Red Memo — gauge-theorist (Opening)

Mode: theory. Round: opening. Side: red. Claim: §Theory of `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` is rock solid and publication ready.

## Steelman of the claim (from this lens)

The strongest gauge-theoretic reading is that §Theory carefully distinguishes the global-diagonal residual subgroup (the bulk $U_i \to U_i g$ under which $\Omega_{ij}$ is invariant) from the local gauge action that introduces a Maurer–Cartan derivative (lines 571, 1229), uses the GL-correct precision transport $\tilde\Lambda_{q_k} = \Omega_{ik}^{-T}\Lambda_{q_k}\Omega_{ik}^{-1}$ throughout the mass-matrix derivation (Eq.~(\ref{eq:precision_transport}) at line 1894), and refuses the orthogonal-group simplification $\Omega^{-T}\to\Omega$ wherever the gauge group is $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ rather than $\mathrm{SO}(K)$. The vanishing-holonomy lemma at line 1211 is stated correctly as a structural consequence of the vertex-local cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = U_iU_j^{-1}$, with the curvature reading $F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu}\equiv 0$ for pure-gauge connections (line 1227) and the path to non-trivial holonomy via edge-local connections $\delta_{ij}$ flagged honestly (line 1227). The dual-role treatment of $\phi_i$ at lines 561–571 is novel for a variational-inference manuscript but is anchored in physics literature with three concrete citations: edge modes (Donnelly–Freidel), quantum reference frames (Bartlett–Rudolph–Spekkens, Vanrietvelde), and Rovelli's relational interpretation.

## Strongest weakness from this lens

The Goldstone / spontaneous-symmetry-breaking language is invoked load-bearingly at three separate points inside §Theory, and only at one of those points is the Zeeman-style explicit-breaking caveat repeated. Lines 1507 and 1518 are the cleanest example. Line 1507: "This vacuum state represents the information-theoretic analog of unbroken gauge symmetry in particle physics, where all field configurations are energetically degenerate under the symmetry group." Line 1518: "The breaking here is \emph{explicit} rather than spontaneous: environmental agents enter the free energy with fixed gauge frames, and these fixed frames play the role of an external source field analogous to a Zeeman term in a ferromagnet, lifting the degeneracy of the vacuum orbit by hand rather than through internal vacuum selection. A genuine spontaneous-symmetry-breaking story would require an internal mechanism by which the rest of the system selects a gauge-orbit representative without an externally fixed frame, together with a derived Goldstone spectrum in linearized fluctuations around the vacuum, neither of which we establish here. The Goldstone parallel is suggestive but is taken here as analogy rather than derivation."

The standard gauge-theory canon (Weinberg 1995 vol. II §19, Peskin–Schroeder ch. 11) is unambiguous: explicit symmetry breaking by an external source field is the textbook *non-example* of Goldstone modes. A massless boson arises from a *spontaneously* broken continuous global symmetry, where the ground state is degenerate under the symmetry but a representative is selected by internal dynamics; an external source field (Zeeman term) lifts the degeneracy and gives a *mass* to what would otherwise have been a Goldstone mode, not a Goldstone mode itself. The manuscript's structure — "the system has a Goldstone mechanism, except actually it doesn't and we are calling that out, but the reading is still suggestive" — is the textbook equivocation pattern that Weinberg's chapter warns against ([Goldstone, Salam, Weinberg 1962, *Phys. Rev.* 127: 965](https://journals.aps.org/pr/abstract/10.1103/PhysRev.127.965)). If the manuscript wants the SSB-analogy reading, it cannot also have the explicit-breaking reality; if it has the explicit-breaking reality, the SSB-analogy must be withdrawn, not retained-as-suggestive. The section labels at line 1503 ("Explicit Symmetry Breaking via Observations") and the figure caption at line 1525 ("Observation-driven explicit symmetry breaking… The breaking is explicit (analogous to a Zeeman term) rather than spontaneous") confirm the explicit reading; what then is the SSB-vacuum picture at lines 1507 and 1529 doing in the section?

A second gauge-theoretic strike, on the dual-role treatment of $\phi_i$:

The manuscript invokes edge modes (Donnelly–Freidel 2016) at line 569 as the principal physics-side licensing for treating $\phi_i$ as both gauge-redundant (Role A) and physical (Role B). Donnelly–Freidel's construction does promote "would-be pure-gauge" boundary data to genuine physical degrees of freedom, but the licensing is concrete: it requires an extended phase space with new boundary fields, a symplectic structure invariant under the boundary action of the gauge group, and an explicit identification of which subset of $\phi_i$ becomes the edge mode. See Donnelly–Freidel 2016 "Local subsystems in gauge theory and gravity," *JHEP* 09: 102, eq. (2.18) and the surrounding apparatus. The manuscript inherits the *language* of edge modes without the *machinery*: there is no symplectic form on the multi-agent configuration space, no Hamiltonian generator of the boundary action, no identification of which slice of $\phi_i$ is "the edge mode" versus "the bulk pure-gauge data." The citation discharges the existential question "do constructions like this exist in physics?" — it does not discharge the constructive question "is the manuscript's construction one of them?"

The same critique applies to the quantum-reference-frame citation (Bartlett–Rudolph–Spekkens 2007, Vanrietvelde 2020): the QRF apparatus involves a *quantum* reference frame with state, transformations under the symmetry group acting on its Hilbert space, and a relational decomposition of the global Hilbert space. The manuscript's $\phi_i \in \mathfrak{g}$ is not a quantum state with a Hilbert-space structure; it is a Lie-algebra-valued classical field. The analogy at line 569 is invoked at the level of the language ("a reference frame is gauge from a global perspective-neutral viewpoint, but is a physical system carrying state from its own perspective") rather than at the level of the structure (no Hilbert space, no QRF transformation rules, no relational decomposition theorem). This is a one-line motivation, not a license for two distinct ontological roles.

A third gauge-theoretic flag, smaller but pointed:

The mass-matrix Hessian at lines 1973–1987 invokes $\Omega^{-T}$ correctly for GL transport, but the relation $[M_{\mu\mu}]_{ii} = \bar\Lambda_{p_i} + \sum_k \beta_{ik}\tilde\Lambda_{q_k} + \sum_j \beta_{ji}\Lambda_{q_i} + \Lambda_{o_i}$ at line 1973 carries an asymmetric structure: the "incoming" sum sends each neighbor's precision *transported into i's frame* ($\tilde\Lambda_{q_k}$), but the "outgoing recoil" sum sends agent $i$'s native precision $\Lambda_{q_i}$ multiplied by the count of senders. Algebraically this comes from the identity $\Omega_{ji}^T\tilde\Lambda_{q_i}^{(j)}\Omega_{ji} = \Lambda_{q_i}$ stated at line 1971, but the asymmetry is acknowledged in §1.19's "asymmetric attention caveat" at line 1991: when $\beta_{ik}\neq\beta_{ki}$, "the resulting flow is no longer the gradient flow of $\mathcal{F}$ alone, and the bilinear form $M_{\mu\mu}$ does not function as the inertia tensor of a conservative Hamiltonian." The Newtonian reading $\omega^2 = k/m$ that the entire "Mass Analogy" subsection rides on is therefore valid only in the symmetric-attention or isolated-agent limit. The transformer attention map is canonically *not* symmetric — softmax-row-normalized attention has $\sum_j\beta_{ij}=1$ but generically $\beta_{ij}\neq\beta_{ji}$ (Vaswani et al. 2017). The "Mass Analogy" subsection therefore applies to a degenerate sub-case of the section's own canonical attention construction. This is not flagged as a scope restriction at the level of the canonical free energy in §1.13 (line 1262); it is flagged 600 lines later. A reviewer asked "where does this analogy hold in your framework?" would have to assemble the answer from line 1262 + line 1991, which is the kind of reconstruction that publication-ready theory sections avoid imposing.

## Primary-source citations

- Goldstone, Salam, Weinberg 1962, "Broken symmetries," *Phys. Rev.* 127: 965 — canonical Goldstone theorem statement. Spontaneous breaking of continuous global symmetry → massless boson per broken generator; explicit breaking → no Goldstone mode.
- Weinberg 1995, *The Quantum Theory of Fields*, Vol. II §19 — explicit discussion of explicit vs. spontaneous symmetry breaking, Zeeman-style external source fields as the textbook non-example of Goldstone phenomena.
- Peskin & Schroeder 1995, *An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory*, Ch. 11 — Goldstone theorem proof and its dependence on the spontaneous-breaking premise.
- Donnelly & Freidel 2016, "Local subsystems in gauge theory and gravity," *JHEP* 09: 102 ([arXiv:1601.04744](https://arxiv.org/abs/1601.04744)) — edge-mode construction with extended phase space; eq. (2.18) and surrounding apparatus.
- Bartlett, Rudolph, Spekkens 2007, "Reference frames, superselection rules, and quantum information," *Rev. Mod. Phys.* 79: 555–609 — quantum reference frames as quantum states transforming under the symmetry group; the relational decomposition theorem.
- Vanrietvelde et al. 2020, "A change of perspective: switching quantum reference frames," *Quantum* 4: 225 — perspective-neutral structure with relational observables; not a classical Lie-algebra-valued field.
- Nakahara 2003, *Geometry, Topology and Physics*, 2nd ed., Ch. 10 — sandwich product for (0,2)-tensor parallel transport, Maurer–Cartan one-form, flat-bundle / vanishing-holonomy structure.
- Vaswani et al. 2017, "Attention is all you need," NeurIPS — canonical scaled-dot-product attention, with the asymmetric softmax row-normalization that the manuscript's mass-analogy subsection requires to be symmetric.

## Falsification condition

The position above is falsified if blue shows, in §Theory: (i) a withdrawal of SSB-vacuum language from lines 1507 and 1529, leaving the entire subsection "Explicit Symmetry Breaking via Observations" framed only as explicit Zeeman-style breaking with no Goldstone-mechanism allusions in the body; (ii) an explicit symplectic form on the multi-agent configuration space inherited from the Donnelly–Freidel edge-mode construction, exhibited in §Theory at the level the citation would license; or (iii) a relocation of the "Mass Analogy" subsection's symmetric-attention scope restriction from line 1991 to the canonical free energy in §1.13, so the reader does not have to assemble the constraint from two locations 600 lines apart.
