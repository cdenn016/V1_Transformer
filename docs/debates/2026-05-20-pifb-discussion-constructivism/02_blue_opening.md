# Blue Opening — pifb-discussion-constructivism

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Discussion already covers the load-bearing epistemological commitments through Kant 1781, Friedman 2001 (relativized constitutive a priori), Rovelli 1996 (relational), Fuchs 2014/2017 (QBism), Clark 2016 / Seth 2021 / Hoffman 2019 (predictive processing), Kuhn 1962, Quine 1951/1975, van Fraassen 1980, Ladyman 2007, and Lahav–Neemeh 2022/2025; the constructivist canon (Glasersfeld, Piaget, von Foerster, Maturana–Varela, Varela–Thompson–Rosch, Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston) duplicates content already credited under those traditions, and adding a constructivism subsection would be padding rather than scholarship.

## Position

The Discussion section of `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` (lines 3080–3460) substantively omits the constructivist canon — Glasersfeld 1989/1995, Piaget 1970, von Foerster 1981, Maturana–Varela 1980, Varela–Thompson–Rosch 1991, Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston 2019/2020, Hohwy 2013 — and that omission is a scholarship debt the manuscript should pay, because (i) the framework's pullback-from-noumenon construction, no-view-from-nowhere thesis, consensus-driven model alignment, and multi-scale meta-agent formation are the canonical operational moves of that tradition, and (ii) the already-cited traditions do not collectively discharge what those primary sources contribute.

The strongest single piece of evidence is the asymmetry between `references.bib` and the manuscript body: the Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston 2019/2020 paper is in the bibliography *twice* (entries `Ramstead2019` at `references.bib:595–602` and `ramstead2020variational` at `references.bib:2459–2466`) under a title that contains the exact word the manuscript needs — "active inference is enactive inference" — and is cited zero times in the manuscript text. Hohwy 2013 (`hohwy2013predictive` at `references.bib:100`) is in the bibliography and is also never cited. A `grep -i` of the manuscript for `constructiv|Glasersfeld|Piaget|Maturana|Varela|Foerster|autopoie|enactiv|Ramstead|Kirchhoff|hohwy` returns zero matches in body text. The only match for "constructi" is "constructive empiricism" (van Fraassen, line 3438), which is a distinct philosophical position.

## Evidence

### E1. The Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston thesis directly implicates the framework's VFE machinery

Ramstead, Kirchhoff, Friston (2019/2020, *Adaptive Behavior*) state their central claim as: "We propose an enactive interpretation of active inference — what might be called *enactive inference*." They argue: "In active inference under the FEP, the generative and recognition models are best cast as realising *inference and control* — the belief-guided selection of action policies," and that "the organism *embodies* the recognition density and *entails* the generative model as a *control system*" [Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston 2020, PMC7418871]. The paper rejects structural representationalism: "generative models are *not structural representations*… The generative model does *not* encode anything. It is *realised by* the statistical relations between states of interest."

This is the formal bridge between the free-energy program the manuscript inherits and the Maturana–Varela enactive tradition. The manuscript's `s_i` (generative models) and `q_i` (recognition densities) are the densities of Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston's title. The manuscript's "agents are sections of associated bundles" is the geometric realization of "the organism embodies the recognition density." The manuscript's `gamma_{ij} KL(s_i || tilde{Omega}_{ij} s_j)` cross-agent model coupling (Eq. at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3245`) is the multi-agent extension of the enactive structural-coupling thesis that the Ramstead paper sets up at the single-agent level. The paper sits in `references.bib` already; failing to cite it while citing Friedman, Clark, Seth, and Hoffman picks the modern philosophical descendant traditions but skips the precise paper that establishes the active-inference-as-enactive equivalence.

### E2. Glasersfeld 1989/1995 is the closest match to the manuscript's "pullback from noumenon" move, not Kant

Glasersfeld's core thesis [Glasersfeld 1989 §1, restated in Glasersfeld 1995]: "(a) knowledge is not passively received but actively built up by the cognizing subject; (b) the function of cognition is adaptive and serves the organization of the experiential world, not the discovery of ontological reality." The manuscript at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3102` writes: "agents have no direct access to the base manifold $\mathcal{C}$ itself, it remains noumenal in the Kantian sense. What agents experience as ambient geometry is the structural pullback metric." At `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3286-3289`: "we discover gauge theories because our generative models $s_{\text{human}}$ must align across individuals." At `3293`: "Different intelligent species with radically different cognitive architectures might construct incompatible physics from the same noumenal substrate. They would not be wrong while we are right, or vice versa."

The Kantian label at line 3181 is structurally weaker than the Glasersfeldian label the framework's content asks for. Kant retains the noumenon as causal source of the phenomena and treats space–time as forms of intuition fixed for all human cognizers; the manuscript's framework allows different cognitive architectures to pull back qualitatively different physics (line 3293), which is the radical-constructivist move Glasersfeld makes beyond Kant. Citing Kant for the noumenal-pullback construction credits the right starting point but skips the philosopher whose mature position the framework actually realizes.

### E3. Maturana–Varela 1980 autopoiesis and structural coupling formalize what the manuscript calls meta-agent emergence

Maturana and Varela (1980, *Autopoiesis and Cognition*) define an autopoietic system as "a machine organized as a network of processes of production… [that] continuously regenerate and realize the network of processes" that produced them, with operational closure meaning "the system maintains sufficient internal processes to sustain itself," and with structural coupling defined as the continuous mutual modification of the system and its environment that "brings forth a world." The manuscript's meta-agent formation construction (Section `sec:meta_agent_emergence` referenced at lines 3132, 3315–3319, 3367, 3398) is the consensus-aggregation construction that builds a scale-$(s{+}1)$ agent from scale-$s$ constituents, with the meta-agent's gauge frame $\phi_I^{(s+1)}$ derived from constituent frames via the cross-scale transport $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)}) \exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$. The cross-scale shadow mechanism the manuscript invokes at lines 3119–3128 is the formal counterpart of Maturana–Varela structural coupling between scales.

The manuscript at line 3398 acknowledges the central open question: "[the framework] must say why scale-0 electrons have the same kind of belief-and-model-bearing structure $(q, s, \phi)$ as scale-$N$ humans, and why aggregating low-scale agents into high-scale meta-agents preserves rather than eliminates phenomenal properties." This is the autopoietic-recursion problem Maturana–Varela posed and that Varela–Thompson–Rosch 1991 *The Embodied Mind* extended. The manuscript carries the problem without naming the tradition that owns it.

### E4. Von Foerster's "objectivity is a delusion" thesis is what the "Physics as Theory of Informational Compatibility" subsection (3287–3289) actually advances

Von Foerster (1981, *Observing Systems*) second-order cybernetics holds that the observer is part of the system observed; the canonical formulation is "objectivity is the delusion that observations can be made without an observer." The manuscript at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3289` writes: "Physical laws read this way as grammatical rules for constructing shareable descriptions within the constraints of human cognitive architecture. Gauge invariance, Lorentz invariance, conservation laws, and other structural features then appear in this reading as necessary conditions for maintaining consensus across diverse observers rather than as derivable intrinsic properties of a mind-independent reality." At line 3442 the manuscript cites Nagel 1986 for "no view from nowhere." Nagel's slogan is the popular name; von Foerster's second-order cybernetics is the technical position that builds an entire research program on it. Citing Nagel without von Foerster takes the catchphrase and skips the tradition.

### E5. Hohwy 2013 names the position the manuscript's predictive-processing citations occupy

Hohwy (2013, *The Predictive Mind*, OUP) explicitly characterizes predictive processing as "neo-Kantian" and as having "a distinct Kantian element" [Hohwy 2013, Ch. 1], with Helmholtz as the ancestral precedent. The manuscript at line 3285 cites Clark 2016, Seth 2021, Hoffman 2019 for the predictive-processing reading of the framework, calling it "the predictive-processing reading of perception… made geometrically explicit through agent-dependent pullback metrics." Hohwy 2013 is in `references.bib:100` (`hohwy2013predictive`) and is the philosophical synthesis paper for the position the manuscript's PP citations express. Omitting it leaves the philosophical lineage of the PP claim implicit.

### E6. The references.bib asymmetry is itself dispositive

`Ramstead2019` (`references.bib:595-602`) and `ramstead2020variational` (`references.bib:2459-2466`) are two bibliography entries — duplicates — for the Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston paper. `hohwy2013predictive` (`references.bib:100`) is also present. None of these three entries is cited by `\cite{…}` anywhere in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex`. A bibliography entry staged but never cited is one of two things: an oversight to be fixed by citing the entry, or a deliberate omission to be justified. The manuscript supplies no justification. The omission is therefore most consistent with an oversight that should be remedied.

## Falsification conditions

I concede this claim and grant red the verdict if any of the following hold under primary-source citation:

1. **Substantive duplication.** If red shows by primary-source quotation that Glasersfeld 1989/1995, Maturana–Varela 1980, von Foerster 1981, Varela–Thompson–Rosch 1991, and Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston 2019/2020 together contribute *no* thesis that is not already contributed by the manuscript's existing Kant 1781, Friedman 2001, Clark 2016, Seth 2021, Hoffman 2019, van Fraassen 1980, Ladyman 2007, and Lahav–Neemeh 2022/2025 citations, then constructivism is not omitted — it is renamed and duplicated, and adding it would be padding. The test: red must produce, for each of the constructivist sources, a verbatim or near-verbatim equivalent in a currently-cited source.

2. **Incompatibility on a load-bearing axis.** If red shows that the constructivist canon disagrees with the manuscript's framework on a substantive commitment the framework cannot retract — for example, if radical constructivism's pragmatist anti-realism is incompatible with the manuscript's pan-agentic structuralism (line 3344, 3386), or if Maturana–Varela's biological autopoiesis is incompatible with the framework's electron-scale agency at line 3398 — then the manuscript is correct to omit those traditions, and citing them would create a contradiction rather than a clarification.

3. **The Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston paper is commentary, not derivation.** If red shows that Ramstead–Kirchhoff–Friston 2019/2020 makes a purely interpretive claim (that active inference can be *read* as enactive) rather than a structural claim (that active inference *is* enactive in a mathematically substantive sense the framework inherits), then the manuscript is free to cite or skip it as an editorial choice. The test: red quotes the paper's central claim and shows it is hedged, not asserted.

4. **The "constructivism" label dissolves under scrutiny.** If red shows the named sub-traditions (radical constructivism, genetic epistemology, second-order cybernetics, autopoiesis, enactivism, predictive-processing constructivism) are mutually incompatible such that citing "constructivism" as a unified position would be sloppy scholarship — and that the manuscript's choice to cite the specific sub-positions it agrees with (Kantian idealism, predictive processing, relational physicalism, structural realism) is the more rigorous move — then the omission is principled rather than a debt.

5. **Editorial saturation.** If red shows the Discussion is already at or beyond the length budget such that adding constructivism citations would force removal of currently-cited material with higher load, the remediation sub-claim (3) fails on editorial grounds. Pure length is not a strong falsifier on its own; the load-comparison must be substantive.

I do not concede on the ground that constructivism is "merely philosophical commentary" — the Ramstead paper is in a peer-reviewed adaptive-behavior journal and asserts a structural identity, and the manuscript has staged it in its own bibliography. Nor do I concede on the ground that constructivism is "too well-known to require citation" — Kant, Quine, Kuhn, and Wheeler are equally well-known and are cited.
