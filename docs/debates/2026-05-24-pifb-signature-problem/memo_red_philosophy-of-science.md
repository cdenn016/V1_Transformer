# Memo — debate-expert-philosophy-of-science — red — opening — pifb-signature-problem

## Lens
Falsifiability, scope, theory-ladenness, content of an existence claim.

## Steelman of the opposing position
The section is the most carefully hedged in the manuscript: it explicitly labels itself an existence demonstration, lists every postulate (imaginary $\phi_\tau$, real-part projection, single-generator collapse, $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ choice, conformal ambiguity, dimension non-selection, first-order tension), and disclaims derivation — so an honest "we can write down a Lorentzian metric in this formalism" is exactly what it claims and nothing more.

## My position (in service of red)
The phrase "structurally compatible **with Lorentzian signature**" overclaims, because once every input knob is a free postulate the construction is compatible with *every* signature, and a construction compatible with everything distinguishes nothing. The honest content is "we can hand-tune a real symmetric bilinear form to any signature we like," which is true of any $2\times 2$ symmetric matrix and carries no information about *this framework* or about *Lorentzian* signature specifically.

The decisive observation is the manuscript's own disclosure at `:2950`: a frame "containing both compact and non-compact real generators in $\mathfrak{gl}(K,\mathbb{R})$ also produces indefinite signature under the $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ convention." No complexification, no imaginary $\phi_\tau$, no real-part projection, no Wick analog. The entire apparatus of Section `sec:worked_signature` — the postulate at `:2874`, the projection flagged as a "derivation gap" at `:2892` — is therefore inessential to the signature outcome. The sign is a function of the generator-and-convention choice alone. A worked example whose central postulates are demonstrably not load-bearing for its conclusion is not an existence proof *of a mechanism*; it is a restatement that one can choose a sign.

A claim is content-bearing only if its negation is conceivable within the framework. Here the negation ("the framework is NOT structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature") is unreachable: by the manuscript's own Case-C admission, indefinite signature is available with real generators, and Riemannian signature is available by choosing compact generators or $-\mathrm{tr}$. The framework is compatible with $(-,+)$, $(+,+)$, and (by the sympy table in the synthesis) $(-,-)$ with equal ease. "Compatible with Lorentzian" is then not a property of the framework; it is a property of the analyst's pen.

## Evidence
- Manuscript `:2950` discloses the real-generator (no-complexification) route to indefinite signature — its own words establish that the complex apparatus is inessential.
- Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959), §6, §31–33: a statement with no possible falsifier in the relevant domain is empirically empty; the demarcation is whether the framework forbids any outcome. Here it forbids none.
- Manuscript `:2892` flags the real-part projection as having "no physical principle in the construction that mandates" it — a self-certified derivation gap; an existence claim cannot rest a load-bearing step on an admittedly unmotivated operation and still claim the result is "structural."

## Newly-discovered canon (for 01b_extended_evidence.md)
- Popper, K. (1959). *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §6 ("Falsifiability as a criterion of demarcation"), §31–33. A theory that excludes no observation in its domain is unfalsifiable and thus empirically contentless. Applies to "compatible with signature S" when the same construction is equally compatible with $\lnot S$.
- Forster & Sober (1994), "How to Tell When Simpler, More Unified, or Less Ad Hoc Theories Will Provide More Accurate Predictions," *BJPS* 45(1): on ad-hoc parameter fitting; an existence demonstration that requires tuning $\ge 5$ free postulates to land on the target signature is the ad-hoc-fit limit.

## Falsification conditions
My position is wrong if (a) the construction privileges $(-,+,+,+)$ over $(+,+,+,+)$ or $(-,-,+,+)$ on some framework-internal ground stated in the section — it does not (the $1{+}3$ vs $2{+}2$ non-selection is admitted at `:2900`); or (b) "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" is read as the trivially-true "a Lorentzian metric can be written in these coordinates," in which case the claim is true but vacuous and the overclaim is in calling it a result about the framework rather than about $2\times2$ symmetric matrices.
