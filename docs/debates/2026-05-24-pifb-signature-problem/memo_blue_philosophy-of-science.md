# Blue Memo — philosophy-of-science

## Steelman of the attack
The phrase "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" is an overclaim: an object that is rank-1 and complex before two unmotivated postulates (imaginary φ_τ + real-part projection) is not "compatible" with anything physical; calling the projected residue a "metric" smuggles in a conclusion the construction has not earned.

## Defense from canon (not from manuscript authority)
The claim is an *existence* statement of the form "∃ a configuration in the admissible parameter space under which the frame-twist form has indefinite signature." Existence claims have a precise falsifiability structure (Popper, *Logic of Scientific Discovery* §15: a strictly existential statement is verified by a single instance and is the dual of a falsifiable universal). The construction exhibits that instance explicitly; the sympy run confirms the instance is non-empty and yields signature (−,+). A derivation claim — "free-energy minimization *selects* this configuration" — would be the universal/lawlike statement; the manuscript explicitly does *not* make it. The scope discipline is therefore correct on its own terms: the demarcation between "compatible-under-postulates" and "derived" is exactly the existential/universal demarcation, and the manuscript places its claim on the existential side at lines 2825, 2861, 2866, 2902, 2907.

The "structurally compatible" phrase is not a blue interpretive gloss — it is the manuscript's own self-description (2825 "demonstrates structural compatibility, not derivation"; 2902 "structural existence proof, not a dynamical derivation"; 2907 "structural compatibility … not … a derivation"). Defending the existence-demonstration framing and defending the phrase are the same act; an attack on the phrase in isolation equivocates between the two readings.

## External primary-source citation
[Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §15, on the asymmetry of existential vs universal statements] — an existential claim is the appropriate, and falsifiable-by-non-existence-of-instance, form for an "it can exhibit X" result.

## Falsification condition (argued unmet)
The frame-claim fails if the manuscript anywhere asserts the stronger universal ("free energy *selects* imaginary φ_τ" / "signature is *derived*") in this span. It does not: every relevant sentence flags the dynamical selection as the *open problem* (2861, 2866, 2906–2907, 2929, 2952). The honesty-of-framing sub-point (claim sub-point 5) is therefore met. The residual risk is the *completeness* of disclosure (a separate sub-claim), not the framing.
