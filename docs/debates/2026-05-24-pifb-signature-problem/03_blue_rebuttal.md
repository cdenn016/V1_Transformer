# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-signature-problem

## Concession

Three concessions, all honest, none cosmetic.

**1. The phrase "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature," taken alone, is stronger than the construction earns.** Red's central word-level objection lands. The object the trace mechanism produces, $G_{\mu\nu}=\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ under the single-generator collapse, is complex-valued and rank-one ($\det=0$); the non-degenerate Lorentzian form exists only after the real-part projection, which the manuscript itself flags as a "derivation gap" with "no physical principle in the construction that mandates" it (`:2892`). The precise, defensible statement is therefore not the bare phrase but the conditional one: *compatible with Lorentzian signature conditional on two postulates — an imaginary $\phi_\tau$ assignment and a rank-changing real-part projection the construction does not motivate*. The manuscript's own qualifier at `:2907` ("structural compatibility … not … a derivation") and at `:2902` ("structural existence proof, not a dynamical derivation") carries exactly this conditional reading; the unqualified gloss does not, and a defense that leaned on the unqualified gloss would overclaim. Blue does not defend the unqualified gloss.

**2. The sign-flip consequence of dropping the separability ansatz is not spelled out at `:2877`.** Red's *narrow* form of the separability objection — that the manuscript states cross-derivatives are "suppressed" but does not state that their *presence can flip the temporal sign* (timelike → spacelike) — survives the disclosure. The text names the suppression; it does not name the consequence. Red's sympy (extended evidence, line 37: $\mathrm{Re}(G_{\tau\tau})=2((\partial_\tau\psi_x)^2-(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)^2)$, sign indefinite) is correct, and the manuscript's gap-list does not register that the temporal direction can become spacelike without separability. This is a genuine granularity gap in the completeness sub-claim (sub-point 3 of the evidence pack), and blue grants it.

**3. The causal-cone route does not apply to the framework's implemented dynamics.** Red is correct that Postulate 3 (finite maximal information speed) is incompatible with the first-order natural-gradient E-step, whose continuum limit is parabolic and propagates signals at infinite speed [Evans, *Partial Differential Equations* (2nd ed.), §2.3: the heat kernel is strictly positive everywhere for any $\tau>0$]. The manuscript states this in the correct technical vocabulary and concedes the three candidate fixes (telegraph limit, second-order hyperbolic dynamics, architectural constraint) are unrealized: "Naive continuum limits of such flows are parabolic and yield infinite signal speed, so the causal-cone route does not apply directly to the current implementation … None of these is currently realized" (`:2929`). Blue does not defend the *applicability* of the causal-cone route to the current implementation. What blue defends is narrower and survives: the route is correctly labelled "conditional on the postulates" (`:2929`), and the disclosure of the tension is complete and correctly named — which is what the claim's honesty-and-completeness sub-points require.

## Core attack

**Red's load-bearing "undisclosed dependency" framing overstates, and the overstatement is the place red's opening is weakest.** Red's Position section asserts an "undisclosed dependency" and frames the separability ansatz, "framed as a display simplification," as "in fact load-bearing" without disclosure (`02_red_opening.md`, Position ¶2). That broader formulation is contradicted by the manuscript text red is characterizing. At `:2877` the manuscript writes, verbatim:

> *We further restrict to the separable ansatz $\psi_\tau = \psi_\tau(\tau)$ and $\psi_x = \psi_x(x)$ for the displayed calculation. Without separability, the connection would carry cross-derivative terms $i(\partial_\tau \psi_x)T + (\partial_\tau \psi_\tau \cdot T)$ along the temporal direction (and similarly for the spatial direction), and the resulting bilinear form $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ would acquire mixed-derivative contributions that the displayed result suppresses.*

The ansatz is named, the cross-derivative terms that would appear are written out explicitly, and the displayed result is stated to *suppress* them. An assumption that is named, its dropped-term structure written out, and flagged as a restriction on "the displayed calculation" is not an *undisclosed* dependency. Red's own Evidence section silently retreats from the broad claim to the narrow one ("the section flags that cross-derivatives are 'suppressed' but does not disclose that their presence can flip the temporal sign"). The two are not the same objection: the narrow one is a granularity complaint about the gap-list (which blue concedes above), the broad one is an undisclosed-assumption complaint (which `:2877` refutes). Red's opening trades on the broad framing in the Position and supports only the narrow one in the Evidence.

This matters beyond the separability point because it is the pattern of red's whole second prong. Red's signature-engineering attack (Position ¶1, Evidence bullets 1–2) demonstrates — correctly, and reproduced by sympy — that the generator choice and the $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ convention drive the sign, so that compact generators give $(-,-)$, real-mixed generators give $(-,+)$ with no complexification, and real single generators give $(+,+)$. But every one of these is the manuscript's *own disclosure*, not a discovery against it: the single-generator-collapse sensitivity at `:2872` ("with a generic admissible alternative such as $T_x=\bigl[\begin{smallmatrix}0&1\\-1&0\end{smallmatrix}\bigr]$ … the trace identities below would change sign, and the indefinite signature would not emerge") and again at `:2902` ("with a compact-subalgebra $T_x$ the trace identities would change sign and the indefinite signature would not emerge"); the $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ convention dependence at `:2868` ("under the standard positive-definite inner product on a compact form … the sign relation … would be reversed, and the construction would not produce the desired Lorentzian split"); the $1{+}3$ vs $2{+}2$ non-selection at `:2900`; and the real-mixed-generator alternative route to indefinite signature, which red calls "the spine" at `:2950`, is the manuscript's own paragraph. The trace-form sign dependence is moreover canonical Lie theory, not a framework-specific weakness: by Cartan's criterion the form $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ is indefinite on a non-compact real form and the compact-form invariant is $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ [Knapp, *Lie Groups Beyond an Introduction* (2002), Ch. I; Ch. VI for the $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})$ Killing-form signature $(2,1)$]. Red's sympy *reproduces the manuscript's stated sensitivities*. Reproducing what a text discloses is confirmation that the disclosure is accurate, not a refutation that the disclosure is incomplete.

## Defense

The claim under evaluation is an existence statement — "$\exists$ a configuration in the admissible parameter space under which the frame-twist form has indefinite signature" — and the correct test for an existence statement is whether the instance exists and whether the framing claims more than existence. Both come out for the claim. An existential statement is verified by a single instance and is the dual of a falsifiable universal [Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §15, on the existential/universal asymmetry]; the construction exhibits the instance, and sympy confirms it is non-empty and yields signature $(-,+)$ (extended evidence, lines 8–10). The *universal* statement — "free-energy minimization *selects* this configuration" — is the derivation the manuscript explicitly disclaims at `:2866`, `:2902`, `:2907`, and the central open question at `:2907` ("whether a free-energy mechanism selects an imaginary $\phi_\tau$ … the signature is a chosen input, not a derived output"). Red and blue agree the derivation is not made. The dispute is only over whether the *existence-plus-disclosure* claim is sound, and red's "engineered, not derived" objection is, read precisely, a statement that the configuration is *selected by input* rather than *forced by dynamics* — which is the manuscript's own characterization. "The signature is a chosen input, not a derived output" (`:2907`) is the manuscript saying what red says. An attack that re-states the target's self-description is not a counterexample to it.

Two further canonical facts hold the existence demonstration's internal correctness, neither resting on manuscript authority. First, the rank arithmetic and the signature count are both correct under standard linear algebra. The signature of a real symmetric form is a congruence invariant [Horn & Johnson, *Matrix Analysis* (2nd ed.), §4.5, Sylvester's law of inertia]; sympy confirms the unprojected complex form has $\det=0$ (rank one) and $\mathrm{Re}(G)=\mathrm{diag}(-2(\partial_\tau\psi)^2,+2(\partial_x\psi)^2)$ has $\det=-4(\partial\psi)^4<0$ (rank two, signature $(-,+)$), exactly as `:2887` states, and that the causal-cone metric $\mathrm{diag}(-c^2,h)$ has signature $(-,+,\dots,+)$, exactly as `:2923` states. Second, the sector split is the mathematically forced conservative choice, not an arbitrary restriction: the Fisher–Rao metric is the unique sufficient-statistic-invariant metric and is positive-(semi)definite by construction [Čencov (1972); Amari & Nagaoka, *Methods of Information Geometry* (2000), Ch. 2], and the closed-form Gaussian KL acquires a complex value through its log-determinant term the moment $\Sigma=\Omega\Sigma_0\Omega^\top$ becomes non-Hermitian under complex $\Omega$ [Amari & Nagaoka (2000), Ch. 2]. Keeping belief transport in $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ while only the connection complexifies is therefore the move that *preserves* $\mathrm{KL}\ge 0$ and $\mathcal{F}\ge 0$; red's "decoupled from the statistics" objection is correct as a description and is exactly what the manuscript asserts at `:2902` ("not … the statistical geometry within a single fiber"), and whether that decoupling is a defect or a faithful base-versus-fiber separation is the manuscript's acknowledged open problem (info-geometer memo, honest-concession paragraph), not a defeated claim.

The net position. Blue concedes the bare phrase overstates, the separability sign-flip is a granularity gap, and the causal-cone route does not apply to the implemented dynamics. Blue defends, on external canon, that (i) the worked-example algebra, rank arithmetic, group facts, and Sylvester counts are correct [Horn & Johnson §4.5; Knapp Ch. I; Hall, *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* (2015), Ch. 5 for $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})\cong\mathrm{Spin}^+(1,3)$]; (ii) the framing is an existence claim of the correct Popperian form, not a derivation [Popper §15]; and (iii) every sensitivity red treats as a hidden engineering choice is disclosed in the text red is attacking (`:2868`, `:2872`, `:2900`, `:2902`, `:2950`). The claim as written — "correct existence demonstrations … structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature, NOT derivations … and every required postulate and gap … is accurately and completely disclosed" — survives on the *correctness* and *existence-scope* halves, and takes one real hit on the *completeness* half: the separability sign-flip consequence is not in the gap-list. The defensible verdict is that the claim is sound except for that single completeness omission, which is a one-sentence manuscript fix (add to `:2877` that dropping separability can render the temporal direction spacelike), not a structural failure of the section.

## Falsification conditions

Blue's defense is wrong if any of the following holds.

- If the manuscript anywhere in `:2863–2932` asserts the *universal* ("free energy selects imaginary $\phi_\tau$" / "signature is derived"). It does not; every relevant sentence flags selection as the open problem (`:2866`, `:2907`, `:2929`, `:2952`). If red exhibits such a sentence, the existence-scope defense fails.
- If the trace algebra, rank count, or Sylvester count is wrong. Sympy confirms all three (extended evidence, lines 8–11). If red supplies a corrected computation, the internal-correctness defense fails.
- If the $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ / generator sensitivities red reproduces are *not* in the manuscript text. They are, at `:2868`, `:2872`, `:2902`, `:2950`. If those lines say otherwise, the disclosure-completeness defense fails on the generator axis (it already takes the hit on the separability axis).
