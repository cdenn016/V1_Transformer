# Blue Opening — pifb-signature-problem

## Steelman (opposing position)

The section dresses a linear-algebra maneuver as physics: a rank-one, complex, gauge-noninvariant trace form is reduced by two unmotivated postulates — an imaginary φ_τ and a rank-changing real-part projection the manuscript itself admits has "no physical principle mandating it" — to a (−,+) diagonal, and the residue is then called a "Lorentzian metric." Confining complexification to the connection while the Fisher fiber stays real-positive-definite means the indefinite signature is a free-floating sign game in tr(A_μ A_ν), decoupled from the statistical content, so even the hedged phrase "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" overstates the result.

## Position

The claim is sound and should be upheld, in two separable halves. (a) The mathematics is correct and the result is exactly what it is labelled — an *existence demonstration* of structural compatibility with Lorentzian signature, not a derivation of signature from variational dynamics; this half is verified symbolically and grounded in standard Lie-theory, linear-algebra, information-geometry and PDE canon. (b) The postulates and gaps are disclosed accurately and, on the present evidence, completely; every vector an attacker can raise is already named in the text at a specific line. I defend both halves, and I state up front the narrow ground I do *not* defend, so the boundary of the claim is sharp.

What I do not defend, and the claim does not assert: that free-energy minimization *selects* the imaginary φ_τ or the 1+3 split (the manuscript's central open problem, :2861, :2906, :2907, :2929, :2952); that the real-part projection has a physical motivation (the manuscript calls it a derivation gap with no mandating principle, :2892); that the conformal-factor scale or dimension count is fixed (:2926); that the connection-sector signature is physically *coupled* to the statistical content rather than a parallel structure on the same base (the evidence pack flags this unsettled, line 30). Conceding these denies the attacker the easy strawman: none of them is part of the claim.

The manuscript's characterization at :2887 — "a rank-changing operation on the bilinear form (rank one complex to rank two real), not merely a discard of an off-diagonal imaginary piece" — is therefore exactly right, including the subtlety that the projection changes rank. [geometer memo; sympy in `01b_extended_evidence.md`.]

**The signature counts rest on Sylvester's law, correctly applied in both directions.** Signature is a congruence invariant [Horn & Johnson, *Matrix Analysis* §4.5]. This grounds the manuscript's no-go (a real GL(K,ℝ) frame transports a positive-definite Σ to ΩΣΩ⊤, which is congruent, hence still positive-definite — a real frame *cannot* manufacture a timelike direction, :2837, :2950) and the causal-cone count (g=diag(−c², h) with c²>0 and h positive-definite has signature (−,+,…,+); sympy: eigenvalues {−c², h₁, h₂, h₃}, :2923). The same theorem is what *forces* the construction outside the compact subgroup to obtain an indefinite form — which is the honest reason the imaginary postulate is needed, not an accident. [geometer memo.]

**The group-theory facts are standard and correctly stated.** SL(2,ℂ) ≅ Spin⁺(1,3) is the spin double cover and SO⁺(1,3) is the identity component of O(1,3) [Hall, *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* ch. 5; independently confirmed against Lorentz-group canon]. The manuscript keeps the spinor representation (in GL(2,ℂ)) and the vector representation (in GL(4,ℝ)⊂GL(4,ℂ)) explicitly distinct (:2900) — a distinction many informal treatments blur, and the manuscript does not. The non-compactness analysis is correct Lie theory: T=diag(1,−1) is symmetric-traceless, hence in 𝔰𝔩(2,ℝ) and *not* in the compact 𝔰𝔬(2); tr(T²)>0 is available only because the generator is non-compact, and the +tr(AB) vs −tr(AB) convention dependence is stated as a postulate, not hidden [Knapp, *Lie Groups Beyond an Introduction* ch. Wick rotation between real forms of SO(4,ℂ) is the standard statement [Streater-Wightman / Folland]. [gauge-theorist memo.]

**The sector split is the mathematically forced conservative choice, and its content is analytic, not merely numerical.** The closed-form Gaussian KL `½[tr(Σ_p⁻¹Σ_q) + (Δμ)⊤Σ_p⁻¹Δμ − K + log(|Σ_p|/|Σ_q|)]` [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2; KingmaWelling2014 App. B] goes complex the instant Σ=ΩΣ₀Ω⊤ is non-Hermitian — the log-determinant term alone is complex — so keeping KL≥0 and ℱ≥0 on the real GL(K,ℝ) sector (:2823, :2856) is forced, not arbitrary. The Fisher-Rao metric is positive-definite and unique under sufficient statistics [Cencov1972], so the fiber *cannot* be complexified without leaving information geometry. ## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds.

1. **The trace algebra, rank count, Lorentz invariance, or Sylvester count is wrong.** All four are verified by executed sympy (`01b_extended_evidence.md`); this condition is unmet.

2. **A group-theory fact is misstated** — e.g., SL(2,ℂ) is not Spin⁺(1,3), or tr(T²) is not available with the stated sign, or the +tr/−tr convention does not flip the signature. Confirmed correct against Hall and Knapp and against the Lorentz-group canon; unmet.

3. **An undisclosed assumption exists** — red exhibits a postulate or gap that is load-bearing for the (−,+) conclusion and is *not* in the ledger above. This is the live risk. If red produces one, the *completeness* clause of the claim fails and I concede that specific gap; the existence-demonstration core (half a) survives regardless. Candidate undisclosed items an attacker should probe: whether "A_μ = ∂_μφ" silently assumes the connection is the Maurer-Cartan form of an abelian/commuting frame (BCH corrections to exp(φ) are higher order, but the linearization's domain of validity is asserted, not bounded); whether identifying the trace form as a "metric" presupposes a non-degeneracy that the rank-one starting point violates before projection. I do not presently find either to be undisclosed — the linearization is flagged (:2877) and the rank-one→rank-two change is flagged (:2887) — but these are where the completeness clause is thinnest.

4. **The phrase "structurally compatible" is shown to assert more than the existential claim** — i.e., the manuscript somewhere in this span asserts dynamical selection or derivation. It does the opposite at :2861, :2866, :2906–2907, :2929, :2952; unmet.

Honest concession registered in advance: if the debate's standard for "structurally compatible" requires the signature to be *coupled to the statistical content* (not merely co-located on the same base), then the phrase should read "compatible after two postulates, including an unmotivated rank-changing projection," and only the existence-demonstration core is defensible. That core — the math is correct and the gaps are correctly named — stands on the verified computation and the external canon regardless of how that phrasing question is resolved.
