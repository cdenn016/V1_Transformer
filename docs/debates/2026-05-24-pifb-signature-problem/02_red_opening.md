# Red Opening — pifb-signature-problem

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §"Temporal Structure and the Signature Problem" treatment is the most carefully hedged section in the manuscript: it verifies its trace algebra correctly, states every standard group fact accurately, labels itself an existence demonstration rather than a derivation, and discloses each postulate and gap (imaginary $\phi_\tau$, real-part projection, single-generator collapse, $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ choice, conformal ambiguity, dimension non-selection, first-order tension, gauge-noninvariance of $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$) explicitly — so calling the result "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" is a fair, fully-disclosed claim.

## Position

The algebra, the group facts, the Sylvester count, and the "existence demonstration, not derivation" framing are all correct, and red concedes them without reservation. But the phrase **"structurally compatible *with Lorentzian signature*"** overclaims, and on two independent grounds the section's disclosure is incomplete:

1. **The signature is engineered by free input choices and is not a property of the framework.** Varying only the postulates the section itself lists — generator in {compact, non-compact}, bilinear form in {$+\mathrm{tr}$, $-\mathrm{tr}$}, imaginary placement, single-generator collapse, $1{+}3$ vs $2{+}2$ — the *same* construction produces Riemannian $(+,+)$, Lorentzian $(-,+)$, and anti-Lorentzian $(-,-)$ signatures with equal ease. The manuscript's own `:2950` disclosure proves the complex apparatus is inessential: indefinite signature follows from a purely real frame mixing compact and non-compact generators under $+\mathrm{tr}$, with no imaginary $\phi_\tau$ and no real-part projection. A construction compatible with every signature via input tuning is not "structurally compatible with *Lorentzian* signature" in any content-bearing sense; the honest content is "a $2\times2$ symmetric form can be given any signature," which is true of any symmetric matrix and says nothing about this framework.

2. **The genuine object is not a metric, and the metric appears only via an operation the section admits is unmotivated.** The object the framework produces, $G_{\mu\nu}=\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$, is complex-valued and degenerate (rank-1, $\det=0$). A Lorentzian metric is by definition real, symmetric, and non-degenerate. The non-degeneracy is *created* by the real-part projection — a rank-changing operation the section certifies has "no physical principle in the construction that mandates" it (`:2892`). A metric obtained by projecting a non-metric is one the analyst writes down, not one the framework produces.

Two corollaries sharpen this. First, the signature is **disconnected from the framework's information content**: it lives on the gauge-noninvariant connection trace form, while the Fisher–Rao metric — the unique statistical metric, the thing that makes this "it from bit" — contributes only the positive-definite block, and the manuscript confirms the free-energy functional is *blind* to the signature-producing choice (`:2952`, "the KL functional between real Gaussian beliefs is invariant under that choice"). Second, an **undisclosed dependency**: the separability ansatz (`:2877`), framed as a display simplification, is in fact load-bearing — without it $\mathrm{Re}(G_{\tau\tau})=2((\partial_\tau\psi_x)^2-(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)^2)$, whose sign is not guaranteed negative, so the temporal direction can become spacelike. And the **causal-cone route is vacuous for this framework**: its finite-speed Postulate 3 contradicts the framework's own first-order natural-gradient dynamics, whose parabolic continuum limit has infinite propagation speed and hence no causal cone.

## Evidence

- **Manuscript `:2950` (the spine).** "An alternative route in which a frame contains both compact and non-compact real generators in $\mathfrak{gl}(K,\mathbb{R})$ also produces indefinite signature under the $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ convention." Red sympy reproduces this: mixed real generators give $\mathrm{Re}(G)$ eigenvalues $\{-2(\partial_\tau\psi)^2,+2(\partial_x\psi)^2\}$ with no $i$ and no projection. The imaginary-$\phi_\tau$ postulate of `:2874` and the real-part projection flagged at `:2892` are therefore not load-bearing for the signature outcome. *(philosophy-of-science, gauge-theorist)*

- **The convention drives the sign; this is the generic situation, not a discovery.** The trace form $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ is indefinite on a non-compact real form by Cartan's criterion; on $\mathfrak{sl}(2,\mathbb{R})$ the Killing form has signature $(2,1)$, on compact $\mathfrak{su}(2)$ signature $(0,3)$ negative-definite [Knapp, *Lie Groups Beyond an Introduction* (2002), Ch. VI]. The manuscript itself certifies the dependence at `:2868`: under the compact-form inner product $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ "the sign relation… would be reversed, and the construction would not produce the desired Lorentzian split." Red sympy: a compact $T_x\in\mathfrak{so}(2)$ flips $\mathrm{Re}(G)$ to eigenvalues $\{-2(\partial_\tau\psi)^2,-2(\partial_x\psi)^2\}$ — signature $(-,-)$, not Lorentzian. *(gauge-theorist)*

- **The genuine object is degenerate; a metric needs non-degeneracy.** A (pseudo-)metric is by definition a smooth symmetric *non-degenerate real* $(0,2)$-tensor [Lee, *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* (2013), Ch. 13; O'Neill, *Semi-Riemannian Geometry* (1983), Ch. 2–3]. Red sympy: the complex $G$ has eigenvalues $\{0,\,-2(\partial_\tau\psi-\partial_x\psi)(\partial_\tau\psi+\partial_x\psi)\}$ — a genuine zero eigenvalue. The Lorentzian form $\det(\mathrm{Re}\,G)=-4(\partial_\tau\psi)^2(\partial_x\psi)^2<0$ exists only after $\mathrm{Re}(\cdot)$, which the manuscript admits has no mandating principle (`:2892`). *(geometer)*

- **Signature decoupled from statistics.** The signature lives on $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$, gauge-noninvariant (`:2829`, `:2904`) and "not of the statistical geometry within a single fiber" (`:2902`); the Fisher–Rao metric, the unique sufficient-statistic-invariant metric [Čencov (1972); Amari & Nagaoka (2000), Ch. 2], stays positive-definite. The free energy cannot see the choice that produces the signature (`:2952`). For an "it from bit" derivation this is the load-bearing gap: the signature is not an information-geometric quantity. *(info-geometer)*

- **Causal-cone route contradicts the framework's dynamics.** Postulate 3 (finite max speed) requires a finite causal cone; the framework's first-order natural-gradient E-step (`:2928`, "implemented in the working code") has a parabolic continuum limit, and parabolic equations propagate disturbances instantaneously (everywhere-supported heat kernel) [Evans, *PDE* (2010), §2.3–2.4]. The manuscript concedes the three candidate fixes are unrealized (`:2929`). An existence statement conditioned on dynamics the framework does not have is not an existence statement about the framework. The Sylvester count itself is correct (red sympy: $\mathrm{diag}(-c^2,h)$ has signature $(-,+,\dots,+)$) — conceded. *(numerical-analyst)*

- **Undisclosed dependency on separability.** Red sympy, dropping the `:2877` separability ansatz: $\mathrm{Re}(G_{\tau\tau})=2((\partial_\tau\psi_x)^2-(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)^2)$, sign indefinite. The section flags that cross-derivatives are "suppressed" but does not disclose that their presence can flip the temporal sign — a gap in the gap-list. *(numerical-analyst)*

## Falsification conditions

Red's position is wrong if any of the following holds.

- **(Vacuity vector)** The section privileges $(-,+,+,+)$ over $(+,+,+,+)$, $(-,-,+,+)$, or $2{+}2$ on a framework-internal ground. It does not: the $1{+}3$ vs $2{+}2$ non-selection is admitted at `:2900`, and the real-generator indefinite route is admitted at `:2950`. If blue exhibits a selection principle in the section, this vector fails.

- **(Non-metric vector)** $\mathrm{Re}(\cdot)$ is shown to be a gauge-covariant geometric operation (a genuine bundle map) rather than a coordinate-frame projection, so that the rank-2 real form is in the image of the framework's construction. The section argues the opposite at `:2892`. If blue supplies such a covariance argument, this vector fails.

- **(Causal-cone vector)** The framework's implemented dynamics admit a finite-speed (hyperbolic or telegraph) continuum limit. The section concedes none is realized (`:2929`). If blue exhibits a realized finite-speed limit, this vector fails.

- **(Concession boundary)** If "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" is read in the weak sense "a Lorentzian metric can be written in these coordinates," the claim is true but vacuous, and red's objection narrows to the word "structurally" carrying more than that. Red concedes the trace algebra (`:2883–2887`), the Sylvester count (`:2923`), the standard group facts ($\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})\cong\mathrm{Spin}^+(1,3)$, $\mathrm{SO}^+(1,3)$ embedding, Wick rotation between real forms [Hall (2015); Knapp (2002)]), and the honest "existence demonstration, not derivation" framing — none of these is the target. The target is the inference from a hand-tuned, statistics-blind, projection-dependent symmetric form to the assertion that *this framework* is structurally compatible with *Lorentzian* signature specifically.
