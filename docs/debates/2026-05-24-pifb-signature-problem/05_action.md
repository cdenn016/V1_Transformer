## Recommended manuscript edits (two)

1. **Conditionalize the "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" wording** at :2907, :2932, :2952, and the downstream echoes at :3029 and :3109. Decisive canon [Lee 2013 Ch. 13]: a (pseudo-)Riemannian metric is by definition a smooth, symmetric, *non-degenerate*, *real* (0,2)-tensor. The framework's native object $G_{\mu\nu}=\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ is complex and rank-1 ($\det=0$, the manuscript's own :2887) — not a metric on two counts — and the Lorentzian form appears only after a real-part projection the manuscript itself admits (:2892) has "no physical principle that mandates it." Both sides conceded the bare phrase overstates. Reword to: "compatible with Lorentzian signature *conditional on* the imaginary-frame postulate and the unmotivated real-part projection," i.e. the property belongs to the analyst's construction, not to the framework. (The manuscript already says this at :2892/:2907; the fix is to propagate that conditionalization to the summary sentences that drop it.)

2. **Add the separability sign-consequence at :2877.** The manuscript discloses the separable ansatz and that it "suppresses cross-derivative terms," but — asymmetrically with :2872, which discloses the single-generator collapse's *sign* consequence — it does NOT disclose that dropping separability can flip the temporal sign. Add one sentence stating this, restoring the "accurately and completely disclosed" claim. One-sentence editor fix, not a re-derivation.
