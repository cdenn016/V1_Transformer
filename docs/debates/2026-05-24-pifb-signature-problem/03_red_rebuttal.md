# Red Rebuttal — pifb-signature-problem

## Concession

Blue's two-half partition is sound, and the bulk of half (b) — completeness of disclosure — survives. I concede the following, plainly and without reservation.

The trace algebra is correct. With $T=\mathrm{diag}(1,-1)$, $\mathrm{tr}(T^2)=2$; $A_\tau=i(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)T$, $A_x=(\partial_x\psi_x)T$ give $G_{\tau\tau}=-2(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)^2$, $G_{xx}=+2(\partial_x\psi_x)^2$, $G_{\tau x}\in i\mathbb{R}$, the complex form has $\det=0$ (rank one), and $\mathrm{Re}(\cdot)$ yields signature $(-,+)$ (rank two). Both panels' sympy agree, line for line with :2883–2889 (`01b_extended_evidence.md`). The Sylvester count for the causal-cone metric is exact [Horn & Johnson, *Matrix Analysis* 2nd ed. §4.5]. The group facts are standard and correctly stated: $\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb{C})\cong\mathrm{Spin}^+(1,3)$ [Hall, *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* ch. 5], $\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)$ as the local frame group with $\Lambda^\top\eta\Lambda=\eta$ verified, the spinor/vector representation distinction at :2900. The "existence demonstration, not derivation" framing is the manuscript's own repeated self-description (:2866, :2902, :2907, :2952), and it is honest.

I retract one line from my own panel. My gauge-theorist and philosophy-of-science memos treated the single-generator collapse and the $\pm\mathrm{tr}$ convention as buried or under-disclosed dependencies. They are not. The manuscript discloses the convention dependence explicitly at :2868 ("the sign relation in the calculation below would be reversed, and the construction would not produce the desired Lorentzian split"), discloses the collapse as load-bearing for the sign at :2872 ("the signature conclusion of this worked example depends on this single-generator choice ... the indefinite signature would not emerge") and repeats it at :2902, and discloses the real-only mixed-generator route at :2950. On those four items the *completeness* clause holds. The algebraic observation that indefinite signature is generic for a non-compact generator under $+\mathrm{tr}$ [Knapp, *Lie Groups Beyond an Introduction* 2nd ed. ch. VI, Cartan's criterion] is correct, but it is an overclaim point, not an undisclosure point, because the manuscript states the dependence at :2868 and :2950.

## Core attack

Two prongs survive. The first is a single, precise failure of the *completeness* clause. The second, which carries the weight, is that even granting complete disclosure, "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" is not earned.

**Prong one — the one ledger item that is genuinely incomplete: the sign consequence of the separability ansatz.** Blue's 14-item ledger lists the separability ansatz at :2877 and credits it as disclosed. Read the actual sentence: the manuscript restricts to $\psi_\tau=\psi_\tau(\tau)$, $\psi_x=\psi_x(x)$ "for the displayed calculation," and states that without it the form "would acquire mixed-derivative contributions that the displayed result suppresses." That is a disclosure of a *display simplification* — terms dropped to keep the displayed equation clean. It is not a disclosure that those terms can flip the sign of the temporal entry. They can. Dropping separability, $\mathrm{Re}(G_{\tau\tau})=2\big((\partial_\tau\psi_x)^2-(\partial_\tau\psi_\tau)^2\big)$ (sympy, `01b_extended_evidence.md`, red panel), which is *positive* whenever the cross-derivative dominates — the timelike direction becomes spacelike and the signature is no longer guaranteed $(-,+)$. The ledger item names the ansatz; it does not disclose that the ansatz is load-bearing for the *sign*, which is the only quantity the section is about. Contrast :2872, where the manuscript does exactly the right thing for the single-generator collapse — names the dependence *and* states the sign consequence ("the trace identities below would change sign"). The asymmetry is the strike: the collapse's sign-dependence is disclosed; separability's sign-dependence is not. The completeness clause — disclosure "accurately and completely" — fails on this one item, and that is the surviving falsifier Blue itself named in its Falsification condition 3.

**Prong two — the load-bearing line: "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" overclaims even under complete disclosure.** A pseudo-Riemannian metric is by definition a smooth, symmetric, *non-degenerate*, *real* $(0,2)$-tensor [Lee, *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* 2nd ed. ch. 13; O'Neill, *Semi-Riemannian Geometry* 1983 ch. 3]. The object the framework's construction actually produces is $G_{\mu\nu}=\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$, and that object is rank-one and complex — the manuscript's own $\det=0$ at :2887. It is not a metric, by two independent failures (degeneracy and non-reality). The Lorentzian metric of :2889 is not in the image of the framework's construction; it is in the image of (framework) composed with the map $G\mapsto\mathrm{Re}(G)$, and the manuscript certifies at :2892 that this map has "no physical principle in the construction that mandates" it, with "no Wick counterpart." So the honest content is: the framework produces a non-metric, an admittedly unmotivated rank-changing projection is applied by hand, and the *result of the projection* is Lorentzian. "Structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" attributes to the framework a property that belongs to the analyst's projection step. The accurate phrasing is the one Blue conceded in advance: "compatible after two postulates, including an unmotivated rank-changing projection."

The info-geometer prong sharpens why this is not pedantry. The signature lives entirely on $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$, which the manuscript states (:2902) is "not of the statistical geometry within a single fiber," while the Fisher-Rao metric — the unique sufficient-statistic-invariant metric [Čencov 1972; Amari & Nagaoka, *Methods of Information Geometry* 2000 ch. 2], the object that makes this *information* geometry — stays positive-definite throughout. The variational principle is blind to the sign-producing choice: :2952, "the KL functional between real Gaussian beliefs is invariant under that choice." A signature placed on a sector that the unique information metric and the free-energy functional do not touch is not an information-geometric quantity. For an "it from bit" program, "structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" then means only that one can adjoin an indefinite non-statistical bilinear form to a positive-definite statistical one — compatible in the sense that appending $-dt^2$ to a Riemannian metric is "compatible with Lorentzian signature."

## Defense

Blue's strongest move is the disclosure ledger, and I have conceded thirteen of its fourteen items. But the ledger answers a question the claim does not ask. The claim asserts the construction is "structurally compatible **with Lorentzian signature**." Complete disclosure of the postulate stack establishes "structurally compatible with [imaginary $\phi_\tau$ + single-generator collapse + separability + unmotivated $\mathrm{Re}(\cdot)$ projection] $\Rightarrow$ Lorentzian." Those are different propositions, and the gap between them is the overclaim. Disclosing that a step is unmotivated does not convert the result into a property of the framework; it documents that the result is a property of the chosen step.

The decisive test is Popper's demarcation [Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* §6, §31–33]: a statement is content-bearing about a framework only if the framework forbids its negation. This framework forbids no signature. By the manuscript's own disclosures it yields $(-,+)$ with an imaginary generator (:2889), Riemannian $(+,+)$ with a real or compact generator (:2872 names the compact $T_x$ that prevents the indefinite split), and — per red-panel sympy (`01b_extended_evidence.md`) — $(-,-)$ with a compact $T_x$ and diagonal $T_\tau$. A construction equally compatible with $(-,+)$, $(+,+)$, and $(-,-)$ does not possess "compatibility with Lorentzian signature" as a feature; that selectivity is supplied by the analyst's generator-and-projection choices. Blue grants exactly this in its registered concession ("if the standard ... requires the signature to be coupled to the statistical content ... then the phrase should read 'compatible after two postulates'"). The debate's standard *is* that the phrase be earned by the framework rather than by the pen, because the section sits inside an "it from bit" derivation of spacetime from information, where the whole point is that the signature reflect the statistical content. Under that standard the phrase overclaims and the existence-demonstration core is the only defensible residue — which is the outcome Blue itself flagged as the live one.

The causal-cone route does not rescue the phrase. Its Postulate 3 (finite maximal information speed) contradicts the framework's own dynamics: the implemented first-order natural-gradient flow is parabolic in the continuum limit, with infinite propagation speed and no characteristic cone [Evans, *Partial Differential Equations* 2nd ed. §2.3–2.4], a tension the manuscript discloses and concedes is unresolved — "none of these is currently realized" (:2929). A route conditional on dynamics the framework does not have is an existence statement about a hypothetical replacement framework, not about this one; "along either pathway the framework is structurally compatible with Lorentzian signature" (:2932) inherits the same overclaim through the second pathway as through the first.

I discount no red memo. The geometer prong (non-metric native object) and info-geometer prong (decoupling) are merged into prong two; the gauge-theorist prong is demoted to supporting the genericity point with its "undisclosed" framing retracted per the concession above; the philosophy-of-science prong (Popper demarcation) is the load-bearing thread of the defense; the numerical-analyst prong supplies the causal-cone closing.
