# Red Rebuttal ‚Äî subclaim-C-qk-identification

## Concession

I grant blue's first evidence point in full: the algebraic identity `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T = U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` is exact for any thin SVD of `W_Q^a, W_K^a` with full column rank, and therefore the bilinear form `Œº_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T Œº_j = (U_Q^{aT} Œº_i)^T M_h^a (U_K^{aT} Œº_j)` holds as a matrix identity (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1247‚Äì1252`). No falsification on the bilinear-form identity itself is available.

I also grant blue's own pre-concession in falsification condition 6 and in the explicit final paragraph: the stronger reading ‚Äî gauge identification on `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T ‚àà GL(d_model)` ‚Äî is indefensible. Blue states this verbatim at lines 41 of `02_blue_opening.md`. That admission is doing all the work in the rest of the rebuttal.

## Core attack

Blue's load-bearing move is the elision between two distinct objects: the standard's inner-product score (a `d_model`-vector inner product) and the head-space bilinear form (a `d_head`-vector inner product). The claim under debate states the identification "recovers the standard inner-product score `Q_i^T K_j = Œº_i^T Œ©^{-T} Œº_j`" (`00_claim.md:11`). The standard inner-product score in [Vaswani2017 ¬ß3.2.1] is the `d_k`-vector dot product `Q_i^T K_j` where `Q_i = (W_Q^a)^T Œº_i ‚àà ‚Ñù^{d_head}` and `K_j = (W_K^a)^T Œº_j ‚àà ‚Ñù^{d_head}`. The full ambient-space bilinear form is `Œº_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T Œº_j ‚àà ‚Ñù`. Blue's identity rewrites this as `(U_Q^{aT} Œº_i)^T M_h^a (U_K^{aT} Œº_j)`, which is a bilinear form on the *images* `U_Q^{aT} Œº_i, U_K^{aT} Œº_j ‚àà ‚Ñù^{d_head}`. These projected vectors are **not** the standard's `Q_i, K_j` ‚Äî those are `(W_Q^a)^T Œº_i = A_Q^{aT} U_Q^{aT} Œº_i`, with the invertible factor `A_Q^a` *folded into the query/key vector*, not into the kernel. The gauge identification puts `M_h^a = A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T` on the kernel side and leaves bare projections `U_Q^{aT} Œº_i` on the vector side; the standard puts the identity kernel on the kernel side and leaves the full `(W_Q^a)^T Œº_i` on the vector side. These are not the same parameterization of the same operation ‚Äî they re-allocate the invertible factor across kernel vs. vector. The identity holds at the level of the scalar logit; it does not hold at the level of "the standard inner-product score."

The evidence pack itself flags this in ¬ß5 (lines 73‚Äì74 of `01_evidence.md`): "Operationally, `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` with `U_Q^a` orthonormal columns; the standard transformer learns `W_Q^a` as a single parameter, and `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)` are derived via SVD ... Mathematically related by a deterministic decomposition; structurally different." Canon pitfall #10 (`01_evidence.md:55`) is exactly the failure mode: "Conflating learned attention with derived attention. ... When comparing, ensure the comparison is apples-to-apples." The standard's learned object is `W_Q^a`, full stop; `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)` are post-hoc SVD factors. The gauge framework's `(œÉ, Œ©)` are also learned (or derived from learned beliefs). The identification between them passes through the SVD-derived `M_h^a`, not through a learned-parameter correspondence. This is not apples-to-apples in the canon's sense.

Blue's defense of falsification condition 6 ("operational equivalence above parameterization identity") cannot survive a re-reading of [Vaswani2017 ¬ß3.2.1]: the standard defines `W_Q^i ‚àà ‚Ñù^{d_model √ó d_k}` as a single learned matrix (canon ¬ß1 at `01_evidence.md:46`). The thin-SVD factorization exists for any matrix because every matrix has an SVD ‚Äî that is a property of `‚Ñù^{m√ón}`, not a structural property of multi-head attention. Blue's argument that "the standard's operation is decomposable, even if the standard's parameterization is not" (`02_blue_opening.md:39`) is the precise definition of a post-hoc identification: a property of `‚Ñù^{m√ón}` is being read backward into [Vaswani2017]'s design.

## Defense

My opening's central charge ‚Äî that the identification operates on a derived sub-form rather than on the standard's actual content ‚Äî survives blue's defenses for three reasons.

First, blue's "reachable bilinear-form sets coincide" argument (`02_blue_opening.md:19`) is a value-level equivalence claim, not the parameter-level identification the claim asserts. Two parameterizations that produce identical bilinear-form images give identical attention logits and identical forward passes; they do not give identical gradients, identical training dynamics, or identical structural content. The first-debate verdict restricted scope to forward attention (`01_evidence.md:68`), and I accept that scope. Within forward attention, the value-level claim is true; the structural claim ("recovers the standard inner-product score") requires reading "standard inner-product score" as "scalar attention logit," not as Vaswani's `Q_i^T K_j`. The claim's wording at `00_claim.md:11` writes `Q_i^T K_j = Œº_i^T Œ©^{-T} Œº_j` ‚Äî but the right-hand side is a `d_model`-space bilinear form while the left-hand side is a `d_head` dot product. They are scalar-equal under the SVD identity; they are not the same algebraic object.

Second, blue's "the manuscript discloses the structural decomposition" defense (`02_blue_opening.md:17`) is itself an admission that the manuscript needed extra paragraphs at lines 1243‚Äì1250 to repair an over-strong claim at line 1240. Line 1240 writes `W_Q W_K^T = œÉ^{-2} Œ©^{-T} ‚àà GL(d_k)`. Read literally, `d_k` is the standard's per-head dimension; under that reading the equation is consistent with the manuscript's later clarification. But the prose at line 1240 does not announce that `W_Q W_K^T` denotes anything other than what the symbol means in [Vaswani2017] ‚Äî the full ambient-space kernel `‚Ñù^{d_model √ó d_model}` of rank ‚â§ `d_head`. The clarification at lines 1243‚Äì1250 narrows the identification to `M_h^a ‚àà GL(d_head)`, which is the invertible factor of the SVD, not the standard's parameter. Blue calls this "honest scoping." It is honest, but it is also a downgrade: the identification originally written as `W_Q W_K^T = œÉ^{-2} Œ©^{-T}` becomes `M_h^a = œÉ^{-2} Œ©^{-T}`, and the lift `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T = U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` is the part that the gauge framework does *not* match ‚Äî it is shared with the standard by construction (every matrix has an SVD), so "sharing the isometric factor verbatim" is content-free.

Third, canon pitfall #2 (`01_evidence.md:51`) is not "a separate concern" as blue claims. The pitfall says "many Œ© satisfy this" because `W_Q W_K^T` is rank-deficient. The gauge ‚Üí transformer direction blue defends (every `M_h^a ‚àà GL(d_head)` lifts to a unique `U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` for fixed isometric factors) is well-defined only after one *chooses* the isometric factors. Those factors are derived from `W_Q^a, W_K^a` via SVD ‚Äî they are not part of the gauge framework's learnable content. So the lift "gauge ‚Üí transformer" reads: pick a thin SVD of the standard's `W_Q^a, W_K^a`, extract the isometric factors, then declare `M_h^a = A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T = œÉ^{-2} Œ©^{-T}`. The gauge framework supplies the head-space invertible factor; the standard supplies the isometric subspace embedding. Half of the standard's content (the column-space selector) is borrowed; half (the head-space transform) is matched. That is a sub-form match, not a full match.

The claim is defensible at the level of the scalar attention logit value (granted to blue via the SVD identity), and not defensible at the level of "recovers the standard inner-product score `Q_i^T K_j`" as written in `00_claim.md:11`. The standard's `Q_i, K_j` are `d_head`-vectors built from `(W_Q^a)^T Œº_i = A_Q^{aT} U_Q^{aT} Œº_i`; the gauge framework's analog is `U_Q^{aT} Œº_i` with the invertible piece pushed into the kernel. The claim conflates "scalar logit equality" with "inner-product score recovery." Granted on the former, falsified on the latter.
