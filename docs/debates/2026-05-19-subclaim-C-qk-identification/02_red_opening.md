# Red Opening — subclaim-C-qk-identification

## Steelman (opposing position)

Every matrix admits an SVD, so for any rectangular learned `W_Q^a, W_K^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` of full column rank, the thin-SVD factors `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` and `W_K^a = U_K^a A_K^a` exist with `A_Q^a, A_K^a ∈ GL(d_head)`; the bilinear form `μ_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T μ_j` is identically equal to `(U_Q^{a,T} μ_i)^T M_h^a (U_K^{a,T} μ_j)` with `M_h^a = A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T ∈ GL(d_head)`, so identifying `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ ↔ M_h^a` is a value-exact reading of the attention score in the invertible head-space factor that Vaswani's construction produces in every instance.

## Position

The identification `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ ↔ M_h^a` is a value-level equality of the bilinear form on a derived sub-object, not a structural recovery of the standard transformer's per-head construction; calling it "recovery of the standard inner-product score" overstates what is reduced, because (i) Vaswani's per-head `W_Q^a, W_K^a` are *atomic learned parameters*, not compositions `U_Q^a A_Q^a` factored by the framework, (ii) the standard ambient logit kernel `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_model}` is rank-deficient and is not in `GL(d_model)`, so the asserted membership in `GL(d_k)` at `:1240` either silently switches to `d_k = d_head` (a redefinition) or is false on `d_k = d_model`, and (iii) the framework's mapping `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ ↔ M_h^a` is many-to-one on the standard side (the SVD factor `A_Q^a` is determined by `W_Q^a` only up to right-orthogonal mixing on degenerate singular subspaces, and `(σ, Ω)` is determined by `M_h^a` only up to the scale-transport trade `σ² ↦ cσ², Ω ↦ c·Ω`), so the identification is neither injective forward nor backward as a structural map.

## Evidence

- **Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1 — atomic learned parameters.** `external_canon_transformers.md:25–30` reproduces the standard multi-head construction verbatim: `head_i = Attention(X W_Q^i, X W_K^i, X W_V^i)` where "each head uses its own `W_Q^i, W_K^i, W_V^i ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_k}` with `d_k = d_model/h`." The standard's learnable parameters are the rectangular matrices `W_Q^i, W_K^i, W_V^i` themselves. No `U_Q^a, A_Q^a` split appears in [Vaswani2017]; the SVD decomposition is a *derived* algebraic identity introduced by the gauge framework, not a feature of the standard construction. The manuscript's own multi-head text at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1709` acknowledges this: "Each head computes attention independently using separate projection matrices `(W_Q^a, W_K^a, W_V^a)`" — there is no `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)` pair on the standard side.

- **Canon pitfall #10 — apples-to-apples.** `external_canon_transformers.md:147` (verbatim): "Conflating learned attention with derived attention. The user's `GL(K)` gauge transformer has *no* learned QKV projections — attention emerges from the KL on gauge-transported beliefs. Standard transformers have learned QKV. When comparing, ensure the comparison is apples-to-apples." Identifying `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` (a gauge object derived from the KL on transported beliefs) with `M_h^a` (a *derived* invertible factor of the SVD of the standard's learned parameter) is exactly the conflation this pitfall warns against: the standard's *learned* parameter is `W_Q^a`, and the identification is being asserted at the level of `A_Q^a`, which is one factor of an SVD that the standard never constructs.

- **Canon pitfall #2 — non-uniqueness.** `external_canon_transformers.md:139` (verbatim): "`W_Q W_K^T = σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` ... is an *identification* between learned projections and gauge transformations. The agent should check whether the identification is unique (it isn't — `W_Q W_K^T` is rank-deficient in general; many `Ω` satisfy this)." This is the canon's own anticipation of exactly the rank gap the subclaim now tries to repair by descending to `M_h^a`. The repair does not resolve the non-uniqueness: even on `M_h^a`, the equation `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ = M_h^a` is invariant under the rescaling `(σ², Ω) → (c σ², c Ω)` for any `c > 0`, so the gauge object that the standard's `M_h^a` corresponds to is determined only up to a one-parameter family — i.e., the identification is many-to-one as a structural map.

- **Manuscript admission of the gap (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1252`).** The manuscript itself states: "The gauge-theoretic identification `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ ↔ M_h^a` operates at the level of the invertible head-space factor, not the ambient low-rank kernel." This is a *disclosure of the descent*, not a closure. The descent moves the identification off the actual learned parameter (`W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T`, low-rank in `ℝ^{d_model × d_model}`) and onto a derived invertible factor (`M_h^a ∈ GL(d_head)`) that the standard transformer's training procedure does not see as a parameter.

- **Dimension switch in the GL membership (`:1240`).** The headline `W_Q W_K^T = σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ ∈ GL(d_k)` at line 1240 places the product in `GL(d_k)`. With Vaswani's per-head matrices `W_Q^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}`, the ambient product `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_model}` has rank ≤ `d_head < d_model = d_k`, so it is *not* in `GL(d_k) = GL(d_model)`. The only way to read line 1240 as true is to interpret `d_k` as `d_head` — but then the equation is on the invertible head-space factor `M_h^a`, which is precisely the descent the rectangular-projection paragraph at `:1245–1252` discloses. The headline therefore either misstates the ambient dimension or silently denotes `d_k = d_head` (a redefinition relative to Vaswani's `d_k = d_model/h` for the per-head dimension *only when interpreted as per-head*; in many conventions `d_k = d_model` for the un-split QK space). The claim cannot be true on `GL(d_model)`; it is true only after the SVD-factor descent, which is a value-level reading of the bilinear form, not a structural reduction.

- **Value-equivalence ≠ parameter-equivalence.** The bilinear-form equality `μ_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T μ_j = (U_Q^{a,T} μ_i)^T M_h^a (U_K^{a,T} μ_j)` is an SVD identity and holds for any rectangular `W_Q^a, W_K^a`. This is a value-level fact and is not in dispute. What *is* in dispute is the framework's identification of `M_h^a` with `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ`: this is a parameter-level claim about a derived object. Standard transformer training updates `W_Q^a` directly via gradients of the cross-entropy loss; the implied gradients on `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)` are coupled (the SVD factors are not independent learnable quantities) and differ in their natural-gradient geometry from any direct update on `M_h^a`. The claim phrase "recovers the standard inner-product score" is true only in the sense that "every bilinear form has an SVD"; it does not establish that the standard transformer's per-head construction *is* a gauge-theoretic construction, only that one can write the standard's bilinear form as a contraction with a derived invertible factor.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1 (or its accepted reading in the literature) defines `W_Q^a` as a composition `U_Q^a A_Q^a` with `U_Q^a` and `A_Q^a` as separately-learned parameters or as a structural decomposition rather than as a single rectangular learned matrix. If blue produces a citation to [Vaswani2017] or a standard textbook that defines `W_Q^a` as a compositional `(subspace embedding) ∘ (invertible head-space map)`, my "atomic-parameter" claim collapses.

2. The thin-SVD identification `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ = M_h^a` is shown to be uniquely defined as a structural map from `(σ², Ω)` to `M_h^a` (and inversely), without the `(c σ², c Ω)` rescaling indeterminacy I cited. If blue shows that an additional gauge-fixing constraint pins down `σ²` separately from `Ω` so that the map is bijective on the relevant parameter spaces, the non-uniqueness leg of my attack falls.

3. The headline equation at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1240` is read consistently with the multi-head SVD descent at `:1245–1252` *and* with the line `:1713–1721` (`Ω = (σ² W_K W_Q^T)⁻¹ ∈ GL(d_k)`) such that `d_k` everywhere denotes the per-head dimension `d_head`. If `d_k = d_head` is the manuscript's consistent convention throughout §5.2.2, my "dimension-switch" objection becomes a notation-clarification request rather than a structural defeat.

4. Blue produces an argument that "value-level equality of the bilinear form on the SVD's invertible factor" *is* the appropriate definition of structural identification in the gauge-theoretic framework — i.e., that the descent to `M_h^a` is *the* structural content of the claim, and the claim is correctly phrased as such. If the claim is read narrowly as "the gauge framework's transport `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` plays the role of the invertible factor `M_h^a` of the standard's bilinear form" — without the further assertion that this is a reduction to Vaswani's *parameter-level* construction — then the claim is defensible and my attack is reduced to a labeling objection. The judge must decide whether the subclaim, as stated, asserts the stronger structural identification or the weaker value-level identification.
