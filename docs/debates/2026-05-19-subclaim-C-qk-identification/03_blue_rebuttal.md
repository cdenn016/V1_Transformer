The bilinear form `őľ_i^T M_h^a őľ_j` IS the standard attention logit on the head subspace, up to the ŌÉ‚ĀĽ¬≤ scale and the ŌĄ = ‚ąöd_k temperature. This is not a contested algebraic identity ‚ÄĒ red's steelman concedes it (`02_red_opening.md:5`), and the canon's standard-form excerpt at `external_canon_transformers.md:38‚Äď40` (`01_evidence.md:37‚Äď40`) defines the attention score as `Q_i^T K_j = (őľ_i^T W_Q)(őľ_j^T W_K)^T = őľ_i^T W_Q W_K^T őľ_j`, which equals `őľ_i^T M_h^a őľ_j` on the head subspace by the thin-SVD identity (every full-column-rank rectangular matrix admits this factorization). Two functions that compute the same scalar on the same input are the same function. Identifying maps at the level of what they compute is the operational meaning of "recovers the standard inner-product score."

The choice between "atomic `W_Q^a`" and "compositional `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)`" is a parameterization choice, not a fact about the underlying object. Every full-column-rank rectangular matrix `W_Q^a ‚ąą ‚ĄĚ^{d_model √ó d_head}` admits a thin SVD `W_Q^a = U_Q^a ő£_Q^a V_Q^{a,T} = U_Q^a (ő£_Q^a V_Q^{a,T}) = U_Q^a A_Q^a` with `U_Q^a` having orthonormal columns and `A_Q^a ‚ąą GL(d_head)`. The standard transformer parameterizes the underlying linear map by its full rectangular matrix because SGD's Euclidean geometry is convenient on `‚ĄĚ^{d_model √ó d_head}`. The gauge framework parameterizes the same map by `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a)` because the natural-gradient geometry on `GL(d_head)` is the relevant Riemannian structure for the variational free-energy descent. Both parameterizations describe the same linear map `őľ ‚Üí W_Q^a őľ = U_Q^a A_Q^a őľ`. Red's claim that "the standard's *learned* parameter is `W_Q^a`, and the identification is being asserted at the level of `A_Q^a`, which is one factor of an SVD that the standard never constructs" (`02_red_opening.md:15`) confuses the parameterization of the linear map with the linear map itself. The linear map `W_Q^a` and the composition `U_Q^a A_Q^a` are the same map; SGD on the former and natural gradient on the latter are different optimizers acting on the same operational object.
