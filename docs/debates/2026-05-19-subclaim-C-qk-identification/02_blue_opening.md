# Blue Opening — subclaim-C-qk-identification

## Steelman (opposing position)

The thin-SVD factorization `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` is a derived post-hoc decomposition of an atomic learned parameter; [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] defines per-head `W_Q^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` as a single rectangular learnable, not a composition, so identifying the gauge transport with `M_h^a = A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T` matches a sub-form of the standard kernel rather than the standard's structural content, and the resulting GL(d_head) identification operates on a quantity SGD never updates as a unit.

## Position

The identification `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T} ↔ M_h^a` recovers the standard inner-product score `μ_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T μ_j` exactly — not approximately — as an algebraic identity on the bilinear form that determines attention logits. The standard's rectangular `W_Q^a` is operationally identical to the composition (isometric subspace embedding) ∘ (invertible head-space map); the gauge framework matches the invertible factor while sharing the isometric factor verbatim with the standard. The manuscript's lines 1245-1252 disclose this structural decomposition rather than paper over a gap, and the disclosure is what makes the identification precise.

## Evidence

- **Bilinear-form identity is exact.** For any rectangular `W_Q^a, W_K^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` with thin SVDs `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` and `W_K^a = U_K^a A_K^a`, where `U_Q^a, U_K^a` have orthonormal columns and `A_Q^a, A_K^a ∈ GL(d_head)` (assuming full column rank, which holds for generic SGD-trained matrices), the kernel admits the lift `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T = U_Q^a A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T (U_K^a)^T = U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1252`). The attention logits depend only on the bilinear form `μ_i^T W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T μ_j`, which equals `(U_Q^a^T μ_i)^T M_h^a (U_K^a^T μ_j)`. The two parameterizations produce identical logits for any input `(μ_i, μ_j)`.

- **The standard multi-head per-head map IS a composition.** [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] (canon `external_canon_transformers.md` §1): "each head uses its own `W_Q^i, W_K^i, W_V^i ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_k}` with `d_k = d_model / h`." The rectangular shape with `d_model > d_head` forces the column space of `W_Q^a` to be a `d_head`-dimensional subspace of `ℝ^{d_model}`; the action on `μ` therefore factors operationally as "project onto the column space (an isometric embedding after orthonormalization), then apply an invertible map within that subspace." Whether [Vaswani2017] writes the composition explicitly does not change the operation. The thin SVD names the two factors of the composition that the standard already performs.

- **The manuscript discloses the structural decomposition rather than hiding it.** `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1245-1252` states verbatim: "This identification concerns the bilinear form `M = W_Q W_K^T` that determines the attention logits, not the individual matrices `W_Q` and `W_K` separately. In standard transformer implementations, the per-head projection matrices `W_Q^a, W_K^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` are rectangular (with `d_model ≫ d_head`), and neither is individually invertible in the `d_model`-dimensional space. The ambient-space logit kernel for head a is the low-rank matrix `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_model}` with rank ≤ d_head, which is not an element of `GL(d_model)`." The manuscript then defines `M_h^a := A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T ∈ GL(d_head)` at `:1247-1250` and states the identification operates on the invertible head-space factor at `:1252`. This is honest scoping, not concealment.

- **Reachable bilinear-form sets coincide.** Standard SGD on `(W_Q^a, W_K^a)` parameterizes the bilinear form `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T`, which ranges over `{U_Q M U_K^T : U_Q, U_K ∈ St(d_model, d_head), M ∈ ℝ^{d_head × d_head}}` (Stiefel manifolds for orthonormal-column factors). The gauge framework parameterizes `Ω^{-T} ∈ GL(d_head)` per head (or, equivalently after multi-head restriction, the block-diagonal `Ω^a ∈ GL(d_head)` at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1721`). For fixed isometric factors `U_Q, U_K`, the reachable head-space bilinear forms `M_h^a` and the standard's invertible factor span identical sets (`GL(d_head)`, dense in `ℝ^{d_head × d_head}`). Since `M ∈ ℝ^{d_head × d_head}` is generically invertible (Lebesgue-almost-surely under SGD), the reachable attention-logit set is the same modulo measure-zero degenerate configurations. The parameterization differs (`(W_Q^a, W_K^a)` vs `(U_Q^a, A_Q^a, U_K^a, A_K^a)` vs `Ω^a`); the bilinear-form image does not.

- **Canon §10 pitfall #2 does not falsify the claim at the level it is stated.** The canon entry notes "`W_Q W_K^T` is rank-deficient in general; many `Ω` satisfy this." This is a statement about non-uniqueness of the inverse map (transformer → gauge), which the evidence pack §72 explicitly flags as "a separate concern" from the forward direction asserted by the claim. The claim is well-defined going gauge → transformer (every `M_h^a ∈ GL(d_head)` lifts to a unique `U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` for fixed isometric factors), and that is the direction asserted at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1252`.

- **Canon §10 pitfall #10 (no learned QKV in the gauge framework) is orthogonal to subclaim C.** That pitfall warns against conflating learned QKV with derived attention when comparing training dynamics or representational origin. Subclaim C is a *forward-attention identification* of bilinear forms, not a claim about how the bilinear form is learned. The first debate's verdict explicitly restricted scope to "forward attention, not gradients" (evidence pack §68), and subclaim C inherits that scope.

## Falsification conditions

The position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **The bilinear-form lift `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T = U_Q^a M_h^a (U_K^a)^T` fails as an algebraic identity for any choice of thin SVD.** A sympy session producing a rectangular `W_Q^a, W_K^a` for which the lift does not hold would falsify the structural claim. (This is a matrix-algebra identity; falsification is mathematically impossible, but I list it for completeness.)

2. **The claim is actually about ambient-space invertibility, not head-space invertibility.** If the manuscript at `:1240` is read as asserting `W_Q W_K^T ∈ GL(d_model)` rather than `∈ GL(d_head)`, then it conflicts with the rectangular rank gap and the claim collapses. The manuscript labels the equation `GL(d_k)` at `:1240` — if `d_k` is interpreted as `d_model` rather than `d_head` (the per-head dimension), the claim is false. The lines `:1245-1252` resolve the ambiguity by restricting to `GL(d_head)`, but a reader who stops at `:1240` may legitimately object.

3. **Reduction of attention is required at the parameter level, not the bilinear-form level.** If "reduces to standard attention" is read as "the gauge framework's learned parameters are isomorphic to the standard's learned parameters" rather than "produces the same attention logits as a function of belief means," then the SVD-factor identification fails. The first debate's verdict already restricted scope to forward attention; if a later judge re-opens the scope to parameter identity, this defense does not survive.

4. **The isometric factor `U_Q^a` is not shared between the gauge framework and the standard.** If the gauge framework's `Ω^a ∈ GL(d_head)` operates intrinsically on per-head subspaces but the standard's `W_Q^a` lift uses a *different* isometric embedding, then the bilinear-form match holds only at the head-space-projected level, and the lifted ambient forms differ. The manuscript at `:1252` states the isometric factor "selects head subspaces, consistent with a `GL(d_head)^H ⊂ GL(d_k)` decomposition" — if this multi-head block-diagonal embedding does not match the standard's per-head subspace partition, the lift-level identification fails.

5. **Generic SGD trajectories produce rank-deficient `A_Q^a` or `A_K^a`.** If, contrary to the Lebesgue-almost-sure expectation, training drives the head-space factors to singular configurations, then `A_Q^a, A_K^a ∉ GL(d_head)` and `M_h^a` is not in `GL(d_head)`. The claim conditions on invertibility of the SVD invertible factor; empirical evidence that real transformer training collapses this factor would falsify the identification at the level asserted.

6. **A reading of [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] insists `W_Q^a` is "atomic" in a structural sense that forbids decomposition.** If standard transformer scholarship is interpreted as forbidding the SVD identification on the grounds that `W_Q^a` is a "single learned parameter" rather than a composition, then the identification operates on a sub-form of the standard's content. This is the red reading. The defense rests on the fact that the SVD is an algebraic identity present whether or not [Vaswani2017] writes it: the standard's operation is decomposable, even if the standard's parameterization is not. If a judge weighs "parameterization vs operation" in favor of parameterization, the claim is downgraded from "recovers standard attention" to "recovers a derived sub-form of standard attention."

I cannot defend the stronger reading — that the gauge identification operates on `W_Q^a (W_K^a)^T ∈ GL(d_model)` — because that reading is false by the rectangular rank gap. The claim as stated (at the level of `M_h^a`) is defensible under conditions 1-5 above; it survives condition 6 only if the judge weights operational equivalence above parameterization identity.
