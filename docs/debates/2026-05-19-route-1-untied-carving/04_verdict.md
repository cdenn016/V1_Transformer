## Reasoning

The headline at `00_claim.md:11` states that Route 1 "reduces *exactly* to Vaswani et al. (2017) §3.2.1 scaled dot-product attention." The primary-source definition of that destination, fixed by canon §1, is the boxed form with no additive bias on the logits. Both sides agree on the underlying algebra: sub-claims α, β, γ, δ are conceded by both teams; the absorption of `r_j` into `log π_{ij}` is an exact softmax shift-invariance identity (not an approximation, in contrast to Route 2's LN-cancellation); the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` collapses `H_j` to constant `C^{-1}` exactly. The dispute is over sub-claim ε, and reduces to the question: is a content-dependent additive logit bias of the form `−‖K_j^sym‖² / (2τ)` within the strict reading of Vaswani §3.2.1?

Red's argument is that the strict reading is the primary-source reading: canon §1 fixes the base form as `softmax(QKᵀ/√d_k) V` with no bias, and canon §6 restricts the prior-as-bias equivalence to position-dependent biases. Content-dependent biases (a function of `μ_j` through `r_j`) are a step beyond what canon §6 sanctions, and red's sympy verification (`02_red_opening.md:16-24`) shows `∂r_j/∂μ_j = 2 Σ_j^{-1} μ_j ≠ 0` — the absorbed term is content-dependent, not a fixed prior. Blue conceded all three of these points: the strict-reading interpretation (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:4-5`), the canon-§6 scope restriction (`02_blue_opening.md:35`, falsification condition 2), and the RoPE-is-not-Vaswani-2017 point (`02_blue_opening.md:39`, falsification condition 4).

Blue's defended position narrows to "structural identification, not strict identity" (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:27`) — that Route 1 produces the canonical `QᵀK` inner-product structure with shared bilinear `C^{-1}`, and the resulting attention rule is a member of the family `{ softmax(QᵀK/√d_k + bias_j) V }` that Vaswani §3.2.1 sits inside as the constant-prior special case. This narrower claim is defensible on the evidence and the manuscript at line 1158 supports it (the manuscript itself names the j-only term a "key-side prior bias," distinct from the structural-bilinear recovery). But it is not the headline. The headline is the strict-identity claim with the word "exactly," and blue's own rebuttal grants that headline is "indefensible on the evidence" (`03_blue_rebuttal.md:49`).

The first debate's precedent reinforces this verdict but is not load-bearing. Route 2's RED_WINS was decided on the manuscript's "approximate" language at line 1198 — a different failure mode than Route 1's. Route 1 makes no approximation; the absorption is algebraically exact. Red's Evidence 3 cross-reference to the first debate's verdict argues that both routes land at the same quantity (`‖K_j‖²`), which red and blue both grant. But the cross-reference is not what decides this case. What decides this case is canon §1 directly: the strict-reading form has no additive bias, the absorbed bias is content-dependent (sympy-verified), and blue concedes the strict reading.

The judge does not split differences here. Red cited canon §1, canon §6, canon §10 pitfall 1, sympy verification, manuscript line 1158 self-characterization ("key-side prior bias"), and the manuscript's own restriction of the canon §6 equivalence to position-dependent biases. Blue cited the manuscript's narrower claim at line 1158 and the algebraic exactness of softmax shift-invariance — both of which red granted in the rebuttal (concessions 1-3 of `03_red_rebuttal.md:5-13`). The headline claim under adjudication is not the narrower manuscript claim; it is the debate-headline claim at `00_claim.md:11`, which both sides now agree is false under primary-source reading.
