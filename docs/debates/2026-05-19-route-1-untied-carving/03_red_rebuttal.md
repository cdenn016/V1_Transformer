The factorization through per-token vectors is structurally what Vaswani §3.2.1 requires for the `QK^T` inner-product slot. Sub-claim β is granted as a structural matter.

Third, softmax shift-invariance is an algebraic identity: `softmax_j(s_{ij} - c_j) = π_j' · exp(-s_{ij}) / Σ_k π_k' · exp(-s_{ik})` with `π_j' = exp(c_j)`. Sub-claim γ is mathematically valid as an algebraic move. The absorption of `r_j, log det Σ_j, log|det U_j|` into `log π_{ij}` is not approximate in the way Route 2's LN cancellation was approximate. Blue's manuscript-line distinction at the bottom of the Evidence section (Route 2 line 1198 "approximate" vs. Route 1 line 1158 no approximation language) is correct, and the first debate's load-bearing piece of evidence — the manuscript's own concession of "approximate" — does not transfer literally to Route 1.

These concessions narrow the contested ground to one issue: what kind of attention rule Route 1 actually recovers after the algebraically-exact absorption is performed.

## Core attack

Blue's own falsification condition 1 (`02_blue_opening.md` lines 33–35) concedes the headline under the strict reading. Blue writes: "If 'Vaswani §3.2.1' is read strictly as the uniform-prior boxed `softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` formula with NO additive bias permitted, then Route 1's `−r_j / (2τ)` bias falsifies the headline. The closure makes `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²` content-dependent (Evidence Pack lines 51–59); a content-dependent additive bias on logits is not in the boxed form. Under strict reading, the recovery is 'Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent key-norm regularizer,' not Vaswani §3.2.1."

This is the rebuttal. Blue concedes that under the strict reading of the headline, the recovery fails. The headline at `00_claim.md:11` says "reduces *exactly* to Vaswani et al. (2017) §3.2.1 scaled dot-product attention." The primary source — Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1, canonized at `01_evidence.md:94–102` and `external_canon_transformers.md` §1 — is the boxed form `Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(Q K^T / √d_k) V` with no additive bias. The canon at `01_evidence.md:86` is explicit: "No additive bias on the logits. Mask (`-∞` for masked positions) is an inequality-constraint extension, not a constituent of the base form." The strict reading is the primary-source reading, and blue grants that under it the recovery is to "Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent key-norm regularizer."

Blue's broad-reading defense rests on three load-bearing claims, each of which fails under primary-source scrutiny:

The "practical transformers always use additive biases" argument (`02_blue_opening.md:9`, ground (i)) conflates two distinct objects: (a) deployed transformer variants that stack additive biases on top of Vaswani §3.2.1 (causal masking, ALiBi, T5 relative bias) and (b) the Vaswani §3.2.1 base form itself. The headline says "Vaswani §3.2.1," not "any deployed transformer." Canon §6 at `01_evidence.md:88–92` is explicit: "Causal masking, ALiBi, and relative-position biases are *extensions* of the Vaswani §3.2.1 base form, implemented via additive logit biases. They are not constituents of the base form." Blue's argument is that the recovery target should be redefined from "the Vaswani §3.2.1 base form" to "practical attention with one bias slot occupied." That redefines the headline rather than satisfying it.

The "canon §6 sanctions the prior interpretation" argument (`02_blue_opening.md:9` ground (ii), expanded at lines 21) is a misapplication of the canon's scope. The canon at `01_evidence.md:90` establishes the prior↔additive-bias equivalence specifically for the causal-mask case (`π_j = 0 for j > i` ↔ `-∞ logit shift`). That equivalence is established for *position-dependent* `c_j` — a function of token position, not of the learned representation. The Evidence Pack flags this distinction explicitly at lines 74–78: "Position-dependent `c_j` (only depends on `j`'s position, not content) → ALiBi / causal mask / relative-position bias (Vaswani §3.2.1 + standard extensions). Content-dependent `c_j` (depends on `μ_j` through `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²`) → NOT a fixed prior; it's a content-dependent bias on the logits." Blue acknowledges this in falsification condition 2: "The canon §6 equivalence between 'non-uniform prior' and 'additive bias' was demonstrated for position-dependent biases; extending it to content-dependent biases is the broad-reading move the judge may reject." That extension is a move blue is asking the judge to grant, not a fact the canon supplies.

The "RoPE identification" defense (`02_blue_opening.md:9` ground (iii), expanded at line 23) lands at recovery of RoPE attention, which is Su et al. 2024 — a 2024 extension explicitly listed in canon §5 as a positional-encoding variant on top of the base Vaswani 2017 form. Blue's own falsification condition 4 grants this: "If the judge reads the headline 'Vaswani §3.2.1' as the specific 2017 construction with sinusoidal positional encoding (canon §5), then the RoPE identification at line 1160 recovers [Su2024RoPE] rather than [Vaswani2017]. RoPE is an extension of the Vaswani base architecture, not the base architecture itself." The headline says "Vaswani et al. (2017) §3.2.1," with a specific paper and a specific section. The strict reading is that the headline refers to that paper and that section, not to a 2024 paper that postdates it by seven years.

Blue's "frame-invariant function of raw content" gloss (`02_blue_opening.md:9`, mid-paragraph) requires examination. Blue writes: "`r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖² = μ_j^T C^{-1} μ_j` reduces to a *frame-invariant* function of the raw content `μ_j` — not a position-dependent bias added on top, but the same key-norm-squared scalar that the standard form's keys also carry in their inner-product expansion `‖Q_i - K_j‖² = ‖Q_i‖² + ‖K_j‖² - 2 Q_i^T K_j` before the i-cancellation step." This argument is mathematically backwards. In Vaswani §3.2.1 there is no `‖Q_i - K_j‖²` distance computation; the standard form is `Q^T K` directly, never expanded through a difference. The `‖K_j‖²` term blue references appears in the framework's KL-based derivation, not in Vaswani's construction. Pointing out that `‖K_j‖²` "would appear" in a hypothetical distance expansion that Vaswani §3.2.1 does not perform does not place that term inside the §3.2.1 base form. It instead identifies precisely the place where the gauge-theoretic derivation diverges structurally from Vaswani: the framework's reduction begins from a Bregman/KL distance whose expansion inevitably produces a key-norm term, while Vaswani §3.2.1 begins from a bilinear inner product whose construction never introduces one.

The cumulative picture is the load-bearing rebuttal. The first debate adjudicated Route 2's headline RED_WINS on the same content-dependent key-norm bias — `‖μ_j‖²` in the trivial-frame setting — that this debate's Route 1 produces as `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²` under the closure (`01_evidence.md:56–59`). Evidence Pack line 108 makes the equivalence explicit: "Under the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`, Route 1's `r_j` equals `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` — the same key-norm quantity Route 2 needed to cancel. Route 1 handles it by absorption into `log π_{ij}` rather than by approximate cancellation. The relocation does not eliminate the quantity; it relabels its handling." Both routes hit the same quantity; both reach the same structural endpoint of "Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent key-norm bias." The framework has no clean reduction to the strict §3.2.1 form on either route — and that is what the headline claims.

## Defense

My opening's load-bearing assumption was that the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` collapses `r_j` to a content-dependent scalar `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` of the j-th token's belief mean, and that this scalar — placed into the softmax's prior slot via absorption into `log π_{ij}` — is structurally an additive content-dependent logit bias and not a uniform prior. Blue's opening does not attack this calculation. Blue grants it explicitly at falsification condition 1: "The closure makes `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²` content-dependent (Evidence Pack lines 51–59)." The factual core of my opening is uncontested.

Blue's strategy is to argue that this content-dependent additive bias is *within* Vaswani §3.2.1 under the broad reading. That strategy has three failure modes, each documented in blue's own falsification conditions.

Against blue's "practical transformers" framing, the strict reading is what the headline requires. The claim at `00_claim.md:11` says "reduces *exactly* to Vaswani et al. (2017) §3.2.1," not "reduces to a practical transformer with one extension slot occupied." The Evidence Pack at line 128 fixes the reading discipline: "The headline claim says 'Vaswani §3.2.1' — primary-source reading is the strict one." Under the strict reading, the recovery target is the boxed `softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` with no additive bias, and blue's falsification condition 1 grants that Route 1 does not reach it.

Against blue's "canon §6 sanctions content-dependent priors" framing, the canon's actual demonstration is restricted to position-dependent biases (`01_evidence.md:88–92`). Blue's falsification condition 2 grants that the extension from position-dependent to content-dependent biases is "the broad-reading move the judge may reject." The canon does not perform that extension; blue asks the judge to perform it. Under primary-source reading discipline, the judge is not licensed to extend canon-demonstrated equivalences beyond their established scope.

Against blue's "RoPE identification" framing, RoPE is by canon §5 a positional-encoding variant on top of Vaswani — and the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` is automatic under RoPE precisely because `U_j ∈ O(d_k)` is orthogonal (`02_blue_opening.md:23`). Generic transformers without RoPE — including the original Vaswani 2017 with sinusoidal positional encoding — do not have per-token `U_j` at all (`01_evidence.md:120`). The closure is either vacuous or undefined in that setting, which means the reduction's natural-architecture identification is to a 2024 extension, not to the 2017 base form the headline cites. Falsification condition 4 grants this directly.

Falsification condition 5 (`02_blue_opening.md:41`) is the further structural problem blue concedes: "Outside the RoPE case ... the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` for shared SPD `C` is a substantive assumption on the variational posterior covariance field, not a derivable consequence. If the framework's E-step does not enforce this closure (or even structurally produce it as a fixed point), then Route 1's 'reduction' applies to a manifold of (`U_j`, `Σ_j`) tuples that the actual algorithm does not stay within." So the reduction either (a) lands at RoPE attention (not Vaswani §3.2.1, by condition 4) or (b) requires a substantive non-RoPE closure assumption the algorithm does not enforce (by condition 5). The two horns exhaust the case, and neither lands at the strict §3.2.1 headline.

The first debate's adjudication on Route 2 is the precedent that fixes the disposition. That verdict was RED_WINS because the framework's reduction to Vaswani §3.2.1 produced a content-dependent key-norm bias `‖μ_j‖²` that Vaswani §3.2.1 does not contain. Route 1, under the closure, produces the same quantity `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` (Evidence Pack lines 56–59). Blue's defense is that Route 1 differs by handling the quantity via algebraic absorption rather than approximate cancellation. That distinction is correct algebraically and irrelevant to the headline: the question is not how the quantity is handled but whether the resulting attention rule equals Vaswani §3.2.1. Algebraic-exact absorption of `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` into the softmax prior slot produces `softmax((Q^T K - ‖K_j^{sym}‖²/2 + const) / √d_k) V`, not `softmax(Q^T K / √d_k) V`. The first form is not the second.

The framework has a defensible reduction story — to "Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent key-norm regularizer," or equivalently to RoPE-style attention under the orthogonal-`U` identification. Blue's narrowed claim at `02_blue_opening.md:11` makes this scope explicit: "This is not the uniform-prior boxed form. It is the structural form of standard attention with the recognized additive-bias slot occupied by a key-norm regularizer." That narrowed claim is correct. The headline claim, that Route 1 "reduces *exactly* to Vaswani §3.2.1," is not.
