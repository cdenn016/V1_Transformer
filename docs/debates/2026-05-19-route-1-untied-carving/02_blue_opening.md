# Blue Opening — route-1-untied-carving

## Steelman (opposing position)

Red's strongest move: under the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`, the j-only term `r_j = μ_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j` evaluates to `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` (Evidence Pack §"What is `r_j`", lines 51–59), which is a content-dependent function of the j-th token's belief mean. The boxed form in [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] is `softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` with no additive bias on the logits (canon §1). Absorbing `r_j(μ_j)` into `log π_{ij}` puts a content-dependent quantity into the softmax's prior slot — that is not a prior in the standard sense (a prior is fixed before evidence), so the headline "Route 1 reduces *exactly* to Vaswani §3.2.1" is at best a recovery of "Vaswani plus a content-dependent additive bias" — i.e., the same key-norm-bias the first debate's verdict already adjudicated against on Route 2.

## Position

Route 1 reduces to standard scaled dot-product attention as written in Vaswani §3.2.1, with the j-only `r_j` placed in the additive-prior/masking slot that the standard form algebraically supports through softmax shift-invariance. The recovery is structural: the same softmax-of-inner-products factorization through per-token vectors `Q_i`, `K_j` derived from per-token frames. Whether the headline "exactly Vaswani §3.2.1" is sustained depends on whether the additive-prior slot is read as "uniform π only" (strict reading — recovery fails) or "any j-additive logit bias, including the position-dependent biases used universally in practical transformers" (broad reading — recovery holds). I defend the broad reading on three grounds: (i) every practical Vaswani-style transformer uses an additive logit bias (causal mask, ALiBi, T5 relative bias) — uniform-prior pure §3.2.1 is a theoretical limit, not the deployed architecture; (ii) the canon itself (`external_canon_transformers.md` §6) treats the "non-uniform attention prior π_j" framing as algebraically equivalent to the standard "additive bias" interpretation; (iii) under the RoPE identification of `U_i` at manuscript line 1160, the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` holds automatically because `U_j` is orthogonal, and `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖² = μ_j^T C^{-1} μ_j` reduces to a *frame-invariant* function of the raw content `μ_j` — not a position-dependent bias added on top, but the same key-norm-squared scalar that the standard form's keys also carry in their inner-product expansion `‖Q_i - K_j‖² = ‖Q_i‖² + ‖K_j‖² - 2 Q_i^T K_j` before the i-cancellation step. The recovery is therefore of the *practical* Vaswani-§3.2.1 architecture, with the j-additive slot occupied — not of the uniform-prior theoretical limit.

The honest scope of the defended claim: Route 1 produces standard scaled dot-product attention with per-token frame-derived Q and K projections (`Q_i = C^{-1/2} U_i^{-1} μ_i`, `K_j = C^{-1/2} U_j^{-1} μ_j`, manuscript line 1158) and an additive j-side logit bias `−r_j / (2τ) = −‖K_j^{sym}‖² / (2τ)`. This is not the uniform-prior boxed form. It is the structural form of standard attention with the recognized additive-bias slot occupied by a key-norm regularizer.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds.

1. **Strict-reading constraint.** If "Vaswani §3.2.1" is read strictly as the uniform-prior boxed `softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` formula with NO additive bias permitted, then Route 1's `−r_j / (2τ)` bias falsifies the headline. The closure makes `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²` content-dependent (Evidence Pack lines 51–59); a content-dependent additive bias on logits is not in the boxed form. Under strict reading, the recovery is "Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent key-norm regularizer," not Vaswani §3.2.1.

2. **Content-dependent prior is not a "prior."** If the judge holds that "prior" must mean a quantity fixed before observing the j-th token's belief — i.e., a function of position or token-identity but not of the learned representation `μ_j` — then `log π_{ij} = −‖K_j^{sym}‖² / (2τ)` is not a prior. It is a content-dependent logit bias, structurally distinct from causal masking (position-dependent) and ALiBi (position-dependent) and T5 relative bias (position-dependent). The canon §6 equivalence between "non-uniform prior" and "additive bias" was demonstrated for position-dependent biases; extending it to content-dependent biases is the broad-reading move the judge may reject.

3. **First-debate verdict generalizes.** If the judge holds that the first verdict on Route 2 (RED_WINS on Vaswani §3.2.1 reduction) implicitly settled the more general question "does any reduction route that produces a content-dependent key-norm bias recover Vaswani §3.2.1?" then Route 1's reduction is settled adversely by stare decisis, regardless of whether Route 1's absorption-into-`log π_{ij}` is algebraically exact or Route 2's LN-cancellation was approximate. The mathematical content is the same `‖K_j‖²` quantity in both cases; only the rhetorical labeling differs.

4. **RoPE is not Vaswani §3.2.1.** If the judge reads the headline "Vaswani §3.2.1" as the specific 2017 construction with sinusoidal positional encoding (canon §5), then the RoPE identification at line 1160 recovers [Su2024RoPE] rather than [Vaswani2017]. RoPE is an extension of the Vaswani base architecture, not the base architecture itself. Under this reading, the natural-architecture recovery of Route 1 is RoPE-attention, which is one step removed from the strict §3.2.1 headline.

5. **Closure does not hold generically.** Outside the RoPE case (where `U_j` is orthogonal and the closure is automatic for any `C` commuting with the rotation block structure), the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` for shared SPD `C` is a substantive assumption on the variational posterior covariance field, not a derivable consequence. If the framework's E-step does not enforce this closure (or even structurally produce it as a fixed point), then Route 1's "reduction" applies to a manifold of (`U_j`, `Σ_j`) tuples that the actual algorithm does not stay within. The recovery would then be a recovery of a constrained subset of the framework, not of the framework itself.
