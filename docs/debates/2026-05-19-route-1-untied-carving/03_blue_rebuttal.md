# Blue Rebuttal — route-1-untied-carving

## Concession

I concede the strict-reading attack. Under the primary-source reading of "Vaswani §3.2.1" as the boxed formula `Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(Q K^T / √d_k) V` with no additive logit bias ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.1], `external_canon_transformers.md` §1), Route 1's reduction is **not** to Vaswani §3.2.1. The j-only term `r_j = μ_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j` evaluates under the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` to `‖K_j^{sym}‖² = ‖C^{-1/2} U_j^{-1} μ_j‖²`, which has nonzero gradient `2 Σ_j^{-1} μ_j` with respect to `μ_j` (red Evidence 1, sympy session, verified). The absorbed bias is content-dependent on the j-th token's belief mean. The headline's word "exactly" is therefore false under the strict reading. The honest destination is "Vaswani §3.2.1 plus a content-dependent additive key-side bias `-‖K_j^{sym}‖²/(2τ)`," which is a generalization of §3.2.1 (the additive-logit-bias slot is the same slot used by causal masking, ALiBi, and T5 relative bias — all introduced as extensions on top of §3.2.1, not as constituents of it; `external_canon_transformers.md` §6).

I additionally concede:
- Red Evidence 5 (the closure destroys the "genuinely untied" structural feature): correct as stated. Under `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`, the symmetric form `Q_i = C^{-1/2} U_i^{-1} μ_i`, `K_j = C^{-1/2} U_j^{-1} μ_j` makes the post-closure W_Q and W_K the same per-token projection up to the constant `C^{-1/2}`. The "inverse-transpose tying broken by the precision factor `Σ_i^{-1}`" claim at manuscript line 1148 holds for the *pre*-closure carving (Eq. `eq:gauge_qk`), not the *post*-closure symmetric form.
- The key-norm quantity `‖K_j^{sym}‖²` is the same family of content-dependent term as Route 2's `‖μ_j‖²/(2σ²)` (red Evidence 3, F5). Both are squared norms of the j-th key in their respective recovered attention rules.

These concessions narrow the defensible position. What remains is identifying what Route 1 actually achieves, and where red's argument over-reaches.

## Core attack

Red's argument has two load-bearing weaknesses.

**Attack 1 — the first-debate cross-reference imports the wrong verdict.** Red Evidence 3 imports the first debate's RED_WINS verdict on Route 2 and applies it to Route 1 as if the two operations were mathematically equivalent. They are not. The first debate's decisive evidence (`docs/debates/2026-05-19-reduction-to-standard-transformer/04_verdict.md`, line 19) was the manuscript's own "approximate" language at lines 1198, 1213, 1271, 1289 — specifically, the failure of approximate cancellation of `‖μ_j‖²/(2σ²)` under the LN+isotropic closure. The Route 2 quantity was supposed to be *cancelled* (set to zero, modulo approximation); the failure mode was that the cancellation only holds approximately and the manuscript admitted this in writing.

Route 1 makes no cancellation claim. The manuscript at line 1158 is verbatim: "Terms depending on j alone, namely `r_j`, `log det Σ_j`, and `log|det U_j|`, contribute a **key-side prior bias** that is absorbed into `log π_{ij}`." This is the *exact* softmax shift-invariance identity `softmax_j(s_{ij} + c_j) = π_j' · exp(s_{ij}) / Σ_k π_k' · exp(s_{ik})` with `π_j' = exp(c_j)`. There is no approximation. The first debate ruled on whether `‖μ_j‖²/(2σ²)` cancels (it does not); Route 1's question is whether `‖K_j^{sym}‖²/(2τ)` is legitimately re-labeled as a "prior bias" (a different question). The cross-reference confuses two different mathematical operations: approximate cancellation (Route 2, falsified at lines 1198–1289) versus exact absorption-by-shift-invariance (Route 1, line 1158).

**Attack 2 — red attacks a headline the manuscript itself does not assert.** The manuscript line 1158 states "standard attention with a single shared bilinear `C^{-1}` is recovered." This is the *structural-bilinear* claim: the post-closure form `μ_i^T M_{ij} μ_j` factors through per-token Q and K vectors with shared bilinear `C^{-1}`, i.e., the canonical `Q^T K` inner-product structure. The manuscript does *not* claim at line 1158 that the recovered attention has uniform prior; it explicitly names the j-only contribution a "key-side prior bias" — a separate, named feature of the post-closure form. The structural-bilinear recovery and the prior-bias feature are stated in the same sentence as two distinct items. The headline of this debate ("Route 1 reduces exactly to Vaswani §3.2.1") is a tighter claim than the manuscript's text at line 1158; red is correct that the tighter claim fails, but the manuscript's actual claim at line 1158 is structural (canonical `Q^T K` form, shared bilinear `C^{-1}`), not strict-Vaswani.

This matters because red's Evidence 4 reads the manuscript's "key-side prior bias" phrase as a concession that the destination is `softmax(QK^T/√d_k + bias)` rather than `softmax(QK^T/√d_k)`. That reading is correct — and the manuscript *says so*. The manuscript is not claiming strict Vaswani §3.2.1; the manuscript is claiming the canonical bilinear `Q^T K` structure with a key-side bias term it names explicitly. Red's attack on a stricter claim than the manuscript actually makes is sound against the debate headline; it is not sound against the manuscript's line-1158 text.

## Defense

The defensible blue position is **structural identification, not strict identity**: Route 1 carves the gauge-covariant KL into a form whose cross-term factors as a per-token `Q^T K` inner product (`eq:gauge_qk`, manuscript line 1145), with belief-covariance couplings vanishing under the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` to the constant `C^{-1}` (line 1158), so the recovered attention rule is `softmax( μ_i^T C^{-1/2} U_i^{-T} U_j^{-1} C^{-1/2} μ_j / τ + log π_{ij}^{eff})` — the canonical scaled-dot-product structure with a content-dependent additive prior bias absorbing the j-only residual. This is *not* Vaswani §3.2.1 strictly; it is structurally a member of the family `{ softmax(Q^T K / √d_k + bias_j) V }` that Vaswani §3.2.1 sits inside as the constant-prior case.

Four pieces of evidence support the structural claim and bound what is being defended:

1. **The KL decomposition is exact.** Manuscript line 1136: Eq. `eq:full_kl_general` "has been verified symbolically against the direct Gaussian KL to machine precision." Red does not contest this (sub-claim α stands). The cross-term factorization `x_i^T k_j = Q_i^T K_j` at line 1145 (Eq. `eq:gauge_qk`) is a clean algebraic identity, not an approximation. The structural carving into `Q^T K` form is exact.

2. **The covariance closure makes `H_j` constant.** Under `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`: `H_j = U_j^T Σ_j^{-1} U_j = U_j^T (U_j C U_j^T)^{-1} U_j = U_j^T U_j^{-T} C^{-1} U_j^{-1} U_j = C^{-1}`. The terms `tr(H_j P_i)` and `x_i^T H_j x_i` therefore become `tr(C^{-1} P_i)` (i-only, vanishes under j-softmax via shift-invariance) and `x_i^T C^{-1} x_i` (i-only, vanishes likewise). This is exact, not approximate. Sub-claim δ stands.

3. **The bilinear `M_{ij} = U_i^{-T} U_j^T Σ_j^{-1}` ranges over all of `GL(d_k)`.** Manuscript lines 1150–1155: "polar decomposition shows that `M_{ij}` ranges over all of `GL(d_k)`. The expressive power of the gauge-derived bilinear is therefore identical to that of an independently learned `W_Q W_K^T ∈ GL(d_k)` in standard attention." The pre-closure carving (before applying `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`) has the *full* expressive power of independently-learned untied projections; the closure constrains this to the shared-bilinear `C^{-1}` case. The general untied form is available without closure; the recovery to a *standard*-attention bilinear requires the closure.

4. **The rotary identification at line 1160 is one realization, not the destination.** Red Evidence 6 is correct that the rotary identification yields RoPE-style attention rather than vanilla Vaswani §3.2.1 with sinusoidal encoding. I grant that. But line 1160 is presented as *one* concrete identification of `U_i` with a real architectural choice, not as the unique destination of Route 1. The trivial-frame specialization at line 1161 (`U_i = U` for all `i`) is presented as a *separate* limit that hands off to Section `dot_product_derivation` (Route 2). Red Evidence 7 is correct that this hand-off inherits Route 2's failure mode for the residual key-norm bias; I grant that too. What survives is the *general* Route 1 form at lines 1145–1158: canonical `Q^T K` structure with the j-only term explicitly named as a key-side prior bias.

**Falsification scope of the defended position.** The structural-identification position is wrong if:
- (D1) The cross-term `-2 x_i^T k_j` does not factor as `Q_i^T K_j` for the per-token vectors at line 1145. (Refuted by line 1136's symbolic verification.)
- (D2) The closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` does not collapse `H_j` to a constant in `j`. (Refuted by direct algebra above.)
- (D3) The post-closure form is not in the family `{ softmax(Q^T K / √d_k + bias_j) V }` — e.g., contains an i-j cross-term that does not absorb into either `Q^T K` or the j-only slot. (Not refuted by any evidence in the pack; the absorption is into either the `Q_i^T K_j` term or the j-only `log π_{ij}` slot, with `tr(H_j P_i)` and `x_i^T H_j x_i` becoming i-only under the closure.)

**What the defended position does *not* claim.** It does not claim:
- That the recovered attention rule has uniform prior. It does not. The prior `π_{ij}^{eff} = exp(-r_j/(2τ) - log det Σ_j/(2τ) + ...)` is content-dependent on `μ_j` through `r_j = ‖K_j^{sym}‖²`. This is the same conclusion red reaches; I agree.
- That Route 1 recovers strict Vaswani §3.2.1. It recovers a canonical-`Q^T K`-structured attention rule whose prior slot contains a content-dependent key-side bias. This is one specific member of the broad family of additive-bias attention extensions (`external_canon_transformers.md` §6: causal mask, ALiBi, T5 relative bias all live in this slot), not the uniform-prior base form.
- That the "genuinely untied" feature survives the closure. Red Evidence 5 establishes it does not, and I conceded this above.

**Bottom line.** The strict-reading headline ("Route 1 reduces *exactly* to Vaswani §3.2.1") is indefensible on the evidence and red wins it. The manuscript's line-1158 text ("standard attention with a single shared bilinear `C^{-1}` is recovered") is a weaker, structural claim that survives: the carving exactly produces the canonical scaled-dot-product structure with shared bilinear `C^{-1}` and a separately-named key-side prior bias. The judge should rule on which claim the debate is adjudicating. If the headline as written, RED_WINS by the strict-reading argument red advances. If the manuscript's line-1158 claim as the manuscript states it, the structural-bilinear recovery stands but the headline's "exactly" word remains overstated.
