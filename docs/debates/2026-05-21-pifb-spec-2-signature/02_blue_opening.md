# Blue Opening — pifb-spec-2-signature

## Steelman (opposing position)

A subsection that confesses it does not derive Lorentzian signature has nevertheless slipped a derivation-shaped argument past the disclaimer: an imaginary Lie-algebra component plus a real-part projection plus a single generator are sequentially stacked as if each were a small technical step, and the cumulative result is presented as exhibiting Lorentzian structure rather than as four independent postulates with no dynamical glue.

## Position

Under the operational reading of the claim (rock-solid for a section on an open problem — open problem correctly named, every postulate explicitly listed, math under those postulates correct, no false claim of derivation), §2773-2903 carries. Specifically: (a) the worked-example algebra is symbolically verified, (b) the four loose postulates are each named in the text the moment they enter the derivation, (c) the standard-Wick distinction is stated as a non-equivalence rather than as an analogy, and (d) the section closes the loop by explicitly labeling itself "structural compatibility" rather than "derivation." The section is not rock-solid as a derivation of Lorentzian signature from variational dynamics — but the section does not claim to be such a derivation, and the operational reading binds.

## Evidence

- **Worked-example algebra verified symbolically.** With `T = diag(1,-1) ∈ sl(2,R)`, `tr(T^2) = 2`. With `A_τ = i(∂_τ ψ_τ) T` and `A_x = (∂_x ψ_x) T`: `tr(A_τ²) = -2(∂_τ ψ_τ)²`, `tr(A_x²) = +2(∂_x ψ_x)²`, `tr(A_τ A_x) = 2i(∂_τ ψ_τ)(∂_x ψ_x)`. Sympy session (executed): `T.is_symmetric()=True, trace(T)=0, trace(A_τ²) = -2*dpsi_t**2, trace(A_τ A_x) = 2*I*dpsi_t*dpsi_x`. The boxed metric at line 2843 follows. The Lorentz-boost check `Λ^T η Λ - η = 0` for `Λ = ((cosh ξ, sinh ξ),(sinh ξ, cosh ξ))` also verifies symbolically. This matches manuscript lines 2837-2839, 2843, 2851-2854 exactly.

- **SL(2,C) ≅ Spin+(1,3) is canonical.** Standard from [Nakahara2003 §10] on Lie groups and spinors (covering map, restricted Lorentz group), corroborated by Wikipedia "Lorentz group": "the indefinite spin group Spin(1, 3) is isomorphic to SL(2, C)" and "PSL(2,C) = SL(2,C)/{±I} is isomorphic to SO+(1,3)." The manuscript at 2799 and 2854 makes this identification correctly, and at 2854 explicitly notes the construction uses the vector representation in GL(4,R) ⊂ GL(4,C) rather than the spinor representation in GL(2,C) — separating two facts that are routinely conflated.

- **Sylvester's law claim at 2791 and 2874 is correctly applied.** Sylvester's law of inertia: for real symmetric `A` and any non-singular real `P`, the matrix `P A P^T` (a congruence, not a similarity in general) has the same number of positive, negative, and zero eigenvalues as `A` (Wikipedia "Sylvester's law of inertia"). The manuscript at 2791 invokes this for `Ω Σ Ω^T` with `Ω ∈ GL(K,R)` — exactly the congruence case Sylvester covers — concluding that real `GL(K,R)` transport on positive-definite `Σ` cannot flip the signature. At 2874 the same theorem is applied to the diagonal form `g = diag(-c_I², h_{ab})` with `h_{ab}` positive-definite, giving signature `(-,+,...,+)`. Both uses are textbook.

- **The Wick distinction is stated as a non-equivalence, not glossed.** Line 2820: "structurally analogous to a Wick-like continuation performed inside the gauge frame rather than on the base coordinates, with an additional real-part projection step that has no Wick counterpart." Line 2846: "The construction here is therefore not a direct Wick analog --- it is a Wick-like continuation in the Lie algebra plus an additional real-projection step that has no Wick counterpart." The standard Wick rotation `τ → iτ` on the base manifold yields a real metric without complex off-diagonal pieces; the manuscript states this explicitly and labels the projection step as a derivation gap.

- **Each postulate is named in-line at the point of use.** (i) Sector split at 2776-2781 ("the gauge action that transports beliefs ... is restricted to Ω ∈ GL(K,R)"). (ii) Imaginary `φ_τ` postulated at 2826: "we now \emph{postulate} that the temporal component is imaginary." (iii) Separable ansatz called out at 2831: "We further restrict to the separable ansatz." (iv) Sign-convention `+tr(AB)` vs `-tr(AB)` flagged at 2822 and at 2783. (v) Real-part projection flagged at 2846: "an additional choice, separate from Eq. complex_gauge_frame ... We flag this as a derivation gap." (vi) The 1+3 split flagged at 2812 as "fixed by the choice of a single imaginary frame direction rather than derived from free-energy dynamics." Each is named, none is silent.

- **The Regime-I status disclaimer at 2783 forecloses a standard reviewer objection.** A natural attack is "you call this a frame-twist signature mechanism but really you have a Yang-Mills `tr(FF)` action in mind." The manuscript pre-empts this at 2783: `A = U^{-1} dU`, `F_{μν} ≡ 0`, so `tr(F F^{μν})` vanishes identically and is not the mechanism; the mechanism is the gauge-noninvariant frame-twist `tr(A_μ A_ν)`. This is structurally honest — the frame-twist quadratic form is gauge-noninvariant under non-trivial gauge transformations (it transforms as a tensor only under base-coordinate changes that leave the frame `U` fixed), and the manuscript acknowledges this at 2783 by pointing to §consensus_metric.

- **The causal-cone route at 2860-2883 is independently rigorous and uses disjoint postulates.** The ADM-style decomposition `g = -c_I² dτ² + h_{ab} dx^a dx^b` is standard general relativity (Wikipedia "ADM formalism"; the metric form is also the rest-frame Bondi-Sachs form). Signature `(-,+,...,+)` follows by Sylvester. The conformal-class ambiguity is explicit at 2876, the dimension-count openness at 2877, and the tension with the framework's parabolic first-order dynamics is acknowledged at 2879-2880 with three candidate resolutions (telegraph limit, hyperbolic second-order dynamics, architectural finite-speed constraint), none claimed to be realized. The phrase "structural compatibility, not derivation" at 2883 binds.

- **The temporal-direction subsection at 2885-2903 explicitly resists self-promotion.** Eq. `lor_belief_metric` (2898) is labeled at 2900: "the leading minus sign is imposed by ansatz, not derived from the Fisher-Rao geometry." The Sylvester-blocks-sign-flip-under-SO(K) observation at 2901 is correct, and the alternative mixed-compact/non-compact route in `gl(K,R)` (with the Killing form indefinite on the symmetric complement of `so(K)`) is correctly noted as a parallel pathway — not claimed to extend to the full nonlinear case.

## Falsification conditions

This defense collapses under any of the following findings:

1. **Algebra error.** If the trace computation `tr(A_τ²) = -2(∂_τ ψ_τ)²` or `tr(A_τ A_x) = 2i(∂_τ ψ_τ)(∂_x ψ_x)` is wrong under the stated `+tr(AB)` convention with `T = diag(1,-1)`, the worked example collapses. (Verified symbolically — falsification cannot land here under the stated conventions.)

2. **Hidden derivation.** If any sentence in §2773-2903 says or implies that variational free-energy minimization \emph{selects} the imaginary `φ_τ` over real `φ_τ` of the same magnitude, or selects the 1+3 split over 2+2, the disclaimer at 2858 and 2903 is contradicted by the body, and the operational claim fails. Red must produce the offending sentence with line number.

3. **Standard-Wick conflation.** If any sentence in §2773-2903 asserts that the construction is the standard Wick rotation, rather than a Wick-like Lie-algebra continuation plus a real-part projection, the construction's distinction at 2820 and 2846 is contradicted and the section misrepresents the literature. Red must produce the offending sentence with line number.

4. **Sylvester misuse.** If the manuscript applies Sylvester's law in a setting where it does not apply (e.g., to a complex congruence, or to a non-symmetric form), the 2791/2874/2901 invocations fail. Verified above: 2791 invokes Sylvester for real `Ω ∈ GL(K,R)` acting by congruence on positive-definite `Σ`; 2874 invokes it for a real diagonal metric with positive `h_{ab}` and negative `-c_I²`. Both are textbook applications.

5. **Vector/spinor confusion at 2854.** If the claim that "the construction here uses the vector representation on the four-dimensional base" is internally inconsistent with the worked example actually presented (which is `T ∈ sl(2,R)` acting on `R²`), the section conflates the 2D worked example with the 4D extension. Reading 2854 carefully: the sentence at 2854 makes a forward-looking statement about the 4D extension and the parenthetical at 2799 confirms that the spinor `SL(2,C)` representation "is not directly invoked in the present construction." The 2D worked example is the defining rep of `sl(2,R)` acting on `R²` and is consistent with the 4D extension's vector rep — no inconsistency is present.

6. **Sector-split inconsistency across the manuscript.** If §consensus_metric or §pullback construction elsewhere in the paper uses a `GL(K,C)` transport on the belief fiber (contradicting the 2776-2781 restriction to `GL(K,R)` on the fiber), the sector split is locally honest but globally incoherent. This is the most credible attack vector — out-of-section consistency is what blue cannot fully audit from §2773-2903 alone. Red has a path here if such an inconsistency exists.

7. **A standard reference contradicts the Lie-algebra Wick literature claim.** If the manuscript's claim that "no Wick counterpart" exists for the Lie-algebra continuation is contradicted by a standard reference (e.g., a Schlingemann-type analytic continuation in the connection sector, a Donaldson-Witten Lagrangian continuation, or an explicit appearance in twistor theory), the "no Wick counterpart" sentence at 2846 is overstated. Red must produce the reference. If it exists, the disclaimer needs to be weakened to "no direct Wick counterpart in the standard QFT presentation."

The defense rests on the operational reading: the section is rock-solid \emph{as a section about an open problem with explicit postulates and verified math}, not as a derivation of Lorentzian signature from variational dynamics. Red wins by finding (1)-(7); otherwise the claim carries.
