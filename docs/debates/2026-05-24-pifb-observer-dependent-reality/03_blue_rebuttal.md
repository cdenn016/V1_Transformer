# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-observer-dependent-reality

## Concession

Three concessions, two substantive.

First, on Attack 2's strongest point. The literal ontological language at :3098 — "the noumenal manifold $\mathcal{C}$ exists as a mathematical substrate" — claims more than the mathematical role of $\mathcal{C}$ licenses. What the construction actually establishes is that $\mathcal{C}$ is the common base manifold of the belief bundle: a bare smooth manifold over which the agents' sections are taken, carrying differentiable structure but no metric [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4; Lee2013, smooth-manifold-vs-Riemannian-manifold]. An existence assertion phrased as ontology ("exists as a mathematical substrate") reads as a derived metaphysical conclusion when the apparatus supplies only a structural one. The honest reading — and the one the manuscript's own deferral to §phenomenological\_interpretation at :3098 signals — is the Kantian-analogy register: $\mathcal{C}$ is the perspective-independent "something" that grounds comparison without being knowable in metric terms, the structural parallel the manuscript elsewhere labels a "formal analogy," not a literal ontological derivation. The fix is a wording trim at :3098 so the existence clause travels in the analogy register rather than asserting a freestanding ontology. We grant this.

Second, on Attack 1's narrow form. Read in isolation, the bare headline "within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" (:3094) would overstate, because the within-species shared-geometry clause rests on the consensus construction that Debate 3 downgraded to a regulator-conditional heuristic (`docs/debates/2026-05-24-pifb-consensus-gauge-invariance/04_verdict.md`; canon chain [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4; PeskinSchroeder1995 §9.4; non-compact $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ infinite Haar]). The honest discipline is that the "not unconditional relativism" assertion must always travel with its conditional. We grant that this is a wording-consistency obligation — and observe that the :3094 passage already discharges it locally, conditioning the consensus anchor on "a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply" in the same sentence.

Third, the carry-over from the opening: pairwise $\beta_{ij}$ coupling is strictly weaker than a shared objective geometry [KullbackLeibler1951; BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]. It delivers frame-invariant pairwise disagreement and consistency pressure, not a single geometry all agents agree on. The manuscript says "informationally coordinated," not "geometrically agreed," and routes the shared-geometry ambition to the conditional consensus construction. Conceded, and owned in the text.

## Core attack

Red's Attack 1 turns entirely on relabeling an explicitly-flagged in-sentence conditional as a "concealed" or "promissory" debt. That relabeling is rhetorical, not a finding, and it misapplies its own demarcation citation.

The demarcation criterion red imports from Massimi is cross-perspectival *assessability* plus world-involving constraints, not a delivered shared metric. Perspectival realism is committed to "the existence of mind-independent things and to the historical and cultural situatedness of scientific knowledge"; it escapes relativism because "no scientific perspective can sanction the truth of its own claims; claims of knowledge must instead be assessable from the point of view of other scientific perspectives" [Massimi 2018, *Phil. & Phenom. Research* 96(2); Massimi, *Perspectival Realism*, OUP 2022]. The SEP states the negative form red relies on: relativism is the denial of "a neutral standpoint for adjudication between conflicting frameworks" [SEP, "Relativism"]. What demarcates is the existence of an assessment relation across perspectives, anchored to a shared world — not the prior delivery of a unique consensus metric.

The framework supplies exactly that assessment relation, and supplies it unconditionally. The cross-perspectival comparison is $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$: a frame-invariant measure of inter-agent disagreement, invariant because the Fisher metric and the KL divergence it generates are the unique structure invariant under sufficient statistics, present at $N=1$ before any consensus [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]. After transport $\Omega_{ij}$ aligns frames, this is a genuine standpoint from which agent $j$'s perspective is assessable from agent $i$'s — Massimi's "context of assessment." Red concedes this coupling is real ("nonzero coupling means beliefs constrain one another") but then treats it as irrelevant to demarcation. That is the error: the coupling is precisely the demarcating ingredient on red's own cited criterion.

Once that is seen, red's "IOU" reframing collapses to a claim about a single clause. Within-frame validity holds unconditionally (each $G_i$ is self-consistent). Cross-perspectival assessability holds unconditionally (frame-invariant KL after transport). Only the *within-species shared-geometry* clause rests on the regulator — and the manuscript flags it, in the same sentence, as conditional on "a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply" (:3094). A statement of the form "we are not unconditional relativists, because there is a within-species consensus structure conditional on a regulator we have not built" is the textbook structure of an honestly-qualified conditional, not a concealed debt. An explicitly-flagged conditional within the asserting sentence cannot simultaneously be a hidden promissory note; red's own quotation of :3094 supplies the flag.

Red's Attack 2 eliminability test is ill-typed and proves less than claimed. Red proposes to "keep only the frames $\phi_i$, the beliefs $q_i$, and the fixed Fisher fiber metric $g$, and never name $\mathcal{C}$." But each $\phi_i$ is a section $\sigma_i: \mathcal{C} \to E$ of the bundle — a map whose domain is the base manifold [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4]. A section with no named base is not a well-defined object, and the pullback $G_i = \sigma_i^* g$ is undefined without the domain it pulls back to. More directly: the manuscript's own load-bearing phrase "$G_i \neq G_j$ over the same underlying substrate" (:3086) has no referent if no common base is named — "the same substrate" is exactly the role $\mathcal{C}$ plays, and the disagreement relation red wants to preserve becomes ill-formed the moment the common domain is deleted. Red's test does not eliminate $\mathcal{C}$; it silently retains it inside the type of every $\sigma_i$ while declining to write its name.

## Defense

The position the section occupies is a named, coherent middle position in philosophy of science, and the manuscript's structure maps onto it one-to-one. Perspectival realism: perspective-independent states of affairs, perspective-dependent knowledge claims, cross-perspectival assessment [Massimi 2018; Massimi, *Perspectival Realism*, OUP 2022]. The manuscript's $\mathcal{C}$ is the perspective-independent states of affairs; the pulled-back geometries $G_i$ are the perspective-dependent knowledge claims (no agent's frame "sanctions its own truth" — :3086, "no privileged 'true' geometry"); the transport-mediated coupling $\beta_{ij}$ is the cross-perspectival assessment relation. "Within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" (:3094) is the position the canon names, qualified exactly as the canon qualifies it: assessability anchored to a shared substrate, not a god's-eye metric.

On the substrate role that survives the Attack 2 concession: $\mathcal{C}$ is metric-idle but structurally non-eliminable. The transport operator $\Omega_{ij}$ that makes "not solipsism" work compares fiber elements across frames using the connection, not a base metric — associated-bundle parallel transport requires the common base and a connection, and requires no metric on $\mathcal{C}$ [Nakahara2003 §10.3; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I]. So the structure that does the comparing (common base plus connection) exists, while the structure that would constitute a privileged global geometry (a distinguished metric on $\mathcal{C}$) does not — which is precisely "no view from nowhere" with inter-agent comparison intact. The fixed Fisher fiber metric being shared does not smuggle in a god's-eye base metric, because a fixed fiber metric does not descend to a canonical base metric absent a canonical section; distinct sections produce distinct pullbacks $\sigma_i^* g \neq \sigma_j^* g$ from one fixed $g$ [Lee2013, pullback of tensor fields; doCarmo1992 §1]. The concession we granted is therefore narrow and correct: the *ontological-existence language* at :3098 overstates, but the *role* of $\mathcal{C}$ as common base is mathematically required, not decoration. The metric-idleness of $\mathcal{C}$ is the content of "no intrinsic metric," not evidence of eliminability.

On "not solipsism" earned by the coupling, the variational reading confirms the manuscript's restraint. Coupling distinct variational factors $q_i$ via KL after a transport map is the standard multi-agent generalization of graphical/hierarchical FEP: it constrains approximate posteriors toward one another without producing a shared ground truth, because $q$ carries an irreducible approximation gap [Friston2017Graphical; JordanGhahramaniJaakkolaSaul1999; BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]. The manuscript's "informationally coordinated … not identical perceptions" (:3094) is exactly what coupled-$q$ semantics license — more than solipsistic isolation (the $q_i$ are genuinely constrained by one another), less than a shared posterior (no privileged frame). The section claims the band the canon supports and conspicuously does not claim the stronger shared-geometry reading.

## Falsification conditions

This defense fails, and the section is not "internally coherent and appropriately qualified," if any of the following holds:

1. The :3094 conditional is shown to be non-load-bearing — for instance, if the consensus construction is invoked elsewhere in the manuscript *without* the regulator hedge, asserting within-species consensus as a present property. That would convert the headline from an honestly-flagged conditional into a genuine concealed overclaim. (Argued unmet: the consensus anchor and its conditional appear in the same sentence at :3094.)

2. A formal construction is exhibited that reproduces $\Omega_{ij}$, $\beta_{ij}$, and "$G_i \neq G_j$ over the same underlying substrate" with no common base manifold named — which would falsify the bundle-base non-eliminability of $\mathcal{C}$ and vindicate Attack 2 in full. (Argued unmet: sections $\sigma_i: \mathcal{C} \to E$ are ill-typed without the base [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4].)

3. The cross-perspectival coupling $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ is shown to be frame-*dependent* — an artifact of arbitrary frame choice rather than a frame-invariant assessment relation — which would gut the perspectival-realism mapping and reduce "not solipsism" to a frame artifact. (Argued unmet: Cencov uniqueness gives GL(K)-invariance intrinsic to the divergence, present at $N=1$ [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2].)

4. The :3098 ontological-existence language is left unqualified in the final manuscript. This is the one condition we concede is currently met at the level of wording; the defense holds for the *role* of $\mathcal{C}$ and for the rest of the section, contingent on the :3098 register trim.
