# Memo — philosophy-of-science (BLUE) — pifb-observer-dependent-reality

## Steelman of the attack
The strongest red position: "within-frame validity + within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" is a distinction without a difference, because the only non-relativist anchor — the consensus metric — was downgraded in Debate 3 to a regulator-conditional heuristic that the manuscript does not construct (:3094). With the regulator absent, all that exists is per-agent perspectives plus pairwise coupling, which is de facto relativism wearing a promissory note. The Kantian appropriation, on this reading, dresses a relativist position in respectable transcendental-idealist clothing.

## Defense, derived from external canon
The claim under evaluation is a meta-claim: that the §"Observer-Dependent Reality" *presentation* is internally coherent and appropriately qualified. It is not the claim that observer-dependent reality is metaphysically correct. On that bar, the load-bearing fact is the position the manuscript actually occupies, which is a recognized, named, coherent position in philosophy of science: **perspectival realism** (Massimi).

Massimi's perspectival realism holds that "states of affairs about the world are perspective-independent, whereas our scientific knowledge claims about these states of affairs are perspective-dependent," and the perspective-dependence "doesn't lead to relativism because no scientific perspective can sanction the truth of its own claims; claims of knowledge must instead be assessable from the point of view of other scientific perspectives" — cross-perspectival assessment. This is explicitly framed as avoiding "both relativism and naive objectivism" [Massimi, *Perspectival Realism* (OUP 2022); Massimi 2018 "Four Kinds of Perspectival Truth," *Phil. & Phenom. Research*; CORDIS Perspectival Realism Project summary].

The manuscript's structure maps onto this one-to-one. The perspective-independent substrate is $\mathcal{C}$ (states of affairs); the perspective-dependent knowledge claims are the pulled-back geometries $G_i$ (no agent's frame "sanctions its own truth" — :3086 "no privileged 'true' geometry"); and the cross-perspectival assessment is the transport-mediated coupling $\beta_{ij}$ (:3088–3094, "agents can disagree about geometry while remaining informationally coordinated"). That is perspectival realism, not unconditional relativism. The position the manuscript names ("within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism," :3094) is exactly the position the canon names.

On the Kantian mapping: the manuscript says the noumena/phenomena identification is "taken up" in §phenomenological_interpretation and elsewhere calls the parallels "structural" (:3111, "structural parallels … remain formal analogies"). Checked against Kant: things-in-themselves exist but are unknowable and lack the spatio-temporal/metric structure the mind imposes — "Space and time are merely the forms of our sensible intuition of objects. They are not … properties of, nor relations among" things in themselves [SEP, *Kant's Transcendental Idealism*; Kant *CPR*, Transcendental Aesthetic]. The manuscript's "$\mathcal{C}$ … possesses no intrinsic metric, no preferred notion of distance, no distinguished temporal direction; all such structure is induced by agent beliefs through the pullback" (:3098) is a faithful structural parallel: metric/distance/time are contributed by the cognizing system, not intrinsic to the substrate. "No view from nowhere" is Kant's "no coherent sense to questions about how objects are independent of any perspective on them" [SEP Kant]. The manuscript labels this "structural"/"identification taken up later" rather than asserting it derives Kant — that is the appropriate qualification.

## The honest concession (anti-relativism is conditional)
"Within-species consensus" is the only clause doing strong anti-relativist work, and it currently rests on the consensus construction that Debate 3 (BLUE_WINS) established is a regulator-conditional heuristic, not a finite gauge-invariant observable. The manuscript states this in the same sentence: the construction is "conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply" (:3094). So the honest reading is: the *strong* anti-relativism (a shared gauge-invariant geometry within a species) holds *if* the regulator exists, and the manuscript flags this as conditional. What exists unconditionally right now is within-frame validity plus pairwise cross-perspectival coupling — which is itself the coherent perspectival-realist position [Massimi], not "anything goes." The claim does not overstate, because it explicitly conditions the strong clause and labels the position "within-frame validity," not "shared objective geometry."

The claim fails if the manuscript asserts within-species consensus *unconditionally* (no regulator hedge) — that would be an overclaim the Debate 3 finding forbids. Direct quotation shows it does not: ":… conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply … within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" (:3094). The hedge is present and in the same sentence as the consensus clause. Condition unmet.
