## Round 2 (red opening) — newly-discovered canon

### Philosophy of science — relativism / perspectivism / objectivity

- **SEP, "Relativism"** (plato.stanford.edu/entries/relativism). Objectivism: "cognitive, ethical and aesthetic norms and values in general, but truth in particular, are independent of judgments and beliefs at particular times and places." Relativism denies this: truth/knowledge/justification are "products of differing conventions and frameworks of assessment and that their authority is confined to the context giving rise to them." To escape relativism a position requires a frame-independent standard: "There could be no framework-independent vantage point from which the matter of whether the thing in question is so can be established"—relativists deny such a vantage point exists. The distinguishing feature of relativism: "no neutral standpoint for adjudication between conflicting frameworks." [Verified via WebFetch 2026-05-24.]

- **Massimi, "Four Kinds of Perspectival Truth," Phil. & Phenom. Research XCVI(2), 2018; Perspectival Realism (OUP 2022).** Perspectival realism is "committed to both the existence of mind-independent things and to the historical and cultural situatedness of scientific knowledge." Massimi denies the account is relativist. The load-bearing anti-relativist device is the perspective's double role: it functions both as a context of use (fixing its own standards of performance adequacy) and as a context of assessment from which other perspectives can be evaluated. Cross-perspectival assessability — a claim made in one perspective must be assessable from others against standards — is what distinguishes perspectival realism from relativism; world-involving constraints anchor the perspectives to a shared mind-independent world. [Sourced from WebSearch summary of the Wiley/PhilPapers abstracts + perspectivalrealism.org 2026-05-24; primary PDF returned 404, so cited at abstract level as paraphrase, not verbatim quotation.]

- **SEP, "Scientific Objectivity"** (plato.stanford.edu/entries/scientific-objectivity). Candidate grounds for perspectival realism over relativism: convergence across perspectives (Bayesian convergence "agents with very different initial attitudes who observe the same evidence will obtain similar posterior degrees of belief in the long run" — asymptotic, limited applicability); reproducibility / intersubjective agreement; "a shared world as anchor" constraining legitimate interpretation. The entry leaves open whether multiple valid perspectives WITHOUT a coordination mechanism collapse into relativism. [Verified via WebFetch 2026-05-24.]

### Philosophy — Kant primary

- **Kant, Critique of Pure Reason** (via SEP "Kant", plato.stanford.edu/entries/kant). Transcendental aesthetic: space and time are not properties of things-in-themselves but a priori forms of intuition contributed by the subject — "space and time are its pure forms" (A42/B59–60); "we can cognize of things a priori only what we ourselves have put into them" (Bxviii). The noumenon / thing-in-itself is fundamentally unknowable: it "has no knowable intrinsic properties accessible to us — it remains entirely independent of our cognition." Practical (regulative) use of the noumenal concept is permitted despite theoretical unknowability. [Verified via WebFetch 2026-05-24.]

### Information geometry — fixed fiber metric carries the invariant content

- **[Cencov1972]** (Cencov, Statistical Decision Rules and Optimal Inference; tagged external_bibliography.md:20). The Fisher information metric is the unique Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold invariant under sufficient statistics (Markov morphisms). Corollary load-bearing here: the divergence/metric structure on the belief fiber B is canonically determined and present at N=1, before any second agent or any consensus. Corroborated [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2].

### Gauge theory / differential geometry — pullback and the substrate

- **[Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4]; [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III.2].** A pullback metric G_i = φ_i^* g on the base is fully determined by the fixed fiber metric g and the section/frame φ_i. Standard fact: if the base manifold carries no intrinsic metric and all metric structure is the pullback of a fixed fiber metric through a chosen section, the base contributes only its smooth-manifold (index/topology) structure, not metric content.

## Per-lens memos (red, round 2)

### Memo — philosophy-of-science (LEAD)

**Steelman.** The section is honestly hedged at the sentence level: :3094 says the consensus construction is "conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply," and the Kantian identification is deferred ("taken up in §phenomenological_interpretation") rather than asserted. Within-frame internal consistency (a single agent's geometry is self-consistent) is uncontroversial, and perspectival realism is a recognized coherent position in philosophy of science (Massimi).

**Falsification.** The headline claim is "within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" (:3094). For this to be perspectival realism rather than relativism, the manuscript needs a cross-perspectival assessment / convergence ingredient anchored to a shared world [Massimi 2018; SEP Scientific Objectivity]. SEP Relativism: escaping relativism requires a frame-independent standard, "a neutral standpoint for adjudication between conflicting frameworks." The manuscript's only candidate for that standard is the consensus metric. Debate 3 established the consensus metric is regulator-conditional and not a finite observable [canon chain: Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4; PeskinSchroeder1995 §9.4; non-compact Haar]. The :3094 prose admits the regulator is "not supplied." So the anti-relativist anchor is an admitted-absent construction. The position is structurally indistinguishable from relativism-with-an-IOU until the regulator is exhibited. The hedge is honest locally but inadequate to the headline: the headline asserts "not unconditional relativism" as if the apparatus were present; the same sentence concedes it is not.

**Falsification condition.** Red is wrong if blue exhibits a finite, regulator-free, gauge-invariant cross-perspectival convergence construction within the manuscript's existing apparatus, OR shows the manuscript already supplies a regulator Debate 3 missed.

**Citation.** SEP Relativism (frame-independent standard); Massimi 2018 (context of assessment / cross-perspectival assessability); :3094 (manuscript, as claim under evaluation).

### Memo — info-geometer

**Steelman.** Distinct gauge frames φ_i legitimately pull back distinct metrics G_i; that G_i ≠ G_j is a correct consequence of frame-dependence, not an error.

**Falsify.** The "within-species consensus" anchor and the "fixed fiber metric" both rest on the Fisher–Rao metric. By Cencov uniqueness [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2], the fiber metric is canonically fixed and present at N=1 — before any agent-to-agent coupling. The invariant content of the whole construction lives in the fiber, which every agent shares identically. This cuts against the anti-relativism rescue routed through consensus: the genuinely frame-invariant object (the Fisher fiber divergence) is already shared and needs no consensus; the object that consensus is supposed to produce (a shared metric on the base C) is exactly the regulator-conditional one Debate 3 downgraded. So the manuscript anchors anti-relativism on the construction that does NOT exist as a finite observable, while the construction that DOES exist invariantly (the fiber metric) is not a base-level "shared geometry" at all.

**Falsification condition.** Red is wrong if the shared invariant content can be located in a finite base-level object rather than only in the fixed fiber metric.

**Citation.** [Cencov1972]; [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2].

### Memo — variational

**Steelman.** β_ij ∝ exp[−KL(q_i ‖ Ω_ij q_j)] does couple agents; nonzero coupling means agent i's belief is constrained by agent j's transported belief, which is genuinely "not solipsism" in the minimal sense that beliefs are not causally sealed.

**Falsify.** β_ij delivers pairwise KL-proximity after transport, a local consistency constraint on neighbor pairs. It does not deliver a shared objective geometry: agents can be pairwise KL-close while each inhabits a distinct G_i, and pairwise constraints do not compose into a single frame-independent metric (transitivity/global-section obstruction is exactly the holonomy/consensus problem Debate 3 addressed). "Not solipsism" (beliefs are coupled) is a strictly weaker claim than "shared reality" (a common objective geometry). The manuscript's prose slides from the defensible weaker claim to the headline-adjacent stronger one. This is a genuine but secondary point: the manuscript does say "informationally coordinated," not "identical," so it is partially hedged — the gap is the implicit upgrade from coordination to consensus.

**Falsification condition.** Red is wrong if pairwise β_ij constraints provably compose into a unique frame-independent metric without the regulator.

**Citation.** [Friston2010] (variational coupling = constraint, not identity); [KullbackLeibler1951] (KL is an asymmetric proximity, not a shared coordinate).

### Memo — gauge-theorist

**Steelman.** "No God's-eye metric on C" is a correct reading of a genuinely gauge-covariant construction: there is no canonical section, so no canonical pullback metric, so no privileged frame. That part is sound.

**Falsify.** The same gauge-covariance that grounds "no view from nowhere" also makes the noumenal base C gauge-idle. With no intrinsic metric on C and all G_i = φ_i^* g pulled back from the fixed fiber metric g [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4], C contributes only smooth-manifold/index structure. Every metric fact is carried by (g, φ_i). The base manifold C is not doing ontological work as a "shared substrate that grounds objectivity" — it is a bare index set. The manuscript's strongest ontological assertion ("C exists as a mathematical substrate") is the least qualified clause in the section, and gauge theory says that assertion is empirically and geometrically inert.

**Falsification condition.** Red is wrong if C carries any gauge-invariant structure not reducible to (fixed fiber metric, choice of section).

**Citation.** [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4]; [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III.2].

### Memo — geometer

**Steelman.** A base manifold with structure induced by pullback from a fiber metric is a standard and coherent differential-geometric setup; nothing is mathematically ill-formed.

**Falsify (eliminability test).** Construct the eliminativist model that keeps only the sections/frames φ_i, the beliefs q_i, and the fixed Fisher fiber metric g, and never names C. Every G_i = φ_i^* g, every KL(q_i ‖ Ω_ij q_j), every β_ij is computable without reference to any intrinsic structure on C. Nothing observable changes. By the standard methodological criterion (a posit that does no work is eliminable), the noumenal C is metaphysical decoration. This directly contradicts the claim's "appropriately qualified": the section's boldest ontological move — positing a noumenal substrate — is precisely where qualification is absent, and the posit is idle. Kant primary sharpens the irony: Kant's noumenon is unknowable AND contributes nothing knowable (space/time are the subject's forms) [CPR A42/B59–60, Bxviii]. The manuscript's C is faithfully Kantian in being unknowable but then is asked to do realist anti-relativist work ("shared substrate") that Kant's noumenon explicitly cannot do. The mapping is over-extended at exactly the load-bearing point even though the headline Kantian identification is deferred.

**Falsification condition.** Red is wrong if removing C from the construction changes any computable belief, metric, or coupling quantity.

**Citation.** [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4] (pullback metric determined by fiber metric + section); Kant CPR (A42/B59–60, Bxviii) on the inert noumenon.

## Round 2 (blue opening) — newly-discovered canon (deduplicated against red block above)

Most blue canon overlaps the red harvest (Massimi, Kant, Cencov, Nakahara, the Debate-3 chain). Two non-duplicate additions and one sharpening:

- **Massimi perspectival realism — the affirmative anti-relativist content, stated as the manuscript's actual position.** "States of affairs about the world are perspective-independent, whereas our scientific knowledge claims about these states of affairs are perspective-dependent" [Massimi, *Perspectival Realism*, OUP 2022; CORDIS "Placing realism in perspective"]. The cross-perspectival assessment device ("no scientific perspective can sanction the truth of its own claims; claims of knowledge must be assessable from the point of view of other scientific perspectives") is the recognized middle position avoiding "both relativism and naive objectivism." Blue maps this onto the manuscript one-to-one: $\mathcal{C}$ = perspective-independent states of affairs; $G_i$ = perspective-dependent knowledge claims; $\beta_{ij}$ transport-coupling = cross-perspectival assessment.

- **[Lee2013] pullback-of-tensor-fields, section-dependence.** $G_i = \sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal{B}}$ is section-dependent; a *single fixed* fiber metric does not descend to a canonical base metric absent a canonical section. This dissolves the "fixed fiber metric is a hidden god's-eye structure" version of the idleness charge: fixed fiber metric is consistent with no canonical metric on $\mathcal{C}$ and with agent-relative base geometry. Distinct sections give distinct base metrics from one fixed $g_{\mathcal{B}}$.

- **[Friston2017Graphical]; [JordanGhahramaniJaakkolaSaul1999]; external_canon_inference.md §8 sharpening.** Coupling distinct variational factors via the generative/graphical structure constrains approximate posteriors without producing shared ground truth; $q$ carries an irreducible approximation gap. The standard VI semantics license exactly "coordination among approximations," which is the manuscript's "informationally coordinated, not identical" reading — and forbid the stronger "shared posterior/geometry" reading, which the manuscript does not assert.

- **[Nakahara2003 §10.3] sharpening of the substrate role (responding to the idleness charge, not duplicating the pullback fact).** Associated-bundle parallel transport compares fibers across frames *using the connection, not a base metric*. So $\Omega_{ij}$ and hence $\beta_{ij}$ are well-defined and require the common base $\mathcal{C}$ (as the shared domain over which the sections $\sigma_i, \sigma_j$ are both taken) without requiring any metric on $\mathcal{C}$. $\mathcal{C}$ is structurally load-bearing as the common base, even though it is metric-idle.

## Round 3 (red rebuttal) — newly-discovered canon (deduplicated against rounds above)

The red panel was re-embodied across the same 5 lenses (philosophy-of-science lead, gauge-theorist, geometer, info-geometer, variational) to answer blue's opening. Two non-duplicate additions sharpen the surviving attack; the rest of the rebuttal reuses canon already in the rounds above.

- **[SEP, "Kant's Transcendental Idealism"] — "noumenon" is an ontological posit, not a mathematical index set.** Verified via WebFetch 2026-05-24. The thing-in-itself / noumenon carries genuine metaphysical status: "things in themselves affect us, activating our sensible faculty" (A190, A387) — mind-independent causal agency — while remaining unknowable: "we cannot cognize things in themselves" (A239). The term denotes a mind-independent reality (thinkable via the categories, A254, but not knowable), not a coordinate domain or scaffold. This is the denotation the manuscript invokes when it writes "shared noumenal substrate" (:3094) and "noumenal manifold $\mathcal{C}$" (:3098). The word imports ontological commitment beyond "smooth manifold serving as the common base of the sections."

- **[Massimi 2018 §"context of assessment"; SEP "Relativism" §"escaping relativism"] — the perspectival-realist anti-relativist device must be a delivered cross-perspectival assessment, not a flagged candidate.** Already harvested in Round 2; sharpened here for the rebuttal: Massimi's exit from relativism is the perspective functioning *as a context of assessment* from which other perspectives' claims are actually adjudicated against world-involving standards. A construction the same sentence concedes "the present manuscript does not supply" (:3094) cannot serve as the operative context of assessment. The named-but-absent regulator is a promissory note, not the delivered cross-perspectival machinery the position requires; so the *unconditional* perspectival-realist label is not yet earned, even though the *conditional* one is.
