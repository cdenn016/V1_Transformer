## False dichotomies / equivocations detected

The claim's only structural hazard is the **conjunction** "coherent AND appropriately qualified" treated as a single binary. It is not binary: on the verbatim text the first conjunct holds and the second does not (as-written). Both sides correctly de-conflated these — red attacks only the second, blue defends both but concedes the second needs two trims. No party equivocated between "the manuscript claims X" and "X is true": both repeatedly separated the meta-claim (presentation) from the metaphysics (observer-dependent reality is true), and both placed the metaphysics out of scope. No "manuscript-as-authority" circularity surfaced (Debate 3's external canon chain — Cencov, Nakahara, Peskin-Schroeder, non-compact Haar — carries the regulator-conditional finding, not the verdict file as authority).

One real equivocation lives *inside the manuscript*, not in the debate: at :3094 the parenthetical "(conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply)" governs the consensus-metric *candidacy*, but the following clause "The position is therefore one of ... within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" asserts present band-membership *outside* the conditional's syntactic scope. The conditional does not govern the "therefore." This is the seam red identified; it is a within-text qualification leak, repairable by re-scoping the conditional. The agreed fix changes the conclusion from a present property to a conditional one — a substantive repair packaged as a wording trim, not a cosmetic edit.

## Scope leakage detected

None that defeats the claim. The evidence base sits inside scope: a theory/presentation meta-claim is adjudicated on philosophy-of-science canon (Massimi, Kant/SEP) and the differential-geometry/info-geometry canon fixing what the apparatus does and does not deliver — not on a small-N empirical observation, and not by attempting to settle the metaphysics. The one genuine out-of-scope object — the metaphysical truth of observer-dependent reality, which turns on a regulator that does not exist as a finite observable [Debate 3 canon chain] — is correctly quarantined by both sides and is not the claim under evaluation. Treating that unadjudicable metaphysics as grounds for OUT_OF_SCOPE would be using a non-claim to dodge an answerable claim; forbidden.

The "appropriately qualified" conjunct survives as a property the manuscript *can hold* but does not hold *as written*, on two seams both sides agree on: the :3094 "therefore" draws an unconditional band-conclusion from an admitted-absent antecedent (a qualification leak, not a coherence failure), and "noumenal" (:3094, :3098) imports Kantian ontological/causal commitment [SEP Transcendental Idealism, A190/A239] that the apparatus discharges only as "base manifold." Blue conceded both — FC#4 grants the :3098 overclaim "is currently met," and grants the :3094 conditional "must always travel with its conditional." This is the identical disposition the sibling Debate 3 resolved as BLUE_WINS-with-bound-lexical-repairs ("must NOT be read as endorsing the prose verbatim"; residue "lexical, not structural"). The REMAND trigger in my mandate — two sides arguing *different propositions* packed in one claim, talking past each other — is absent here: they argue the same conjunction and converge. Voting REMAND when blue has carried the meta-claim on frame-correct canon and owes only two bounded edits would be REMAND-to-avoid-a-position, which is forbidden. The honest outcome is BLUE_WINS, with the two trims bound to the disposition so the verdict is not misread as endorsing the prose verbatim.
