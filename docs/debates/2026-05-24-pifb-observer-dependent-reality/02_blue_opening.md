# Blue Opening — pifb-observer-dependent-reality

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §"Observer-Dependent Reality" treatment is relativism wearing a transcendental-idealist costume: its one anti-relativist anchor, within-species consensus, rests on the consensus metric that Debate 3 (BLUE_WINS) downgraded to a regulator-conditional heuristic the manuscript does not construct, so with the regulator admittedly absent (:3094) all that remains is per-agent geometries plus pairwise coupling — and the noumenal substrate $\mathcal{C}$, carrying no intrinsic metric while every $G_i$ is pulled back from a fixed Fisher fiber metric, is an idle posit eliminable without changing any computable quantity.

## Position

The claim under evaluation is a meta-claim about the manuscript's *presentation* — that the section is internally coherent and appropriately qualified — not the metaphysical thesis that observer-dependent reality is true. On that bar the section is defensible: the position it occupies is the recognized middle position **perspectival realism** [Massimi 2022], its noumena/phenomena mapping is a faithful structural parallel to Kant, its "no view from nowhere" and its idle-substrate worry both dissolve under standard fiber-bundle structure, and every clause that would constitute an overclaim is explicitly conditioned in the text. The one place the section does anti-relativist work beyond what currently exists — "within-species consensus" — is conditioned in the same sentence on a regulator the manuscript states it does not supply (:3094), which is honest qualification, not concealed overclaim. The strong anti-relativism holds *if* the regulator exists; what holds unconditionally now is within-frame validity plus frame-invariant pairwise coupling, which is itself coherent perspectival realism, not unconditional relativism.

## Evidence

**1. The position is perspectival realism, a named coherent middle position — not unconditional relativism (philosophy-of-science lens).** Massimi's perspectival realism holds that "states of affairs about the world are perspective-independent, whereas our scientific knowledge claims about these states of affairs are perspective-dependent," and avoids relativism because "no scientific perspective can sanction the truth of its own claims; claims of knowledge must instead be assessable from the point of view of other scientific perspectives" — cross-perspectival assessment — thereby steering clear of "both relativism and naive objectivism" [Massimi, *Perspectival Realism*, OUP 2022; Massimi 2018, *Phil. & Phenom. Research* 96(2); CORDIS "Placing realism in perspective"]. The section maps onto this structure point for point: the perspective-independent substrate is $\mathcal{C}$; the perspective-dependent knowledge claims are the pulled-back geometries $G_i$ with "no privileged 'true' geometry" (:3086, no perspective sanctions its own truth); and the cross-perspectival assessment is the transport-coupled comparison "agents can disagree about geometry while remaining informationally coordinated" (:3094). The manuscript's own label — "within-frame validity and within-species consensus, not unconditional relativism" (:3094) — names exactly the canonical position.

**2. The Kantian mapping is a faithful structural parallel, and the manuscript qualifies it as such (philosophy-of-science lens).** Kant: things-in-themselves exist but are unknowable and lack the spatio-temporal/metric structure the mind imposes — "Space and time are merely the forms of our sensible intuition of objects. They are not … properties of, nor relations among" things-in-themselves; there is "no coherent sense to questions about how objects are independent of any perspective on them" [SEP, *Kant's Transcendental Idealism*; Kant, *CPR*, Transcendental Aesthetic]. The manuscript: "$\mathcal{C}$ exists as a mathematical substrate, but it possesses no intrinsic metric, no preferred notion of distance, no distinguished temporal direction; all such structure is induced by agent beliefs through the pullback mechanism" (:3098). Metric/distance/time contributed by the cognizing system, not intrinsic to the substrate — the parallel is exact. The section defers the identification ("taken up in §phenomenological_interpretation," :3098) and elsewhere labels the analogies "structural" and "formal" (:3111), which is the appropriate qualification for an analogy rather than a derivation.

**3. The noumenal substrate $\mathcal{C}$ is the required common base of the bundle, not idle (gauge-theorist + geometer lenses).** In the standard fiber-bundle construction the base manifold is a constitutive datum, and a section pulls back fiber-side structure to it via $G_i = \sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal{B}}$ [Nakahara2003 §10.1–10.4; Lee2013, pullback of tensor fields]. The phrase "$G_i \neq G_j$ over the same underlying substrate" (:3086) is only meaningful because both sections $\sigma_i, \sigma_j$ are taken over a single base $\mathcal{C}$; remove $\mathcal{C}$ and "the same substrate" loses its referent and the two metrics could not be said to disagree. The non-idleness of $\mathcal{C}$ is that it supplies the common domain over which distinct pullbacks are comparable and the carrier of the connection: associated-bundle parallel transport compares fibers across frames using the connection, not a base metric [Nakahara2003 §10.3]. So $\Omega_{ij}$ and $\beta_{ij}$ require the common base $\mathcal{C}$ but require no god's-eye metric on it — which is precisely "no view from nowhere" with inter-agent comparison intact. The "fixed fiber metric is a hidden global structure" sharpening also fails: a single fixed $g_{\mathcal{B}}$ does not descend to a canonical metric on $\mathcal{C}$ because there is no canonical section, and distinct sections yield distinct base metrics from one fixed fiber metric [Lee2013]. A bare smooth manifold carrying no distinguished metric until one is supplied is the routine situation, not a contradiction.

**4. "Not solipsism" is earned by a frame-invariant coupling, on the weaker informational reading the manuscript actually states (info-geometer + variational lenses).** Solipsism is the denial of any genuine mind-independent other-relation; refuting it requires only a well-defined frame-independent relation between agents. The comparison functional $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ is exactly that: by Cencov's uniqueness theorem the Fisher metric and the KL divergence it generates are invariant under sufficient statistics, hence under the GL(K) frame action, and this invariance is intrinsic to the divergence and present at N=1 [Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2], verified to 4×10⁻¹⁵ on K=4 in `docs/debates/2026-05-24-pifb-consensus-gauge-invariance/04_verdict.md`. After transport aligns frames, the inter-agent disagreement $\beta_{ij}$ measures is not an artifact of either agent's arbitrary frame — it is a genuine frame-independent other-relation, which a solipsist lacks. This is the standard semantics of coupling distinct variational factors: it constrains approximate posteriors without producing shared ground truth [Friston2017Graphical; JordanGhahramaniJaakkolaSaul1999; BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]. The manuscript claims exactly this — "informationally coordinated," "mutual consistency constraints without requiring identical perceptions" (:3094) — and does not claim the coupling produces a shared metric, which it routes to the (conditional) consensus construction.

**5. The anti-relativism is conditional, and the manuscript says so in the same sentence (philosophy-of-science lens, honest qualification).** The only clause doing strong anti-relativist work is "within-species consensus," and it rests on the consensus construction Debate 3 established is a regulator-conditional heuristic, not a finite gauge-invariant observable (`docs/debates/2026-05-24-pifb-consensus-gauge-invariance/04_verdict.md`, binding Action). The manuscript states this within the clause itself: the construction is "conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply" (:3094). So the honest reading, which the text supports verbatim, is: strong within-species consensus holds *if* the regulator exists; what exists unconditionally is within-frame validity plus frame-invariant pairwise coordination — itself the coherent perspectival-realist band [Massimi 2022], strictly more than isolated solipsistic priors and strictly less than a god's-eye shared geometry. The claim of "appropriately qualified" is therefore satisfied: the conditional is named, not buried.

## Concessions

Blue concedes three points, none of which defeats the meta-claim.

Pairwise $\beta_{ij}$ coupling is strictly weaker than shared objective geometry — it yields frame-invariant pairwise disagreement and consistency pressure, not a single geometry all agents share. This is owned: the manuscript writes "informationally coordinated," not "geometrically agreed," and hands the shared-geometry ambition to the conditional consensus metric.

The strong anti-relativist clause ("within-species consensus") is conditional on an unbuilt regulator. The anti-relativism that holds *now* is the perspectival-realist within-frame-validity-plus-coordination position; the stronger species-wide shared geometry is a flagged conditional, not a present achievement.

There is a privileged geometry on the belief *fiber* (the Cencov-unique Fisher metric), so "no view from nowhere" is precisely a claim about the *base* $\mathcal{C}$ (no privileged metric there), not about belief space. The manuscript's prose is about $\mathcal{C}$ (:3098, "the true state of $\mathcal{C}$"), so this is consistent — but a careful reader should hold the scope: privileged fiber geometry, no privileged base geometry.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds.

The section asserts within-species consensus *unconditionally* — without the regulator hedge. Unmet by direct quotation: the consensus clause is qualified in the same sentence, "conditional on a regulator that the present manuscript does not supply" (:3094).

The section claims $\mathcal{C}$ carries its own intrinsic metric (or a fixed fiber metric forces a canonical base metric), producing an internal contradiction with "all structure is pulled back." Unmet: "$\mathcal{C}$ … possesses no intrinsic metric … all such structure is induced by agent beliefs through the pullback" (:3098); and standard pullback geometry shows a fixed $g_{\mathcal{B}}$ does not descend to a canonical base metric [Lee2013].

The section claims $\beta_{ij}$ coupling delivers shared *geometry* / inter-agent objective truth rather than informational coordination — an overclaim exceeding what coupled approximate-$q$ inference licenses. Unmet: "agents can disagree about geometry while remaining informationally coordinated" (:3094); coordination among approximations is the standard VI reading [BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017; external_canon_inference.md §8].

The Kantian mapping is asserted as a derivation rather than a structural analogy. Unmet: the identification is deferred to a later section (:3098) and the parallels are labelled "structural"/"formal analogies without quantitative predictions" (:3111).

The position is wrong in the strong (non-meta) sense — i.e. it does establish objective shared reality — only if the regulator is exhibited; absent that, the defensible claim is the meta-claim (coherent + qualified), which is what this opening defends.
