# Blue Rebuttal — canonical-F-vs-surrogate

## Concession

Three concessions, made plainly.

First, red is right that the framework's iterative trajectory under the autograd convention adopted at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:874` does **not** descend on `F_red` off-equilibrium. The vector field `-η ∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` differs from `-η ∇F_red` by `+ητ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)`, a quantity that is non-zero almost everywhere by sub-claim β. Sub-claim ε is granted: the implementation's E-step does not execute descent on `F_red` between iterations; it executes descent on `⟨E⟩_{β*}`.

Second, red is right that the line at `:874` — "Standard transformer training follows the autograd convention ... and we adopt the same convention" — is a description of computational practice imported from standard transformers, not a first-principles derivation of why the autograd objective is the correct variational descent objective. The manuscript imports a convention; it does not derive that the convention is correct as a variational solver.

Third, the manuscript's prose at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:967–972` ("the global free energy is minimized by gradient descent on the beliefs") and `:2008–2014` ("E-step (Belief Inference): ... natural gradient descent on the free energy") needs reconciliation with `:874`. As written, those passages name `F` as the descent target without flagging that the gradient computed in the algorithm is the autograd gradient of `⟨E⟩_{β*}`, which is not `∇F_red`. This is an editorial gap and should be reconciled in revision.

These concessions are real. None of them falsifies the claim under debate.

## Core attack

Red's load-bearing argument misidentifies what the claim asserts. The claim is precisely that the gradient gap exists, has the covariance form, and is disclosed in the manuscript — and that the framework adopts the autograd convention with this gap as an explicit choice. Red's evidence (the `:874` adoption, the `:870–872` formula, the supp §B.1 surrogate admission) is the claim's evidence, not its falsifier. The five sub-claims α, β, γ, δ, ε are factual; red concedes α, β, γ, δ. The only sub-claim red attacks is ε ("the E-step gradient flow under the autograd convention does not descend on F_red off-equilibrium") — and red affirms ε rather than refuting it. Red's prose at `02_red_opening.md:9` calls this "an unresolved theoretical commitment," but the manuscript at `:874` resolves it: the framework's analytical results characterize `F_red` and its stationary points; the framework's *implementation gradient* is `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}`; the two differ off-equilibrium by `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)`; this is stated explicitly in the same paragraph that adopts the autograd convention. Red treats disclosure of the gap as evidence the gap is fatal. Disclosure is evidence the gap is acknowledged, derived, and made part of the framework's structural account.

Red's attack point 3 — "stationary-point results presuppose F-descent" — conflates two distinct operations the framework performs. The softmax-β derivation at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:766–771` establishes **what β is** at the stationary point of the alignment free energy `F_align^(τ)`. This is a closed-form result: substitute `β = β*` into `F_align`, get `-τ log Z`. The implementation does not arrive at `β*` by gradient descent on `F_align` in `β`; it computes `β* = softmax(-E/τ + log π)` in closed form at each E-step iteration. The closed-form `β*` is exact under either gradient convention because **β is not a gradient-descent variable in the implementation; it is a closed-form function of the current belief parameters**. The gradient gap at `:870–872` concerns the gradient with respect to the **belief parameters** `x ∈ {μ_i, Σ_i, φ_i}`, not with respect to `β`. Red's "the analysis does not characterize the algorithm except at the joint fixed point" treats the closed-form β-update and the gradient-flow on `x` as if they were a single coupled descent. They are not — they are alternating substeps in the standard variational-EM pattern, where the closed-form step (β-substitution) is exact and the gradient step (on `x`) is what the autograd convention applies to.

Red's attack point 4 dismisses the GLU identification at `:1937` as post-hoc relabeling. This misreads the framework's structural account. The GLU/SiLU/GELU family identification at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1925–1931` is independent of the autograd-vs-envelope choice. The additional softmax-gradient correction at `:1935–1946` is the *autograd-retained term* — exactly the term that distinguishes `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` from `∇F_red`. If the framework descended on `∇F_red` instead, this correction would be absent by the envelope theorem (`:1937`: "this term is absent from the gradient of the reduced free energy F_red by the envelope theorem, but present in standard autograd implementations"), and the framework's prediction at `:1944` — that VFE attention exhibits the same softmax-gradient nonlinearity that appears in standard transformers during backpropagation — would fail. The framework's correspondence with standard transformer architecture is the prediction the framework makes; that prediction requires the autograd-retained term; descent on `F_red` would falsify the prediction. Red's "a residual that has a name is still a residual" frames the issue as if the choice of gradient convention were independent of the framework's structural claims. It is not. The framework's identification of the VFE iterations with FFN layers (`:1948`, "FFN depth from VFE iterations") is built on the autograd-retained correction being present.

## Defense

The claim under debate has five sub-claims. Sub-claims α (covariance form), β (vanishing locus), γ (autograd-convention adoption), δ (Σ_i gradient identity for `β` held fixed), and ε (E-step descends on the surrogate off-equilibrium) are all factually correct. Red concedes α, β, γ, δ directly (`02_red_opening.md:9`). Red argues against the *framing* of ε, not against ε itself — red affirms that the E-step under the autograd convention descends on `⟨E⟩_{β*}` rather than `F_red`. The claim is therefore unfalsified on its sub-claims.

The remaining question is whether the gap, as disclosed, makes the manuscript internally inconsistent. Red asserts it does, citing `:967–972` and `:2005–2014` as "minimize F" claims that contradict `:874`. I read these passages differently. The variational principle the framework is built on is "minimize F" — this is what `:967` and `:2008` state. The specific gradient-computation choice at `:874` is the **autograd-convention realization** of that variational principle, which differs from the literal `∇F_red` flow by a term the framework explicitly retains as a structural feature. The contradiction red identifies dissolves under the following reading: the variational *principle* is to minimize `F`; the variational *algorithm* — by the convention adopted at `:874` and by the GLU correspondence at `:1937–1946` — computes the autograd gradient of `⟨E⟩_{β*}`, which inherits `F_red`'s stationary points (at joint equilibrium) while retaining the softmax-gradient correction the framework identifies as the FFN nonlinearity. Whether this reading is editorially clean in the current manuscript text is a separate matter; the substantive position is consistent.

Red's falsification condition 1 ("first-principles surrogate-derivation") asks for a derivation that the surrogate `⟨E⟩_{β*}` is the variational descent target. The framework's derivation at `:1925–1946` provides exactly this. The autograd-retained softmax-gradient correction at `:1939` is what implements the framework's predicted Boltzmann-gate activation function (`:1928`); without it, the framework's correspondence with standard transformer FFN structure fails. The autograd convention is not merely "imported from standard transformers" as descriptive convention; it is the convention that makes the VFE iterations produce the GLU-family nonlinearity the framework predicts. The framework's structural account therefore requires the autograd-retained term — equivalently, descent on `⟨E⟩_{β*}` rather than `∇F_red`. This is a first-principles argument for the surrogate as the correct descent target: the variational construction recovers standard FFN nonlinearity only when the autograd convention is used.

Red's falsification condition 3 (gap small in operating regime) is a falsification of the *worry*, not of the claim. The claim asserts the gap is non-zero off-equilibrium. The claim does not assert the gap is large. The covariance bound `|Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)| ≤ sqrt(Var_{β*}(E) · Var_{β*}(∂E/∂x))` shows the gap is bounded by a τ⁻¹-suppressed energy-variance product. Whether this is small empirically at training temperatures is testable — but the testability is itself a strength of the disclosed gradient gap, not a weakness.

Red's falsification condition 4 (surrogate-stationarity equals softmax-stationarity) is satisfied by the framework's actual usage. The framework uses the canonical `F_align` *with* the entropy term to derive softmax-β at `:766`, then substitutes `β = β*` into `F` to obtain `F_red`, then computes the autograd gradient of `⟨E⟩_{β*}` = `Σ β*(x) E(x)` for the belief-parameter updates. Softmax is the stationary point of `F_align` (with entropy term), as the framework states; the surrogate `Σ β E` *without* the entropy term has its argmin at a delta on argmin_j E_j, as red and the canon both note. The framework does not use that delta-form surrogate. It uses `β = softmax(-E/τ + log π)` — the softmax-β from `F_align`'s stationary point — and then computes the autograd gradient of the expression `Σ β*(x) E(x)` with β set to this softmax value. This is not "the surrogate without entropy term"; it is `F_red` differentiated by the autograd product-rule rather than by the envelope theorem. The difference is exactly `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)`, and that is the term the framework identifies as the softmax-gradient nonlinearity at `:1937–1946`.

The strongest honest defense of the claim is therefore: the claim is correct on all five sub-claims; red affirms four directly and affirms the fifth in substance while contesting the framing; the manuscript's prose at `:967` and `:2008` should be reconciled with `:874` in revision for editorial clarity, but the substantive position — "the framework discloses a gradient gap, derives its form, adopts the autograd convention with the gap as a feature implementing the GLU softmax-gradient nonlinearity, and characterizes the framework's correspondence with standard transformer architecture as requiring exactly this gap" — is internally consistent and is exactly what the claim asserts.

The claim under debate is not "the manuscript has zero editorial issues." It is "the gradient gap exists, has the covariance form, and is disclosed; the autograd convention is explicitly adopted; the two objectives share critical points only at joint equilibrium or τ → ∞." Every clause is verified, including by red.
