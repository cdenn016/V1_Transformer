# Red Opening — canonical-F-vs-surrogate

## Steelman (opposing position)

The compound claim is correctly stated: the manuscript discloses, derives, and quantifies the gradient gap between the autograd objective `⟨E⟩_{β*}` and the reduced free energy `F_red`; the gap is the framework's GLU/softmax-gradient nonlinearity (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1937`); the autograd convention is the standard transformer convention; and the implementation therefore computes a well-defined and disclosed gradient that coincides with the canonical one at the joint stationary point.

## Position

I concede sub-claims α, β, γ, and δ as written, with one narrowing of δ noted below. I attack sub-claim ε and the framing surrounding it.

The manuscript's prose names `F_red` as the descent target in two places where it cannot be reread as anything else — the belief dynamics equation at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:967–972` (`∂q_i/∂t = -η_q · δF/δq_i`, "the global free energy is minimized by gradient descent on the beliefs") and the E-step definition at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2005–2014` ("E-step (Belief Inference): Given current parameters, update agent beliefs `{q_i}` via natural gradient descent on the free energy ... `q_i^{(t+1)} ← q_i^{(t)} - η_E · ∇̃_{q_i} F`"). The implementation, by the manuscript's own admission at `:874`, does not compute `∇F_red`. It computes `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}`, which differs from `∇F_red` by `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)` (verified below). The two prose narratives — "we minimize F" at `:967, :2008` and "the gradients below are not gradients of F_red" at `:874` — are mutually incompatible off-equilibrium. The framework's analytical results that depend on F-stationarity (the softmax-β derivation at `:766`, the envelope-form gradient at `:859–866`) describe the F-gradient flow that the implementation does not execute. The framework's iterative belief updates therefore descend on a *different* objective than the one the variational construction nominates.

Concretely: this position is the claim that **the framework's narrative ("we minimize F") is not supported by its algorithm (it does not minimize F); the algorithm's invariants are those of the surrogate, not of F_red; and the gap between the two is not a benign disclosure but an unresolved theoretical commitment.**

## Evidence

**1. Sub-claim α verified by sympy.** Three-source toy with `β_j = π_j exp(-E_j(x)/τ)/Z`, `Z = Σ_k π_k exp(-E_k(x)/τ)`:

```
F_red gradient matches envelope sum beta*dE/dx: True
Gap equals -Cov_beta(E, dE/dx)/tau:           True
Symbolic residual:                             0
```

The covariance identity `∇⟨E⟩_{β*} − ∇F_red = -τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)` at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:870–872` is algebraically exact. I grant α.

**2. The descent-target contradiction.** The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:967–972` declares:

> "The global free energy [eq:free_energy_final] is minimized by gradient descent on the beliefs `{q_i}`: `∂q_i/∂t = -η_q · δF/δq_i`."

At `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2008–2014` the E-step is restated:

> "E-step (Belief Inference): Given current parameters, update agent beliefs `{q_i}` via natural gradient descent on the free energy: `q_i^{(t+1)} ← q_i^{(t)} − η_E · ∇̃_{q_i} F|_{q_i^{(t)}}`."

At `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:874` the manuscript states the implementation does not compute that gradient:

> "The gradient expressions below are therefore gradients of the attention-weighted energy `Σ_j β_{ij}* E_{ij}` ... not of the reduced free energy `F_red`; the two differ by [eq:autograd_envelope_gap]."

The vector field `−η · ∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` is not the vector field `−η · ∇F_red`. The trajectory generated by the former does not in general descend on `F_red`. The framework cannot consistently claim both `:967` and `:874`.

**3. Stationary-point results presuppose F-descent.** The softmax-β derivation at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:766–771` shows that `F_align^(τ)*` substituted at `β = β*` equals `-τ log Z_i`, with the entropy term `τβ log(β/π)` essential because — per supp §B.1 at `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex:183` — "the canonical free energy of the main text adds the `τβ_{ij} log(β_{ij}/π_{ij})` entropy term to make the softmax form of `β` a stationary point." The surrogate `Σ_j β_{ij} E_{ij}` without that entropy term has its arg-min at `β` concentrated on `argmin_j E_{ij}` (a delta on the lowest-energy source) per `01_evidence.md:71–73`. Softmax is the stationary point of `F`, not of the surrogate. The envelope-theorem form at `:859–866` likewise requires `∂F/∂β = 0` at `β = β*`, which holds for `F` (with the entropy term) but does not hold for `Σ β E` alone. The framework's stationary-point analysis is an analysis of `F_red`'s flow; the algorithm executes the surrogate's flow; the analysis therefore does not characterize the algorithm except at the joint fixed point both flows share.

**4. The "GLU activation" identification at `:1937` is post-hoc.** The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1935–1946` names the residual `∂β/∂μ` terms as a "softmax-gradient correction" inside the framework's GLU/FFN structure. This identification re-labels the gap as a desired feature. It does not justify replacing `∇F_red` with `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` in the descent algorithm. The framework's GLU derivation in the surrounding section is independent of the autograd-vs-envelope choice — it analyzes the form of the message-passing energy `e · exp(-‖e‖²/τ')/Z` `:1928`. Re-identifying the gap as "the GLU" does not show that gradient descent on `⟨E⟩_{β*}` lands at the same iterates as gradient descent on `F_red`; it shows only that the *additional terms* in the autograd gradient have a recognizable functional form. A residual that has a name is still a residual.

**5. The supplementary's δ-form concession is narrower than it reads.** Supp §B.1 at `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex:183` states the `Σ_i` gradient "is identical under both forms because the attention entropy does not depend on `Σ_i`." This holds when `β` is treated as fixed. Under the autograd convention adopted at `:874`, `β_{ij}` depends on `Σ_i` through `E_{ij} = D_KL(q_i ‖ Ω_{ij} q_j)` — Gaussian KL depends on `Σ_i` — so autograd through `β(Σ_i)` re-introduces the same `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂Σ_i)` gap for the covariance channel. The supplementary's "identical under both forms" statement is a statement about the partial derivative with `β` held fixed; it is not a statement that the implementation's `Σ_i` update matches `∇_{Σ_i} F_red`. The clean-`Σ` concession that sub-claim δ encodes is real for the partial but does not transfer to the implementation.

**6. Canon citation: envelope theorem.** `external_canon_inference.md` §5 makes the distinction explicit: at a fixed point `s* = T(s*)`, the IFT through the fixed point gives the well-defined M-step gradient; a single backprop through one iteration of the E-step is amortized inference, not IFT, and "the two have different bias/variance properties." The framework's autograd-convention E-step at `:874` is the latter pattern. Standard variational inference treats `F` as the descent target during the E-step; the framework's E-step at `:2008–2014` claims the same — natural-gradient descent on `F` — but at `:874` declares the actually-computed gradient is of a different functional. Per `external_canon_inference.md` §10 pitfall 1 ("Form-1 vs Form-2 conflation"), this is precisely the failure mode the canon warns against — describing one functional and computing another.

## Falsification conditions

My position is wrong if any of the following hold.

1. **First-principles surrogate-derivation.** If the framework supplies a derivation showing that the surrogate `⟨E⟩_{β*}` *is* the correct variational descent objective — that is, that the variational problem the framework solves is naturally posed as minimization of `⟨E⟩_{β*}` rather than `F_red` — my attack on the prose contradiction at `:967, :874, :2008` falls. The current `:874` text imports the convention from standard transformers ("Standard transformer training follows the autograd convention ... and we adopt the same convention"), which is descriptive, not derivational. A first-principles argument that the surrogate is the right objective would dissolve the contradiction.

2. **No prose claim of F-descent.** If `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:967–972` and `:2008–2014` were rewritten to say "we descend on `⟨E⟩_{β*}` ≠ `F_red`" — and the framework's stationary-point and reduction analyses were restated in terms of the surrogate's fixed points — the contradiction would be removed by surrender of the F-descent claim. As the manuscript currently stands, those prose claims are present and uncorrected.

3. **Gap vanishes in the operating regime.** If the framework establishes that the covariance `Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂x)` is small or zero throughout the training trajectory (not only at the fixed point), the gap is benign in practice. Sub-claim β rules this out generically; an empirical demonstration that `Cov_{β*}` is small in the operating regime would weaken the attack. The manuscript does not make this claim.

4. **Surrogate-stationarity equals softmax-stationarity.** If, under the surrogate `Σ β E` without the entropy term, the optimal `β` were still the softmax `π exp(-E/τ)/Z`, the surrogate would inherit the framework's `β`-derivation. It does not — the surrogate's arg-min is the delta on `argmin_j E_{ij}`, per `01_evidence.md:71–73` (Boyd-Vandenberghe; Cuturi 2013). The framework's `β`-derivation requires the entropy term, which the surrogate omits.

5. **Coincident trajectories, not just coincident fixed points.** If the two gradient flows produce the same iterate sequence (not just the same fixed point), the off-equilibrium concern dissolves. They do not, generically — the vector fields differ by the non-zero covariance term at every off-equilibrium point.

If none of 1–5 obtains, the framework's algorithm executes descent on the surrogate while the framework's prose claims descent on `F_red`. The two are non-equivalent off-equilibrium, by the manuscript's own derivation at `:870–872`. The "we minimize F" narrative at `:967, :2008` is then not supported by the algorithm at `:874`; the framework's variational commitment is a commitment to `F_red` in the prose and to `⟨E⟩_{β*}` in the implementation; the two coincide only at the joint stationary point they presuppose.
