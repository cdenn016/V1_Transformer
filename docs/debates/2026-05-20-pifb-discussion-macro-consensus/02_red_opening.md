## Position

The Discussion subsection at 3136-3153 is **not** correctly calibrated. The 3138 disclaimer registers one hedge (the rock-as-agent stipulation) and the 3142 sentence registers a second (the dimensional bridge), but the load-bearing math sentence at 3148 ("dominates i's total free energy ... no freedom to maintain alternative priors") inherits an **asymptotic regime claim** from the appendix without stating it. The appendix's `Asymptotic reading` paragraph at 3906 is explicit that the inertia, consensus, and back-action results are stated in the `ε → 0` rock-limit and that at finite ε all three are nonzero at O(ε). The Discussion at 3148 asserts dominance in absolute terms (no `ε → 0`, no β-regime condition, no cross-reference to 3906). A second defect: the subsection's title ("Consensus Enforcers") imports the cross-observer-consensus reading derived at appendix Eq.~rock_consensus (3886) into a paragraph that develops only the single-coupling pinning mechanism. The two defects are independent.

## Evidence

- **Appendix asymptotic caveat is explicit, Discussion subsection does not inherit it.** `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3906` (paragraph `Asymptotic reading`) reads: *"The three derivations above are stated in the asymptotic $\epsilon \to 0$ rock-limit. At finite $\epsilon$ corresponding to thermal motion of constituent atoms, both the coupling pull on $\mu_R$ and the back-action on $(\mu_R, \Sigma_R)$ are nonzero at $O(\epsilon)$; the rock-limit claim is that this scaling sets the regime in which inertia, consensus, and negligible back-action emerge, not that the corresponding quantities are identically zero at finite temperature."* The Discussion subsection 3142 cross-references `sec:scope_limitations` for the dimensional bridge, and 3138 cross-references `app:examples` for the meta-agent construction generally — but neither cross-reference points to 3906 specifically, and the regime condition is nowhere repeated. A reader of 3136-3153 alone is given an unconditional dominance claim at 3148.

- **The Discussion's math claim at 3148 is a regime-conditional statement, verified symbolically.** Using the canonical Gaussian KL gradient `[Amari2016 §2.4]` and the natural-gradient flow on observer agent $i$ with own covariance $\Sigma_i$, prior precision $\bar\Lambda_{p_i}$ (O(1)), and rock-coupling at the Mahalanobis precision $\Lambda_{\mathrm{rock}} = \epsilon^{-1} I$, the observer's $\mu_i$ update obeys
  $$\dot\mu_i = -\eta\,\Sigma_i\left[\bar\Lambda_{p_i}(\mu_i - \bar\mu_i) + \beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}}\,\epsilon^{-1}(\mu_i - \tilde\mu_{\mathrm{rock}})\right].$$
  The second term dominates only when $\beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}}/\epsilon \to \infty$. Sympy session (executed 2026-05-20):
  ```
  Observer i grad on mu_i: (Lambda_p_i*epsilon*(mu_i - mu_p_i) + beta*(mu_i - mu_r))/epsilon
  Observer i mu update step: -Lambda_p_i*Sigma_i*eta*mu_i + Lambda_p_i*Sigma_i*eta*mu_p_i
                              - Sigma_i*beta*eta*mu_i/epsilon + Sigma_i*beta*eta*mu_r/epsilon
  ```
  Dominance of the third/fourth (rock-coupling) terms over the first/second (prior-anchor) terms requires both $\epsilon \to 0$ AND $\beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}} = O(1)$. The Discussion at 3148 states neither condition.

- **Softmax normalization on $\beta$ is the second piece of the regime condition that 3148 elides.** The canonical multi-agent F at `sec:framework` defines $\beta_{ij}$ as a row-softmax over all $j$ neighbors of agent $i$; CLAUDE.md `## Mathematical Reference` reproduces this as $\beta_{ij} = \mathrm{softmax}(-\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij} q_j)/\tau)$. The Mahalanobis term inside the rock-KL grows as $\epsilon^{-1}$, which in the softmax numerator becomes $\exp(-\epsilon^{-1}\,d^2/\tau)$ — vanishing exponentially as $\epsilon \to 0$ if $d \neq 0$ at the row-equilibrium. Unless one stipulates that agent $i$'s belief has already aligned to the rock (so the rock-KL is small in the exponent), the softmax-normalized $\beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}}$ is not guaranteed to be O(1) — it can be exponentially suppressed precisely because the rock has high precision. The appendix sidesteps this by treating $\beta_{ij}$ as an externally given coupling and by working at the rock's stationary point ($\mu_i \to \tilde\mu_{\mathrm{rock}}$ where $d \to 0$); the Discussion subsection 3148 inherits this silently and presents the dominance claim as if it held for any agent $i$ at any belief state.

- **Title vs. paragraph content: the cross-observer consensus reading lives only in the appendix.** `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3879-3888` derives `Cross-observer consensus via photon-mediated coupling` via the composition $\mu_{h_k} \to \Omega_{h_k\gamma}\Omega_{\gamma R}\mu_R = \Omega_{h_k R}\mu_R$ at Eq.~rock_consensus (3886). This composition through a photon-mediated channel is what licenses calling the rock a "consensus enforcer" — multiple observers equilibrate to gauge-transported copies of the same rock state. The Discussion subsection 3140-3148 develops only the single-observer pinning mechanism ($\beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}}\,\mathrm{KL}$) and never composes through a channel. A reader of 3136-3153 alone is given a title whose substantive justification (multi-observer composition) is absent from the paragraph.

- **Presentation defect, stipulated.** Lines 3152-3153 read `extreme self-precision leaves\nNo room for disagreement` — the capital `N` mid-sentence is either a stray paragraph break or a sentence-internal capitalization error. Both teams stipulate this is a typographic defect to be repaired regardless of verdict.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any one of the following obtains:

1. **R2 (regime inheritance) fails** if the Discussion subsection 3136-3153, read in isolation, already conveys the regime condition (either by explicit $\epsilon \to 0$ language at 3148 or by a targeted cross-reference to the `Asymptotic reading` paragraph at 3906 or to the appendix Eqs.~rock_inertia / rock_back_mu specifically, rather than to `app:examples` generally). 2. **The softmax-suppression objection fails** if blue cites a primary-source argument that the row-softmax $\beta_{i,\mathrm{rock}}$ remains O(1) at agent $i$'s stationary point under the rock-limit, or that the Discussion subsection is meant to be read at the post-alignment stationary point (in which case the dominance claim becomes a self-consistency statement that should be labelled as such at 3148).

4. **The whole attack collapses** if blue shows that the 3138 disclaimer's phrase "framework-internal interpretation rather than a derivation" is broad enough to discharge regime conditions in addition to the rock-as-agent stipulation. I read 3138 as registering the stipulation only — the disclaimer says the rock-as-agent move is by analogy from the appendix, not that the math claim at 3148 is itself unconditional or hand-wavy. If blue reads 3138 as a global "everything in this subsection is interpretive" hedge that subsumes the regime, the attack on calibration is weakened (the subsection becomes uniformly hedged), but the title problem at R1 survives because the hedge does not repair the coverage gap.

I concede in advance: the 3138 stipulation hedge is real and does discharge the rock-as-agent move (sub-claim 1 in the claim); the 3142 dimensional-bridge hedge is real and does discharge the Planck-scale claim (sub-claim 2). My attack is on sub-claim 3 (regime conditions on the dominance math) and sub-claim 4 (title coverage gap), with the typo at sub-claim 5 stipulated.
