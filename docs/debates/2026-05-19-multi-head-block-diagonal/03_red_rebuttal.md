The remaining attack surface is sub-claims γ, δ, and ε — the off-diagonal-discard framing, the before-vs-after head-mixing claim, and the per-head temperature reading. Each fails under blue's own defense.

## Core attack

The load-bearing weakness in blue's opening is the conflation, across sub-claims γ, δ, and ε, of three distinct comparison frames: parameter-space comparison, operator-space comparison, and function-class comparison. Blue picks whichever frame favors the claim under attack and ignores when it fails the other claims.

**Sub-claim γ — the "87.5% discarded" framing is parameter-frame inconsistent.** Blue defends the "discard" language by appealing to the operator-space frame (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1781` measures the codimension of `⊕_a gl(d_head)` inside `gl(d_k)`, and the off-diagonal generators "do act non-trivially on the `d_k`-dimensional belief space"). But blue's own opening for sub-claim α explicitly grounds the identification in the per-head operator independence at [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2] — the standard transformer's parameter manifold is `(W_Q^a, W_K^a, W_V^a)_{a=1}^H` plus `W_O`, not the `d_k²`-dimensional `gl(d_k)`. Under typical sizes (`d_model = d_k = 512, H = 8, d_head = 64`), the standard's parameter count is `3 · H · d_model · d_head + d_model² = 3 · 8 · 512 · 64 + 512² = 786,432 + 262,144 = 1,048,576`, which is *four times* the `262,144` parameters of `gl(d_k)`. The standard does not "discard" `87.5%` of `gl(d_k)` because the standard never lived in `gl(d_k)` — its parameter manifold is larger by raw count and incommensurable in structure. The "discard" framing only makes sense from inside the gauge framework's parameterization, treating `gl(d_k)` as the canonical reference and the standard as a restriction. The evidence pack flags this exactly: "the gauge framework's 'discard' language treats the gauge framework as the canonical reference, which is a framing choice" (evidence pack §"What this evidence does NOT settle" item 2).

**Sub-claim δ — "more expressive" is per-layer-exact and cross-layer-attenuated, but the manuscript states it as a function-class claim.** Blue's defense at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1797` argues the before-vs-after distinction is "per layer" and "strictly precise." I grant the per-layer statement. The defect is that the manuscript at `:1797` does not say "per-layer more expressive" — it says "the full-`GL(d_k)` transport is therefore more expressive than the block-diagonal-plus-output-projection factorization, as it couples the attention computation across heads rather than only the output computation." That language is a function-class claim, and the function-class claim fails. Across two consecutive standard-transformer layers `ℓ` and `ℓ+1`, the composition `W_O^{(ℓ)} → next-layer-W_Q^{(ℓ+1), a}` operates on the concatenated head outputs of layer `ℓ` and projects them into head `a`'s subspace at layer `ℓ+1` — this is arbitrary linear cross-head mixing entering the KL (or scaled dot product) that determines `β` at layer `ℓ+1`. The framework's single-layer full-`GL(d_k)` is matched by the standard's two-layer composition. Blue's falsification condition F4 itself concedes that this "attenuates 'qualitatively different' to 'different per-layer but composable across layers'"; that attenuation is the falsification of the function-class reading. The expressiveness gap is at the *depth-cost* level, not the *function-class* level. The manuscript at `:1797` overstates the gap because it makes no depth-cost qualifier.

**Sub-claim ε — "implicit in the weight scales" is a translation, not a retention.** Blue's defense at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1757` reads `‖W_Q^a‖² ‖W_K^a‖²` as the implicit standard form of `σ_a^{-2}`. The defect: standard transformers, per [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1], apply a *single* dimensional factor `1/√d_k` uniformly across all heads, and have no per-head temperature parameter slot. The variation that ends up per-head in the trained `‖W_Q^a‖² ‖W_K^a‖²` is an emergent consequence of the optimization, not an architectural degree of freedom that the standard exposes. The framework's `σ_a²` is a novel parameter slot — `H` additional scalars whose role in the standard is absorbed into the `H · d_model · d_head` projection magnitudes. Making the implicit explicit *is* adding a parameter slot, even if its function captures variation previously absorbed elsewhere. The evidence pack flags this exactly: "Whether this is a *retention* of structure standard transformers already implement implicitly, or a *novel* additional degree of freedom that standard transformers don't have, is interpretive" (evidence pack §"What this evidence does NOT settle" item 4). The manuscript at `:1757` chooses the "retention" framing without justification; the "novel degree of freedom" framing is at least as defensible.

## Defense

My opening attack stands against blue's defenses.

On sub-claim γ, blue's argument that "the off-diagonal block-Lie-algebra generators *do* act non-trivially on the `d_k`-dimensional belief space" and that `W_O` "lives outside the attention computation" is operator-frame reasoning that does not rescue the parameter-frame "discard" claim. The standard transformer never instantiates an element of `gl(d_k)` at the attention-score-defining level; it instantiates a product of two rectangular projections `W_K^a (W_Q^a)^T` per head, which lives on a rank-`d_head` subspace of `d_k × d_k`. The off-diagonal generators of `gl(d_k)` are not "discarded" by the standard — they are absent from its parameterization entirely, because the standard's parameterization is not a subset of `gl(d_k)` at any reference frame. The arithmetic at `:1781` is correct; the rhetorical reading at `:1781` is not.

On sub-claim δ, blue's argument that `W_O` is "applied to the concatenation of head outputs" and "cannot" depend on head `b`'s content within a single layer is *exactly* the per-layer-exact claim I conceded. The defect is that the manuscript's text at `:1797` ("more expressive than the block-diagonal-plus-output-projection factorization") is a function-class claim, not a per-layer claim. Standard transformers compose layers; the function-class implementable by the standard at depth `L+1` strictly exceeds what it implements at depth `L` precisely because `W_O^{(ℓ)} → W_Q^{(ℓ+1)}` mixes heads before subsequent attention. The framework's single-layer full-`GL(d_k)` gives cross-head awareness at depth `1`; the standard's two-layer composition gives it at depth `2`. That is a depth-efficiency claim, not an expressiveness claim. The manuscript at `:1797` needs the qualifier "per layer" or "at fixed depth" to be honest; without it, the function-class reading is false.

On sub-claim ε, blue's appeal to the variance of `Q^a (K^a)^T` scaling with `‖W_Q^a‖² ‖W_K^a‖²` for unit-variance inputs is the correct algebraic translation, but does not establish equivalence at the parameter-slot level. [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] writes the attention as `softmax(Q K^T / √d_k) V` with `1/√d_k` as the only explicit temperature; the per-head magnitudes are properties of the trained `W` matrices, not of the architecture. The framework's `σ_a²` is `H` distinct architectural scalars — a structurally different parameterization. The evidence pack's neutral statement that this is "interpretive" rules out blue's strong "retention" framing in favor of the weaker "translation" framing.

The compound claim "exactly the gauge-theoretic block-diagonal restriction" at `00_claim.md:11` is therefore established only on sub-claims α, β, and the per-head holonomy decomposition. Sub-claims γ, δ, and ε are framing choices the manuscript states as facts. The judge should split: BLUE on α, β, holonomy; RED on γ, δ, ε; with the compound "exactly" verdict depending on whether the framing claims are load-bearing for the architectural identification or commentary on it.
