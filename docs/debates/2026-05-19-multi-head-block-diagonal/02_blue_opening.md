# Blue Opening — multi-head-block-diagonal

## Steelman (opposing position)

Standard multi-head attention is parameterized by `H` independent rectangular `d_model × d_head` matrices `W_Q^a, W_K^a, W_V^a` plus a single dense `W_O ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_model}` post-concatenation projection ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.2]); it has no parallel transport, no holonomy, no Lie-algebra decomposition, and no per-head covariance — so the framework's "block-diagonal subgroup of `GL(d_k)`" is a structural reading layered on top of the standard, the "87.5% discarded" figure measures distance from a reference frame the standard never adopted, and the per-head `σ_a²` is a new parameter, not a "retained" one, with the cross-layer composition `W_O ∘ next-layer-W_Q` already implementing exactly the cross-head mixing the framework claims is absent.

## Position

Sub-claims α, β, γ, δ, ε are each defensible on the evidence: (i) per-head attention is independent across heads at the operator level (sub-claim α), so `Ω = block-diag(Ω^a)` is the unique gauge-group restriction compatible with that independence; (ii) the thin-SVD lift `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` (sub-claim β) is the identification already adjudicated BLUE on sub-claim C at the §5.2.2 level, applied verbatim at §5.4; (iii) the dimensional counting `d_k² − H·d_head² = 229,376` (sub-claim γ) is arithmetically exact and the "discard" framing is the honest statement of the architectural constraint, not a framing choice that depends on which space is canonical; (iv) the before-vs-after head-mixing distinction (sub-claim δ) is *per layer* and is precise — within a single attention layer, off-diagonal `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` would enter the KL that sets `β`, while standard `W_O` cannot; (v) the per-head temperature reading (sub-claim ε) is the natural Bayesian translation of what the standard already does implicitly via `‖W_Q^a‖, ‖W_K^a‖`. The compound claim stands.

## Evidence

- **Sub-claim α — operator-level independence.** [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2] defines `head_i = Attention(X W_Q^i, X W_K^i, X W_V^i) = softmax(Q^i (K^i)^T / √d_k) V^i`. The expression contains no cross-head terms: head `i`'s softmax never sees `Q^j, K^j, V^j` for `j ≠ i`. Any operator commuting with this independence structure must act block-diagonally on the `d_k`-dimensional Q/K/V representation in which the heads form orthogonal sub-blocks. Therefore the unique `GL(d_k)` gauge-group restriction realized by the standard is exactly `GL(d_head)^H`, and the manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1716` writes precisely this:
  > `Ω = diag(Ω^1, …, Ω^H), Ω^a = (σ² W_K^a (W_Q^a)^T)^{-1} ∈ GL(d_head)`.

- **Sub-claim β — thin-SVD lift.** The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1727` factors the rectangular per-head projection as `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` with `U_Q^a ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_head}` isometric (orthonormal columns) and `A_Q^a ∈ GL(d_head)`. The isometric factor `U_Q^a` is the "split into `H` heads" of [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2] — it is the embedding of the head subspace into `d_model`. The invertible factor `A_Q^a` is the gauge transport within the head. This is the identification accepted under BLUE_WINS in sub-claim C at §5.2.2 (manuscript lines 1245–1252); the §5.4 application of the same identification inherits that verdict.

- **Sub-claim γ — dimensional accounting.** `dim gl(d_k) = d_k² = 512² = 262,144`. `dim(⊕_a gl(d_head)) = H · d_head² = 8 · 64² = 32,768`. Complement `dim m = 229,376 = 262,144 − 32,768`, and `229,376 / 262,144 = 0.875` exactly. The manuscript at `:1781` reports this. The arithmetic is exact and the evidence pack confirms it ("The arithmetic is correct"). The "discard" language is honest because the off-diagonal block-Lie-algebra generators *do* act non-trivially on the `d_k`-dimensional belief space (they are not in any meaningful sense already present in the standard's parameterization at the gauge level). The standard transformer's `W_O ∈ ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` lives outside the attention computation — it is applied *after* the head-attention scores have been used to aggregate values — and therefore does not parameterize the off-diagonal sub-algebra `m` at the level of the attention-defining KL.

- **Sub-claim δ — before-vs-after head mixing is per-layer exact.** The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1797` states that off-diagonal `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` enters the KL divergence that determines `β`. The standard transformer's `W_O` cannot do this: in [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2], `W_O` is applied to the *concatenation of head outputs* `Concat(head_1, …, head_h)`, which has already executed `softmax(Q^a (K^a)^T / √d_k) V^a` independently for each head. The attention weights `softmax(Q^a (K^a)^T / √d_k)` therefore depend only on head-`a` projections of the input, never on head-`b` content. The framework's full-`GL(d_k)` `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` couples the *attention-score-determining* KL across heads at the same layer. This is what the manuscript means by "before vs after" — strictly per-layer, and strictly precise.

- **Sub-claim ε — per-head temperature.** The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1745–1757` derives `D_KL^{(a)} = (1/(2σ_a²)) ‖·‖²` in the isotropic limit, identifying `σ_a²` with the per-head temperature. The standard transformer applies a single dimensional factor `1/√d_k` uniformly across heads ([Vaswani2017 §3.2.1]), and any *per-head* temperature variation in the standard is implicit in the learned magnitudes `‖W_Q^a‖, ‖W_K^a‖` — for unit-variance inputs, the variance of the head-`a` dot product `Q^a (K^a)^T` scales with `‖W_Q^a‖² ‖W_K^a‖²`, which is precisely the role `σ_a^{-2}` plays in the framework. The manuscript at `:1757` reads this scaling as the standard absorbing `σ_a^{-2}` into the projections, which is a faithful translation: the framework makes explicit what the standard expresses implicitly via projection magnitudes.

- **Sub-claim — per-head holonomy decomposition (`:1737–1743`).** Block-diagonal `φ_i, G_a, δ_{ij}^{(a)}` implies block-diagonal `exp(φ_i) exp(δ_{ij}^{(a)} G_a) exp(−φ_j)`, because the matrix exponential preserves block-diagonal structure (`exp(block-diag(X)) = block-diag(exp(X_a))`). The triangle holonomy `H_{ijk} = Ω_{ij} Ω_{jk} Ω_{ki}` therefore factors as `⊕_a H_{ijk}^{(a)}` and the Wilson observable `tr(H_{ijk} − I) = Σ_a tr(H_{ijk}^{(a)} − I)` is additive. The decomposition is mathematically exact, as the evidence pack confirms.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following are demonstrated.

1. **F1 — operator-level cross-head dependence in the standard.** Show a primary-source line in [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2] or its canonical implementations in which head `a`'s pre-softmax logit `Q^a (K^a)^T / √d_k` is a function of head-`b` content with `b ≠ a` *within a single attention sublayer* (i.e., not via cross-layer composition). If standard multi-head has within-layer cross-head terms, sub-claim α and sub-claim δ both fall.

2. **F2 — `W_O` parameterizes the off-diagonal sub-algebra.** Exhibit a parameterization in which `W_O` plus the per-head projections jointly span the same operator space on the `d_k`-dimensional belief representation as the full `GL(d_k)`. If the standard's `H × 3 × (d_model × d_head) + d_model²` parameter manifold has the same image on the attention-score-defining operator as `GL(d_k)` would, then sub-claim γ's "discard" language is wrong and sub-claim δ's "more expressive" claim collapses. The evidence pack flags this as the contested framing; concretely, the falsification would be a constructive bijection between standard-transformer parameter configurations and `GL(d_k)` Lie-algebra elements at the attention-score-defining level.

3. **F3 — rank-deficient `W_K^a (W_Q^a)^T` defeats the identification.** The inverse `(σ² W_K^a (W_Q^a)^T)^{-1}` at `:1716` is taken on a `d_k × d_k` matrix that, when the per-head W matrices are interpreted as block-diagonal embeddings, has rank `d_head` per block. If the inverse cannot be consistently restricted to the rank-`d_head` image of the block (and the thin-SVD lift at `:1727` does not, in fact, provide the resolution), sub-claim β fails. This is the same caveat as sub-claim C; falsifying β here would require overturning the BLUE_WINS verdict on sub-claim C.

4. **F4 — multi-layer composition recovers off-diagonal at single-layer cost.** If a one-layer attention sublayer plus `W_O` is shown to be operator-equivalent to a two-layer block-diagonal stack — i.e., the "before vs after head mixing" distinction dissolves under composition without depth penalty — then sub-claim δ's "more expressive" claim is weaker than stated. (Note: this would attenuate "qualitatively different" to "different per-layer but composable across layers"; it would not falsify the per-layer claim itself.)

5. **F5 — standard transformer has explicit per-head temperature.** If [Vaswani2017] or canonical implementations show an explicit per-head temperature parameter `1/τ_a` distinct from the implicit absorption into `‖W_Q^a‖, ‖W_K^a‖`, then the framework's `σ_a²` is not a retention but a duplication, and sub-claim ε's "implicit in the weight scales" framing is wrong.

6. **F6 — block-diagonal holonomy non-factorization.** If `exp(φ_i)`, `exp(δ_{ij} G)`, `exp(−φ_j)` fail to remain block-diagonal under the manuscript's discrete Regime II construction (e.g., because `G_a` couples across heads in a way the manuscript does not flag), the per-head holonomy decomposition at `:1737–1743` fails.

I do not anticipate F3, F5, or F6 being satisfiable on the evidence pack as written. F1 is precluded by the standard form at [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2]. F2 is the live attack surface — the strongest possible red argument is that "discard" presupposes a canonical reference frame, and the standard's parameter manifold may span the same operator space as `GL(d_k)` once `W_O` and depth are included. F4 attenuates rather than falsifies and is the second-strongest live attack.
