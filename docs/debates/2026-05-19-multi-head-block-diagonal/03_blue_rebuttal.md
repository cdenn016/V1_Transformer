# Blue Rebuttal — multi-head-block-diagonal

## Concession

I grant red's Citation 3 framing point. The "87.5% discarded" language at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1781` is framework-internal: it measures the off-diagonal complement against `gl(d_k) = ℝ^{d_k × d_k}` as the ambient algebra, not against the standard transformer's parameter manifold. The standard's manifold is `3 H d_model d_head + d_model^2 = 1{,}048{,}576` parameters for `d_k = 512, H = 8` (Q, K, V per head plus `W_O`); the framework's per-edge `GL(d_k)` has `d_k^2 = 262{,}144` parameters. These are different manifolds with different dimensions, so "87.5% discarded" is not a statement about the standard losing parameters relative to itself. Canon pitfall #10 in `external_canon_transformers.md §10` applies: the manuscript should label the percentage as a framework-internal restriction measure on `gl(d_k)`, not as a deficit of the standard. This is a labeling fix on the sentence at `:1781`, not a correction of the dimensional arithmetic (the `87.5%` figure on `gl(d_k)` is exact; evidence pack §"Off-diagonal dimensionality" confirms `262144 - 32768 = 229376`).

I do not grant red's attacks on sub-claims δ or ε. Both reach further than the manuscript text actually says.

## Core attack

Red's δ attack (Citation 4) reads the manuscript's "more expressive" sentence at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1797` as a function-class supremacy claim across all multi-layer stacks. The sentence does not make that claim. The full line:

> "The full-`GL(d_k)` transport is therefore more expressive than the block-diagonal-plus-output-projection factorization, as it couples the attention computation across heads rather than only the output computation."

The comparison object is "the block-diagonal-plus-output-projection factorization" — a single-layer architectural unit. The preceding sentences make the locus explicit: "Off-diagonal gauge transport, however, mixes heads before attention" and "head `a`'s attention pattern can depend on the content of head `b` through the transport, before any aggregation occurs at all." Both clauses are about what happens within a single attention computation — `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` entering the KL `before` the softmax that produces `β`, versus `W_O` mixing `after` the softmax has already produced per-head `β^a` and per-head aggregated `Σ_j β_{ij}^a V_j^a`.

Red's rebuttal in Citation 4 is correct that multi-layer standard transformers achieve cross-head awareness in layer `ℓ+1`'s attention scores because `W_O^{(ℓ)}` has mixed the heads in layer `ℓ`'s output. That is a true statement about depth-`N` standard stacks. It is not a refutation of the manuscript's sentence, because the manuscript's sentence is about a single-layer comparison: single-layer full-`GL(d_k)` against single-layer block-diagonal-plus-`W_O`. Red's δ falsification condition demands "a family of attention patterns realizable in single-layer full-`GL(d_k)` that no multi-layer stack of standard MHA with `W_O` can realize." That is a strictly stronger claim than the manuscript makes. Setting that bar and then noting the manuscript does not clear it is a strawman of the type Section §"Roles" of `debate_methodology.md` explicitly forbids.

The honest reading is that the manuscript's sentence is correct at the single-layer level it is stated at: at one layer, `W_O` cannot make head `a`'s attention scores depend on head `b`'s pre-attention content, because `W_O` operates only after both heads' softmaxes have run. Off-diagonal `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` makes head `a`'s KL — the argument to the softmax that produces `β^a` — depend on head `b`'s belief through cross-head transport. That is a precise, single-layer architectural distinction. It is not a global function-class theorem and the manuscript does not assert one.

Red's ε attack (Citation 5) overreads the verb "retain" at `:1757`. The manuscript's full sentence is:

> "In standard transformers, `σ_a^{-2}` is absorbed into the learned projections `W_Q^{(a)}, W_K^{(a)}`, making the per-head temperature implicit in the weight scales `‖W_Q^{(a)}‖, ‖W_K^{(a)}‖`. ... The gauge framework retains the per-head covariance structure that standard transformers absorb."

The factual content of this sentence is: standard transformers exhibit per-head attention temperature variation through `‖W_Q^a‖ · ‖W_K^a‖`, and the framework's `Σ^{(a)} = σ_a² I` makes that same variation explicit as a covariance parameter. Vaswani §3.2.1 specifies the uniform `1/√d_k`, but the dot product `Q^a (K^a)^T` for head `a` scales with `‖W_Q^a‖ · ‖W_K^a‖` because Q and K are the outputs of learned linear maps applied to the same input. So in a trained standard transformer head `a`'s softmax sees logits with effective scale `‖W_Q^a‖ · ‖W_K^a‖ / √d_k`, which differs across heads precisely because the weight norms differ across heads. This is the standard explanation for why some heads end up sharp and others diffuse — it is in the attention-analysis literature and is consistent with Vaswani §3.2.1's single `√d_k` factor being a normalization, not the only contributor to logit scale.

Red's Citation 5 argument — that `κ_a` is "novel structure, not retained structure" — collapses two different questions. Question (a): does per-head temperature variation exist in standard transformers? Yes, implicitly via weight norms. Question (b): is per-head temperature a separately named parameter slot in standard transformers? No, only the weights are named. The framework's `κ_a` is a separately named slot for (b) that exposes (a) explicitly. "Retain" in the manuscript refers to the empirical content (a); red reads it as referring to (b). Both readings are available; the manuscript's reading is the more charitable one and the one consistent with the rest of the section's identification language.

## Defense

The remaining live claims after the concession are sub-claim γ (with the labeling caveat), sub-claim δ (single-layer), and sub-claim ε (empirical-content-retention reading). All three survive.

Sub-claim γ survives in the form "the framework's full-`GL(d_k)` gauge algebra has `d_k^2 = 262{,}144` generators; the multi-head restriction to `⊕_a gl(d_head)` retains `H · d_head^2 = 32{,}768` of these; the complement, comprising `229{,}376` generators or `87.5%` of `gl(d_k)`, is the off-diagonal subspace that the block-diagonal restriction excludes." Evidence pack §"Off-diagonal dimensionality" verifies the arithmetic and red's opening §"Position" item 1 does not contest the arithmetic, only the framing. The framing fix — label the percentage as framework-internal — is the post-debate action. The substantive content of γ is exact under that labeling.

Sub-claim δ survives as a single-layer architectural statement. The manuscript's line at `:1797` reads "more expressive than the block-diagonal-plus-output-projection factorization" — a single-layer comparison. Within one layer, off-diagonal `Ω_{ij}^{ab}` enters the argument of the softmax that produces `β`; `W_O` enters after the softmax. This is a non-trivial structural difference: the attention pattern itself becomes cross-head-content-aware before any per-head aggregation in the framework's full-`GL(d_k)` form, and it does not in the block-diagonal-plus-`W_O` form. Red's Citation 4 establishes that multi-layer stacks can achieve cross-head-awareness in subsequent layers, which is a true and orthogonal statement. The manuscript does not claim otherwise; the §"What this evidence does NOT settle" item 3 of the evidence pack flags exactly this scope question and the manuscript's text is consistent with the single-layer scope.

Sub-claim ε survives as an empirical-content-retention claim. Standard transformer per-head temperature is implicit in `‖W_Q^a‖ · ‖W_K^a‖ / √d_k`; the framework's `Σ^{(a)} = σ_a^2 I` (with `κ_a` as the related learnable scalar at `:1751`) makes the same per-head scale explicit. The manuscript's `:1745–1757` passage names this identification: `σ_a^{-2}` is "absorbed" into the standard's weight norms; the framework retains it as an explicit per-head parameter. Red is correct that `κ_a` as a separately named parameter is framework-novel naming; red is incorrect that the underlying per-head temperature variation is novel to the framework. The variation is empirically present in trained standard transformers and is the basis on which "retain" is the right verb for the empirical content.

Sub-claims α and β are conceded by red's opening §"Position" first paragraph and inherit from the sub-claim C BLUE_WINS verdict. They do not need re-defense here.

On red's falsification condition (4) — "block-diagonal embedding" requires a primary source for the disjoint-block-subspace interpretation. The evidence pack §"What this evidence does NOT settle" item 1 records that the manuscript's `:1716` identification requires interpreting the rectangular per-head W matrices as block-diagonal embeddings. The thin-SVD lift at `:1727` is the manuscript's mechanism for making this identification rigorous: `W_Q^a = U_Q^a A_Q^a` with `U_Q^a` isometric and `A_Q^a ∈ GL(d_head)`. Sub-claim C's BLUE_WINS verdict accepted this lift at the value level on the head-space factor. Red's Citation 1 concedes sub-claims α and β at the value level, which is the same lift. Re-attacking the lift in falsification condition (4) is litigating ground already adjudicated; the user-context paragraph of `00_claim.md` is explicit that "sub-claims α and β here inherit that structure" from sub-claim C.

Net position after concession: the manuscript's `:1781` "87.5% discarded" sentence needs a one-clause labeling fix to mark the measure as framework-internal on `gl(d_k)`. The dimensional arithmetic and the structural content of sub-claims γ, δ, ε are correct under that labeling. The "more expressive" sentence at `:1797` is correct as a single-layer architectural statement and does not assert a multi-layer function-class supremacy. The "retain" sentence at `:1757` is correct as an empirical-content statement and does not assert that `κ_a` predates the framework as a named parameter.

The claim, with the `:1781` labeling fix, is defensible on the cited evidence.
