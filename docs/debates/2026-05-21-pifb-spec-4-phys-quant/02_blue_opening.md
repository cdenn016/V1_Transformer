# Blue Opening — pifb-spec-4-phys-quant

## Steelman (opposing position)

The four subsections lean on the strongest philosophical commitments in the manuscript — information-as-primal, mass-as-Fisher, dimensionless-constant derivability, a Kantian/structural-realist framing — and any one of these can be attacked as either (i) unfalsifiable in principle (the framework is compatible with any measurement by reinterpretation), (ii) trading on a citation that does not bear the claim (Cencov scale fixing, Worrall/Ladyman attribution, Amari natural gradient as action principle), or (iii) silently re-asserting an empirical claim that the manuscript explicitly disavows (mass-Fisher operational independence), so "publication-ready" is the wrong adjective for material whose load-bearing moves are interpretation rather than derivation.

## Position

The four subsections at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` lines 3051-3157 are publication-ready in the only sense available to honest speculative philosophy of physics: every novel identification is labeled novel, every postulate is labeled a postulate, the one testable prediction is given a symmetric success-failure criterion, the empirical claim the text could plausibly be read as making is explicitly disavowed in matching language at both the speculative-section locus (§3070) and the upstream §sec:mass disavowal (line 1904), the structural-realism family is invoked with the canonically correct attributions and the canonically correct agnosticism among variants, the boxed postulate list (i)-(iv) at line 3141 matches the upstream constructions it points to (§sec:observable_sectors, §sec:signature_resolution, §sec:worked_signature), and the framework's potential unfalsifiability is acknowledged in writing. The text is not "rock-solid as physics" — no honest reading of the text claims that — but the claim under debate is that the text is "publication-ready and rock-solid," and the relevant standard for these subsections is publication-ready as labeled speculation, which is what the text supplies.

## Evidence

### 1. The load-bearing mass-Fisher disavowal at line 3070 is consistent with the upstream §sec:mass disavowal at line 1904 and forecloses the operational-overclaim attack

The §3070 disavowal reads:
> "We do not claim a computational test of $M_{\mathrm{eff}} \propto \Sigma_p^{-1}$ in which $\omega^2$ and $M_{\mathrm{eff}}$ are measured as operationally independent quantities; the within-framework reading above identifies the Fisher mass with the Hessian by construction on this slice (Section~\ref{sec:mass}, Section~\ref{sec:velocity_quadratic}), so the scaling is a definitional consequence of that identification rather than an empirical dispersion law, and no operationally independent measurement is reported in this manuscript."
(`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3070`)

The upstream §sec:mass disavowal at line 1904 reads in parallel language:
> "We do not claim a dynamical test of $\omega^2 \propto m_{\text{eff}}^{-1}$ in which $\omega$ and $m_{\text{eff}}$ are measured as operationally independent quantities, because the present construction sets the inertia tensor equal to the potential Hessian by postulate (Section~\ref{sec:velocity_quadratic}) and therefore makes the scaling a definitional consequence of that identification rather than an empirical dispersion law; no operationally independent measurement is reported in this manuscript."
(`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1904`)

These two disavowals use the same operational-independence language and reach the same conclusion (definitional, not empirical). Read together they pre-empt the attack that §3070's disavowal is undercut by §sec:mass — the two sections speak in one voice. The mass-precision identification on which §3070 rests is the standard FEP precision-as-rigidity reading: high posterior precision resists update, low precision updates readily ([Friston2010] Eq. 2.2 and surrounding discussion of precision-weighted prediction errors; [FristonEtAl2017]). The novel move is identifying that resistance with inertial mass; the manuscript labels the novelty by writing "formally analogous to inertial mass" at 3068 rather than "is inertial mass," and the explicit disavowal at 3070 closes the empirical reading.

### 2. The natural-gradient invocation at 3068 is verbatim Amari 1998

Line 3068 writes the natural gradient as `d\theta/dt = -\mathcal{M}^{-1} \nabla_\theta \mathcal{F}` with `\mathcal{M}_{ij}[q] = E_q[(\partial_i \log q)(\partial_j \log q)]`. This is verbatim the Amari natural gradient `~∇L = G^{-1} ∇L` ([Amari1998] §2; [AmariNagaoka2000] Ch. 3.5; `external_canon_math.md` §1, "Natural gradient [Amari1998]"). The Fisher matrix `M_ij[q] = E_q[(∂_i log q)(∂_j log q)]` is the standard expected-outer-product form ([AmariNagaoka2000] Ch. 2.2). The action-principle reading at 3076 — that the Euler-Lagrange equations from `δF = 0` are the natural-gradient flow equations on the statistical manifold — is the standard reading of gradient flow under the Fisher metric ([AmariNagaoka2000] Ch. 3; the standard textbook statement that natural-gradient descent is steepest descent in the Fisher–Riemannian sense). The text qualifies this as "mathematically analogous to Hamilton's principle" rather than "is Hamilton's principle" and explicitly disavows Lagrangian recovery, Newton's-laws recovery, path-integral connection, and prediction of physical quantities (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3078`).

### 3. The symmetric success-failure criterion at 3084 is a genuine falsifiability commitment

Line 3084 imposes that "failure of the derivation under a fully developed information-geometric construction --- one in which the relevant statistical manifolds, comparison structure, and Cencov scale fixings are all specified --- would constitute substantive evidence against the phenomenological interpretation" and that "the burden is on the program to develop the construction to the point where the test is symmetric, not to indefinitely defer the test by appeal to incompleteness." This is the textbook Popperian move applied to the one testable prediction the framework offers (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3084`). The clause "the burden is on the program ... not to indefinitely defer the test by appeal to incompleteness" forecloses the moving-goalpost reading that "fully developed construction" is an indefinitely receding standard. The Cencov reference at 3084 is correctly applied: [Cencov1972] establishes the Fisher metric as the unique invariant Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold up to a scalar, and "Cencov scale fixings" is the natural label for that residual scalar — the scale that must be specified to convert a Fisher-invariant structure into a numerical prediction ([AmariNagaoka2000] Ch. 2, statement of Cencov uniqueness up to positive scalar). The manuscript does not claim Cencov's theorem itself supplies the scale; it specifies that the scale must be supplied as part of the construction to which the test applies.

### 4. The structural-realism framing at 3092 has canonically correct attributions and agnosticism

Line 3092 reads:
> "The framework recasts this in the structural-realism family — Cassirer's neo-Kantian variant, Worrall's epistemic structural realism~\cite{Worrall1989}, and the ontic structural realism of Ladyman \& Ross~\cite{Ladyman2007} (remaining agnostic among these variants) — rather than adopting Kant's stronger constitutive-a-priori commitment, which Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations} explicitly disavows."

The Worrall 1989 attribution is canonical for epistemic structural realism — Worrall, J. (1989). "Structural Realism: The Best of Both Worlds?" *Dialectica* 43(1-2): 99-124 (`references.bib:241-249`; verified in standard philosophy-of-science encyclopedic treatments of structural realism). The Ladyman & Ross 2007 attribution is canonical for the book-length statement of ontic structural realism — Ladyman, J. & Ross, D. (2007). *Every Thing Must Go: Metaphysics Naturalized*. Oxford. The "remaining agnostic among these variants" parenthetical is the philosophically careful move: ESR is agnostic about non-structural objects and OSR positively denies them, so agnosticism between the two variants is a genuine theoretical commitment, not a hedge. The "rather than adopting Kant's stronger constitutive-a-priori commitment, which Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations} explicitly disavows" clause closes the attack that the framework is dressed as Kant despite invoking structural realism: it states the position is structural-realist family, not full Kantian.

### 5. The boxed postulate list at 3141 matches the upstream constructions

The four postulates listed at 3141 are:
- (i) eigenvalue hierarchy with three large eigenvalues in $V_{\text{obs}}$. This matches §sec:observable_sectors at lines 2972-3013, where the spectral decomposition into observable / subthreshold / internal sectors is set up with the explicit eigenvalue hierarchy `λ_obs ≫ λ_subthresh ≫ λ_internal ≈ 0`.
- (ii) Lorentzian signature `(-,+,+,+)` under SO(3) restriction. This matches §sec:signature_resolution at 2774 and the worked example at §sec:worked_signature 2818-2858. The boxed phrasing "as a separate postulate" is accurate: §sec:worked_signature 2820 states explicitly that "the imaginary assignment along the temporal direction is a postulate" and 2856 reiterates "Second, the construction is a structural existence proof, not a dynamical derivation."
- (iii) imaginary frame component with `tr(T^2) > 0` and a real-part projection. This matches §sec:worked_signature Eq. \ref{eq:complex_gauge_frame} at line 2828 and the real-part projection at line 2841. The box's phrasing is correct.
- (iv) under the 4D extension, three mutually trace-orthogonal spatial generators $T_x, T_y, T_z$. The box explicitly labels this "under the 4D extension of the worked signature example," and the worked example at §sec:worked_signature uses only a single 2D generator `T = diag(1,-1)` along both directions. Line 2854 reads "For four-dimensional base manifolds with one imaginary and three real gauge components, the local frame group at each non-degenerate point is the full proper orthochronous Lorentz group $\mathrm{SO}^+(1,3)$; the question of which global metrics with this local frame group can be obtained ... is open." The box's (iv) is therefore correctly labeled as an extension postulate, not a current postulate, matching the open-question status the upstream section gives the 4D case.

The closing line of the box — "We do not claim to have derived physical spacetime, the Lorentz signature, or the dimensionality count from the framework; the construction shows that the framework is compatible with such a reading under the postulates listed above" — is the philosophically correct statement of what a compatibility result delivers as opposed to a derivation.

### 6. The plural-time concession at 3149 closes the obvious relativity-clash attack

Line 3149 reads: "Relativistic proper time is not reproduced under the present compact-group construction — Fisher arc length is Riemannian and positive-definite while proper time is Lorentzian, and the two run in opposite directions with motion." This is a direct concession against the strongest reviewer attack one could mount at this section — that calling Fisher arc length "structurally analogous to proper time" is a category mistake because the two have opposite signatures and opposite monotonicity under motion. The concession is in writing, with the specific failure mode (Riemannian vs Lorentzian, opposite sign under motion) named, and the conditional pathway (the §sec:signature_resolution indefinite-metric extension) flagged as open. This is the level of honesty the structural-realist framing of 3092 commits to.

### 7. The scope qualifier at 3157 prevents the perpetual-motion attack on the non-equilibrium subsection

Line 3157 reads: "we make no claim about thermodynamic perpetual motion or about cosmological complexity growth, both of which are outside the scope of the present construction." This prevents the natural attack — that "sustained non-equilibrium" via a self-excited circuit smells like perpetual motion if read as a thermodynamic claim — by stating in writing that the claim is about the simulation regime's threshold-detector single-seed dynamics (§sec:results) and not about thermodynamic or cosmological complexity growth. The Wheeler 1983 "Law without law" citation for the self-excited circuit at 3155 is canonical (`references.bib:434-443`; this is the Wheeler paper that introduced "the universe as a self-excited circuit" language).

### 8. The unfalsifiability acknowledgement at 3060 and 3102 is the correct philosophical-status statement

Line 3060: "This interpretation may be unfalsifiable as any measurement can be reinterpreted as labeling information geometry, making the framework compatible with any result by construction." Line 3102: "the framework may ultimately function as philosophical interpretation rather than scientific theory. If it makes no falsifiable predictions beyond qualitative structural analogies, it occupies a different epistemic category than physics proper." Together these acknowledge in writing that the broader phenomenological interpretation may be metaphysical rather than scientific. This is exactly the disclosure that distinguishes responsible speculative philosophy of physics from overreach. The text does not claim to have shown the framework is falsifiable as a whole; it claims that *one component* (dimensionless constants) supplies a symmetric test, and that the broader interpretation may not be falsifiable. Both claims are correctly stated.

## Falsification conditions

The position above is wrong if any of the following is established by red on primary-source evidence:

1. **Self-undercutting language for the mass disavowal.** A sentence in the speculative section (or in §sec:mass cross-referenced from it) asserts $M_{\mathrm{eff}} \propto \Sigma_p^{-1}$ as an *empirical* dispersion-law claim — i.e., as something measured rather than identified by construction — and is not glossed by the disavowal at 3070 or 1904. Pointing to upstream language that *describes* the structural identification (e.g., 3068 "formally analogous to inertial mass") is not sufficient; the attack requires an empirical assertion that survives both disavowals. If red can produce such a sentence at a specific line, the rock-solid defense fails on this front.

2. **Misattribution of structural realism.** [Worrall1989] does not in fact ground epistemic structural realism (e.g., if the cited *Dialectica* paper is mistitled or makes a different claim), or [Ladyman2007] does not in fact ground ontic structural realism in the book-length sense, or "remaining agnostic among these variants" is logically incoherent given the manuscript's other commitments. The defense relies on these attributions being canonical; verified against `references.bib:241-249` and the Wikipedia philosophy-of-science treatment, they are. If a primary-source citation contradicts the attribution, the defense fails on this front.

3. **Boxed postulate (iv) does not match the upstream construction.** If §sec:worked_signature uses three mutually trace-orthogonal spatial generators in 2D in a way that makes (iv) a *current* postulate rather than an *extension* postulate, the box's phrasing "under the 4D extension" is wrong and the box-vs-construction match fails. The verification at §sec:worked_signature 2822 shows a single 2D generator `T = diag(1,-1)` used for both directions, so (iv) is correctly an extension postulate; if red shows otherwise, the defense fails on this front.

4. **Cencov misapplication.** If "Cencov scale fixings" at 3084 misrepresents [Cencov1972] — i.e., if Cencov's theorem does not in fact admit a residual positive-scalar freedom that the construction must specify — the citation at 3084 fails. The defense rests on Cencov's uniqueness theorem stating uniqueness up to a positive scalar ([AmariNagaoka2000] Ch. 2 statement), which is the natural locus for "scale fixing." If red shows Cencov uniqueness is *not* up to a scalar (i.e., that the metric is uniquely fixed including the scalar), the defense fails on this front. (I assess this risk as low — the up-to-scalar caveat is standard in the textbook statement — but the falsification condition is open if red can show otherwise.)

5. **The symmetric success-failure criterion is a moving goalpost.** If "fully developed information-geometric construction" can be re-defined ad hoc to exclude any specific failed derivation, the symmetry at 3084 is rhetorical only. The "burden is on the program ... not to indefinitely defer the test by appeal to incompleteness" clause is supposed to foreclose this, but if red produces a concrete instance where the manuscript or its supporting literature *has* indefinitely deferred a failed derivation by exactly this appeal, the falsifiability commitment is hollow and the defense fails on this front.

6. **The plural-time concession is undercut elsewhere.** If a sentence elsewhere in the section asserts that the framework reproduces relativistic proper time (without the indefinite-metric caveat of 3149), the rock-solid claim fails. Verified: §sec:fisher_arc_length at 2609 and §3149 make the same distinction, and the worked-signature subsection registers the existence-toy-model status of any Lorentzian construction. No contradicting sentence has been found.

7. **Action-principle conflation.** If the §3076 text claims that natural-gradient flow *is* Hamilton's principle (second-order Newtonian dynamics from `δS=0`) rather than the gradient-flow reading of `δF=0` on the statistical manifold, the action-principle reading is wrong by the textbook standard ([Amari1998]; the natural-gradient flow is first-order on the Fisher-Riemannian statistical manifold, not second-order Newtonian). The text writes "mathematically analogous to Hamilton's principle" and disavows Lagrangian, Newton's-laws, and path-integral recovery, so the conflation is in writing pre-empted. If red can show the text in fact conflates these despite the "mathematically analogous" language, the defense fails on this front.

If none of (1)-(7) is established, the four subsections meet the standard of publication-ready labeled speculative philosophy of physics, and the claim stands.
