## Decisive evidence

Two primary-source contradictions of assertive sentences in the manuscript, each conceded by Blue in the rebuttal:

1. **Line 3076 ("become"):** The text reads "The Euler-Lagrange equations from $\delta\mathcal{F} = 0$ **become** our natural-gradient flow equations." Per [Amari1998 §2], the natural gradient $\tilde\nabla L = G^{-1}\nabla L$ is first-order steepest descent on the Fisher–Riemannian statistical manifold. Per [Goldstein-Poole-Safko 2002 §2.3], the Euler-Lagrange equation derived from an action functional $S = \int L\,dt$ via Hamilton's principle is second-order in $q$. The two are not the same equation; "become" asserts identity, not analogy. The manuscript's own line 1906 ("no Euler-Lagrange equations are derived") and line 3172 ("an intrinsic Riemannian gradient flow on the statistical manifold in the sense of Amari (1998) §2, not the overdamped limit of a Newtonian SDE") confirm that line 3076 is the outlier — internally inconsistent with two other locations in the same manuscript.

2. **Line 3092 ("remaining agnostic"):** The text invokes "the structural-realism family — Cassirer's neo-Kantian variant, Worrall's epistemic structural realism, and the ontic structural realism of Ladyman & Ross (remaining agnostic among these variants)." Per the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Structural Realism (Ladyman), ESR retains unobservable individuals with intrinsic but inaccessible natures, while eliminative OSR denies the existence of individuals beneath structure. These positions are mutually exclusive on the ontological question of whether there are individuals; agnosticism between $p$ and $\neg p$ is the absence of a position. The manuscript's own line 3090 ("information geometry on principal bundles ... constitutes the noumenal realm. This structure exists independently but has no accessible content") commits to the OSR side substantively, undercutting the agnosticism clause two sentences later.

Blue's rebuttal explicitly concedes both strikes: "I concede both of red's load-bearing strikes. They are well-cited, they identify real prose-level defects in lines 3076 and 3092, and they are publication-blocking in their current form."

## Reasoning

The claim under debate is conjunctive: "publication-ready **and** rock-solid." Blue's rebuttal concedes that both of Red's strikes are "publication-blocking in their current form" — that concession alone disposes of the conjunction. Source-of-truth precedence binds the judgment: Red cited [Amari1998 §2] and the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Structural Realism (Ladyman, current edition) — standard external canon for both claims — while Blue's defense after concession reduces to an attempt to redefine "publication-ready" as "analytical core holds, prose defects are line-edits." Two assertive sentences in a 100-line section whose verbs are stronger than the surrounding hedging cannot be deflected to the section's average disclaimer level; a reviewer assembles the section's commitment from its strongest assertions. The mass-Fisher disavowal, Wheeler 1983 attribution, boxed-postulate scope-matching, plural-time concession, and Cencov scale-fixing usage all stand — Red conceded these explicitly. But the claim is not "the section's analytical core is defensible"; it is "publication-ready and rock-solid." On the cited primary sources, both halves of the conjunction fail at lines 3076 and 3092, and Blue has conceded as much. Splitting the verdict to acknowledge that the analytical core survives would violate the forbidden-synthesis rule when one side has cited Amari 1998 plus SEP and the other has formally conceded.
