### Edit 1 — Action-principle wording at PIFB:3076

Current: "The Euler-Lagrange equations from `delta F = 0` become our natural-gradient flow equations: Fisher-Rao natural gradient on the Gaussian sector (mu, Sigma) and Lie-group natural gradient on the gauge-frame sector G, the latter applied via the right-trivialized group retraction of Eq. gauge_group_retraction."

The verb "become" asserts identity. Per Amari 1998 §2, natural gradient is first-order Riemannian steepest descent on the statistical manifold; per Goldstein, Poole & Safko 2002 §2.3, Euler-Lagrange from Hamilton's principle is second-order conservative dynamics. They are not the same equation. The manuscript's own §sec:mass at line 1906 ("no Euler-Lagrange equations are derived") and §sec:gravity at line 3172 ("intrinsic Riemannian gradient flow ... not the overdamped limit of a Newtonian SDE") confirm 3076 is the outlier.

Repair: replace "become" with "are mathematically analogous in form to" and tighten the surrounding context: "The variational principle for the free-energy functional defines its critical points by `delta F = 0`; the corresponding dynamics in our framework are not the second-order Euler-Lagrange equations of Hamilton's principle, but rather the first-order Riemannian natural-gradient flow of Amari (1998): Fisher-Rao natural gradient on the Gaussian sector `(mu, Sigma)` and Lie-group natural gradient on the gauge-frame sector G, the latter applied via the right-trivialized group retraction of Eq. gauge_group_retraction. The mathematical analogy with Hamilton's principle is one of form rather than of derivation."

### Edit 2 — Structural-realism positioning at PIFB:3092

Current: "The framework recasts this in the structural-realism family — Cassirer's neo-Kantian variant, Worrall's epistemic structural realism~\cite{Worrall1989}, and the ontic structural realism of Ladyman \& Ross~\cite{Ladyman2007} (remaining agnostic among these variants) — rather than adopting Kant's stronger constitutive-a-priori commitment."

ESR retains the existence of unobservable individuals whose intrinsic natures are inaccessible; OSR (eliminative) denies that there are unobservable individuals beneath structure (Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Structural Realism", Ladyman). These positions are not jointly tenable as "either is fine". The manuscript's own line 3090 ("information geometry constitutes the noumenal realm") substantively commits to OSR, so the "agnosticism" claim is undercut.

Repair: replace the parenthetical "(remaining agnostic among these variants)" with a positional commitment: "The framework recasts this in the ontic-structural-realism family of Ladyman & Ross~\cite{Ladyman2007} — information geometry is the structure that constitutes the noumenal realm, with no further individuals beneath it — with Cassirer's neo-Kantian variant and Worrall's epistemic structural realism~\cite{Worrall1989} as related precursors. We explicitly disavow Kant's stronger constitutive-a-priori commitment, which Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations} addresses directly."
