## Core attack

Blue's defense rests on a per-construction reading of the existential quantifier (Blue Opening, "Position", lines 8-11). Under that reading Blue then concedes outright **two** distinct gaps where no toggle in `VFEConfig` realizes the manuscript construction (Blue Opening, "Falsification conditions", items 1 and 2). The claim text in `00_claim.md` lines 23-25 makes one concession sufficient to falsify: "Blue (defender) loses if red identifies *any single* mathematical gap, missing term, incorrect gradient flow, broken gauge equivariance, or theoretical construction in `participatory_it_from_bit.tex` that has *no corresponding code path* in `transformer/vfe/` under any toggle setting." Blue's own concessions therefore satisfy red's burden.

I will not rest the attack on those concessions alone. Three additional load-bearing weaknesses survive Blue's defense.

**First** — Blue's reading of the existential is malformed. The claim text reads "There exists *a* configuration path (set of toggle/flag values) through the `transformer/vfe/` module that exactly realizes the variational free energy minimization framework" (`00_claim.md` line 11). The singular indefinite article governs a single SET of values; the construction-by-construction structure inside is the quantifier scope WITHIN that set, not OVER configurations. The natural-language reading is: "∃ one config s.t. ∀ X in PIFB, X is realized," not "∀ X ∃ X-specific config." Under the simultaneous reading the load-bearing mutual exclusion is binding. The omega-direct path (`gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'`) is rejected at config-construction time unless `diagonal_covariance=True` (`transformer/vfe/config.py:566-573`: `raise ValueError("gauge_parameterization='omega_direct' currently requires diagonal_covariance=True")`). The exact full-cov sandwich decode is rejected at config-construction time unless `diagonal_covariance=False` (`transformer/vfe/config.py:491-495`: `raise ValueError` listing "diagonal_covariance=True" as incompatible). These two raises are mutually contradictory under a single config — no single `VFEConfig` instance instantiates both. PIFB:2566-2570 (`eq:gauge_group_retraction`) prescribes the group-level retraction as canonical; PIFB:1619-1626 and Nakahara 2003 §10.3 prescribe the (0,2)-tensor sandwich `\Sigma \mapsto \Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top` as canonical. No single config realizes both [Nakahara2003 §10.3; Amari1998 §3.4 right-invariant retraction]. Blue concedes this in falsification condition 7 ("If the judge reads the existential as 'one config realizes all constructions simultaneously', blue concedes") — the load-bearing question is which reading binds, and the claim text says one config.

The cocycle parameterization `\Omega_{ij} = U_i \exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G) U_j^{-1}` (PIFB:eq:edge_relaxed_omega at line 833) is realized at `transformer/vfe/non_flat.py:1-80` under `use_non_flat_transport=True`. ## Defense

The defense of the simultaneous reading of the existential rests on the claim text and on the falsification conditions in `00_claim.md`. The claim says "There exists a configuration path (set of toggle/flag values)" — singular, indefinite, governing the set. Re-reading "for every construction X" as a separate ∃ per X requires reading the indefinite article distributively over X, which is a non-standard logical scope. The judge should read the existential as binding on a single config.

Even if the judge accepts Blue's per-construction reading: under the falsification text "Blue (defender) loses if red identifies *any single* mathematical gap, missing term, incorrect gradient flow, broken gauge equivariance, or theoretical construction in `participatory_it_from_bit.tex` that has *no corresponding code path* in `transformer/vfe/` under any toggle setting" (`00_claim.md` lines 23-25), the four gaps identified above — Wilson holonomy-penalty regularizer (PIFB:863), cocycle-homotopy parameter `\alpha` (PIFB:871), per-dim Bayesian α product-rule correction not derived in the manuscript (Blue's own concession 1), and pullback preconditioner unreachable in the inner E-step (Blue's own concession 2, plus `transformer/vfe/config.py:597-609` `raise ValueError`) — each individually satisfies the "single gap" trigger.

Blue's primary external citations are sound on the canon side: Friston 2010 Eq. 2.2, Nakahara 2003 §10.3, Amari 1998 §3.4, Hall 2015 §7.3, Bishop 2006 §10.1.1 are all correctly invoked and standardly understood. The attack is not against the canon Blue cited; the attack is that the /vfe code does not realize the full set of constructions PIFB asserts, even under maximally permissive toggle choices, and that two of Blue's three sub-defenses (sandwich vs. retraction; standard attention emergence) require mutually incompatible toggle settings or never reach the manuscript's stated limit. PIFB:2572-2576 explicitly labels the chart-coordinate `\phi`-update as "the first-order approximation"; the canonical group-level retraction (PIFB:2566-2570) is only reachable under `omega_direct`, and that path is gated to `diagonal_covariance=True` (`config.py:566-573`), foreclosing the full-cov sandwich `\Sigma \mapsto \Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top` ([Nakahara 2003 §10.3]). The two canon-prescribed forms cannot coexist under any single `VFEConfig` instance.
