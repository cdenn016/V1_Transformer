On the real algebra `gl(K, R)` and on its semisimple summand `sl(K, R)`, every reachable option is signature-indefinite as a metric on the full algebra: the Killing form on `sl(n, R)` is `B(X,Y) = 2n·tr(XY)`, which is positive on the symmetric-traceless cone and negative on the skew-symmetric subspace under the Cartan decomposition `sl(n,R) = so(n) ⊕ p` [Hall 2015 §7.6 Theorem 7.36]. Even with `phi_project_slk=True` (`transformer/vfe/config.py:171`) projecting φ onto `sl(K)`, the metric remains signature-indefinite as a bilinear form on real `sl(K)`. The `killing_per_block` route (built at `transformer/core/gauge_preconditioner.py:348-467` and applied via `transformer/core/phi_evolution.py:61-65`) does center-regularize the degenerate trace direction of each `gl(d_h)` summand, but center-regularization does not flip the symmetric-vs-skew signature on `sl(d_h, R)`. So red is correct that no φ-chart preconditioner combination supplies a positive-definite invariant inner product on real `gl(K)`.

This concession is genuine. Hall 2015 §7.6 is decisive against that line of defense.

## Core attack

Red's falsification condition 5 is precisely the load-bearing question: "a toggle combination that yields some other manuscript-endorsed positive-definite invariant metric on `gl(K)`." Red's opening frames `pullback` as "the unique positive-definite option supplied by the manuscript." That framing is too narrow on two counts.

First, the manuscript explicitly licenses an open choice of inner product. PIFB:2558-2564 (Eq:pullback_metric) introduces the pullback construction with the parenthetical "for any choice of inner product `⟨·,·⟩_G` on `g`." The pullback formula `G_{ab}(φ) = ⟨Ψ(ad_φ) T_a, Ψ(ad_φ) T_b⟩_G, Ψ(z) = (e^z - 1)/z` is a chart-coordinate operator that translates a fixed inner product on `g` (at the identity) into a position-dependent right-invariant metric in `φ`-coordinates. The manuscript is offering the inner-product slot for instantiation — it has not committed to a single closed form.

Second, the manuscript's canonical update equation is written at the group level, not the chart level. PIFB:2546-2552 (Eq:gauge_natural_gradient) gives
```
dU_i/dt = -η_φ · U_i · ∇̃_{φ_i} F
```
as the continuous flow. PIFB:2566-2570 (Eq:gauge_group_retraction) gives the discrete-time analogue `U^{t+1} = U^t · exp(-η · ∇̃_φ F)`. PIFB:2572-2576 then states explicitly: "Truncating to first order recovers the Euclidean-in-`g` form `φ̇_i = -η_φ ∇̃_{φ_i} F` that has been written in earlier sections of this manuscript and is implemented in the working code; the chart-coordinate truncation is exact in the abelian sector and is a first-order approximation otherwise. **The canonical theoretical update is the group-level form Eq.~\eqref{eq:gauge_natural_gradient}-\eqref{eq:gauge_group_retraction}, with the chart-coordinate truncation serving as the implementation specialization.**" Red's attack treats the chart truncation as the canonical form and the pullback as the only escape; the manuscript treats the group-level retraction as the canonical form and the pullback as one chart-coordinate translation of it.

`transformer/vfe/omega_direct.py:331-395` (`omega_natural_grad_step`) implements the canonical group-level retraction directly:
```
X = proj_{span(G^a)}( Ω^{-1} · ∂F/∂Ω )
Ω^{new}     = Ω · exp(-η · X)
Ω^{-1, new} = exp(η · X) · Ω^{-1}
```
The toggle `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'` (`transformer/vfe/config.py:277` Literal definition, validator at lines 561-591) routes the inner E-step through `_forward_omega_direct` at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:774-775` and `1981+`, with the retraction call at `e_step.py:2272-2278` consuming `self.e_phi_lr` as the algebra step. The retraction is exact on the group manifold at every step — no BCH residual, no chart truncation. This matches PIFB:2566-2570 line for line.

The algebra-projection step uses a per-block **Frobenius gram** `M_{ab} = ⟨G^a, G^b⟩_F` on the generator basis (`transformer/vfe/omega_direct.py:234-292`, with the function-name disclaimer at lines 242-251 stating the implementation "builds the Frobenius gram, not the Killing form on `gl(n)`"). For a linearly independent generator basis the Frobenius gram is positive-definite by construction (gram matrix of independent vectors in an inner-product space) [Hall 2015 §A.7, standard linear algebra]. Frobenius `⟨X,Y⟩_F = tr(X^T Y)` is also right-invariant under the right action because it is bilinear and the inner product is fixed at the identity — right-invariance of a Riemannian metric on a Lie group is equivalent to specification of an inner product on the Lie algebra at the identity [Absil/Mahony/Sepulchre 2008 Ch. 3 §3.6; do Carmo 1992 Ch. 3]. The pullback construction is the chart-coordinate translation of that identity-inner-product to a position-dependent `φ`-coordinate metric; when the descent lives on `G` directly the position dependence is absorbed into the group element and the unmodified Frobenius gram at the identity suffices.

Therefore the toggle `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'` realizes Eq:gauge_natural_gradient (PIFB:2547-2552) — the canonical form of the manuscript's φ-sector update — under a positive-definite right-invariant inner product (Frobenius) that PIFB:2558 explicitly licenses ("any choice of inner product `⟨·,·⟩_G` on `g`"). Red's framing that the pullback is "the only positive-definite option" applies only inside the chart-coordinate specialization, which PIFB:2576 itself labels the first-order approximation, not the canonical update.

## Defense

The defense rests on three points:

**(1) The canonical form is the group-level flow, not the chart-coordinate truncation.** PIFB:2547-2552 writes the canonical equation as `dU/dt = -η_φ U_i ∇̃_φ F` — the dynamical variable is `U_i ∈ G`, not `φ_i ∈ g`. PIFB:2566-2570 gives the discrete retraction at the group level. PIFB:2572-2576 explicitly assigns the chart form the role of "implementation specialization" / "first-order approximation" / "exact in the abelian sector and a first-order approximation otherwise." The `omega_direct` path is the literal realization of the parent equation in code; the φ-chart path is the truncation. Red's attack on the absent `'pullback'` value targets the latter — but the user's claim is existential ("a configuration path exists"), and the manuscript's canonical configuration is the group-level path.

**(2) Frobenius is a valid right-invariant positive-definite inner product on `g` that PIFB:2558 licenses by name.** Right-invariance of a Riemannian metric on a Lie group is equivalent to a fixed (position-independent) inner product on `g` at the identity, transported by right translation [Absil/Mahony/Sepulchre 2008 §3.6.2]. The Frobenius inner product `tr(X^T Y)` is positive-definite on real matrices and therefore positive-definite on the generator span [Hall 2015 §A.7]. The manuscript's parenthetical "for any choice of inner product `⟨·,·⟩_G` on `g`" at PIFB:2558 is the manuscript's own affirmation that the inner-product slot is open; the pullback is one chart-coordinate translation, Frobenius-at-identity is another (and degenerates to the simpler identity-metric case when working on `G` directly).

**(3) The determinant drift that the indefinite signature would cause on `gl(K)` is controlled by `omega_normalize_every` and `project_omega_to_slk` at `transformer/vfe/omega_direct.py:403-463`.** The known degeneracy of any bi-invariant form on the `R·I` center of `gl(K) = sl(K) ⊕ R·I` is the only obstacle to a true Riemannian descent at the group level; the determinant projection step removes that direction on a configurable cadence (`config.py:277+` — `omega_normalize_every`, `omega_project_slk`, `phi_strict_glplus`). The trajectory then lives effectively on the `SL_+(K)` slice with Frobenius right-invariant metric, which is positive-definite on `sl(K, R) ⊕ R · I` modulo the projected-out center.

**Pre-empt red's likely counter-attack** that omega_direct still uses a "Killing-preconditioner" per `omega_direct.py:22-23`. That docstring fragment is followed by the function-level disclaimer at `omega_direct.py:242-251` stating "The implementation builds the **Frobenius gram** `M_{ab} = ⟨G^a, G^b⟩_F`, not the Killing form on `gl(n)`. The two coincide up to a positive factor only for trace-free skew bases — neither holds for `GL(K)`. The function is suitable as a projection metric (which is what callers need), not as a natural-gradient preconditioner derived from the Killing form." The code is canonical (per CLAUDE.md: "do NOT rely on code comments... focus on the actual code"); `transformer/vfe/omega_direct.py:284-289` literally computes `gram = einsum('akl,bkl->ab', G_block_subset, G_block_subset)` — that is the Frobenius gram, not `B(X,Y) = tr(ad_X ad_Y)`.

**Red's falsification condition 1** asked for a code path that threads `structure_constants` and `gram` to make `mode='pullback'` work inside the inner E-step. Blue does not produce that. Blue produces falsification condition 5: a manuscript-endorsed positive-definite invariant metric reachable under a different toggle. The `omega_direct` toggle is reachable (config.py:561-591 accepts it; `e_step.py:774-775` dispatches to it; `e_step.py:2272-2278` runs the canonical retraction at every E-step iteration with a positive-definite Frobenius gram as the algebra-projection inner product). Condition 5 is met.

The compound existential claim does not require the φ-chart path to admit a positive-definite metric; it requires the existence of **some** toggle setting that realizes the manuscript construction. PIFB:2547-2576 writes the canonical update as the group-level retraction with the chart-coordinate truncation labeled "implementation specialization." The `omega_direct` toggle realizes the canonical form. Red's attack on the missing `'pullback'` option targets a code path the manuscript itself does not designate as canonical.

### Falsification of this defense

This defense fails if (a) red produces a primary source showing Frobenius `tr(X^T Y)` is not a valid right-invariant Riemannian metric on a matrix Lie group, contradicting do Carmo 1992 Ch. 3 / Absil/Mahony/Sepulchre 2008 §3.6.2; or (b) red shows PIFB:2576's "canonical theoretical update is the group-level form" sentence is contradicted by a stronger manuscript passage that names the φ-chart pullback as the canonical form; or (c) red demonstrates that `omega_direct` does not in fact descend on the canonical retraction Eq:gauge_group_retraction because the algebra-projection step at `omega_direct.py:295-328` introduces a non-canonical pre-conditioning step the manuscript does not license.
