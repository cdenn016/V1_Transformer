# Blue Opening — vfe-module-purity-for-pifb

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest red attack reads the claim's existential quantifier *simultaneously* — "one toggle setting realizes every PIFB construction at once" — and points out that the codepaths for full-covariance sandwich transport (`diagonal_covariance=False`, gated at config.py:543-548) and group-level retraction (`gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'`, gated to `diagonal_covariance=True` at config.py:566-573) are mutually exclusive, so no single config realizes both the Nakahara-canonical (0,2)-transport rule and the canonical group-level retraction at the same time; further, three constructions in PIFB have no realizing flag at all — the per-dim Bayesian α (e_step.py:50-66 admits this is a generalization of the scalar PIFB:1311 form), the pullback preconditioner inside the E-step (config.py:597-609 raises at runtime), and the per-K-dim α product-rule correction not derived in the manuscript.

## Position

The user's claim is per-construction, not whole-path: the sub-claim structure in `00_claim.md` reads "for every construction X in PIFB, ∃ a toggle setting that realizes X in `transformer/vfe/`" — a separate ∃ per X. Under that reading the claim is defensible for the in-scope (language-modeling) constructions of PIFB §"Complete Free Energy Functional", §"Recasting External Observations", §"Transformer Architectures as the Zero-Dimensional Limit", and §"Natural Gradient Dynamics", with two specific concessions enumerated under Falsification Conditions. The defense rests on external canon: Friston 2010 Form 3, Amari 2016 Ch. 2-3, Nakahara 2003 §10.3, Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1, Bishop 2006 §10.1.1, and Hall 2015 §7.3.

I defend the claim *as a per-construction existential* and concede outright the per-dim Bayesian α and the E-step pullback preconditioner as the gaps red should win on if pressed. Defending those would be sycophantic. The remaining constructions are realized.

## Evidence

### Canonical F decomposition (Friston 2010 Form 3) — observation term as cross-entropy

Friston 2010 Eq. 2.2 writes free energy in Form 3 as `F = E_q[-log p(o|s)] + KL(q‖p)`; the accuracy term is the negative log-likelihood — cross-entropy with the observation density. PIFB:1441-1446 (`eq:xent_resolution`) makes this identification explicit: `-E_{q_i}[log q_{e_k}]` "is structurally identical to term 5 of the canonical free energy at Eq.~\eqref{eq:free_energy_functional_final}". The /vfe outer M-step at `transformer/vfe/model.py:227-306` computes `F.cross_entropy(logits, targets)`; this realizes the canonical observation operator under the manuscript's own xent-resolution identification, grounded in the external canon (Friston 2010 Form 3). The E-step does not see `targets` (Law 1, enforced by signature at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:13-18`).

### Self-coupling KL(q‖p) and gauge-coupled KL term — analytic kernel

PIFB:1247-1392 enumerates the canonical five-term F. The first two implemented terms `α·KL(q‖p)` and `Σ_ij β_ij KL(q_i ‖ Ω_ij q_j)` are assembled in the inner loop at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1043-1073` (fused single-softmax dispatch, `lambda_softmax=0.0` at the envelope-identity β fixed point) and `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:954-1042` (per-head softmax at `τ_h = κ·√d_head`). The attention-entropy term `τ·β·log(β/π)` is assembled at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1574-1593` under the `include_attention_entropy=True` toggle; this is the standard maximum-entropy Lagrangian for soft assignment under uniform prior `π_ij = 1/N` (Bishop 2006 Ch. 10 — entropy-regularized soft assignment is the unique stationary point of the row Lagrangian, derivation matches PIFB:1282 `eq:beta_optimal`).

### Covariance transport via the sandwich identity (Nakahara 2003 §10.3)

Nakahara 2003 §10.3 establishes parallel transport of a (0,2)-tensor under structure group action as the two-sided conjugation `T_new = ρ(g⁻ᵀ) T ρ(g⁻¹)`; equivalently for the (2,0) identification under `ρ(g) = Ω`, the rule is `Σ_new = Ω Σ Ω^T`. The transport kernel at `transformer/vfe/attention.py:82-121` constructs `compute_gauge_transport` as block-diagonal `(exp(φ·G_h), exp(-φ·G_h))` pairs, and the full-cov branch at `transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:291-372` is the exact sandwich `Σ_v = A_v diag(σ_0) A_v^T` per block. Toggle: `diagonal_covariance=False, use_prior_bank=True, gauge_fixed_priors=True` activates the exact sandwich on encode; under `exact_full_cov_decode=True` (validated by `transformer/vfe/config.py:484-507`) the decode KL is the exact block-Gaussian KL. This realizes the canonical (0,2)-transport rule [Nakahara2003 §10.3].

### Group-level retraction `U^{t+1} = U^t · exp(-η·∇̃F)` — Lie-group natural gradient

Amari 1998 §3.4 and Absil/Mahony/Sepulchre 2008 give the right-invariant retraction `g^{t+1} = g^t exp(η X)` as the canonical Lie-group natural-gradient update. PIFB:2566-2570 (`eq:gauge_group_retraction`) prescribes this; PIFB:2572-2576 explicitly labels the chart-coordinate `dφ/dt = -η_φ ∇̃_φ F + O(‖·‖²)` as "the implementation specialization", "exact in the abelian sector and a first-order approximation otherwise". The toggle `gauge_parameterization='omega_direct'` at `transformer/vfe/omega_direct.py` stores Ω directly on the group manifold with right-invariant retraction `Ω ← Ω · exp(-η·X_proj)`. Validated by config gating at `transformer/vfe/config.py:561-565`. Under this toggle the BCH-truncation residual is zero by construction — the retraction is on the group, no chart-coordinate truncation is applied.

### Multi-head `GL(d_head)^H` reduction and Killing form on `sl(d_head)`

PIFB:1590, 1785 reduce the multi-head transformer to the block-diagonal subgroup `GL(d_head)^H ⊂ GL(K)`. Hall 2015 §7.3 gives the Killing form on `gl(K)` as `B(X,Y) = 2K tr(XY) - 2 tr(X) tr(Y)`. The decomposition `gl(K) = sl(K) ⊕ ℝ·I` (Hall 2015 Ch. 7) means: restricted to `sl(K)` (traceless), `tr(X) = 0` kills the second term, leaving `B(X,Y) = 2K tr(XY)`. Since `sl(K)` is semisimple, this form is positive definite on `sl(K)` (Hall 2015 §7.3 — Cartan's criterion). The toggle `phi_preconditioner='killing_per_block'` combined with `phi_project_slk=True` realizes a positive-definite, block-diagonal Killing metric on `sl(d_head)⊕...⊕sl(d_head)`, which is the canonical natural-gradient metric on the working subalgebra. Toggle composition is reachable in the E-step path (config.py:597-609 accepts `'killing_per_block'`).

### Standard scaled dot-product attention as zero-dim limit (Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1)

PIFB:1571-1842 derives Vaswani 2017 Eq. 3 `softmax(QK^T/√d_k)V` as the trivial-frame, isotropic-Gaussian, shared-bilinear limit of the gauge KL attention. The `τ = κ·√K` form realized at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:954-1042` is the standard `√d_k` scaling with the learnable `κ` (PIFB:1571 labels this as the standard scaling). The value-aggregation `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_ij Ω_ij μ_j` (PIFB:1816 `eq:glk_mixture_aggregation_itfb`) is the gauge-covariant generalization of standard `Σ_j β_ij V_j`. Under `gauge_fixed_priors=False, use_prior_bank=True, gauge_parameterization='phi'`, with the gauge attention head producing β over KL on transported beliefs, the canonical attention emerges.

### Parallel mean-field non-monotonicity is canonical, not a deviation

Bishop 2006 §10.1.1 establishes that variational message passing (CAVI) is *only* monotone in F under sequential coordinate-wise updates; parallel updates are a fixed-point iteration on the simultaneous stationarity conditions and have no guaranteed monotone descent. The /vfe E-step is parallel (all tokens update simultaneously per iteration); the non-monotone-warning at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:200-214` documents a canonical property of parallel VMP, not a deviation from the manuscript.

### Adiabatic timescale separation: model-channel and hyper-prior terms not required in the per-token transformer limit

PIFB:1502-1551 establishes timescale separation `η_q : η_s : η_φ ~ 1 : ε : ε²` with the canonical slow primitive being the generative model `s`. PIFB:1533 explicitly states: "Per-token priors $p_i$ also evolve on a learning timescale (in the transformer limit they appear as the PriorBank entries, updated by gradient descent over training data)". The model channel `s` is, in the transformer-limit reading, absorbed into the M-step embedding parameters (PriorBank entries `μ_v`, `σ_v`, and the gauge generator basis). On the perceptual timescale `τ_q` the manuscript itself prescribes `s_i` and `φ_i` frozen — so `λ_h · KL(s‖h)` and `γ_ij · KL(s_i ‖ s_j)` have *no per-iteration content to assemble inside the E-step* under the language-modeling reduction. They are realized over training as the outer-loop gradient through the unrolled E-step iterations into the embedding parameters and PriorBank entries (`transformer/vfe/prior_bank.py:408-433`). External canon support: standard variational EM with frozen latent at the perception timescale (Beal 2003 §2.3; Friston 2010 §3) defines the M-step over the slow latent as gradient descent on the marginalized F, which is what the /vfe outer M-step does on the PriorBank embeddings.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong (and red wins) if any of the following hold.

1. **Per-dim Bayesian α has no canonical reduction.** I concede this gap outright. PIFB:1311 `eq:state_dependent_alpha` derives the scalar `α_i* = c₀/(b₀ + KL(q_i‖p_i))` with a single scalar log-barrier `R(α_i) = b₀ α_i − c₀ log α_i`. The /vfe implementation at e_step.py:50-66 admits per-K-dim `raw_c0, raw_b0 ∈ ℝ^K` and a per-K-dim correction `(α*)² · b₀/c₀ · ∂KL_k/∂θ`. For K > 1 the per-dim form does *not* reduce to the scalar form (per-dim `c₀/(b₀ + KL_k)` is not equal to `c₀/(b₀ + Σ_k KL_k)`). No toggle in `VFEConfig` makes `raw_c0` / `raw_b0` scalar. If red attacks here, blue concedes.

2. **Pullback preconditioner inside E-step.** PIFB:2559-2564 `eq:pullback_metric` is presented as the canonical positive-definite metric on non-compact `gl(K)` via `Ψ(ad_φ) = (e^z − 1)/z` pullback. `transformer/vfe/config.py:597-609` raises at runtime if `phi_preconditioner='pullback'`. The Killing-on-`sl(d_head)` fallback is positive-definite on the traceless subalgebra (Hall 2015 §7.3) and is the canonical metric *for the semisimple part* — but it does not realize the pullback construction for general `gl(d_head)` (the trace direction is left preconditioned by `phi_trace_clamp`, not by the pullback Gram form). If red insists the canonical metric is the pullback form *and* that the semisimple-reduction is non-equivalent, blue concedes this specific construction.

3. **The manuscript prescribes posterior-φ → next-layer prior-φ cascade.** `transformer/vfe/stack.py:145` hard-wires `new_prior_phi = initial_priors.phi`; there is no toggle to propagate posterior φ across the boundary. If red produces a manuscript line where the L-block cascade explicitly prescribes posterior-φ as next-layer prior-φ, blue concedes. PIFB:1535-1545 is silent on this in the L-block reduction (frames "may evolve" under `F_frame`); blue reads silence as no construction-to-realize. If red finds a binding prescription, this becomes a real gap.

4. **The cross-entropy decode is not the canonical observation operator.** If red produces a Friston / Parr-Pezzulo-Friston primary source stating Form 3's `E_q[-log p(o|s)]` is *not* cross-entropy with the observation likelihood (it is), or that the M-step CE in `/vfe model.py:227-306` lacks the `E_q[...]` expectation in some load-bearing way, blue concedes.

5. **The sandwich transport is silently violated under some active branch the claim requires.** If red produces a path under the in-scope toggles where covariance is transported one-sidedly or without the inverse-transpose for the (0,2) reading, blue concedes. The diagonal-σ approximation is *not* a sandwich violation — under `diagonal_covariance=True, exact_diagonal_transport=False` the per-block sandwich is approximated by `diag(A diag(s) A^T)`, which is the standard mean-field approximation for diagonal-σ regimes (the exact path is reached via `exact_diagonal_transport=True` or `diagonal_covariance=False`).

6. **Multi-head Killing-on-sl is non-positive-definite under the active basis.** If red produces a `sl(d_head)` basis or computation where `tr(X_i X_j) ≤ 0` for some independent pair (which would falsify Hall 2015 §7.3 Cartan's criterion on the simple Lie algebra `sl(K)`), blue concedes.

7. **A simultaneous-composition reading of the claim is binding.** If the judge reads the existential as "one config realizes all constructions simultaneously", blue concedes that no such config exists (omega_direct ↔ diagonal-σ vs. full-cov sandwich is the load-bearing mutual exclusion). Blue's defense is explicitly per-construction; if the judge reads the claim as whole-path simultaneous, the defense is malformed.
