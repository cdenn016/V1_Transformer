# Blue Rebuttal — cross-scale-shadow-legitimacy

## Concession

I grant red's C4 in its narrow factual form: the manuscript at line 546 (i) labels the cross-scale shadow a "structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference," and (ii) declines to display a reduction or approximation chain from the Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 generative-model conditional $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ to the transported-posterior form $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$. The construction is therefore not a *theorem* of standard hierarchical VI and the reduction is not exhibited.

I also grant the narrow factual content of red's C2 and C3 quotations: in parametric empirical Bayes (Carlin–Louis §5.4) the data-driven object is a hyperparameter estimate $\hat\eta(y)$ under a *fixed* generative model, and in Sønderby et al. 2016 §3 the level-$\ell$ generative density is a Gaussian $\mathcal{N}(z_\ell;\,\mu_\theta(z_{\ell+1}),\,\sigma^2_\theta(z_{\ell+1})I)$ parameterized by $\theta$. These literature objects are not pointwise identical to the cross-scale shadow.

I do *not* concede that any of these facts entails the conclusion red draws from them — namely that the construction is an *undeclared* substitution or that it falls outside the deductive closure of recognizable hierarchical-Bayesian objects. That step is where red's argument breaks.

## Core attack

Red's C4 is the load-bearing strike and it is self-falsifying on red's own evidence. Re-read the claim under debate, verbatim from `00_claim.md` line 16: the construction is "a *legitimate refinement* … rather than an *undeclared substitution* of the level-$\ell$ prior $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ in the standard generative model with the level-$(\ell{+}1)$ posterior $q(s_{\ell+1})$." The word that the literature-as-source-of-truth test must turn on is *undeclared*. Red's own C4 evidence is line 546, which red itself quotes in full: the manuscript explicitly states that the standard scheme uses $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$, that the present construction differs from it, and that the difference is a structural commitment of this framework. Whatever else line 546 is, it is *the declaration*. The "undeclared substitution" half of red's reading is contradicted by the very passage red cites against the claim. Red's argument therefore needs a different attack vector — namely that the *declared* commitment falls outside the deductive closure of recognizable hierarchical-Bayesian constructions — and red supplies no primary-source citation that closes that closure off.

Red's C1 collapses under the same standard red invokes. Red quotes [Friston, FitzGerald, Rigoli, Schwartenbeck, Pezzulo 2017, *Active Inference: A Process Theory*, *Neural Comput.* 29:1–49, §"Generative models and (deep) temporal models"] as defining the hierarchical density via the *posited* factorization
$$p(\tilde o, \tilde s) = p(s_L)\prod_\ell p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})\,p(o \mid s_1).$$
Friston 2017 does not *derive* this factorization from any deeper variational principle. It *posits* it as a modeling commitment about the world's deep temporal structure. Parr, Pezzulo & Friston 2022 Ch. 8 (which red also cites) does the same — the hierarchical generative model is a modeling commitment, not a theorem of variational inference. Red's literature-as-source-of-truth standard, applied symmetrically, treats line 546 (the manuscript's structural commitment) and Friston 2017's §"Generative models and deep temporal models" (its posited factorization) as the *same kind of move* at different points in the design space. Red's standard cannot accept Friston 2017's posit as legitimate while rejecting the manuscript's posit as illegitimate without invoking a distinction red has not stated and cannot derive.

Red's C2 attack on empirical Bayes is undefended against the non-parametric branch of the family. Red cites parametric EB (Carlin–Louis §5.4–5.5) where a hyperparameter is point-estimated. The empirical-Bayes family is wider than this. Robbins's original non-parametric empirical Bayes [Robbins 1956, *Proc. Berkeley Symp.*] estimates the *full prior distribution* $G(\theta)$ from the data marginals, not a hyperparameter — confirmed in the standard EB reference literature ([Efron 2010, *Large-Scale Inference*, Ch. 1], which red itself listed in the evidence pack at line 38). The cross-scale shadow generalizes Robbins's move along one axis: replace the prior with an estimate informed by an inferential subsystem rather than the raw data marginal. The mathematical *family* (use inferential output to shape the prior) is the EB family; the family choice (which inferential output) is the manuscript's modeling commitment. Red's "EB requires a fixed generative model with marginal-MLE" reads parametric EB as if it were the whole family. It is not.

Red's C3 attack on the ladder-VAE analogue ignores that red's own quoted equation establishes the family membership. Red quotes [Sønderby, Raiko, Maaløe, Sønderby, Winther 2016, *Ladder Variational Autoencoders*, *NeurIPS* 2016, §3]: the level-$\ell$ generative prior is $\mathcal{N}\!\big(z_\ell;\, \mu_\theta(z_{\ell+1}),\, \sigma^2_\theta(z_{\ell+1})I\big)$. The structural slot occupied by $\mu_\theta(\cdot)$ — a *deterministic function of the next-level latent* — is exactly the slot the gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I}[\cdot]$ occupies in the cross-scale shadow. Sønderby chooses $\mu_\theta$ as a learned $\theta$-parameterized map; the manuscript chooses $\Omega_{i,I}$ as a gauge-transport map. Both are deterministic functions from $z_{\ell+1}$ (resp. $q_I^{(s+1)}$) to a level-$\ell$ prior parameter. Red is correct that the manuscript identifies the *full* prior with the transported posterior rather than wrapping the transport inside a Gaussian conditional — but this is a *narrower* construction within the same family (a degenerate Gaussian whose mean and variance are read off the parent posterior), not a different family. Red's "this is not the construction in Sønderby" treats family membership as if it required pointwise functional identity. That is not what the family relation is.

## Defense

The defensible reading of the claim is the one set out in `00_claim.md` Blue reading: the cross-scale shadow is a *labeled* structural commitment — labeled at line 546 — that triangulates three recognizable hierarchical-Bayesian literature anchors. (i) The non-parametric empirical-Bayes family ([Robbins 1956]; [Efron 2010 §1.1]): inferential output, not marginal MLE, shapes the level-$\ell$ prior. (ii) The ladder-VAE generative prior family ([Sønderby et al. 2016 §3], the equation red itself quotes): the level-$\ell$ prior is a deterministic function of the level-$(\ell{+}1)$ latent, with the manuscript replacing $\mu_\theta(\cdot)$ by $\Omega_{i,I}[\cdot]$. (iii) Variational EM with parameter learning ([Bishop 2006, *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning*, §10.2]; [Wainwright & Jordan 2008, §4 — as cited in red's C5]): the "generative model" can carry parameters that are themselves updated as inference proceeds; the coupled fixed-point structure is the standard variational-EM object, not a violation of the variational principle. None of these three is by itself a derivation of the cross-scale shadow. Together they place the construction inside the deductive closure of recognizable hierarchical-Bayesian objects and discharge red's "parallel construction" framing.

The Ouroboros identification at line 2216, which red attempts to confine to "weighting structure," does more than red allows. The manuscript identifies the construction with four standard hierarchical-Bayesian objects simultaneously — West–Harrison per-step discount [West–Harrison 1997 Ch. 2 §"Discount factors"], Genest–Zidek log-linear opinion pool [Genest–Zidek 1986 §3], Hinton product-of-experts [Hinton 2002 §2], and Bissiri–Holmes–Walker tempered Bayes [Bissiri–Holmes–Walker 2016 §2]. Red is correct that none of these four *substitutes* a posterior for a prior. They do something different but adjacent: they establish that the *resulting* tempered-pool free-energy functional is a recognizable object in the hierarchical-Bayesian literature. Combined with the EB / ladder-VAE / variational-EM anchoring of the upstream substitution, the construction is recognizable as a hierarchical-Bayesian object at both the prior-formation step *and* the pooling-of-priors step. That is what *refinement consistent with the literature* means under a literature-as-source-of-truth standard — recognizability of each move in the literature, not a single end-to-end theorem from a single source.

Red's C5 (no proper joint generative model under fixed generative parameters) is the one strike I cannot fully neutralize on the primary-source materials I have in hand. The reading of [Wainwright–Jordan 2008 §3.4] that red supplies — variational inference fixes $p$ and optimizes $q$ — is correct as stated for the *plain* variational setup. The relevant counter-anchor is variational EM ([Bishop 2006 §10.2]; the corresponding §4 / §5 chapters of Wainwright–Jordan on variational methods with parameter learning, which I rely on red's own citation in C5 to invoke rather than quoting first-hand). In that family, the parameters of the model are updated as inference proceeds, and the resulting object is a coupled fixed-point system that is *still* a standard variational object. The cross-scale shadow is the same construction with the "parameters" identified as the level-$(\ell{+}1)$ posterior parameters of a coupled inferential subsystem. I cannot, on the primary-source materials available in this rebuttal window, exhibit a literature passage that makes this identification *explicit* for the coupled-subsystem case. The strongest honest defense available is the family-membership argument above; the strongest honest concession is that this is the point at which red's strike survives best.

## Falsification conditions (carried over)

The claim is falsifiable on the conditions set out in `02_blue_opening.md` (which I have not read for this rebuttal, per the dispatch).
