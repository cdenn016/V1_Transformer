# Blue Opening — cross-scale-shadow-legitimacy

## Steelman (opposing position)

The cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ replaces the standard hierarchical-VI / hierarchical-AIF generative-model conditional $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ — a fixed object of a stipulated joint $p(o, s_1, \dots, s_L)$ — with the level-$(\ell{+}1)$ variational posterior, and the manuscript admits at line 546 that no reduction or approximation chain from the standard scheme to the shadow construction is displayed; therefore the manuscript is silently working with a different probabilistic model and the "ELBO" it later writes is not the Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 ELBO.

## Position

The cross-scale shadow is a *legitimate refinement* of standard hierarchical variational inference, not an undeclared model substitution. It sits inside three independently-grounded primary-literature constructions — empirical Bayes (Robbins 1956; Carlin–Louis Ch. 5; Efron 2010 §1.1), the ladder-VAE deterministic-prior architecture (Sønderby et al. 2016 §3), and Genest–Zidek log-linear opinion pooling / Hinton product-of-experts (Genest–Zidek 1986; Hinton 2002) — and the manuscript's own labeling at line 546 is honest scholarship that *names* the structural-commitment status of the move rather than concealing it. The standard hierarchical-AIF construction itself is posited as a structural commitment in Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 Ch. 8 (the hierarchical generative model is *assumed*, not derived); the manuscript's construction is doing analogous work at a different point in the architecture.

## Evidence

**C1 — Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 are themselves structural commitments, not theorems.** The deep hierarchical generative model $p(o, s_1, \dots, s_L) = \prod_\ell p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1}) \cdot p(o \mid s_1)$ in [Friston2017 §"Hierarchical generative models"] and [ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 8] is *assumed*, not derived from a more primitive principle. The relevant passage in Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 §8 introduces the deep model with language like "we assume" / "we let the generative model factorize as." The structural-commitment status is therefore shared between the standard scheme and the manuscript. Manuscript line 546 acknowledges the departure point and labels it; this is the same kind of move the standard literature itself makes at its own structural-commitment level. Departure from the standard form, when explicitly declared and accompanied by independent identification with other standard objects (which the manuscript supplies, see C2, C3, C6), is refinement, not substitution.

**C2 — The shadow construction is in the empirical-Bayes family.** Robbins 1956 "An Empirical Bayes Approach to Statistics" [Robbins1956, Proc. Berkeley Symp.] establishes the foundational move: replace the unknown hyperprior by an estimate derived from data (the prior is *not* a primitive of the model but is inherited from a posterior of a higher-level inference). The Wikipedia-summarized canonical form gives the EB structure as the substitution $p(\theta \mid y) \simeq p(y \mid \theta) p(\theta \mid \eta^*) / p(y \mid \eta^*)$ with $\eta^*$ a point estimate of the hyperparameter from data [Carlin–Louis, *Bayesian Methods for Data Analysis* 3rd ed. Ch. 5; Efron 2010 §1.1]. The manuscript's $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is the *distributional* analog of this move within an interacting-agent hierarchy: the level-$(\ell{+}1)$ posterior $q_I^{(s+1)}$ plays the role of the EB hyperparameter estimate $\eta^*$, and the gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ is the frame change needed when constituent $i$ and meta-agent $I$ use different internal coordinates. Crucially, this is *distributional* EB rather than point-EB — closer to "parametric empirical Bayes" / "hierarchical empirical Bayes" in the modern treatment [Carlin–Louis Ch. 5, §5.1–5.4] than to Robbins' original point-estimate form. The manuscript-family slot is therefore precedented; the gauge transport is the new ingredient on top of an established structural pattern.

**C3 — The shadow is structurally analogous to ladder-VAE prior assignment.** Sønderby, Raiko, Maaløe, Sønderby, Winther [Sonderby2016, *NeurIPS* 2016, §3 "The Ladder Variational Autoencoder"] specify the hierarchical generative prior at each ladder layer as a *deterministic-network function* of the next-level latent: $p_\theta(z_\ell \mid z_{\ell+1}) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_{p,\ell}(z_{\ell+1}), \sigma^2_{p,\ell}(z_{\ell+1}))$ where $\mu_{p,\ell}$ and $\sigma^2_{p,\ell}$ are MLPs of $z_{\ell+1}$ (Sønderby et al. 2016, Eqs. 2–4). The level-$\ell$ prior is shaped by the next-level latent through a learned deterministic map. The manuscript's construction replaces the learned MLP $\mu_{p,\ell}(\cdot)$ with the gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I}[\cdot]$ — a principled, group-equivariant, non-learned deterministic map. This is a *constraint-respecting refinement* of the ladder-VAE prior architecture: the structural slot (deterministic map from next-level latent to current-level prior) is preserved; the contents (gauge-covariant transport vs. learned MLP) are upgraded to respect the principal-bundle structure of Section~\ref{sec:agents_as_sections}. Both Sønderby and the manuscript use a deterministic function of the higher-level latent to define the lower-level prior; in the Sønderby case the "level-$(\ell{+}1)$ latent" is a *sample* and in the manuscript case it is the full posterior $q_I^{(s+1)}$ (which generalizes the sample-based form to the distribution-valued form), but the structural pattern is the same.

**C4 — Line 546 is honest scholarship, not disqualification.** The line 546 admission ("we do not display the reduction (or approximation) of the standard hierarchical scheme to the present cross-scale shadow construction") is a labeling of the construction's epistemic status — it locates the construction in the same structural-commitment slot that Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 occupy when they posit their deep hierarchical generative model. The literature-as-source-of-truth standard does not require every construction to be derived from a literature theorem; it requires constructions to be *consistent with* the literature where they intersect [debate_methodology.md, §"The source-of-truth rule"]. The manuscript's construction intersects the literature at three independently-identifiable points (EB at C2, ladder VAE at C3, log-linear opinion pooling at C6), and the manuscript performs the identification work in each case explicitly (line 2216 for the Ouroboros pooling identification; the EB and ladder-VAE structural-analogy identifications are implicit in the construction itself and have been made explicit here). The line 546 admission therefore tells the reader: "this is a structural commitment, here is where it sits relative to the standard scheme, and the displayed reduction is left as open mathematical work." That is honest scholarship; it is the *opposite* of an undeclared substitution.

**C5 — The resulting joint is well-defined as a variational fixed-point system.** The manuscript's construction defines an implicit joint via the coupled fixed-point dynamics of the message-passing variational inference scheme — the standard form for variational EM with coupled latents [Bishop, *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning*, 2006, §10.2 "Variational EM"; Wainwright & Jordan, *Graphical Models, Exponential Families and Variational Inference*, 2008, §3.4 "Variational Characterization of Belief Propagation"]. The joint distribution $p(\{q_i\}_i, \{s_i\}_i)$ does not need to be exhibited in closed form — it is *implicit*, given by the stationary distribution of the message-passing dynamics. This is the standard form for loopy belief propagation [Pearl 1988, *Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems*, Ch. 4 "Belief Propagation"; Bishop 2006 §8.4 "Sum-Product Algorithm"]. There is no circularity: the prior $p_i^{(s)}$ at the lower scale is a function of the posterior $q_I^{(s+1)}$ at the higher scale, but the higher-scale posterior is computed in *its own* E-step against *its own* prior $p_I^{(s+1)} = \Omega_{I,J}[q_J^{(s+2)}]$ from yet another scale, terminating at the boundary $s = s_{\max}$ where $r_i^{(s_{\max})}$ is a fixed boundary condition (manuscript line 548). The boundary condition resolves what would otherwise be a circular reference into a properly-grounded recursion analogous to the boundary conditions of any iterative variational scheme.

**C6 — The Ouroboros line 2216 identification is the most rigorous literature-match the manuscript supplies.** At line 2216 the manuscript performs the explicit identification work: the geometric weighting $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$ matches the West–Harrison [WestHarrison1997, *Bayesian Forecasting and Dynamic Models*, 2nd ed., per-step discount in the dynamic linear model] discount; the additive KL contributions $\sum_k \lambda_k \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| p_k)$ correspond under the standard additive-KL-to-product-prior equivalence [Friston2010; Blei–Kucukelbir–McAuliffe 2017 *J. Am. Stat. Assoc.* "Variational Inference: A Review for Statisticians"] to a Gaussian log-linear pool / product-of-experts prior [GenestZidek1986 *Stat. Sci.* "Combining probability distributions"; HintonPoE2002 *Neural Comput.* 14:1771 "Training products of experts by minimizing contrastive divergence"]; the strictly-normalized special case $\lambda_0 = 1 - \rho$ recovers the externally-Bayesian log-linear pool of Genest–Zidek; the unrestricted default $\lambda_0, \rho > 0$ is a tempered free energy in the generalized-Bayesian sense of [BissiriHolmesWalker2016, *JRSS-B* 78:1103 "A general framework for updating belief distributions"]. Four independent primary-source anchors for one construction. This is the manuscript actively doing the literature-grounding work that line 546 punts on for the more general cross-scale shadow form, and the four anchors are mutually-reinforcing rather than mutually-redundant: West–Harrison supplies the discount semantics, Genest–Zidek supplies the pooling semantics under the normalized constraint, Hinton supplies the product-of-experts semantics for the additive-KL form, Bissiri–Holmes–Walker supplies the tempered-Bayes legitimacy of dropping the normalization constraint. The cross-scale shadow inherits the legitimacy of all four through the Ouroboros special case.

**Strongest single defense.** The literature-as-source-of-truth standard, as articulated in debate_methodology.md §"The source-of-truth rule," requires constructions to be evaluated *against* the standard treatments — not derived *from* them. The manuscript's construction passes this evaluation: it is identified with the empirical-Bayes family (C2), the ladder-VAE deterministic-prior family (C3), the variational message-passing implicit-joint family (C5), and the Genest–Zidek / Hinton / West–Harrison / Bissiri–Holmes–Walker pooling family (C6); and the standard hierarchical-AIF scheme itself is a structural commitment of the same epistemic status (C1). The line 546 admission (C4) is the manuscript locating itself honestly within this landscape rather than concealing its structural-commitment status. The construction is in continuity with the canonical literature; it is a refinement, not a substitution.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **The empirical-Bayes analogy fails structurally.** If Robbins 1956 / Carlin–Louis Ch. 5 / Efron 2010 §1.1 specifies a *point-estimate* of the hyperparameter rather than a distribution-valued substitution, and the distributional generalization to "posterior of higher level as prior of lower level" is *not* present in the modern parametric-empirical-Bayes literature [Carlin–Louis §5.1–5.4], then the EB analogy at C2 is too weak to legitimize the manuscript's distributional shadow. Red must cite Carlin–Louis or Efron explicitly to establish this.

2. **The ladder-VAE analogy collapses.** If Sønderby et al. 2016 §3 (Eqs 2–4) does *not* specify the prior $p_\theta(z_\ell \mid z_{\ell+1})$ as a deterministic function of the next-level latent — i.e., if the ladder-VAE prior is in fact a sampled / stochastic conditional rather than a deterministic transform — then C3 collapses. Red must read Sønderby §3 and quote the prior form to refute the analogy.

3. **The implicit-joint argument fails.** If the cross-scale shadow construction violates Kolmogorov consistency or fails to define a normalizable joint distribution under the boundary condition $r_i^{(s_{\max})}$ — i.e., if there exists a configuration of the message-passing fixed-point dynamics for which the implicit joint is not a valid probability distribution — then C5 collapses. Red must exhibit such a configuration explicitly or cite Wainwright–Jordan §3.4 with a counter-condition.

4. **The Ouroboros pooling identification is bogus.** If the additive-KL-to-product-prior correspondence at line 2216 fails because the gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ destroys the log-linear pooling structure (e.g., if $\Omega$ is not a positive-definite linear operator on the Gaussian belief manifold), then C6 collapses. Red must show the failure mode of the pooling identification with reference to Genest–Zidek 1986 (external Bayesianity condition) or Hinton 2002 (PoE normalization).

5. **The standard hierarchical scheme is in fact a theorem rather than a structural commitment.** If Friston 2017 §4 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 Ch. 8 in fact *derive* the hierarchical generative model factorization $\prod_\ell p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ from a more primitive principle (e.g., a maximum-entropy argument or a Markov-blanket NESS argument), then C1 collapses and the standard scheme is on a different epistemic footing than the manuscript's structural commitment. Red must cite the specific Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 derivation to establish this.

6. **The reduction is provably impossible.** If there is a no-go theorem in the variational-inference literature showing that "transported posterior as prior" cannot be derived as a limit or approximation of "generative-model conditional" under any consistent set of assumptions, then the manuscript's open-problem framing at line 546 is unrecoverable. Red must cite the no-go result with a specific reference.

Absent at least one of these falsifications cited from the external primary literature, the cross-scale shadow construction stands as a legitimate refinement of standard hierarchical variational inference: it is in the empirical-Bayes structural family, it is a gauge-covariant upgrade of the ladder-VAE deterministic-prior pattern, it admits a well-defined implicit joint via variational message passing, and it inherits four independent primary-source anchors through the Ouroboros pooling identification.
