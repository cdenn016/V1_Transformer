# Verdict — cross-scale-shadow-legitimacy

## Outcome

RED_WINS

## Decisive evidence

Two converging primary-source facts, paired with blue's own rebuttal concession on C5, decide the verdict.

1. **Type-signature distinction (Sønderby et al. 2016 §3 vs Eq.~\ref{eq:cross_scale_shadow}).** Both teams quote the ladder-VAE generative prior in the same form: $p_\theta(z_\ell \mid z_{\ell+1}) = \mathcal{N}(z_\ell;\, \mu_\theta(z_{\ell+1}),\, \sigma^2_\theta(z_{\ell+1}) I)$ — a Gaussian density on $z_\ell$ whose parameters are deterministic MLP functions of the next-level latent *sample* $z_{\ell+1}$ ([Sønderby et al. 2016, *NeurIPS*, §3], `02_red_opening.md` C3 and `02_blue_opening.md` C3 quote identical equation forms). The manuscript's $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ identifies the level-$s$ prior with the *transported variational posterior* on the level-$(s{+}1)$ latent. The input type and output type both differ: Sønderby maps a sample $z_{\ell+1}$ to a density on $z_\ell$; the manuscript maps a full distribution $q_I^{(s+1)}$ on the level-$(s{+}1)$ latent to a distribution that is identified with the level-$s$ prior. Red's `03_red_rebuttal.md` (lines 53–65) makes the type-signature distinction explicit; blue's `03_blue_rebuttal.md` (lines 20–22) concedes the narrow factual content of the Sønderby quotation and defends only via family-membership argument that "$\mu_\theta(\cdot)$ and $\Omega_{i,I}[\cdot]$ are both deterministic functions of the next-level latent" — which absorbs neither the sample-vs-distribution input asymmetry nor the on-$z_\ell$-vs-on-$z_{\ell+1}$ output asymmetry.

2. **Blue's C5 concession (`03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 28–30).** Blue states verbatim: "Red's C5 (no proper joint generative model under fixed generative parameters) is the one strike I cannot fully neutralize on the primary-source materials I have in hand. … I cannot, on the primary-source materials available in this rebuttal window, exhibit a literature passage that makes this identification *explicit* for the coupled-subsystem case." Red's C5 citation to [Wainwright & Jordan 2008, *Graphical Models, Exponential Families, and Variational Inference*, §3.4] — the variational principle presumes a fixed underlying joint whose marginals the variational dynamics target — is therefore unrebutted on the primary literature.

## Reasoning

The user's directive treats the external literature as source of truth and asks whether the cross-scale shadow is a *legitimate refinement* of standard hierarchical VI or an *undeclared model substitution*. Six sub-claims structure the adjudication; on five of six the evidence tilts toward red, and on the sixth (C4) the disagreement reduces to a vocabulary point that does not carry the claim for blue.

C1 (Friston 2017 / Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 use a conditional $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$, not a transported posterior). Both teams agree the standard scheme posits a hierarchical generative model whose level-$\ell$ factor is a conditional density. The canon file `external_canon_inference.md` §3 confirms the standard form: "Cross-level couplings come from the generative model $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$." Red shows the type signature differs; blue's symmetric-posit defense in `03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 15–17 establishes only that *both* constructions are posited, not that they posit *the same kind of object*. Posit-equivalence is not type-equivalence. C1 favors red.

C2 (empirical Bayes). Red's C2 attack (`02_red_opening.md` lines 30–36; reinforced in `03_red_rebuttal.md` lines 22–41) shows the EB substitution operates on a *hyperparameter* of a *fixed* generative model, with the estimator computed from *data marginals*. Blue's strongest defense in `03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 18–19 invokes non-parametric Robbins: "Robbins's original non-parametric empirical Bayes estimates the *full prior distribution* $G(\theta)$ from the data marginals, not a hyperparameter." Even granting blue this broader reading, the manuscript's construction substitutes the prior with a *posterior of a coupled inferential subsystem* (whose own prior depends on yet another subsystem's posterior, terminating at the boundary at line 548). Robbins's $\hat G(\theta)$ is computed from observable data; the cross-scale shadow's $q_I^{(s+1)}$ is computed from a coupled variational fixed-point system whose dynamics are reciprocally bound to the prior it is being identified with. This is a mutual-fixed-point coupling, not an estimator-from-data move. C2 favors red.

C3 (ladder VAE). Decided by the type-signature distinction documented in the Decisive evidence section.

C4 (manuscript line 546 admission). Blue is correct (`02_blue_opening.md` C4; `03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 12–13) that the construction is *declared*, so the word "undeclared" in the claim is contradicted by the line-546 disclosure. But the claim being adjudicated is the disjunction "legitimate refinement … *rather than* an undeclared substitution." Honest labeling does not convert a substitution into a refinement; it converts an undeclared substitution into a *declared* substitution. The remaining question — whether the declared object falls within the deductive closure of recognizable hierarchical-Bayesian objects — is settled by C1, C2, C3, C5, and C6. C4 is partially conceded by red (`03_red_rebuttal.md` lines 5–14) and does not carry the claim for blue alone.

C5 (joint distribution). Decided by blue's explicit concession quoted in the Decisive evidence section. Variational message passing in [Pearl 1988 Ch. 4], [Bishop 2006 §8.4], and [Wainwright & Jordan 2008 §3.4] computes marginals of a *fixed* underlying factor graph. The cross-scale shadow has no fixed factor graph — the factors at level $\ell$ are equated to the variational posteriors at level $\ell{+}1$, which are themselves outputs of an optimization whose factors are equated to level-$\ell{+}2$ posteriors. Red's reading (`03_red_rebuttal.md` lines 66–87) that "implicit joint" presupposes a fixed underlying model is faithful to the canonical literature. Blue offers variational EM ([Bishop 2006 §10.2]) as a counter-anchor but concedes the literature passage making this identification explicit for the *coupled-subsystem* case is not in hand.

C6 (Ouroboros four-anchor identification at line 2216). Both teams agree the four anchors — [West & Harrison 1997] discount factors, [Genest & Zidek 1986] log-linear pool, [Hinton 2002] product-of-experts, [Bissiri, Holmes & Walker 2016] tempered Bayes — operate on *given* component distributions to produce a pooled or tempered prior/posterior. Blue's `03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 26–27 grants: "Red is correct that none of these four *substitutes* a posterior for a prior. They do something different but adjacent." The four-anchor identification covers the downstream weighting and pooling of priors; it does not legitimize the upstream substitution at Eq.~\ref{eq:cross_scale_shadow}. C6 favors red on the substitution question; blue's pooling-form defense is genuine but addresses a different structural feature.

Combining the six sub-claims: the cross-scale shadow is a *declared* structural commitment (C4 blue) that produces an object of a different mathematical type than the standard hierarchical-VI literature (C1, C3 red) via a different substitution pattern than parametric or non-parametric empirical Bayes (C2 red), without a fixed factor-graph underlying joint of the kind variational message passing presupposes (C5 red, conceded by blue), with the four-anchor downstream identification at line 2216 covering pooling but not substitution (C6, conceded by blue). The honest labeling at line 546 makes this a *declared* substitution rather than an undeclared one; the type-signature, joint-distribution, and substitution-form evidence makes it a substitution rather than a refinement. The claim under debate — "legitimate refinement of standard hierarchical VI rather than undeclared substitution" — fails on the refinement half even after the declaration half is granted.

## Action

Recommended manuscript edit at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` line 546 and at line 2216: replace the implicit framing of the cross-scale shadow as a refinement of hierarchical VI with the explicit framing as a *separate, declared framework* in continuity with the empirical-Bayes, ladder-VAE, and log-linear-pool families at downstream pooling steps but distinct from each at the upstream substitution step. Three concrete steps follow.

First, at line 546, retain the structural-commitment admission but add a sentence locating the construction outside the deductive closure of [Friston 2017, *Neural Comput.* 29:1–49] / [Parr, Pezzulo, Friston 2022, *Active Inference*, MIT Press, Ch. 8] at the type-signature level: the standard scheme's $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ is a conditional density of a fixed joint, while $\Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ is a transported variational posterior. The two are not the same mathematical type, even up to gauge transformation.

Second, where the manuscript invokes the FEP citations at line 1015 and line 1023, sharpen the disclaimer further: the cross-scale shadow's ELBO is an ELBO of a *coupled variational fixed-point system*, not of any hierarchical generative model in the [Friston 2017] or [Parr–Pezzulo–Friston 2022 Ch. 8] sense; the FEP citations remain valid as the variational-principle backbone but do not legitimize the upstream substitution at Eq.~\ref{eq:cross_scale_shadow}.

Third, at line 2216, retain the four-anchor identification of the Ouroboros tower's pooling/weighting structure but add the corresponding scope statement: the four anchors legitimize the additive-discounted-KL pooling form *given* the cross-scale shadow substitution, not the substitution itself. The substitution is a separate structural commitment that, on the present primary-source survey, does not have a single end-to-end primary-source anchor — and an honest manuscript labels this gap.

If the user wishes the construction to be re-framed as a refinement rather than a separate framework, the obligation that follows from this verdict is to exhibit either (a) a reduction or approximation chain from $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$ to $\Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ under stated limits, derivable from a primary source, or (b) a primary-source treatment of variational message passing with *coupled-subsystem* prior formation that establishes the implicit joint as a well-defined probability distribution. Without one of these, the manuscript's most defensible posture is the parallel-framework framing.
