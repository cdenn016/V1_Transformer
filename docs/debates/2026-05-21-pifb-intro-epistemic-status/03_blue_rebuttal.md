# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-intro-epistemic-status

## Concession

Red's attack A on the §sec:worked_signature postulate inventory lands a partial hit. §sec:worked_signature itself (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2735, 2774, 2783`) runs an explicit postulate-naming pass that surfaces three further restrictions — (i) Regime I / $F_{\mu\nu} \equiv 0$ pure-gauge regime, (ii) the $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ vs $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ sign convention on $\mathfrak{g}$, and (iii) the separable ansatz $\psi_\tau = \psi_\tau(\tau), \psi_x = \psi_x(x)$ — that the §1.7 enumeration at line 142 ("a 2D linearized toy model with a constant generator and an imaginary temporal gauge component plus a real-part projection as postulates") does not name. The §1.7 entry names three of the six body-flagged postulates and omits three.

I grant that this is asymmetric reporting at the level of the postulate count. I do not grant that it falsifies conjunct (ii) of the claim, because §1.7 explicitly routes the reader to §sec:signature_resolution with the section reference embedded, and conjunct (ii) asserts that the §1.7 framing is "correctly bounded given §sec:worked_signature's content" — the framing is "Worked example only," which is the epistemic bucket. Whether the bucket holds three postulates or six, the bucket itself ("worked example, not derivation, all postulates explicitly stated in the cited section") is correctly assigned. The §1.7 enumeration is summary prose, not an inventory; the inventory lives where §sec:worked_signature itself runs it. Conjunct (ii) requires correct bucketing, not exhaustive postulate enumeration in the summary.

## Core attack

Red's strongest move — attack C, the §1.6 Level 1 mislabeling charge — fails on the canonical text it relies on. Red cites Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1 as the standard for "what 'standard scaled dot-product attention' means" and asserts that the PIFB sweep does not validate that object because the sweep trains the general gauge model rather than the trivial-frame reduction. Two problems with this attack.

First, Vaswani §3.2.1 itself defines scaled dot-product attention as $\mathrm{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \mathrm{softmax}(QK^\top / \sqrt{d_k}) V$ on raw $Q, K, V$ — the learned linear projections $W_Q^i, W_K^i, W_V^i$ belong to §3.2.2 (Multi-Head Attention), where they wrap §3.2.1 as outer learned linear maps. [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] is therefore the inner kernel; [Vaswani2017 §3.2.2] supplies the learned bilinear similarity via the composition $Q = X W_Q$, $K = Y W_K$, so $QK^\top = X W_Q W_K^\top Y^\top = X M Y^\top$ with $M = W_Q W_K^\top$. The §1.6 Level 1 disclosure that the recovery is "up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility $M$" exactly maps to the §3.2.1 / §3.2.2 split: the §3.2.1 kernel is recovered by the gauge-theoretic construction, and the §3.2.2 learned bilinear is the disclosed qualifier. Red's framing that the §1.6 Level 1 statement "mislabels" by separating these two is the opposite of what the canon supports — the canon itself separates them.

Second, the §sec:transformers passage red cites (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1609`: "The empirical experiments in Section~\ref{sec:scaling_validation} implement the general gauge model without these limits") is a statement that the trained model is the broader gauge-covariant KL-attention. The §1.6 Level 1 prose reads (line 125): "Standard scaled dot-product attention is recovered as a zero-dimensional isotropic-Gaussian limit of the KL-consensus construction up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility $M$ and the standard normalization and bias assumptions (Section~\ref{sec:transformers}). The empirical support is a multi-seed embedding-dimension sweep on WikiText-103." The two claims in Level 1 are (a) the analytic recovery in §sec:transformers, and (b) the empirical sweep on WikiText-103 — not (c) "the sweep validates the standard-attention reduction in its trivial-frame form." Red is reading (c) into the text. The text instead places the analytic recovery and the empirical sweep adjacently, with the sweep validating that the broader construction containing the trivial-frame reduction as a sub-case is implementable, trainable, and exhibits a clean scaling law. That is the honest reading: an analytic limit is mathematically derived, and the larger construction containing it is empirically probed. Conjunct (i) does not require the trivial-frame limit itself to be empirically probed — it requires the placement at Level 1 to be correct under the disclosed qualifiers. "Up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility $M$ and the standard normalization and bias assumptions" is the explicit qualifier, and it covers the §3.2.2-wrapper that is what bridges the §3.2.1 kernel to the broader trained construction.

Red's attack B, the consensus-metric regulator gap, also misfires on the structure of §1.7. §sec:consensus_metric (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2880`) places the consensus metric inside the speculative-extensions program (§sec:speculative_extensions starts at line 2531: "Readers seeking the empirically anchored content of the framework should read this section as Outlook rather than as Results"). Everything past line 2531 is by §sec:speculative_extensions' own framing not in-scope for the "Implemented and Empirically Probed" or "Working Code" tiers; it is part of the Level 3 / "Worked example only" / "No quantitative physics predictions" content. §1.7's entry "No quantitative physics predictions" (line 144: "The framework does not currently yield numerical values for physical constants, particle masses, coupling strengths, or dimensionless ratios. Dimensional analysis bridging information-geometric quantities and SI units is unresolved") already covers the status of the consensus metric: it does not yet produce numerical predictions. The regulator gap is a technical incompleteness within an already-Level-3 program, not a load-bearing claim that escapes the §1.7 bucketing. Red's framing that the consensus metric is "the only candidate gauge-invariant geometric structure the framework produces" and is "load-bearing for the 'objective reality' interpretation" is correct as a description of the body, but the body's load-bearing interpretation is itself Level 3 content, and §1.7's Level 3 framing ("Worked example only" plus "No quantitative physics predictions") covers it categorically.

## Defense

The Level 1 disclosure language matches canonical scientific practice for stating reductions to standard architectures. [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1 vs §3.2.2] separates the scaled dot-product kernel from the learned projections that supply the trainable similarity. The PIFB construction does the same separation: §sec:transformers line 1631 develops two complementary routes — a per-token-frame route in §sec:untied_qk that retains $\Omega_{ij} = U_iU_j^{-1}$ non-trivially and "produces untied query-key projections $Q_i = U_i^{-1}\mu_i$, $K_j = U_j^\top\Sigma_j^{-1}\mu_j$ directly from the transported KL", and a trivial-frame route in §sec:dot_product_derivation that introduces "a learned shared bilinear compatibility $M = W_QW_K^\top$ acting on the means". The §1.6 Level 1 disclosure that recovery is "up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility $M$" is technically precise about which canonical object is recovered (the §3.2.1 kernel) and which is added by separate introduction (the §3.2.2 wrapper). Red's reading collapses the §3.2.1 / §3.2.2 distinction in Vaswani that the manuscript itself preserves.

The scaling-fit honesty at $N = 11$ axis points exceeds typical practice. §sec:scaling_validation (line 2520) reports BOTH the bootstrap percentile CI ($b \in [-1.103, -0.998]$, brackets $-1$ at the upper edge) AND the nested $F$-test ($F(1,8) = 9.73, p = 0.014$, rejects $b = -1$ at $\alpha = 0.05$), and writes the disagreement into the published text: "the choice of which framing to emphasize is the choice of which uncertainty quantification one trusts in the finite-$N$ regime with $N = 11$ axis points." [Kaplan2020] and [Hoffmann2022] each report a single criterion and a point estimate; the PIFB text reports two criteria, names the disagreement, and discloses the finite-sample regime. The §1.6 Level 1 disclosure (line 125: "the two finite-sample criteria disagree at $N = 11$ axis points, and we report $b \approx -1.05$ as the point estimate") and the §1.7 entry "Scaling-study scope" (line 154) jointly carry forward the methodological caveat, the absence of a matched vanilla baseline (line 154: "no baseline single-layer vanilla transformer at matched configuration was trained"), and the regime conditionality ("the fitted floor $c \approx 61$ is the model's measured ceiling under the present regime rather than a structural floor"). The within-architecture regime is disclosed both in §1.6 ("The exponent characterizes within-architecture scaling, not competitive language-modeling quality") and in §1.7 line 154 ("does not assert competitive language-modeling quality"). Conjunct (iii) requires honest reporting of the $b \approx -1.05$ result and the bootstrap-vs-$F$-test disagreement against §sec:scaling_validation's full numerics. The §1.6 / §1.7 prose matches the §sec:scaling_validation numerics line-for-line on the four reported quantities ($b$, CI, $F$, $p$) and discloses the disagreement.

The Ouroboros Tower framing at §1.6 Level 2 ("runs the fast subsystem only ($\gamma_{ij}=0$, slow subsystem frozen) on a single seed") is bounded by §1.7's matching entry at line 140: "Demonstrated in working code (single illustrative run) ... conditional on the threshold-based consensus detector of Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_threshold}. The variational free-energy improvement criterion that this detector approximates ... is not itself evaluated continuously in the simulation; whether the discrete detector and the continuous variational rule produce the same hierarchical organization is open." This is honest reporting: a single seed, a threshold-detector approximation of a variational criterion, an open question about whether the discrete and continuous rules agree. Conjunct (iv) requires the framing to be consistent with §sec:participatory's actual deliverables. §sec:participatory line 2509 confirms the framing ("an ablation experiment in which the top-down channel is forcibly suppressed --- which would isolate the empirical contribution of the participatory loop above its structural definition --- is not provided here and is left to future work").

What red's attack A actually establishes is that §1.7's three-postulate enumeration is shorter than §sec:worked_signature's six-postulate inventory. What it does not establish is a falsification of conjunct (ii). Conjunct (ii) reads: "the placement of the Lorentzian-signature construction (§sec:signature_resolution) at Level 3 ('Speculative Physical Interpretation') with the §1.7 'Worked example only' framing is correctly bounded given §sec:worked_signature's content (an imposed imaginary frame component, a real-part projection, and a 2D linearized setup)." The "correctly bounded" predicate is about whether the construction belongs in the Level 3 / Worked-example-only bucket, not about whether the §1.7 summary itemizes every postulate the body section flags. The §sec:worked_signature self-audit at line 2808 — "the construction is a structural existence proof, not a dynamical derivation: the assignment of an imaginary component along $\tau$ in Eq.~(2) is a postulate, and the projection to the real part of $G_{\mu\nu}$ is a further postulate; neither follows from variational free-energy minimization in the present formulation" — is the load-bearing characterization, and §1.7's "Worked example only" bucket correctly carries it.

## Falsification conditions (restated)

The claim is defensible under: the §1.6 / §1.7 prose accurately reports the manuscript's deliverables to the canon-set standard of [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1/§3.2.2] for the attention recovery and [Kaplan2020]/[Hoffmann2022] for scaling-fit disclosure. The claim is not defensible if (a) red shows that §1.7's omission of three §sec:worked_signature postulates is itself a structural mis-bucketing rather than a summary-vs-inventory asymmetry (i.e., the omitted postulates would change the Level 3 placement), (b) red shows that the consensus-metric regulator gap is a stand-alone in-scope claim outside the speculative-extensions program (i.e., the body relies on a finite consensus metric for an empirically anchored result), or (c) red shows that the §1.6 Level 1 prose itself states or implies that the trivial-frame standard-attention reduction is empirically probed by the sweep (i.e., the disclosure "up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility $M$" is read in context as misleading rather than precise). On the canon-strict reading of Vaswani §3.2.1 / §3.2.2, on the structural placement of §sec:consensus_metric inside §sec:speculative_extensions, and on the §1.7 summary-bucket function, none of (a)–(c) is established by red's opening.
