# Red Opening — pifb-discussion-philosophy-of-science

## Steelman (opposing position)

The subsection opens with an explicit Friedman-style relativized-a-priori framing and a hedge ("we register this as a question rather than a position; see Section sec:scope_limitations") at line 3401, which performs the philosophical bracketing the full anti-realist conclusion at 3422 and 3436 would otherwise need to earn — so blue can argue that the local strength of phrases like "literally inhabit different phenomenal realities" (3420) and "rejecting convergent realism" (3436) are downstream consequences of a constitutively-hedged framing, and that the Ladyman & Ross 2007 citation at 3448 is invoked at the narrow level of "epistemic constraints arising from the structure of information geometry," not as an endorsement of their full ontic structural realism.

## Position

The subsection is *not* correctly calibrated. Two specific calibration faults are load-bearing: (R1) the Ladyman & Ross 2007 citation appears in two semantically opposite roles inside the same subsection (as neo-Kantian structural-realism kin at 3401 and as authority for "fundamental epistemic constraints" at 3448), which mis-maps their ontic structural realism onto the framework's anti-realist conclusion at 3422 / 3436; and (R2) the 3420 "literally inhabit different phenomenal realities" formulation is stronger than the cited Hoyningen-Huene 1993 reconstruction supports, and stronger than Kuhn's own "local incommensurability" retraction permits. The 3401 hedge brackets the *positioning* question but does not retroactively soften the categorical claims at 3420 / 3422 / 3436 / 3448, which are stated as substantive conclusions, not as interpretive registrations.

## Evidence

- **Manuscript line 3401** (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3401`) groups Cassirer, Worrall, and Ladyman & Ross under "neo-Kantian structural realism" as the framework's nearest philosophical kin. Worrall 1989 is *epistemic* structural realism; Ladyman & Ross 2007 is *ontic* structural realism. These are distinct positions and the manuscript collapses them.
- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Structural Realism"** (verified via `WebFetch`, 2026-05-20): "Worrall's structural realism is a purely semantic and epistemological theory" while "Ladyman (1998) argues that structural realism ought to be developed as a metaphysical position." Worrall is agnostic about underlying reality; Ladyman & Ross claim "structure actually *is* what exists." Source: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/structural-realism/.
- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Scientific Realism"** (verified via `WebFetch`, 2026-05-20): "Ontic Structural Realism (Ladyman & Ross 2007) — Realist." It maintains "epistemic commitment to mind-independent structural relations described by theories — a core realist position." Constructive empiricism and Longino-style pluralism are classified as anti-realist on the same axis. Source: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/scientific-realism/.
- **Manuscript line 3422**: "not necessarily truth-convergent (we may not approach objective reality, only optimized shared models)" — this is incompatible with Ladyman & Ross's position that mind-independent modal structure is what the theory is converging on. Citing Ladyman 2007 at 3448 for the framework's "fundamental epistemic constraints" then asks an ontic structural realist to underwrite an anti-realist conclusion at 3422 / 3436. The Worrall 1989 citation at 3401 would carry this work; the L&R 2007 citation at 3448 reverses direction.
- **Manuscript line 3420**: "Scientists on opposite sides of a revolution literally inhabit different phenomenal realities despite observing the same noumenal substrate." Cites Hoyningen-Huene 1993 at 3422.
- **Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "The Incommensurability of Scientific Theories"** (verified via `WebFetch`, 2026-05-20): Hoyningen-Huene's reconstruction is a "metaphorical-psychological interpretation of world change," not literal-different-realities; Kuhn himself "insisted that the version of incommensurability he championed had always been 'local incommensurability,' restricting conceptual change to a few, typically interconnected concepts"; and Hoyningen-Huene treats lexicons as "*constitutive* of the objects of knowledge" with "the logical status of a lexical structure... is that of convention" rather than literal world-constitution. The adverb "literally" at 3420 is precisely the inflation the canonical reconstruction was designed to avoid. Source: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/incommensurability/.
- **Hedge scope**: The 3401 hedge ("register this as a question rather than a position") is bracketed to the *positioning* claim — Friedman vs. realism vs. holism. It does not appear at 3420 ("literally inhabit"), 3422 ("not necessarily truth-convergent"), 3436 ("rejecting convergent realism"), or 3448 (L&R cited for an anti-realist conclusion), each of which is delivered as a substantive consequence of the framework, not as an interpretive registration. A single opening hedge does not cover four downstream categorical claims placed three to five paragraphs later under their own subsubsection headings.

1. 2. The Hoyningen-Huene 1993 reconstruction, read directly, *does* contain a passage endorsing the literal-phenomenal-realities reading rather than the local/metaphorical/lexical-constitutive reading that the SEP entry attributes to him.
3. The 3401 sentence "We register this as a question rather than a position" can be shown — by manuscript structure, not by paraphrase — to scope over the conclusions at 3420, 3422, 3436, and 3448 in addition to the local Friedman-positioning claim. A single forward cross-reference to `sec:scope_limitations` is not by itself sufficient; the scope-limitations section would have to explicitly disclaim these four conclusions.

If none of these can be shown, the targeted edits required are: (a) replace the L&R 2007 citation at 3448 with Worrall 1989 (epistemic SR is the position the manuscript's anti-realist conclusion actually requires), or restate the 3422 / 3436 conclusion as compatible with structural realism rather than as rejection of convergent realism; (b) remove "literally" at 3420 or replace with "on the framework's reading"; (c) at 3401, distinguish Worrall's epistemic SR from L&R's ontic SR rather than grouping them.
