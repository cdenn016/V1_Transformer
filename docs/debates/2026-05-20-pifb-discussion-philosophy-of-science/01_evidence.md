## What this evidence does NOT settle

1. Whether the Ladyman & Ross 2007 citation at 3448 is consistent with the framework's anti-realist tendencies. L&R 2007 advance ontic structural realism — they are REALIST about structure (the structure is what's really there). The framework's position at 3410 ("not correspondence with the unknowable noumenal manifold") is closer to van Fraassen 1980 constructive empiricism. Citing L&R for "fundamental epistemic constraints" may be misappropriated — they would say the structure is real, not just an epistemic constraint.

2. Whether the "Scientists on opposite sides of a revolution literally inhabit different phenomenal realities" claim at 3420 is consistent with Hoyningen-Huene 1993's careful Kuhn reconstruction, which is generally taken to soften the incommensurability thesis to "local incommensurability" / "translation difficulty" rather than literal inhabit-different-realities. The "literally" adverb is strong.

3. Whether the framework's anti-realist conclusion at 3422 ("not necessarily truth-convergent") is sufficiently hedged given that the cited canon (van Fraassen, Laudan, Longino, L&R) spans both anti-realist and realist positions.

4. Whether the "Worrall" citation at 3401 should be elaborated. Worrall 1989 advances structural realism (epistemic), which is closer to the framework's position than L&R 2007 ontic structural realism. The manuscript cites both with similar weight.

5. Whether the "Reichenbach" reference at 3401 needs an explicit citation (currently implicit via Friedman).
