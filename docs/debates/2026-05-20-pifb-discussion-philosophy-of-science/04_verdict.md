## Reasoning

The 3401 hedge ("We register this as a question rather than a position; see Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations}") is scoped, by its own grammar, to the Friedman-positioning question of the same paragraph — whether the cognitive-shareability constraint plays a relativized-a-priori role or "remains a notational/transcendental constraint with no falsification surface of its own." Blue argues this hedge brackets the entire subsection through the cross-reference to `sec:scope_limitations`. The textual reading does not support this scope. The four downstream claims at 3420 ("literally inhabit different phenomenal realities"), 3422 ("not necessarily truth-convergent"), 3436 ("rejecting convergent realism"), and 3448 ("claims to access absolute objective truth ... lack foundation") are delivered as substantive conclusions under their own subsubsection headings, not as interpretive registrations.

Blue's strongest move is the structure-real / noumenon-unknowable split: OSR is realist about structure and agnostic-to-eliminativist about ordinary objects, and the framework's anti-realism is restricted to the noumenal manifold, so the L&R citation at 3448 is consistent with OSR on this internal logic. This defense is coherent. The defense fails on a different ground: the subsection does not draw the split. The 3448 sentence reads L&R as authority for an epistemic-constraints claim positioned three sentences before an anti-realist closing ("claims to access absolute objective truth beyond phenomenal appearances lack foundation"), and the 3422 conclusion ("not necessarily truth-convergent... we may not approach objective reality, only optimized shared models") is incompatible with OSR's commitment to mind-independent structural relations in the form it is stated. Either the structure-real / noumenon-unknowable split has to be made explicit in the prose at 3448, or the L&R citation has to be moved to Worrall 1989 ESR which carries the agnosticism the conclusion requires.

On the 3420 "literally" question, blue cites the SEP "Thomas Kuhn" entry's Hoyningen-Huene paraphrase ("the world-in-itself" versus "the 'world' of our perceptual and related experiences") and argues the noumenal-substrate qualifier in the same sentence restricts "literally" to phenomenal-world register. This gives blue primary-source backing for the formulation. Red cites the SEP "Incommensurability" entry directly, which records Kuhn's later retraction to "local incommensurability, restricting conceptual change to a few, typically interconnected concepts" and Hoyningen-Huene's lexicon-as-convention framing — the canonical reconstruction softens incommensurability rather than asserting literal phenomenal-reality inhabitation. Red's citation is more directly on point for the adverb-strength question. The adverb "literally" is still stronger than Hoyningen-Huene's careful register supports, even granting the phenomenal restriction. A weakening to "on the framework's reading" or "in the framework's vocabulary" would land the manuscript inside the cited canon's tolerance band without losing the claim's force.

On the third red attack (3401 lumping), red wins on a direct SEP citation: Worrall ESR and Ladyman & Ross OSR are distinct positions in the structural-realism family, and the manuscript groups them with Cassirer under a single "neo-Kantian structural realism" label held together only by "closer to." Blue's "closer to" defense is partial: the phrase performs neighborhood-not-identification work for the framework's relationship to the group, but does not erase the internal distinction the SEP draws between ESR (agnostic about underlying reality) and OSR (committed to structure as ontologically fundamental). A single parenthetical distinguishing the two positions would discharge the red attack.

The subsection's overall calibration — Friedman positioning at 3401, three-criteria reconception at 3408, gauge-frame-distance formalization of incommensurability at 3420, equal-KL formalization of underdetermination at 3428, Quine / van Fraassen / Longino citations doing the work they claim to do — is sound. The verdict is narrow: three targeted edits land the subsection inside the cited canon's tolerance without restructuring it.
