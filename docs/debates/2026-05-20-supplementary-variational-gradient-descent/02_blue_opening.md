## Position

I restate the claim: §D of `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex` (lines 617–670) presents (α) the canonical Fisher-Rao natural-gradient projections for Gaussian (μ, Σ), (β) the affine-invariant whitening with Frobenius trust region, (γ) the affine-invariant SPD exponential-map retraction, (δ) the closed-form left-trivialised differential `Ψ_L(ad_X)` on so(3), (ε) the trivialization identity relating left- and right-form gauge-frame gradients, and (ζ) a three-mode taxonomy of preconditioners whose derivations live in §C.5.

My thesis: sub-claims α through ε hold under primary-source verification with no substantive defect. Sub-claim ζ holds *as a taxonomy and as a cross-reference*, but the line-665 inline summary equation for mode (iii) is stale relative to the corrected §C.5 form, and the cross-reference "App.~C.3" is a wrong section label. These two defects are forward-reference drift, not derivations introduced in §D. The decisive distinction is that §D.3 does not derive the pullback metric — it lists the preconditioner names and points the reader to §C.5 for the derivation, which is correct post-§C debate. The chapter therefore qualifies as "complete and mathematically pure" in the same sense as the prior narrow-verdict chapters: substantive math derivations are correct; editorial / cross-reference fixes are required at two lines.

## Evidence

**Sub-claim α — Fisher-Rao natural gradient (Eq. 623–626).** The projections `δμ = -Σ ∇_μ` and `δΣ = -2 Σ sym(∇_Σ) Σ` follow directly from the Fisher metric on the Gaussian manifold `g(δΣ_1, δΣ_2) = (1/2) tr(Σ^{-1} δΣ_1 Σ^{-1} δΣ_2)` per the canonical treatment [Amari 2016 §2.3, §4.3] and the explicit derivation in [Sun-Marchand-Reilly 2014 "On the natural gradient"]. Verification: setting `g(2 Σ sym(∇_Σ) Σ, v) = (1/2) tr(Σ^{-1} · 2 Σ sym(∇_Σ) Σ · Σ^{-1} v) = tr(sym(∇_Σ) v) = tr(∇_Σ v)` for symmetric v recovers the Euclidean pairing, so `2 Σ sym(∇_Σ) Σ` is the Fisher-natural representative of `∇_Σ`. The µ-block is `F_µ = Σ^{-1}` so `nat_grad_µ = Σ ∇_µ`. Both signs are descent. The form is also derivable from the Bonnabel / Pennec affine-invariant Riemannian structure on SPD per `[external_canon_math.md §1 "Natural gradient"]`. Eq. (623–626) matches the canonical form line-for-line.

**Sub-claim β — whitening and trust region (Eq. 631).** `B = Λ^{-1/2} V^⊤ δΣ V Λ^{-1/2}` is the matrix representation of `Σ^{-1/2} δΣ Σ^{-1/2}` in the eigenbasis of Σ. This is the canonical "horizontal" tangent at Σ ∈ SPD(K) under the affine-invariant metric [Pennec 2006 "Intrinsic statistics on Riemannian manifolds" §3]; under reparametrization `Σ → A Σ A^⊤`, B is invariant ([Bhatia 2007 "Positive Definite Matrices" §6.1]). The Frobenius-norm clipping on B is a standard trust-region safeguard on matrix-manifold descent [Absil-Mahony-Sepulchre 2008 "Optimization Algorithms on Matrix Manifolds" §7].

**Sub-claim γ — affine-invariant SPD retraction (Eq. 637–639).** The closed-form `exp_Σ_k(τ δΣ) = Σ_k^{1/2} exp(τ Σ_k^{-1/2} δΣ Σ_k^{-1/2}) Σ_k^{1/2}` is the affine-invariant exponential map on SPD per [Pennec 2006 §3], [Bhatia 2007 §6.1, Theorem 6.1.6], and [Absil-Mahony-Sepulchre 2008 §5.4.6 "The set of symmetric positive-definite matrices"]. Direct substitution `Σ_k^{1/2} = V Λ^{1/2} V^⊤` and `Σ_k^{-1/2} δΣ Σ_k^{-1/2} = V B V^⊤` with `B = U Λ_B U^⊤` yields `V Λ^{1/2} U exp(τ Λ_B) U^⊤ Λ^{1/2} V^⊤`, the exact form at line 637. SPD-preservation follows from `exp(τ Λ_B) ≻ 0` componentwise and from the congruence sandwich `V Λ^{1/2} (·) Λ^{1/2} V^⊤` preserving positive-definiteness ([Bhatia 2007 §1.3]).

**Sub-claim δ — SO(3) closed-form `Ψ_L` (Eq. 661).** The left-trivialised differential is `Ψ_L(z) = (1 - e^{-z})/z = Σ_k (-z)^k / (k+1)!`, so `Ψ_L(ad_X) = I - ad_X/2 + ad_X²/6 - ad_X³/24 + ad_X⁴/120 - ...`. On so(3) with `θ = ‖φ‖`, direct computation on the standard generators gives `ad_X³ = -θ² ad_X`, hence `ad_X^{2k+1} = (-θ²)^k ad_X` and `ad_X^{2k+2} = (-θ²)^k ad_X²`. The series in `ad_X` resums to `c_1(θ) = 1/2 - θ²/24 + θ⁴/720 - ... = (1 - cos θ)/θ²` and the series in `ad_X²` resums to `c_2(θ) = 1/6 - θ²/120 + θ⁴/5040 - ... = (θ - sin θ)/θ³`. These are the Rodrigues coefficients ([Hall 2015 "Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations" §2.7]; see also [Iserles-Nørsett-Munthe-Kaas Acta Numerica 2000 §2.3 for the dexp series]). Eq. (661) matches exactly. The Taylor fallback for `θ < 1e-4` is the standard numerical safeguard against the `0/0` removable singularity.

**Sub-claim ε — trivialization relation (line 659).** From the canonical identity `Ad_{exp(X)} = e^{ad_X}` ([Hall 2015 Proposition 2.25; canonical in any matrix Lie-group text]), one obtains `Ad_{exp(-X)} · (e^{ad_X} - I)/ad_X = e^{-ad_X} · (e^{ad_X} - I)/ad_X = (I - e^{-ad_X})/ad_X = Ψ_L(ad_X)`. Equivalently, `Q_a^R · exp(φ) = exp(φ) · Q_a^L`. The sign flip of the `c_1` term between Appendices C and D is the direct consequence of the convention difference (right- vs left-trivialised dexp), and the chapter acknowledges this at line 663. Both forms reproduce the numerical Fréchet derivative under their respective conventions; this is verifiable by direct sympy substitution using `Ψ_R(z) = (e^z - 1)/z` versus `Ψ_L(z) = (1 - e^{-z})/z = e^{-z} Ψ_R(z)`.

**Sub-claim ζ — preconditioner cross-references (line 665).** The three modes are taxonomy items, not new derivations. The derivations live at §C.5 ("Gauge Frame Preconditioning for GL(K)", label `sec:glk_preconditioning`). For mode (i), §C.5 line 590 carries the corrected Cartan-involution-modified inner product `g̃_ab = 2K tr(T_a^⊤ T_b) - 2 tr(T_a) tr(T_b)`, positive-definite on sl(K). For mode (iii), §C.5 line 606 carries the corrected pullback metric `G_ab(φ) = tr(Ψ(ad_φ)(T_a)^⊤ · exp(φ) exp(φ)^⊤ · Ψ(ad_φ)(T_b))` with the `exp(φ) exp(φ)^⊤` factor required for non-compact GL(K). A reader following the §D.3 cross-reference therefore lands on the canonical form. The substantive content of sub-claim ζ — that §D.3 *defers to* §C.5 — is satisfied.

**Pre-emption of the strongest red attack.** Red's strongest move is to argue that the line-665 inline summary equation for mode (iii), reading `G_ab(φ) = ⟨Ψ(ad_X)(T_a), Ψ(ad_X)(T_b)⟩` without the `exp(φ) exp(φ)^⊤` factor, is itself the canonical statement of mode (iii) within §D.3, and therefore §D.3 propagates the same bug that the §C debate adjudicated against the manuscript.
