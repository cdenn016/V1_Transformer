## False dichotomies / equivocations detected

1. **"Circular" vs "not a derivation" is not a genuine dichotomy.** Red's class-(b) circularity (conclusion = premise) is the *mechanism* of non-derivation; "rather than a derivation" is the *label*. They are the same operative claim at two grains. Red's rebuttal treats them as opposed ("names the wrong defect") but blue correctly shows the operative half of the flag ("rather than a derivation") IS red's finding. The only genuine residue is the secondary clause "may not be falsifiable," which is a weaker co-disclosure, not the whole flag. Red's "wrong flag" attack truncates the :2992 sentence to its trailing clause — confirmed against the .tex: :2992 reads "this is a metaphysical interpretation rather than a derivation, and the hypothesis may not be falsifiable." Both phrases are present in the same sentence.

2. **"Lexical vs structural" is the real binary, and it is correctly binary.** Blue's falsification condition 3 makes the test explicit: the residue is structural iff removing the production verbs and SM enumeration *still* leaves an empirical/derivational claim resting on the unfalsifiable interpretation. It does not — the section rests no prediction on the thesis (both readings give identical frame-independent observables, conceded by both sides). So the residue is lexical. No equivocation here; the dichotomy is well-posed and resolves to lexical.

3. **Genuine internal inconsistency exists on the page** (your question (a), confirmed): :2990 "arises," :2992 "emerges" are production verbs; the same :2992 paragraph carries "rather than a derivation." This contradiction is real and undisputed by both sides. The dispute is solely over its *weight* — voids the characterization (red's strong reading) vs requires a one-word trim (blue's weak reading).

## My verdict reasoning

The entire debate reduces to one unpinned term: "correctly flagged." Under the plain (weak) reading — the flag is present and its operative content is accurate — blue carries the claim, because the :2992 sentence does contain "rather than a derivation," that phrase is the operative disclosure, and it states exactly the non-derivational property red proves via circularity; red's class-(a)/class-(b) Popper sharpening agrees with the "not a derivation" half and disputes only the secondary "may not be falsifiable" half, which is a co-disclosure, not the whole flag. Under the strong reading — the section must be internally consistent — red would carry it, because the production verbs "arises/emerges" do contradict the flag in the same paragraph. The plain reading governs: "correctly flagged" most naturally means the flag is present and operatively accurate, not that the prose is verb-perfect. This is reinforced by convergence, which is the decisive frame signal. Both sides ended at the same proposition: the thesis is not a derivation, the residue is a fixed set of lexical trims (verb fix at :2990/:2992, SM-subgroup enumeration trim at :2992, inline hedge at the earlier "objective reality" prose). Blue adopted red's circularity finding as the *reason* for its verb concession; red conceded it "does not need to defeat the subsection outright." That is a label dispute over a threshold ("substantially correct modulo trims" vs "fails by its own count"), not two sides arguing different propositions packed into one claim. REMAND is therefore the wrong tool — no sub-claim would require evidence the debate has not already produced, and the scope mandate forbids REMAND when the verdict is clear. OUT_OF_SCOPE is wrong — the claim is answerable from the .tex and the canon, and the metaphysical interpretation itself is correctly quarantined by the manuscript rather than rested upon (so it generates no unanswerable math claim). BLUE_WINS, with the residue moved to the Action where it belongs.
