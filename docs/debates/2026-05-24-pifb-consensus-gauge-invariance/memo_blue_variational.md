# Blue memo — variational

## Steelman of the attack
Red's strongest move: "consensus" does no inferential work — multi-agent agreement on a
frame-independent structure is automatic once each agent minimizes the same GL(K)-invariant
free energy, so calling gauge invariance a "consensus requirement" is vacuous.

## Defense from external canon

**Multi-agent free energy reduces to single-agent under N=1.** The framework's collective
free energy is a principled extension only if it reduces to standard single-agent variational
free energy when there is one agent [external_canon_math.md §4: a multi-agent F that reduces to
single-agent F at N=1 is a principled extension]. The consensus construction is built on top of
that, and the per-agent objective is the standard FEP free energy `F = KL(q ‖ p) − log p(o)`
[Friston2010; external_canon_inference]. Nothing non-standard is smuggled into the per-agent
inference.

**The honest content of the "consensus" framing is weak but not malformed.** Read minimally:
because every agent's divergence is invariant under its own frame choice, the frame-independent
content is exactly the gauge-invariant content; agents that report only frame-independent
quantities necessarily agree on them. That is a true conditional. Read maximally — as if
consensus *causes* or *derives* physical gauge invariance — it is vacuous, because the
invariance was already imposed. The manuscript adopts the minimal reading and explicitly flags
the maximal one as "metaphysical … may not be falsifiable" (:2992). The claim under evaluation
asserts the self-flag is the honest characterization; on the variational/inference reading it is.

**Falsifiability assessment.** The manuscript's own admission that "the hypothesis may not be
falsifiable from within the framework" is the correct epistemic status. There is no internal
experiment that distinguishes "gauge invariance imposed on nature" from "gauge invariance
emergent from consensus" — both predict the same frame-independent observables. Conceding
unfalsifiability *in place* is the honest move, and the manuscript makes it.

CONCESSION the variational lens notes: the section would be stronger if it stated plainly that
the consensus framing adds no predictive content beyond the GL(K)-invariance already built in.
It hedges to "metaphysical interpretation" rather than "predictively empty," which is slightly
softer than the strongest honest statement — but it is not an overclaim.

## Falsification condition (argued unmet)
The defense fails if the consensus framing makes a falsifiable prediction that the manuscript
failed to flag as metaphysical (it makes none — both views predict identical frame-independent
observables), OR if the per-agent objective departed from standard FEP (it does not).
