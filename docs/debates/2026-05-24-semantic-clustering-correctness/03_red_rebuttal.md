Every expert is cited. This rebuttal is shaped by blue's opening, not by red's own opening.

## Concession

Red concedes four things cleanly, and one of them is forced.

The Pennec/Fillard/Ayache 2006 citation from red's opening is withdrawn (gauge-theorist). The canon-cop is right (`02b_canon_cop_openings.md:12`): Pennec's affine-invariant geodesic lives on the SPD cone Sym⁺(K) and is invariant under the congruence action P → A P Aᵀ, whereas the code at `geometry.py:349-352` computes ‖log(Ωᵢ⁻¹Ωⱼ)‖_F, a distance on GL⁺(K) *group elements* invariant under left translation Ω → A Ω. Wrong manifold, wrong symmetry. Red also does not call the Ω distance "bi-invariant": there is no bi-invariant Riemannian metric on non-compact GL⁺(K), exactly as blue states at `02_blue_opening.md:25`.

Red further concedes, on the merits: (1) the per-head quadrature d = sqrt(Σ_h d_h²) is the exact product-manifold metric on GL(10) × GL(10) ⊂ GL(20), not an approximation [Lee 2013, product-manifold construction]; (2) block-diagonal exp factors exactly, exp(A₁ ⊕ A₂) = exp(A₁) ⊕ exp(A₂) [Hall 2015 §2-3; Nakahara 2003 §5], so the two Ω code paths compute the same object under this config and the 4.4e-16 agreement is structural; (3) the index alignment is verified with no off-by-one (`extract.py:98-106`, `pipeline.py:62-80,182,193-222`), so the contextual duplicate labels are the structural per-occurrence output, not data corruption — falsification condition (b) is dead. The Ω-panel's geometry is internally sound, and red does not contest it.

## Core attack

Blue's own opening names the ground on which the debate turns and then cedes it. The last sentence of `02_blue_opening.md:51` states that falsification condition (c) — whether a completeness item is actually a correctness/scientific-integrity defect — "is the live question on which the debate most plausibly turns" and concedes the judges must weigh it. So blue does not claim the numbers settle (c). The numbers settle the Ω-panel geometry; they are silent on whether the figures are correct. Red presses (c), and it survives any number blue produces.

"Correct-by-design" is unfalsifiable as constructed (philosophy-of-science). The claim partitions every property of the artifact so that present-and-accurate features score toward "correctness" while present-and-inaccurate features are reassigned to a "completeness" bucket and excused (`02_blue_opening.md:33` does exactly this: "the geometry computed on those rows is still correct, it is the selection of rows that is poor"). Under that partition no observable defect can falsify "correct," because any inaccuracy a critic produces is, by the claim's own rule, relabeled completeness. A statement no observation can contradict is a definition dressed as a finding, not an empirical claim about the artifact [Popper 1959 §§4, 6, 19-21].

The vocab figure is the present, accurate-yet-false feature the relabeling hides (implementation-engineer, code-quality). Executed under the active config (cl100k_base, n=200): 107/200 ids decode to U+FFFD, 23/200 to empty, 72/200 unique, 130/200 duplicates. `_sanitize_label` (`pipeline.py:88-93`) strips C0+DEL but does not strip or flag U+FFFD or empty, so the unrenderable rows reach the figure as-is — reached at runtime, not latent. The annotation path compounds it deterministically: `plotting.py:224-235` annotates the first 30 *array-order* points, and the active subsample is sorted ascending by id, so the 30 labels a reader actually sees are the lowest ids — ASCII punctuation/digit fragments — the least semantically meaningful 30 of an already-65%-junk set. The figure does not merely omit salience; it annotates anti-salient points. A figure where the rendered conclusion does not match the data lacks graphical integrity [Tufte 1983], and analysis outputs shipped as results are products judged as results, not decoration [Wilson et al. 2017, PLOS Comput Biol 13(6):e1005510]. "The per-row arithmetic is correct" is not the unit of correctness for a figure; "a competent reader reaches a true conclusion" is, and this figure fails it.

## Defense

Blue's Vector 1 leans on D_φ(whitened) vs D_Ω Spearman 0.952–0.972 to certify the two panels depict "the same cluster structure." Red concedes the number and contests the inference (info-geometer, gauge-theorist). Rank-agreement of the two *input* distance matrices is necessary but not sufficient for the two *output* UMAP layouts to be visually comparable, for two reasons drawn from blue's own canon and blue's own concession.

First, the φ-panel metric is admitted non-canonical. Blue concedes at `02_blue_opening.md:39` that PCA-whitening "is not a canonical metric ... no Cencov-invariance standing." That concession is correct: the unique reparameterization-invariant metric on a parametric manifold is Fisher–Rao [Cencov 1972; Amari 1998, Neural Computation 10(2):251-276], and sample-covariance preconditioning — the same family as the whitening at `geometry.py:215-270` — is not Fisher and not invariant. The Lie-algebra inner product that induces a left-invariant metric is the Frobenius/Killing form at the identity, not an empirical per-sample singular-value rescaling [Hall 2015 §3]. So the φ-panel (chart-dependent whitened Euclidean) and the Ω-panel (left-invariant GL⁺(K) geodesic) are *different geometric objects*; the side-by-side display invites the reader to treat them as one, which they are not.

Second, Spearman is the wrong statistic to rescue a *visual* comparison, by blue's own UMAP citation. UMAP optimizes a fuzzy-topological cross-entropy built from actual neighbor distances, not their ranks, and produces output that is not distance-preserving with free global rotation/reflection [McInnes, Healy, Melville 2018, arXiv:1802.03426, §2-3]. A high input rank-correlation is fully consistent with two visibly different layouts. The measured decomposition confirms the mechanism rather than rescuing the comparison: whitening moves the φ-distance only ~0.005 in Spearman from un-whitened φ (D_φ_whitened vs D_φ_unwhitened = 0.9849, evidence pack), so the surviving rank-agreement is the *expected* behavior of a near-rank-preserving linear rescale on this sample — a property of the rescale, not independent evidence that the panels are the same object. (Stated precisely, to avoid overclaim: whitening is a linear map and the induced pairwise distance is not in general a monotone function of the un-whitened distance; the 0.9849 says it nearly preserves ranks on this sample, not that it must.) The 0.952 confirms the orderings nearly agree; blue's McInnes citation forbids reading it as certification that the picture is faithful.

One latent exposure remains live as a correctness exposure, not a UX item (implementation-engineer). The block-restriction at `geometry.py:337-340` is unconditional — it slices A_full[a:b,a:b] then exp regardless of the bank, with no guard. Under the active config (`cross_couplings=[]`, bank max off-block 0.0 exact) the result is exact and red concedes that. But under the shipped toggle `auto_close_cross_head_basis=True` (`model.py:447-454`, adds super-block-spanning generators) the slice silently discards off-block algebra and the geodesic is computed on the wrong group element, diverging from `pipeline._per_token_omega`'s full-matrix exp (`pipeline.py:111-122`) with no error raised. The 4.4e-16 path agreement is a property of the current bank, not of the code. A distance routine that goes silently wrong under a shipped toggle, unguarded, is a latent correctness defect.

No memo is discounted.
