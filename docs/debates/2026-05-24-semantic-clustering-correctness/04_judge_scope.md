That is unfalsifiability-by-construction for the compound claim, even though each extracted sub-claim is falsifiable.

2. **Accuracy-vs-completeness false partition (on the vocab axis only).** 00 sub-prop 3 files the byte-fallback vocab view under "completeness." The claim never asserted metric identity; it named the metric difference as the *cause* of layout divergence (`00:32`). This dichotomy is real and correctly drawn.

## My verdict reasoning

The claim is a compound conjunction of three propositions adjudicated under one "correct-by-design" headline, and the three do not stand or fall together. Sub-claims A (φ/Ω encode the same cluster structure; divergence is a projection artifact) and B (contextual duplicates are per-occurrence semantics; index alignment verified) are well-framed and carried by Blue on frame-correct evidence: Red withdrew its only metric-mismatch canon (Pennec, struck by canon-cop), conceded the product-manifold quadrature, the block-diagonal exp factorization, and the off-by-one-free alignment, leaving the geometry uncontested and the contextual duplicates structurally guaranteed. These two are precisely the phenomena the user flagged, so the user's actual question resolves BLUE. But sub-claim 3's universal quantifier — "none of which is a correctness bug" — is false as stated, and the defender says so: Blue's rebuttal concedes the byte-fallback vocab figure is "an accuracy defect on the vocab figure's presentation, not a completeness gap" (`03_blue:7`), directly contradicting the claim text. The "correct-by-design" label equivocates between math-correctness (true) and figure-as-scientific-artifact correctness (false on the vocab panel), and the claim's partition rule immunizes the figure axis from falsification — a frame defect Red correctly identifies via Popper. Because the compound claim packs a mostly-true geometry/semantics proposition together with a false universal-quantifier proposition, the correct scope outcome is not to pick a single winner over the whole conjunction but to decompose it: REMAND, adopting sub-claims A/B (blue) and C (the vocab accuracy defect, red) as separate crisp claims. This is the highest-value move — it tells the user the two things they flagged are right-by-design, and the one thing that is actually wrong (the unflagged vocab figure) is wrong on the accuracy axis they explicitly asked about.

## Decisive evidence

## If REMAND or OUT_OF_SCOPE

The compound claim decomposes into the following crisp sub-claims. The chief-judge defers to scope on REMAND-for-equivocation (methodology reconciliation rule 2) and adopts this list.

- **Sub-claim A (geometry — BLUE-leaning, the user's first flagged phenomenon):** Under the active config (K=20, irrep_dims=[10,10], cross_couplings=[], block-diagonal bank), the φ-distance matrix and the Ω-geodesic distance matrix encode the same cluster structure (Spearman 0.952–0.972, both panels independently pick k=2), and the residual *visual* divergence between the `phi_vector_clustering` and `omega_clustering` panels is a non-isometric-projection (UMAP) layout artifact, not a geometry or index bug. The per-head quadrature `d=√(Σ_h d_h²)` is the exact product-manifold metric on GL(10)×GL(10), and the two Ω code paths agree to 4.4e-16. (PCA-whitening on φ is conceded non-canonical but rank-cost ≈0.005, so it does not break structure-agreement.)

- **Sub-claim B (contextual semantics — BLUE-leaning, the user's second flagged phenomenon):** The duplicate token labels (`the`, `of`, `Atlantic`) in the *contextual* figures are the defined per-occurrence semantics of the `(B,N)->(B·N)` flatten in `extract_contextual` (`extract.py:98`), with verified off-by-one-free index alignment `strings[i] ↔ coords[i] ↔ token_ids[i]`. This is correct-by-design data, not corruption.

- **Sub-claim C (vocab figure accuracy — RED-leaning, conceded by Blue; NOT a phenomenon the user flagged):** The `vocab` figure is an *accuracy* defect, not a completeness gap: under cl100k_base the first-256-id extraction yields 107/200 U+FFFD, 23/200 empty, 72/200 unique (`01:54`), `_sanitize_label` does not strip the fallbacks (`pipeline.py:88-93`), and `_MAX_ANNOTATIONS=30` annotates the lowest (anti-salient) array-order ids (`plotting.py:224-235`). A figure titled around vocabulary semantics that renders ~65% unrenderable glyphs and annotates the least-meaningful tokens licenses a false reader inference. Therefore the claim's "none of which is a correctness bug" is false on this axis; recommended action is to fix the id-selection / fallback-stripping / salience-annotation, or to relabel and scope the figure honestly.

- **Sub-claim D (block-restriction guard — latent, agreed by both; completeness/robustness):** The unguarded block-restriction at `geometry.py:337-340` is exact under the active config (off-block max = 0.0) and is a *latent* correctness exposure that surfaces only under the opt-in `auto_close_cross_head_basis=True` toggle. Both sides agree a guard (assert off-block ≈ 0, or raise `NotImplementedError`) belongs there. This is a robustness gap, not a present bug.
