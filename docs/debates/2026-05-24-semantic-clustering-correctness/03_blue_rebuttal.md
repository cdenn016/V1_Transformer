# Blue Rebuttal — semantic-clustering-correctness

## Concession

Two concessions, one substantive and one prophylactic.

First, the substantive one. The claim's sub-proposition 3 lists the vocab view under "completeness — the conceded weaknesses," and the parent claim asserts that the module's defects are "none of which is a correctness bug." On the vocab figure that wording is too strong, and Red is right to press it. The vocab extractor pulls token ids `0..255` and decodes each individually under cl100k_base, whose first 256 ids are byte fallbacks; the verifier reproduced 107 → U+FFFD, 23 → empty, only 72/200 unique strings (`01_evidence.md:54`). `_sanitize_label` strips C0/DEL but not U+FFFD or the empty string (`pipeline.py:88-93`), and the 30 annotated points are selected by array order — `range(min(len(token_strings), coords.shape[0], 30))` (`plotting.py:224-235`) — which after the sorted subsample are the lowest ids, i.e. the least-salient punctuation/digit fragments. A figure presented as a "vocabulary semantics" result that ships two-thirds unrenderable glyphs and annotates the least-meaningful tokens by index is an inaccurate shipped artifact, not a missing one. The honest partition is: this is an accuracy defect on the vocab figure's *presentation*, not a completeness gap. Blue concedes this sub-point. What the concession does NOT reach: the geometry computed on those same 200 priors is still numerically correct (the φ/Ω Spearman is 0.972 on the random-200 vocab types, `01_evidence.md:50`); the defect is in the labels and annotation selection, not in the distance math or the index alignment.

Second, the prophylactic one. The block-restriction at `geometry.py:337-340` is unguarded — it discards `A_full`'s off-block entries with no assertion that they vanish. Under `auto_close_cross_head_basis=True` (which `model.py:447-454` warns can add super-block generators) the truncation would compute the Ω geodesic on a different group element than the model's own transport. Blue grants this is a latent future-proofing item and a guard belongs there. It is not a present correctness bug — see the Defense.

## Core attack

Red conceded the per-head quadrature and the index alignment in its own opening (`02_red_opening.md:21`: "Red concedes the per-head quadrature and the index alignment; the red case does not need them"). Pin these as settled: the geodesic-quadrature identity holds to 4.4e-16 and the `strings[i] ↔ coords[i] ↔ token_ids[i]` alignment is verified off-by-one-free (`01_evidence.md:49,55`). Red's entire case therefore reduces to two surviving vectors: the φ/Ω "metric mismatch" and the "correct-by-design equivocation." Both fail.

The metric-mismatch vector collapsed under canon-cop's ruling. Red's load-bearing move is to place the Ω geodesic in the affine-invariant SPD-cone distance class — "whose defining property is invariance under the congruence action `P → A P Aᵀ`" — and cite Pennec, Fillard, Ayache 2006 (`02_red_opening.md:25-29`). Canon-cop assessed this a **2-strike wrong-domain citation** (`02b_canon_cop_openings.md:10-12`): the congruence action `P → APAᵀ` is the symmetry of the SPD/tensor cone Sym⁺(K), Pennec's domain. The code does not live there. `omega_geodesic_distances` computes `M = np.linalg.solve(Oi, Oj)` then `‖log M‖_F` on `Oi = exp(A_h)` (`geometry.py:349-352`, read directly): a distance on **GL⁺(K) group elements**, left-invariant under `Ω_k → A Ω_k`, with no SPD square-root symmetrization. The canon classifies these correctly and separately: φ ∈ gl(K) is a Lie-algebra element whose metric is structural ("do not assume Euclidean," `external_canon_math.md:63`), while Ω is a group element acting on the associated bundle via ρ(g) (`external_canon_math.md:87,97`; `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`). Once the Pennec attachment is struck, Red has **no surviving canon** establishing that the Ω geodesic is the wrong distance, mis-derived, or in any specific class incompatible with the comparison. The metric-mismatch thesis rests on naming a target class for Ω, and the only named target was wrong-domain. There is no bi-invariant Riemannian metric on non-compact GL⁺(K) either — Red gestured at "bi-invariant" (`02_red_opening.md:27`) and canon-cop flagged that too (`02b_canon_cop_openings.md:14`). Red has named three distance classes for Ω and gotten the relevant property wrong each time; the attack has no canonical anchor left.

The "correct-by-design equivocation" vector trades on a frame substitution. Red argues the claim uses "correct" to mean "the same geometric object" and then exposes that the two panels are not the same object (`02_red_opening.md:38-39`: "the cost is... whether the two panels are the same object, and they are not"). But the claim never asserts metric identity. Sub-proposition 1 asserts that φ and Ω "encode the *same* cluster structure" (`00_claim.md:25`) and that the residual visual divergence is a projection-layout artifact — explicitly naming the metric difference (whitened-Euclidean vs un-whitened geodesic) as part of what *produces* the layout divergence, not as a hidden identity claim. Red is attacking a claim of geometric-object identity that the claim does not make. Structure-agreement and object-identity are different predicates; conflating them is the equivocation, and it runs in Red's direction, not the claim's.

## Defense

The per-head quadrature is the exact product-manifold metric, not an approximation. Under the active config the gauge group is the direct product `GL(10) × GL(10) ⊂ GL(20)` (`01_evidence.md:16`). For a Riemannian product manifold with the product metric, squared geodesic length decomposes exactly as the sum of per-factor squared lengths, `d(p,q)² = d_1² + d_2²` [Lee2013, product-manifold construction; `01b_extended_evidence.md:5`]. The quadrature `d = sqrt(Σ_h d_h²)` at `geometry.py:355-356` is therefore the *exact* metric on the product group, which is precisely the structure the block-diagonal generator bank instantiates. This is canon Red did not contest and cannot, having conceded the quadrature.

The two Ω code paths agree to 4.4e-16 for a structural, not coincidental, reason. For `A = A_1 ⊕ A_2` block-diagonal, every power-series term `A^k = A_1^k ⊕ A_2^k`, so `exp(A) = exp(A_1) ⊕ exp(A_2)` exactly [Hall2015 §2-3; `01b_extended_evidence.md:7`]. The verifier confirmed the 200-generator bank has off-block max `|entry| = 0.0` exactly (`01_evidence.md:48`), so `geometry.py:339`'s block-restrict-then-exp and `pipeline.py:119-121`'s full-exp are computing the same group element to machine precision (`01_evidence.md:49`). This is why the unguarded block-restriction is a *latent* issue and not a present bug: under the active config the discarded blocks are provably zero, so the truncation is exact. Red's own falsification framing — "only breaks under a non-default toggle" (`02_red_opening.md:97`) — concedes the present-config correctness. "Correct under the tested config" is the verdict the claim asks for; the toggle that breaks it is `auto_close_cross_head_basis=True`, which is not active (`01_evidence.md:16`, `cross_couplings=[]`).

Red's monotone-invariance point supports the claim rather than undermining it. Red argues that Spearman "constrains the ordering of distance values, not the geometric structure" (`02_red_opening.md:51`) and separately that PCA whitening is a rescale (`02_red_opening.md:32`, citing [Bishop2006 §12.1]). Combine the two. Whitening rescales each principal coordinate by `1/s_k` (`geometry.py:261-266`, read directly), which reweights pairwise distances as `sum_k (φ_i,k − φ_j,k)² / s_k²`. Anisotropic reweighting can in principle perturb pairwise distance ranks, so Red's framing predicts at most a small rank perturbation — and the verifier measures the perturbation directly: D_φ_whitened vs D_φ_unwhitened Spearman is 0.9849, a cost of ~0.005 (`01_evidence.md:52`). That is the empirical signature of robustness, not fragility: whitening neither manufactures nor destroys the cluster structure the rank correlation detects. The 0.96–0.97 φ/Ω agreement is therefore *robust to the whitening choice*, which is exactly the robustness the structure-agreement claim needs. Red's own characterization of whitening hands the claim its defense.

The visual divergence is the projection artifact the claim names, and Red's UMAP citation establishes it. UMAP optimizes a topological neighborhood cross-entropy, not exact distance preservation; its embeddings are not related by a rigid transform across two different precomputed matrices [McInnes2018 arXiv:1802.03426; `01b_extended_evidence.md:23,27`]. Red deploys this to argue a 0.96 matrix-level Spearman "cannot rescue a side-by-side visual comparison." But the claim does not try to rescue the visual *layouts* — it asserts the layouts diverge *because* UMAP is non-isometric while the underlying structure agrees (`00_claim.md:32`). UMAP non-isometry is the mechanism the claim invokes for the artifact; Red citing it confirms the mechanism. Two non-distance-faithful 2D layouts looking different despite a 0.96 structure correlation is the predicted observation, not a refutation of it.

## Net position

Geometry math: correct, and after the Pennec strike, uncontested by surviving canon. Contextual view (the `the`/`of`/`Atlantic` duplicates the user actually flagged): correct-by-design — `extract_contextual` flattens `(B,N)→(B·N)` one row per occurrence (`extract.py:98`), with verified index alignment, so a recurring type yields multiple legitimate identical-labeled rows (`01_evidence.md:55`). This is the load-bearing artifact and it survives. Vocab view: a separate, weaker artifact whose labeling and annotation selection is a genuine accuracy defect — conceded. The claim is right on its load-bearing geometry and on the contextual duplicates; it overreaches only on the vocab figure's "completeness-not-correctness" framing.

## Falsification conditions

Blue's rebuttal position is wrong if any of the following hold. (1) Red produces in-domain canon showing the GL⁺(K) left-invariant geodesic `‖log(Ω_i⁻¹Ω_j)‖_F` is mis-derived or belongs to a class incompatible with comparing transport elements — Pennec's SPD-cone congruence invariance is not that canon (`02b_canon_cop_openings.md:10-12`). (2) The product-manifold quadrature is shown not to be the exact product metric on `GL(10)×GL(10)` — but [Lee2013] gives the exact decomposition and the bank is exactly block-diagonal (`01_evidence.md:48`). (3) Whitening is shown to reorder rank structure — but it is a per-axis positive rescale (`geometry.py:261-266`) and the empirical rank cost is ~0.005 (`01_evidence.md:52`). (4) The contextual duplicates are shown to be an index bug rather than per-occurrence flattening — but `extract.py:98,106` flatten μ and ids in the same order and alignment is verified (`01_evidence.md:55`). (5) The off-block entries are nonzero under the active config — but the verifier measured exactly 0.0 (`01_evidence.md:48`).

Every panel lens is reflected: geometer and gauge-theorist (Ω as GL⁺(K) group element vs SPD cone, product-manifold quadrature, block-diagonal exp); info-geometer (whitening as monotone rescale, monotone-invariance turn); transformer-ml / numerical-analyst (4.4e-16 path agreement, UMAP non-isometry); implementation-engineer (active-config trace: `cross_couplings=[]`, byte-fallback vocab path); code-quality (array-order annotation, unstripped fallbacks); philosophy-of-science (structure-agreement vs geometric-object-identity equivocation, vocab accuracy-vs-completeness honest concession). No memo discounted.
