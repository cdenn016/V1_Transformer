## Steelman (opposing position)

The module ships two figures that a competent reader will misread — a vocab view whose first-256 cl100k_base ids are 53% byte-fallback junk (107/200 decode to U+FFFD, 23/200 to empty, only 72/200 unique) and a contextual view with undisclosed per-occurrence duplicate labels — while the φ and Ω panels look visually different despite Ω being a deterministic function of φ; calling such a module "correct" papers over the fact that PCA-whitening on φ is not a canonical Lie-algebra metric, that the per-head geodesic quadrature is asserted rather than derived, and that a user looking at these figures draws wrong conclusions, which for a scientific-visualization tool is a correctness failure, not a cosmetic one.

## Position

Under the active config (K=20, irrep_dims=[10,10], diagonal_covariance=True, cross_couplings=[], cl100k_base; verified `system_info.json` + `config.py:893-902` returns `irrep_dims` because `cross_couplings` is empty), the two flagged phenomena are correct-by-design and the geometry math is sound: (1) the φ–Ω visual divergence is a projection-layout artifact of a non-distance-preserving embedding acting on two metrics whose rank structure agrees at Spearman 0.952–0.972 with both panels independently selecting k=2, and (2) the contextual duplicate labels are the structurally-guaranteed per-occurrence semantics of a `(B,N)->(B·N)` flatten. The genuine defects (byte-fallback vocab view, undisclosed per-occurrence semantics, array-order label selection, unguarded block-restriction) are completeness/UX/latent-config items, not correctness bugs in the geometry or the index alignment under the active config. The one place where the steelman lands a real hit — PCA-whitening's lack of canonical-metric standing — is conceded below as a visualization choice justified empirically, not theoretically.

## Evidence

**Vector 1 — the φ and Ω panels encode the same cluster structure; the visual gap is an embedding artifact (gauge-theorist, ml-engineer, info-geometer).**

The transport is `Ω = exp(Σ_c φ_c G_c)`, so Ω is a deterministic, *nonlinear* (matrix-exponential) function of φ. Two reductions of the same underlying object cannot be expected to be metrically identical after a nonlinear map followed by a non-isometric embedding, but they should share rank structure — and they do. The executed evidence (investigator A + independent verifier, real checkpoint φ) gives D_φ(whitened) vs D_Ω Spearman **0.952** (first-200 ids) and **0.972** (random-200 types), with the two contributing nonlinearities isolated: the exp/BCH curvature costs ~0.03 of the correlation (D_φ_unwhitened vs D_Ω = 0.9696) and whitening costs ~0.005 (D_φ_whitened vs D_φ_unwhitened = 0.9849), even at 93% off-diagonal (non-commutative) generator energy where BCH corrections are largest [Hall2015 §5: `log(exp(A)exp(B)) = A+B+½[A,B]+…`, so the exp map is rank-preserving but not distance-preserving when generators do not commute]. Both panels independently select k=2 in *both* views (vocab: φ silhouette 0.282 / Ω 0.260, both k=2; contextual: φ 0.351 / Ω 0.299, both k=2 — verified in `vocab/metrics.json` and `contextual/metrics.json`). The clustering — the analytically meaningful content — agrees; only the 2D layout differs.

That residual layout difference is the expected behavior of the projector, not a math bug. UMAP with `metric="precomputed"` (`projection.py:125-137`) produces embeddings that are explicitly not distance-preserving and carry free global rotation/reflection: the algorithm optimizes a fuzzy-topological cross-entropy on local neighborhoods, with no constraint fixing global orientation or preserving inter-cluster distances [McInnes2018 arXiv:1802.03426, §2–3]. Feeding two rank-correlated-but-not-identical dissimilarity matrices (whitened-Euclidean on φ vs affine-invariant geodesic on Ω) into an orientation-free embedder yields two pictures that look different while certifying the same partition. Silhouette read off a precomputed dissimilarity is itself a heuristic comparison, not a metric statement [Rousseeuw1987 J. Comput. Appl. Math.], so "the silhouettes are close and both pick k=2" is exactly the right grain of evidence to compare the two panels — and it agrees.

**Vector 2 — the two Ω code paths are numerically identical under the active config; the geodesic is the standard one (gauge-theorist, implementation-engineer).**

The generator bank is exactly block-diagonal: max off-block |entry| of the 200-generator bank = **0.0 (exact)**, 0 generators with cross-block support (executed, real bank from `generate_glK_multihead_generators(20,2)` at `math_utils/generators.py:870-953`, selected at `model.py:419-491` because the spec is a single multihead block with empty `cross_couplings`). For a block-diagonal algebra element `A = A_1 ⊕ A_2`, the matrix exponential factors block-wise, `exp(A_1 ⊕ A_2) = exp(A_1) ⊕ exp(A_2)`, because the off-block products in the power series vanish identically — this is the direct-product group structure `GL(10) × GL(10) ⊂ GL(20)` [Nakahara2003 §5 on matrix Lie groups and the exponential map; Hall2015 §2-3]. Therefore `geometry.omega_geodesic_distances` (which slices `A_full[a:b,a:b]` at `geometry.py:339` then exponentiates) and `pipeline._per_token_omega` (which exponentiates the full 20×20 A at `pipeline.py:119-121`) compute the same Ω blocks — confirmed numerically at `max|expm(A_full) − blockdiag(expm(A_h))| ≈ 4.4e-16` over random φ at scale 0.05–0.3. There is no inconsistency between the two paths under this config.

The per-head distance `d_h = ‖log(Ω_{h,i}⁻¹ Ω_{h,j})‖_F` (`geometry.py:349-352`) is a canonical left-invariant Frobenius distance on a matrix Lie group: left-translating Ω_i to the identity gives Ω_i⁻¹Ω_j, and the Frobenius norm of its logarithm is the length scale induced by the Frobenius inner product at the identity, transported left-invariantly [Nakahara2003 §5-6; Hall2015]. On non-compact GL⁺(K) there is no *unique* bi-invariant Riemannian metric (no compact form), so this is a principled standard choice rather than the only canonical one — `‖log(Ω_i⁻¹Ω_j)‖_F` coincides with the left-invariant geodesic length when the per-pair generators commute and is the standard Lie-group clustering distance otherwise; the claim's falsification condition (a) explicitly keeps "mis-derived against the canonical affine-invariant / log-Euclidean metric" as a live red win. Because the group is a direct product `GL(10) × GL(10)`, the product metric is the Pythagorean sum of the per-factor metrics, `d = sqrt(Σ_h d_h²)` (`geometry.py:355-356`) — the quadrature is not an approximation, it is the metric on a Riemannian product manifold [Lee2013 on product manifolds; the product of left-invariant metrics is left-invariant with the factors orthogonal]. The non-finite-logm chordal fallback (`geometry.py:354`) only fires when `Ω_i⁻¹Ω_j` has no real logarithm, which is the documented non-surjectivity of exp on GL⁺(K) [external_canon_math.md §2; Hall2015]; on the real run the verifier reproduced `omega_dist_from_identity_mean ≈ 2.39` against `metrics.json` 2.43 (gap = raw `phi_embed` vs `encode`-applied trace control), confirming the live path does not hit the fallback materially.

**Vector 3 — contextual duplicate labels are structurally-guaranteed per-occurrence semantics; index alignment is verified (implementation-engineer).**

`extract_contextual` reshapes `(B,N,*) -> (B·N,*)` for mu, sigma, phi and flattens token_ids in the identical order (`extract.py:98,102,104,106`). One row per occurrence is therefore the *definition* of the contextual view, not a bug: a recurring type (`the`, `of`, `Atlantic`) appears once per position it occupies in the wikitext passage, yielding multiple legitimate rows with identical decoded labels. The purpose of the contextual view is precisely to show the same type relaxed under different contexts, so duplicate labels are the intended output. Index alignment `strings[i] ↔ coords[i] ↔ token_ids[i]` is verified correct with no off-by-one: `_subsample` reindexes mu/sigma/phi/token_ids (and strings) with one shared `idx` (`pipeline.py:62-80`), `_decode_strings` runs once after subsample on the already-reindexed ids (`pipeline.py:182`), the same `strings` list is passed to all four plot calls (`pipeline.py:193,202,210,222`), and `project()` preserves row order (`projection.py` returns one coord row per input row). No data corruption exists.

**Vector 4 — the conceded defects are completeness/UX/latent, not correctness (philosophy-of-science, implementation-engineer).**

Four items red is right to flag, none of which is a math or alignment bug under the active config. The vocab view is near-meaningless because the first 256 cl100k_base ids are byte-fallbacks (107→U+FFFD, 23→empty, 72/200 unique) — this is a *completeness/scientific-communication* defect (the chosen id range is uninformative), not an arithmetic error; the geometry computed on those rows is still correct, it is the *selection* of rows that is poor. The per-occurrence semantics are never disclosed in the figure — a *disclosure/UX* defect. `_MAX_ANNOTATIONS=30` annotates the first 30 array-order points (`plotting.py:226-235`) rather than cluster-representative points — a *label-salience/UX* defect. The `omega_geodesic_distances` block-restriction at `geometry.py:337-340` is unguarded (no assertion that off-block entries are zero) — but under the active config (`cross_couplings=[]`, block-diagonal bank, max off-block 0.0) the restriction is exact, so this is a *latent* defect that would only surface under the opt-in `auto_close_cross_head_basis=True` toggle that can add super-block-spanning generators (`model.py:447-454`). A latent defect under a non-default toggle is a robustness gap, not a present correctness bug.

The frame point (philosophy-of-science): the claim does not equivocate when it separates "correct-by-design" (the math and alignment) from "complete/well-communicated" (the four conceded items). The danger the steelman raises — that "correct" misleads — is real only if the two are conflated. The claim explicitly does not conflate them: it asserts geometric/alignment correctness *and* lists the presentation defects as defects. That is the honest framing, not a dodge.

## Concession (where red lands)

PCA-whitening on φ (`geometry.py:215-270`, `whiten=True` default) is not a canonical metric on the Lie algebra. It is not the Killing form, not an Ad-invariant inner product, and not the Fisher metric on the induced distribution family; whitening rescales each principal direction of the *empirical* φ-sample by its singular value, which is a data-dependent reweighting with no Cencov-invariance standing [external_canon_math.md §1, Cencov1972: the Fisher metric is the unique reparameterization-invariant metric on a statistical manifold; Bishop2006 PRML §12.1: whitening is a metric re-choice, not distance-preserving]. The defensible blue position is narrow: whitening is a *visualization* preconditioning choice, and its legitimacy here rests entirely on the empirical fact that it costs only ~0.005 Spearman against the unwhitened φ-distance and the whitened φ-panel still tracks the canonical Ω geodesic at 0.952–0.972. If a reviewer demands that the φ panel be the *algebra-image* of the Ω panel under a principled metric, the honest answer is that the unwhitened φ-distance (or the Ω geodesic itself) is the principled object and whitening is a cosmetic rescale that happens not to break the rank structure. We do not defend whitening as theoretically principled.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds (the first four are red's own win conditions from the claim, reused verbatim; the fifth is derived from the whitening concession):

(a) the φ/Ω divergence reflects a real metric/math error — e.g. the whitening makes the comparison *invalid* (not merely cosmetic), or the geodesic `‖log(Ω_i⁻¹Ω_j)‖_F` is mis-derived against the canonical affine-invariant / log-Euclidean metric, or the per-head quadrature is not the product-manifold metric;
(b) the duplicate labels indicate a genuine data bug (an off-by-one in `extract.py` reshape order, or a `_subsample`/`_decode_strings` misalignment) rather than per-occurrence semantics;
(c) a "completeness" item is actually a correctness/scientific-integrity defect severe enough that calling the module "correct" is misleading — specifically, if shipping the byte-fallback vocab view constitutes presenting a meaningless figure *as if* meaningful;
(d) the index alignment or block-restriction has a *present* (not merely latent) bug under the active config — e.g. if `cross_couplings` were non-empty at runtime so the block-restriction silently dropped off-block algebra;
(e) PCA-whitening on φ destroys the algebra-image character of the φ panel — if the whitened φ-distance failed to track the Ω geodesic (Spearman well below the measured 0.95), the projection-artifact defense in Vector 1 would collapse and the divergence would be a metric-choice artifact promoted to a content disagreement.

The measured numbers (0.952–0.972 Spearman, both k=2, 4.4e-16 path agreement, 0.0 exact off-block, verified alignment) falsify (a), (b), and (d) under the active config and bound (e) far from its trigger. (c) is a scientific-communication judgment the judges must weigh, and is the live question on which the debate most plausibly turns.
