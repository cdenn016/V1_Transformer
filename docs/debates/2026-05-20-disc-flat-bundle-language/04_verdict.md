# Verdict — disc-flat-bundle-language

## Outcome

RED_WINS

## Decisive evidence

Primary-source verification of the Coecke--Sadrzadeh--Clark (2010) abstract via WebFetch on `https://arxiv.org/abs/1003.4394` confirms that the cited sentence ("meanings of whole sentences live in a single space, independent of the grammatical structure of the sentence") establishes **codomain uniformity** of the categorical meaning map, not **derivation-equivalence** in the gauge-transport sense. The abstract does not assert that two distinct derivations yield identical vectors, and does not invoke gauge theory, parallel transport, or path-independence. This is Blue's load-bearing canonical-semantics citation, and it does not say what Blue's opening claimed it said. Combined with the manuscript's own admission at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2228` that the concept of "compositional-semantic component" "currently lacks an operational definition that would permit clean falsification," and with Popper 1959 §6's demarcation criterion (a hypothesis must admit a class of basic statements that could empirically contradict it), the working hypothesis at `:2226` fails the demarcation criterion on its own primary sources.

## Reasoning

The debate strips to the question whether the claim at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2226` survives in a form substantively equivalent to what the manuscript asserts, once both sides' concessions are applied. Blue conceded the manuscript's own falsifier at `:2232` is ill-posed (the per-token-frame parameterization telescopes to identity around any closed triangle, so the framework cannot instantiate a non-cocycle transport from within itself) and conceded sub-claim (b) as non-derivational teleology. Red conceded the "in part because" framing of (c) blunts the exhaustive-cause attack and conceded `transformer/core/connection.py:113-121` does mechanically admit a non-cocycle MLP-mode connection. What remains is whether (a) and (c) survive on the canonical-semantics bridge Blue offers and on Blue's re-anchored paraphrase-invariance falsifier.

On the bridge, the primary-source verification establishes that CSC's "single space" property is codomain uniformity of a categorical functor, not the discrete-graph gauge-transport invariance the manuscript requires. Blue's rebuttal pivot to Partee 1984 and Janssen 1997 (substitution salva veritate, "different derivations, same meaning") imports a homomorphism statement about syntactic-tree equivalence into a claim about gauge transport on a token graph; the standard formulation of the Principle of Compositionality is a homomorphism statement, not a path-independence statement, and the mapping from one to the other is precisely the novel construction the manuscript acknowledges. Blue's opening already conceded this in writing: "The manuscript is novel in mapping this onto a discrete gauge bundle on a token graph." That clause is the load-bearing inference, and the canonical-semantics literature does not deliver it.

On the falsifier, Red's rebuttal correctly identifies that Blue's paraphrase-invariance protocol presupposes the missing operational primitive at the triple-tagging step rather than supplying it. The protocol requires partitioning attention triples or relay sets into "compositional" versus "pragmatic" classes; neither COGS (Kim & Linzen 2020) nor SCAN (Lake & Baroni 2018) supplies such a labelling, and the manuscript at `:2228` explicitly admits the concept is undefined. Blue's defense relabels the gap rather than closing it. Under Popper's criterion in *Logic of Scientific Discovery* §6, a hypothesis whose only proposed falsifier presupposes the very definition the hypothesis is missing does not yet admit a class of basic statements that could contradict it. Red's primary-source citation (Popper 1959 §6) plus the manuscript's self-admission at `:2228` together establish that the hypothesis as written has not yet entered the class of empirical claims.

The remainder after concessions is: (a) compositional content is approximately path-independent in a sense the formal-semantics canon does not establish, with no non-circular operational criterion; (b) conceded as non-derivational; (c) flatness is a contributing inductive bias with weight bounded below by zero, where the parametric cocycle identity is shared by both standard transformers and the gauge VFE by construction of $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$, telescoping by elementary identity per Nakahara 2003 §10.4. This remainder is substantially weaker than the manuscript's claim at `:2226` that natural language "is approximately path-independent in the sense of gauge transport" and that "transformers are architecturally matched to this regime." The hedge language at `:2228` ("working hypothesis," "in principle, testable given an operational criterion") admits the gap, but the gap is not yet closed; on the canon's evidence, the hypothesis at `:2226` does not survive in the form the manuscript asserts.

The COGS / SCAN evidence sits at the operational level the manuscript itself invoked. Kim & Linzen 2020 Table 2 reports transformer exact-match accuracy on the COGS structural-generalization split collapses to roughly 35% from near-perfect IID performance, and Lake & Baroni 2018 reports comparable collapse on SCAN. Blue's reading that IID near-ceiling is consistent with sub-claim (a) is defensible but pays a price: it reduces (a) to "transformers compose meanings consistently on the observed manifold," a far weaker statement than "natural language is approximately path-independent in the sense of gauge transport." Under that weaker reading, (a) is the empirical observation that transformers fit the training distribution, which is not the manuscript's claim. The structural-generalization failure is the operational signature that maps closest to "path-independence of the compositional component," and that signature is negative for vanilla transformers.

Blue's defense of the hedged-scope reading would be defensible if the manuscript's hedge language at `:2228` did the work of restating the claim to the remainder Blue retreats to. It does not: `:2228` labels the work as future and labels the operational definition as missing, but `:2226` is the asserted hypothesis. The hedge admits the gap; it does not close it. A claim that admits its own falsifier is unimplementable, that admits its own operational criterion is undefined, and whose canonical bridge is misread on primary source has not earned the manuscript's hypothesis status.

## Action

Revise `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2221-2232` to one of the following two forms.

The first form preserves the manuscript's intent and restates the claim at the scope the evidence supports. Replace the hypothesis statement at `:2226` with a structural observation: that the GL(K) gauge transformer and standard transformers both implement transport in the flat-bundle class by construction of their respective parameterizations (per-token-frame coboundary for the former, position-independent $W_Q W_K^\top$ for the latter), and that this is compatible with — but does not derive from — the algebraic-homomorphism reading of the Principle of Compositionality in Frege--Montague--Partee. Drop the "approximately path-independent in the sense of gauge transport" framing of natural language as a linguistic claim; the formal-semantics canon does not supply that bridge and Coecke--Sadrzadeh--Clark 2010 does not establish it on primary source. Drop sub-claim (b) in its current form or restate it explicitly as a teleological motivation with no information-theoretic derivation. Replace the falsification protocol at `:2232` with the paraphrase-invariance dispersion measurement Blue proposed in rebuttal, but only after supplying an independent operational definition of "compositional" versus "pragmatic" triples that does not presuppose the hypothesis.

The second form retracts §8.3 as a substantive linguistic hypothesis and presents it as a speculative connection in a clearly-labeled "open conjectures" subsection, with the explicit acknowledgment that (i) the gauge-transport reading of compositionality is a novel construction not derivable from the formal-semantics canon, (ii) the falsification protocol at `:2232` is ill-posed within the user's framework, and (iii) the architectural-matching claim has empirical content only conditional on an operational criterion not yet supplied. The COGS / SCAN results should be cited with their actual sign (transformer failure on structural generalization) and the carve-out for the "compositional-semantic core" should be motivated independently of the hypothesis it is invoked to save.

A focused follow-up debate on `disc-paraphrase-invariance-protocol` may be warranted if the user wishes to formalize Blue's re-anchored criterion into a manuscript-grade falsifier; the current §8.3 text does not contain that criterion.
