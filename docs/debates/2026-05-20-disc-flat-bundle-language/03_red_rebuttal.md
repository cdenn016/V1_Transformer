# Red Rebuttal — disc-flat-bundle-language

## Concession

I grant two of Blue's narrower points.

First, sub-claim (c) at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2228` is phrased as "transformers succeed *in part because* their flat bundle structure matches this property of language" rather than as an exhaustive-cause claim. Blue is right that the contributing-cause reading survives the most aggressive over-strong attack. I will not press the exhaustive-cause attack.

Second, on the implementation layer: the MLP-mode connection at `transformer/core/connection.py:113-121,147-153` does mechanically parameterize a non-cocycle $\delta_{ij}$. The forward pass computes $\delta_{ij} = \mathrm{MLP}([\mu_i; \mu_j])$ with no enforced antisymmetrization (the `antisymmetrize` flag at line 137-139 applies only to the bilinear branch), so the connection-holonomy $C_{ijk} = \exp(\delta_{ij})\exp(\delta_{jk})\exp(\delta_{ki})$ is not forced to identity by the parameterization. Blue's narrowest claim — that the code admits a non-cocycle $\Omega_{ij}$ — is correct at the mechanical level.

These concessions narrow but do not save the position.

## Core attack

Blue's load-bearing citation is Coecke–Sadrzadeh–Clark (2010, arXiv:1003.4394), which Blue uses to argue that "the path-independence reading of compositional semantics has a non-trivial mapping to the canonical Principle of Compositionality." The cited sentence — "meanings of whole sentences live in a single space, independent of the grammatical structure of the sentence" — is being misread. The CSC abstract establishes **codomain uniformity** (all derivations land in the same target vector space, enabling inner-product comparison), not **derivation-equivalence** (two distinct derivations of the same sentence yield the same vector). These are inequivalent properties. Verified via direct query against the primary source: when asked whether CSC's framework guarantees that ambiguous derivations of the same sentence produce identical vectors, the abstract supplies no such guarantee — it only says all meanings inhabit one space.

The gauge-transport notion of path-independence at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2226-2230` requires the *stronger* property: $\Omega_{ij_1 j_2 \cdots j_n k} = \Omega_{ij'_1 j'_2 \cdots j'_m k}$ for two relay paths $i \to k$ through different intermediates. This is the discrete analogue of vanishing curvature [Nakahara2003 §10.4; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.9 — holonomy is trivial iff the connection is flat]. CSC's "single space" property is **not** this. Type-theoretic homomorphism into a shared codomain is implied by the categorical functoriality of the pregroup-to-vector-space mapping; it does not entail that the morphism itself is path-independent in the gauge-transport sense.

Blue's own opening already concedes this on inspection: "The manuscript is novel in mapping this onto a discrete gauge bundle on a token graph." That single clause does the work I am attacking. The mapping from "algebraic property of meaning composition" to "flat connection on a discrete bundle" is precisely the load-bearing inference that the formal-semantics canon does not establish. Blue cannot use the canon to support the mapping by treating the mapping itself as the canonical content.

A secondary load-bearing weakness: Blue's pre-registered falsification protocol requires partitioning attention triples $(i, j, k)$ into "compositional" versus "pragmatic-inference" subsets and predicting $\|H_{ijk} - I\|$ is larger on the latter. COGS [Kim & Linzen 2020] and SCAN [Lake & Baroni 2018] supply *sentence-level* compositional-generalization labels, not triple-level tags on the attention graph. The mapping from a benchmark instance to a holonomy triple is undefined: which $(i, j, k)$ triple in the attention graph of the sentence "the cat that the dog chased fled" is the *compositional* triple, and which is the *pragmatic* triple? The protocol presupposes an operational labelling that neither benchmark provides and that the manuscript at `:2228-2232` does not construct.

The architecture also produces $H_{ijk} = \exp(\phi_i) \cdot C_{ijk} \cdot \exp(-\phi_i)$ per the connection module's docstring at `transformer/core/connection.py:13-14`. This is a similarity-conjugate of $C_{ijk}$. The norm $\|H_{ijk} - I\|$ in any unitary-invariant norm equals $\|C_{ijk} - I\|$ only if $\exp(\phi_i)$ is orthogonal; for GL($K$) it is not in general. The protocol therefore conflates "holonomy of the learned transport" with "edge-curvature in $\delta$-space" — these differ by a frame-dependent conjugation, and the conjugation's interaction with the proposed correlate is unanalyzed.

## Defense

Strengthening my Phase 2 position against Blue's argument.

The structural unfalsifiability claim in my Phase 2 opening survives Blue's defense for two reasons.

First, the operational criterion Blue offers — train MLP-mode, measure $H_{ijk}$, stratify by compositional vs pragmatic — fails the criterion stated at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2228` ("testable given an operational criterion for compositional semantics") because Blue has not supplied the operational criterion; Blue has supplied a measurement procedure that *presupposes* the criterion. The criterion is exactly what the manuscript admits is missing. Blue's protocol re-states the existence of the gap as if it were a closure of the gap. This is the same circularity I attacked in Phase 2 ("any empirical correlate can be re-described as either match or mismatch by adjusting the boundary of what counts as compositional"); Blue's protocol re-encodes the boundary at the triple-tagging step rather than at the benchmark-instance step. The unfalsifiability is preserved.

Second, on sub-claim (b), Blue offers the narrowest defense: "the manuscript already labels (b) as a plausibility argument." I grant that label. But a plausibility argument that the manuscript itself does not derive cannot be added to the strength of (a) and (c) — it is a free-floating claim that the user does not stand on. Stripped of (b), the working hypothesis at `:2226` reduces to: (a) compositional-semantic content is approximately path-independent, in a sense the canon does not establish, with no operational criterion; (c) transformer success is *in part* attributable to flat-bundle structure, where "in part" admits any non-negative weight including arbitrarily small. The defensible scope Blue retreats to is so narrow that it is consistent with "transformers succeed for entirely unrelated reasons and the flat-bundle property is incidental." That is not a substantive scientific claim about language; it is a structural observation about a parameterization choice.

On the MLP-mode citation, Blue is correct that non-cocycle $\Omega$ is implementable, but the test Blue stakes the position on requires a triple-level labelling and a norm-invariance argument that the citation does not supply. The mechanical existence of a non-cocycle parameterization is the cheap half of the falsification problem; the operational stratification is the hard half, and the canon Blue cites does not deliver it.

Position retained: the working hypothesis at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2226` is, in its current form, a structural observation about transformer parameterization re-described in the language of formal semantics without the bridge that would make it a falsifiable claim about natural language. The Coecke–Sadrzadeh–Clark citation does not provide that bridge — it provides codomain uniformity, not path-independence. Sub-claims (a) and (c) are not testable as stated under the proposed protocol, and sub-claim (b) is conceded by both sides as non-derivational.
