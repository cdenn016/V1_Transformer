# Blue Rebuttal — disc-flat-bundle-language

## Concession

Red is correct on two points, and the defense will not pretend otherwise.

First, the falsification protocol the manuscript proposes at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2232` — "train a non-flat gauge architecture and measure learned holonomy on pragmatic-inference tasks" — is ill-posed *within* the user's framework. Red's algebraic argument lands: $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ telescopes to identity around any closed triangle by the elementary identity $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j) \cdot \exp(\phi_j)\exp(-\phi_k) \cdot \exp(\phi_k)\exp(-\phi_i) = I$ [Nakahara2003 §10.4], so the user's per-token-frame parameterization cannot instantiate a non-cocycle transport. The manuscript-as-written therefore points at a falsifier it cannot execute without exiting the framework, and a result outside the framework is silent on the framework's claim. That is a real defect in the manuscript's testability proposal and the defense will re-anchor the operational criterion below.

Second, sub-claim (b) — "communicative systems face functional pressure toward path-independence" at `:2228` — does not follow from any canonical result in information theory. Shannon's channel-capacity theorem [Shannon1948 §I] is silent on the cocycle structure of meaning composition on a discrete graph; it concerns the bit rate at which messages can be transmitted with bounded error, not the algebraic structure of semantic transport. The manuscript already self-labels (b) as a plausibility argument rather than a derivation; the defense grants the teleology critique and will not defend (b) as anything stronger than a plausibility claim.

These concessions do not, however, touch sub-claim (a) or the load-bearing reading of sub-claim (c). The core attack below explains why.

## Core attack

Red's second evidence point — the "cocycle by construction" attack — conflates two distinct propositions, and the entire weight of red's case rests on this conflation. Call them:

**(A) Architectural cocycle.** For both the user's framework and standard transformers, the implemented transport satisfies $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}\Omega_{ki} = I$ as a parametric identity. In the user's framework this follows by elementary identity from $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$; in standard transformers it follows from $W_Q W_K^\top$ being a position-independent constant. Red's argument at the cocycle-by-construction step establishes (A) and the defense grants it.

**(B) Linguistic path-independence.** Natural-language compositional semantics, viewed as a transport on the graph of contexts, is approximately path-independent — i.e., the meaning of a target token reached by varied relay contexts converges, modulo the pragmatic-inference residual the manuscript carves out at `:2232`. Whether (B) holds is an empirical claim about language, not an algebraic claim about a parameterization.

The architectural-matching claim at sub-claim (c) lives in the intersection of (A) and (B), not in (A) alone. Red's argument reads: "(A) is an identity, therefore (c) is empty." This step does not go through. The empirical content of (c) is the conjunction of (i) the architectural fact (A), which is conceded, and (ii) the linguistic fact (B), which is the substantive part. The matching claim is then: language has property (B); the architecture's expressive class is the flat-bundle class, which can exactly represent transports satisfying (B); therefore the architecture has an inductive-bias advantage on language tasks to the extent (B) is a good approximation. Red's algebraic identity argument falsifies (A) being empirically informative on its own; it does not falsify the conjunction, because (B) is not algebraically forced.

This is the same logical structure as: "a convolutional network has translation-equivariance by parametric construction, therefore the claim 'CNNs succeed on images because images have approximate translation invariance' is empty." That argument is wrong for the same reason. The parametric property is an identity; the linguistic-or-visual property is not; the matching claim is the conjunction. Red's attack does not separate these and so cannot land on (c) as written.

Red's COGS/SCAN attack inherits this conflation and adds a second one: it treats systematic compositional generalization (an out-of-distribution extrapolation property) as equivalent to path-independence on the observed context graph (an in-distribution invariance property). Kim & Linzen 2020 [Table 2] report that vanilla transformers achieve near-perfect accuracy on the COGS IID split and fail principally on the structural generalization split. The IID-vs-structural gap is the empirical signature: the model composes meanings consistently on the observed manifold and fails to extrapolate. Sub-claim (a) is the first of these — composition on the observed manifold — and the COGS IID result is consistent with it, not against it. The structural-generalization failure tests a stronger property (the learned $\Omega$'s extrapolate to combinatorially novel structures) that sub-claim (a) does not require.

## Defense

### Re-anchored operational criterion

The defense owes a replacement falsifier after conceding `:2232` is ill-posed. The criterion is paraphrase-invariance of learned transport under varied relay context. Fix a source token $i$ and a target token $k$ with shared compositional meaning. Vary the intermediate token sequence over a set of relays $\{j^{(1)}, j^{(2)}, \dots\}$ chosen to be semantically inert with respect to the $i \to k$ composition (e.g., synonymous paraphrases of the relay material). Measure the dispersion of $\Omega_{ij^{(m)}} \Omega_{j^{(m)} k}$ across relays $m$ in the operator norm. Sub-claim (a) predicts that this dispersion is small for compositional-semantic transport and large for the carved-out pragmatic residual. The test is instantiable within the framework because the cocycle holds for *any* sequence of frames; what varies between paths is the *content* of the frames induced by different relay material, not the parametric structure. A finding that the dispersion is large across paraphrase-invariant relays falsifies (a). A finding that it is small but uncorrelated with paraphrase invariance falsifies (a) more weakly. This criterion does not require leaving the framework and does not collapse to "benchmark accuracy is high."

### Popper: underconfirmed versus metaphysical

Red's first evidence point treats §8.3 as failing the demarcation criterion. Popper's criterion in *Logic of Scientific Discovery* §6 [Popper1959] is the existence of a class of basic statements that could empirically contradict the hypothesis — not the present availability of a refined operational definition. A claim with a stated falsification protocol that the manuscript labels as future work is an underconfirmed hypothesis, not a metaphysical assertion. The defense concedes the manuscript's `:2232` protocol is ill-posed and replaces it with the paraphrase-invariance criterion above; the existence of this criterion places sub-claim (a) into the class of empirical hypotheses with potential falsifiers, which is the demarcation Popper requires. Red's quoted manuscript admission at `:2228` — that the concept "currently lacks an operational definition that would permit clean falsification" — establishes that the manuscript has not finished the operationalization, not that one is impossible.

### Canonical-semantics bridge

Red argues no formal-semantics canon establishes path-independence of compositional meaning. The strongest available canonical bridge is the substitution-salva-veritate principle in compositionality [Partee1984 "Compositionality"; Janssen 1997, "Compositionality" in *Handbook of Logic and Language*]: synonymous expressions can be substituted into compositional contexts without altering the truth conditions of the host expression. Janssen's "different derivations, same meaning" treatment of the Montagovian framework gives the same content from another angle: distinct syntactic derivations of an expression yield identical denotations under a homomorphic semantic interpretation. Both are invariance statements about *meaning under choice of compositional path*, which is the linguistic content sub-claim (a) restates in gauge-theoretic vocabulary on a token graph. The defense does not claim Partee or Janssen *defined* path-independence in the gauge sense; the claim is that the relevant compositional invariance is present in the canon and the manuscript's framing is a graph-theoretic restatement of it. This is the canonical primitive red said was absent.

### Pre-emption: bag-of-words and "in part"

Red did not press the bag-of-words concession at `:2230` and could have. The manuscript explicitly acknowledges that path-independence is *necessary but not sufficient* for language-modeling success — a bag-of-words model has trivial path-independence yet poor language-modeling performance. Sub-claim (c) does not require flatness to be the *whole* explanation; the hypothesis text at `:2226` reads "transformers succeed *in part* because their flat-bundle structure is matched to this property." Red's COGS/SCAN OOD failures and the bag-of-words observation are both consistent with this weaker reading: flatness is a contributing inductive bias matched to one component of language (the compositional core); other architectural choices (depth, attention non-linearity, embedding dimension) and other linguistic phenomena (pragmatics, deixis, anaphora) account for the residual on both sides. A red counter that would land would have to show flatness *anti-correlates* with language-modeling success — not that it is insufficient.

### Falsifiers re-stated

Sub-claim (a) is falsified if the dispersion of $\Omega_{ij^{(m)}} \Omega_{j^{(m)} k}$ across paraphrase-invariant relays is large for compositional content, measured on a controlled corpus with explicit paraphrase annotations. Sub-claim (c) is falsified if an ablation that preserves all transformer machinery but breaks the flat-bundle structure (e.g., by injecting an explicit non-zero curvature term into the attention computation in a controlled architectural variant such as a non-Abelian holonomy attention head) leaves language-modeling perplexity unchanged or improves it. The framework's own parameterization cannot instantiate the second ablation, as red correctly observed — but a published ablation in a non-gauge architectural family would speak to (c) directly, and the absence of such a result in the current literature is a state of underconfirmation, not of refutation.
