# Red Opening — disc-flat-bundle-language

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest form of the claim is this: among all the architectural choices that distinguish neural language models, the cocycle property $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}=\Omega_{ik}$ implemented by per-token-frame transport encodes a non-trivial, content-sensitive form of context-invariance that aligns with the Fregean–Montagovian compositional core of natural language, and this alignment is a contributing cause — not the sole cause — of transformers' empirical effectiveness, with the carve-out for irony, deixis, and pragmatics acknowledged as a known residual rather than a fatal exception.

## Position

The §8.3 working hypothesis at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2226` is not a scientific claim under the canonical Popperian criterion because (i) it has no operational definition of "compositional-semantic component" — the manuscript itself concedes this at `:2228`; (ii) its central architectural mechanism, the cocycle $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}\Omega_{ki}=I$, holds *by construction* of $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ at `:2224` and is therefore not a feature that can be present or absent in the user's framework; and (iii) the only empirical operationalization it points to — compositional generalization benchmarks COGS and SCAN — yields the opposite sign to sub-claim (c): standard transformers *fail* systematic compositional generalization without architectural intervention, which is direct counter-evidence to the claim that transformers' flat-bundle structure is a contributing cause of their success on compositional language. The combination of (i), (ii), and (iii) makes the hypothesis simultaneously unfalsifiable in its general form and falsified on its sole offered operational criterion.

## Evidence

- **Popperian falsifiability** [Popper1959, *Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §6 "Falsifiability as a Criterion of Demarcation"]: a statement that lacks a class of basic statements that could contradict it is not empirical science. The manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:2228` writes that it frames §8.3 as a working hypothesis "rather than a formal conjecture, since the key concept of 'compositional-semantic component' currently lacks an operational definition that would permit clean falsification." That sentence is a primary-source admission that the claim as stated does not yet meet the demarcation criterion. Until an operational definition is supplied, sub-claim (a) is not falsifiable, and the manuscript itself acknowledges this.

- **Cocycle is structural, not empirical** [Nakahara2003, *Geometry, Topology and Physics*, §10.4 "Flat connections and holonomy"]: a connection is flat iff its curvature 2-form vanishes, and on a discrete graph this is captured by $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}\Omega_{ki}=I$ around every closed triangle. The user's parameterization $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ at `:2224` makes the triple product telescope to the identity: $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)\cdot\exp(\phi_j)\exp(-\phi_k)\cdot\exp(\phi_k)\exp(-\phi_i)=I$. This is an algebraic identity, not a fact about language. Sub-claim (c) at `:2228` — that transformers succeed *because* their flat-bundle structure matches a property of language — therefore conflates two distinct things: (1) the architectural fact that *any* parameterization of $\Omega$ as a coboundary $g_i g_j^{-1}$ is flat by elementary group-theoretic identity, and (2) the linguistic fact that natural language semantics is flat-transport-like. The architectural fact is true by definition for both the user's framework and standard transformers; the linguistic fact has no canonical formal-semantics statement. The proposed falsification at `:2232` — train a non-flat gauge architecture and measure holonomy — is ill-posed within the user's framework, because the framework derives $\Omega$ from per-token frames and so cannot instantiate a non-cocycle parameterization without leaving the framework. Once you leave the framework, the negative result tells you nothing about the original hypothesis.

- **The only proposed operational criterion goes the wrong way** [Kim & Linzen 2020, "COGS: A Compositional Generalization Challenge Based on Semantic Interpretation," EMNLP, Table 2; Lake & Baroni 2018, "Generalization without Systematicity," ICML, §4]. The manuscript at `:2228` offers COGS and SCAN as the operational handle for "compositional semantics." On both benchmarks, vanilla transformer architectures fail systematic compositional generalization in the absence of architectural augmentation (specialized attention biases, auxiliary losses, or data augmentation). Kim & Linzen report transformer exact-match accuracy on COGS structural generalization splits dropping to roughly 35% from near-perfect IID performance; Lake & Baroni report comparable collapse on SCAN length and primitive splits. If transformer success on language *were* causally explained by their flat-bundle structure matching a path-independent compositional-semantic core, the same architectures should also succeed on the benchmark designed to measure compositional semantics. They do not. This is direct counter-evidence to sub-claim (c).

- **No formal-semantics canon establishes the bridge** [Montague1970, "Universal Grammar," *Theoria* 36; Partee1984, "Compositionality"]. The Principle of Compositionality says the meaning of a complex expression is a function of the meanings of its parts and their syntactic composition. It does not say that the meaning function is invariant under choice of relay context through intermediate tokens. The user's reframing of compositionality as "path-independent gauge transport on a token graph" is a *novel construction*, not a reading of existing canon, and as the methodology requires for novel constructions, it needs a derivation from canonical primitives — which the manuscript does not supply.

- **Sub-claim (b) is teleological without derivation.** "Communicative systems face functional pressure toward path-independence" at `:2228` is, by the manuscript's own admission, "a plausibility argument, not a derivation." Standard information-theoretic treatments of communication [Shannon1948, *A Mathematical Theory of Communication*] give no functional-pressure argument for path-independent semantic transport on a discrete graph; they give a channel-capacity argument that says nothing about the cocycle structure of meaning composition. Worse, the counter-evidence is everywhere: deixis, anaphora, irony, indexicality, and pragmatic inference are functional, abundant, and explicitly path-dependent — and conceded as such at `:2232`. The carve-out for a "compositional-semantic core" that excludes these phenomena is not motivated by any linguistic boundary in the canon; it is an ad-hoc rescue.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds:

1. Blue produces an operational, non-circular definition of "compositional-semantic component" — one that does not presuppose a flat bundle, does not reduce to "whatever benchmark accuracy is high on," and admits a class of basic statements that could empirically contradict the hypothesis. If such a definition exists, sub-claim (a) becomes falsifiable and the Popper attack collapses.

2. Blue exhibits a primary-source result from formal semantics or theoretical linguistics that derives path-independence of compositional meaning from independent linguistic principles — i.e., a Montague-style or type-theoretic statement that compositional content factors through a flat-bundle-like structure on context. If such a derivation exists in the canon, the "novel construction without canonical primitive" attack weakens.

3. Blue demonstrates that the cocycle $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk}=\Omega_{ik}$ is *not* algebraically forced by $\Omega_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ — for example, by exhibiting a per-token-frame parameterization in the user's framework that yields non-trivial triple-product holonomy. If the cocycle is shown to encode empirical content rather than a coboundary identity, the "structural, not empirical" attack collapses.

4. Blue cites a published empirical result in which transformers succeed on a controlled compositional-generalization benchmark *because of* an identifiable flat-bundle property of their attention transport, with a non-flat ablation that demonstrably degrades performance. The COGS/SCAN counter-evidence is rebutted only by counter-evidence at the same operational level.

If none of (1)–(4) is supplied, the §8.3 hypothesis at `:2226` should be marked as unfalsifiable-in-its-general-form and unsupported-on-its-sole-operational-criterion, with sub-claim (c) actively contradicted by the cited benchmark literature.
