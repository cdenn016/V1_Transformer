# Blue Rebuttal — subclaim-B-degenerate-sigma

## Concession

I grant red's narrowest point: the paragraph header at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1026` ("Deterministic Beliefs via Scaled Limit"), the §5.7 phrase "deterministic, isotropic limit" at `:1957`, and the §5.7 enumeration "successive limits: (i) isotropic beliefs with σ⁻² absorbed into learned projections" at `:1962` are stylistic infelicities. A reader who stops at the title obtains a wrong impression — the body uses `q_i = N(μ_i, σ² I)` with `σ² > 0` finite (`:1040`), which is not a Dirac measure, and the operation at `:1252` is in operative content a reparameterization, not an analytic limit. A clean revision would replace the section header with "Deterministic Beliefs via Implicit-Variance Reparameterization" and would rewrite `:1962` to read "Under the successive reductions: (i) isotropic beliefs with `σ⁻²` absorbed into learned projections..." This is a labeling defect in the manuscript and the author should fix it.

That concession does not survive contact with the claim. Sub-claim B asserts something about the **operation**, not about its name.

## Core attack

Red's load-bearing move is the third bullet of its evidence list (red opening, §"External canon — limit vs reparameterization distinction") and falsification condition (2): the assertion that `(σ, Ω) → σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` is "many-to-one in the forward direction" and therefore "not a reparameterization in the canon-standard bijective sense." This is the move on which red's entire position depends — without it, the section-header objection collapses into a stylistic note about naming, which red itself concedes is consistent with a rescaled-KL reading (red opening, second bullet on `:1114`).

The "bijective only" definition of reparameterization is wrong on the canon red invokes. Amari's information geometry treats reparameterization as a coordinate change on a statistical manifold, and explicitly admits **quotient parameterizations** when the original coordinate system is redundant under a symmetry group [Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2 §2.5 "Affine connections and coordinate transformations"; Amari 1998, "Natural Gradient Works Efficiently in Learning," §4 on the parameter-space metric being defined modulo the kernel of the Fisher information]. In gauge theory the canonical analogue is even more explicit: gauge-fixing a redundant parameterization is itself a change of parameterization that quotients out the gauge orbit [Nakahara 2003, §10.1 on gauge-equivalence classes; Peskin & Schroeder 1995, §9.4 on the Faddeev-Popov procedure as a reparameterization onto the gauge-fixed slice]. None of these treat "many-to-one in the forward direction" as disqualifying.

The operation in §5.2.2 is a quotient map onto the orbit space of the scaling action `(σ, Ω) ↔ (cσ, c² Ω)` for `c > 0` (which is precisely the redundancy red identifies as a falsification condition (2)). The fibre `{(σ, Ω) : σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ = M}` is exactly a one-parameter family, parametrized by the positive scalar `c`, and the operation projects onto the orbit space. On the orbit space, the map is bijective. Red's falsification condition (2) is therefore satisfied by the canonical structure of the operation — red itself wrote the condition that breaks its own attack.

Red's secondary move is to parse `:1254` ("the full limit need not be taken") as "the grammar of a partial limit, not the grammar of a change of variables." This is a reading of English prose, not of mathematics. The mathematical content of `:1252`–`:1254` is `M := σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ`, with `M` absorbed into `W_Q W_K^T`, with `σ` finite throughout. The verbal hedge "the full limit need not be taken" is the author's accommodation to the reader who came in via the section header expecting a limit; it is not a mathematical operator. Red's preferred phrasing ("no limit is required; the substitution `M := σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` is bijective on `GL(d_k)` for any finite `σ > 0`") is a recommendation for cleaner prose — it is not evidence that the operation is anything other than a substitution at finite `σ`. The substitution at `:1252` is the operative mathematics; the section header at `:1026` is decoration on top of it.

Red's third move conflates two different objects (red opening, bullet on `:1114`). The rescaled-KL `D̃_KL := σ²·D_KL` is a *consequence* of the absorption — it shows that the rescaled object has a finite `σ → 0` limit equal to `½‖Ω⁻¹μ_i − μ_j‖²`. The operation at `:1252` does not invoke that limit; it absorbs `σ⁻²` into the learned matrices at finite `σ`. Both readings — analytic limit of the rescaled KL, and finite-`σ` reparameterization — terminate at the same Mahalanobis-quadratic expression, because that expression is what `σ²·D_KL` equals when `Σ_i = Σ_j = σ² I` for finite `σ`, not only in the `σ → 0` limit. The expression `σ²·D_KL(N(μ_i, σ²I) ‖ Ω·N(μ_j, σ²I)) = ½‖Ω⁻¹μ_i − μ_j‖² + O(σ²·tr-and-log-terms)` is exact for finite `σ` up to terms that depend on `tr(Ω⁻ᵀΩ⁻¹) − K` and `log|Ω⁻ᵀΩ⁻¹|`, both of which are mean-independent and therefore absorbed into the softmax normalization. This is the substantive content of `:1252` and it does not require `σ → 0`.

Red's claim that "either `τ` is being silently re-scaled and the manuscript needs to track that, or `:1114`'s `σ → 0` is a non sequitur" is a false dichotomy. The third reading — that `:1114` is a sanity check showing the rescaled KL has a well-defined limit, while the actual reduction at `:1252` operates at finite `σ` — is the reading sub-claim B endorses and is consistent with both lines. The `τ = κ√K` of the softmax already absorbs the `σ²` factor: the manuscript's softmax temperature at `:1252` is whatever positive number renders the absorbed `σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` into the standard `W_Q W_K^T`, and that absorption is the *content* of the reparameterization, not a tracking error.

## Defense

Sub-claim B asserts three things: (i) the operation is not an analytic `Σ → 0` limit, (ii) it is a literal change of parameterization in which `σ⁻²` and `Ω⁻ᵀ` appear jointly and are absorbed into `W_Q W_K^T`, and (iii) the reduction is exact for finite `σ > 0`. Each is independently verifiable from the manuscript's own text.

For (i), `:1030`–`:1036` ("Naively setting `Σ_i → 0` yields Dirac delta beliefs ... `D_KL(δ(k − μ_i) ‖ δ(k − μ_j)) = +∞` for `μ_i ≠ μ_j`. This divergence reflects the absolute continuity requirement in the definition of KL") is the manuscript's own disavowal of the analytic limit. The canon citation `[Kullback & Leibler 1951; Amari & Nagaoka 2000 Ch. 2]` is on point: KL between distinct Diracs is undefined or `+∞`. Red does not contest (i).

For (ii), `:1252` ("σ⁻² and Ω⁻ᵀ always appear together in the combination σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ. The learned matrices `W_Q, W_K` can then be considered to parametrize this combined quantity directly, rendering σ an implicit (finite) scale factor absorbed into the learned weights") is a textbook reparameterization in the Amari-quotient sense. The manuscript writes the substitution explicitly. Red's objection is not to the substitution itself but to whether "reparameterization" is the right word for a many-to-one substitution — and that objection, as shown above, fails on the canon definition.

For (iii), `:1112` ("The rescaled KL divergence `D̃_KL(q_i ‖ Ω_{ij}q_j) := σ²·D_KL(q_i ‖ Ω_{ij}q_j) → ½‖Ω_{ij}⁻¹μ_i − μ_j‖²` as `σ → 0` remains finite") plus the explicit finite-`σ` computation in the surrounding paragraphs establishes that the Mahalanobis term is the dominant piece of `σ²·D_KL` for any `σ > 0`, with subleading trace-and-log corrections that are mean-independent and absorbed into the softmax normalization. The manuscript at `:1133` independently certifies the full-`KL` identity has been "verified symbolically against the direct Gaussian KL to machine precision" — this is canon-grade sympy validation, the strongest evidence category in `theory` mode per `debate_methodology.md` §"math mode."

The standing of the section header is a separate matter from the standing of the operation. Red's opening argues correctly that the header is a category error against its own body — I conceded that. But the debate is whether sub-claim B accurately describes the operation, and the operation is the substitution at `:1252`, not the title at `:1026`. Sub-claim B says "the manuscript's claim at line 1252 ('the full limit need not be taken') correctly characterizes the operation"; it does not say "the section title at line 1024 correctly characterizes the operation." The two are separable, and red's evidence attacks the second while sub-claim B defends the first.

The decisive evidence is `:1252` itself, read together with `:1036`–`:1038`. The manuscript states the literal limit is ill-defined, then performs a substitution at finite `σ`. That substitution is what sub-claim B calls "literal change of parameterization." Under Amari's broad definition of reparameterization and the standard gauge-quotient construction, that nomenclature is canon-appropriate. The section title's "Scaled Limit" framing is a separable wart that should be patched in revision, but it does not change the mathematical content of the operation, and it does not invalidate sub-claim B's characterization of `:1252`.

This position is wrong if any of the following hold. First, if the operation at `:1252` actually requires `σ → 0` to reach the Mahalanobis-quadratic form (it does not — the form is exact for finite `σ` up to mean-independent terms absorbed by softmax). Second, if the canon definition of "reparameterization" strictly requires bijective forward maps with no quotient construction (it does not — Amari, Nakahara, and Peskin-Schroeder all admit quotient/projection reparameterizations). Third, if the section header at `:1026` is load-bearing for the reduction (it is not — the substitution is at `:1252`, not at `:1026`). Fourth, if the resulting softmax-of-Mahalanobis formula differs under the reparameterization reading versus the rescaled-KL-limit reading (it does not — both produce `softmax(−‖Q − K Ω⁻¹‖²/(2τ))` with the same `τ` modulo a normalization-absorbed constant).
