# Red Opening — subclaim-B-degenerate-sigma

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §5.2.2 reduction is mathematically a bijective change of variables `(σ, Ω) → M := σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` valid for any finite `σ > 0`, the manuscript explicitly disavows the literal `Σ → 0` limit at `GL(K)_attention.tex:1036–1038`, and line 1254's hedge "the full limit need not be taken" — read together with `:1114` (`σ²·D_KL → ½‖Ω⁻¹μ_i − μ_j‖²` as `σ → 0`) — coherently characterizes the operation as a reparameterization in which the algebraically well-defined rescaled-KL is what carries through to the softmax.

## Position

The claim is **false as stated** on two falsifiable grounds: (a) the manuscript's own naming at `:1026` ("Deterministic Beliefs via Scaled Limit"), at `:1957` ("the deterministic, isotropic limit"), and at `:1962` ("successive limits: (i) isotropic beliefs with σ⁻² absorbed into learned projections") frames the operation as a limit, not a reparameterization, and the beliefs `q_i = N(μ_i, σ² I)` with `σ² > 0` finite (`:1040`) are not deterministic in any KL-canonical sense; (b) line 1254's locution "the full limit need not be taken" is the grammar of a partial limit, not the grammar of a change of variables — the claim that this "correctly characterizes the operation" as a reparameterization is contradicted by the manuscript's own text three sections later at `:1962`, which still calls (i) a limit.

## Evidence

- **Section header at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1026`** — `\paragraph{Deterministic Beliefs via Scaled Limit.}` The header names both the operation ("Scaled Limit") and the resulting beliefs ("Deterministic"). The beliefs that actually appear in the body of the section are `q_i = N(μ_i, σ² I)` with `σ² > 0` (line 1040). A Gaussian with strictly positive variance is, by definition, not a deterministic (Dirac) measure. The section's title is therefore a category error against its own body. There is no rescued reading: "Deterministic Beliefs" cannot refer to finite-variance Gaussians.

- **Manuscript admission of ill-definition at `:1030–1036`** — The text states that `D_KL(δ(k−μ_i) ‖ δ(k−μ_j)) = +∞` for `μ_i ≠ μ_j`, citing the absolute-continuity requirement of KL `[KullbackLeibler1951, AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]` (canon `external_canon_math.md` §1: "Undefined when q(x) > 0 and p(x) = 0 (or returns +∞)"). The "remedy" introduced at `:1038` ("joint scaling limit where the belief variance σ² remains finite but is absorbed into learned parameters") is a reparameterization in operative content but is still introduced under the noun phrase "joint scaling **limit**." This is the manuscript using the word "limit" to denote an operation that is not a limit.

- **`:1114` — the rescaled-KL limit is genuinely a limit, but a different object** — `D̃_KL := σ² · D_KL → ½ ‖Ω⁻¹ μ_i − μ_j‖²` as `σ → 0`. This *is* an analytic limit and it *is* finite, but the object whose limit is being taken is `σ² · D_KL`, not `D_KL`. Substituting `σ² D_KL` for `D_KL` inside the softmax `exp(−D_KL/τ)` rescales the temperature by `σ²`. So either: (i) the operation is a `σ → 0` limit of the rescaled KL — in which case `τ` is being silently re-scaled and the manuscript needs to track that; or (ii) the operation is a reparameterization at finite `σ`, in which case `:1114`'s `σ → 0` is a non sequitur and the manuscript should not invoke it as the justification for the reduction. The two readings are not interchangeable. Sub-claim B forces the second reading and ignores that the manuscript reaches for the first at `:1114`.

- **`:1254` — "the full limit need not be taken"** — The phrase presupposes a partial limit *is* being taken. A pure change of variables would read "no limit is required; the substitution `M := σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` is bijective on `GL(d_k)` for any finite `σ > 0`." The manuscript does not say that. It says "the full limit need not be taken," which is the grammar of "we take a limit, but not all the way." Sub-claim B's gloss "correctly characterizes the operation" therefore misreads the manuscript's own framing as cleanly reparameterizational when it is in fact framed as a softened limit.

- **`:1962` — Summary still calls (i) a limit** — The Summary §5.7 lists the reduction as: "Under the successive **limits**: (i) isotropic beliefs with σ⁻² absorbed into learned projections, ..." The word "limits" is the manuscript's own diagnostic. If sub-claim B were correct that the operation is a reparameterization rather than a limit, the manuscript would not name (i) as a limit in its own summary. The §5.7 framing is incompatible with the reparameterization reading sub-claim B endorses.

- **`:1957` — "deterministic, isotropic limit (Σ_p^c → σ² I, φ_c absorbed)"** — This is the same category error compounded: the parenthetical `Σ_p^c → σ² I` is a substitution to finite-`σ²` covariance, *not* a deterministic limit (which would be `Σ_p^c → 0`). The manuscript writes the substitution but names it the deterministic limit. Sub-claim B inherits this naming and asserts it "correctly characterizes the operation"; on canon, it does not.

- **External canon — limit vs reparameterization distinction** — `external_canon_math.md` §1 (KL properties, `[AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]`) treats KL as a function of two distributions; reparameterization invariance of KL `[Amari1998 §4]` is invariance under a bijective change of the underlying variable, not a relabeling of the divergence's value. The map `(σ, Ω) → σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` is **many-to-one** in the forward direction (multiple pairs collapse to the same `M`), as the evidence pack notes (item 3 of "What this evidence does NOT settle"). A many-to-one collapse is not a bijective reparameterization. The claim's characterization "literal change of parameterization" therefore overstates the structure: the operation is a *projection* `(σ, Ω) ↦ M`, not a reparameterization in the canon-standard bijective sense.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. The manuscript explicitly states at some line we have not located that "the operation in §5.2.2 is a change of variables, not a limit; the section header is a stylistic infelicity to be corrected." If such an erratum or footnote exists, the claim's reading of the manuscript is the author's own and the framing is not a misnomer but a known imprecision.

2. The map `(σ, Ω) → σ⁻² Ω⁻ᵀ` is in fact bijective onto its image under a suitable quotient (e.g., modulo the scaling action `(σ, Ω) ↔ (cσ, c² Ω)` for `c > 0`), and this quotient is the canonical parameterization the manuscript intends. If so, "literal change of parameterization" is defensible at the level of the quotient, and the many-to-one objection collapses.

3. The §5.7 summary's use of "limits" is a metonym for "successive reductions" with at least one author-acknowledged non-limit, and standard mathematical writing tolerates that metonym in expository contexts. (Canon position would need to support that reading; on `external_canon_math.md` §1 and `external_canon_math.md` §4 — "If [a claim] differs and does not reduce to standard, or claims standard-equivalence without proof: novel claim. The manuscript must label it as novel and provide independent justification" — the metonym reading is the one that needs justification, not the literal one.)

4. The "deterministic beliefs" naming is a fixed term of art elsewhere in the gauge / FEP literature for finite-variance Gaussians whose variance has been absorbed into a precision-weighted projection. We have not located such a usage; if blue cites one (e.g., a Friston or Da Costa paper using "deterministic limit" for `σ⁻²` absorption at finite `σ`), the category-error objection weakens.
