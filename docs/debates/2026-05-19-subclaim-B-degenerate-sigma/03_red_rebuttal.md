## Core attack

The claim under debate asserts two things, and blue's opening defends only the first while conceding the second. The first is operational: the algebra at finite `σ` is a reparameterization, not an analytic limit. The second is textual: the manuscript "correctly characterizes the operation." These are not equivalent, and the second fails on blue's own evidence.

**Attack 1 — "literal change of parameterization" requires bijectivity, which blue's evidence denies.** The claim's exact wording at `00_claim.md` line 11 is "a **literal change of parameterization** in which `σ^{-2}` and `Ω^{-T}` always appear together as `σ^{-2}Ω^{-T}` and are jointly absorbed into the learned projections `W_Q W_K^T`." A change of parameterization in the textbook sense is a bijection between coordinate charts on the same parameter manifold; absent bijectivity, the operation is a *projection* or *quotient*, not a reparameterization. The evidence pack at `01_evidence.md` lines 74 and 86 records that `(σ, Ω) → σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` is "many-to-one in the forward direction" — multiple `(σ, Ω)` pairs collapse to the same `M`. Blue's own falsification condition 4 acknowledges this: "any `(λσ, λ²Ω⁻ᵀ)`-type rescaling produces the same `M` after redefinition" (`02_blue_opening.md:37`). A many-to-one map is by definition not a change of parameterization; it is a *quotient* that absorbs a one-dimensional scaling redundancy. The claim's word choice ("literal change of parameterization") is therefore inaccurate independent of the section-title question.

**Attack 2 — the §5.7 summary frames the construction as "successive limits," not "successive reparameterizations."** The canonical recap of the construction at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1958` reads "Under the successive limits: (i) isotropic beliefs with σ⁻² absorbed into learned projections, (ii) constant gauge (Ω_{ij} = Ω for all pairs), and (iii) learned gauge via projections..." The summary explicitly calls the operation a *limit*. Blue argues at `02_blue_opening.md:23` that this is "consistent with the reparameterization reading" because limit (i) names "absorption." That reading does not survive the syntax: the head noun is "successive limits"; "absorption" is the gerund inside the description of limit (i). The summary's grammar puts the reparameterization *inside* a limit, not as a substitute for it. The claim's wording "the manuscript correctly characterizes the operation" collides with the summary's own characterization.

**Attack 3 — blue's pre-concession is load-bearing.** At `02_blue_opening.md:43`, blue concedes "the section header at `:1026` is stylistically imperfect. 'Deterministic Beliefs via Scaled Limit' puts the word 'Limit' in the title while the operative line at `:1254` says 'the full limit need not be taken.' A reader who stops at the header gets a different mental picture than a reader who reaches `:1254`." This concession is dispositive for the textual half of the claim. The claim asserts the manuscript "correctly characterizes the operation." If the section header — the primary signpost for the operation — is "stylistically imperfect" and produces a "different mental picture" than the operative line, then the manuscript does *not* correctly characterize the operation; it characterizes it two different ways and the two are in tension. Blue tries to repair this by labeling the title "stylistic packaging," but a textual claim about correctness cannot be repaired by relegating the offending text to stylistic register. Either the section header is part of the manuscript's characterization or it is not. Blue's own concession says it is — and that it conflicts with `:1252`.

## Defense

My opening's load-bearing assumption was that "literal change of parameterization" is a precise mathematical statement and that the manuscript's section-title language at `:1024` ("Scaled Limit") together with the §5.7 recap at `:1958` ("successive limits") shows the manuscript itself does not treat the operation as a reparameterization throughout. Blue's defense rests on two moves: (i) the operational algebra is finite-σ; (ii) the title is loose but the math is exact. Neither move addresses the load-bearing assumption.

On (i): finite-σ execution is necessary but not sufficient to make the operation a *bijective* change of parameterization. The σ-scaling redundancy `(σ, Ω) ↔ (λσ, λ²Ω⁻ᵀ)` collapses to the same `M`, as blue acknowledges. The right name for "quotient by a one-parameter scaling action followed by absorbing the orbit representative into a learned matrix" is *gauge fixing followed by absorption into a learned parameter*. The standard transformer's `W_Q W_K^T` is the orbit representative; the gauge-theoretic `(σ, Ω)` pair is one lift back into the cover. This is structurally identical to projective-gauge fixing in physics (`external_canon_math.md` on bundle reductions and gauge fixing — and the project's own [Nakahara2003 §10.3] canonical citation for transport/gauge structure). It is not a reparameterization.

On (ii): the claim's text is not "the math is exact." It is "the manuscript correctly characterizes the operation." Blue's own pre-concession at `02_blue_opening.md:43` admits the section header gives a "different mental picture" than `:1252`. Two mental pictures that differ cannot both be "correct characterizations" of the same operation; at most one is, and blue concedes the header is the imperfect one. The §5.7 recap at `:1958` reinforces the header's framing ("successive limits"), not `:1252`'s ("the full limit need not be taken"). On the question of which framing the manuscript treats as canonical, the votes are 2 (title at `:1024`, summary at `:1958`) to 1 (joint-absorption line at `:1252`). The claim picks the minority framing and calls it the manuscript's characterization.

**Falsifiable form of my position, sharpened:** the claim fails if either (a) "change of parameterization" requires bijectivity and `(σ, Ω) → σ⁻²Ω⁻ᵀ` is many-to-one, or (b) the manuscript's own section title at `:1024` and §5.7 summary at `:1958` collectively frame the operation as a limit rather than a reparameterization. Both are checked. (a) is conceded by blue at `02_blue_opening.md:37`. (b) is conceded by blue at `02_blue_opening.md:43` and is reinforced by the §5.7 summary's "successive limits" wording at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1958`. The claim's text is overreached: the operational math is sound, but the textual claim that the manuscript "correctly characterizes the operation" is contradicted by the manuscript's own inconsistent framing — an inconsistency blue grants.
