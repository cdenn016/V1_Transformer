# Blue Opening — pifb-spec-ext-signature-collective

## Steelman (opposing position)

The "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra" framing at line 2745 stretches the Wick analogy past its load-bearing standard usage (Wick 1954; Zinn-Justin 2002 §3 continues a real base coordinate to imaginary, producing a real Euclidean metric), the real-part projection at 2766 has no Wick counterpart and is admitted as such at 2771, and the subsection's title and surrounding rhetoric imply Lorentzian (1+3) selection that the construction does not derive (it reaches arbitrary (1, n-1) signature and is silent on 2+2 vs 1+3); these residual rhetorical leaks make the signature subsection underregistered relative to what is actually proved.

## Position

The signature/collective-geometry subsections (lines 2698-2868) are correctly calibrated: every load-bearing postulate is explicitly named at the point of use, the Regime I status is registered before the construction proceeds, the alternative causal-cone route at sec:causal_cone_route is presented with a disjoint postulate set rather than as an independent derivation, and the consensus metric is registered as a heuristic target conditional on a regulator. Two narrow editorial tightenings are available (relabel "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra" → "imaginary-frame postulate with no Wick counterpart for the real-part projection"; add a cross-reference at 2864 to the Gauge Invariance as Consensus discussion), but these do not alter any mathematical claim and do not constitute a calibration failure.

## Evidence

- **Sector split (line 2701-2702):** "the gauge action that transports beliefs between agents... is restricted to Ω ∈ GL(K, R). A genuinely complex Ω would render Ω Σ Ω^T non-Hermitian and complex valued, and the standard Gaussian Kullback-Leibler formula evaluated on such an object returns a complex number rather than a real divergence; numerical verification at K=2 and K=3 ... produces a sandwich with imaginary part of the same order as its real part and a formal Gaussian KL with both a nonzero imaginary component and a negative real part." This is the right boundary: complex transports break KL nonnegativity and the F ≥ 0 inequality, so the complexification is confined to the connection sector. The numerical check is concrete, not aspirational. The canonical reference for the GL(K,R) sandwich on real SPD covariances preserving definiteness is Sylvester's law of inertia (Horn-Johnson 2013, *Matrix Analysis*, §4.5.8); the manuscript invokes it at 2716 explicitly.

- **Regime I admission (line 2708):** "the field strength F_{μν} remains identically zero throughout. The signature mechanism operates not through F_{μν} but through the frame-twist quadratic form tr(A_μ A_ν), which is gauge-noninvariant ... and is therefore not a Yang-Mills kinetic invariant -- the genuine Yang-Mills action density tr(F_{μν}F^{μν}) vanishes identically here." The construction is honestly named: it is not a Yang-Mills derivation, and the gauge-noninvariance of tr(A_μ A_ν) is registered before it is used. The bilinear choice +tr(AB) on gl(K,C) versus -tr(AB) on a compact form (which would reverse all signs) is named at the same paragraph.

- **Worked-example postulate enumeration (line 2742-2771):** The four postulates are each explicitly labeled at the point of use:
  - "the imaginary assignment along the temporal direction is a postulate" (2745);
  - "T = diag(1, -1) ... is symmetric and traceless, hence T ∈ sl(2, R) and not in the compact so(2)" (2747);
  - "We now postulate that the temporal component is imaginary" (2751);
  - "We further restrict to the separable ansatz" (2756);
  - "The real-part projection is an additional choice, separate from Eq. eq:complex_gauge_frame ... Calling Eq. eq:lorentzian_metric the 'physical metric' requires both the imaginary gauge-frame postulate and the real-part projection. We flag this as a derivation gap" (2771).

  The sentence at 2771 — "The construction here is therefore not a direct Wick analog --- it is a Wick-like continuation in the Lie algebra plus an additional real-projection step that has no Wick counterpart" — discharges the Wick-analogy concern in the same paragraph that constructs the metric. Compare Hartle-Hawking 1983 (real Wick rotation on path-integral measure, real metric output) and Zinn-Justin 2002 §3 (real τ → iτ, real Euclidean metric): the manuscript does not claim correspondence with these; it claims structural analogy with explicit divergence.

- **Conformal-class observation (line 2771):** "The ±2 coefficients in Eq. eq:lorentzian_metric are artefacts of the unnormalized generator T = diag(1, -1) ... under the standard normalization tr(T^2) = 1 the coefficients become ∓1 ... The construction therefore fixes the metric up to a conformal class (the signature and the relative sign of the temporal and spatial coefficients are intrinsic; the overall scale and the absolute coefficient values track the choice of normalization of T and are not derived from the framework)." This is the correct epistemic register: a conformal class is a coordinate-invariant statement; a specific metric is not. Canonical reference: Hawking-Ellis 1973 §4 (causal cones determine conformal class, not metric); Penrose 1972 (conformal compactification).

- **Alternative causal-cone route (line 2785-2808):** The four postulates are enumerated at 2791 (shared τ, positive-definite h_ab, finite max speed c_I, inner-product not Finsler), with the shared τ explicitly registered as "substantive" because it tensions per-agent Fisher arc length elsewhere in the manuscript. The 2799 invocation of Sylvester's law is correct for a block-diagonal real symmetric form g = diag(-c_I^2, h_ab): the signature (1, dim Σ, 0) is intrinsic to g and invariant under coordinate change (Horn-Johnson 2013 §4.5.8). The 2804 tension paragraph is the load-bearing honesty: "The E-step natural-gradient update ... is the discrete-time Euler scheme for a first-order natural-gradient flow. Naive continuum limits of such flows are parabolic and yield infinite signal speed, so the causal-cone route does not apply directly to the current implementation. Three routes could in principle restore consistency. ... None of these is currently realized." This is exactly the right register for an existence proof with a disjoint postulate set, not a competing derivation.

- **Consensus-metric regulator dependence (line 2853, 2862):** "an honest gauge-orbit average would have to integrate over all maps g: C → G, not over a single copy of G. That is an infinite-dimensional functional integral over a space of gauge-group-valued fields and requires a gauge fixing or a regulator to be well-defined ... The non-compact SO(1,3) ⊂ GL(K, C) case carries the additional obstruction that the Haar measure is infinite even for constant g. We therefore retain the construction below as a heuristic for what gauge-invariant content the horizontal block could be reduced to under a chosen regulator, but explicitly do not claim it produces a finite, regulator-free gauge-invariant metric." This is the Faddeev-Popov / Gribov obstruction registered explicitly (Faddeev-Popov 1967; Gribov 1978). The non-compactness Haar-volume infinity follows directly from Helgason 1978 (Haar measure on non-compact reductive Lie groups is unbounded). The 2862 paragraph then re-registers the conditional status: "those statements should be read in this conditional sense: the construction is what such a structure *would* look like under a successful regulator, not a finite gauge-invariant observable that the present manuscript exhibits." This is the correct epistemic register; calling the consensus metric a finite observable would be the calibration failure, and the manuscript explicitly does not.

- **Connection-to-physical-gauge-invariance closing (line 2868):** "Whether specific subgroups are dynamically selected by free energy minimization remains an open question for future investigation." The selection problem is held open.

## Pre-empted strongest attack

The strongest red attack is that the 2745 phrase "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra rather than on the base coordinates" stretches the Wick label past what canonical Wick rotation (Wick 1954, Zinn-Justin 2002 §3) supports: standard Wick continues a real base coordinate to imaginary and produces a real metric, while the manuscript's construction continues a Lie-algebra element to imaginary and produces a complex bilinear form that requires an additional real-projection step with "no Wick counterpart" (2771). The pre-emption is that the 2771 paragraph explicitly states this divergence within the same subsection, in the same paragraph as the metric is constructed, and labels it a "derivation gap." Calibration requires that the divergence be named where it occurs; the manuscript does this. A one-clause editorial tightening at 2745 — replacing "equivalent to a Wick rotation performed inside the gauge frame rather than on the base coordinates" with "structurally analogous to a Wick rotation but performed inside the gauge frame, with an additional real-projection step that has no Wick counterpart (see paragraph below)" — would make the lead-in match the 2771 honest admission. This is an editorial improvement, not a calibration failure: the load-bearing math is unchanged and the disanalogy is already in the text.

## Falsification conditions

This defense is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **Sector split is not actually the boundary used.** If the manuscript elsewhere treats complex Ω as acting on the real Gaussian belief fiber (e.g., a covariance transport Ω Σ Ω^T with complex Ω flowing through the variational free energy F), then the 2701-2702 boundary is rhetorical rather than load-bearing, and the F ≥ 0 inequality is broken silently. Test: grep the codebase and the rest of the manuscript for any complex Ω flowing into a Gaussian KL computation.

2. **Worked example postulates are not all named.** If a load-bearing assumption in the 2742-2779 construction is used but not registered as a postulate — for example, the choice of T = diag(1, -1) as opposed to other non-compact generators with tr(T^2) > 0, or an implicit constant-coefficient assumption hidden in "the local frame group" claim at 2773 — then the postulate enumeration is incomplete and calibration fails.

3. **Causal-cone construction has a hidden postulate.** If the 2791-2796 derivation relies on a structural assumption beyond the four enumerated (shared τ, positive-definite h_ab, finite c_I, inner-product not Finsler) — for example, an implicit assumption that the cone is symmetric under τ → -τ, which would smuggle in time-orientation — then the disjoint-postulate-set claim is overstated.

4. **Sylvester invocation at 2799 fails as written.** If the form g_{μν} dc^μ dc^ν at 2796 is not block-diagonal in (τ, x^a) coordinates (i.e., if a cross term -c_I^2 dτ dx^a appears under the postulates), then Sylvester's law of inertia (Horn-Johnson 2013 §4.5.8) does not directly give signature (1, dim Σ) without further computation. The block-diagonal form follows from the inner-product postulate, but the dependency should be stated.

5. **Consensus metric regulator obstruction is finite.** If a finite, regulator-free gauge-orbit average over local g(c): C → G exists for some specific subgroup of GL(K, C) used in the construction (i.e., the Faddeev-Popov / Gribov obstruction can be sidestepped for the manuscript's specific G), then the 2853 hedging is overstated and the consensus metric could be promoted from "heuristic target" to "completed observable." Test: check whether any standard cohomological or BRST construction would give a finite gauge-orbit volume for the manuscript's specific G.

6. **The 2868 cross-reference omission is load-bearing.** If the Gauge Invariance as Consensus discussion subsection (debated separately, commit 68ebfec8) directly contradicts or weakens the 2864-2868 paragraph, then omitting the cross-reference is a substantive defect rather than an editorial one. Test: read the Discussion subsection and check for explicit contradictions or strengthenings.

If none of (1)-(6) hold, the subsections are correctly calibrated and the two editorial tightenings (Wick-rotation lead-in at 2745, cross-reference at 2864) are sufficient. If any of (1)-(6) hold, the calibration is broken in a localized way and the corresponding paragraph requires substantive revision.
