# Red Opening — pifb-spec-ext-signature-collective

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Signature and Collective Geometry subsections (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2698-2868`) are honestly calibrated because every load-bearing postulate -- the sector split, the imaginary frame component, the choice of $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ over $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$, the real-part projection, the shared-$\tau$ assumption, the regulator obligation on the gauge-orbit average -- is explicitly named within the subsection where it does work, with the subsection's framing repeatedly emphasizing "existence demonstration, not derivation" and "heuristic target rather than completed observable," so a reader cannot mistake the construction for a derivation of Lorentzian signature from variational dynamics.

## Position

The Signature/Collective Geometry block is materially well-hedged but is **not** fully calibrated: three localized framings still overreach the underlying mathematics. Specifically (R1) the line 2745 label "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra rather than on the base coordinates" mis-labels the construction, because standard Wick rotation produces a real metric on the base, whereas the construction here produces a complex-valued bilinear form that requires an additional real-part projection -- as the same paragraph at line 2771 explicitly admits has "no Wick counterpart"; (R2) the subsection title "Temporal Structure and the Signature Problem" and the line 2737 enumeration of "subgroup restriction to $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$" implies the construction selects the physical $(1,3)$ signature, but the construction reaches a generic Lorentzian conformal class $(1, n-1)$ for any $n$ and is silent on $2{+}2$ versus $1{+}3$ as line 2779 itself concedes; (R3) the closing subsubsection at 2864-2868 advances the "gauge invariance as cognitive-consensus requirement" hypothesis without inheriting any of the hedging carried by the parallel Discussion subsection at 3230-3302 (line 3233: "this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation"; line 3302: "Above all, this view may be unfalsifiable") and contains no cross-reference to `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus`.

## Evidence

- **Manuscript line `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2745`** -- "the imaginary assignment along the temporal direction is a postulate, equivalent to a Wick rotation performed inside the gauge frame rather than on the base coordinates. Where the standard physicist's prescription continues $\tau \to i\tau$ on the base manifold, the construction below continues $\phi_\tau \to i\phi_\tau$ in the Lie algebra." This is the load-bearing rhetorical analogy.

- **Manuscript line `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2771`** -- "The real-part projection is an additional choice, separate from Eq. (complex_gauge_frame): $G_{\mu\nu}$ as defined by the trace is genuinely complex-valued ... The construction here is therefore not a direct Wick analog -- it is a Wick-like continuation in the Lie algebra plus an additional real-projection step that has no Wick counterpart." The manuscript itself documents the disanalogy. The 2745 framing names the construction "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra"; the 2771 admission says the construction has a step with no Wick counterpart. The first framing is stronger than the construction supports.

- **Canon citation [Wick 1954, *Phys. Rev.* 96, 1124] and [Zinn-Justin 2002 §3]** -- Standard Wick rotation continues a real time coordinate $t \to -i\tau$ (or $\tau \to it$ in the inverse direction) on the base manifold. The resulting Euclidean action is real-valued; the metric is real throughout. There is no "real-part projection" step in the standard prescription. The same applies to [Hartle-Hawking 1983, *Phys. Rev. D* 28, 2960] for the gravitational path integral. The manuscript's construction is structurally different: it complexifies an element of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$, produces a complex-valued horizontal bilinear form $G_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu) \in \mathbb{C}$, and then defines $G^{\mathrm{Lor}}_{\mu\nu} := \mathrm{Re}(G_{\mu\nu})$. Calling this a "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra" elides the second step.

- **Manuscript line `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2779`** -- "The signature split $1{+}3$ is selected by the input choice (one imaginary direction); the construction does not currently distinguish $1{+}3$ from $2{+}2$ on dynamical grounds." The honest sentence is here. But the subsection's title is "Temporal Structure and the Signature **Problem**" -- the framing problem is the Lorentzian $(-,+,+,+)$ signature called out at 2712, not the existence of an arbitrary indefinite signature.

- **Manuscript line `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2737`** -- step 3 of the pathway is named as "Subgroup restriction to $\mathrm{SO}(1,3) \subset \mathrm{GL}(4, \mathbb{C})$." The construction at sec:worked_signature does not perform this restriction; it produces a generic indefinite-signature bilinear form whose local orthonormal-frame group at non-degenerate points is $\mathrm{O}(1, n-1)$ in $n$ dimensions for one imaginary direction, or $\mathrm{O}(2, n-2)$ for two imaginary directions. The line 2740 sentence "whether step 3 picks out $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ over other $\mathrm{SO}(p,q)$ subgroups is unresolved" is the correct hedge. The earlier framing (title, step-list) implies more than the worked example delivers.

- **Manuscript line `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2864-2868`** -- the Speculative Extensions closing paragraph: "Our cognitive-first perspective offers an alternative hypothesis: gauge invariance arises as a consistency requirement for multi-agent consensus." No "may be unfalsifiable" disclaimer, no "metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation" hedge, no cross-reference to `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus`.

- **Manuscript lines `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3233` and `:3302`** -- the parallel Discussion subsection opens with "Status: this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation ... may be unfalsifiable" and closes with "Above all, this view may be unfalsifiable. Any observed physics can be interpreted as reflecting cognitive constraints rather than external reality." The Speculative Extensions paragraph at 2864-2868 advances the same claim without these disclaimers, creating an internal calibration mismatch: the same hypothesis is hedged in one section and presented without hedge in the other.

- **Sylvester / Hawking-Ellis canon** -- [Sylvester 1852]; [Horn-Johnson 2013 §4.5.8]; [Hawking-Ellis 1973]. The line 2799 "by Sylvester's law" invocation establishes signature from the diagonal form $\mathrm{diag}(-c_\mathcal{I}^2, h_{ab})$ where $h_{ab}$ is positive definite. Sylvester's law gives the invariance of the count of positive/negative eigenvalues under non-singular real congruence, so the citation is courtesy-level rather than load-bearing. The signature of $\mathrm{diag}(-c^2, h)$ is determined directly by the definition. This is the smallest of the three concerns and is granted to blue as a defensible expository convenience, not a falsifying issue.

## Falsification conditions

This position (the subsection is not fully calibrated) is wrong if any of the following holds:

1. **R1 fails** if standard usage in the physics literature labels a continuation of a Lie-algebra element to imaginary with subsequent real-part projection of a complex bilinear form as "Wick rotation." A primary-source citation (a textbook or review using the phrase in this generalized sense) would defeat R1. The line 2771 in-manuscript admission "no Wick counterpart" makes this hard for blue to establish; even granting "Wick-like," the title language at 2745 is "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra," not "Wick-like continuation." If blue produces a canon citation supporting the looser usage, R1 is granted as cosmetic.

2. **R2 fails** if the manuscript explicitly states elsewhere in the subsection that the target signature is the **conformal class of indefinite metrics** rather than the physical $(1,3)$ Lorentzian signature, with $1{+}3$ selection deferred to a separate mechanism that the manuscript does not provide. Line 2779 carries this admission but it appears mid-construction rather than in the subsection title or opening paragraph; if the framing at 2698-2712 is read as carrying the 2779 disclaimer by reference, R2 reduces to a presentation issue.

3. **R3 fails** if cross-section coherence within the manuscript is not a calibration criterion under the debate's epistemic register, i.e. if the Speculative Extensions subsection at 2864-2868 is permitted to be read in isolation from the Discussion subsection at 3230-3302. The methodology document treats internal consistency as on-mode; the absence of cross-reference is therefore a real (though localized) miscalibration.

If R1, R2, and R3 all fail, the claim is correctly calibrated. If any one of R1, R2, R3 holds, the claim is RED_WINS_NARROW: specific edits to (a) downgrade "Wick rotation in the Lie algebra" to "Wick-like continuation with real-projection" or "imaginary-frame postulate with real-part projection" at line 2745, (b) explicitly state in the subsection opening that the target is the Lorentzian conformal class with $1{+}3$ selection deferred, and (c) insert a cross-reference at 2864-2868 to `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus` with the matching unfalsifiability disclaimer.
