## Core attack

Blue's defense of sub-claim B is load-bearing on a single move: the sympy verification (`02_blue_opening.md:27-40`) showing that the symbolic Gaussian forward-KL barycenter at Eq.~\ref{eq:meta_agent_sigma_barycenter} reduces to $\Sigma_I^* = \sum_i w_i(\Sigma_i + (\mu_i - \mu_I)(\mu_i - \mu_I)^\top)/\sum_i w_i$. That computation is correct. It does not defend the claim.

The compound claim asserts the meta-agent **definition** is coherent, where the meta-agent in the framework is the object actually constructed and detected — not the symbolic optimization target floated in Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_variational}. The manuscript at line 2127 states verbatim: "The implementation formula~\eqref{eq:meta_agent_sigma_impl} corresponds to neither [forward-KL barycenter nor moment-matched mixture nor precision-weighted product]: it is an implementation heuristic that agrees with the variational forward-KL barycenter at $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ when constituent post-transport means coincide" (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2127`). The implementation drops the cross-term $\sum_i w_i (\mu_i - \mu_I)(\mu_i - \mu_I)^\top$ that blue's sympy session produces as the unique forward-KL solution. Blue's own derivation therefore demonstrates the gap rather than closing it: the *only* condition under which Eq.~\ref{eq:meta_agent_sigma_impl} equals the forward-KL barycenter is post-transport mean coincidence, in which case the dispersion term vanishes identically and the formula is vacuous content.

Blue points to line 2114 ("heuristic and partial rather than a derivation") and treats labelling as sufficient. This conflates two distinct objects under the same name: the *variational* meta-agent of Eq.~\ref{eq:meta_agent_barycenter} (existence subject to the FE-improvement test of Eq.~\ref{eq:meta_agent_FE_criterion}) and the *operational* meta-agent of Eq.~\ref{eq:meta_agent_sigma_impl} that the threshold detector at line 2114 instantiates. Sub-claim B's wording — "the meta-agent definition ... is mathematically coherent" — has two referents. If the referent is the variational object, blue's sympy is fine but the manuscript's "meta-agent" in the simulations is a different object. If the referent is the operational object, blue's sympy is irrelevant because the object it solves for is not the one instantiated. The claim as written does not separate the two, and the manuscript at line 2127 confirms they diverge in the standard case (means do not coincide, otherwise the cluster would already be a single belief and meta-agent formation would be trivial). Under `external_canon_math.md §4`'s source-of-truth rule, calling the operational object a "gauge-covariant variational barycenter" while admitting on the next page that it solves neither the variational objective nor the moment-matched mixture nor the product-of-experts is precisely the labelling slippage the canon flags.

Compounding this, blue invokes the Karcher mean defense by appeal to "the framework's empirical work uses $G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$, where the cut-locus issue is bounded by the manifold diameter" (`02_blue_opening.md:42`). The diameter of $\mathrm{SO}(N)$ with bi-invariant metric is $\pi\sqrt{N/2}$ for the standard normalization, which exceeds the Karcher uniqueness radius $\pi/2$ for $N \geq 2$. `[Karcher1977 Thm. 1.2]` requires the support of the measure to lie in a convex normal ball of radius $< \pi/2$, not that the manifold be compact. The manuscript at line 2100 invokes the radius condition but does not show it is satisfied at runtime when constituents are formed by the detector $\Gamma > \Gamma_\min = 0.5$ (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2114`). The threshold $\Gamma_\min = 0.5$ corresponds to $C_q C_s P \geq 0.5$, which permits the average post-transport pairwise KL to be as large as $-\tau_q \log 0.5 \approx 0.69 \tau_q$ before formation is suppressed; this is unbounded above as $\tau_q$ grows and gives no guarantee that constituent frames $U_i, U_j$ satisfy $\|\log(U_i^{-1}U_j)\|_F < \pi/2$. Blue's defense omits this check.

## Defense

Against blue's defense of sub-claim C, my opening's load-bearing point stands. Blue concedes the RG-as-analogy framing is the manuscript's posture (`02_blue_opening.md:48`, citing line 2137), then argues that under the canon's source-of-truth rule the claim only requires labelling. This is the move the dispatch instruction flags as illegitimate: the compound claim asserts "well-motivated by the literature on the renormalization group `[Wilson1971, Cardy1996]`" — a positive claim about literature alignment — not "labels its departure from RG accurately." Those are not the same proposition.

`[Cardy1996 §3.1]` defines an RG transformation as a map $\mathcal{R}$ on the space of couplings whose iterates determine a flow with computable $\beta$-functions; the structural content RG provides is precisely the recursion $f_\beta$. `[Wilson1971 Eq. 3.3]` constructs the explicit block-spin transformation that yields $f_\beta$ for the Ising model. The manuscript at line 2139 states: "$\beta_{IJ}^{(s+1)} = f_\beta(\{\beta_{ij}^{(s)}\})$ ... where the functions $f_\beta$ and $f_\gamma$ depend on how informational coupling aggregates across scales" — i.e., the recursion that *is* the RG content is asserted to exist without being specified. Blue's appeal to "structural parallel" (`02_blue_opening.md:50`) cannot rescue this: a structural parallel to RG is a parallel to *something*, and the thing RG is — per Wilson and Cardy — is the explicit recursion that the manuscript declines to provide. The Mehta-Schwab and similar deep-learning-as-RG analogies blue might invoke either supply the recursion explicitly (Mehta-Schwab tie RBMs to Kadanoff block spins with an exhibited block-spin map) or label themselves analogies without claiming RG-literature motivation. The compound claim asks for both analogy status and full motivation by `[Wilson1971, Cardy1996]`, and the absence of $f_\beta$ blocks the latter.

On sub-claim A's residual issue, blue's defense of the dual-role gauge frame as "the discrete analog of edge modes / QRF / relational QM" (`02_blue_opening.md:19`) collapses under the manuscript's own concession at line 565. `[DonnellyFreidel2016 §2.1]` constructs edge modes as symplectic data localized at the *boundary* $\partial\Sigma$ between a region and its complement, with the edge-mode field transforming under the *boundary-localized* gauge subgroup; the bulk gauge action is fixed. The manuscript's $\phi_i: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathfrak{g}$ is a smooth bulk field on each agent's domain, not a boundary mode. Line 565 itself states: "Quantities like $\mathrm{tr}(A^{(i)}_\mu A^{(i)}_\nu)$ are not invariant under the full local gauge $g_i = g_i(c)$ that introduces a Maurer--Cartan derivative term, but are invariant under the constant-per-agent residual subgroup" (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:565`). The constant-per-agent residual subgroup is not the gauge group `[DonnellyFreidel2016]` works with; it is a global subgroup of a discrete product, not the local symmetry whose breaking by a boundary the edge-mode literature addresses. The "discrete analog" claim is therefore structural at best — and the manuscript itself concedes the analog breaks under the full local gauge. This makes the QRF / edge-mode citation interpretive rather than load-bearing for the dual-role construction.

The shared thread tying these three points together is that blue's defense systematically substitutes "the manuscript labels its departure" for "the literature motivates the construction." The compound claim contains the conjunction "*and* well-motivated by the literature," which is the proposition under debate. Honest labelling of novelty is a *prerequisite* for the claim being defensible, not a substitute for its second half. The manuscript discharges the labelling prerequisite; it does not discharge the motivation conjunct for sub-claims B (the implementation barycenter is not the variational barycenter and is not the moment-matched mixture and is not the product-of-experts — line 2127) or C (the RG recursion that is the literature's content is asserted but not provided — line 2139). The compound claim, as written, is therefore false on its second half even when its first half is granted.
