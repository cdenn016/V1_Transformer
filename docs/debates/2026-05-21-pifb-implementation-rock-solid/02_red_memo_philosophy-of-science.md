## Steelman of the claim from this lens

The strongest reading: §Implementation explicitly tags each weak claim as a weak claim. The Wheeler picture at line 2106 is qualified as "a toy model demonstrating possibility, not a claim about physical reality"; the RG framing at line 2197 is qualified as "RG-inspired rather than a literal RG analysis"; the IB Lagrangian at lines 2138 is qualified as "a research direction"; the dropped dispersion term at line 2153 is qualified as "leading-order approximation"; the threshold detector at line 2174 is qualified as "heuristic and partial rather than a derivation"; the simulator-vs-transport question at line 2284 is qualified as "not independently verified." Under a Lakatosian "honest scope" criterion, this is a well-behaved manuscript section: it states what it has established and what it has not, and a Popperian falsificationist should accept the demarcation.

## Strongest weakness from this lens

The disclosures are not honest scope statements; they are load-bearing escape hatches that erase the demonstration claim. The §Implementation section opens at line 2106 with "demonstrates that participatory-like dynamics are consistent with variational free energy minimization in the post-detection regime", and at line 2228 with "We expect, though do not directly measure in the single-seed run of Section~\ref{sec:results}, that meta-agents exhibit properties not present at lower scales. … The whole becomes qualitatively different from the sum of its parts." The first sentence is a demonstration claim ("we demonstrate"). The second sentence is an empirical-emergence claim ("the whole becomes qualitatively different") backed by an undisclosed expectation ("we expect, though do not directly measure"). These two cannot both be true: a demonstration claim that is conjoined with "we expect, though do not directly measure" is a non-demonstration. This is the **manuscript-as-authority circularity** pattern under a different name: the manuscript asserts an emergence proposition and cites itself ("the framework's structural expectations are…") rather than the experiment. The methodology's claim-drift criterion is explicit: "If the sur-rebuttal opens a new line of attack, it is malformed" — but here the *opening* opens a demonstration claim and the *implementation* closes it with an undisclosed expectation. The drift is internal to the section.

A second falsification-condition problem: the RG-inspired disclosure at line 2197 says "we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance beyond the parametric form." But line 2210 then writes down the scale-$(s+1)$ free-energy functional $S^{(s+1)}$ in exactly the same form as $S^{(s)}$, and line 2213 says "This scale invariance of the functional form suggests the framework may exhibit critical phenomena, fixed points, or universal behavior." This is **claim drift** in the strict sense — the disclosure at 2197 rules out exactly the claims the prose at 2210–2213 then proposes. Wilson's renormalization-group programme requires three ingredients ([Wilson1971 Phys. Rev. B 4, 3174–3183]; [Wilson1982 Nobel Lecture]; [Cardy1996 Ch. 3]): a rescaling transformation $\mathcal{R}_b$ acting on the action, iteration to find fixed points $\mathcal{R}_b S^* = S^*$, and linearization to compute critical exponents. The manuscript provides none of these and disclaims all three, then writes the scale-$(s+1)$ functional as if the iteration had been performed. "RG-inspired" cannot be a scope hedge that licenses writing down an RG iteration without verifying the iteration converges.

A third disclosure-vs-claim collision is at the consensus detector. Line 2174 self-discloses: "we do not establish that the detector's product form exactly tracks the variational-criterion savings even in the high-coherence limit." But line 2129 has already asserted "the savings scale as $\sim |I|(|I|-1)\varepsilon$, growing quadratically with cluster size, while $C(I)$ for an MDL-style choice grows much more slowly." The quadratic-scaling argument is what licenses the threshold detector as a surrogate. If the detector's product form is not established as tracking the savings, the quadratic-scaling argument is decoupled from the implementation. The manuscript has cited its own quadratic-scaling argument as the load-bearing motivation for the threshold detector while disclosing that the detector does not track the savings.

A fourth, the self-disclosed wound at line 2284: "Whether the released simulator code realizes the full transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ or a frame-trivial substitute is not independently verified in this manuscript; the simulator code release is deferred to a follow-up." The §Top-Down Participation subsection's central object is the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ at Eq. eq:topdown_priors (line 2247). If the simulator might be a frame-trivial substitute (i.e., $\Omega_{i,I} = I$), then the simulator's "top-down participation" does not test the framework's claim — it tests an identity-copy with damping, exactly what line 2284 says the transformer codebase is. Deferring the verification "to a follow-up" while still claiming that §Implementation "demonstrates that participatory-like dynamics are consistent with variational free energy minimization" is a load-bearing deferral. The demonstration claim requires the transport to be non-trivial, and the manuscript has not verified that the simulator realizes a non-trivial transport.

## Falsification condition

The claim is wrong if any one of:
(i) the §Implementation opening sentence's demonstration claim is conjoined elsewhere in the section with an undisclosed-expectation hedge that the demonstration claim does not survive (line 2228 supplies this);
(ii) the RG-inspired disclosure at line 2197 disclaims ingredients that the prose at lines 2210–2213 then re-introduces under the cover of "scale invariance of the functional form";
(iii) the simulator at `MAgent_Model-main/gauge_agent/` realizes a frame-trivial substitute for $\Omega_{i,I}$ rather than the full cross-scale transport (and line 2284 itself certifies this is not verified).

Any one of these is a publication-readiness wound.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Wilson, Renormalization Group and Critical Phenomena, Phys. Rev. B 4 (1971) 3174–3183.** The defining requirements of an RG analysis: rescaling transformation $\mathcal{R}_b$, iteration to fixed points $\mathcal{R}_b S^* = S^*$, linearization to compute critical exponents. The "scale invariance of the functional form" claim at PIFB line 2210 is necessary but not sufficient for any of these.
- **Wilson, The Renormalization Group and Critical Phenomena (Nobel Lecture, 8 December 1982).** Available at nobelprize.org. States explicitly that the substantive content of RG is the location of fixed points and the computation of critical exponents; the parametric form-invariance of the action is preserved by any local rescaling and is not itself a non-trivial RG statement.
- **Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959) §6.** Falsifiability requires that a theory specify the conditions under which it would be false, *before* the experiment. A theory that is qualified by "we expect, though do not directly measure" while simultaneously claiming "the whole becomes qualitatively different" has emptied the falsification condition: any outcome of the single-seed run can be retrofit to the expectation.
- **Lakatos, *The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (1978) §1.4.** Distinguishes "progressive" from "degenerating" research programmes; a programme that protects its core by ad-hoc auxiliary hypotheses ("the simulator realization is deferred to a follow-up") is degenerating. The "deferred to a follow-up" qualifier at PIFB line 2284 is exactly this pattern.
