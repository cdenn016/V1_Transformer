# Red Memo — info-geometer (rebuttal)

## (i) Concession from blue's opening

Blue's defense of the closed-form Gaussian-precision-pool — $\Sigma_\star^{-1} = \sum_k \lambda_k \Sigma_k^{-1}$, $\mu_\star = \Sigma_\star \sum_k \lambda_k \Sigma_k^{-1} \mu_k$ — by direct differentiation of $\sum_k \lambda_k \mathrm{KL}(q \| p_k)$ for Gaussian $q, p_k$ is correct. The precision-additive form is textbook [Bishop2006 §10.7; Jordan-Ghahramani-Jaakkola-Saul 1999 §3.1]. Hinton 2002 [PoE, *Neural Computation* 14:1771-1800, §2] is correctly cited for the additive-KL ↔ product-prior correspondence. Two of the four pooling-anchor citations at PIFB line 2275 stand.

## (ii) Strongest attack on blue's core defense

The remaining two — West-Harrison 1997 [§6.3] and Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 [§2] — do not stand on blue's verification standard. Blue's opening at line 59 (Falsification conditions) admits: "West-Harrison is cited at the textbook-section level [§6.3] without the agent retrieving the book directly." That is a self-disclosed verification gap. Under canon-cop scoring (strike weight 2 for hand-wave-with-citation), this is exactly the kind of citation that the agent claiming canonical fidelity has *not* verified to the lemma level.

There are two substantive citation issues, not only one:

**West-Harrison 1997 dynamic discount [§6.3].** The West-Harrison discount factor $\delta \in (0, 1]$ in dynamic linear models multiplies the *prior precision matrix* at each time step: $\Sigma_{t|t-1}^{-1} = \delta R_t^{-1}$ where $R_t$ is the prior covariance after evolution [West-Harrison 1997 §6.3, Eq. 6.3]. It is a *discount on the variance of the previous posterior carried forward into the current prior*. The PIFB Ouroboros fragment at Eq. 2270 uses $\lambda_0 \rho^k$ as a *geometric discount across hierarchical scale $k$* — a different object. In West-Harrison the discount lives along the *temporal* axis of a single dynamic linear model; in PIFB the discount lives along the *scale-hierarchical* axis of an infinite tower of meta-agents. The textbook citation is a structural-form analogy ("there exists a geometric-discount construction in Bayesian statistics"), not the canonical-form invocation a §Implementation subsection demands. Sub-claim 2 (canonical fidelity) wounds here.

**Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 [§2].** The tempered Bayes update $\pi(\theta | y) \propto \pi(\theta) \exp[-w \ell(\theta, y)]$ has $w$ as a learning rate over a *single* loss-based update, with formal justification via decision-theoretic coherence (Theorem 1 in the JRSSB paper). The PIFB use at Eq. 2270 sums KL terms *across an infinite tower of generations* with geometric weights $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$. The convergence of the geometric series for $\rho < 1$ is elementary, but the *interpretive identification* of $\rho^k$ with Bissiri-Holmes-Walker's $w$ requires either (a) treating each scale-step as an independent generalized-Bayesian update with its own learning rate (which the manuscript does not formally do), or (b) a generalized Bissiri-Holmes-Walker treatment for nested losses (which the manuscript does not cite). The standard reference for *discounted infinite-horizon Bayesian updating* is the discounted-data literature [Smith 1979 *J. Forecasting* 1:31-39; West-Harrison 1997 §10.7], not Bissiri-Holmes-Walker.

Two of the four pooling-anchor citations at PIFB line 2275 are off-target by construction-versus-form: the form of geometric discounting is correct, but the *canonical paper* for the form being used (infinite-horizon discounted updating across nested hierarchical levels) is not West-Harrison 1997 §6.3 and not Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 §2. Sub-claim 2 takes a wound.

## (iii) Strongest defense against blue's strongest attack

Blue's strongest attack on red's info-geometric position would be: *the pooling-citation block at PIFB line 2275 is gestural — naming the closely-related canonical objects as a reading guide, not committing to each as the lemma-level source for the ouroboros construction; reviewer-acceptable for a §Implementation section that has already invoked the formal closed form.*

Red's counter-defense: the manuscript at line 2275 does not frame the four citations as a reading guide. The line is part of the operative justification of the ouroboros free-energy fragment at Eq. 2270 — the construction is presented as the discounted-Gaussian pool of which "the closed form is the precision-weighted product-of-Gaussians" and the four citations anchor *that* closed-form claim's canonical fidelity. Sub-claim 2 reads: "Where the section invokes a canonical form (...) the invocation is faithful — the cited construction applies under the section's actual setup, not loosely." The West-Harrison and Bissiri-Holmes-Walker invocations are loose: structural-form analogy, not the cited construction applied under the section's actual setup.

A secondary defense is that the *information-geometric* identity of the forward-KL barycenter as the m-projection onto the e-flat Gaussian family [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 Ch. 2] gives PIFB Eq. 2142 a clean canonical home — but the m-projection literature does not justify the *dispersion term being dropped* at line 2179. Dropping the law-of-total-variance correction $(\mu_I - \tilde\mu_i)(\mu_I - \tilde\mu_i)^\top$ is a deviation from the moment-matched mixture barycenter [Bishop2006 §10.7] of order $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ in pairwise dispersion *only in the strict high-coherence limit*. The manuscript labels this "leading-order approximation in the high-coherence regime" at line 2179, but does not state at what coherence threshold the approximation is faithful — sub-claim 1 (mathematical correctness) takes a wound on the unstated quantitative scope of the approximation.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Smith, "A generalization of the Bayesian steady forecasting model", *J. Royal Statistical Society B* 41 (1979) 375-387.** Discounted-data Bayesian updating with geometric weights along the temporal axis. The standard predecessor to West-Harrison 1997 for the geometric-discount construction PIFB Eq. 2270 actually uses.
- **West, Harrison, *Bayesian Forecasting and Dynamic Models* (2nd ed., Springer, 1997), §10.7 "Discounted Likelihoods".** Section 10.7 (not §6.3) is the canonical home of the *infinite-horizon discounted updating* construction with geometric weights, which is closer to PIFB Eq. 2270's use than the dynamic-linear-model discount of §6.3. The §6.3 citation in PIFB at line 2275 is the dynamic-linear-model discount; the §10.7 reference would be the cleaner anchor for the ouroboros construction.
- **Amari, Nagaoka, *Methods of Information Geometry* (AMS Translations, 2000), Ch. 3 §3.5 "Projections and divergence-minimization".** The forward-KL barycenter $q_I^* = \arg\min_{q_I} \sum_i w_i \mathrm{KL}(\tilde q_i \| q_I)$ is the m-projection onto the e-flat exponential family; for Gaussian $\{\tilde q_i\}$ this is the moment-matched mixture, *with* the law-of-total-variance dispersion correction. Dropping the dispersion term is not a moment-matched m-projection — it is a *truncation* of the m-projection that is exact only at strict consensus.
