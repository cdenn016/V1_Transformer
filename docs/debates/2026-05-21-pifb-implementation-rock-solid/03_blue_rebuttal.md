## Core attack

Red's Vector 2 is the load-bearing weakness in red's opening. Vector 2 argues that the manuscript's line 2284 self-disclosure ("Whether the released simulator code realizes the full transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ or a frame-trivial substitute is not independently verified") leaves open the case of identity-copy substitute, in which case "the participatory loop reduces to mean-passing — a property of any hierarchical Bayesian model with point-passing across levels." Red's gauge-theorist memo (Phase 2) supports this with Nakahara §10.3 [Nakahara 2003] and Kobayashi-Nomizu Vol. I §II.7 [Kobayashi, Nomizu 1963] on trivial-connection-trivial-holonomy.

Red did not read the simulator code on this question. At `MAgent_Model-main/gauge_agent/meta_agents.py:226-227`, the cross-scale transport operator is constructed:

```python
omega_ij = torch.linalg.solve(
    agent.omega.data.transpose(-2, -1),
    ref_omega.transpose(-2, -1),
).transpose(-2, -1)
```

The transposed-solve gives $\omega_{ij}^\top = (\omega_i^\top)^{-1} \omega_{\mathrm{ref}}^\top$, so $\omega_{ij} = \omega_{\mathrm{ref}} \cdot \omega_i^{-1}$. This is the non-trivial product-of-exponentials form $U_I U_i^{-1}$ that the manuscript prescribes at line 2254 (taking the meta-agent's frame as the reference). The transports at `meta_agents.py:229-236` then apply `transport_mean(omega_ij, …)` and `transport_covariance(omega_ij, …)`, where the latter is the gauge-equivariant sandwich $\Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top$ — the Nakahara §10.3 canonical covariance-transport form [Nakahara 2003, §10.3]. The implementation-engineer memo §Defense and the gauge-theorist memo §Defense corroborate this from independent code reads.

Vector 2's strongest implication — that if $\Omega_{i,I} = I$, then $p_i^{(s)} = q_I^{(s+1)}$ verbatim with no gauge content — is contradicted by `path:line` evidence. The simulator implements non-trivial frame-change, not identity-copy. What remains genuinely unverified per line 2284 is the stricter claim that this frame-change is parallel-transport-of-a-specified-connection in the Nakahara §10.3 sense — a connection 1-form on a principal bundle whose base relates scale-$s$ to scale-$(s+1)$ structures is not provided in the section. That distinction the gauge-theorist memo concedes. But "frame-change in canonical $U_I U_i^{-1}$ form" is mathematically substantive content that red's identity-copy reading erases. Vector 2 should be re-stated as "the cross-scale $\Omega_{i,I}$ is not a parallel-transport map of a connection," not "may be identity-copy."

## Defense

Red's overarching philosophical move — Lakatosian "degenerating-programme" framing of PIFB §Implementation [Lakatos 1978, §1.4] — mischaracterizes the section's epistemic structure. Lakatosian degeneration is the protective belt absorbing falsifying evidence by post-hoc auxiliary hypotheses without independent empirical content. PIFB §Implementation does the structurally opposite move: it enumerates its own falsification-target gaps in advance and labels them as such. Line 2197 declares "we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance beyond the parametric form, and the analogy is structural rather than computational" — a published falsification target. Line 2138 enumerates "three ingredients remain to be supplied" before the IB Lagrangian is more than a research direction. Line 2174 declares "we do not establish that the detector's product form exactly tracks the variational-criterion savings" and "Whether a continuous-time evaluation of [the FE-improvement criterion] reproduces the same hierarchical organization that the threshold-based detector produces is open." Line 2284 declares the simulator transport "not independently verified." These are publicly listed falsification targets in advance of empirical work, which is the Popperian discipline [Popper 1959, §6] that publication-grade theory sections are supposed to exhibit. The philosophy-of-science memo §Defense develops the genre distinction between "ad-hoc auxiliary hypothesis" and "publicly enumerated falsification target."

On Vector 3, the manuscript's invocation of variational principles is the canonical principled-object-then-tractable-surrogate pattern of variational inference [Wainwright, Jordan 2008 *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning* 1(1-2), §3; Blei, Kucukelbir, Jordan 2017 *JASA* 112, §2]: write down the principled object (Eq. 2123, FE-improvement); flag computational intractability (line 2167); use a tractable surrogate (the threshold detector); acknowledge the gap to the principled object (line 2174). This is the foundational move of mean-field VI [Jordan, Ghahramani, Jaakkola, Saul 1999, *Machine Learning* 37(2), §3] performed correctly. The variational memo §Defense develops this with the BHW tempered-Bayes mapping at line 2275 (where the manuscript itself discloses the analogy "the role of historical time is played here by the hierarchical scale-distance $k$"). The variational barycenter at Eq. 2142 is the canonical Amari forward-KL barycenter [Amari, Nagaoka 2000, §3.4; Amari 2007, *Neural Computation* 19(10)]; the dispersion-term drop at line 2179 is the $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ truncation in the high-coherence regime, explicitly flagged with its error order. This is canonical information geometry, not deviation from it.

On Vector 3b (IB closed form), red's strongest specific attack is the canon-strict judge's wrong-domain-citation flag for Chechik-Tishby-Weiss [Chechik 2005, *JMLR* 6, §3]. The manuscript at line 2138 says exactly what red says: "the group-valued analogue of Gaussian-IB requires the Riemannian structure of [the] frame barycenter," "three ingredients remain to be supplied," "the present manuscript stands on the FE-improvement criterion." The manuscript and red agree that the closed form does not apply; the manuscript's invocation is performed under the "Information-bottleneck refinement (research direction)" paragraph header at line 2131. This is honest scope flagging, not citation misuse — exactly the canon-strict-compliant move. The info-geometer memo §Defense develops this.

On Vector 3c (Karcher non-compact), red's [Milnor 1976, *Adv. Math.* 21, 293-329] citation is correct: $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ admits no bi-invariant Riemannian metric. The manuscript at line 2160 itself says this and offers substitute constructions. The simulator at runtime operates in $G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$ (per manuscript line 2160 parenthetical: "$G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$ in our simulations"), the compact regime where the Karcher mean exists and is unique on balls of radius $< \pi/2$ [Karcher 1977, *CPAM* 30, 509-541]. The non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat is an anticipatory flag for broader applicability, not a wound on the simulations. The gauge-theorist memo §Counter-attack develops this and the corroboration from [Helgason 1978, §III.6] and [Pennec 2009, *Statistical Computing on Manifolds*].
