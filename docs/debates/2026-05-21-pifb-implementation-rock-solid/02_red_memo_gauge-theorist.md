## Steelman of the claim from this lens

The strongest reading: the §Implementation section keeps the gauge-equivariance discipline visible. The variational barycenter at Eqs. 2142, 2147, 2150 transports each constituent moment to the meta-agent's reference frame via $\Omega_{Ii}$ before averaging, and the covariance step uses the sandwich $\Omega_{Ii} \Sigma_i \Omega_{Ii}^\top$ — the canonical (2,0)-tensor transport [Nakahara2003 §10.3]. The frame barycenter at Eq. 2156 is the Karcher mean on the gauge group, the canonical group-valued aggregation. The Lie-algebra-additive frame $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i$ at line 2191 is correctly labeled as the first-order BCH approximation. The cross-scale shadow at Eq. 2247 transports both belief and model fibers through paired connections $\Omega_{i,I}$ (state fiber) and $\tilde\Omega_{i,I}$ (model fiber). On the gauge-equivariance surface, the section is structurally clean.

## Strongest weakness from this lens

The cross-scale transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ is the load-bearing gauge object across §Top-Down Participation, but the manuscript itself certifies at line 2284 that it has not verified the released simulator code realizes the full transport rather than a frame-trivial substitute. This makes the gauge-equivariance of the whole §Top-Down Participation subsection an unverified construction, and a frame-trivial substitute ($\Omega_{i,I} = I$) is gauge-trivial — it does not implement parallel transport at all.

(1) **The transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ across scales is *not* a connection on a single principal bundle.** A connection on a principal $G$-bundle $\pi: P \to M$ gives parallel transport between fibers over points of *the same base manifold $M$* [Nakahara2003 §10.3]. The manuscript's $\Omega_{i,I}$ transports between scale-$s$ agent $i$ (one fiber) and scale-$(s+1)$ meta-agent $I$ (a different fiber over a *different* base — the scale-$(s+1)$ structure). There is no underlying principal bundle defined in the manuscript whose connection would give such a transport, and the manuscript at PIFB line 2254 says $\Omega_{i,I}$ is "written as products of gauge-frame exponentials in the canonical form $U_i U_I^{-1}$ with $U = \exp(\phi)$." This is a *relative gauge change* between two frames, not a parallel transport along a base-space path; calling it "transport" is a category overload. The product $U_i U_I^{-1}$ is well-defined as a group element, but identifying it with the parallel-transport map of a connection requires specifying the connection 1-form on the cross-scale bundle, and no such 1-form is provided. The cross-scale shadow's "gauge covariance" is therefore the equivariance of the *frame-change formula* — a tautology — not the equivariance of a connection-induced transport. This is the **gauge invariance vs gauge equivariance** confusion from `external_canon_math.md` §2.

(2) **The frame-trivial substitute at line 2284 is gauge-trivial.** If the simulator implements $\Omega_{i,I} = I$ (identity-copy), then the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ reduces to $p_i^{(s)} = q_I^{(s+1)}$ — the constituent's prior is the meta-agent's posterior verbatim. The manuscript at PIFB line 2284 itself identifies this case for the transformer codebase: "the transformer codebase referenced in the abstract is a separate code path with its own cross-layer prior handoff (an identity-copy with damping, not a multi-scale transport) and should not be read as the simulator implementation of the present subsection." The manuscript says the transformer code-path is identity-copy with damping. The manuscript says the simulator's realization is "not independently verified." Without verification, the simulator could be the same identity-copy substitute, in which case the §Implementation section's central transport object is realized as the trivial connection. A trivial connection has trivial holonomy ([Nakahara2003 §10.3 holonomy definition]) and the participatory loop carries no gauge-theoretic content beyond mean-passing. The "demonstration that participatory-like dynamics are consistent with variational free energy minimization" (line 2106) is then a demonstration of mean-passing, which is true of any hierarchical Bayesian model and is not a participatory framework.

(3) **The Karcher mean on $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ has no canonical construction.** Concur with the info-geometer memo: $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ is non-compact and not a product of a compact group with an abelian group, so no bi-invariant Riemannian metric exists ([Milnor1976 *Adv. Math.* 21, 293–329]). The Lie-algebra-additive $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i$ at line 2191 is a *first-order* BCH approximation, but the underlying Karcher mean it approximates is itself unspecified. From the gauge-theorist's lens, the additional concern is that the first-order BCH approximation breaks at second order by $\frac{1}{2}\sum_{i<j} w_i w_j [\phi_i, \phi_j]$ (BCH expansion, [Hall2015 §5.3]), and the bracket $[\phi_i, \phi_j]$ on the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$ is non-trivial for non-abelian frames. Whether the constituent frames the simulator produces are close enough to make the second-order BCH term negligible is not measured in the manuscript. The "accurate to $\mathcal{O}(\|\phi_i\|^2)$ when constituent frames are close in the compact case" qualifier at line 2160 is a compact-group statement; in the non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ regime the second-order bound may be unbounded (the conditioning depends on the spectral radius of $\mathrm{ad}_{\phi_i}$, which is unbounded on $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$).

(4) **The Ouroboros free-energy fragment at Eq. 2270 lacks a gauge-equivariance proof.** The fragment is $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{ouro}} = \sum_k \lambda_0 \rho^k [\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}]) + \mathrm{KL}(s_i \| \tilde\Omega_{i,I_k}[s_{I_k}^{(s+k)}])]$. Under a gauge change at scale $s$ — call it $G_i$ acting on agent $i$ — each KL term should transform covariantly. The manuscript writes each $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ as a frame-change, so a gauge transformation on the agent $i$ would require simultaneous frame changes on $I_1, I_2, \ldots, I_k$ at scales $s+1, s+2, \ldots, s+k$ for the KL terms to remain invariant. The manuscript does not specify what gauge group acts at each scale or how the cross-scale frame changes are coordinated. The "self-referential closure" at Eq. 2279 ($p_i^{(\text{top})} = \sum_j w_j \Omega_{i,j}[q_j]$, sum over *all scales*) compounds this — a single gauge transformation at scale $s$ must induce coherent frame changes at every scale up to the top, and the manuscript provides no coordination rule. This is sub-claim 3 (internal consistency) on the gauge structure.

## Falsification condition

The claim is wrong if any one of:
(i) the cross-scale $\Omega_{i,I}$ is not the parallel-transport map of a specified connection on a specified principal bundle but is instead a frame-change formula misidentified as transport;
(ii) the simulator at `MAgent_Model-main/gauge_agent/` realizes $\Omega_{i,I}$ as a frame-trivial substitute (identity-copy) — the manuscript at line 2284 admits this is not verified;
(iii) the Karcher frame on $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ has no bi-invariant Riemannian metric (this is [Milnor1976 *Adv. Math.* 21, 293–329]) and the implementation's choice of substitute is unspecified;
(iv) the gauge group acting at each scale and the coordination rule for cross-scale frame changes are unspecified, leaving the Ouroboros fragment's gauge equivariance unverified.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Milnor, "Curvatures of Left Invariant Metrics on Lie Groups", *Advances in Mathematics* 21 (1976) 293–329**: a connected Lie group admits a bi-invariant Riemannian metric iff isomorphic to $G_c \times A$ with $G_c$ compact and $A$ abelian (standard result; section/lemma number to be confirmed against the printed source).
- **Nakahara, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* (2003), §10.3 "Parallel Transport and Holonomy"**: parallel transport is defined as the horizontal lift of a curve on the base manifold of a principal bundle; the connection 1-form must be specified. A "frame-change" $U_i U_I^{-1}$ is not parallel transport unless an underlying connection identifies it as such.
- **Hall, *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* (2015), §5.3 "The Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff Formula"**: $\log(\exp X \exp Y) = X + Y + \frac{1}{2}[X,Y] + \frac{1}{12}([X,[X,Y]] - [Y,[X,Y]]) + \ldots$. The first-order truncation $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i$ loses the $\frac{1}{2}\sum_{i<j} w_i w_j[\phi_i, \phi_j]$ correction; for non-abelian $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$ this is non-vanishing.
- **Kobayashi, Nomizu, *Foundations of Differential Geometry* Vol. I, §II.7 "Holonomy Group"**: the holonomy group of a flat connection (curvature $F = 0$) on a contractible base is trivial; an identity-copy "transport" has trivial holonomy and carries no parallel-transport content.
- **Pennec, "Bi-invariant Means on Lie Groups with Cartan-Schouten Connections" (CIS-6100 notes, U. Penn., 2012)**: for non-compact Lie groups, the bi-invariant mean must be constructed via the Cartan-Schouten 0-connection (an affine connection without a Riemannian metric), and existence is only *local* — within a normal convex neighborhood, with linear-rate fixed-point convergence.
