## Why each defense fails

On the **Lakatos-progressive** defense, the discriminator in Lakatos 1970 (in *Criticism and the Growth of Knowledge*, Lakatos & Musgrave eds., Cambridge UP) §1 is *resolution*, not *enumeration*. A progressive programme enumerates problems and resolves them with independently testable content; a degenerating programme enumerates problems and absorbs them without resolution. PIFB §Implementation spans exactly lines 2101–2304. Within that 203-line span, line 2197 declines to exhibit a $\beta$-function or fixed points; line 2138 declines to supply the three IB ingredients; line 2174 declines to establish that the threshold detector tracks the FE-improvement criterion; line 2284 declines to verify the simulator transport. None of the four enumerated deferrals is subsequently resolved within §Implementation. Four unresolved enumerations is the degenerating-programme signature. Blue's appeal to Popper 1959 §6 ("publicly listed falsification targets") conflates ex-ante specification of *what would falsify the theory* (Popper's canonical criterion) with ex-ante enumeration of *what the present manuscript does not yet do* (a deferral). The line 2228 conjunction — substantive existential assertion plus declined measurement — is the textbook conventionalist-twist case [Popper 1959, Routledge 2002 ed., §15, p. 70].

On the **canonical-VI** defense, Wainwright-Jordan 2008 *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning* 1(1-2) §3 develops the mean-field surrogate as a *bounded* surrogate with explicit error analysis: the variational free energy lower-bounds the log-evidence by Jensen, and the bound is tight at the true posterior. PIFB line 2174 in full says: "we do not establish that the detector's product form exactly tracks the variational-criterion savings even in the high-coherence limit. Whether [it] reproduces the same hierarchical organization … is open." No bound, no one-sided inequality, no monotonicity certificate, no error analysis — only an open question about whether the surrogate tracks the principled object at all. This is the unbounded-heuristic pattern, not the canonical VI pattern. The threshold detector at line 2174 (and the simulator's two-factor form at `meta_agents.py:55-91`) is a candidate-selection surrogate without a Jensen-style bound to the FE-improvement criterion at line 2123.
