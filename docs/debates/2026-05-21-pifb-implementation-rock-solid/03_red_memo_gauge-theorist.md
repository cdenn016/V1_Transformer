# Red Memo — gauge-theorist (rebuttal)

## (i) Concession from blue's opening

Blue's defense of the Karcher non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat at PIFB line 2160 as "textbook-correct" — the Killing form on $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{R})$ is indefinite for $K \geq 2$ [Helgason 1978 §III.6], no bi-invariant Riemannian metric exists [Milnor 1976], and the two enumerated substitutes (left-invariant alternative; polar-decomposition / SPD-restricted construction) are standard [Pennec 2009; Bonnabel-Sepulchre 2009] — is correct on the underlying differential geometry. The negative obstruction (no bi-invariant metric on $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ for $K \ge 2$) is real and the manuscript states it correctly. The first-order BCH approximation $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i$ at PIFB line 2191 is correctly characterized as $\mathcal{O}(\|\phi_i\|^2)$ accurate [Hall 2015 §5.3].

## (ii) Strongest attack on blue's core defense

Blue's load-bearing claim is: *the manuscript at line 2160 openly discloses the unadjudicated choice; empirical work uses compact $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ where the Karcher mean exists and is unique on convex normal balls of radius $< \pi/2$ [Karcher 1977; Moakher 2002 for $\mathrm{SO}(3)$]; therefore the caveat is reviewer-grade scope honesty, not a sub-claim-2 wound.*

The counter-attack is that the manuscript *frames itself* as a $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ framework, with $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ as a *computational restriction*. PIFB §Theory at line 593 reads: "The natural gauge group for KL-based variational inference on multivariate Gaussians is the real $G = \mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ acting on the belief fiber, with the proof-of-concept simulations in this work restricted to the compact subgroup $G = \mathrm{SO}(3)$ for computational tractability and visualizability." The framework's natural gauge group is $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ by the manuscript's own framing.

The §Implementation Karcher caveat at line 2160 — "For non-compact $G$ (such as $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$) (...) the choice is a modeling decision the present implementation does not adjudicate" — is therefore the *load-bearing case*, not the marginal case. The compact $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ regime is the *fallback for computational tractability*, not the regime the framework's variational-inference-on-Gaussians setting actually requires. Under the manuscript's own §Theory framing, the §Implementation section's claim to "rock solid" should be evaluated against the $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ regime where Karcher fails, not the $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ regime where it works.

The deeper attack: the manuscript at §Implementation has no empirical exercise of the $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ Karcher caveat. No simulation in the released code (`MAgent_Model-main/gauge_agent/`) runs in the non-compact regime — the simulator code at `meta_agents.py:358` computes `omega_avg = (w * omega_stack).sum(dim=0)` extrinsically, never invoking the Karcher mean at all, and the simulations operate in $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ per §Theory line 593. The §Implementation Karcher caveat at line 2160 is therefore *unfalsifiable as stated*: it describes an obstruction (no bi-invariant metric on $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$) that the framework's natural variational-Gaussian setting requires confronting, but no concrete experiment in the manuscript exercises that setting. Sub-claim 4 (falsifiability and scope) takes a wound: the "leading-order approximation" labeling on the BCH-first-order frame average and the "modeling decision not adjudicated" labeling on the Karcher non-compact substitute are not faithful to *what the framework requires* — they are scope retreats from $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ to $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ disguised as honest disclosures.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Moakher, "Means and averaging in the group of rotations", *SIAM J. Matrix Anal. Appl.* 24(1) (2002) 1-16.** Section 3 enumerates four distinct mean constructions on $\mathrm{SO}(N)$: the extrinsic Euclidean mean (followed by polar projection), the projected arithmetic mean, the log-Euclidean mean (BCH-first-order Lie-algebra-additive), and the Karcher (Riemannian) mean. These are *different* objects in general, and the relationship between them is non-trivial. PIFB line 2191 claims the BCH-first-order form; `meta_agents.py:358` implements the extrinsic Euclidean form. These are two of Moakher's four distinct constructions.
- **Pennec, Fillard, Ayache, "A Riemannian framework for tensor computing", *Int. J. Computer Vision* 66 (2006) 41-66.** Affine-invariant Riemannian framework on SPD matrices; explicit log-Euclidean mean vs Riemannian mean distinction (§4 and §5). The framework PIFB §Theory invokes for the natural $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ setting is closer to the affine-invariant/log-Euclidean SPD construction than to the compact-Lie-group Karcher mean; the substitute the §Implementation caveat at line 2160 "does not adjudicate" has a canonical literature.
