## (ii) Strongest attack on blue's core defense

Blue's load-bearing move is *concede 6 and 7, defend 1-5, and rest the case on "publication ready under reasonable reading."* This is a category error against the operationalization. The claim at `00_claim.md` line 24 declares: "any one [sub-claim] failing falsifies the whole." That is a pre-debate conjunctive predicate — Popper's falsification condition stated *before* the experiment [Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959), §15: "every imaginable basic statement [...] which contradicts the theory" must be enumerated *ex ante*]. Blue concedes two of seven sub-claims. Under the operationalization, the verdict is RED_WINS on those two conceded sub-claims alone, regardless of how the other five stand.

Blue's escape route — "sub-claim 7 is a frame defect of the operationalization" — is exactly the move Lakatos [*The Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (1978), §1.4] identifies as a degenerating programme: ad-hoc auxiliary hypotheses (here: "every reviewer-honest section discloses follow-up work") introduced to protect the core after the falsification condition triggers. The methodology document at `debate_methodology.md` is explicit: blue is *forbidden* from challenging the operationalization mid-debate. Blue's opening at line 52 admits this in writing: "blue is forbidden from challenging the operationalization, so this is a concession." That is concession enough.

Blue's parallel move on sub-claim 6 — "the manuscript at line 2284 self-discloses ('Whether the released simulator code realizes the full transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ or a frame-trivial substitute is not independently verified in this manuscript'); concession is honest, not capitulation" — is a second instance of the same pattern. Self-disclosure inside the manuscript does not discharge a falsification condition that was specified outside the manuscript and before the debate. The "honest" framing is rhetorical; the operationalization is binding.

Citation: [Popper 1959 §15; Lakatos 1978 §1.4]. The honest-disclosure-as-publication-virtue argument is correct for a manuscript-reviewer context; it is not correct for a pre-specified conjunctive falsification predicate.

## (iii) Strongest defense against blue's strongest attack

Blue's strongest attack on red's position would be: *the conjunctive operationalization is itself malformed (well-formedness failure at scope-judge level), so the debate should be REMANDed rather than declared RED_WINS.* Red's defense: the operationalization was authored before the debate, both teams had the opportunity to challenge well-formedness at intake, and the scope-judge channel exists for exactly this purpose. The scope judge in this debate has not declared OUT_OF_SCOPE. Furthermore, the manuscript at line 2284 *itself* labels the simulator-realization status as a "deferred to a follow-up" — the manuscript pre-stipulates the wound the operationalization names. A claim cannot be both rock-solid and pre-stipulate a deferred wound on the same line.

Secondary defense: even granting blue's "5-of-7" frame, the four-citation defense on sub-claim 2 is weakened by blue's own admission that West-Harrison 1997 [§6.3] and Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 [§2] were verified "at textbook-section level without the agent retrieving the book directly" (blue opening, line 59). Under Popper's falsifiability standard, a citation that the citing party has not verified to the lemma level is hand-wave-with-citation, which canon-cop scores at strike-weight 2. The 5-of-7 raft is leaky on sub-claim 2 even if 6 and 7 were dischargeable.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (Routledge Classics ed., 2002, original 1959), §15 "Strictly universal and strictly existential statements"** — "Every imaginable basic statement which is incompatible with the theory must be excluded by the theory" (page 70 in the Routledge edition). The falsification condition is *ex ante*; relaxing it after partial falsification is not honest disclosure but conventionalist twist (§19).
- **Lakatos, *Falsification and the Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (in *Criticism and the Growth of Knowledge*, ed. Lakatos & Musgrave, 1970), §1** — distinguishes "progressive" from "degenerating" research programmes; ad-hoc auxiliary hypotheses introduced solely to absorb anomalies (without independent testable content) characterize the degenerating case. Source: <https://www.csun.edu/~vcsoc00i/classes/s497f09/s2/Lakatos.pdf>.
