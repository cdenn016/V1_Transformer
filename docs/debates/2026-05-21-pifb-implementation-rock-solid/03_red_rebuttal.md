These are not the load-bearing wounds.

## Core attack

Blue's opening rests its entire case on a single move: *concede sub-claims 6 and 7, defend sub-claims 1-5, and rest on "publication-ready under reasonable reading."* This is incompatible with the operationalization. The claim at `00_claim.md` line 24 — pre-debate, agreed-to by both teams at intake — declares: "any one [sub-claim] failing falsifies the whole." Blue's opening at line 52 admits in writing: "blue is forbidden from challenging the operationalization, so this is a concession." Blue concedes two of seven sub-claims. The conjunctive predicate fires on either concession alone. Sub-claim 7 ("no `TODO`, no 'future work', no 'deferred to a follow-up' placeholder") fails on four explicit lexical hits — PIFB lines 2138 ("research direction"), 2174 ("natural follow-up project"), 2213 ("important direction for future work we are currently engaged in"), 2284 ("deferred to a follow-up"). Sub-claim 6 ("Where §Implementation states what the simulator implements [...] the simulator at `MAgent_Model-main/gauge_agent/` actually realizes those constructions — not a frame-trivial substitute") fails on three independently verified code paths admitted by blue's own implementation-engineer memo: `meta_agents.py:56-66` (`1 - E` form, the form PIFB line 2174 explicitly rejects); `meta_agents.py:89-91` (two-factor product $C_b \cdot C_m$, not the three-factor $P \cdot C_q \cdot C_s$ PIFB line 2174 specifies); `meta_agents.py:343-359` (extrinsic Euclidean mean of group elements, with the comment at lines 344-355 admitting "the previous docstring claim of 'Lie-algebra-additive average' was wrong; corrected here," not the BCH-first-order form PIFB line 2191 specifies).

Blue's "self-disclosure ≠ wound" escape route on these sub-claims is the move Popper [*The Logic of Scientific Discovery* 1959, §15] and Lakatos [*Falsification and the Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* 1970, §1] flag as conventionalist twist: a falsification condition stated *ex ante* cannot be relaxed *ex post* by introducing the auxiliary "self-disclosed limitations are reviewer-acceptable." That auxiliary may be true for journal review; it does not discharge a pre-debate falsification predicate. The manuscript's own Implementation Note at line 2284 — "Whether the released simulator code realizes the full transport $\Omega_{i,I}$ or a frame-trivial substitute is not independently verified in this manuscript" — pre-stipulates exactly the wound the operationalization names. A claim cannot be both rock-solid and pre-stipulate a deferred wound on the same line.

The conjunctive operationalization binds. Red wins on sub-claim 6 (manuscript-vs-code consistency falsified on three code paths, two of them with code-level admission of the divergence) *and* sub-claim 7 (no unresolved gaps falsified on four lexical hits). Either suffices.

## Defense

Beyond the operationalization, blue's 5-of-7 defense leaks on at least three of its claimed-defended sub-claims.

**Sub-claim 2 (canonical fidelity).** Blue's defense of the four pooling-anchor citations at PIFB line 2275 admits in blue's own falsification conditions (line 59): "West-Harrison is cited at the textbook-section level [§6.3] without the agent retrieving the book directly." Under canon-cop strike-weighting, a citation that the citing party has not verified to the lemma level is hand-wave-with-citation (strike weight 2). The substantive issue is sharper than blue's verification gap: West-Harrison 1997 §6.3 is the *dynamic-linear-model discount* (geometric weights along the temporal axis of a single DLM); PIFB Eq. 2270 uses geometric weights $\lambda_0 \rho^k$ across *the scale-hierarchical axis of an infinite tower of meta-agents*. The canonical home for the infinite-horizon discounted updating construction is [West-Harrison 1997 §10.7 "Discounted Likelihoods"] or [Smith 1979 *J. R. Statist. Soc. B* 41:375-387], not §6.3. The Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 [§2] citation has the same structural issue: tempered Bayes with learning rate $w$ is a *single-update* construction with decision-theoretic justification (JRSSB Theorem 1); the PIFB use sums an infinite tower of generalized-Bayesian updates with geometric weights, which Bissiri-Holmes-Walker §2 does not formally treat. The Chechik-Tishby 2005 closed-form Gaussian-IB attribution at PIFB line 2138 fails as cited: the Chechik-Tishby Theorem 3.1 requires *jointly Gaussian random vectors $X, Y$ in a common Euclidean space* [Chechik-Tishby 2005 *JMLR* 6:165-188, §3]; the §Implementation parent state $T = (q_I, s_I, U_I)$ is a tuple of (distributions, distributions, group elements), not such an object.

**Sub-claim 4 (falsifiability and scope).** Blue defends the Karcher non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat at line 2160 as reviewer-grade scope honesty bounded to compact $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ where the issue does not arise [Karcher 1977; Moakher 2002 for $\mathrm{SO}(3)$]. The defense ignores PIFB §Theory line 593: "The natural gauge group for KL-based variational inference on multivariate Gaussians is the real $G = \mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ acting on the belief fiber, with the proof-of-concept simulations in this work restricted to the compact subgroup $G = \mathrm{SO}(3)$ for computational tractability and visualizability." The framework's natural gauge group is $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$; $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ is the *fallback for computational tractability*. The §Implementation Karcher caveat at line 2160 describes an obstruction in the regime the framework's natural variational-Gaussian setting requires — but no concrete experiment exercises that regime. The substitute the manuscript says is "a modeling decision the present implementation does not adjudicate" has a canonical literature [Pennec-Fillard-Ayache 2006 §4-§5 affine-invariant SPD framework; Moakher 2002 §3 four distinct $\mathrm{SO}(N)$ mean constructions]; the §Implementation does not engage it. Sub-claim 4 wound: the "leading-order approximation" and "modeling decision not adjudicated" labelings are scope retreats from $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ to $\mathrm{SO}(3)$ disguised as honest disclosures.

**Sub-claim 1 (mathematical correctness).** Blue's defense of the IB Lagrangian framing at line 2138 as Popperian falsifiability disclosure misses that Eq. 2133 is *displayed as a math equation* `eq:meta_agent_IB`, and prose at line 2136 reads "the IB Lagrangian is the variational closure of the FE improvement criterion." This is load-bearing on the interpretation of $C(I)$ in the operative criterion at Eq. 2123. The three unsupplied ingredients at line 2138 are open derivation gaps, not separable research deferrals: ingredient (a) makes $I(T; Y)$ undefined, (b) makes Chechik-Tishby Theorem 3.1 inapplicable, (c) leaves the threshold detector's identification with the IB-optimum asserted-not-derived. Tishby-Pereira-Bialek 1999 §2 requires a tractable encoder family $p(T \mid X)$ which §Implementation does not supply for the (distribution, group-element) joint state. The compounded wound: ingredient (b) — the gauge-frame component of $T$ under encoder noise — is the same Karcher / log-Euclidean / extrinsic-Euclidean disagreement the gauge-theoretic memo documents at `meta_agents.py:358`. Two lenses, one wound: the framework's IB and Karcher constructions are both unspecified on the gauge-frame component, and the simulator implements an extrinsic Euclidean form whose result is not in the gauge group.

Secondary wound on sub-claim 1: the closed-form forward-KL barycenter at PIFB Eqs. 2147-2152 is the *moment-matched mixture m-projection* onto the e-flat Gaussian family [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 Ch. 3 §3.5; Bishop 2006 §10.7, law-of-total-variance form]. Dropping the dispersion correction $(\mu_I - \tilde\mu_i)(\mu_I - \tilde\mu_i)^\top$ at line 2179 is a truncation of the m-projection, not the m-projection. The "leading-order approximation in the high-coherence regime" labeling is scaling-correct ($\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ in pairwise dispersion) but does not specify the quantitative coherence threshold below which the approximation is unfaithful; the simulator at `meta_agents.py:217-321` operates this truncation without runtime check that the approximation regime holds.

The case is structural: the §Implementation section's load-bearing equations (FE-improvement criterion Eq. 2123; IB Lagrangian Eq. 2133; forward-KL barycenter Eq. 2141; Karcher frame Eq. 2156; threshold detector lines 2168-2174; cross-scale shadow priors Eq. 2247; ouroboros fragment Eq. 2270) are each invoked with canonical-form citations that, on close reading, do not apply as cited (Chechik-Tishby 2005 on a non-Gaussian-vector state; West-Harrison 1997 §6.3 on the wrong section; Bissiri-Holmes-Walker 2016 §2 on a single-update theorem extended to infinite tower without proof) or are not implemented as displayed (Lie-algebra-additive frame at line 2191 → extrinsic Euclidean at `meta_agents.py:358`; Gibbs three-factor detector at line 2174 → `1-KL` two-factor at `meta_agents.py:91`). The "rock solid and publication ready" bar is not cleared.
