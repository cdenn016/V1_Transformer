## Position

Blue's rebuttal §Defense reframes PIFB §Implementation's four self-disclosed deferrals (RG fixed points at line 2197; IB ingredients at line 2138; threshold-vs-FE-criterion gap at line 2174; simulator-transport verification at line 2284) as Popperian publicly-listed falsification targets, and brands red's Lakatosian reading "degenerating-programme" framing a mischaracterization. This move fails on Lakatos's own text.

## Why the move fails

Lakatos 1970 (`Falsification and the Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes`, in *Criticism and the Growth of Knowledge*, ed. Lakatos & Musgrave, Cambridge UP, 1970), §1, distinguishes a **progressive** programme — which both enumerates problems *and* successfully resolves them with independently testable content — from a **degenerating** programme — which enumerates problems and absorbs them without resolution. The discriminator is *resolution*, not *enumeration*. PIFB §Implementation runs exactly 2101–2304: 203 lines. Within that span, none of the four enumerated targets (RG fixed points; IB tractable encoder and joint $p(X,Y)$ and gauge-frame encoder; threshold-detector tracking of the variational criterion; simulator-transport verification) is resolved. Line 2197 explicitly says "we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance beyond the parametric form" — and §Implementation does not subsequently exhibit one. Line 2138 says "three ingredients remain to be supplied" — and §Implementation does not subsequently supply them. Line 2284 says "not independently verified in this manuscript" — and §Implementation does not subsequently verify it. The enumeration-without-resolution pattern is the Lakatosian degenerating-programme signature, not the progressive one.

Blue's invocation of Popper 1959 §6 ("publicly listed falsification targets") conflates two distinct things: (a) ex-ante specification of *what would falsify the theory* (Popper's canonical falsifiability requirement, §6 of *Logik der Forschung*), and (b) ex-ante enumeration of *what the present manuscript does not yet do*. (a) is the falsifiability criterion. (b) is a deferral. Listing deferrals is honest scholarly disclosure; it is not Popperian falsifiability discipline. The line 2228 "we expect, though do not directly measure, that meta-agents exhibit properties not present at lower scales" remains the textbook Popper §15 conventionalist-twist case [Popper 1959, Routledge 2002 ed., §15, p. 70]: a strictly existential statement asserted without a corresponding basic statement that would refute it.

## Concessions of blue's other moves

Blue's Hempel 1962 / Frigg-Hartmann SEP citation for "toy model" genre is correct *as far as it goes* — toy models do have their own publication standard. But Hempel's "explanation-by-illustration" requires the illustrated mechanism to actually work in the toy. PIFB line 2228's "qualitatively different from sum of its parts" is the substantive content the toy is supposed to illustrate; line 2228 then declines to measure it. A toy that declines to measure its own load-bearing illustrative content is not in the Hempel-canonical toy-model genre — it is in the deferral genre, which Hempel does not authorize.

## Final position

The Lakatos-progressive defense requires resolutions, not enumerations. The Popper §6 defense requires ex-ante falsification conditions, not ex-ante deferrals. Sub-claim 4 stands wounded at line 2228 (blue conceded) and at lines 2197, 2138, 2174, 2284 considered jointly under the Lakatos criterion. Combined with the literal-reading falsification of sub-claim 6 (blue conceded) and the canonical-fidelity wound on sub-claim 2 (the gauge-theorist memo carries this), the conjunctive operationalization at `00_claim.md` line 24 is breached.
