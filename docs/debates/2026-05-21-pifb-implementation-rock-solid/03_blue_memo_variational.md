# Blue memo — variational (rebuttal)

## Concession from red's opening

Red's Vector 3a is correct on its facts: the simulations of PIFB do not evaluate the FE-improvement criterion at Eq. 2123 directly; they use the threshold-based detector at line 2174 as a practical surrogate. The manuscript at line 2174 itself states "we do not establish that the detector's product form exactly tracks the variational-criterion savings even in the high-coherence limit" and at line 2174 says "Whether a continuous-time evaluation of [the FE-improvement criterion] reproduces the same hierarchical organization that the threshold-based detector produces is open." The simulations therefore test the threshold detector, not the variational principle from which it is motivated. This is a real epistemic gap.

## Strongest defense against red's core attack with citation

The defense is not "the gap doesn't exist" — it does. The defense is that this exact pattern of motivation-by-principled-object-then-implementation-by-surrogate is canonical practice in variational inference and is publication-grade when the surrogate-vs-principled-object relationship is disclosed.

[Blei, Kucukelbir, Jordan 2017, *JASA* 112: 859-877, §2] is the canonical reference: VI replaces the intractable exact posterior with a tractable variational family (the surrogate), and the literature spends considerable space adjudicating when the surrogate tracks the principled object. The Jordan-Wainwright "Graphical Models, Exponential Families and Variational Inference" [*Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning* 1(1-2), 2008, §3] develops the mean-field approximation as a deliberate departure from the exact posterior, with explicit gap analysis. The pattern PIFB enacts — write down the principled object (Eq. 2123, FE-improvement); flag computational intractability (line 2167); use a surrogate (the threshold detector); acknowledge the surrogate does not provably track the principled object (line 2174) — is canonical VI practice, not a Lakatosian protective hypothesis.

The Bissiri-Holmes-Walker tempered Bayes framework [Bissiri, Holmes, Walker 2016, *JRSSB* 78: 1103-1130, §2] develops the same pattern explicitly: a principled Bayesian update may be intractable; a "loss-based" generalized update is the operational surrogate; the relationship to the principled update is asserted and validated separately. Red's variational-memo critique (round 2) that the manuscript's Eq. 2270 uses $\rho^k$ as a geometric discount across generations, not a learning rate in BHW's specific sense, is technically correct — but the manuscript at line 2275 explicitly maps "the role of historical time is played here by the hierarchical scale-distance $k$," disclosing the analogical mapping. This is honest analogy, not citation misuse.

The variational barycenter at Eq. 2142 is itself canonical: it is the forward-KL barycenter $q_I^* = \arg\min \sum_i w_i \mathrm{KL}(\tilde q_i \| q_I)$, which for Gaussians is the moment-matched mean $\mu_I = \sum_i w_i \tilde\mu_i$, $\Sigma_I = \sum_i w_i [\tilde\Sigma_i + (\tilde\mu_i - \mu_I)(\tilde\mu_i - \mu_I)^\top]$ [Bishop 2006, §10.7]. The manuscript at line 2179 explicitly drops the dispersion term in the implementation as "leading-order approximation in the high-coherence regime where meta-agents form (the dropped term is $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ in the post-transport pairwise dispersion $\varepsilon$)" and at line 2187 says reinstating it "gives the strict moment-matched solution at modest computational cost and is the natural refinement once the threshold detector is replaced." The approximation is mathematically standard (Amari α-divergence framework, α = -1 forward-KL is m-projection [Amari 2007, *Neural Computation* 19(10): 2780-2796]) and explicitly flagged with its dropped-term error estimate.

## Strongest counter-attack on red's weakest evidence

Red's claim that "the manuscript at line 2106 frames the demonstration claim around variational free energy minimization, not around the threshold detector" misreads line 2106. The actual sentence: "we demonstrate that our gauge-theoretic framework supports structural parallels to this vision: when coherent agent clusters satisfy the variational free-energy improvement criterion of Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_variational}, they aggregate into hierarchical meta-agents that propagate information back to constituents." The very next sentence: "The simulations of this paper trigger the aggregation through the threshold-based surrogate of Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_threshold} rather than through a continuous-time evaluation of the variational criterion; the threshold detector approximates the variational rule in the high-coherence regime, but whether the two produce identical hierarchical organization is open." Line 2106 itself disclaims the strong identification red attributes to it. The "demonstration claim" framing red imputes is not in the text.

## Falsification conditions for this defense

This defense is wrong if (i) the manuscript's surrogate-vs-principled relationship is loadbearing in a downstream subsection (e.g., §Results) that re-asserts the variational principle as tested by the simulations — would need to be shown; (ii) the high-coherence-regime $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ error estimate at line 2179 is wrong about its order of magnitude (canonical Bishop §10.7 derivation gives a $\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^2)$ error for the squared dispersion term — manuscript is within the canonical error budget); (iii) the threshold detector's $\Gamma_{\min} = 0.5$ is shown to systematically deviate from the variational threshold in a regime the simulations probe.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Wainwright, Jordan, "Graphical Models, Exponential Families, and Variational Inference", *Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning* 1(1-2) (2008): 1-305, §3.** Canonical reference for the surrogate-vs-principled-object pattern in variational inference.
- **Amari 2007, "Integration of stochastic models by minimizing α-divergence", *Neural Computation* 19(10): 2780-2796.** α = -1 (m-projection) recovers the forward-KL barycenter; the moment-matched mean is the closed form. The manuscript's Eq. 2147-2151 are the canonical Amari closed form.
- **Jordan, Ghahramani, Jaakkola, Saul 1999, "An Introduction to Variational Methods for Graphical Models", *Machine Learning* 37(2): 183-233, §3.** Original Saul-Jaakkola-Jordan-Ghahramani VI framework. Establishes that the principled-object-with-tractable-surrogate pattern is the foundational move of mean-field VI.
