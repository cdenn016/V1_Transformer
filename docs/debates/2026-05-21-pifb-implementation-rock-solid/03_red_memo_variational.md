# Red Memo — variational (rebuttal)

## (i) Concession from blue's opening

Blue's defense of the variational FE-improvement criterion at PIFB Eq. 2123 as canonical Friston form [Friston2010 Eq. 2.2], and of the forward-KL barycenter at PIFB Eq. 2141-2152 as a standard variational mean-field problem with prior regularization [Beal 2003 §2.2.2; Blei-Kucukelbir-Jordan 2017 §3], is correct on the canonical-form invocation. The pairwise-to-parent collapse argument at PIFB line 2129 is structurally consistent with hierarchical variational inference, and the cross-scale information flow at Eq. 2219 with the non-mutual-information disclaimer at line 2222 matches the standard Cover-Thomas 2006 §2.3 caveat on mutual information requiring a joint distribution.

## (ii) Strongest attack on blue's core defense

Blue's defense of the IB Lagrangian framing — that line 2138's enumeration of three unsupplied ingredients (the natural-gradient transition kernel; the gauge-frame component under encoder noise; the empirical comparison) is "reviewer-acceptable epistemic discipline" [Popper, *Conjectures and Refutations* 1963 Ch. 1] — is the load-bearing escape on sub-claim 1.

The counter-attack is structural: the IB Lagrangian at Eq. 2133 is *displayed as a math equation* `eq:meta_agent_IB`, equation-numbered, and prose at line 2136 reads "Reading $C(I)$ in~\eqref{eq:meta_agent_FE_criterion} as the IB rate term $\beta_{\mathrm{IB}}^{-1}I(T;X)$ and the free-energy savings as a proxy for the predictive term $I(T;Y)$, the IB Lagrangian is the variational closure of the FE improvement criterion." This is not a research-direction footnote — it is *load-bearing* on the variational closure of the operative FE-improvement criterion at Eq. 2123. The manuscript identifies $C(I)$ in the operative criterion with the IB rate term, and the FE savings with the IB predictive term; the FE-improvement criterion's interpretation as "the variational closure" rests on this identification.

Three unsupplied ingredients are not separable research deferrals; they are *open derivation gaps in §Implementation*:

**(a)** "A precise specification of the natural-gradient transition kernel that defines $X \to Y$ in the gauge-covariant setting." Without this, the identification of FE savings with $I(T; Y)$ is undefined — $I(T; Y)$ requires a specified joint distribution of $T$ and $Y$, which the unspecified transition kernel must produce.

**(b)** "A treatment of the gauge-frame component of $T$ under encoder noise (the group-valued analogue of Gaussian-IB requires the Riemannian structure of Section [meta-agent variational]'s frame barycenter)." Without this, the Gaussian-IB closed-form attribution at line 2138 [Chechik-Tishby 2005 §3.1, Theorem 3.1] does not apply — Chechik-Tishby requires *jointly Gaussian random vectors* $X, Y$ *in a common Euclidean space* [Chechik-Tishby 2005 §3]; the §Implementation parent state $T = (q_I, s_I, U_I)$ is a tuple of (distributions, distributions, group elements), not a jointly Gaussian random vector.

**(c)** "An empirical comparison of the IB-optimal coarse-graining with the threshold-detector hierarchy." Without this, the identification of the threshold detector (the operative mechanism for cluster formation) with the IB-optimal coarse-graining is *asserted, not derived*.

Three unsupplied ingredients with this much load-bearing on the variational closure of the FE-improvement criterion are not "Popperian falsifiability disclosure." Popper [*The Logic of Scientific Discovery* 1959, §15] requires falsification conditions for *the claim made*. The claim made at line 2136 is that the IB Lagrangian *is* the variational closure of the FE-improvement criterion. The three unsupplied ingredients are not falsification conditions for that claim — they are *open construction steps* without which the claim has no formal content. Sub-claim 1 (mathematical correctness) takes a wound: equation `eq:meta_agent_IB` is displayed but not derived under the section's setup. The Tishby-Pereira-Bialek 1999 §2 IB Lagrangian requires a tractable encoder family $p(T \mid X)$, which the §Implementation does not supply for the (distribution, group-element) joint state.

The wound compounds with the gauge-theorist's $\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ wound: the gauge-frame component of $T$ that ingredient (b) names as unsupplied is exactly the Karcher / log-Euclidean / extrinsic-Euclidean disagreement that the gauge-theorist memo documents. The IB "research direction" framing and the Karcher "modeling decision not adjudicated" framing are the same wound in different lenses.

## (iii) Strongest defense against blue's strongest attack

Blue's strongest attack on red's variational-closure critique would be: *the manuscript at line 2138 explicitly states "the present manuscript stands on the FE-improvement criterion" — i.e., the load-bearing operative content is Eq. 2123, not Eq. 2133; the IB Lagrangian is an honest flag of a stronger claim the manuscript declines to make.*

Red's counter-defense: the manuscript indeed stands on Eq. 2123, but then *displaying* the IB Lagrangian as Eq. 2133 with the "variational closure of the FE-improvement criterion" prose at line 2136 is a category error. If the manuscript stands on Eq. 2123, the IB material at lines 2131-2138 is rhetorical scaffolding for a claim the manuscript declines to make — which makes the displayed equation Eq. 2133 ornamental, not load-bearing for sub-claim 1, but still subject to sub-claim 2 (canonical fidelity) and sub-claim 4 (faithful labeling of "research direction" vs operative form). The Tishby-Pereira-Bialek 1999 sign convention is correct; the Chechik-Tishby 2005 closed-form attribution is not applicable under the §Implementation parent-state structure. Sub-claim 2 wound on the Chechik-Tishby citation: cited paper proves a theorem for jointly Gaussian random vectors in a common Euclidean space; PIFB invokes it for a parent state that is not a jointly Gaussian random vector in a common Euclidean space.

A second wound on the forward-KL barycenter: the closed form at PIFB Eqs. 2147-2152 is the *moment-matched mixture barycenter* [Bishop 2006 §10.7, the law-of-total-variance form]. Dropping the dispersion term at line 2179 as "leading-order approximation in the high-coherence regime" is a deviation from the m-projection onto the e-flat Gaussian family [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 Ch. 3 §3.5]. The m-projection of a mixture onto an e-flat exponential family is unique and includes the dispersion correction by construction; truncating it is not an m-projection. The "leading-order" labeling at line 2179 is correct in scaling ($\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)$ in pairwise dispersion) but does not specify the coherence threshold below which the approximation is unfaithful. Sub-claim 1 wound on the unstated quantitative scope.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Friston, "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* 11 (2010) 127-138, §2 Eq. 2.2.** Canonical FE form $F = \mathrm{KL}(q \| p) - \mathbb{E}_q[\log p(o|s)]$. Hierarchical extension is the *recursive* application of this form across levels with the parent's posterior becoming the child's prior — *not* the pairwise-to-parent collapse of $\mathcal{O}(|I|^2)$ pairwise alignment KL terms PIFB Eq. 2123 invokes. The collapse is a *modeling choice* (replace pairwise with hub-and-spoke alignment), not the canonical hierarchical-VFE form.
- **Tishby, Pereira, Bialek, "The Information Bottleneck Method", *Allerton* (1999), §2 (preprint <https://arxiv.org/abs/physics/0004057>).** The IB Lagrangian variational closure requires "tractable encoder family $p(T \mid X)$" and joint distribution $p(X, Y)$ inducing the rate $I(T; X)$ and predictive $I(T; Y)$. For PIFB $X = \{q_i, s_i, U_i\}_{i \in I}$, the tractable encoder family is unsupplied.
- **Chechik, Globerson, Tishby, Weiss, "Information Bottleneck for Gaussian Variables", *JMLR* 6 (2005) 165-188, §3 Theorem 3.1.** Closed-form Gaussian-IB requires *jointly Gaussian random vectors $X, Y$ in a common Euclidean space*. PIFB's parent-state $T = (q_I, s_I, U_I)$ is not such an object; the Chechik-Tishby Theorem 3.1 does not apply at line 2138 as cited.
