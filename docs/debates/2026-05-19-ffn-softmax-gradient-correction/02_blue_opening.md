# Blue Opening — ffn-softmax-gradient-correction

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §5.3 derivation is complete on its own terms: the boxed GLU form `Eq. (eq:vfe_glu, line 1903)` is one channel, the "softmax-gradient correction" paragraph (lines 1936–1947) names the centered covariance term explicitly, and the closing paragraph "FFN depth from VFE iterations" (lines 1949–1950) states that each VFE iteration applies the GLU map *composed with* the softmax-gradient correction. The manuscript therefore neither suppresses the correction nor relegates it to a vanishing remainder; it documents it as a co-occurring channel, and the user's request for a "more careful formulation" is satisfied by lines 1949–1950 already.

## Position

The claim — that §5.3's editorial separation of "GLU as the carrier of the FFN nonlinearity" + "softmax-gradient correction as a higher-order addition" is *not* warranted off-equilibrium, and that a more careful formulation must derive the inner-loop μ-update from `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` directly so the GLU and the covariance term emerge as co-equal pieces of the same belief-update vector field — is defensible on three pieces of primary-source evidence: (i) the manuscript's own line 1945 ordering language ("higher-order non-linearity beyond the first-order GLU gate"); (ii) the manuscript's own line 870–873 gap formula stating the two vector fields "share critical points only where this covariance vanishes ... but not generically off-equilibrium"; (iii) the implementation at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1883` which detaches `beta_g` (`beta_d_in_graph = beta_g.detach()  # envelope at softmax fixed pt`), enforcing the *envelope-form* gradient in the trainer despite the manuscript's line 874 statement "we adopt the [autograd] convention." The claim's two sub-claims are therefore defensible: sub-claim A (envelope geometry — the joint-stationary qualifier) holds because the line 870–873 gap formula is non-zero off-equilibrium and the inner-loop iterate `μ_t` is by construction off the joint stationary point until convergence; sub-claim B (FFN derivation completeness — that a unified formulation would carry both terms in one update) is supported by the fact that the actual trainer drops the correction via `detach()`, creating a measurable asymmetry between the manuscript's claimed adopted convention (line 874) and the implementation's executed gradient (e_step.py:1883).

## Evidence

- **Manuscript line 1945** (Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex, "Softmax-gradient correction" paragraph): the correction is labeled "a higher-order non-linearity beyond the first-order GLU gate." The phrases "higher-order" and "beyond" are ordering language. They commit the §5.3 derivation to a hierarchy in which the GLU is leading-order and the correction is subleading. The user's claim is that this ordering is unwarranted off-equilibrium — and the manuscript's own line 870–873 gap formula is the receipt: the gap is `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E_{ij}, ∂E_{ij}/∂μ_i)`. The covariance is `O(1/τ)` times the *variance* of `E_{ij}` under `β*` times the per-edge gradient. When `β*` is diffuse (early iterates, before `μ_t` has concentrated `β`), the variance of `E_{ij}` is the same magnitude as the mean of `E_{ij}` — both are `O(KL)`. The correction is therefore not subleading in any well-defined sense off-equilibrium; "higher-order" reflects the *editorial* order in which the §5.3 derivation introduces the two terms, not a mathematical order of magnitude.

- **Manuscript line 870–874** (`Eq. eq:autograd_envelope_gap`):
  ```
  ∇_x ⟨E⟩_{β*} - ∇_x F_red = -τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E_{ij}, ∂E_{ij}/∂x).
  ```
  The manuscript text at line 873 then states verbatim: "The two objectives therefore share critical points only where this covariance vanishes; this holds at the joint stationary point of `F_red` in `x` and in the high-temperature limit `τ → ∞`, but not generically off-equilibrium." This is the manuscript's own statement that the two gradient fields differ off-equilibrium. The user's sub-claim A — that the envelope-form §5.3 result is exact only at the joint stationary point — is the manuscript's own line 873 statement, restated as a complaint about §5.3's editorial structure.

- **Manuscript line 973** (Belief Dynamics restatement): the belief-dynamics equation `∂q_i/∂t = -η_q ∇_{q_i}[Σ_j β_ij* E_ij + α_i KL(q_i ‖ p_i) - E_{q_i}[log p(o_i | k_i)]]` is *not* gradient descent on `F_red`. The line 973 text states: "This differs from the gradient of the reduced free energy `F_red` (Eq. eq:free_energy_final) by the covariance term `-τ⁻¹ Cov_{β*}(E_ij, ∂E_ij/∂q_i)` of Eq. eq:autograd_envelope_gap; the two vector fields coincide at joint stationary points and differ off-equilibrium by the softmax-gradient nonlinearity identified at Section sec:ffn_nonlinearity." The user's claim that the FFN-as-VFE-iterations identification needs the covariance term as a structural piece is exactly what the line 973 text requires: the belief dynamics that the FFN implements is `⟨E⟩_{β*}` gradient flow, not `F_red` gradient flow, and the two differ by the covariance term off-equilibrium.

- **Implementation `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1883`**:
  ```python
  beta_d_in_graph = beta_g.detach()  # envelope at softmax fixed pt
  ```
  followed by line 1886 `alignment_term = self.lambda_align * (beta_d_in_graph * kl_g).sum()`. The trainer's autograd path uses `beta.detach()`, which severs the `∂β/∂μ` derivative chain. This means the trainer computes `∇_μ Σ_j β_ij* E_ij` *with β held as a constant* — i.e., the envelope-form gradient `∇F_red = Σ_j β_ij* ∂E_ij/∂μ`, not the autograd-form gradient `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` which would include the `Σ_j (∂β/∂μ) E_ij` correction. This contradicts the manuscript's line 874 declaration "we adopt the [autograd] convention." The trainer in practice executes the envelope form; the manuscript in §5.3 derives the FFN from the envelope-form GLU; and the correction term (line 1936–1947) is acknowledged but neither integrated into the boxed result nor present in the trainer's autograd path. The user's claim — that a more careful formulation would integrate the correction so the GLU and the covariance term emerge as co-equal pieces — is supported by the existence of this asymmetry: the framework currently has a *triple* split between (a) the boxed GLU result, (b) the named-but-not-boxed covariance correction, (c) the trainer's actual descent direction (envelope form via `detach()`).

- **Canonical envelope theorem [Milgrom-Segal 2002; Boyd-Vandenberghe 2004 §5.5]** as documented in `01_evidence.md`: the envelope identity `dV/dx = ∂f/∂x|_{y=y*(x)}` is a statement about the gradient *of the value function* `V(x) = min_y f(x, y)` at parameter `x`, given that `y*(x)` is the minimizer for that `x`. It is *not* a statement about the relation between `∇V(x)` and `∇_x f(x, y(x))` for trajectories `y(x)` that are not minimizers. The §5.3 derivation invokes the envelope theorem to write the FFN as the gradient of `F_red = -τ log Z_i` — i.e., as the gradient of the *value function* of the row-β-Lagrangian. But the trainer's per-iterate update uses `μ_t` that is *not* the joint-stationary minimizer of `F_red` (only `β_t = softmax(-KL_t/τ)` is the row-stationary minimizer of the *β*-block at the current `μ_t`). The envelope theorem therefore licenses the equation `∇F_red|_{μ_t} = Σ_j β_t · ∂E_t/∂μ` *for the gradient of the row-β-reduced free energy at the current μ_t* — which is what `F_red` is — but the §5.3 derivation moves silently from "gradient of the row-β-reduced free energy" to "the iterative belief-update dynamics" (line 1949–1950). These two are *not* the same vector field off-equilibrium: line 973 of the manuscript itself says the belief dynamics gradient is `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}`, which differs from `∇F_red` by exactly the covariance correction.

- **Variational EM / coordinate ascent [Wainwright-Jordan 2008 §3, Bishop 2006 §10.1, Neal-Hinton 1998]** as documented in `01_evidence.md`: standard VBEM iterates block-stationary updates. The red-team will argue that `β_t = softmax(-KL_t/τ)` is the *partial* stationary point of the row-β-Lagrangian at `μ_t`, so the envelope theorem applies *at every iterate*. This is correct as a statement about the gradient *of the row-β-reduced* free energy `F_red(μ_t)`. But it is *not* correct as a statement about the dynamics the trainer actually runs: the trainer updates `μ` using `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` (per line 967 of the manuscript), not `∇F_red`. The two coincide at joint stationarity; they differ off-equilibrium by the covariance correction. The "envelope applies at every iterate" reading defends `∇F_red`, not the inner-loop belief-update dynamics.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **If the trainer's actual descent direction differentiates through `β` somewhere on the active code path** — i.e., if some downstream call in `e_step.py` recovers the `Σ_j (∂β/∂μ) E_ij` term that `beta_g.detach()` discards at line 1883 — then the trainer is computing `∇⟨E⟩_{β*}` (autograd form) after all, the covariance correction is in the executed gradient field, the manuscript's line 874 claim "we adopt the autograd convention" is honored in code, and the user's complaint about an editorial-vs-implementation gap collapses. The red team should be invited to find such a call site if it exists; a single `path:line` where `β` is *not* detached and is multiplied by `KL` inside the autograd graph would defeat this point.

2. **If a single equation in the manuscript explicitly carries both the GLU term `e_ij · gate(e_ij)` AND the centered-covariance term in one boxed update for `Δμ_i`** — i.e., if the boxed Eq. (eq:vfe_glu, line 1903) or a near-neighbor equation already integrates the correction — then the editorial separation the user complains about does not exist as a feature of the manuscript. Currently the boxed result at line 1903 is *only* the GLU; the correction at line 1941 (eq:softmax_gradient_nonlinearity) is `∂β/∂μ`, not the full per-edge `Δμ` update; the two are never written as a single equation. If such an equation is present elsewhere (e.g., in `Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex` at a line not in the evidence pack), this position weakens.

3. **If `Cov_{β*}(E, ∂E/∂μ) = O(1/τ²)` rather than `O(1)`** uniformly across the inner-loop iterates — i.e., if the covariance is genuinely subleading in `1/τ` over the regime the trainer operates in — then "higher-order" at line 1945 is mathematically correct, not merely editorial. The covariance is bounded by `Var_{β*}(E) · ‖∂E/∂μ‖`. For the trainer's typical setting `κ ≈ 1`, `τ = κ√K ≈ √K`, and β is a softmax over `N` rows of KL energies. When `β` is concentrated (one row dominates), `Var_β(E) → 0` and the correction is genuinely small; when `β` is diffuse (early iterates, large KLs of comparable magnitude across `j`), `Var_β(E) = O(⟨E⟩²) = O(KL²)` and the correction is the same order as the per-edge gradient times `KL/τ`. A finite-difference test in the trainer's actual regime, showing the covariance is uniformly small, would weaken this position; showing it is comparable to the GLU term in early iterates would strengthen it.

4. **If `n_e_steps = 1`** (single E-step iteration with the prior μ as the initialization, so `μ_t = μ_p` at the only inner update) — and the user always runs this configuration — then the "off-equilibrium" regime is *the* operating regime and the GLU+correction unification is essential. Conversely, if `n_e_steps ≫ 1` and the trainer reliably runs to convergence such that `β_t → β*(μ*)` before the M-step backward, the covariance correction tends to zero and the §5.3 editorial separation is empirically harmless. The user's actual `n_e_steps` in production should be checked; if it is small (1–4), this position is strengthened; if it is large (≥ 16) and convergence is reached, this position weakens to "true but practically irrelevant."

5. **If a standard reference (e.g., Boyd-Vandenberghe §5.5 or Wainwright-Jordan §3) treats per-iterate partial-stationarity as removing the off-equilibrium gap in the value-function gradient** — i.e., if the standard envelope theorem statement *does* license the move from "β is partially stationary at every iterate" to "the iterative belief-update dynamics equal the gradient of `F_red`" — then sub-claim A is wrong. The standard references do not say this (the envelope theorem is about the gradient of the value function `V(x)`, not about iterative update dynamics on `x`), but if such a reference exists, this position fails.
