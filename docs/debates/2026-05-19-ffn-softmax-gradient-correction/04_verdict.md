## Reasoning

The compound claim has two pieces. Sub-claim A — the envelope-theorem absence of $\partial\beta/\partial\mu$ from $\nabla F_{\text{red}}$ requires joint stationarity, so the correction $\mathrm{Cov}_\beta(\mathrm{KL},\nabla\mathrm{KL})/\tau$ is non-negligible off-equilibrium — is falsified by primary-source evidence. The envelope theorem is pointwise in the outer parameter [Milgrom-Segal 2002 Thm 1; Boyd-Vandenberghe 2004 §5.5.4]; block-stationarity in $\beta$ at the current $\mu_t$ is the hypothesis and is built into the iteration by $\beta_t := \mathrm{softmax}(-E(\mu_t)/\tau)$ at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:828`. Blue conceded this explicitly. The user's intuition that "$\beta$ has not converged before $\mu$ updates" misreads the hypothesis: the relevant convergence is row-stationarity in $\beta$ for fixed $\mu_t$, which holds by construction, not joint convergence in $(\mu,\beta)$.

Sub-claim B — the §5.3 boxed GLU is presented as headline and the correction as "additional," and a more careful formulation would unify them — survives only as an editorial complaint. Red's symbolic-plus-numerical evidence establishes the stronger pointwise identity: under the canonical entropy-augmented F that the manuscript explicitly adopts at lines 855 and 874 ("we adopt the [autograd] convention"; the gradient expressions "are gradients of the attention-weighted energy $\sum_j \beta_{ij}^* E_{ij}$, evaluated at $\beta = \beta^*$"), the autograd gradient evaluated with $\beta$ recomputed at every iterate equals the envelope gradient at machine precision. The bracket $E_{ij} + \tau\log\beta_{ij} - \tau\log\pi_j$ is $j$-independent at $\beta^*$, so the correction term identically vanishes in the gradient of the entropy-augmented F. The §5.3 derivation is therefore mathematically exact under the manuscript's adopted canonical F.
