# Red Opening — ffn-softmax-gradient-correction

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §5.3 derivation invokes the envelope theorem to identify the boxed GLU-with-Boltzmann-gate form (Eq. eq:vfe_glu, line 1903) as *the* FFN nonlinearity, and the envelope identity is only exact at joint `(μ*, β*)` stationarity, so the §5.3 picture is leading-order-only and a "more careful formulation" should fold the covariance correction into the boxed GLU form.

## Position

The claim is false on both load-bearing pieces. Sub-claim A misstates the envelope theorem: the envelope identity is a pointwise statement in the eliminated variable's parameter, not a statement that holds only at joint stationarity, and `β_t = softmax(-KL(μ_t)/τ)` satisfies the envelope-theorem hypothesis at every inner-loop iterate `t` by construction. Sub-claim B misreads the manuscript: the §5.3 derivation of the boxed GLU form (line 1903) does not invoke the envelope theorem at all — it identifies the form of `β_ij e_ij` for the currently-evaluated `β_ij`, which is a structural decomposition independent of whether `∂F/∂β = 0`. The envelope theorem appears in the §5.3 narrative only to *label* the correction term's status under `∇F_red` versus autograd (lines 1938–1939, parenthetical), not to *justify* the GLU identification. The manuscript already presents the correction as a separate channel of the per-iteration nonlinearity (lines 1947, 1949–1950); the "unification" the user asks for is editorial, not mathematical.

## Evidence

- **Envelope theorem (canonical form).** Milgrom-Segal 2002, "Envelope Theorems for Arbitrary Choice Sets", Econometrica 70:583–601, Theorem 1: for `V(t) = sup_{x ∈ X} f(x, t)` with `x*(t)` an interior selection and `∂f/∂t` continuous at `(x*(t), t)`, `V'(t) = ∂f/∂t(x*(t), t)`. This holds *pointwise in t*, at every t for which `x*(t)` is the maximizer. The envelope identity is not a "joint stationarity" statement. Boyd-Vandenberghe 2004 §5.5.4 (Sensitivity Analysis via the Dual) states the same identity for parametric convex programs: `dp*(u, v)/du = -λ*` where `λ*` solves the dual at the current `(u, v)` — pointwise in the perturbation, not only at the unperturbed optimum.

- **Coordinate-ascent VB satisfies the envelope hypothesis at every iterate** [Wainwright-Jordan 2008 §3.2; Bishop 2006 §10.1.1]. Standard mean-field VB recomputes each `q_i^{(t+1)} = argmin_{q_i} F` with `{q_{j≠i}}` fixed. Each iterate is the block-stationary point in the eliminated block; the envelope identity applies at that iterate. The `vfe/e_step.py` loop (`for t in range(self.n_e_steps)` at line 828) recomputes `β_t = softmax(-KL(μ_t)/τ)` at every iteration. At each iterate, β_t is the unique stationary point of the row-Lagrangian for the current `μ_t` (the soft-assignment KKT gives a single fixed point because the row Lagrangian is strictly convex in β over the simplex). The envelope hypothesis is satisfied at every t < n_e_steps, not only at convergence.

- **§5.3 boxed Eq. (eq:vfe_glu, `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1903`) is not derived from the envelope theorem.** The boxed expression is the per-edge contribution `Δμ_i|_j = β_ij · [Σ_i (Ω_ij Σ_j Ω_ij^⊤)^{-1} (μ_i − Ω_ij μ_j)]` from Eq. eq:glu_message (line 1888), read as `(linear residual) × (Boltzmann gate)`. The factorization is structural: it holds for *any* β_ij, optimal or not, because the per-edge term has the form `β_ij · e_ij` with `β_ij` a softmax over KL energies. No envelope step is invoked between Eq. eq:glu_message (line 1888) and Eq. eq:vfe_glu (line 1903). The envelope theorem is mentioned, at line 1938, only inside the parenthetical labeling the correction term: "this term is absent from the gradient of the reduced free energy `F_red` by the envelope theorem, but present in standard autograd implementations." That sentence labels the correction term's status under two different objectives; it does not justify the GLU identification.

- **The manuscript already integrates the correction into the per-iteration FFN map.** Line 1947: "Each variational channel (μ, Σ, φ) thus contributes its own GLU-type activation and the composition of these channels within each VFE iteration produces a richer non-linear map than any single channel alone." Line 1949–1950: "Each iteration applies the GLU map (eq:vfe_glu) composed with the softmax-gradient correction (eq:softmax_gradient_nonlinearity)." The composition of GLU and correction *is* what the manuscript identifies as the FFN. The user's "more careful formulation should unify them" is a request that the manuscript already satisfies.

- **The autograd-versus-`F_red` decomposition is explicitly stated in §4.7 with the exact covariance term the user wants exposed** (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:868–874`, Eq. eq:autograd_envelope_gap): `∇_x ⟨E⟩_{β*} − ∇_x F_red = −τ^{−1} Cov_{β*}(E_ij, ∂E_ij/∂x)`. Line 874: "Standard transformer training follows the autograd convention (differentiating through the softmax), and we adopt the same convention in the gradient expressions and algorithm below." The manuscript's *adopted* gradient (line 967, eq:belief_dynamics) is the autograd form `∇[Σ_j β*_ij E_ij + α D_KL(q‖p) − E_q log p(o|k)]` — which contains the softmax-gradient correction by construction (the product rule on `β*_ij(μ_i) E_ij(μ_i)` produces the centered-covariance term). The §5.3 FFN derivation operates on this autograd gradient, not on `∇F_red`. There is no suppression to undo.

- **The dual envelope identity in the supplementary** (`Attention/GL(K)_supplementary.tex:1190–1199`) is the *other* envelope identity — the derivative w.r.t. β at stationary q (used for the dual cost-of-attention interpretation). It does not bear on the §5.3 FFN derivation. Sub-claim A's "envelope theorem is exact only at the joint stationary point" framing conflates these two distinct identities and applies the wrong hypothesis to the §5.3 setting.

## Falsification conditions

The position above is wrong if any of the following can be cited:

1. A point in `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex` between line 1881 and line 1907 where the derivation of the boxed Eq. eq:vfe_glu actually depends on `∂F/∂β = 0` (rather than on the structural fact that the per-edge contribution `β_ij e_ij` factors into a Boltzmann gate times a linear residual for whatever `β_ij` is currently evaluated). If blue can show the boxed GLU form requires β to be at its stationary point, Sub-claim B has substance.

2. A demonstration that the row-Lagrangian `F_align(β; μ_t)` does *not* have `β_t = softmax(-KL(μ_t)/τ)` as its unique stationary point for the current `μ_t` — i.e., that the simplex-constrained minimization in β has a non-trivial interior critical structure such that `β_t` is not block-stationary at every iterate. If so, the envelope hypothesis fails at the iterates and Sub-claim A is not a misstatement.

3. A canonical reference establishing that the envelope theorem requires *joint* stationarity in both the parameter and the eliminated variable (as Sub-claim A asserts) rather than block-stationarity in the eliminated variable at the current parameter value (the Milgrom-Segal 2002 / Boyd-Vandenberghe 2004 §5.5.4 reading). I am unaware of any such reference and the standard sources are unanimous against it.

4. A demonstration that the user's stated "more careful formulation" — one that does not assume β has converged before μ updates — produces a per-iteration map different from `GLU ∘ softmax-gradient-correction` as already described at lines 1947 and 1949–1950. If blue can write down such a map and show it is not already what §5.3 identifies, the editorial-vs-mathematical distinction collapses.
