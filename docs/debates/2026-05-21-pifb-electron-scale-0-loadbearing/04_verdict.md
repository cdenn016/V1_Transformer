# Verdict — pifb-electron-scale-0-loadbearing

## Outcome

REMAND

## Decisive evidence

Two citations together break the compound claim into a defended part and a textually-broken part:

1. **Defending sub-claim (b):** [Nakahara 2003, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* (2nd ed.), §10.1–10.3] defines a principal $G$-bundle for *arbitrary* Lie group $G$, with connection one-form valued in $\mathfrak{g}$ and parallel transport in $G$; the construction's *form* is independent of which specific $G$ is chosen. Yang–Mills theories with structure groups $U(1)$, $SU(2)$, $SU(3)$ — compact, non-compact-via-complexification, abelian, non-abelian — all instantiate "the same gauge-theoretic dynamics" in the canonical structural sense. This is the canon's reading of "gauge-theoretic," and it admits scale-stratified structure groups (e.g., $U(\mathcal{H})$ at scale-0 alongside $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ at higher scales). Red did not cite a canonical source establishing the fixed-group reading as the operative one; blue cited the standard textbook for the structural reading.

2. **Breaking sub-claim (a):** `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:119`. The sentence "Physical reality is constituted by agents-of-agents at hierarchically organized scales, all participating in the same gauge-theoretic dynamics" is universally quantified with no textual scope-split between ontological commitment and constructive deferral. Blue conceded this in the rebuttal: "Red's reading of `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:119` is textually accurate ... the parenthetical scope-down ('the manuscript does not develop the quantum case') scopes the *construction*, not the *commitment*." The claim's framing of sub-claim (a) as "illustrative rather than load-bearing on the current implementation" is not what the text says.

## Reasoning

The compound claim splits cleanly. Sub-claim (b) is the load-bearing technical leg, and it survives on the source-of-truth canon: Nakahara 2003 §10 establishes the structural reading of "gauge-theoretic," and the density-operator-manifold lift assembled from textbook objects — skew-Hermitian frame field $\phi_i^q = iH_i$, unitary transport $U_{ij} = \exp(iH_i)\exp(-iH_j)$ acting by sandwich conjugation $\rho \mapsto U\rho U^\dagger$ per Bengtsson–Życzkowski 2017 §15, Umegaki 1962 quantum relative entropy, Lindblad 1975 / Uhlmann 1977 monotonicity — is canonically available. The Petz 1996 monotone-metric non-uniqueness is real, conceded by blue, and constitutes a research-program parameter (which member of the family to select), not a coherence-blocker; the classical case also requires choices (which exponential family, which chart) that the §1.5 wording does not foreclose.

Sub-claim (a) as written by the claim — "illustrative of the ontology rather than load-bearing on the current implementation" — does not match the manuscript text. Red is correct that the §1.5 paragraph reads as a load-bearing ontological commitment, not as an illustrative aside. Blue's honest concession in Phase 3 grants exactly this point and proposes a textual fix. The framework's coherence is recoverable by adopting the scope-split reading that blue defends — load-bearing on the ontology, scope-deferred on the construction — but the current §1.5 wording does not yet signal that split.

Sub-claim (c) follows: the §1.7 line 146 disclaimer scopes the construction honestly under the scope-split reading; it does not yet bound the ontological commitment because the §1.5 wording does not yet make the split explicit.

The correct verdict is therefore REMAND with a specific textual fix, not RED_WINS (because sub-claim (b)'s in-principle coherence is canonically defensible and red's fixed-group attack does not survive the Nakahara 2003 §10 structural reading) and not BLUE_WINS (because sub-claim (a) as written by the claim does not match the manuscript text and blue has conceded the need for a textual amendment).
