# Red Rebuttal — pifb-electron-scale-0-loadbearing

## Concession

Granted: Hudson 1974 does not block density-operator hosting of generic quantum states. Blue's first evidence bullet is correct on the narrow point that any $\rho \in \mathcal{D}(\mathcal{H})$ admits a representation regardless of Wigner-function sign; the manuscript's §1.5 move to "density operators with quantum relative entropy as the divergence" therefore neutralises Hudson at the bare-hosting level. The prior debate's Hudson attack does not, on its own, falsify the §1.5 declaration in the form blue defends.

Also granted: blue's volunteered concession (the construction is not exhibited) is the right concession. I do not press blue further on completeness — I press on coherence under the manuscript's own commitments.

## Core attack

Blue's defense rests on three load-bearing moves, and at least two of them collapse on primary-source inspection.

**(1) The unitary action $\rho \mapsto U\rho U^\dagger$ is structurally incompatible with the manuscript's $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ gauge group, which contradicts the §1.5 "same gauge-theoretic dynamics" commitment.**

The §1.5 paragraph asserts that all scales participate in "the same gauge-theoretic dynamics" (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:119`). The framework's gauge group is fixed at $G = \mathrm{GL}(K)$ in `Definition (Representations and Associated Bundles)` (`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:602`–616), with the explicit action $\rho_{\mathrm{state}}(g) \cdot \mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma) = \mathcal{N}(g\mu, g\Sigma g^\top)$ at line 614 and the transport law $\Omega_{ij} \mapsto g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$ at line 610. Blue's defense move 5 (falsification condition on group-action analog) invokes the unitary group $U(\mathcal{H})$ with $H \in \mathfrak{u}(\mathcal{H})$ as the relevant scale-0 symmetry. But $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ is non-compact, real, and contains scaling/shearing/reflection (line 616); $U(\mathcal{H})$ is compact, complex, and trace-preserving. These are not the same gauge group, and a principal $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$-bundle does not aggregate up from a principal $U(\mathcal{H})$-bundle by any canonical procedure. Blue's defense therefore offers a *different* gauge-theoretic dynamics at scale-0 from the dynamics specified at higher scales, in direct contradiction of the §1.5 universal-dynamics clause. The scope-split reading of (a) does not patch this: the construction-debt is not the absence of an implementation, it is the absence of a coherent group-theoretic story under which the same dynamics could in principle be instantiated.

**(2) The quantum analog of Fisher–Rao is not unique, and the manuscript's primitives are calibrated to the classical unique Fisher–Rao geometry.**

Petz 1996, "Monotone Metrics on Matrix Spaces" (Linear Algebra Appl. 244:81–96), proves that monotone Riemannian metrics on $\mathcal{D}(\mathcal{H})$ form a one-parameter family parameterised by operator-monotone functions $f:[0,\infty)\to\mathbb{R}$ with $f(x)=xf(x^{-1})$. Members include the Bures (SLD) metric, the Kubo–Mori (BKM) metric, the right-logarithmic-derivative (RLD) metric, and the maximal metric. Čencov 1982 by contrast establishes that the classical Fisher–Rao metric is unique up to a positive constant under monotonicity under Markov morphisms (see also Ciaglia–Di Cosmo arXiv:2305.12482 "Can Čencov meet Petz?" for the modern formal statement of the contrast). Blue cites Petz 1996 as supplying "the canonical quantum analog of the Fisher–Rao metric" (singular). This is wrong on the primary source: Petz 1996 supplies *the family*, not *the metric*. The framework's transport law $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ and its sandwich-KL coupling are derived from a unique Fisher–Rao geometry where natural-gradient flow is well-defined without metric choice. Lifting the framework to scale-0 requires picking one element of the Petz family, and the choice is not metaphysically free: different monotone metrics produce different natural gradients, different geodesics, and different transports. The §1.5 declaration "the manifold of density operators with quantum relative entropy as the divergence" silently picks a divergence (Umegaki) but says nothing about the metric — the divergence-metric pair is canonical in the classical case (Fisher = Hessian of KL) but underdetermined in the quantum case. Blue's "canonical substitution" is therefore not canonical; it is a choice the manuscript does not make and that the §1.5 wording does not acknowledge.

**(3) The Friston-Wiese-Hobson 2020 / Friston 2019 precedent does not license gauge-frame primitives at every scale.**

Friston 2019 (arXiv:1906.10184) "A Free Energy Principle for A Particular Physics" derives variational dynamics from a Langevin SDE on a state space with a Markov blanket; the steady-state density factorises and the variational free energy is read off the Helmholtz decomposition. Friston-Wiese-Hobson 2020 (Entropy 22:516) "Markovian Monism" reuses the same Langevin construction and asserts that any Markov-blanket-bearing system has gradient-flow dynamics on its internal states. Both papers commit only to (i) a state space, (ii) a Markov-blanket partition, and (iii) a Langevin SDE; the principal-bundle structure with a Lie-algebra-valued gauge frame field $\phi_i \in \mathfrak{g}$ is not part of the published Markovian-monism commitment. Blue's defense bullet "the manuscript's pan-agentic commitment is the same structural move applied with a gauge-frame field on top" elides this: the gauge-frame addition is precisely the part that Markovian monism does not license, because Langevin SDEs do not have Lie-algebra-valued frame fields as primitives. The Sengupta et al. 2016 "Neuronal Gauge Theory" paper does add gauge structure at the FEP level, but its scope is single-scale (neuronal coordination), not pan-agentic across electrons to ecosystems. Blue cannot transfer pan-agentic dynamics-license from FWH 2020 and gauge-structure-license from Sengupta 2016 and treat the conjunction as canonically supported — the two papers' scopes do not overlap on the load-bearing intersection.

## Defense

The Phase-2 attack stands and is strengthened by blue's volunteered concession.

The load-bearing question, framed by sub-claim (b), is whether "in-principle coherence" of the density-operator generalisation is supported under canonical quantum information geometry. The Phase-2 attack identified the gap between *naming* the density-operator manifold and *exhibiting* gauge-frame structure plus inter-agent transport on it. Blue's defense affirms that the manuscript names but does not exhibit, and grounds in-principle coherence on Petz 1996 + Amari–Nagaoka ch. 7 + FWH 2020. The core-attack moves above show all three grounding citations are weaker than blue's defense claims:

- Petz 1996 supplies a one-parameter family of monotone metrics, not a canonical metric. The framework's primitives are calibrated to a unique-metric setting (Čencov-Fisher) and the choice of which Petz family member to use at scale-0 affects observables — natural gradients, β-coupling weights, and meta-agent aggregation rules all depend on metric choice. The manuscript does not pick one, so the in-principle coherence is shifted onto a research-choice the manuscript does not make.
- Amari–Nagaoka 2000 ch. 7 develops dual-flat quantum exponential and mixture families on $\mathcal{D}(\mathcal{H})$, but the dual-flat structure there is built from the Bogoliubov–Kubo–Mori (BKM) metric specifically — one element of the Petz family. The chapter does not generalise to "any quantum exponential family with any monotone metric"; the canonical structure is metric-specific. Blue's "direct substitution" reading of (b) underrepresents what Amari–Nagaoka actually prove.
- Friston-Wiese-Hobson 2020 licenses pan-system variational dynamics on Markov-blanket-bearing systems via Langevin SDE; it does not license the gauge-frame extension. The gauge-frame field is the manuscript's own addition without canonical FEP-literature precedent at pan-agentic scope.

On sub-claim (a) (load-bearing vs illustrative), the §1.5 text "Every system at every scale is treated as an agent ... an electron is a scale-0 agent" is a flat declarative across both ontological and constructive dimensions. The text does not signal a scope-split between ontological commitment and constructive deferral; blue imports that split from outside the text. The §1.7 disclaimer at line 150 "A rigorous quantum version of the framework does not currently exist" is a statement about quantum-extension *existence*, not about the §1.5 declaration being aspirational. Under the source-of-truth rule, the manuscript text controls — and the text reads as a load-bearing ontological commitment that requires constructive coherence at scale-0 to survive. Blue's defense imports the scope-split reading that the text does not authorise.

On sub-claim (c), the §1.7 disclaimer is honest about absence of construction but is silent on the gauge-group incompatibility (move 1) and on the Petz-family non-uniqueness (move 2). The disclaimer bounds completeness but not coherence; the §1.5 commitment is to "the same gauge-theoretic dynamics" at every scale, and the dynamics at scale-0 cannot be the same as at scale-$n$ if the gauge group changes from $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ to $U(\mathcal{H})$. The construction-debt acknowledgment cannot rescue a coherence-debt.

**Verdict on the compound claim under the source-of-truth rule.** Sub-claim (a) is false on the manuscript text (no scope-split signal in §1.5). Sub-claim (b) is supported in the weakest form (a density-operator manifold exists and has *some* monotone metric and *a* divergence) but not in the form the framework requires (a canonical metric-divergence pair compatible with $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{R})$ gauge-frame transport and inter-agent KL coupling). Sub-claim (c) covers the construction-debt but not the gauge-group-incompatibility coherence-debt. The compound claim that the position is internally coherent therefore fails on the gauge-group axis (move 1) and on the metric-non-uniqueness axis (move 2), independently of whether the construction is exhibited.

The position blue defends is recoverable, but only under a textual amendment to §1.5 that either (a) scopes the universal-dynamics clause to classical-mesoscale agents and demotes "an electron is a scale-0 agent" to acknowledged-aspirational, or (b) commits to a specific quantum gauge group (e.g., $\mathrm{GL}(K,\mathbb{C})$ or a unitary representation of a complexified gauge group) and a specific Petz monotone metric, with an exhibited reduction-to-classical at higher scales. The manuscript as written does neither.
