# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-intro-pan-agentic

## Concession

Red's ground (i) lands on the wording, not on the program. The manuscript's own text contains a genuine inconsistency between the substrate clause at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:488` ("The base space $\mathcal{C}$ is a smooth $n$-dimensional manifold representing the noumenal substrate over which agents maintain probability distributions") and the no-substrate clause at line 3414 ("there is no observer-independent physical substrate sitting underneath the agents, and physical reality is itself constituted by the gauge-theoretic information geometry of agents-at-scales"). Red is right that these two sentences cannot both be read at face value: the word "noumenal substrate" at line 488 is doing exactly the work that line 3414 disclaims. The §1.5 declaration "there are no non-agent physical things in the framework" inherits that tension. Blue grants this and treats it as a wording-level fault that the framework must repair textually by reclassifying $\mathcal{C}$ as a structural / parameter-space object (an abstract domain of relations, in line with the actual conceptual gloss already present at line 491: "$\mathcal{C}$ is not physical spacetime ... the abstract domain of inquiry upon which informational structure will be built"), and by retracting "noumenal substrate" at line 488 in favor of "parameter space / index manifold." The ontological program survives this repair; the present wording does not.

Blue does not concede grounds (ii) or (iii).

## Core attack

Red's load-bearing claim on ground (iii) is that "deferring phenomenal predicates while retaining the constitutive-by-aggregation move is constitutive structural panpsychism with phenomenal properties bracketed" (red opening, ground (iii)). This claim is wrong on the canonical taxonomy of philosophy of mind, and red's own citation to SEP §2.2 mis-locates the position.

Per the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Panpsychism (Goff, Seager, and Allen-Hermanson, 2024 revision), three distinctions are operative. Constitutive panpsychism (§2.2) is the view that macro-level consciousness "wholly consists in" facts about micro-level consciousness. Panprotopsychism (§2.3) is the view that "proto-consciousness is fundamental and ubiquitous," where protophenomenal properties are "properties that are not themselves forms of consciousness but which in combination give rise to forms of consciousness." Chalmers 2016 ("Panpsychism and Panprotopsychism," in Brüntrup and Jaskolla eds., *Panpsychism*, OUP) treats these as separate ontological commitments, with panprotopsychism explicitly *not* requiring that micro-systems carry phenomenal character.

The §1.5 paragraph at line 119, read together with §sec:consciousness_section line 3426 ("nothing in the formal construction settles whether the meta-agent's pullback metric $G_I$ inherits anything experiential from the constituent metrics $\{G_i\}$, or whether the inheritance fails at certain scale jumps, or whether scale-0 frames carry phenomenal character at all"), is in the panprotopsychism category and not in the constitutive-panpsychism category. The structural primitive ascribed to scale-0 agents is the tuple $(q, s, \phi)$ — not phenomenal experience, and not even proto-experience. The structural primitive is gauge-frame structure plus participation in variational dynamics. Whether anything experiential is to be ascribed at any scale, including scale-0, is left as an open research question — the SEP entry treats exactly this move ("suspending judgment about consciousness at fundamental levels") as "align[ing] with panprotopsychism or related views like mysterianism, which bracket consciousness itself while positing underlying properties," not as constitutive panpsychism.

Red's argument that "deferring the predicate is not denying it" attempts to convert panprotopsychism into constitutive panpsychism by stipulation. That move requires arguing that the framework's structural primitives $(q, s, \phi)$ themselves *are* proto-phenomenal or phenomenal properties, which red does not argue and which the framework does not claim. The framework's commitment is that scale-0 systems carry gauge-frame structure; whether that structure is protophenomenal in the Chalmers 2016 sense is precisely the open question that the manuscript registers as open. Red's own falsification condition #3 in the red opening anticipates this distinction and grants that "a citation from Chalmers 2016 or Goff 2017 establishes that deferring phenomenal predicates while retaining the constitutive-aggregation structure is not constitutive panpsychism in the technical sense, and falls into a recognized fourth category (e.g., panprotopsychism with the protophenomenal predicates not yet assigned)" would partially defeat ground (iii). That citation is the SEP §2.3 entry as quoted above, plus Chalmers 2016 §§II–III where the constitutive / panprotopsychist distinction is drawn explicitly. Ground (iii) is therefore defeated *in red's own falsification terms*: the framework is panprotopsychist with the proto-phenomenal predicate left unassigned, which is a different position than constitutive panpsychism and which red has conceded is a fourth category.

## Defense

Ground (ii) — the constructive-insufficiency objection — does not falsify the claim. Two defenses, stated jointly.

First, the type-theoretic reading of §1.5 is the load-bearing reading and it is intact. The agent definition at lines 617-625 attaches the tuple $(q_i, p_i, s_i, r_i, \phi_i)$ to any smooth section of the associated bundles $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{state}} \times \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{model}}$ over any subdomain $\mathcal{U}_i \subseteq \mathcal{C}$. Nothing in that definition restricts the support domain to cognitive systems or to any particular physical scale; the definition is universally applicable as a type signature. The §1.5 commitment that "every system at every scale is treated as an agent" is therefore a coherent universal-applicability claim about a definition that is itself constructed independently of any particular scale instantiation. Constructive exhibition at every scale is not what licenses a type-theoretic ontology; the type signature is what licenses it. Compare physicalism: physicalism does not require that every macroscopic system be reductively exhibited as a Hamiltonian over its constituent quarks before the physicalist ontology can be coherently held. The reducibility-in-principle is the ontological commitment; the constructive exhibition is the research program. Red's argument that "the electron is not even constructed at this degenerate level" (red opening, ground (ii)) is a research-program complaint, not an ontology-falsifier.

Second, the manuscript at line 146 honestly registers the constructive limit: "A rigorous quantum version of the framework does not currently exist; the structural parallels with QBism and other relational interpretations are suggestive rather than derived." The pan-agentic claim does not assert that quantum systems (including electrons) are already mapped to the framework's current real-Gaussian sections — the current framework is classical. The pan-agentic commitment is that the framework, properly extended to quantum dynamics, applies. This is structurally identical to Friston, Wiese, and Hobson 2020 ("Sentience and the Origin of Consciousness," *Entropy* 22:516), whose Markov-blanket-everywhere claim does not require that every physical system have a currently-specified Langevin SDE before the Markovian-monism commitment is coherently held. The same in-principle / research-program structure is operating in both cases.

On the Whitehead positioning attack: red argues that "the framework's reliance on a noumenal base manifold $\mathcal{C}$ as the domain of agent sections is the opposite of Whitehead's no-substrate posture." Per the Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Whitehead (Desmet and Irvine, "Alfred North Whitehead"), Whitehead's extensive continuum is "*not* an ontological substrate but rather a structural/relational framework that emerges from actual occasions," with continuity being "*not* something which is 'given' [but] something which is achieved." The extensive continuum is the system of part-whole and topological relations among actual occasions, not a separate ontological layer. Under the textual repair conceded above (line 488 reclassified from "noumenal substrate" to "parameter space / index manifold," consistent with the conceptual gloss already at line 491), $\mathcal{C}$ plays exactly Whitehead's extensive-continuum role: an abstract framework of relations among agentic sections, not a substrate beneath them. The Whitehead positioning is then correct under the repaired wording. Red's attack succeeds against the present wording of line 488 (which blue conceded above) but does not survive the wording repair.

On the Lahav-Neemeh "stronger / weaker" attack: red argues that "denying a substrate posits *fewer* entities, not more," and concludes the position is ontologically weaker than Lahav-Neemeh, not stronger. This conflates strength-of-commitment in the entity-counting sense with strength-of-commitment in the explanatory-reach sense. The §1.5 claim "stronger than Lahav-Neemeh's relational physicalism" is explicated at line 3392 to mean that pan-agentic structuralism makes the multi-scale Alice/Bob composition (eq:alice_bob_composition at line 3398) derivable as a composition of transports, where Lahav-Neemeh's single-scale relational frame leaves the cross-scale factor unspecified. That is a strength along the explanatory-reach axis (more cross-scale derivations covered), not along the entity-counting axis (where red is correct that fewer entities are posited). The text's word "stronger" is doing work along the explanatory-reach axis. This is a defensible reading; red's argument requires that "stronger" can only mean entity-counting strength, which is not the only standard reading.

## Falsification conditions

Blue's position fails if any of the following holds.

1. The SEP entry §2.3 on panprotopsychism is shown to require that protophenomenal predicates be *assigned* to micro-systems for the category to apply, rather than allowing the assignment question to remain open. If so, the framework would fall outside panprotopsychism into "structural realism with phenomenology omitted," which is also distinct from constitutive panpsychism but would force a more substantial textual repair to §1.5.
2. The line-488 / line-3414 substrate contradiction is shown to be load-bearing in the actual mathematical construction (not just in the wording) — for instance, if the meta-agent formation definition at lines 685-691 cannot be reformulated without treating $\mathcal{C}$ as a non-agent substrate. If so, the textual repair conceded above is not available and ground (i) is sustained against the program, not just the wording.
3. A primary-source citation from Chalmers 2016 or Goff 2017 is shown to treat "structural primitives with phenomenal character left open" as falling specifically inside constitutive panpsychism (i.e., red's reading of SEP §2.2 is shown to be the technically correct one and the SEP §2.3 panprotopsychism category does not apply). If so, ground (iii) is sustained.
