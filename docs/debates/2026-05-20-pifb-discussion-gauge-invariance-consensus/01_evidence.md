## Canon excerpts (teams should expand)

### Kretschmann-Norton-gauge canon

- **Kretschmann, E. (1917)**, "Über den physikalischen Sinn der Relativitätspostulate," *Annalen der Physik* 53, 575. The original Kretschmann objection — already cited in manuscript.
- **Norton, J. D. (1993)**, "General covariance and the foundations of general relativity: eight decades of dispute," *Reports on Progress in Physics* 56(7), 791-858. Already cited.
- **Brown, H. R., Brading, K. (2003)**, "General covariance from the perspective of Noether's theorems," *Philosophical Studies*. Modern treatment of the issue.
- **Earman, J. (2003)**, "The cosmological constant, the fate of the universe, unimodular gravity, and all that," *Studies in History and Philosophy of Modern Physics* 34(3), 559-577. Discusses gauge-invariance philosophical issues.

### Gauge group canon (relevant to 3297 GL(K,C) ⊃ U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3))

- **Peskin, M. E., Schroeder, D. V. (1995)**, *An Introduction to Quantum Field Theory*. Standard QFT textbook covering the Standard Model gauge groups.
- **Weinberg, S. (1996)**, *The Quantum Theory of Fields, Volume II: Modern Applications*. Comprehensive treatment of gauge theories.
- **Mathematical fact**: GL(K, C) for K ≥ 3 contains U(K) as a maximal compact subgroup, which contains U(1) × SU(K) × SU(K-2) ... etc. For K large enough, all Standard Model groups embed. The specific claim at 3297 is correct but requires K ≥ some threshold; the manuscript does not specify K.

The concession says cognitive shareability "does not deliver" gauge invariance proper; the subsequent paragraphs continue to develop the cognitive-shareability reading. 4. Whether the "intelligent species might construct incompatible physics" claim at 3291 should be tagged as thought experiment (the 3281-3283 paragraph already does this; 3291 is in a separate paragraph and not similarly tagged).
5. Whether the GL(K,C) ⊃ Standard Model claim at 3297 requires a K-dimensional specification. The manuscript's K is the belief-fiber dimension; for K small (e.g., K=4) the embedding may not hold for all three groups.
6. Whether the Carhart-Harris and Friston 2019 "REBUS and the anarchic brain" paper should be cited as the closer comparison literature for the framework's altered-states interpretation. REBUS treats psychedelics via the Free Energy Principle directly.

Teams should verify points 1-3 against primary sources; points 4-6 are editorial improvements.
