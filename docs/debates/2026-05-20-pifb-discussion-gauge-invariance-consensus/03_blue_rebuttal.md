(verbatim, `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3275`). The phrase "Within the interpretive reading explored here, altered states are modeled as..." marks the gauge-frame-shift mechanism explicitly as framework-internal modeling, not as a claim about what the cited Carhart-Harris 2014 paper itself establishes. That bracket precisely addresses the worry red raises — that the cited paper's mechanism vocabulary is cortical entropy / self-organized criticality / DMN disorganization (`Carhart-Harris, R. L., et al. (2014), Frontiers in Human Neuroscience 8, 20`, evidence pack line 36) while the manuscript proposes a gauge-frame-shift mechanism. The 3275 opening clause "Within the interpretive reading explored here, altered states are modeled as..." is the bracket; "represents these states as different agent-relative geometries; it does not adjudicate their phenomenal status or their veridicality" is a second, additional bracket on phenomenal status.

What does survive of R2 is the citation gap to REBUS. The manuscript cites the entropic-brain paper but not the Free-Energy-Principle paper that ties psychedelics directly to the framework's own register (precision-weighting / prior collapse, which is the FEP-native account of psychedelic action). Without `CarhartHarrisFriston2019`, the citation chain from Carhart-Harris 2014 (entropic, neuroimaging mechanism) to the framework's gauge-frame reading (information-geometric mechanism) skips the bridge paper. That is an editorial gap, not a calibration failure of the 3275 prose itself.

We acknowledge this at the outset rather than at the end of the section. D. (1993), Reports on Progress in Physics 56(7), 791-858`, the canonical modern treatment of the Kretschmann objection). At 3299 the closing paragraph reasserts "this view may be unfalsifiable" and at 3303 it admits "Whether this constitutes genuine explanation or merely relabeling remains debatable" (verbatim, `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3299, 3303`). Red explicitly concedes that the 3263 paragraph is correctly registered and that the 3267 walk-back attack from the dispatch brief is the weakest available and drops it (red opening evidence bullet 4). So both teams agree the Kretschmann-Norton concession holds in place.

Given the triple bracket, the three live red attacks all reduce to small in-place editorial calibration patches rather than structural failures of the subsection's epistemic register. Specifically:

For R3: a parenthetical at 3291 of the form "(this remains a counterfactual thought experiment, as flagged in the preceding subsubsection)". The sentence retains its content but the tag becomes in-place rather than relying on cross-subsubsection inheritance.

The honest concession across the three attacks is that R1 + R2 (citation) + R3 are three small editorial patches the manuscript owes. The structural defense is that the triple bracket at 3230 + 3263 + 3299-3303 globally calibrates the subsection's epistemic status correctly, and that the 3275 in-place hedge "Within the interpretive reading explored here, altered states are modeled as..." already brackets the gauge-frame-shift mechanism as framework-internal.
