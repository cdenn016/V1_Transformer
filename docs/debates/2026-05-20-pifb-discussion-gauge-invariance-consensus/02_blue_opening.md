## Evidence

- **Opening Status disclaimer is maximally strong.** `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3230` opens with "Status: this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation." The paragraph closes with "Read the following as a permitted framework-internal interpretation that the formalism is consistent with, not as a claim the formalism establishes." A reader encounters the unfalsifiability admission, the metaphysical-interpretation tag, and the consistent-with-not-establishing register in the first paragraph.

- **Kretschmann-Norton concession is in-place and load-bearing.** `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3263` cites Kretschmann 1917 [Kretschmann1917] and Norton 1993 [Norton1993] and registers the (a)/(b) distinction explicitly: "(a) coordinate invariance / general covariance, which is essentially a notational requirement on the formulation of physical laws, and (b) gauge invariance proper, which is a structural property of the action functional with specific empirical consequences (mass generation via Higgs, confinement in QCD, the chirality of the weak interaction). Cognitive shareability, as an intersubjectivity constraint on the form of theoretical statements, would at most motivate (a); it does not deliver (b)." The paragraph closes with "Whether the cognitive-shareability criterion can be sharpened into a constraint that selects among gauge structures with empirical content remains open." The (a)-only restriction is the binding concession, and the manuscript states it without qualification.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold.

1. **Kretschmann-Norton walk-back.** If primary-source review of Norton 1993 [Norton1993 §5] establishes that the (a)/(b) distinction has a more specific technical form (e.g., requiring the action functional to be expressed in a particular form for (b) to hold) and the prose at 3267 implicitly violates that specific form — for instance, by treating gauge invariance as a property of the *formalism* rather than of the *action functional* — then the concession at 3263 is honored only in name and the substantive prose at 3267-3269 quietly walks it back. The conditional "consistent with --- but does not establish" at 3269 reads to me as the correct (a)-register vocabulary, but a Norton-1993 scholar could establish that the manuscript's "cognitive shareability constraint" framing is itself an instance of the (b)-style claim Norton forbids.

2. **Altered-states over-claim.** If Carhart-Harris and Friston 2019 [REBUS, Pharmacological Reviews 71(3)] establishes that the active-inference / Free Energy treatment of psychedelics has a specific structure (relaxed beliefs under psychedelics, REBUS = RElaxed Beliefs Under pSychedelics) that contradicts or substantively differs from the manuscript's "gauge-frame shifts breaking consensus pullback geometry" interpretation, and the manuscript's framing without citing REBUS misrepresents the active-inference psychedelics canon, then the altered-states paragraph at 3273-3279 has a citation gap that goes beyond editorial improvement — it would mean the manuscript reads the psychedelic phenomenon through Carhart-Harris 2014 (entropic brain, pre-REBUS) and skips the closer active-inference comparison literature, creating an apparent novelty where a closer match exists.

3. **Wigner citation gap.** If the "unreasonable effectiveness" rhetorical question at 3301 is read by a careful reader as quoting Wigner 1960 specifically (rather than alluding to a now-generic phrase) and the absence of the citation [Wigner1960] is therefore a scholarship failure rather than a colloquial-allusion choice, the citation patch is required. The evidence pack at point 3 already flags this; my position is that this is a one-citation editorial improvement, but a strict reading of citation discipline could promote it to a calibration defect.

4. **GL(K,C) ⊃ SM subgroup K-dependence.** If the framework's K is fixed by the construction at a value (e.g., K = 4 for the belief-fiber dimension) at which U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) does not embed in GL(K, C), then the 3297 claim is K-dependently false for the framework's K and the limitation paragraph at 3297 does not discharge the defect — the claim asserts containment "as subgroups" without K specification. I treat this as remote because the manuscript explicitly frames K as variable and registers that selection is not predicted, but a code-side check of the canonical K in `transformer/vfe/` and `BlockConfig` could falsify the position.

5. **Intelligent-species sentence at 3291 escapes the thought-experiment frame.** If a careful reader, encountering line 3291 in isolation, would read "Different intelligent species with radically different cognitive architectures might construct incompatible physics from the same noumenal substrate. They would not be wrong while we are right, or vice versa" as a substantive assertion rather than a continuation of the 3281-3283 Evolutionary Thought Experiment, then the 3291 sentence needs its own parenthetical thought-experiment tag. My position is that the paragraph order (thought-experiment frame at 3281-3283, then "Physics as Theory of Informational Compatibility" at 3285-3291 continuing the thought-experiment register) carries the tag forward; the closing-paragraph "may be unfalsifiable" at 3299 retroactively bounds the entire content. A one-clause tightening at 3291 would be a defensive edit, not a required correction.
