# Red Rebuttal — pifb-theory-section-purity

Blue's reading of the manuscript at line 574 against Kobayashi--Nomizu Vol.~I Ch.~II is correct: the right-action / fiber-preserving axiom $\pi(n\cdot g)=\pi(n)$ matches [KobayashiNomizu1963 Vol.~I Ch.~II §5], the associated-bundle equivalence relation at line 596 matches [Nakahara2003 §10.1.1], and the local-trivialization caveat at lines 577--581 (no global section unless the bundle is trivializable) is honest and well-flagged against [Steenrod1951 §3]. That concession is, however, the maximum I can grant. Blue defends the omnibus claim by sliding it from "the entire Theory section is mathematically pure and correct against the standard literature" to "the standard parts match and the novel parts are correctly labeled." That slide is itself the load-bearing admission: the claim in `00_claim.md` line 11 reads "is theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature," and an omnibus defended only by re-classifying half its content as "labeled extensions / structural commitments / research directions" is not the omnibus the user asked about.

## Core attack

Blue's defenses of C6, C7, and C5 each rely on a literature identification that the manuscript itself partially retracts or contradicts. I take them in order.

**C6. The "gauge-invariant connection coefficient" claim at line 845 contradicts the standard lattice gauge transformation law Blue cites at line 842.** Blue argues at the bottom of its C6 section that the Wilson-link parameterization $\Omega_{ij}=U_i\exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G)U_j^{-1}$ is "a vertex-trivialized parameterization of a lattice gauge link variable" with transformation law $\Omega_{ij}\to g_i\Omega_{ij}g_j^{-1}$, identical to [Creutz1983 Ch.~5, Eq.~(5.1); WilsonConfinement1974 §II, Eq.~(12)]. That is correct for the *link* $\Omega_{ij}$ as a whole. The problem is the next sentence in the manuscript, at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` line 845: *"The connection coefficients $\delta_{ij}$ are gauge-invariant in this parameterization: \eqref{eq:omega_gauge_law} acts entirely through the vertex factors $U_i$ and leaves the central $\exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G)$ untouched."* In the standard lattice gauge theory Blue cites, the connection variable inside a link is **not** gauge-invariant; it transforms covariantly under the vertex group action [Creutz1983 Ch.~5, Eq.~(5.1)], and the gauge-invariant objects are class-function traces of *closed* Wilson loops, not the connection coefficients themselves. The manuscript's "gauge-invariant $\delta_{ij}$" statement is true only *given* a specific convention for which factor of the product $U_i\exp(\delta)U_j^{-1}$ absorbs the gauge dressing — i.e., the connection has been gauge-fixed by hand, not made gauge-invariant. Blue's own citation [Creutz1983 Ch.~5] convicts this passage.

The manuscript then makes the implementation consequence explicit at line 878: *"The implementation parameterizes $\delta_{ij}$ via either a learnable bilinear $\delta_{ij}^a=\mu_i^\top W^a\mu_j$ or a small feedforward network on the concatenated belief means, both of which see the inputs in the embedding ambient frame rather than in a per-vertex rotated frame... Full per-vertex gauge equivariance of the connection module --- passing $U_i^{-1}\mu_i$ rather than $\mu_i$ into the bilinear --- is a separate architectural commitment beyond the scope of the discrete Regime~II structure formalized here."* This is the manuscript admitting in plain text that the Regime~II construction as formalized in §sec:discrete\_regime\_ii is *not* gauge-equivariant under the full local action of [Creutz1983 Ch.~5 Eq.~(5.1)]. C6 therefore fails the literature test under the very source Blue cites in its defense.

**C7a. The Lie-algebra arithmetic average $\phi_I=\sum_i w_i\phi_i/\sum_i w_i$ at line 891 is not "a parameterization" of a Karcher mean; it is a different object.** Blue claims at the end of its C7 section that this average "is the low-curvature linearization of a Riemannian center of mass on the group $G$" and cites [Karcher1977 §1; Pennec2006 §3]. The Karcher mean is the intrinsic Riemannian center of mass $\bar g=\arg\min_g\sum_i w_i d_G(g,g_i)^2$ where $d_G$ is the Riemannian distance on the group, and the first-order optimality condition is $\sum_i w_i\log_{\bar g}(g_i)=0$ in the tangent space at $\bar g$ [Pennec2006 §3, Fréchet-mean characterisation]. The arithmetic Lie-algebra average $\sum_i w_i\phi_i/\sum_i w_i$ coincides with the Karcher mean only when (i) the $\phi_i$ are computed in a *common* tangent frame and (ii) the spread of the $\phi_i$ is small enough that the BCH corrections of order $\frac{1}{2}[\phi_i,\phi_j]$ are negligible. For non-commuting $\phi_i$ with appreciable spread these conditions fail: $\exp(\sum_i w_i\phi_i)\neq$ the Karcher mean of $\{\exp(\phi_i)\}$ in general. Blue concedes this implicitly by acknowledging "the leading-order approximation" language, but the manuscript at line 891 punts the BCH accuracy bound to `Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_variational}`. Whether that section actually proves the bound is a downstream commitment, not a derivation in the Theory section being claimed to be "theoretically pure." Until the bound is supplied, the cross-scale construction $\Omega_{i,I}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_I)$ rests on a meta-frame that is uncontrolled outside the small-spread regime; this is a labeled commitment, not a discharged proof.

**C7b. The "$\Omega_{ij}$ and $\tilde\Omega_{ij}$ act as the same group element in two different representations" reading at line 887 is precise only at the abstract-group level.** Blue's defense at the end of its C7 section ("$\Omega_{ij}$ and $\tilde\Omega_{ij}$ act as the same group element in two different representations (the fundamental on $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}}$ and the model-fiber representation on $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}}$)") is the charitable reading. The manuscript's text at line 887 reads: *"the model-fiber transport $\tilde{\Omega}_{ij}:\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}}\to\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}}$, which equals $\Omega_{ij}$ as a group element (the same gauge transformation) but acts on a different fiber."* The precise statement, written against [Nakahara2003 §10.1, associated-bundle construction], would be: $\Omega_{ij}$ and $\tilde\Omega_{ij}$ are the *images* of the same abstract group element $g_{ij}=\exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)\in G$ under two distinct representations $\rho_{\mathrm{state}}:G\to\mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}})$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{model}}:G\to\mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}})$, and "equals as a group element" is shorthand that requires those representations to act on fibers of dimensions $K$ and $M$ that may differ. The manuscript does not write the two representations $\rho_{\mathrm{state}},\rho_{\mathrm{model}}$ down explicitly anywhere in §sec:transport\_hierarchy; in §sec:working\_framework at line 924 it then *collapses* the two fibers to a single $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}}=:\mathcal{B}$ "by construction" via the cross-scale shadow relation. Blue's two-representation reading is therefore not what the working framework actually uses; the working framework matches state and model on a single fiber. The omnibus claim of literature-match cannot rest on a precise abstract-group reading that the manuscript's own simplifications then discard.

**C5. The closure inequality at line 711 is not a Wilson/Cardy block-spin condition.** Blue cites [Wilson1971 Phys.~Rev.~B 4:3174 §III; Cardy1996 Ch.~3]. The block-spin renormalization-group construction in [Wilson1971] requires a *spectral* separation of scales in the renormalization-group flow — short-wavelength modes are integrated out because their characteristic energy scale is parametrically larger than the modes being retained, and the relevant operator on the flow has a gap separating relevant from irrelevant directions. The inequality in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` at line 711--713 is a *graph-theoretic* statement: $\sum_{i,j\in A}\gamma_{ij}\mathrm{KL}(s_i\|\tilde\Omega_{ij}s_j)/\sum_{i,j\in A}\gamma_{ij}\ll\sum_{i\in A,k\notin A}\gamma_{ik}\mathrm{KL}(s_i\|\tilde\Omega_{ik}s_k)/\sum_{i\in A,k\notin A}\gamma_{ik}$. This is structurally the form of [Newman2006 PNAS 103:8577, modularity-based community detection], not [Wilson1971 §III]. The manuscript at line 715 then claims the relation "is formalized by the constrained spectral gap $\lambda_{I,w}>0$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_constrained_gap}," forward-referencing to the meta-agent RG section. Blue verifies the cross-reference is honored (label exists at line 4383). What the cross-reference does *not* discharge is the gap between the inequality of Eq.~culture\_closure and a derived RG sufficient condition: the manuscript at line 715 calls it "the boundary condition that distinguishes a region of low internal disagreement from a genuine coarse-graining target," which is the language of an *ansatz*, not a theorem. An ansatz consistent with Wilson 1971 in spirit is not "mathematically correct against [Wilson1971]" — that would require deriving the inequality from RG flow, which neither the Theory section nor (under Blue's own reading) §sec:meta\_agent\_rg actually does.

**The omnibus collapse.** Each of C6, C7a, C7b, C5 is "registered as a labeled extension / structural commitment" — exactly as Blue's pre-emption at the bottom of its opening predicts. But the user's claim was not "the labeled extensions are honestly labeled." The user's claim was "the entire \section{Theory}... is theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature." A claim of literature-match cannot be defended by aggregating four registered commitments to the same downstream section (§sec:meta\_agent\_variational, §sec:meta\_agent\_rg, §sec:participatory) and asserting that the registration itself constitutes purity. The Theory section in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` is, under Blue's own concessions, a research program with open commitments at C5 (RG closure), C6 (Regime~II gauge-equivariance of the connection module), C7a (BCH bound on the meta-frame), and C7b (state/model representation pair), plus the labeled-as-novel cross-scale shadow (line 540) and multi-agent free-energy functional (line 1021). Blue's re-framing concedes the omnibus.

## Defense

Against Blue's strongest pushback — that the manuscript registers every divergence as a structural commitment and therefore "labeled correctly + standard parts match" is the right reading of "theoretically pure" — three counter-points.

First, "labeled correctly" is the methodological-honesty test, not the literature-match test the user requested. The user's directive in `00_claim.md` line 16 was unambiguous: *"teams should consult the literature as source of truth."* The literature-as-source-of-truth test for a claim of the form "X matches Y" requires producing the explicit citation where X matches Y, not a registration that X-prime is a novel modification of Y. By that test C1 passes against [KobayashiNomizu1963 Vol.~I Ch.~II §5]; C2 passes against [Amari2016 §2.4 and §3.5]; C3 group-level version passes against [Hall2015 §3.5]. C4 fails because [LahavNeemeh2022 Front.~Psychol.~12:704270] supplies no Lie-algebra-valued field — the manuscript at line 770 explicitly says "their account... does not write such a law down... The construction in this section supplies it," which is exactly the inverse of literature-match: the literature is silent and the manuscript fills the silence with a $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ structure that the literature did not request. C5 fails because [Wilson1971 §III] requires a spectral separation of scales in the RG flow, not a graph-theoretic intra/inter coupling inequality of the form in [Newman2006 PNAS 103:8577]. C6 fails because the manuscript's own line 845 "gauge-invariant $\delta_{ij}$" contradicts [Creutz1983 Ch.~5 Eq.~(5.1)]. C7a fails until the BCH accuracy bound is supplied (Blue's defense punts to §sec:meta\_agent\_variational). C7b fails at the level the working framework uses the two operators (matched-bundle simplification at line 924). That is four to five sub-claim failures by the literature-as-source-of-truth test, plus two labeled-novel extensions Blue's pre-emption section explicitly concedes (cross-scale shadow at line 540, multi-agent functional at line 1021).

Second, Blue's defense of C6 elides the distinction between gauge invariance and gauge fixing. Blue writes that the manuscript's parameterization "exposes the Regime~I cocycle $\Omega_{ij}=U_iU_j^{-1}$ as the $\delta_{ij}=0$ special case, and the curvature-free / vanishing-holonomy structure is recovered exactly there." That is correct — and irrelevant. The issue is not whether $\delta_{ij}=0$ recovers a flat bundle (it does, against [Nakahara2003 §10.5]); the issue is whether $\delta_{ij}$ for $\delta_{ij}\neq 0$ is a gauge-invariant quantity in the sense [Creutz1983 Ch.~5] uses the term. It is not. In [Creutz1983 Ch.~5, Eq.~(5.1)] the gauge-invariant objects are traces of closed Wilson loops; the link-internal connection has the gauge-covariant transformation $\delta\to g_i\,\delta\,g_j^{-1}$ inherited from the link, *not* the trivial transformation $\delta\to\delta$ that line 845 claims. The manuscript's "gauge-invariant in this parameterization" is correct only as a statement about a particular factorization choice that the manuscript has imposed unilaterally; it is not the gauge invariance of [Creutz1983 Ch.~5]. Calling it the latter is a category error.

Third, Blue's defense of C7a invokes Karcher 1977 / Pennec 2006 as if the linear Lie-algebra average were a labeled small-spread approximation of the Karcher mean. The honest reading is that the manuscript does not state which regime of $\{\phi_i\}$ the linearization is valid in, does not bound the error, and does not state in §sec:transport\_hierarchy what intrinsic mean it is approximating. [Pennec2006 J.~Math.~Imag.~Vis.~25:127, §3] makes the intrinsic-extrinsic distinction explicit and warns that extrinsic averages on non-flat manifolds are biased estimators of the Riemannian centre of mass even in the small-spread regime, with the bias controlled by the Riemann curvature of the manifold. For the gauge group $G$ this bias is generically nonzero. The manuscript supplies neither the bound nor the bias correction; Blue's defense punts to a downstream section. That punt is itself the point: the Theory section as it stands does not contain the proof of literature-match Blue's defense invokes.

By the literature-as-source-of-truth test the user demanded, the omnibus claim that the entire Theory section is "theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature" fails at C4, C5, C6, and C7 (a and b). C1, C2, and C3 (group-level) survive. That is a partial-match outcome, not the omnibus the user asked about.
