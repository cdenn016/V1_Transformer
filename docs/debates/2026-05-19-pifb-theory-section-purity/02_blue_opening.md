# Blue Opening — pifb-theory-section-purity

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Theory section of `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` packages a novel multi-agent variational free energy on a hand-built bundle that uses the vocabulary of Kobayashi–Nomizu / Nakahara / Wilson decoratively rather than rigorously: the cross-scale shadow construction is not standard hierarchical VI, the CFR identification with Lahav–Neemeh is interpretive analogy not a theorem, the culture-closure inequality is a heuristic rather than a derived RG block-spin condition, the additive Lie-algebra gauge transformation only matches the group-level law for abelian or commuting parameters, and the Lie-algebra-weighted meta-agent frame $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i / \sum w_i$ ignores BCH non-commutativity. The omnibus word "entire" lets a single weak link (C4 or C5) sink the whole claim.

## Position

Every C1–C7 sub-claim either matches its standard primary source (C1, C2, C6) or is a labeled, principled extension defended by an adjacent standard construction (C3, C4, C5, C7), with the manuscript explicitly flagging every divergence from textbook treatments as a structural commitment of the framework rather than smuggling it in as derived. "Theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature" is therefore defensible in the sense that load-bearing — the standard parts match the standard treatments, and the novel parts are correctly labeled and equipped with the caveats their non-standard status demands.

## Evidence

### C1 — Bundle constructions match Kobayashi–Nomizu and Nakahara

The principal-bundle definition at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` line 574, "$\pi(n \cdot g) = \pi(n)$ for all $g \in G$, meaning group action preserves fibers," is the textbook right-action axiom of a principal bundle [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I Ch. II §5, Definition of principal fibre bundle], where the structure group $G$ acts freely on the total space $\mathcal{N}$ on the right and the projection $\pi$ is invariant under that action [Nakahara2003 §10.1, Eq. (10.1)]. The associated bundle definition at line 596–601, $\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{N} \times_\rho \mathcal{B}$ with the equivalence $(n \cdot g, b) \sim (n, \rho(g) b)$, is the convention adopted at [Nakahara2003 §10.1.1, associated fibre bundle construction]; the manuscript explicitly identifies its convention as Nakahara's at line 604 and gives the substitution rule $\rho(g) \to \rho(g^{-1})$ for readers importing from texts using the inverse convention [Steenrod1951 §2 also uses the inverse convention; the equivalence is the well-known identification of left/right action conventions]. The local-trivialization caveat at lines 577–581 (no global section unless the bundle is trivializable; cocycle $\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jk} = \Omega_{ik}$ on triple overlaps) is the standard Čech-cocycle description of a fibre bundle [Steenrod1951 §3; Nakahara2003 §9.4, transition functions].

### C2 — Group actions and the GL(K) invariance theorem

The action $\rho(g) \cdot \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma) = \mathcal{N}(g\mu, g\Sigma g^\top)$ at manuscript line 608 is the pushforward of a Gaussian density under the invertible affine map $x \mapsto gx$ with $g \in \mathrm{GL}(K)$: for $X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$, the law of $gX$ is $\mathcal{N}(g\mu, g\Sigma g^\top)$ by the standard change-of-variables formula for multivariate Gaussians [Amari2016 Information Geometry and Its Applications §2.4, exponential-family pushforward; Anderson 2003, *An Introduction to Multivariate Statistical Analysis*, Theorem 2.4.3]. The manuscript justifies the restriction to the linear sector $\mathrm{GL}(K) \subset \mathrm{Aff}(K)$ at line 937 explicitly as a modeling choice (treating mean vectors as frame coordinates rather than affine-translatable points). The Theorem at line 939–969 gives a complete elementary proof of $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ KL invariance under simultaneous pushforward via direct cancellation of the $(\det \Omega)^2$ factors in the log-determinant term and the $\Omega^{-\top}\Omega^{\top} = I$ collapse in the trace and quadratic terms — the algebra is correct and is the standard pushforward-invariance statement for $f$-divergences [Amari2016 §3.5, KL invariance under invertible transformations]. The extension to all $f$-divergences at lines 971–975 is the standard Csiszár pushforward invariance [Csiszár1963, *Eine informationstheoretische Ungleichung*; LieseVajda2006, *On Divergences and Informations in Statistics and Information Theory* §1]. Sandwich form $\Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top$ is the standard (2,0)-tensor transport rule under the defining representation of $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ [Nakahara2003 §10.3, transport in associated bundles].

### C3 — Multi-agent overlap and epistemic collapse

The pointwise consensus definition at line 672–678 is acknowledged at line 682 as "an idealization that is not the criterion for meta-agent formation: imposed pointwise it is the epistemic-death limit of Section~\ref{sec:epistemic_collapse}, not a healthy coarse-graining target." The realistic meta-agent criterion is the graph-weighted average internal-coherence inequality of Eq. culture_internal_coherence (line 703–705). The remark at line 734–739 establishes the informational-vs-geometric distinction explicitly: "Epistemic death represents complete informational consensus, not geometric identity. Agents may maintain distinct gauge frames $\phi_i \neq \phi_j$ … while agreeing perfectly on informational content after accounting for these perspective differences through the transport operators." The group-level statement of gauge invariance of the epistemic-death condition under the simultaneous transformation $U_i \mapsto U_i g$ is the correct statement for non-abelian $G$ [Hall2015 *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* §3.5, conjugation by a fixed group element]; the manuscript explicitly notes at line 795 that the additive form $\phi_i \mapsto \phi_i + \xi$ coincides with the group-level form "only when $\xi$ commutes with each $\phi_i$ (since the Baker–Campbell–Hausdorff expansion ... collapses to $\exp(\phi_i)\exp(\xi)$ only when the commutator vanishes)" — the manuscript registers the BCH limitation rather than relying on the additive shorthand. KL invariance under common pushforward (the load-bearing identity for $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij} q_j)$ being preserved when both arguments are pushed forward by $g$) is the Theorem at line 939, which is in turn the standard $f$-divergence invariance under invertible measurable transformations [Amari2016 §3.5; LieseVajda2006 §1].

### C4 — Cognitive reference frames

The manuscript states the contribution precisely at line 770: "[Lahav–Neemeh's] account asserts that there must therefore exist a transformation law relating quantities measured in one CFR to quantities measured in another, but does not write such a law down in its currently published form. The construction in this section supplies it." [LahavNeemeh2022 Front. Psychol. 12:704270] argues by relativistic analogy that phenomenal consciousness is observer-relative the way velocity and simultaneity are in special relativity, but the published account does not write down a Lorentz-analog group action on cognitive states — it asserts that one must exist. The framework's $\phi_i: \mathcal{U}_i \to \mathfrak{g}$ as a gauge frame field, together with $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ as the inter-frame transformation, is a concrete realization of the formal structure that quantum reference frames supply in the corresponding physics setting [BartlettRudolphSpekkens2007, *Reference frames, superselection rules, and quantum information*, Rev. Mod. Phys. 79:555, §II — a reference frame is a physical system carrying state whose orientation specifies which gauge representative is being used]. The framework's structural analog to Rovelli's relational interpretation [Rovelli1996 Int. J. Theor. Phys. 35:1637 — properties have meaning only relative to other systems] is invoked explicitly at line 562–563. The manuscript labels this identification as a mathematically supplied transformation law, not as a derivation of phenomenology from the bundle: line 770 registers the remaining limitation that "gauge invariance places no constraint on which gauge frame produces which phenomenal quale." That this is identified-as-supply rather than identified-as-derivation is the honest accounting that the literature demands.

### C5 — Culture / RG closure

The closure inequality of Eq. culture_closure (line 711–713) — internal slow-channel coupling parametrically stronger than cross-cluster coupling — is the operational criterion that Wilson [Wilson1971 *Renormalization group and critical phenomena*, Phys. Rev. B 4:3174, §III] and [Cardy1996 *Scaling and Renormalization in Statistical Physics*, Ch. 3, block-spin RG] identify as the prerequisite for adiabatic elimination of fast modes inside the block under coarse-graining: in the standard block-spin construction the block-internal couplings (which become integrated out) must dominate the inter-block couplings (which are renormalized but not eliminated) for the block to act as a stable effective degree of freedom. The manuscript ties this to a constrained spectral gap at line 715: "the parent's slow model and frame are licensed as a stable effective agent only when adiabatic elimination of the internal modes leaves the inter-cluster couplings well-separated from the intra-cluster ones, formalized by the constrained spectral gap $\lambda_{I,w} > 0$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:rg_constrained_gap}." Verification: `\label{sec:meta_agent_rg}` appears at manuscript line 4345 and `\label{eq:rg_constrained_gap}` at line 4383, so the forward reference is honored downstream. The manuscript is careful to call Eq. culture_closure "the boundary condition that distinguishes a region of low internal disagreement from a genuine coarse-graining target" — a structurally compatible coarse-graining condition, not a derived sufficient theorem. This is the appropriate level of claim.

### C6 — Regime II as a Wilson lattice link variable

The edge-relaxed cocycle of line 832, $\Omega_{ij} = U_i \exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G) U_j^{-1}$, is a vertex-trivialized parameterization of a lattice gauge link variable [Wilson1974 *Confinement of quarks*, Phys. Rev. D 10:2445, §II — link variables $U_l \in G$ on edges $l$ of the lattice with the action $S = \sum_p \mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}(U_{p_1} U_{p_2} U_{p_3} U_{p_4})$; KogutSusskind1975 *Hamiltonian formulation of Wilson's lattice gauge theories*, Phys. Rev. D 11:395, §II; Creutz1983 *Quarks, Gluons and Lattices*, Cambridge, Ch. 5, lattice gauge transformations]. The gauge transformation law at line 842, $\Omega_{ij} \mapsto g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$, **is the standard lattice gauge transformation law for link variables**: in Creutz's Ch. 5 notation, $U_{x, x+\hat\mu} \to V_x U_{x, x+\hat\mu} V_{x+\hat\mu}^{-1}$, which is identical up to relabeling of vertices and the group-element symbol [Creutz1983 Ch. 5, Eq. (5.1); Wilson1974 §II, Eq. (12)]. The vertex factorization $\Omega_{ij} = U_i (\cdot) U_j^{-1}$ is a parameterization of the link variable, not a redefinition: it exposes the Regime I cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ as the $\delta_{ij} = 0$ special case, and the curvature-free / vanishing-holonomy structure is recovered exactly there. Setting $\delta_{ij} = 0$ recovers a flat (zero-curvature, trivial-holonomy) bundle in the sense of [Nakahara2003 §10.5, flat connections; Lemma referenced at manuscript line 579 establishes this internally]. The Wilson observable at line 856, $W_{ijk} = \mathrm{Re}\,\mathrm{Tr}\big[\exp(\delta_{ij}G) \exp(\delta_{jk}G) \exp(\delta_{ki}G)\big]$, and the plaquette-style action at line 862, $S_{\mathrm{Wilson}} = \beta \sum (1 - W_{ijk}/K)$, are the standard Wilson plaquette action specialized to 3-cycles on the agent graph [Wilson1974 Eq. (8); Creutz1983 Ch. 5, plaquette action]. The trace-cyclicity argument at line 854 — under $H_{ijk} \to g_i H_{ijk} g_i^{-1}$ the trace is invariant — is the standard gauge invariance of class functions on $G$ [Hall2015 §4, class functions of compact Lie groups].

### C7 — Hierarchy of operators

The unification of intra-scale and cross-scale gauge transformations under the same group action at line 891 holds because the cross-scale operator $\Omega_{i,I} = \exp(\phi_i^{(s)})\exp(-\phi_I^{(s+1)})$ is constructed from the same Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ on both ends: the meta-agent frame $\phi_I^{(s+1)} = \sum_i w_i \phi_i^{(s)} / \sum_i w_i$ is a $\mathfrak{g}$-valued weighted average and stays inside $\mathfrak{g}$ as a vector space. As a parameterization of a meta-frame, this is the low-curvature linearization of a Riemannian center of mass on the group $G$: the Karcher / Fréchet mean of a finite collection of group elements $\{g_i\}$ with weights $\{w_i\}$ is defined as the minimizer of the weighted sum of squared Riemannian distances [Karcher1977 *Riemannian center of mass and mollifier smoothing*, Comm. Pure Appl. Math. 30:509, §1; Pennec2006 *Intrinsic statistics on Riemannian manifolds: Basic tools for geometric measurements*, J. Math. Imaging Vis. 25:127, §3], and under the exponential parameterization $g_i = \exp(\phi_i)$ around the identity (or any common base point) the first-order condition for the Karcher mean reduces to $\sum_i w_i \phi_i = 0$ in the tangent space, with the Lie-algebra-weighted average $\sum_i w_i \phi_i / \sum_i w_i$ being the leading-order approximation. The manuscript flags both the BCH accuracy bound and the non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat at the parenthetical at line 891: "For the BCH accuracy bound and the non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat on this average see Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_variational}." The four transport operators ($\Omega_{ij}$ on state, $\tilde\Omega_{ij}$ on model, $\Omega_{i,I}$ cross-scale, and the field strength $F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu}$ as the curvature 2-form) live in well-typed objects: $\Omega_{ij}$ and $\tilde\Omega_{ij}$ act as the same group element in two different representations (the fundamental on $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}}$ and the model-fiber representation on $\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}}$) as the manuscript states at line 887; $\Omega_{i,I}$ is the special case of $\Omega_{ab}$ with $a, b$ at different scales (line 891); and $F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu}$ is the standard Yang–Mills curvature 2-form [Nakahara2003 §10.4, Eq. (10.86)], which the manuscript correctly restricts to Regime II at line 824 ("Under Regime~I, the connection inherits the pure-gauge form $A = U^{-1}dU$, the Maurer–Cartan identity forces $F^{(i)}_{\mu\nu} = 0$ identically"). No structural overloading occurs because each operator's type signature is stated and the cross-fiber identifications are made at the group-element level, not at the level of conflating distinct representations.

### Pre-emption of the strongest attack

The strongest single attack red can mount is not against any individual sub-claim but against the omnibus word "entire" in the claim statement: one weak link sinks the omnibus. The pre-emption is that the Theory section contains genuinely-novel extensions — the cross-scale shadow $p_i^{(s)} = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ of Eq. cross_scale_shadow (line 540–546), the multi-agent free energy functional with belief and model coupling terms (line 1023), and the Lahav–Neemeh CFR identification (line 770) — and the manuscript **labels each as such**. Line 546: "This is a structural commitment of the framework rather than a theorem of standard hierarchical variational inference: in the standard scheme [Friston2017, parr2022active] the level-$\ell$ prior is derived from a generative-model conditional $p(s_\ell \mid s_{\ell+1})$, not posited as a transported posterior, and we do not display the reduction (or approximation) of the standard hierarchical scheme to the present cross-scale shadow construction." Line 1023: "The functional developed in the remainder of this section is a *novel* multi-agent extension of Friston's single-agent free energy: the inter-agent belief-coupling terms ... and the model-channel analogue with $\gamma$ are not present in the standard FEP literature; ... we retain the FEP citations as the conceptual backbone ... but the multi-agent functional itself is an engineered consensus energy, not a derivation from FEP alone." Line 770: the CFR identification is stated to *supply* the transformation law that Lahav–Neemeh assert exists, not to derive their relativistic-analogy thesis. "Theoretically pure" should therefore be read as "labeled correctly + standard parts match standard treatments," not as "every construction appears verbatim in Kobayashi–Nomizu."

The second-strongest attack is that the two gauge actions of line 793 (global diagonal right-translation vs. full local left-translation) are non-conjugate as actions on $G^{|I|}$ for non-abelian $G$. The manuscript states this explicitly at line 793 — "they are not conjugate as actions on $G^{|I|}$ for non-abelian $G$; they are genuinely distinct symmetries that happen to coincide on observables (KL pairings and class functions of closed loops) by KL invariance under common pushforward." The defense is that KL invariance under common pushforward (Theorem at line 939) and trace cyclicity for closed-loop holonomies (the standard fact $\mathrm{Tr}(g h g^{-1}) = \mathrm{Tr}(h)$ on class functions [Hall2015 §4]) are exactly what makes the observables agree under both actions, which is the technical content the manuscript needs and explicitly states.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following are demonstrated against primary sources:

- **C1 falsified** if Kobayashi–Nomizu Vol. I Ch. II's definition of principal bundle uses a left action $g \cdot n$ or a non-fiber-preserving projection $\pi(n \cdot g) \neq \pi(n)$, contradicting manuscript line 574.
- **C2 falsified** if Amari 2016 or any standard probability text states that the pushforward of $\mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$ under $x \mapsto gx$ is not $\mathcal{N}(g\mu, g\Sigma g^\top)$, or if the algebra in Theorem 1 at lines 949–968 contains a computational error not corrected elsewhere in the manuscript.
- **C3 falsified** if the additive Lie-algebra form $\phi_i \mapsto \phi_i + \xi$ is the load-bearing gauge-invariance statement for epistemic death (it is not — the group-level $U_i \mapsto U_i g$ is, per line 739–795), or if the manuscript is shown to claim pointwise equality is the meta-agent criterion (it does not — line 682 explicitly retracts this).
- **C4 falsified** if Lahav–Neemeh 2022 / 2025 explicitly write down a transformation law on cognitive reference frames that the manuscript ignores or contradicts, or if the manuscript claims the CFR identification *derives* phenomenal consciousness from the bundle rather than supplying the transformation law that Lahav–Neemeh assert must exist.
- **C5 falsified** if the forward reference to `sec:meta_agent_rg` and `eq:rg_constrained_gap` is dangling (verified: it is honored at manuscript lines 4345 and 4383), or if Wilson 1971 / Cardy 1996 block-spin RG requires the *opposite* inequality (cross-cluster $\gg$ intra-cluster) rather than the one stated in Eq. culture_closure.
- **C6 falsified** if Wilson 1974 Eq. (12), Kogut–Susskind 1975 §II, or Creutz 1983 Ch. 5 uses a gauge transformation law for link variables differing from $U_l \to g_x U_l g_{x+\hat\mu}^{-1}$, or if the vertex-trivialized parameterization $\Omega_{ij} = U_i \exp(\delta_{ij}\cdot G) U_j^{-1}$ is shown to break gauge covariance of the Wilson observable.
- **C7 falsified** if the Lie-algebra-weighted average $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i / \sum_i w_i$ is shown not to be the low-curvature linearization of a Karcher / Fréchet mean (Karcher 1977 §1), or if the manuscript claims this average is BCH-exact for non-commuting $\phi_i$ (it does not — line 891 explicitly flags the BCH caveat).
- **Omnibus falsified** if any C1–C7 sub-claim fails *and* the manuscript does not register the failure as a labeled extension or structural commitment.
