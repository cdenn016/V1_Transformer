# Verdict — pifb-theory-section-purity

## Outcome

RED_WINS (omnibus), with sub-claim breakdown below.

## Decisive evidence

Two citations break the omnibus.

First, blue's own rebuttal concession at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-pifb-theory-section-purity/03_blue_rebuttal.md` lines 5–13: *"I concede the omnibus framing. Read literally, 'the entire \section{Theory} is theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature' is too strong: the section contains labeled extensions beyond standard textbook material — the cross-scale shadow ..., the multi-agent VFE coupling absent from canonical FEP, the culture-closure inequality ..., the Lahav–Neemeh CFR identification ..., and the Regime II promotion of $\delta_{ij}$ to an independent field ... that are not in Kobayashi–Nomizu, Nakahara, Wilson 1974, or Friston 2010 as stated."* Blue's defense then re-frames the claim to "internally consistent and every step honestly flagged." That re-framing is not the claim the user asked about.

Second, on the central contested C6 sub-claim, the manuscript's own line 878 in `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex`: *"Full per-vertex gauge equivariance of the connection module — passing $U_i^{-1}\mu_i$ rather than $\mu_i$ into the bilinear — is a separate architectural commitment beyond the scope of the discrete Regime II structure formalized here."* This is the manuscript admitting that the "gauge-invariant connection coefficients" framing at line 845 holds only under a vertex-trivialized gauge slice, not under the standard Creutz1983 Ch. 5 Eq. 5.1 / Wilson1974 §II Eq. 12 lattice-gauge transformation law $U_l \to g_x U_l g_{x+\hat\mu}^{-1}$. The "invariance" is invariance of a gauge-fixed representative; in the standard literature the connection variable inside a link is gauge-covariant, and only closed-loop traces are gauge-invariant.

## Reasoning

The user's directive in `00_claim.md` line 16 was unambiguous: "teams should consult the literature as source of truth." The omnibus claim was that "the entire Theory section is theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature." Under literal literature-match, the claim requires that every construction in the Theory section either appear in or derive from the cited canon (Kobayashi–Nomizu, Nakahara, Wilson 1974, Kogut–Susskind 1975, Creutz 1983, Friston 2010, Lahav–Neemeh 2022, etc.). Blue explicitly conceded this is too strong; both teams agree the cross-scale shadow, multi-agent VFE coupling, culture-closure inequality, CFR identification, and Regime II promotion are *labeled extensions* not present in the canon.

The per-sub-claim verification:

- **C1 (bundle constructions):** BLUE. Manuscript line 567–574 matches Kobayashi–Nomizu Vol. I Ch. II §5 and Nakahara2003 §10.1.1; the associated-bundle equivalence relation at line 596 and the local-trivialization caveat at lines 577–581 match Steenrod1951 §3. Red conceded this in rebuttal.

- **C2 (group actions on Gaussian fibers and KL invariance theorem):** BLUE. The Gaussian pushforward at line 608 is the textbook change-of-variables formula (Amari2016 §2.4); the $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ invariance theorem at line 939 is the standard $f$-divergence pushforward invariance (Csiszár1963; LieseVajda2006 §1). Red's secondary point — that line 565 $\mathrm{tr}(A_\mu A_\nu)$ is invariant only under the constant-per-agent residual — is conceded by blue and is not a contradiction of any cited canon.

- **C3 (multi-agent overlap, epistemic collapse, non-abelian additive shift):** BLUE on the group-level statement. Manuscript line 795 explicitly states the BCH limitation of the additive form $\phi_i \mapsto \phi_i + \xi$ relative to the group-level form $U_i \mapsto U_i g$. The group-level statement is correct against Hall2015 §3.5.

- **C4 (Lahav–Neemeh cognitive reference frames):** RED. Manuscript line 772 itself characterizes the identification as "a working correspondence rather than an identity," and line 770 says Lahav–Neemeh *"do not write such a law down. The construction in this section supplies it."* By the literal literature-match standard the user requested, the manuscript filling a silence in Lahav–Neemeh with a $\mathrm{GL}(K)$ Lie-group structure is an imposition of additional mathematical structure, not literature-match. The manuscript's own labeling that this is a supply rather than a derivation is honest scholarship but is the inverse of "matches the standard literature."

- **C5 (culture closure as RG block-spin):** BLUE on the deferred-construction reading. The forward reference from manuscript line 715 to `eq:rg_constrained_gap` is honored at line 4383, and `sec:meta_agent_rg` at lines 4351–4427 supplies the three Wilson1971 / Cardy1996 RG ingredients red claimed were missing: the exact pushforward measure $\widetilde{\rho}_{s+1} = (\mathcal{R}_s)_*(e^{-\mathcal{F}_s/\tau}d\nu_s)$ at line 4357, the Schur-complement effective metric at line 4411, and the Wilsonian relevance criterion linearizing the RG flow at a fixed point at line 4422. The closure inequality of Eq. culture_closure is explicitly named at line 715 as a "candidate-selection surrogate" for the rigorous construction, not as the construction itself. Red's strike that Eq. culture_closure alone is structurally Newman2006 modularity is correct as a literal reading, but it is also what the manuscript itself says.

- **C6 (Regime II edge-relaxed cocycle as Wilson lattice link variable):** RED on the load-bearing point. Manuscript line 842, $\Omega_{ij} \mapsto g_i \Omega_{ij} g_j^{-1}$, matches Creutz1983 Ch. 5 Eq. 5.1 and Wilson1974 §II Eq. 12 for the link variable. But line 845 then claims $\delta_{ij}$ is "gauge-invariant in this parameterization." In standard lattice gauge theory the connection variable inside a link is gauge-covariant, not gauge-invariant; only traces of closed Wilson loops are gauge-invariant (Wikipedia Wilson loop: $W \to g(x_f) W g(x_i)^{-1}$, covariant not invariant). The manuscript's "invariance" is invariance of a gauge-slice representative — a gauge-fixing rather than a gauge-invariant decomposition. The manuscript itself at line 878 admits the implementation-side consequence: *"Full per-vertex gauge equivariance of the connection module ... is a separate architectural commitment beyond the scope of the discrete Regime II structure formalized here."* Setting $\delta_{ij}=0$ recovering "a flat bundle" is technically a trivializable bundle (the cocycle $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ satisfies the algebraic cocycle condition at every triple of vertices, which is strictly stronger than Nakahara §10.5 flatness on multiply-connected base manifolds); the manuscript at line 579 does acknowledge that a globally defined Lie-algebra frame field exists iff the bundle is trivializable, which is consistent with red's reading.

- **C7a (Lie-algebra weighted average as Karcher mean):** BLUE on the deferred-bound reading. Manuscript line 2100 supplies the bound red asked for: the Karcher mean is defined via the bi-invariant metric $d_G(U,V)^2 = \|\log(U^{-1}V)\|_F^2$ on a compact gauge group, exists and is unique on convex normal balls of radius $< \pi/2$ (matching Karcher1977 §1 and Pennec2006 §3), and the Lie-algebra additive form $\phi_I = \sum_i w_i \phi_i$ is the first-order BCH approximation accurate to $O(\|\phi_i\|^2)$ when constituent frames are close in the compact case; the non-compact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat is also stated. Red's strike that this is punted to a downstream section is verified, but the punt is honored.

- **C7b (state vs model fiber representations, $K \neq M$):** BLUE. Manuscript line 596 explicitly defines two distinct representations $\rho_{\mathrm{state}}: G \to \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{state}})$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{model}}: G \to \mathrm{Aut}(\mathcal{B}_{\mathrm{model}})$, matching Nakahara2003 §10.1 Eq. 10.7 associated-bundle construction. The "same group element acting in two representations" reading at line 887 is supported by the line 596 definition. Red's strike that the working framework at line 922 collapses the two fibers is correct as a working-framework simplification but does not invalidate the abstract construction at line 596.

Aggregating: blue dominates the technical sub-claims that match standard textbook constructions (C1, C2, C3, C5, C7a, C7b) when the downstream cross-references are followed. Red wins on the framing-level sub-claims where the manuscript itself labels the construction as a supply rather than a derivation (C4, C6) and on the omnibus where blue conceded the literal claim. By the literal reading the user directive requires, the omnibus claim of "theoretically pure and mathematically correct against the standard literature" is falsified by blue's own concession plus the C4 and C6 framing-level mismatches. The defensible reading — "internally consistent and honestly flagged" — is the reading blue conceded was required to defend the claim, and is not the reading the user asked the debate to evaluate.
