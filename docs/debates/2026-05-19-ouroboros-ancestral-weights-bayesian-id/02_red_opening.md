# Red Opening — ouroboros-ancestral-weights-bayesian-id

## Steelman (opposing position)

The exponential weights $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$ in the Ouroboros are the discrete-index Ornstein-Uhlenbeck / AR(1) stationary covariance restricted to integer scale lags, and under the standard variational correspondence between additive KL terms and log-prior contributions, this matches the negative log-prior of an AR(1) scale-generative process whose stationary covariance is the same geometric kernel `[Hamilton1994 Ch. 3]`, `[RasmussenWilliams2006]`; therefore the Ouroboros is the OU/AR(1) scale-prior dressed in gauge-transport notation, and the disjunctive claim succeeds on identification II.

## Position

All three identifications fail, and the strong proposition fails with them, for four reasons that cannot be simultaneously evaded:
the manuscript line 2208 specifies $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$ as multipliers on free-energy contributions without writing the functional form, so the natural additive-KL reading lands on a product-of-experts log-linear pool `[HintonEtAl2002]` — a construction that is none of DGP, AR(1), or power-prior;
the DGP identification mistakes a hierarchical *depth* index for a GP *input* coordinate, when `[DamianouLawrence2013]` defines depth as function composition $f_L \circ \cdots \circ f_1$ and not as a kernel argument;
the AR(1) identification collides with the manuscript's own "non-Markovian extension" label at line 2196, since the AR(1) generative process is Markovian by definition `[Hamilton1994 Ch. 3 Eq. 3.1.1]`;
the power-prior identification requires re-interpreting ancestral *beliefs* $q_{I_k}$ as historical *likelihoods*, which `[IbrahimChen2000 §2.1]` does not license.
Under any candidate, blue must also account for the gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ sitting inside each KL — an object absent from every cited standard `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]` — so even a partial match requires a modified (not standard) hierarchical-Bayesian object, which fails the claim by definitional default.

## Evidence

- **Manuscript line 2208 specifies multipliers, not a functional form** — `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2208` reads "incorporated into the free energy functional with exponentially decaying weights $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \cdot \rho^k$." No explicit $F$ functional with $\lambda_k$ inserted is written. The shadow assignments at lines 2197–2207 set $p_i^{(s)} \leftarrow \Omega_{i,I_1}[q_{I_1}^{(s+1)}]$, $h_i^{(s,0)} \leftarrow \Omega_{i,I_2}[q_{I_2}^{(s+2)}]$, and so on, treating ancestral shadows as separate prior fields rather than as a marginal of an underlying generative process. The natural additive-KL reading
$$
F_{\text{ouroboros}} \supset \sum_k \lambda_0 \rho^k\, \mathrm{KL}\!\big(q_i \,\big\|\, \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}]\big)
$$
corresponds under the additive-KL ↔ product-of-experts correspondence `[HintonEtAl2002 Eq. 1]`, `[BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §2.3]` to the log-linear pool $p(q_i) \propto \prod_k p_k(q_i)^{\lambda_k}$ with $p_k = \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}]$. A product-of-experts is not a deep GP, not an AR(1), and not a power prior; it is a fourth construction. The strong proposition fails unless blue identifies the Ouroboros with the product-of-experts and renames the candidate set — which is a different debate.

- **Identification I (DGP) is a category error about what "deep" means** — `[DamianouLawrence2013 §2]` defines a deep GP as a stack of GPs composed in the *function* direction: $\mathbf{Y} = f_L(f_{L-1}(\cdots f_1(\mathbf{X})\cdots)) + \boldsymbol{\epsilon}$, with each $f_\ell \sim \mathcal{GP}(0, k_\ell)$. The "depth" $L$ is the count of nested function evaluations; it is not a coordinate fed into a kernel. `[SalimbeniDeisenroth2017 §2]` repeats the construction. A geometric covariance $k(s,s') = \lambda_0 \rho^{|s-s'|}$ over a discrete scale index $s$ is a *single* GP — the OU / Matérn-1/2 process on integer indices `[RasmussenWilliams2006 §4.2.1]` — not a DGP. The evidence pack itself records this collapse at lines 87–88 of `01_evidence.md`: "the AR(1) generative process produces the OU kernel marginally, so I and II coincide." Identification I is therefore not an independent identification; it reduces to Identification II, which has its own problems below.

- **Identification II (AR(1)) collapses against the manuscript's own "non-Markovian" label** — AR(1) is defined by $x_{t+1} = \rho x_t + \varepsilon_t$ with white innovation `[Hamilton1994 Ch. 3]` (the standard reference cited in the evidence pack). The transition density satisfies the Markov property $p(x_{t+1} \mid x_t, x_{t-1}, \ldots) = p(x_{t+1} \mid x_t)$ by construction. The manuscript at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2196` explicitly labels the Ouroboros a "non-Markovian extension" and structurally writes each agent's shadow assignment $p_i^{(s)}, h_i^{(s,0)}, h_i^{(s,1)}$ as *separate* direct dependencies on $q_{I_1}^{(s+1)}, q_{I_2}^{(s+2)}, q_{I_3}^{(s+3)}$ rather than as the marginal of a one-step recursion. Blue must defend one of two horns: either (a) accept AR(1) and concede the manuscript's "non-Markovian" label is wrong, or (b) keep the "non-Markovian" label and concede AR(1) does not match. The evidence-pack escape hatch at lines 88–89 (an AR(1) is Markovian but its marginal has long-range correlations) does not rescue blue here — the manuscript stores each ancestral shadow as an "additional field" (line 2208), not as a marginal of a hidden one-step recursion, so the construction is multi-input by design, not marginalization of a Markov chain.

- **Identification III (power prior) is a category error about what gets re-weighted** — `[IbrahimChen2000 §2.1, Eq. 2.1]` defines the power prior as
$$
\pi(\theta \mid \mathcal{D}_0, a_0) \propto L(\theta \mid \mathcal{D}_0)^{a_0}\, \pi_0(\theta),
$$
i.e., the historical-data *likelihood* $L(\theta \mid \mathcal{D}_0)$ is raised to a discount power. Hierarchical extensions `[ChenIbrahimShao2000]` discount historical likelihoods generation by generation, but the re-weighted object is always a likelihood. The Ouroboros applies $\rho^k$ to KL terms between $q_i$ (the current agent's belief) and $\Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}]$ (the *transported posterior* of an ancestor). $q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}$ is a recognition density at the ancestor — not a likelihood under any standard reading. To equate the two, blue must reinterpret the ancestor's posterior as a likelihood for the current agent, which is the move `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 9]` warns against under canon §10 item 5 of `external_canon_inference.md`: "Hierarchical mean-field vs point-passing. Passing a posterior *mean* (not the full posterior) between levels is a deterministic approximation, not variational inference proper." The Ouroboros passes posterior densities $q_{I_k}$, not historical observations or their likelihoods.

- **Gauge transport obstructs every candidate identification** — the Ouroboros free-energy term has $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ inside each KL: $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \,\|\, \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}])$. None of the three standard objects includes a gauge transport. `[DamianouLawrence2013]` stacks GPs with input-output coupling, no gauge frame. `[Hamilton1994 Ch. 3]` writes scalar (or vector) AR(1) with no fiber bundle and no parallel transport. `[IbrahimChen2000 §2.1]` raises a likelihood to a power on a flat parameter space, no connection. To make any identification work, blue must either drop the $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ (losing the framework's load-bearing structure — covariance transport is the project-wide invariant `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`) or extend the standard object to include gauge transports (which is a *modified* standard object, not the standard one). The claim demands identification with a "standard hierarchical-Bayesian object"; a modified object fails the claim.

- **The additive-KL → product-of-experts correspondence the evidence pack relies on does not name a hierarchical-Bayesian object** — the evidence pack at lines 66–74 of `01_evidence.md` gives the correspondence: a weighted sum of KLs over Gaussian priors corresponds to a Gaussian log-linear pool with precision $\Lambda = \sum_k \lambda_k \Lambda_k$. This is correct algebra. But the resulting object is a *product-of-experts* prior `[HintonEtAl2002]`, sometimes called a logarithmic opinion pool `[GenestZidek1986]` in the statistics literature. It is not a deep GP, not an AR(1), and not a power prior. Naming the Ouroboros honestly requires citing the PoE / log-linear-pool literature — which is not on the candidate menu in the claim.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any one of the following is established by blue with primary-source citation:

1. **Identification III rescue.** Blue produces a primary-source definition of a power prior or discount-factor SSM in which the discounted object is a *recognition density* (not a likelihood, not an observation, not the historical data), and the discount factor sits in front of KL terms (not in front of log-likelihoods). If `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` defines a discount-factor DLM in which the discount sits on the KL-to-prior at each scale, the identification III branch survives and I concede that branch.

2. **AR(1) horn-splitting.** Blue concedes the manuscript's "non-Markovian" label at line 2196 is wrong (i.e., the Ouroboros *is* Markovian-in-scale once one writes the underlying generative process), and produces the explicit one-step recursion $q_{I_k}^{(s+k)} = \rho \cdot \Omega^{-1} q_{I_{k-1}}^{(s+k-1)} + \varepsilon$ such that integrating out intermediate scales yields the additive-KL form with $\rho^k$ weights. If the recursion is exhibited and the marginalization gives the manuscript's term, identification II survives — at the cost of the manuscript's own labeling.

3. **Gauge-transport embedding.** Blue cites a primary-source extension of one of DGP, AR(1), or power-prior that natively includes parallel transport across fibers (e.g., a published gauge-equivariant deep GP, an AR(1) on a vector bundle). If such an extension is cited and named the "standard" object, the modified-vs-standard distinction collapses and I concede the gauge-transport obstruction.

4. **DGP via input-dimension reinterpretation.** Blue cites a published DGP construction in which the depth coordinate is treated as a GP kernel input (so the "deep" reads as kernel-input depth, not as function-composition depth) and the construction is the standard reading in the cited literature, not a one-off generalization.

5. **Product-of-experts is on the menu.** Blue argues that "standard hierarchical-Bayesian object" includes product-of-experts / log-linear pools `[HintonEtAl2002]` and the Ouroboros is identified with that — in which case the strong proposition succeeds and I concede on the strong proposition, while still maintaining that none of the three named candidates (DGP, AR(1), power-prior) is the correct identification.

If blue establishes none of (1)–(5), the disjunctive identification fails and the manuscript's Ouroboros remains a free-form posit beyond the editorial "non-Markovian extension" label — consistent with the prior debate's concession at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/03_blue_rebuttal.md`.
