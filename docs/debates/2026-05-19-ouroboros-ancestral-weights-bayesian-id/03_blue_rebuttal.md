# Blue Rebuttal — ouroboros-ancestral-weights-bayesian-id

## Concession

I grant three of red's points cleanly.

First, **Identification I (deep GP) is dead.** `[DamianouLawrence2013 §2]` and `[SalimbeniDeisenroth2017 §2]` define the "deep" axis of a DGP as function composition $f_L \circ \cdots \circ f_1$, not as a kernel-input coordinate. The evidence pack itself records the collapse at `01_evidence.md:87-88`: under linear-Gaussian models the AR(1) generative process produces the OU kernel marginally, so I is not an independent identification — it reduces to II. Red wins this branch and I do not contest it.

Second, **Identification III in its literal Ibrahim-Chen form is dead.** `[IbrahimChen2000 §2.1, Eq. 2.1]` defines the power prior as $\pi(\theta \mid \mathcal{D}_0, a_0) \propto L(\theta \mid \mathcal{D}_0)^{a_0}\, \pi_0(\theta)$ — the discounted object is a *likelihood*. The Ouroboros discounts a KL between a current belief and an ancestor's transported posterior, not a likelihood. I do not contest red's reading of `[IbrahimChen2000]`. The discount-factor DLM construction in `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` keeps III alive in its broadened sense (variance-inflated priors propagated across scale generations with geometric discount), but the strict power-prior reading on the candidate menu does not survive red's evidence point 4.

Third, I grant that **the cleanest textbook name for the construction red identifies in evidence point 1 is product-of-experts / log-linear pool**, not any of the three candidates as worded. Red's algebra at `02_red_opening.md` evidence point 1 is correct: the additive-KL reading at line 2208 corresponds under the standard additive-KL ↔ log-density correspondence to a Gaussian log-linear pool with precision $\Lambda = \sum_k \lambda_k \Lambda_k$ `[HintonEtAl2002 Eq. 1]`, `[BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §2.3]`, `[GenestZidek1986]`.

These three concessions do not defeat the claim. They reshape it.

## Core attack

Red's falsification condition (5) is a self-defeat. Red writes at `02_red_opening.md:50`: "Blue argues that 'standard hierarchical-Bayesian object' includes product-of-experts / log-linear pools `[HintonEtAl2002]` and the Ouroboros is identified with that — in which case the strong proposition succeeds and I concede on the strong proposition, while still maintaining that none of the three named candidates (DGP, AR(1), power-prior) is the correct identification."

The strong proposition at `00_claim.md:23` is labeled by the claim itself as "load-bearing." Its exact wording: "There exists *some* standard hierarchical-Bayesian generative model under which the negative log-prior of an agent's state, marginalized over ancestral latents, reproduces (up to additive constants and the gauge transports) a free-energy term of the form $\sum_k \lambda_0 \rho^k \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}])$. **If no such model exists, all three identifications fail.**" The contrapositive is decisive: if *some* such model exists, the load-bearing proposition stands, regardless of which of the three illustrative candidates is closest.

Red's own evidence point 1 supplies that model. The Gaussian log-linear pool $p(q_i) \propto \prod_k p_k(q_i)^{\lambda_k}$ with $p_k = \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}]$ and $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$ is a generative-model prior whose negative log-density is, up to a $q$-independent normalizer, the additive-KL sum at issue. The correspondence is standard `[Friston2010]`, the product-of-experts construction is standard `[HintonEtAl2002 Eq. 1]`, and the log-linear opinion pool predates both as a textbook object in Bayesian aggregation `[GenestZidek1986]`.

The claim wording at `00_claim.md:11` is disjunctive — "Candidates the blue team may select from" — and lists three examples. The candidates are illustrative, not exhaustive. The strong proposition is what carries the claim, and the strong proposition is conceded by red under his own falsification condition (5). On red's own terms, the load-bearing sub-claim succeeds.

## Defense

Three reinforcements against the residual obstructions red raised.

**Gauge transport does not block identification under Gaussian congruence.** Red's evidence point 5 argues that no standard object includes a gauge transport $\Omega_{i,I_k}$ inside the KL, and therefore identification requires a *modified* standard object. The objection collapses once one notes that $\Omega \in \mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ acts on Gaussians by the affine pushforward $\Omega \cdot \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma) = \mathcal{N}(\Omega \mu, \Omega \Sigma \Omega^\top)$, which preserves the Gaussian family (the sandwich product the project enforces, `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`). The additive-KL → log-density correspondence at `[Friston2010]` and the product-of-experts construction at `[HintonEtAl2002 Eq. 1]` carry through under any invertible linear reparameterization of the prior components: if $p_k$ is Gaussian then so is $\Omega \cdot p_k$, with the algebra $\Lambda = \sum_k \lambda_k \Lambda_k$ replaced by $\Lambda = \sum_k \lambda_k (\Omega_k^{-\top} \Lambda_k \Omega_k^{-1})$. The identification holds for the transported components; the standard object is applied to the transported priors. Red's "modified object" framing would defeat *every* application of any standard Bayesian construction inside a fiber-bundle framework, which is too strong — it would forbid the project's use of Gaussian inference under transport entirely, contradicting `[Friston2010]` as applied throughout the rest of the manuscript.

**Identification II survives as the marginal-log-prior reading.** Red's evidence point 3 anticipates the AR(1) rescue and rejects it because the manuscript stores each ancestral shadow as a "separate field" at line 2208. The storage choice does not pin down the generative semantics. The manuscript line 2208 names the $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \rho^k$ weights as multipliers on free-energy contributions, not as data-structure layout commitments. Under the standard variational correspondence `[Friston2010]`, the additive-KL form $\sum_k \rho^k \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{i,I_k} q_{I_k})$ is mathematically licensed from a Markovian-in-scale AR(1) latent process $x^{(s+1)} = \rho x^{(s)} + \varepsilon_{s+1}$ with $\varepsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2(1-\rho^2))$ whose marginal joint covariance is $\mathrm{Cov}(x^{(s)}, x^{(s+k)}) = \sigma^2 \rho^k$ `[Hamilton1994 §3.2]`, `[CappeMoulinesRyden2005 §1.3]`. The "non-Markovian" label at line 2196 refers to the agent-level *marginal* dependence — agent $i$ at scale $s$ has dependencies on multiple ancestors — which is consistent with a Markovian-in-scale generative process and a non-Markovian marginal at the agent level, exactly as a stationary AR(1) is Markovian but its marginal observation correlates with all past observations `[Hamilton1994 §3.2]`. The choice between "the generative process is Markov but the marginal is not" and "the prior is genuinely multi-source" is a labeling choice; both readings are consistent with line 2208, and only the second forces departure from identification II.

**Identification III's broadened reading survives via the discount-factor DLM family.** `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` defines the discount-factor DLM by inflating the prior variance at each propagation step by a factor $1/\delta$ with $\delta \in (0,1]$; iterated $k$ steps this produces a $\delta^k$ discount on the effective precision contribution from the $k$-step-removed prior. Under the additive-KL form `[Friston2010]`, the precision-weighted KL contribution from generation $k$ is proportional to $\delta^k$, giving the manuscript's $\rho^k$ structure with $\rho = \delta$. The Ibrahim-Chen power-prior reading red rejected is one form of discount; the West-Harrison DLM discount on the prior (not the likelihood) is another, and it is the one that matches the manuscript's $\rho^k$ on KL terms `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]`. III in its strict Ibrahim-Chen form is dead; III in the broader discount-factor family survives.

**Net position.** The load-bearing strong proposition stands on red's own concession under falsification condition (5), with the log-linear pool `[HintonEtAl2002]`, `[GenestZidek1986]` as the named standard object. Identification I is dead. Identification II survives in the marginal-log-prior reading. Identification III survives in the discount-factor-DLM broadening. The disjunctive claim — that the Ouroboros admits identification with *some* standard hierarchical-Bayesian object — succeeds; the precise textbook name should be "Gaussian log-linear pool over geometrically-discounted transported ancestral posteriors," which is a textbook object in the aggregation literature `[GenestZidek1986]` and the product-of-experts literature `[HintonEtAl2002]`, even if it is not literally one of the three illustrative candidates as worded.

If the judge requires identification with exactly one of the three named candidates as worded — strict DGP, strict AR(1), or strict Ibrahim-Chen power prior — then I cannot defend the claim and concede; the strongest available defense is the log-linear pool, which is on red's falsification-condition-(5) menu but not on the claim's candidate menu. If the judge reads the claim as defended by *any* standard hierarchical-Bayesian identification under the disjunctive wording at `00_claim.md:11` and the load-bearing strong proposition at `00_claim.md:23`, the claim succeeds.
