## Reasoning

The claim has a disjunctive surface structure (any one of three identifications) layered above a load-bearing strong proposition (some standard hierarchical-Bayesian generative model exists). Red and blue converged on substantive agreement on three structural points: Identification I as "deep GP" via function composition `[DamianouLawrence2013 §2]` is dead (blue conceded at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:7`); Identification III in its strict Ibrahim-Chen reading `[IbrahimChen2000 §2.1]` is dead because the discounted object there is a likelihood, not a KL between beliefs (blue conceded at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:9`); the additive-KL form at line 2208 corresponds under the standard correspondence to a Gaussian log-linear pool with precision $\Lambda = \sum_k \lambda_k \Lambda_k$ (both teams cite this).

The disagreement reduces to two structural questions. First, does the discount-factor DLM reading of `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` count as Identification III, or as a fourth identification outside the menu? The claim text at `00_claim.md:26` explicitly names `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` as the standard reference for Identification III alongside `[IbrahimChen2000]`. Red's reframing of the DLM reading as a "fourth identification" requires reading the claim's reference list as restrictive (only Ibrahim-Chen power priors count, not the West-Harrison DLM also cited there). Red provided no citation supporting that restrictive reading; blue's defense at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:33` invoked the exact reference the claim names. A cited claim outweighs an uncited rhetorical recharacterization. Identification III broadened survives.

Second, does the strong proposition succeed? Red's evidence point 1 at `02_red_opening.md` and falsification condition (5) at `02_red_opening.md:50` together concede that the additive-KL form corresponds to a log-linear-pool prior, which is a standard hierarchical-Bayesian object `[HintonEtAl2002, GenestZidek1986]`. Blue's sympy verification at `02_blue_opening.md:13-19` confirms the algebra. The strong proposition at `00_claim.md:23` asks for the existence of *some* such model and explicitly states "If no such model exists, all three identifications fail." Red's concession under falsification condition (5) is dispositive of the contrapositive.

The tempering / normalization gap (question 4) does not flip the verdict. Either $\lambda_0 = 1 - \rho$ is imposed and the construction is a strictly-normalized log-linear pool, or the construction is tempered and named via `[BissiriHolmesWalker2016]`. The West-Harrison DLM-discount reading does not require generalized Bayes — it is a precision-inflation iteration under standard Gaussian DLMs `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]`. Red's "generalized Bayes is not standard" argument at `03_red_rebuttal.md:27` is contestable but does not defeat the DLM reading, which is the strongest available defense of III.

Identification II receives a mixed read. Blue's latent-vs-marginal reading at `02_blue_opening.md:24` and `03_blue_rebuttal.md:31` is mathematically internally consistent and supported by `[CappeMoulinesRyden2005 §1.3]` and `[DurbinKoopman2012]`. Red's counter at `03_red_rebuttal.md:19` is that the manuscript at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2197-2201` structurally stores each ancestral shadow as a *separate* direct dependency on $q_{I_1}^{(s+1)}, q_{I_2}^{(s+2)}, q_{I_3}^{(s+3)}$, which forces a multi-source-prior reading rather than an AR(1) marginalization. Red's textual reading of the manuscript is the stronger of the two on the manuscript-as-written; blue's reading is mathematically available but requires a clarifying revision to be the manuscript's committed semantics. Identification II therefore goes to red on the textual question and is recoverable only via a manuscript revision committing to the latent-Markovian-with-non-Markovian-marginal reading. This does not affect the disjunction, which is carried by Identification III broadened.

Manuscript labeling (question 5): the claim asks whether the weights *admit* identification, not whether the manuscript *names* it. The disjunction is defended at the mathematical level; the manuscript labeling is properly an action item, not a verdict-flipper.

## Action

The verdict requires four concrete manuscript revisions to convert the licensed identification into a published, defensible claim. All four revisions target `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex` in the Ouroboros tower extension section.

First, at line 2208, replace the prose sentence about "exponentially decaying weights $\lambda_k = \lambda_0 \cdot \rho^k$" with the explicit free-energy functional fragment $\sum_k \lambda_0 \rho^k \mathrm{KL}(q_i \,\|\, \Omega_{i,I_k}[q_{I_k}^{(s+k)}])$ inside the $F$ functional, with a parallel fragment for the model-fiber shadows using $\tilde\Omega$ and $s$ in place of $\Omega$ and $q$. Without the explicit functional, the role of $\rho^k$ remains underspecified (red's evidence point 3 at `03_red_rebuttal.md:31` is correct on this).

Second, at the same location, add a citation block identifying the construction as either a Gaussian log-linear pool / product-of-experts with geometric forgetting `[GenestZidek1986, HintonEtAl2002, BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]` (the algebraically cleanest name, on blue's own admission at `02_blue_opening.md:46` and `03_blue_rebuttal.md:11`) or a discount-factor dynamic linear model in the sense of `[WestHarrison1997 Ch. 10]` (the name on the claim's own menu under Identification III). The two are related but distinct; the manuscript should commit to one as primary and cite the other as adjacent.

Third, at the same location, state explicitly whether the normalization $\sum_{k \geq 0} \lambda_k = \lambda_0/(1-\rho) = 1$ is imposed. If imposed, the construction is a strictly-normalized variational free energy under a standard log-linear-pool prior. If not imposed, the construction is a tempered / generalized-Bayesian free energy in the sense of `[BissiriHolmesWalker2016, GrunwaldVanOmmen2017]`, and the citation should say so. The manuscript currently leaves this ambiguous and red's tempering objection at `03_red_rebuttal.md:24` is well-founded on the ambiguity.

Fourth, at line 2196, rewrite the "non-Markovian extension" qualifier to specify whether it refers to the generative scale-process (which would force a long-memory / fractional model per `[Beran2010 §2]`) or the agent-level marginal prior under a Markovian-in-scale AR(1) latent generator (per `[CappeMoulinesRyden2005 §1.3, Hamilton1994 §3.2]`). The current phrasing is ambiguous in a load-bearing way. If the revision commits to the agent-level-marginal reading under an AR(1) latent process, Identification II is also recovered; if it commits to the genuinely-multi-source-prior reading, only Identification III broadened carries the disjunction. The verdict's BLUE_WINS does not depend on this choice — III broadened carries the disjunction either way — but the manuscript's defensibility is improved by committing.

No follow-up debate is required. The four revisions above, made together, convert the Ouroboros from a free-form posit into a named, citable construction (Gaussian log-linear pool with geometric forgetting / discount-factor DLM) consistent with the standard correspondence between additive KL terms and log-density priors. The earlier debate's blue concession at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/03_blue_rebuttal.md` ("free-form posit ... out-of-scope for theory mode") is superseded by the present verdict: the construction is no longer free-form once the revisions above are applied, and the textbook identification is the log-linear pool with geometric forgetting.
