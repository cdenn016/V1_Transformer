## Core attack

Blue's theoretical-justification defense rests on two moves that do not survive scrutiny against the project's own canon.

**Move 1 (the self-evidencing reduction `p^* = p_pred`) is not what the cited self-evidencing literature licenses.** Blue cites `[Friston2010]`, `[FristonEtAl2017 §2.3, §3.4]`, and Hohwy 2016 to support the substitution. The self-evidencing strand of FEP says: an agent's posterior over its own states provides evidence for the agent's generative model — i.e., minimizing variational free energy is equivalent to maximizing `log p(o)` under the model `[Friston2010 Form 2, see external_canon_inference.md §1]`. That is a statement about model evidence under observed data `o`. It is not a license to replace the preference distribution `p^*(o|C)` in expected free energy with the agent's current predictive distribution `p_pred(o|μ)`. The substitution blue defends produces the canonical FEP failure mode known as the dark-room problem: if `p^* = p_pred`, then the pragmatic term `E_q[-log p^*(o)] = E_q[-log p_pred(o|μ)] = H[p_pred]` is minimized by any belief whose readout is sharply peaked, regardless of where it is peaked. The agent is rewarded for confident predictions about *anything*. There is no contact with reality, by construction, because reality (the target token) is excluded by Law 1.

The canon document at `external_canon_inference.md §2` states the canonical form unambiguously: `G(π) = E_{q(o|π)}[-log p(o|C)] − E_{q(o|π)}[KL(q(s|o,π) ‖ q(s|π))]`. The preference distribution `p(o|C)` is fixed by goal context `C`, not generated by the agent's own predictions. The pitfall section `external_canon_inference.md §10.3` is explicit: "Claims that FEP alone implies an architectural choice ... require the explicit generative model that connects them." The codebase produces neither an external `C` nor a generative-model derivation of `p^* = p_pred`; the disclosure docstring at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:28-37` admits exactly this — "without requiring an external preference" is offered as the *motivation* for the substitution, not as a derivation of it. Motivation is not justification.

**Move 2 (Fisher natural-gradient projection unifies the two gradients on one statistical manifold) confuses metric with functional.** Blue's evidence cites `[Amari1998]` for the natural gradient and argues that summing `grad_mu + ai_grad_mu` *before* `compute_natural_gradient_gpu` at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1172-1175` means both terms descend on the same Riemannian manifold. That is true about the *metric* — the Fisher information defines the geometry of the q-parameter space, and any vector field on that space can be whitened by it. It is false about the *functional*. The natural gradient of a sum is the natural-gradient projection of the sum, but the sum being projected is `∂F/∂μ + ∂F_AI/∂μ`, where `F` is the canonical five-term gauge-theoretic free energy (CLAUDE.md, `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex \label{eq:free_energy_functional_final}`) and `F_AI = λ_prag · H[p_pred] − λ_epi · MI` is the augmentation. Descending on `F + F_AI` is descending on a different functional than `F`. Information geometry on q does not turn the augmented functional into the canonical one. The claim under debate is that the implementation is "theoretically justifiable as an active-inference / expected-free-energy variant in the sense of the standard Friston / Parr-Pezzulo-Friston literature" — that is a claim about what is being minimized, not about how the steepest-descent direction is computed.

**The architectural separation is a concession against blue, not a defense.** Blue cites `transformer/vfe/efe.py:28-150` as evidence that the canonical EFE form is implemented correctly elsewhere. That is precisely the point: the canonical form is in `efe.py` (`risk = E_{q(o|a)}[-log p^*(o)]` via `compute_risk`, ambiguity as `E_z[H[p(o|z)]]` with the anti-double-count comment at `90-95`, BALD MI for epistemic value), and what is added to the E-step is the *other* construction — the one the disclosure docstring at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:28-37` explicitly labels "NOT the canonical EFE pragmatic value." If the canonical EFE form belongs in a separate module for the policy role, then the construction being added to the E-step is by the codebase's own architectural choice not canonical EFE. The user's claim asks whether the E-step augmentation is theoretically justifiable in the sense of the standard literature. The disclosure docstring and the module separation are coherent answers — they say "no, this is a self-evidencing surrogate; the canonical form lives over there in `efe.py`." Honest disclosure does not convert a self-acknowledged non-canonical functional into a canonical one.

**The published F functional does not contain the augmentation.** The evidence pack records the grep result across `Attention/*.tex`: the five-term F functional at `\label{eq:free_energy_functional_final}` has α self-coupling, λ_h hyper-prior, β-coupled belief KL + entropy, γ-coupled model KL + entropy, and observation likelihood. There is no `+ λ_prag · H[p_pred] − λ_epi · MI` term. The augmentation is a code-side extension on top of the manuscript F. Under the canon `§10.3` pitfall, an undisclosed-in-the-paper functional extension cannot be defended as "consistent with the standard literature" merely because the code that adds it is honest about the substitution.

## Defense

Blue's strongest anticipated counterattack against my position — articulated in blue's own falsification-condition discussion — is the framing that the self-evidencing strand of FEP "treats the agent's beliefs and policies as continuously coupled" and that Friston has framed perception itself as an active-inference process, so the policy/belief distinction is not as load-bearing as the canon makes it sound. I address this directly.

First, the canon document this debate is anchored to does treat the distinction as load-bearing. `external_canon_inference.md §2` states the policy posterior `q(π) ∝ exp(-G(π))` as part of the canonical form, not as one of several interchangeable interpretations. `external_canon_inference.md §10.4` enumerates "EM vs IFT vs amortized" as a pitfall whose categories "cannot be interchanged"; the same hygiene applies to "policy posterior vs belief retraction" — they are different gradient targets. The relevant primary source is `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 7]`, which the disclosure docstring at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:31-33` cites as the form being substituted from. The codebase cites this source precisely because it provides the canonical policy-posterior form; it would be incoherent to cite it for the substitution and then argue that the policy/belief distinction in the same source does not matter.

Second, the empirical falsifier blue itself proposes in F2 ("ablate `epistemic_weight = 0`, train for a few thousand steps, check whether predictive entropy collapses") is the dark-room problem in operational form. The codebase ships with `λ_prag = 1.0` and `λ_epi = 0.5`, so pragmatic dominates epistemic 2:1 in the augmentation. Under descent on `+ λ_prag · H[p_pred]`, the gradient on μ pushes the belief toward configurations whose readout entropy is small. The epistemic term is the only counterweight, at half the weight. The evidence pack records that the sole unit test (`tests/transformer/test_vfe_package.py:265-280`) asserts only shape; no sign test, no finite-difference agreement, no entropy-trajectory test. The codebase has not demonstrated that the construction avoids the failure mode that the substitution is structurally exposed to. The absence of an empirical demonstration that `p^* = p_pred` does not collapse is not neutral evidence; under the user's compound claim that the implementation is theoretically justifiable, the burden is on the defense to show the construction is well-posed.

Third, my Phase 2 opening's core position survives blue's defense intact: the load-bearing pragmatic half of the augmentation is the non-canonical substitution; the defensible epistemic half (BALD MI with correct sign) does not rescue it; the user's claim requires consistency with the Friston / Parr-Pezzulo-Friston canon; the codebase's own disclosure docstring concedes the construction is not consistent with that canon. The Fisher natural-gradient projection at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1172-1175` is a metric on the q-manifold, not a transformation of the objective functional; it does not bridge the canonical-functional gap.

The claim, on the theoretical-justification half, fails on the codebase's own admission. The wiring half I grant.
