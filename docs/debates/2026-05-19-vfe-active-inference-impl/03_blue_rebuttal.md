# Blue Rebuttal — vfe-active-inference-impl

## Concession

Two concessions, each material.

First, the pragmatic substitution. Red is correct that `transformer/core/active_inference.py:212-218` computes `pragmatic_term = pragmatic_weight * H[p_pred(v|μ_i)]`, which is the Shannon entropy of the model's own readout, not the canonical EFE pragmatic value `E_q[-log p*(o|C)]` over a preference distribution `p*`. The code's own docstring at lines 28-37 states this in plain English: "This is NOT the canonical EFE pragmatic value." I grant that calling this construction "canonical EFE" would be false, and the name `lambda_prag` is borrowed for symmetry rather than earned by derivation.

Second, the manuscript drift. The five-term functional at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex` `\label{eq:free_energy_functional_final}` does not contain a `+λ_prag H[p_pred] − λ_epi MI` augmentation, and the grep results in `01_evidence.md` (lines 108-112) confirm no "active inference" / "pragmatic" / "EFE" passage in `Attention/*.tex` derives the augmentation. The `active_inference=True` path optimizes a functional the manuscript does not currently derive, and this is a real gap between the research-track code and the published statement of F.

I do not concede red's load-bearing claim — the role-shift critique (point 2 of red's opening). That argument fails on its own primary source.

## Core attack

Red's load-bearing argument is the role-shift: "expected free energy `G(π)` is a policy score, not a belief-update objective; the `/vfe` E-step has no action set, no policy posterior, ... adding a G-shaped term to the E-step `(μ, σ)` retraction is a regularized variational inference borrowing the EFE term labels — not active inference." Red cites `external_canon_inference.md §2` and `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 2]`.

This argument overreaches in three ways.

First, it misreads its own headline citation. Red invokes `transformer/vfe/efe.py:1-10` as the project's "internal guidance" condemning the E-step augmentation. The actual docstring at `transformer/vfe/efe.py:4-5` reads: "This is the correct place for active inference in a language model: generation-time action selection, **not target-conditioned E-step inference**." The qualifier "target-conditioned" is doing real work. The `/vfe` `active_inference=True` path is explicitly *not* target-conditioned — `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:725-746` enforces Law 1 (no `targets` parameter exists in `VFEEStep.forward`), and `transformer/core/active_inference.py:171-172` adds an inference-mode guard returning `(None, None)`. The pragmatic and BALD terms are computed only from the belief `(μ, σ)` and the PriorBank readout — there is no target leakage, no preference distribution conditioned on labels. The internal docstring at `vfe/efe.py` rules out *target-conditioned* belief shaping, which is a different operation than what the present code does. Red's citation does not support red's conclusion.

Second, red's primary source itself authorizes extensions of FEP beyond the policy-posterior form. The canon at `external_canon_inference.md §10` (pitfall 3) states: "FEP is a variational principle; specific implementations follow from specific generative-model choices. Claims that FEP alone implies an architectural choice ... require the explicit generative model that connects them." The canon does *not* state that the EFE functional is licensed only as a policy score; it states that any specific extension requires its explicit generative model. The codebase provides exactly this: the generative model is the PriorBank readout `p(v|μ, σ, τ)` defined at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:182-187`, and the augmentation `F_AI = λ_prag H[p_pred] − λ_epi MI(v; μ|q)` is constructed against that explicit model. The construction is novel; the canon authorizes novel constructions if they make their generative model explicit, which this one does (docstring lines 28-37 disclose both the substitution and the generative target).

Third, red's framing of "policy score, not belief-update objective" treats a process-theory implementation choice as a definitional gate. In the continuous-time / belief-propagation formulations of active inference [Friston2010 §self-evidencing; Bogacz2017 tutorial; FristonEtAl2017 process theory], perception (belief inference) and action (policy selection) share a single free-energy budget. The standard active-inference agent minimizes `F` over beliefs and `G` over policies — but when there is no discrete policy variable (the autoregressive LM setting has no action set at the E-step; the action set is the next-token vocabulary, handled separately by `vfe/efe.py` at generation time), the natural translation of "self-evidencing pressure on beliefs" is to fold an EFE-shaped term into the belief gradient. This is what `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1164-1175` does — and the Fisher metric is applied to the *sum* of the analytic VFE gradient and the AI gradient, so all terms share a single information-geometric descent geometry [Amari1998]. The architectural design here is: belief-level self-evidencing at E-step (no discrete action), canonical-EFE policy selection at decode (discrete action over vocabulary, handled by `vfe/efe.py`). Both halves are present; the design is internally consistent.

## Defense

The user's claim has two halves: wiring-correctness and theoretical-justifiability "as an active-inference / expected-free-energy variant." Red conceded the wiring half in its own steelman, and red's opening attacks only the second half. I defend the second half under the actual wording of the claim — "variant," not "identical to canonical EFE."

The defense rests on three citations.

First, the canon's own pitfall list at `external_canon_inference.md §10` (item 3) authorizes principled extensions of FEP to specific generative models: "specific implementations follow from specific generative-model choices ... require the explicit generative model that connects them." The codebase satisfies the canon's standard for an extension. The generative model is the PriorBank readout (`transformer/core/active_inference.py:182-187`); the variational q is the per-token Gaussian `(μ_i, σ_i)` (canon §6, mean-field across tokens); the EFE-shaped augmentation is the self-evidencing surrogate `λ_prag H[p_pred] − λ_epi MI`, with the substitution disclosed at lines 28-37. The canon does not say "the only licensed location for an EFE-shaped term is the policy posterior" — it says any specific location requires its explicit generative model, and this one has it.

Second, the project preserves the canonical EFE form in its proper location at `transformer/vfe/efe.py:28-52`, where `VFEExpectedFreeEnergy` scores candidate next tokens by `G(a) = risk + ambiguity − epistemic_value` with `risk = E_{q(o|a)}[-log p*(o)]` — the canonical pragmatic value of [ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 2]. The architectural split is deliberate: belief-level self-evidencing surrogate at E-step (no discrete action variable), canonical EFE at generation time (discrete action over vocabulary). The two modules share `epistemic_samples` and `epistemic_weight` config fields but compute structurally different functionals — `vfe/efe.py:90-95` explicitly uses *mean predictive entropy* (the canonical ambiguity term per `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022]`) rather than `H[p_bar]`. The codebase is not confused about which form is canonical; it places the canonical form where the canon places it (over actions) and the surrogate where actions are not available (over beliefs).

Third, the natural-gradient projection at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1172-1175` applies `compute_natural_gradient_gpu(grad_mu, grad_sigma, sigma, eps=eps)` to the sum of the analytic VFE gradient and the AI gradient. This is not a naive Euclidean sum of incompatible objectives; it is descent on a single Fisher-Rao geometry [Amari1998, Cencov1972] over the per-token statistical manifold. The augmentation does not violate the information-geometric structure that the surrounding VFE machinery is built on; it shares it.

The honest summary: the `/vfe` `active_inference=True` path is a research-track extension that (a) is wired correctly, end-to-end, with no dead paths (red conceded this), (b) discloses its pragmatic-term substitution in code at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:28-37` rather than misrepresenting it as canonical, (c) preserves the canonical EFE form in `vfe/efe.py` for the location where the canon places it (action selection), and (d) folds a self-evidencing surrogate into the belief gradient through a shared Fisher metric so the descent geometry is coherent. The claim, as worded ("theoretically justifiable ... as a variant"), survives. The claim "identical to canonical EFE" would not, and no one is making that claim — the code's own disclosure rules it out.

What the claim does *not* survive: a stronger reading that requires the manuscript to derive the augmentation. I conceded that gap. The fix is a manuscript supplement deriving `F + F_AI` from the PriorBank generative model under the no-preference setting, with the surrogate explicitly labeled. That is a writing task, not an evidence problem with the implementation.
