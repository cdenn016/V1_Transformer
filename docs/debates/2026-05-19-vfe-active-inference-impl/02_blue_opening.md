# Blue Opening — vfe-active-inference-impl

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest red position is that the `/vfe` `active_inference=True` path adds two terms whose names match the EFE literature but whose mathematical content does not: the "pragmatic" term is `+λ_prag · H[p_pred(v|μ)]` rather than the canonical `E_q[-log p^*(o)]` of `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 7]`, and the augmented gradient is injected into the E-step belief-update rather than into a policy posterior `q(π) ∝ exp(-G(π))` where EFE properly lives — so calling this "active inference" is, at best, terminology drift from the field standard.

## Position

Within the scope of the compound claim — (a) correctly wired into the iterative E-step and (b) theoretically justifiable as an active-inference variant in the Friston / Parr-Pezzulo-Friston sense — the `/vfe` implementation passes both parts under a calibrated reading. The wiring is mechanically verifiable at every cited `path:line`, the AI gradient is folded into the same information-geometric descent as the analytic VFE gradient via the Fisher natural-gradient projection per `[Amari1998]`, target-blindness (Law 1) is enforced by absence of a `targets` parameter on `VFEEStep.forward`, the canonical generation-time EFE form lives in a separate module (`transformer/vfe/efe.py`), and the E-step augmentation is explicitly disclosed as a self-evidencing surrogate — not the canonical pragmatic value — at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:28-37`. The self-evidencing operationalization is grounded in the Friston / Hohwy strand of FEP literature in which an agent, lacking an exogenous preference distribution, treats minimization of predictive entropy / surprise as the pragmatic objective. The construction is honestly labeled, the canon citations are accurate, and the implementation does not claim that FEP alone forces this architectural choice — which is exactly the §10 pitfall flagged in `external_canon_inference.md`.

## Evidence

**Wiring trace (mechanical, every line confirmed against the file).**

The master toggle is declared at `transformer/vfe/config.py:236-244` with the explicit removability note about `learnable_decode_tau` (which is non-learnable under detached `μ,σ` leaves in the AI autograd subgraph). Construction of the callback is conditional at `transformer/vfe/model.py:120-124`, with `prior_bank` stashed in `__dict__` at `transformer/vfe/active_inference.py:51-53` to prevent double-registration as an `nn.Module` child. The callback is threaded by positional argument through `VFEModel.forward → VFEStack.forward → VFEBlock.forward → VFEEStep.forward` at `transformer/vfe/model.py:193-196`, `transformer/vfe/stack.py:76-105`, `transformer/vfe/block.py:157-175`, and `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:725-746`. The application site in the φ-mode inner loop is `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:1164-1170`, immediately followed by `compute_natural_gradient_gpu(grad_mu, grad_sigma, sigma, eps=eps)` at `1173-1175`. The omega-direct path mirrors this exactly at `2207-2218`. The placement is load-bearing: the AI gradient is summed into `grad_mu` / `grad_sigma` **before** the Fisher natural-gradient projection, so the analytic VFE gradient and the AI gradient share the same Riemannian metric per `[Amari1998]` — they are descent components on a single statistical manifold rather than two independently-rescaled directions in Euclidean coordinates.

**Law 1 (target-blindness) enforced by signature, not by convention.**

`VFEEStep.forward` at `transformer/vfe/e_step.py:725-746` has no `targets` parameter. The active-inference callback receives `(mu, sigma)` only (`transformer/vfe/stack.py:53-56`: `Callable[[torch.Tensor, torch.Tensor], Tuple[Optional[torch.Tensor], Optional[torch.Tensor]]]`). The predictive distribution `p_pred(v|μ)` is constructed from the model's own current belief through `PriorBank.decode(mu, sigma, tau)` or the `W_out` fallback at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:182-187`. No target token enters the AI gradient computation, so the self-evidencing surrogate cannot leak labels. This is the standard variational-EM separation `[DempsterLairdRubin1977]`.

**Explicit disclosure of the non-canonical substitution.**

`transformer/core/active_inference.py:28-37` carries a docstring labeled `NOVEL-CONSTRUCTION DISCLOSURE`:

> The lambda_prag term is the entropy of the model's OWN predictive distribution at the current mu_i. This is NOT the canonical EFE pragmatic value, which per `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022]` is `E_q[-log p^*(o)]` — the expected negative log preference under a target distribution. The present construction is a self-evidencing / self-confidence surrogate that drives the readout toward low-entropy (peaked) predictions without requiring an external preference. lambda_prag is named for symmetry with the EFE literature; readers comparing this against `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022]` should note the substitution.

This disclosure is exactly the discipline `external_canon_inference.md §10` demands: it names the substitution, cites the canonical form being replaced, and does not claim that FEP alone implies the substitution. It satisfies the pitfall §10.3 ("Claims that FEP alone implies an architectural choice ... require the explicit generative model that connects them") by refusing the claim rather than evading it.

**Self-evidencing is a recognized strand of the FEP literature.**

`[Friston2010]` defines variational free energy as the upper bound on negative log evidence `-log p(o)`, and the active-inference extension `[FristonEtAl2017 §2.3, §3.4]` treats self-evidencing — the agent acting to make its own observations match its own predictions — as the foundational drive. Under the self-evidencing reading, when no exogenous preference `p^*(o|C)` is supplied, the agent's preferences default to its own predictive distribution, and the pragmatic objective collapses to minimizing the entropy of that distribution (the agent prefers to be certain about its own predictions, which is the operational definition of self-evidencing). This is not a fringe interpretation: Hohwy 2016 ("The self-evidencing brain," *Noûs* 50(2)) makes this explicit, and Friston has framed FEP as a self-evidencing principle in multiple statements of the theory. A language model running unconditionally (training-time forward pass with no externally specified preference distribution over tokens) is exactly the no-exogenous-`C` setting in which the self-evidencing pragmatic reduction applies. The substitution `E_q[-log p^*(o)] → H[p_pred(v|μ)]` is therefore not arbitrary; it is the special case `p^* = p_pred` of the canonical form.

**Epistemic term is standard BALD MI with correct sign and decomposition.**

The epistemic term implements `MI(v; μ | q_i) = E_s[KL(p_s || p̄)]` via the BALD identity `[Houlsby2011]`, with a Welford-style two-pass autograd accumulation (`transformer/core/active_inference.py:269-349`). The sign convention is correct relative to EFE: `epi_per_sample_weight = -epistemic_weight / epistemic_samples` at line `306`. Combined with descent on `F + F_AI`, the negative coefficient on MI is equivalent to maximizing MI in the belief update, which matches `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 7]`'s convention that epistemic value is *subtracted* from G (so minimizing G *maximizes* information gain). The BALD point-conditional decode (`sigma=0` passed to `_readout` in both passes, lines `290` and `318`) prevents double-counting σ between the reparameterization path and the KL-trace term of `PriorBank.decode`, with the exact rationale documented at lines `224-237`. This is a non-trivial correctness detail; the implementation gets it right and explains why.

**Two roles cleanly separated: training E-step vs. generation-time EFE.**

The codebase already implements the canonical-form `G = risk + ambiguity − epistemic_value` for generation-time action selection in `transformer/vfe/efe.py:28-150` (`VFEExpectedFreeEnergy`). Risk is `E_{q(o|a)}[-log p^*(o)]` via `compute_risk` (imported from `transformer/core/expected_free_energy.py`); ambiguity is the canonical mean predictive entropy `E_z[H[p(o|z)]]` (not `H[p̄]`, with the anti-double-count comment at lines `90-95`); epistemic value is BALD MI. The training-time E-step augmentation (`VFEActiveInference`) and the generation-time EFE policy (`VFEExpectedFreeEnergy`) share config fields `epistemic_weight` / `epistemic_samples` but compute different functionals against different operational targets. The architectural separation is exactly what `external_canon_inference.md §2` prescribes for the role of EFE: G scores *policies* (here: candidate next tokens at generation), not beliefs (the training-time E-step uses VFE plus a self-evidencing regularizer, not G). The codebase does not conflate the two.

**Fail-fast guards prevent silent-drop and silent-bias regimes.**

`transformer/core/active_inference.py:514-535` (per the evidence pack; the function `wire_readout_references`) raises `ValueError` on `active_inference=True + closed_form_e_step=True` (EFE would do nothing because there is no iterative belief update to augment) and on `active_inference=True + use_deq=True` (DEQ implicit-diff backward uses VFE-only Jacobian; the AI gradient would not flow back to parameters). The `/vfe` package eliminates the analogous footguns by construction: `VFEConfig` exposes neither flag.

**Inference-mode guard.**

`transformer/core/active_inference.py:171-172` returns `(None, None)` when `torch.is_inference_mode_enabled()` is true, so generation-time forward passes don't crash on `torch.autograd.grad` calls that cannot be promoted. This is defensive engineering, not load-bearing for theory, but it prevents a class of runtime failures that would otherwise nullify the claim.

**Citations.**

`[Amari1998]` — natural gradient as steepest descent on the statistical manifold; supports the claim that summing AI gradient + analytic VFE gradient *before* the Fisher projection is the correct way to combine multiple objectives on one belief manifold (Fisher is a property of the q-parameterization, not the loss).

`[ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 7]` — canonical EFE form G = pragmatic + epistemic with pragmatic = `E_q[-log p^*]` and policy posterior `q(π) ∝ exp(-G(π))`. Cited correctly in the disclosure docstring as the form being substituted from.

`[Friston2010]` — variational free energy as upper bound on `-log p(o)`; self-evidencing framing.

`[FristonEtAl2017 §2.3, §3.4]` — process theory of active inference; self-evidencing as foundational drive; supports the reduction `p^* = p_pred` in the no-exogenous-`C` setting.

`[DempsterLairdRubin1977]` — E-step must not see targets; satisfied by the `VFEEStep.forward` signature.

`external_canon_inference.md §2` — standard EFE decomposition.

`external_canon_inference.md §10.3` — pitfall about FEP-implies claims; the implementation passes this by labeling the substitution and refusing the FEP-implies framing.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following can be demonstrated.

**(F1) The φ-mode E-step does not actually consume the AI gradient under `active_inference=True`.** A runtime trace under the active config in `transformer/vfe/train_vfe.py` showing that `active_inference_fn` is `None` at `e_step.py:1165`, or that `grad_mu` is overwritten before the natural-gradient projection at `1173`, would falsify the wiring half of the claim. The pre-fix protocol requires verifying the line is reached at runtime under the active config; a tracer-injection or `logger.debug` patch at line `1165` returning the count of non-`None` callback returns over a training step would settle this empirically.

**(F2) The self-evidencing reduction `p^* = p_pred` is not defensible under `[Friston2010]` / `[FristonEtAl2017]` / Hohwy 2016 for the no-exogenous-preference setting.** If a primary-source citation can be produced showing that the FEP self-evidencing literature *specifically forbids* setting the preference distribution equal to the current predictive distribution (e.g., on grounds that this produces a trivial fixed point where any belief is preferred so long as it is confident), the theoretical-justification half of the claim fails. A red argument that `H[p_pred]` minimization can drive degenerate belief collapse not balanced by the BALD MI term — and that such collapse is empirically observed in the active config — would also falsify the construction. The relevant empirical test: ablate `epistemic_weight = 0`, train for a few thousand steps, check whether predictive entropy collapses and validation loss degrades versus `active_inference=False`. If entropy collapses *and* validation loss degrades, the self-evidencing surrogate is empirically broken in this setting, regardless of its theoretical pedigree.

**(F3) The published F functional in `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex` is claimed to derive the AI augmentation.** If the manuscript text at the `\label{eq:free_energy_functional_final}` site asserts that the `+λ_prag · H[p_pred] − λ_epi · MI` augmentation is a *consequence* of the gauge-theoretic free energy (rather than an independent active-inference extension on the software side), the honesty-of-disclosure half of the defense fails. Red can falsify by producing a manuscript line that claims the augmentation is derived in the paper. The evidence pack reports the opposite — the published F has five terms (α, λ_h, β-coupled, γ-coupled, observation likelihood), and no `\;\, \!`-style EFE augmentation appears — and the disclosure docstring explicitly attributes the augmentation to active inference, not to the published F. If red shows otherwise, this falsification triggers.

**(F4) The sign convention is internally inconsistent.** If the BALD MI sign at `transformer/core/active_inference.py:306` (`epi_per_sample_weight = -epistemic_weight / epistemic_samples`) produces *minimization* of MI when descended on (e.g., because of an unaccounted sign in `_readout` or in the KL direction), the EFE-correspondence claim fails. The relevant check: a small-dim finite-difference test computing `MI(μ + δ·grad_mu, σ) − MI(μ, σ)` and verifying it is positive (descent on `F + F_AI` increases MI). If this returns a negative number, the sign is wrong and the construction does not implement epistemic-value maximization.

**(F5) The Fisher natural-gradient projection at `e_step.py:1173-1175` does not actually whiten the summed `grad_mu + ai_grad_mu` against the same metric as the analytic VFE gradient.** If `compute_natural_gradient_gpu` is in fact computing two separate projections or applying a different metric to the AI component, the "single descent metric" claim is misleading. Reading the implementation of `compute_natural_gradient_gpu` and verifying it operates on the sum (not on the components) settles this; the cited lines pass `grad_mu` (already incremented by `ai_grad_mu`) and `grad_sigma` (already incremented), which is structurally correct — but if the function internally re-routes the AI component, the claim fails.

If F1, F4, or F5 holds, the wiring half collapses. If F2 or F3 holds, the theoretical-justification half collapses. The strongest single attack — the one I would expect red to mount — is F2 framed via the policy-vs-belief mismatch: EFE in `[ParrPezzuloFriston2022]` scores policies through `q(π) ∝ exp(-G(π))`, not beliefs through `∂G/∂μ`. The defense against that attack is the self-evidencing reduction plus the architectural separation (`VFEExpectedFreeEnergy` for the policy role, `VFEActiveInference` for the belief-regularizer role); if red can produce a primary-source statement that the policy/belief distinction is non-negotiable in the EFE literature, that defense weakens. I do not currently see such a primary-source statement; the self-evidencing strand of the FEP literature treats the agent's beliefs and policies as continuously coupled, and Friston has framed perception itself as an active-inference process. But that is precisely where the debate should focus.
