## Reasoning

The two sub-claims have opposite outcomes on the evidence, and the verdict cannot collapse to a single side without distorting one of them.

For §1.1 line 74, the sentence is grammatically scoped to a two-thread unification: the two `while`-clauses preceding the main clause (quantum-information `bit` + active-inference observer behavior). Sengupta 2016 unites exactly those two threads in a gauge-theoretic framing, citing the same Friston 2010 / Friston 2017 references the line 74 sentence names. The strength of Sengupta's gauge-theoretic construction is irrelevant to this sub-claim because the line 74 sentence does not specify a rigor threshold beyond "rigorous mathematical framework," and Sengupta is published in PLoS Biology with explicit mathematical machinery (variational free energy as Lagrangian, Fisher-information manifold, symmetry-group equivalence classes of generative models). The manuscript also fails to cite Sengupta or any of the gauge-FEP / geometric-Bayesian-mechanics successor literature, which red identifies as a calibration failure: a priority claim that has not surveyed its closest competitor is not a calibrated priority claim. Blue's defense rested on re-quantifying "these threads" to include a four-element conjunction imported from the §1.8 line 172 list; the sentence's grammar does not license that re-quantification, and blue's rebuttal concedes this in its first paragraph.

For §1.8 line 172, the verb "uniquely combines" makes the claim conjunctive by construction. Red's per-ingredient enumeration (Sengupta for (i), Kirchhoff-Parr-Palacios-Friston-Kiverstein 2018 / Ramstead 2020 for (ii), pymdp / RxInfer / SPM for (iii), Tsai 2019 / Ramsauer 2021 for (iv)) demonstrates each ingredient has antecedents but does not produce a single prior work that supplies all four. Conjunctive uniqueness claims are defeated by a single conjunction-bearing counterexample, not by per-ingredient counterexamples, and red's own rebuttal concedes that no such single counterexample exists. Independent WebFetch of Sengupta confirms it operates at conceptual-reframing level — "no mention of transformer models, multi-agent systems with transport operators, sandwich products acting on covariances" — which corroborates blue's primary-source claim that Sengupta supplies (i) only at the level of motivational vocabulary, not at bundle-construction level. The internal canon at `external_canon_inference.md` line 29 independently registers the bundle-level multi-agent `Ω_ij`-coupled construction as user-introduced and not the field standard, which is the strongest available external corroboration that the §1.8 conjunction at the bundle-construction reading is unattested.

Two further factors weigh on the §1.8 outcome. First, blue's falsification condition 5 (single-seed Ouroboros Tower, slow-channel deferred, simulator code unreleased, per `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:127`) is a real internal-honesty gap that red exploits: conjunct (iii) "computational implementation demonstrating participatory bootstrap" is not yet fully delivered on the manuscript's own §1.7 disclosure. Second, the §1.5 line 125 qualification — recovery of attention is "up to a separately introduced learned bilinear compatibility `M` and the standard normalization and bias assumptions" — is not carried forward into §1.8(iv), and red is right that the §1.8(iv) phrasing as written is stronger than what §1.5 admits. Neither factor is sufficient to defeat the conjunction claim — they are honesty/calibration defects that downgrade the strength of (iii) and (iv) without producing a single-work counterexample to the conjunction itself — but they do require textual carry-forward to make the §1.8 claim defensible as written.

The §1.4 line 100 "two radical possibilities" dichotomy is a separate rhetorical concern that both teams agree should be softened to align with the §1.5 line 117 framing (dual-aspect-monism / interacting-agent formalism). This is an edit recommendation, not a sub-claim that drives the verdict.

REMAND is the correct outcome because both teams have cited primary-source evidence on separate parts of the compound claim, and the resolution requires concrete textual edits to two different lines (§1.1 line 74 and §1.8 line 172) plus a calibration edit to §1.4 line 100. A single-side verdict would absolve one defect or overreach the other.
