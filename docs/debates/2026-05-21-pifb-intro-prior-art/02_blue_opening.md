## Steelman (opposing position)

Sengupta, Tozzi, Cooray, Douglas and Friston (2016) already proposed a gauge-theoretic formulation of the Free Energy Principle, Verlinde (2011) already derived gravity from information-theoretic considerations on holographic screens, Friston's active-inference literature already supplies an observer-personal variational dynamics, and Vaswani et al. ## Position

The §1.1 line 74 priority claim and the §1.8 line 172 "Distinguishing Features" claim are defensible *as conjunction claims* against the canonical external literature. Each ingredient — Wheeler's "it from bit" ontology [Wheeler1990], variational free energy [Friston2010, ParrPezzuloFriston2022], gauge-theoretic FEP framing [Sengupta2016], emergent spacetime from information [Jacobson1995, Verlinde2011, Swingle2012, Cao2017], transformer attention [Vaswani2017], and observer-relative frameworks [Rovelli1996, Fuchs2014, LahavNeemeh2022] — exists in the canon. None of the canonical works supplies all four of the manuscript's conjuncts: (i) explicit gauge structure on belief and model fibers, (ii) hierarchical multi-scale meta-agent formation via consensus and renormalization, (iii) a working computational instantiation as a transformer language model with no learned attention projections / no MLPs / no activations, (iv) empirical scaling-law validation. The closest near-miss in the canonical literature is Sengupta et al. 2016, and on inspection it supplies only (i), and only at the level of a geometric reframing gesture; it does not supply (ii), (iii), or (iv). Subject to the falsification conditions below, the conjunction is genuinely novel.

## Pre-emption of the strongest attack

The strongest attack red can mount is: "Sengupta 2016 already supplies the gauge-theoretic FEP framing; therefore the §1.1 line 74 'no rigorous mathematical framework has united these threads' claim is false on its face, because Sengupta is exactly such a framework." This is the most acute potential counterexample and the evidence pack flags it explicitly (line 56).

The pre-emption: the §1.1 line 74 sentence asserts unification of *the threads named in the same sentence and the surrounding paragraph* — Wheeler's participatory bootstrap, the quantum-information formalization of the bit, active inference, and the computational demonstration that "'it' can computationally emerge from 'bit'." Sengupta 2016 supplies a partial geometric reframing of FEP, with hierarchical extension as a gesture and no computational instantiation. Reading the line 74 sentence as a claim about the *conjunction* — Wheeler's bootstrap plus active inference plus computational instantiation plus emergent-spacetime ingredients — Sengupta does not falsify the claim, because Sengupta does not engage Wheeler, does not instantiate computationally, and supplies no empirical demonstration. Reading the sentence as a claim about *any single* prior framework (active inference alone, or gauge-FEP alone) being a unifier, Sengupta is closer but the sentence does not make that claim.

The honest concession blue makes up front: the manuscript should cite Sengupta 2016 in §1.8 to make the conjunction reading explicit. Grepping `Attention/` shows Sengupta is not cited anywhere in the manuscripts (only in a 2026-05-20 edits log). That is a citation gap and red will exploit it. Adding the citation strengthens the manuscript's position rather than weakening it: Sengupta provides the closest non-trivial precedent and the manuscript's contribution is more visible when the precedent is acknowledged.

## Falsification conditions

The §1.1 line 74 claim and the §1.8 line 172 four-item conjunction claim are not defensible if any of the following hold:

1. **A single prior published work supplies all four conjuncts at line 172.** That is, if there exists a citation `[X, year]` in which the same construction supplies (i) explicit fiber-bundle gauge structure with belief and model fibers, (ii) multi-scale meta-agent formation via consensus or renormalization, (iii) a working computational instantiation as a transformer language model, and (iv) scaling-law empirical validation. Red is invited to produce such a citation. None of Sengupta 2016, Verlinde 2011, Cao-Carroll-Michalakis 2017, Ramstead 2020, Sakthivadivel 2022, Knuth 2014, Caticha 2010, Sengupta-Friston 2018, Ren et al. 2025, or the hierarchical-FEP literature (Friston2017Graphical) supplies all four. If red produces one that does, the conjunction claim fails.

2. **The line 74 sentence is read narrowly enough that Sengupta 2016 is itself "a rigorous mathematical framework uniting these threads."** Under that reading, the priority claim is false. Blue resists that reading because line 74 specifies "to show how 'it' can computationally emerge from 'bit'" — the *computational emergence* qualifier requires (iii), which Sengupta does not supply.

3. **The four-item list at line 172 conjuncts are redundantly satisfied by a single sub-conjunct.** For example, if (iii) "computational implementation demonstrating participatory bootstrap" reduces to "computational implementation of attention" — then [Vaswani2017] would satisfy it, and the claim becomes trivial. Blue resists this reading because §1.5 explicitly characterizes the participatory bootstrap as the cross-scale meta-agent feedback loop where higher-scale aggregations propagate updates back to constituents (Ouroboros Tower), not a generic attention implementation. Read with that content, only the manuscript's own §sec:participatory and §sec:results supply (iii).

4. **The manuscript's own §1.5 weakened framing ("we exhibit information-geometric analogues of physics-like structures within an interacting-agent formalism," line 117) undercuts the §1.1 strong priority claim.** This is an *internal tension*, not a falsification of the priority claim against the external canon. Blue grants the tension exists and notes that the resolution is the §1.7 Epistemic Status three-level structure (line 121–131): Level 1 (implemented) covers the transformer recovery; Level 2 (computationally implemented) covers the Ouroboros Tower; Level 3 (speculative) covers the physical interpretation. Under the three-level scoping, the §1.1 priority claim is about the *unified mathematical construction*, not the speculative physical interpretation, and survives. If a judge reads §1.5 line 117 as overriding §1.1 line 74, the claim's *physical* priority weakens, but the *mathematical-framework* priority does not.

5. **The "computational implementation" conjunct (iii) is interpreted as requiring a published peer-reviewed transformer trained at scale.** Under that reading, the manuscript's single-seed Ouroboros Tower simulation (Section §sec:results, fast-channel only, slow-channel coupling deferred, simulator code not yet released — manuscript line 127) does not yet satisfy (iii). Blue treats this as a known epistemic-status limitation already disclosed at line 127 and line 140; it does not falsify the conjunction claim, but it does weaken (iii) until the multi-seed + slow-channel + code-release follow-up is delivered.

If red produces a single citation `[X]` in which (i)+(ii)+(iii)+(iv) are jointly satisfied, blue concedes the conjunction claim outright. Short of that, the conjunction stands.
