# Blue Memo — Variational Inference / FEP

## Lens

Variational-inference / FEP positioning of §Speculative Extensions: whether the section's load-bearing structural content (pullback metric, signature pathway, consensus regulator, eigenvalue decomposition, phenomenological interpretation) belongs to the legitimate territory of FEP / active-inference Outlook-mode extensions, and whether the placeholder-isolation reading holds at the level of the variational scaffolding. The lens also polices the canonical FEP literature's own register for ambitious Outlook-mode extensions.

## External canon (3 sources)

1. **Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, *Active Inference: The Free Energy Principle in Mind, Brain, and Behavior* (2022), Ch. 14 "Active Inference and Theoretical Neurobiology" and the closing "Open Questions" section** — the canonical textbook on active inference. The closing chapters explicitly include speculative-extension territory (consciousness, embodiment, social inference, evolution) flagged as research-programme commitments rather than as derived consequences of the FEP itself. Parr-Pezzulo-Friston: "The free energy principle ... is a normative theory ... It is not, in itself, a complete account of any particular cognitive or biological process; rather it is a framework within which such accounts can be developed." Operative point: Outlook-mode extension chapters are standard practice in the FEP literature and are canonically appropriate provided they are flagged as such.

2. **Friston (2010) *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* 11, 127–138, §"Boxes 1–4" and §"A free-energy formulation of the brain"** — the canonical FEP statement. The original paper itself includes substantial interpretive material flagged as such — Box 4 ("Free-energy and self-organization") explicitly discusses speculative implications for "the origin of life" and "evolutionary selection" without claiming these are derived from the variational principle. The interpretive register is standard.

3. **Blei, Kucukelbir & McAuliffe (2017) *J. Amer. Statist. Assoc.* 112, 859–877, §1 "Introduction" and §5 "Discussion"** — the canonical variational-inference review. The Discussion section explicitly registers VI's role as a *family of approximation methods* with known approximation gaps (the KL between `q` and the true posterior, Form 2 of `external_canon_inference.md`). Operative point: structural existence claims (a variational family exists; an approximation scheme is well-defined) are independent of empirical-fit claims (the specific approximation has small KL on a specific dataset).

## Defense

The variational-inference status of §Speculative Extensions is that of *interpretive extension on top of a variational scaffold*. The scaffold (the multi-agent free-energy functional with gauge-transport coupling terms `Σ_ij β_ij KL(q_i || Ω_ij q_j)`, the natural-gradient dynamics on the product manifold of Gaussian states and gauge frames) is the user's novel construction, labeled as such by the manuscript's own framework chapter, and is the *foundation* on which §Speculative Extensions then erects further structures. Both teams should recognize, per `external_canon_inference.md §1`, that the multi-agent coupling form is user-introduced novel work — but §Speculative Extensions is a downstream construction; its register is "extending the user's framework toward physics and phenomenology", not "claiming standard FEP implies any of this." The 2582 opening framing explicitly registers this: "They take structures established in the preceding theory and implementation sections ... and push them into territory where the framework no longer makes empirically falsifiable predictions in the sense set out in Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations}."

The 3060 admission ("This interpretation may be unfalsifiable") is canonically appropriate for an interpretive extension of a variational framework. [BleiKucukelbirJordan 2017 §5] explicitly registers that variational-inference frameworks come equipped with interpretive choices — choice of variational family, choice of KL direction, choice of mean-field factorization — that are not themselves empirically tested but constitute the framework within which empirical tests acquire meaning. The phenomenological-interpretation commitment at 3056 ("information ... is the dimensionally fundamental quantity") is canonically a framework-constitutive choice in this sense, not a present-tense empirical claim. The 3094–3102 "Philosophical Status and Research Program" subsubsection's "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" and "framework may ultimately function as philosophical interpretation rather than scientific theory" admissions are the canonical hygiene moves for content of this register.

The placeholder-isolation sub-claim has its variational-inference grounding in the fact that the load-bearing structural content of §Speculative Extensions (pullback metric, signature pathway, consensus regulator, eigenvalue decomposition, phenomenological interpretation) does not depend on the specific empirical scaling exponent at 2562–2575 or on the specific phase-transition signature at 2374–2516. The §Speculative Extensions structural claims are mathematical-existence claims about what would follow from the variational scaffold under listed postulates; they are not claims about the specific empirical fit of any particular run. The 2807 reference to "validated empirically in Section~\ref{sec:transformers}" is structural only — it anchors the GL(K,ℝ) step-1 of the Postulates-Required pathway to the observation that the framework "produces meaningful dynamics", not to the specific exponent value `b = -1.049`. Even if the empirical exponent were replaced by the operationally independent `ω² ∝ Σ_p⁻¹` test deferred to MAgent_Model-main, the structural step-1 (full non-compact gauge symmetry produces meaningful dynamics) would either hold or fail on its own terms, independent of the specific scaling-exponent value.

The 3070 reference to the mass-from-precision identification is the only place in §Speculative Extensions where an empirical-test status is explicitly registered, and the manuscript's "no operationally independent measurement is reported in this manuscript" is the strongest available variational-inference hygiene move. Per `external_canon_inference.md §1` Form 3, the Fisher-precision quantity at the heart of the identification is well-defined within the variational scaffold (`Λ_q = Σ⁻¹` for a Gaussian recognition density is canonical [BleiKucukelbirJordan 2017 §2.2]); whether `Λ_q` corresponds to *physical mass* is an interpretive identification that the manuscript explicitly declines to claim as established. The placeholder-isolation reading holds: the structural identification is independent of any specific empirical scaling, and the manuscript registers this correctly.

The 3157 reference to the self-excited circuit anchors to the threshold-detector single-seed dynamics, but the load-bearing content of the 3151–3157 closing is the *structural* observation that variational free-energy minimization plus cross-scale priors plus threshold-triggered meta-agent formation produces a feedback loop. The structural existence of such a loop does not depend on the specific single-seed run; it follows from the architecture itself (free-energy minimization is the gradient flow, threshold detection triggers meta-agent formation, meta-agents feed back priors). The 3157 phrasing "within-framework observation about the threshold-detector single-seed dynamics" is therefore an honest registration that the present empirical instantiation is single-seed; the structural Wheeler-self-excited-circuit framing at 3155 is independent of which specific seed exhibited the pattern.
