# Blue Memo — Information Geometer

## Lens

Information-geometric well-posedness of the Fisher arc length (2600–2611), the block-Fisher form for Gaussian beliefs (2613–2627), the score-function form of the vertical fiber metric (2670–2676), and the Fisher-mass identification with its explicit "no operationally independent measurement is reported" disclaimer (3070). The lens defends the canonical information-geometric primitives used as structural scaffolding in §Speculative Extensions as belonging to standard Amari–Nagaoka treatment, with novel constructions (multi-agent gauge transport, dimensionless-constants research program) labeled as the user's extensions rather than as claimed-standard primitives.

## External canon (3 sources)

1. **Amari & Nagaoka, *Methods of Information Geometry* (2000), Ch. 2 §2.1–2.3** — the Fisher information matrix in score-function form `g_ij(θ) = E_p[(∂_i log p)(∂_j log p)]` is the unique (up to positive scalar multiple) Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold invariant under sufficient statistics [Cencov 1972, theorem stated as Theorem 2.6 in Amari-Nagaoka]. The Fisher metric is the canonical Riemannian primitive on any parameterized family of probability distributions; arc length along a curve `γ(t)` in the statistical manifold is `L(γ) = ∫ √(g_ij γ̇^i γ̇^j) dt`, which is the standard Riemannian length.

2. **Cencov, *Statistical Decision Rules and Optimal Inference* (1972)** — the Fisher metric is *unique up to scalar* under sufficient-statistic invariance. The "up to scalar" is structural: Cencov's theorem fixes the metric to a one-parameter family `c · g_Fisher` with `c > 0`, but does not fix `c` itself. Selecting `c` requires an external scale choice (e.g., normalizing by KL-divergence in nats vs bits gives a fixed factor of `ln 2`).

3. **Bhattacharya & Patrangenaru, *Nonparametric Inference on Manifolds* (2012), §3.3** — the Bures-Wasserstein / Fisher-Rao framework for the manifold of Gaussian distributions. The Fisher-Rao distance between two `K`-dimensional Gaussians is well-defined, the block-Fisher form `g_Fisher((δμ, δΣ)) = δμ^⊤ Σ⁻¹ δμ + ½ tr(Σ⁻¹ δΣ Σ⁻¹ δΣ)` at 2670–2676 is the textbook expression [also in [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §7.2]; cf. also Skovgaard 1984 *Scand. J. Statist.* 11, 211–223], and the geodesic distance has been studied in [Pinele, Strapasson, Costa 2020 *Entropy* 22(4):404] and [Calvo & Oller 1990 *J. Multivariate Anal.* 35, 223–242].

## Defense

The Fisher arc length at 2606 is the canonical Riemannian arc length on the statistical manifold of Gaussian belief states [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §2.1]. The explicit 2609 disclaimer ("We do not identify this quantity with relativistic proper time: Fisher arc length and special-relativistic proper time have opposite character") is information-geometrically essential and correctly stated — Fisher-Rao geometry is positive-definite Riemannian (by Cencov uniqueness, the metric is up to scalar a positive-definite Riemannian metric on the statistical manifold) and proper time is indefinite Lorentzian. The construction declines the over-identification that has bitten other "information-geometric time" proposals in the literature, and is canonically calibrated as a per-agent dimensionless count parameter (the 2588 dimensional disclaimer making `τ_i` dimensionless by construction via normalization by 1 bit).

The block-Fisher form at 2670–2676 is the textbook Fisher metric for the manifold of `K`-dimensional Gaussians [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §7.2; Bhattacharya-Patrangenaru 2012 §3.3]. The natural-gradient update equations at 2623–2627 (`d μ/dt = -η_μ Σ (∇_μ F)`, `d Σ/dt = -2 η_Σ Σ (∇_Σ F) Σ`, the gauge-frame Lie-group natural gradient `d U/dt = -η_φ U (G_κ⁻¹ ∇_φ F)`) are the canonical Fisher-Rao natural gradient on the product manifold of Gaussian states and the Lie group [Amari 1998 *Neural Computation* 10, 251–276]. The `2Σ · Σ` sandwich on the covariance sector is the canonical natural-gradient direction that preserves `Σ ∈ S+_K` (the SPD cone) automatically [Amari-Nagaoka 2000 §7.4]. These are not novel constructions; the manuscript invokes them correctly as load-bearing structural primitives.

The Fisher-mass identification at 3070 is the strongest within-section honesty signal in the manuscript: "We do not claim a computational test of `M_eff ∝ Σ_p⁻¹` in which `ω²` and `M_eff` are measured as operationally independent quantities; the within-framework reading above identifies the Fisher mass with the Hessian by construction on this slice, so the scaling is a definitional consequence of that identification rather than an empirical dispersion law, and no operationally independent measurement is reported in this manuscript." This is the canonical move in information geometry when the structural identification (mass ≡ Fisher precision) is well-posed within the framework but is not yet supported by an operationally independent test. The structural identification is well-posed: the Hessian of free energy and the posterior Fisher precision coincide in the isolated-agent limit by construction [the standard variational relationship `∇²_μ F = Σ_p⁻¹` for a quadratic potential, cf. Bishop *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* (2006) §10.1.1]. The manuscript correctly registers the empirical-test status as deferred.

The placeholder-isolation argument has its strongest information-geometric grounding in the Cencov-up-to-scalar issue at 3084.
