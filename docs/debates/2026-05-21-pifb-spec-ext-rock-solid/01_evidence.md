- **Sandwich identity for covariance transport.** `external_canon_math.md §2`: For a $(0,2)$-tensor / bilinear form / covariance matrix, parallel transport by $g \in G$ in representation $\rho$ acts as $T \to \rho(g^{-\top}) T \rho(g)^{-1}$. The user's framework uses this for $\Sigma$ transport throughout — this is canonical [Nakahara 2003 Ch. 9–10; Kobayashi-Nomizu Vol. I Ch. I–II].
- **Fisher information and Cencov's uniqueness.** `external_canon_math.md §1`: Fisher information matrix $g_{ij} = E[\partial_i \log p \cdot \partial_j \log p]$; Cencov 1972: the Fisher metric is the unique (up to scalar) Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold invariant under sufficient statistics.
- **Wick rotation, canonical form.** `Wick 1954` (*Phys. Rev.* 96, 1124): continuation of real base coordinate $\tau \to i\tau$ produces a real Euclidean metric throughout, with no complex off-diagonal pieces to discard. `Zinn-Justin 2002` (*Quantum Field Theory and Critical Phenomena* §3) codifies the prescription on the gravitational path-integral measure (`Hartle-Hawking 1983` *Phys. Rev. D* 28, 2960).
- **Gauge-orbit / Haar averaging on non-compact groups.** Standard fact (e.g., `Folland 1995, A Course in Abstract Harmonic Analysis` Ch. 2): a non-compact locally compact group has a left-invariant Haar measure but it is infinite. For $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ specifically, the Haar measure is infinite even for constant elements. The manuscript admits this at 2928.
- **Structural realism — ESR vs OSR.** SEP "Structural Realism" (`https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/structural-realism/`): Worrall (1989) develops epistemic structural realism as a "purely semantic and epistemological theory"; Ladyman (1998) develops ontic structural realism as a metaphysical position.
