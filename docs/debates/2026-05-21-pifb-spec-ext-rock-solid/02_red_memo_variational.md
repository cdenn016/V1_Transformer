## Attack — the §Speculative Extensions scaffold inherits an unvalidated multi-agent F functional (sub-claim 8)

The variational free-energy functional that drives §Speculative Extensions is the multi-agent coupled form: F is built from per-agent KL terms plus $\beta_{ij}$-weighted KL couplings of the form $\beta_{ij} \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$, with $\Omega_{ij}$ the gauge-transport operator [PIFB:3039–3045 and §Theory section of the manuscript]. The standard variational free-energy literature [Friston2010, ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 2] is single-agent (or hierarchical with a single ancestral generative model). Multi-agent FEP extensions exist (e.g., variational ecology [Ramstead2020]; graphical brain [Friston2017Graphical]) but use different couplings — they do not employ gauge-transport-coupled KL terms with $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i) \exp(-\phi_j)$. The specific functional form on which §Speculative Extensions rests is a user-introduced novel construction [external_canon_inference.md §1: "Multi-agent coupling terms of the form $\Sigma_{ij} \beta_{ij} KL(q_i || \Omega_{ij} q_j)$. These are user-introduced. The standard FEP is single-agent [...] the *specific* coupling form via gauge transport $\Omega_{ij}$ is a user construction, not the field standard."]. The 2807 forward reference — "validated empirically in Section~\ref{sec:transformers}" — anchors step 1 of the Postulates Required pathway ("$\mathrm{GL}(K, \mathbb{R})$ with real Gaussians") to the §Results multi-seed scaling fit at 2559–2577. That fit is the only empirical validation of the multi-agent F scaffold cited in the section, and the user has flagged it as placeholder. The 3157 closing sentence — "This is a within-framework observation about the threshold-detector single-seed dynamics of Section~\ref{sec:results}" — anchors the self-excited-circuit framing to the §Results single-seed run at 2374–2516, also flagged as placeholder. The §Speculative Extensions section thus rests on (a) a novel multi-agent F functional not in standard FEP, and (b) two §Results forward references whose data the user has marked as placeholder. The 3127–3145 boxed enumeration covers (i)–(iv) about the temporal generator, eigenvalue hierarchy, and trace-orthogonal spatial generators; it does not cover (a) the novelty of the multi-agent F functional, nor (b) the placeholder status of the §Results empirical anchors.

## Attack — the 3084 research program lives at a scale below standard FEP's empirical reach

The 3084 symmetric success-failure criterion for the dimensionless-constants research program reads (paraphrased): success would be "an instance of the derivation the interpretation commits to," and failure "under a fully developed information-geometric construction --- one in which the relevant statistical manifolds, comparison structure, and Cencov scale fixings are all specified --- would constitute substantive evidence against the phenomenological interpretation." Even granting the philosophy-of-science attack (deferred falsifiability) and the info-geometer's attack (Cencov does not provide scale fixings), the variational-inference lens adds: standard FEP / variational inference has nothing to say about deriving dimensionless physical constants from a statistical manifold. The canonical FEP literature [Friston2010, ParrPezzuloFriston2022] connects variational free-energy minimization to perception, action, and learning in biological and artificial agents; it does not connect variational free energy to the fine-structure constant or to mass ratios. The 3082 sentence "Dimensionless ratios between fundamental constants should be derivable from pure information geometry if this interpretation is correct" is a *commitment*, not a derivation — and the commitment is to a derivation that the standard variational-inference literature provides no mechanism for. The 3060 admission "this interpretation may be unfalsifiable" earlier in the same subsection is the manuscript's own acknowledgement that the commitment may not cash out; 3084 then attempts to rescue falsifiability by deferring to a future construction. From the variational-inference lens, the commitment is not just deferred — it is to a derivation whose target (dimensionless physical constants from a statistical-manifold geometry) is far outside the canonical scope of variational inference. Sub-claim 7 is therefore weakened from two independent canon directions.

- [external_canon_inference.md §1, §3]: standard FEP is single-agent or single-ancestral-hierarchy; multi-agent extensions exist [Ramstead2020, Friston2017Graphical] but use different couplings than the gauge-transport-coupled KL form $\beta_{ij} \mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}[q_j])$ that §Speculative Extensions presupposes. The §Speculative Extensions scaffold therefore rests on a user-novel functional, and its only validation in the present manuscript is via §Results data the user has marked as placeholder.

- [BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]: variational inference is *approximate* Bayesian inference; the approximation gap is the KL between $q$ and the true posterior. The user's framework inherits this gap. The 3098 "It makes the framework internally consistent by avoiding the need to derive dimensioned quantities from dimensionless information theory" is internally consistent with the framework's commitment but is not a derivation; the variational-inference scaffold provides no derivation route to dimensioned quantities.

- [ParrPezzuloFriston2022 Ch. 2]: variational free-energy minimization is canonically a perception-action-learning principle in biological and artificial agents; it is not canonically a route to deriving dimensionless physical constants. The 3082 commitment lives outside the canonical scope of variational inference.
