## Concession (one red argument that holds under canon)

Red is correct that PIFB:3024 contains a prose-level base-vs-fiber confusion. The literal sentence reads "Only a tiny sliver (e.g. 4 dimensions out of K) becomes phenomenal spacetime." The 2970 base-vs-fiber clarification establishes that the spectral decomposition lives on the base manifold, at most $\dim(\mathcal{C})$ eigenvalues; K is canonically used elsewhere in the manuscript as the *fiber* dimension (the Gaussian dimension, $K(K+3)/2 \approx 3 \times 10^5$ fiber dimensions). The "4 out of K" phrasing in 3024 reads as if 4 base dimensions are extracted from K fiber dimensions, which is the confusion the 2970 clarification was put in to forestall. The 3018 "Hypothesized $(3+1)$ Structure (Speculative)" subsubsection header is in place, the 3022 "does not explain why exactly three spatial dimensions" admission is in place, but the 3024 prose collapses the base/fiber distinction in a way that is genuinely careless. **I concede this is a prose-level calibration leak.** The editorial fix is to rewrite 3024 as "Only a tiny sub-manifold of the base manifold (e.g., 4 dimensions out of $\dim(\mathcal{C})$) becomes phenomenal spacetime; the remaining base directions carry near-zero information flux and the vast fiber dimension space of the statistical manifold remains internal to belief representation." This is an editorial rewrite at one line.

## Core attack (red's load-bearing weakness under canon)

Red's Vector 3 supporting fire on PIFB:2738 mis-reads the prose seam. The literal sentence reads: "The bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense: a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback of a fiber-respecting metric on an information-bearing bundle along an agent's section, with the horizontal piece encoding how the agent's gauge frame varies across $\mathcal{C}$ and the vertical piece encoding how the relevant statistical field varies. The pulled-back tensors are agent-dependent because both the section and the connection $A^{(i)}$ are."

Red claims this sentence "drops the within-gauge qualifier." That is not what the sentence does. The sentence *explicitly says* "The pulled-back tensors are agent-dependent because both the section and the connection $A^{(i)}$ are." That is the within-gauge qualifier, in the same sentence. The first clause names the construction ("the bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense"); the second clause immediately qualifies what kind of precision is being claimed ("a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback ... along an agent's section"); the third clause closes the loop ("The pulled-back tensors are agent-dependent"). This is the canonical *qualified-precision* register, not an unqualified ontological claim.

The 2722–2723 paragraph is two sentences upstream and explicitly registers the agent-frame-dependent character of the horizontal block ("The horizontal block is consequently agent-frame-dependent whereby different gauge fixings of the same agent yield different horizontal contributions"). The 2724 paragraph closes that thought with "The bundle metric of Eq.~\eqref{eq:bundle_metric} is therefore best read as the metric pulled back *within an agent's gauge fixing*, with agent-independent geometry on $\mathcal{C}$ to be recovered after a regulated consensus averaging." [Lee 2013 *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* (2nd ed.) Prop. 11.25] establishes that the pullback of a covariant tensor field by a smooth map is a covariant tensor field on the source manifold — i.e., the pullback construction is canonically well-defined geometrically; the question of agent-dependence is a separate matter and is handled at 2722–2723.

The "precise sense" phrasing at 2738 is therefore not an unqualified ontological lift; it is a structural-mathematical claim that the pullback is a well-defined geometric object [Lee 2013 Prop. 11.25], with the agent-dependence carried by the same sentence and the surrounding paragraphs. Red's argument requires the reader to ignore the within-sentence qualifier ("agent-dependent because both the section and the connection $A^{(i)}$ are") and the surrounding paragraphs (2722–2723, 2724, 2660–2662). That is a possible reading but not the canonical reading of well-qualified mathematical prose.

The 2660–2662 over-arching frame for the entire pullback subsection — "this is a toy model demonstration that spacetime-like geometry can emerge from information, not a claim that this is how physical spacetime actually arises ... The reader should interpret the following sections as exploring what would follow if this problem could be solved" — is the section-level Outlook bracket that downstream prose inherits.
