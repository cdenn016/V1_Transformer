# Blue Memo — Geometer (Differential Geometry)

## Lens

Differential-geometric well-posedness of the load-bearing structural constructions in §Speculative Extensions: the associated-bundle horizontal-vertical decomposition (2682–2685), the bundle metric in a fixed gauge (2687–2689), the (0,2)-tensor pullback by a smooth section (2705), the eigenvalue / spectral decomposition of the pulled-back tensor (2950–2996), and the Lorentzian-conformal-class causal-cone alternative (2860–2883). The lens defends the mathematical-existence content as well-posed independent of the empirical-fit content.

## External canon (3 sources)

1. **Nakahara, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* (2003), §10.3 "Associated fiber bundles" and §9.4 "Connections on principal bundles"** — the horizontal-vertical decomposition `T_e P = H_e ⊕ V_e` and its descent to associated bundles `E = P ×_ρ V` is the standard construction; the connection one-form `A ∈ Ω¹(P; g)` specifies the horizontal complement, and the induced bundle metric `g_E = π* g_M ⊕_⊥ g_F` (horizontal plus vertical, with orthogonality enforced by the connection) is a textbook construction once a horizontal metric `g_M` and a fiber metric `g_F` are specified.

2. **Lee, *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* (2nd ed., 2013), Ch. 11 "The Cotangent Bundle"** — pullback of a (0,2)-tensor by a smooth map is a well-defined tensor on the source manifold. For a smooth section `σ : C → E` of a fiber bundle and a (0,2)-tensor `g_E` on the total space, the pullback `(σ)*g_E` is a (0,2)-tensor on `C`. This is Lee Proposition 11.25 (the pullback construction for covariant tensor fields).

3. **Sylvester's Law of Inertia** [O'Neill, *Semi-Riemannian Geometry* (1983), §3.3] — the signature of a non-degenerate symmetric bilinear form on a finite-dimensional vector space is invariant under change of basis; in particular, an indefinite form cannot be transformed to a definite form (or vice versa) by a basis change. The manuscript invokes this correctly at 2791 ("real GL(K,ℝ) on real Gaussian fibers is non-compact yet still produces positive-definite transport") and at 2874 ("$g$ has one negative eigenvalue and $\dim\Sigma$ positive eigenvalues, hence Lorentzian signature").

## Defense

The bundle-metric construction at 2687–2689 is differential-geometrically well-posed as a metric on the total space of the associated bundle `E_q = P ×_G B_state`, given (i) a connection on `P` specifying the horizontal subspace `H_(c,q) ⊂ T_(c,q) E_q`, (ii) a horizontal metric specified by the frame-twist quadratic form `κ(A_μ, A_ν)` with the piecewise `κ` convention at 2696–2700, and (iii) the Fisher-Rao vertical metric `g_B` on the fiber. The pullback `G_i^(q) = (σ_i^(q))* g_E_q` to the base manifold `C` is then a (0,2)-tensor on `C` by Lee Proposition 11.25 [Lee2013 Ch. 11]. The boxed expression at 2717 (`G_{i,μν}(c) = κ(A_μ^(i), A_ν^(i)) + E_{q_i(c)}[(∇_μ log q_i)(∇_ν log q_i)]`) is the explicit component form of this pullback, with the horizontal contribution coming from the connection and the vertical contribution from the score expectation. The construction is standard pullback geometry [Nakahara2003 §10.3], not novel, and is therefore not the locus where the "calibrated Outlook" claim could fail on differential-geometric grounds.

The gauge-non-invariance disclosure at 2722–2723 is differential-geometrically correct as stated: under a local gauge transformation `U_i → U_i · g(c)`, the connection one-form transforms with the Maurer-Cartan piece, `A → g⁻¹ A g + g⁻¹ dg`, [Nakahara2003 §10.3, Kobayashi-Nomizu Vol. I §III.2]. The first term is the adjoint action (which leaves `tr(A_μ A_ν)` invariant by cyclicity of the trace, for constant `g`), but the second is inhomogeneous. The 2722–2723 paragraph identifies this inhomogeneous term correctly and denies the Yang-Mills escape hatch on the grounds that `F_μν = 0` identically in Regime I — also a correct statement, since `F = dA + ½[A,A]` and a pure-gauge `A = U⁻¹ dU` has zero curvature [Nakahara2003 §10.4]. The Yang-Mills invariant `tr(F_μν F^μν)` is therefore identically zero in the present construction and supplies no gauge-invariant content; the routing of "the genuinely gauge-invariant ontological content" through the consensus/Haar-averaged construction at 2722–2723 is the only available canonical fallback, and the manuscript names it correctly.

The Lorentzian-signature causal-cone alternative at 2866–2877 is differential-geometrically straightforward as an existence demonstration: given a product manifold `M ≅ ℝ_τ × Σ` with positive-definite spatial metric `h_ab`, the indefinite form `g_μν = -c_I² δ_μ^τ δ_ν^τ + h_ab δ_μ^a δ_ν^b` has Lorentzian signature `(-,+,+,…,+)` by Sylvester's law of inertia [O'Neill1983 §3.3]. The 2876–2877 conformal-class observation ("the cone determines `g` only up to a positive conformal factor") is also standard: two metrics with the same null cone differ by a positive conformal factor [O'Neill1983 §2.5, Hawking-Ellis *The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time* (1973) §4.1]. The construction does not derive a unique metric, only a conformal class, and the manuscript explicitly registers this at 2876.

The eigenvalue-hierarchy decomposition at 2950–2996 is the spectral decomposition of a symmetric `n × n` bilinear form on `T_c C`, where `n = dim(C)`. The 2970 paragraph "What lives where: base vs fiber dimensions" is differential-geometrically essential and is correctly stated: the induced metric `G_i(c) = σ_i* g_B|_{q_i(c)}` is a tensor on `C`, with at most `dim(C)` eigenvalues, and the "vast majority of dimensions" reading refers to directions in the fiber tangent space `T_{q_i(c)} B` not sampled by `dσ_i`. This distinction is exactly the standard distinction between the rank of the pullback and the dimension of the target [Lee2013 Ch. 11, immersion vs submersion vs neither], and the manuscript states it correctly.

The placeholder-isolation argument follows on differential-geometric grounds because all of the above are *existence and structural* claims, not empirical-fit claims. The associated-bundle metric exists in a fixed gauge as a tensor calculation; the pullback exists as a tensor calculation; the causal-cone metric exists by Sylvester's law; the spectral decomposition exists by the spectral theorem for symmetric matrices. None of these depends on the specific numerical value of the scaling exponent at 2562–2575 or on the specific phase-transition signature at 2374–2516.

## Falsification conditions

The defense fails if any of the following hold under canon:

1. **(0,2)-tensor pullback type error.** If the 2705 pullback is type-erroneously identified — for instance, if `G_i^(q)` is implicitly treated as a metric on the fiber `B` rather than on the base `C`, or if the horizontal block's `κ` is treated as living in the wrong tensor slot — then the pullback is malformed. Verified by reading: the 2970 paragraph explicitly states the correct identification (a tensor on `T_c C` with at most `dim(C)` eigenvalues). Defense holds.

2. **Maurer-Cartan inhomogeneity not properly disclosed.** If the 2722–2723 disclosure misidentifies the inhomogeneous Maurer-Cartan piece (e.g., conflates `g⁻¹ dg` with `dg · g⁻¹`, or misstates the cyclicity property), the gauge-invariance hygiene is broken. Verified by reading 2722: "the connection $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1} dU_i$ acquires a Maurer-Cartan piece $A^{(i)} \to g^{-1} A^{(i)} g + g^{-1} dg$" — this is the correct local transformation [Nakahara2003 §10.3]. Defense holds.

3. **Sylvester misapplied to the causal-cone construction.** If the 2874 signature count is wrong (e.g., if `g` is actually degenerate or has the wrong number of negative eigenvalues), the causal-cone existence claim fails. Verified: with `c_I² > 0` and `h_ab` positive definite, the diagonal-block form has exactly one negative and `dim Σ` positive eigenvalues, which is correct by Sylvester [O'Neill1983 §3.3]. Defense holds.

4. **The real-part projection at 2841–2843 is not a well-defined operation on a complex (0,2)-tensor in any canonical sense.** This is the strongest potential differential-geometric attack red can mount. The honest geometric position is that `Re(g_μν)` on a complex bilinear form is *not* the result of any canonical operation in semi-Riemannian geometry — it is a choice that has no Wick analog (and the manuscript admits this explicitly at 2820 and 2841–2846). Defense: this is registered as a *postulate*, not as a derivation; the manuscript's "We flag this as a derivation gap" at 2846 is the canonical hygiene move. The construction is therefore an existence demonstration under a stated postulate, not a derivation. The claim is calibrated as Outlook.

5. **The 1+3 vs 2+2 ambiguity at 2812.** If the manuscript claimed to derive 1+3 from the construction, this would be false (and red can refute it via Sylvester — Sylvester's law fixes signature but not the partition `p + q` of an indefinite form). The manuscript explicitly admits this at 2812 ("the construction does not currently distinguish 1+3 from 2+2 or other indefinite splits on dynamical grounds"). Defense holds.
