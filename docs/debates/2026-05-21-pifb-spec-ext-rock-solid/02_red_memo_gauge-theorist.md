## Attack — eq:consensus_metric earns more status than the regulator caveat supports (sub-claim 5)

The 2928 paragraph correctly admits four obstructions: (a) constant-$g$ averaging is trivial-or-unnecessary, (b) local-$g(c)$ averaging is an infinite-dimensional functional integral, (c) the local case requires a gauge fixing or a regulator, and (d) non-compact $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ has infinite Haar measure even for constant $g$. The 2937 paragraph correctly registers the construction as "a heuristic target rather than a completed observable." So far so good. The leak is that eq:consensus_metric at 2934 is given a numbered equation label and is *named* — "the collective consensus metric" — and is then *referenced* in downstream prose ("the consensus / Haar-averaged construction" at 2723, "the candidate gauge-invariant fallback" at 2937) as if it were a defined object. A numbered, named, downstream-referenced equation is the canonical signature of a *completed* construction, not a heuristic target. By contrast, the Yang-Mills action $\int \mathrm{tr}(F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu})$ that the manuscript correctly refuses to invoke at 2723 is, in fact, a fully-defined gauge-invariant integral when the curvature is non-zero. The construction the manuscript actually offers — a local-$g(c)$ gauge-orbit average — is not in the same category as Yang-Mills; it is in the category of *Faddeev-Popov gauge-fixed functional integrals*, and the Gribov ambiguity is precisely the statement that "a gauge fixing submanifold may not intersect a gauge orbit at all or it may intersect it more than once" [Gribov 1978; Singer 1978; see standard QFT references on non-abelian gauge fixing]. In non-abelian gauge theory, *no globally well-defined gauge-fixed functional integral over the orbit space exists* without restricting to the first Gribov region, and even that restriction does not solve the problem globally [Singer 1978 "Some Remarks on the Gribov Ambiguity"]. The manuscript's "needs a gauge fixing or a regulator to be well-defined" at 2928 is structurally accurate but understates the difficulty: this is not "an engineering choice to be specified later", this is "an open problem on which 50 years of lattice gauge theory has spent considerable effort." The combination (numbered equation + named object + downstream references + this obstruction) means that the consensus metric is doing more rhetorical work in §Speculative Extensions than its admitted status supports. The fact that the manuscript also defers the same construction on $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ where the additional infinite-Haar obstruction holds [Folland1995 Ch. 2: non-compact locally compact groups have left-invariant Haar measure but it is infinite] compounds the issue.

## Attack — "Wick-like" continuation in the Lie algebra is a different operation in a different space (sub-claim 3)

The 2820 sentence reads: "structurally analogous to a Wick-like continuation performed inside the gauge frame rather than on the base coordinates, with an additional real-part projection step that has no Wick counterpart." Two structural disanalogies with Wick rotation are at issue. The first — the real-part projection — is admitted explicitly and the manuscript flags it as "no Wick counterpart." Conceded. The second — that Wick canonically continues a base coordinate $\tau \to i\tau$, whereas the construction continues a Lie-algebra component $\phi_\tau \to i\phi_\tau$ — is *not* admitted as a structural disanalogy. The manuscript labels it as "performed inside the gauge frame rather than on the base coordinates" but treats this relocation as preserving the Wick-analogy character. It does not. Standard Wick rotation [Zinn-Justin 2002 *Quantum Field Theory and Critical Phenomena* §3; Hartle-Hawking 1983 on the gravitational path-integral] continues a *real spacetime coordinate* to an imaginary value, producing a real Euclidean metric on the *base manifold* (or, in the inverse Wick rotation, recovering a Lorentzian metric from a Euclidean computation). The continued coordinate remains a coordinate; the result is a real metric throughout. The construction at 2826–2841 continues a Lie-algebra component $\phi_\tau$, which is *not* a base coordinate — it is a parameter living in a different vector space ($\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$ in this case). The result is then *not* a real metric on the base manifold; it is a complex-valued bilinear form (eq 2837–2839) whose real part is then projected out. The Wick analogy survives only at the surface level "continue something to an imaginary value"; the structural content (which space, what becomes complex, what remains real) is genuinely different. Calling this "structurally analogous to a Wick-like continuation" — even with the "in the gauge frame rather than the base coordinates" qualifier — overstates the analogy. The accurate phrasing is closer to "a hand-imposed imaginary component of the Lie-algebra-valued gauge field, by separate analogy with Wick rotation's continuation of base coordinates," which is *no analogy at all* in the formal sense and is rather a separate independent choice. The "additional real-part projection step that has no Wick counterpart" at 2846 retracts one of the two structural disanalogies; the other (which space gets continued) remains uncovered.

## Newly-discovered canon

- [Gribov 1978 "Quantization of non-abelian gauge theories", Nucl. Phys. B 139, 1; Singer 1978 "Some Remarks on the Gribov Ambiguity", Comm. Math. Phys. 60, 7; see Wikipedia "Gribov ambiguity" for the modern statement]: in non-abelian gauge theory, no globally well-defined gauge-fixing condition exists; "a gauge fixing submanifold may not intersect a gauge orbit at all or it may intersect it more than once." The Faddeev-Popov procedure is locally well-defined; the global obstruction to defining gauge-fixed functional integrals over the orbit space is a 50-year-old open problem. Operative point for sub-claim 5: the manuscript's "needs a gauge fixing or a regulator" at 2928 understates the difficulty.

- [Folland1995 *A Course in Abstract Harmonic Analysis* Ch. 2]: a non-compact locally compact group has a left-invariant Haar measure but it is infinite. For $\mathrm{SO}(1,3)$ specifically, the Haar measure is infinite — manuscript 2928 cites this correctly. Operative point: the constant-$g$ obstruction *compounds* the local-$g(c)$ functional-integral obstruction; both are present in the construction the manuscript names "consensus metric."

- [Zinn-Justin 2002 *Quantum Field Theory and Critical Phenomena* §3; Hartle-Hawking 1983 *Phys. Rev. D* 28, 2960]: Wick rotation canonically continues a *base spacetime coordinate* and produces a *real* metric throughout. The construction at 2820–2846 continues a *Lie-algebra component* and produces a complex-valued bilinear form whose real part is projected. These are different operations in different spaces.

- [Nakahara2003 §10.3, §11.1; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III.2]: the Maurer-Cartan piece $g^{-1} dg$ is the standard inhomogeneous term in the local gauge transformation of a connection. The manuscript identifies this correctly at 2722; the question is downstream prose.
