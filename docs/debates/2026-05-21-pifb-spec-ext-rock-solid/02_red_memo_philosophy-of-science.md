## Attack — falsifiability deferred is not falsifiability delivered (sub-claim 7)

The 3084 sentence reads: "Failure of the derivation under a fully developed information-geometric construction --- one in which the relevant statistical manifolds, comparison structure, and Cencov scale fixings are all specified --- would constitute substantive evidence against the phenomenological interpretation." Per Popper [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Karl Popper" §3]: "if a theory is incompatible with possible empirical observations it is scientific; conversely, a theory which is compatible with all such observations [...] is unscientific." The Popperian criterion requires the theory be *logically structured* to permit refutation by *possible* observations. The 3084 criterion does not permit refutation by any presently-available observation — it permits refutation only after the "fully developed information-geometric construction" is built, and the building of that construction is itself the open program. This is the structure of a Lakatosian *promise* of a progressive shift, not a progressive shift itself. Lakatos [SEP, "Imre Lakatos" §2.2] requires that a programme be *theoretically progressive* — "each successive theory must exhibit excess empirical substance by forecasting previously unknown phenomena" — *and* *empirically progressive*: "some of those novel predictions must be validated through observation and testing." The 3084 criterion forecasts no presently-checkable novel phenomenon; it defers both the construction and the test. Under Lakatos, this is the diagnostic signature of a programme in degenerating territory — accommodation-via-future-construction rather than presently-corroborated novel prediction. The 3060 "may be unfalsifiable" admission earlier in the same subsection is the manuscript's own acknowledgement; the 3084 criterion does not remediate it, it restates the same gap in stronger procedural form.

## Newly-discovered canon

- Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* (1959), as paraphrased in [SEP "Karl Popper" §3, "The Problem of Demarcation"]: "if a theory is incompatible with possible empirical observations it is scientific; conversely, a theory which is compatible with all such observations [...] is unscientific." Operative point for sub-claim 7: falsifiability requires the theory be *logically structured* to permit refutation by *possible* observations, not by hypothetical observations conditional on future theory development.

- Lakatos, *Methodology of Scientific Research Programmes* (1978) FMSRP:33–34, as paraphrased in [SEP "Imre Lakatos" §2.2]: "a research programme is progressive if it is both theoretically and empirically progressive, and degenerating if it is not." Operative point: a programme that "fails to generate new predictions or produces novel forecasts that are systematically falsified" is degenerative; deferring the falsifiable construction is the diagnostic signature.

- SEP "Structural Realism" §§3, 3.2 [Worrall1989, Ladyman1998] on the in-principle non-agnostic character of the ESR/OSR distinction: "Ladyman argues that in general epistemological forms of structural realism do not significantly improve the prospects of standard scientific realism and that structural realism should be thought of as metaphysically rather than merely epistemically revisionary." This is admissible against the 3092 "remaining agnostic among these variants" claim — but for the current round the ESR/OSR distinction is correctly registered, so this is held in reserve.
