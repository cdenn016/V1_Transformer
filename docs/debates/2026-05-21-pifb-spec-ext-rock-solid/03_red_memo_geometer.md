## Core attack

Blue's Falsification Condition 4 reads (verbatim from `02_blue_opening.md`):

> The 2722–2723 gauge-invariance disclosure does not actually subordinate the elsewhere-prose at 2738 ("gives 'it from bit' a precise sense") or at 3056 ("information ... is the dimensionally fundamental quantity"). The 2937 paragraph explicitly re-anchors the consensus-metric elsewhere-prose. If red can produce an elsewhere-prose lift that is *not* subordinated to a within-section or within-cross-reference hedge, the calibration mesh has a gap. Verified by reading: the 2738 lift is followed within the same subsection (2740 onward) by the three-tiers framing that explicitly registers agent-dependence; the 3056 lift is registered within the same subsection (3060) as "may be unfalsifiable" and within the cross-referenced 3094–3102 as "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result." Both lifts are subordinated.

Blue's claim is "both lifts are subordinated." This is half-correct and half-wrong. The 2738 lift is partially subordinated by the 2749 within-subsection clause "there is no agent-independent metric on $\mathcal{C}$, only the family of agent-dependent induced metrics" — verified from `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2749`. The 3056 lift is *not* subordinated by 3060 in the sense Falsification Condition 4 requires.

Verbatim from `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3056`:

> A central philosophical commitment of this framework is that information (specifically, the variational free energy and KL divergences measured in nats) is the dimensionally fundamental quantity. Physical dimensions (kilograms, meters, seconds) are not ontologically primitive categories requiring conversion *from* information-theoretic quantities, but rather emergent phenomenological labels that agents collectively construct through consensus formation (Section~\ref{sec:dimensional_structure}).

Verbatim from `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3060`:

> This stance faces limitations. We have not established dimensional conversion between bits and kilograms, derived numerical values of physical constants, or specified how to measure "beliefs" of physical systems. [...] This interpretation may be unfalsifiable as any measurement can be reinterpreted as labeling information geometry, making the framework compatible with any result by construction.

The 3060 sentence does not subordinate the *ontological commitment* in 3056. The 3060 sentence subordinates the *predictive content* and the *empirical reach* of the commitment. These are two distinct epistemic objects. An honest read of 3060 is: "the commitment is held, but no predictions follow from it yet, and it may not generate predictions in principle." That is *concession of unfalsifiability*, not subordination of the commitment to a within-section hedge. The commitment remains stated at PIFB:3056 as a positive, present-tense claim about ontology ("Physical dimensions [...] are not ontologically primitive categories [...] but rather emergent phenomenological labels"). The "may be unfalsifiable" phrasing concedes that this stated commitment may sit outside scientific demarcation; the commitment itself is not retracted, not qualified within the same paragraph, and not relativized to a regulator or to a within-framework reading.

Blue's defense also invokes the 3094–3102 cross-reference to "Philosophical Status and Research Program" as a cross-section subordination. The 3094 subsubsection sits two subsubsections downstream of 3053. Falsification Condition 4 requires subordination "within a within-section or within-cross-reference hedge." The 3094 paragraph reads (verbatim from `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3096`):

> The phenomenological interpretation (that physical quantities are cognitive labels for information geometry) is a metaphysical postulate, not a derived result.

This subordinates the *derivation status* (postulate, not theorem). It does not subordinate the *truth status* of the commitment, nor does it relativize the commitment to a fixed gauge or to a regulator the way the 2722–2937 differential-geometric content is relativized. The 3056 commitment is therefore stated as a substantive ontological position with the manuscript declining to retract it, declining to relativize it to a within-framework reading, and declining to attach a same-paragraph regulator-conditional flag.

Compare PIFB:2737–2738 (the "it from bit" lift):

> The bundle-pullback formulation gives "it from bit" a precise sense: a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback of a fiber-respecting metric on an information-bearing bundle along an agent's section, with the horizontal piece encoding how the agent's gauge frame varies across $\mathcal{C}$ and the vertical piece encoding how the relevant statistical field varies. The pulled-back tensors are agent-dependent because both the section and the connection $A^{(i)}$ are.

The 2738 lift is followed in the very next sentence ("The pulled-back tensors are agent-dependent because both the section and the connection $A^{(i)}$ are") by an explicit relativization to agent-dependent quantities — *same-paragraph hedge attached*. The 3056 commitment has no analogous same-paragraph relativization. The "agents collectively construct through consensus formation (Section~\ref{sec:dimensional_structure})" cross-reference is precisely the consensus construction that PIFB:2928–2937 admits is regulator-conditional and not delivered in the present manuscript. The 3056 lift therefore rests on a structure that PIFB itself flags as not-yet-constructed; the lift's "agents collectively construct" rescue does not deliver subordinating content.

The calibration mesh has a gap at 3056. Falsification Condition 4 trips.

## Defense

The reading blue's defense requires (3056 subordinated by 3060 and 3094–3102) reads the manuscript more generously than the canon-cop discipline rule permits. The discipline rule from `debate_methodology.md` is that the user's manuscript is the claim under evaluation, not the canon; defense vectors that read structural concessions *into* the manuscript that the manuscript does not contain are post-hoc rationalization. Blue's 3056 defense does this: the "may be unfalsifiable" at 3060 is not within-paragraph, and the "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" at 3094–3102 is two subsubsections downstream and addresses derivation status, not the commitment's substantive content.

The structural fact is that PIFB:3056 deploys a present-tense, positive-mood ontological commitment ("is the dimensionally fundamental quantity") without a same-paragraph hedge of the form deployed at 2737–2738 (agent-dependence in the next sentence) or at 2687 (within-gauge-fixing in the equation introduction). The commitment is the manuscript's strongest "it from bit" assertion at the ontological register; it sits outside the differential-geometric scaffolding's clean within-gauge framings.

Sub-claim 7 therefore fails not because the differential-geometric primitives are misstated (they are not), but because the ontological commitment at 3056 is not subordinated to a within-paragraph or same-subsection hedge. The PIFB section delivers excellent local hygiene on the math; it slips at the ontological lift. The "rock-solid" framing therefore does not hold at the calibration-mesh level on the present text.
