The differential-geometric content of §Speculative Extensions is mostly correct: the associated-bundle / horizontal-vertical decomposition machinery at 2682–2701 is standard, the section's pullback expression at 2717 is a correct Fisher-Rao score-function computation, and the base-vs-fiber dimension clarification at 2970 is geometrically precise. The remaining differential-geometric leak is the prose at 2738: "The bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense." This sentence elevates a *within-gauge* construction — exactly as the 2722–2723 disclosure flags it — to an ontological claim about how a metric on the noumenal substrate is constructed. Sub-claim 2 of the rock-solid claim — "the bundle-metric construction at 2678–2738 is calibrated" — is partially weakened by the 2738 prose seam.

## Attack — "precise sense" is a within-gauge precision; the prose at 2738 does not retract this (sub-claim 2)

The 2738 sentence reads: "The bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense: a metric on the noumenal substrate is the pullback of a fiber-respecting metric on an information-bearing bundle along an agent's section, with the horizontal piece encoding how the agent's gauge frame varies across $\mathcal{C}$ and the vertical piece encoding how the relevant statistical field varies." On the differential-geometric content: the construction does specify a bilinear form on the total space $E_q$ (the direct sum at 2687–2689, agent-frame-dependent horizontal block plus Fisher vertical block) and does pull it back along the section. As a *mathematical* operation, this is well-defined. The leak is in the rhetorical lift: "a metric on the noumenal substrate" is what one obtains, and the sentence does not in the same breath repeat the qualifier "within agent $i$'s gauge fixing $U_i$." That qualifier *is* present in the subsection heading at 2678 ("in a Fixed Gauge"), in the equation introduction at 2687 ("within an agent's gauge fixing $U_i$"), and at the gauge-invariance disclosure at 2722–2723. The 2738 sentence stands at the *end* of the subsection as a summary statement, and the summary drops the qualifier. A geometrically precise summary would read: "The bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense *within an agent's gauge fixing*: a metric on the noumenal substrate *as represented in that gauge fixing* is the pullback [...]". The current 2738 sentence allows the reader to carry forward a metric-on-the-noumenal-substrate sense that the 2722–2723 disclosure has just retracted, then transitions into the three-tier interpretation at 2740–2753 which is *built* on the within-gauge metrics. The interpretation survives the gauge-fixed reading; the 2738 summary as written carries weight the 2722–2723 disclosure has already removed.

## Attack — the eigenvalue-hierarchy decomposition at 3000–3014 mixes base and fiber notions (sub-claim 6, narrow)

The 2970 paragraph correctly clarifies that the induced metric $G_i(c)$ has at most $\dim(\mathcal{C})$ eigenvalues, and that the "vast majority of dimensions" of the fiber refers to directions in $T_{q_i(c)}\mathcal{B}$ not in the image of $d\sigma_i$. The 3024 closing paragraph then reads: "In this view the vast majority of the statistical manifold's dimensions remain invisible. They exist mathematically but carry zero information flux. Only a tiny sliver (e.g. 4 dimensions out of K) becomes phenomenal spacetime. The remainder is internal structure that never manifests spatially or perceptually. In this view our perceptual experiences comprise only a tiny sub-manifold of the whole." The "4 out of K" phrasing refers to base-manifold eigenvalues — the (3+1) hypothesis is about $|\mathcal{D}_{\text{obs}}| = 4$ from the eigenvalues of $G_i(c)$ as an $n \times n$ tensor on $T_c \mathcal{C}$. The "K" in "4 out of K" reads as the fiber dimension $K$ from the surrounding subsection (where $K = 768$ produces a fiber dimension $\approx 3 \times 10^5$). These are different dimensions in different spaces — the base $\mathcal{C}$ versus the fiber $\mathcal{B}$ — and the 3024 prose conflates them. The 2970 clarification is in place but the 3024 summary undoes it by writing the dimensional split as "4 out of K" when K is the fiber dimension. This is a small slip in a section the manuscript has otherwise carefully kept apart, and the 3018 "Speculative" subsubsection title covers most of the load, but the 3024 sentence is an editorial leak in sub-claim 6.

## Conceded ground

The associated-bundle horizontal-vertical decomposition at 2682–2685 is correctly stated as the standard split into horizontal subspace $H_{(c,q)}$ (determined by the connection) and vertical subspace $V_{(c,q)} \cong T_q \mathcal{B}$ [Nakahara2003 Ch. 10.3, KobayashiNomizu Vol. I Ch. III]. The connection one-form $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1} dU_i$ at 2686 is the standard pullback of the Maurer-Cartan form along the gauge section [Nakahara §10.3]. The piecewise $\kappa$ convention at 2697–2700 ($-\mathrm{tr}$ for compact real forms, $+\mathrm{tr}$ for non-compact) is canonically correct: the standard positive-definite inner product on a compact form $\mathfrak{so}(N)$ is $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$, while on non-compact forms with $\mathrm{tr}(T^2) > 0$ the positive-trace convention is required for an indefinite signature. The 2705 expression $G_i^{(q)} := (\sigma_i^{(q)})^* g_{E_q}$ is the standard pullback of a tensor by a smooth section [Lee2013 Ch. 11]. The 2717 boxed expression is the correct evaluation of this pullback in the score-function form. The Lorentz-group $\mathrm{O}(1,1)$ local frame statement at 2848–2854 is correct: $\Lambda^\top \eta \Lambda = \eta$ for $\eta = \mathrm{diag}(-1, +1)$. The qualification at 2848 that "Whether the full metric (with $c$-dependent coefficients) admits $\mathrm{SO}^+(1,1)$ as a global isometry group depends on the spatial dependence" is honest.

## Newly-discovered canon

- [Nakahara2003 §10.3]: the connection one-form on a principal bundle pulled back to a base section gives $A^{(i)} = U_i^{-1} dU_i$ in the local gauge $U_i$; the Maurer-Cartan piece appears in the transformation $A \to g^{-1} A g + g^{-1} dg$ under local gauge change. Manuscript 2722 cites this correctly.

- [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III.2]: the standard horizontal-vertical decomposition of the tangent bundle on a principal bundle, with horizontal subspaces specified by the connection. Manuscript 2682–2685 cites this correctly via the associated-bundle construction.

- [external_canon_math.md §2; Lee2013 Ch. 11]: pullback of a (0,2)-tensor by a smooth section is a well-defined tensor on the base manifold. The construction at 2705 is geometrically correct as far as it goes; the rhetorical lift at 2738 is the issue, not the math.
