## Concession (one red argument that holds under canon)

Red's narrow reading of Popper's demarcation criterion at PIFB:3084 has canonical support that I cannot displace. [Popper 1959 *The Logic of Scientific Discovery* §§4, 6; SEP "Karl Popper" §3] is unambiguous that a theory must be *logically structured* to permit refutation by *possible observations* — observations made under the present logical structure of the theory, not under a future construction. The 3084 sentence "Failure of the derivation under a fully developed information-geometric construction --- one in which the relevant statistical manifolds, comparison structure, and Cencov scale fixings are all specified --- would constitute substantive evidence against the phenomenological interpretation" is structured as a falsifiability *promise* conditional on a future construction. Red is correct that this is a Lakatosian "promise of a progressive shift" rather than a present progressive shift [SEP "Imre Lakatos" §2.2]. The defense cannot be that 3084 alone delivers present-tense falsifiability. The narrower defense is that the section as a whole canonically registers the deferred status — see Defense below.

## Core attack (red's load-bearing weakness under canon)

Red's Vector 2 *applies the wrong level of the Popperian criterion to the wrong object.* The demarcation criterion in [Popper 1959 §§4, 6] adjudicates *theories,* not individual *sentences* or *sub-programmes within an Outlook section.* The relevant Popperian test is whether the *theory the manuscript proposes* — variational free-energy minimization on a gauge-equivariant statistical manifold with multi-agent coupling — is structured to permit refutation by presently-available observations. The §Results section claims it is (multi-seed scaling fit, threshold-detector dynamics, language-model performance); whether those specific claims hold is the topic of *other* debates, not this one. The 3084 dimensionless-constants research program is registered explicitly at 3094–3102 as "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" and as "framework may ultimately function as philosophical interpretation rather than scientific theory." This is the canonical Popperian *self-classification* move: the manuscript places the 3084 programme outside the immediately-testable empirical envelope and inside the heuristic-content envelope explicitly. [Popper 1963 *Conjectures and Refutations* Ch. 1 §I] distinguishes "heuristic content" (need not be presently testable) from "empirical content" (must be); a manuscript that honestly draws the boundary between the two is in canonical Popperian compliance even when the heuristic content is large. The 2582 Outlook bracket and the 3127–3145 boxed enumeration *are* that boundary-drawing.

Red's Lakatos move is also misapplied. [Lakatos 1978 *FMSRP* pp. 47–49; SEP "Imre Lakatos" §2.2] explicitly defines the *hard core / protective belt* distinction precisely to accommodate research programmes with hard-core commitments that are *methodologically untestable.* Lakatos: "the hard core ... is irrefutable by the methodological decision of its protagonists." Empirical testing in a Lakatosian programme runs through the *protective belt* of auxiliary hypotheses, not through the hard core. The PIFB:3141 four-postulate enumeration with closing "None of (i)–(iv) is derived from variational free-energy minimization in the present formulation" *is the canonical protective-belt identification.* Red treats Lakatos as a stick to beat the manuscript with; canonically, Lakatos is the *authorization* of exactly the move the manuscript is making.

The Friedman [Friedman 2001 *Dynamics of Reason* Ch. III §3] *relativized constitutive a priori* lens provides the third independent direction: a physical theory comes equipped with a stratum of conceptual postulates that are not themselves empirically tested but that *constitute* the framework within which empirical tests acquire meaning. The PIFB:3056 commitment ("information ... is the dimensionally fundamental quantity") reads as a Friedman-style framework-constitutive choice; the 3094–3102 "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" admission is the canonical philosophical-status registration this move requires. Red's argument would condemn most of theoretical physics — string theory's hard core, the AdS/CFT conjecture as a research program, conformal-field-theory bootstrap as a programmatic commitment — by the same logic; canonically, philosophy of science accommodates such programmes via Lakatos and Friedman.

## Defense (citation that strengthens blue's position)

The strongest defense of the section's calibration of the 3084 research program is the layered hedging structure:

- PIFB:2582 brackets the entire section as Outlook;
- PIFB:3060 admits "may be unfalsifiable";
- PIFB:3094–3102 registers the phenomenological interpretation as "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" and acknowledges "framework may ultimately function as philosophical interpretation rather than scientific theory";
- PIFB:3127–3145 enumerates the four load-bearing postulates and explicitly disclaims their derivation;
- PIFB:3084 itself draws the symmetric success-failure criterion in a register that names "fully developed information-geometric construction" — i.e., explicitly acknowledges the construction is not yet fully developed.

This is the canonical Popperian-with-Lakatosian-protective-belt drawing of the boundary [Popper 1963 *Conjectures and Refutations* Ch. 1; Lakatos 1978 *FMSRP* pp. 47–49]. Honest drawing of the heuristic/empirical boundary is canonical practice, not a calibration failure.

The Friedman [Friedman 2001 Ch. III §3] *relativized constitutive a priori* register additionally licenses the 3056 dimensional-primacy commitment as framework-constitutive rather than framework-empirical — and the 3094–3102 admissions explicitly route the commitment through that register.

The narrow vulnerability red identifies is *prose wording* at 3084, not *programme structure.* "Cencov scale fixings" mis-attributes a canonical info-geometric primitive (see `03_blue_memo_info-geometer.md`); rewording 3084 to say "a specified normalization convention" or "a normalization chosen on Cencov-uniqueness-up-to-scalar grounds" preserves the research-programme logic while curing the prose. This is an editorial fix at one line, not a structural collapse of sub-claim 7.

## Newly-discovered canon

- **Popper 1963, *Conjectures and Refutations*, Ch. 1 §I.** The heuristic/empirical content distinction. Popper: "Bold conjectures with high empirical content [...] are the engine of science." A manuscript that draws the boundary between heuristic content (not presently testable) and empirical content (presently testable) is in canonical compliance with the demarcation rule, even when the heuristic content is large. Operative point: red applies the demarcation rule to the heuristic content (3084) rather than the empirical content (Results section); this is a misapplication of the canon.

- **Lakatos 1978, *FMSRP*, pp. 47–49 (hard core / protective belt).** Hard core "irrefutable by the methodological decision of its protagonists." Empirical tests run through the protective belt of auxiliary hypotheses. The PIFB:3141 four-postulate enumeration is the canonical Lakatosian protective-belt identification.

- **Friedman 2001, *Dynamics of Reason*, Ch. III §3 (relativized constitutive a priori).** Physical theories come equipped with constitutive postulates that constitute the framework within which empirical tests acquire meaning. The PIFB:3056 information-as-primal commitment reads as a Friedman-style constitutive choice, which canon admits as legitimate provided the manuscript registers it as such (which 3094–3102 does).

- **SEP "Karl Popper" §3 ("The Problem of Demarcation").** A theory must be logically structured to permit refutation. Red's quoted Popperian principle is correct; what red elides is that the rule applies to theories, not to sub-sentences within a theory's Outlook chapter.
