## Position

The Fisher-Rao pullback machinery at 2664–2738 is correctly stated as far as it goes — the boxed equation 2717 $G^{(q)}_{i,\mu\nu}(c) = \kappa(A^{(i)}_\mu, A^{(i)}_\nu) + \mathbb{E}_{q_i(c)}[(\nabla^{(i)}_\mu \log q_i)(\nabla^{(i)}_\nu \log q_i)]$ correctly identifies the vertical Fisher contribution. But two distinct information-geometric leaks remain. First, the 2680 promise that the construction "earns the name 'pullback of a fiber-bundle metric', not merely an $L^2$ pullback of densities" is overstated: the bundle metric of eq:bundle_metric is a *direct sum* of two independently constructed bilinear forms (a Lie-algebra trace form and the Fisher metric), not a metric on a total space whose pullback is then derived. Second, the 3084 dimensionless-constants research program invokes "Cencov scale fixings" as a load-bearing ingredient of the future construction, but Cencov uniqueness on a statistical manifold does not provide a scale — it provides a unique-up-to-scalar metric. The "scale fixing" the manuscript will need is not what Cencov delivers, and the absence of any present construction of such a scale fixing is what makes 3084 a deferred-falsifiability claim. Sub-claims 2 (the "precise sense" of "it from bit" at 2738) and 7 (the dimensionless-constants research program) inherit these gaps.

## Attack — what Cencov actually gives, and what 3084 needs

[Cencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000 §2.3] establishes that the Fisher information metric is the unique Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold invariant under sufficient statistics, up to a positive scalar multiple. Cencov uniqueness fixes the metric's *form* (the bilinear form on score functions), not its *normalization*. To derive a *dimensionless* ratio like $\alpha \approx 1/137$ from "ratios of coupling strengths or Fisher information scales" (3082), one would need a canonical scale to take the ratio against. Cencov does not provide that scale; it provides invariance of the metric *direction* in the space of bilinear forms on the tangent bundle. The 3084 claim that "Cencov scale fixings" are part of the load-bearing future construction is therefore a category error in the present formulation: Cencov is a scale-removing theorem, not a scale-fixing one. Operationalizing "scale fixings" in a way that derives $1/137$ would require an additional structure — a preferred parameterization, a normalization condition tied to a physically meaningful invariant, or a measure of "information" with a canonical unit. None of these is exhibited. The 3084 sentence therefore promises a derivation conditional on a construction whose load-bearing primitive (the scale) is not delivered by the named canonical theorem (Cencov). This sharpens the philosophy-of-science attack on sub-claim 7: 3084 is not just deferred-falsifiable in the Popperian sense, it is deferred to a construction whose stated primitive does not in fact deliver the needed structure.

## Attack — "bundle pullback" vs $L^2$ pullback (sub-claim 2)

The 2680 paragraph distinguishes "the $L^2$ pullback of score functions along the parameterized family $c \mapsto q_i(c)$" from "the pullback of a fiber-bundle metric" and asserts the construction is the latter. Examining the bundle metric of eq:bundle_metric: it is defined as $g_{E_q}(X_H + X_V, Y_H + Y_V) := g_{\mathcal{C}}^{\mathrm{tw}}(\pi_* X_H, \pi_* Y_H) + g_{\mathcal{B}}(X_V, Y_V)$. The vertical block $g_{\mathcal{B}}$ is the Fisher-Rao metric on the fiber — a legitimate Riemannian metric on the statistical fiber. The horizontal block $g_{\mathcal{C}}^{\mathrm{tw}}(c) := \kappa(A^{(i)}_\mu(c), A^{(i)}_\nu(c))$ is built from the Lie-algebra trace form $\kappa$ evaluated on the connection one-form. The horizontal block is *not* a metric pulled back from any pre-specified geometry on $\mathcal{C}$; it is a *connection-dependent* bilinear form, agent-frame-dependent as the manuscript itself flags at 2722–2723. The "bundle metric" of eq:bundle_metric is therefore constructed by *stipulating* the horizontal block via $\kappa(A, A)$ and *stipulating* that horizontal-vertical decomposition is orthogonal. Both stipulations are choices in the construction, not consequences of a pre-existing metric on the total space $E_q$. The pullback by $\sigma_i^*$ of this stipulated total-space tensor is then a tensor on $\mathcal{C}$. Read accurately, the construction is: define a fiber-respecting bilinear form on the total space by direct sum of (a) a connection-form bilinear on the horizontal block and (b) the Fisher metric on the vertical block; pull this direct sum back along the section. This is *one* legitimate construction of a tensor on $\mathcal{C}$, but it does not earn the rhetorical distinction at 2680. The "merely $L^2$ pullback" the manuscript dismisses has exactly the same Fisher-vertical content; the only difference is the additional $\kappa(A, A)$ horizontal piece, which is not a pullback but a direct-sum addition. The 2738 prose-seam claim that "the bundle-pullback formulation gives 'it from bit' a precise sense" overstates the construction: it gives one stipulated definition of a base-manifold tensor a precise sense, with the horizontal block carrying agent-dependent gauge-fixed content. This is exactly what the 2722–2723 disclosure says, but the 2738 sentence carries it forward without retraction.

The scale is not fixed by Cencov; any "Cencov scale fixing" referenced at 3084 requires additional canonical structure beyond Cencov uniqueness.
