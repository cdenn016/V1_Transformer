This is a prose fix at one sentence, not a structural collapse of the dimensionless-constants research programme.

Three concessions, three editorial-line fixes. None require structural overhaul of the section. ## Core attack on red's position

Red's three vectors carry editorial weight at three specific lines. **Vector 2 (sub-claim 7) — red applies Popper and Lakatos to the wrong object.** Red's claim is that PIFB:3084 "defers falsifiability to a future construction" and is therefore a "Lakatosian *promise* of a progressive shift rather than a progressive shift itself" [Lakatos 1978 FMSRP:33–34]. The philosophy-of-science memo (`03_blue_memo_philosophy-of-science.md`) develops the canonical correction: [Popper 1959 *Logic of Scientific Discovery* §§4, 6; Popper 1963 *Conjectures and Refutations* Ch. 1 §I] adjudicates *theories*, not individual *sentences* within an Outlook chapter. Popper explicitly distinguishes "heuristic content" (need not be presently testable) from "empirical content" (must be testable); a manuscript that honestly draws the boundary between them is in canonical Popperian compliance even when heuristic content is large. The PIFB:2582 Outlook bracket and the PIFB:3127–3145 boxed enumeration *are* that boundary-drawing.

[Lakatos 1978 *FMSRP* pp. 47–49] is even more directly inverted by red. Lakatos defines the *hard core / protective belt* distinction precisely to accommodate research programmes with hard-core commitments that are *methodologically untestable*: "the hard core ... is irrefutable by the methodological decision of its protagonists." Empirical testing runs through the protective belt of auxiliary hypotheses. The PIFB:3141 four-postulate enumeration with closing "None of (i)–(iv) is derived from variational free-energy minimization in the present formulation" *is the canonical Lakatosian protective-belt identification.* Red treats Lakatos as a stick to beat the manuscript with; canonically, Lakatos is the *authorization* of exactly the move PIFB:3127–3145 makes. The same logic that would condemn PIFB:3084 would condemn the hard cores of string theory, the AdS/CFT conjecture as a research programme, and the conformal-bootstrap programme — none of which generates presently-checkable novel predictions in the way Lakatos demanded for the empirical content of the protective belt, all of which are canonically authorized by the hard-core / protective-belt distinction.

[Friedman 2001 *Dynamics of Reason* Ch. III §3] adds the third canonical direction: relativized constitutive a priori. Physical theories come equipped with constitutive postulates not themselves empirically tested but that constitute the framework within which empirical tests acquire meaning. The PIFB:3056 information-as-primal commitment reads as a Friedman-style constitutive choice, and the PIFB:3094–3102 "metaphysical postulate, not a derived result" plus "framework may ultimately function as philosophical interpretation rather than scientific theory" is the canonical philosophical-status registration this register requires.
