## Core attack

Blue's structural defense is correct but does not discharge the load-bearing prose-level defects. The verb-mood argument (every load-bearing verb at 3219 is conditional) is true sentence-by-sentence but fails at the paragraph level once the noun phrase "information-theoretic necessity rather than cultural convention" enters the sentence. The conditional verb governs the **emergence** of grammar, not the **categorical dichotomy** between necessity and convention. Replacing "would then be **X** rather than **Y**" with a hedge on the verb does not hedge the dichotomy; the dichotomy itself is the unsupported assertion. [Bickerton1984] and [McWhorter2001] establish that creole grammars form contingently under specific sociolinguistic conditions; [Kirby2017] establishes cultural transmission as a primary shaping force. The hedged-verb version still asserts that grammar would either be information-theoretic necessity OR cultural convention, exclusively. That false dichotomy is what overshoots the canon.

Three specific edits discharge the remaining defects:

**(1) 3219 BERTology patch.** The current text reads "thresholding this coupling matrix $A_{ij} = \mathbb{I}[\beta_{ij} > \theta]$ may yield dependency graphs that closely match syntactic parse trees" as a prospective hypothesis. [Clark2019 ACL BlackboxNLP] showed specific attention heads track specific syntactic dependencies; [Hewitt2019 NAACL] showed parse-tree distance is linearly recoverable from BERT embeddings; [Voita2019 ACL] showed head specialization for syntactic functions; [Rogers2020 TACL] surveys the partial-not-universal picture. The minimal patch is to add a clause acknowledging the empirical state: "Empirical attention analyses have shown that specific heads track syntactic relations \cite{clark2019does, Voita2019} and that BERT embeddings linearly encode parse-tree distance \cite{Hewitt2019}, though no universal one-threshold-yields-parse-trees relation has been established \cite{Rogers2020}." This converts the 3219 hypothesis from "prospective" to "partially empirically supported, with a sharper open question about thresholding."

**(2) 3219 lexical tightening on the necessity/convention dichotomy.** Replace "Grammar would then be an information-theoretic necessity rather than cultural convention" with a degrees-of-constraint formulation such as "Grammar would then be shaped by information-theoretic pressures alongside cultural transmission, with the geometry constraining the space of stable conventions." This preserves the geometric reading compatible with [Gibson2019 *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*] and [Piantadosi2011 *PNAS*] without contradicting the sociolinguistic canon.

**(3) 3223 superlative tightening.** Replace "represents the strongest testable prediction of our framework" with "represents the most readily testable conditional prediction within the framework's speculative-claims set, once the Regime II extension of Section \ref{sec:discrete_regime_ii} is implemented and a connection field $\delta_{ij}$ is estimable from natural-language corpora." This makes the superlative comparative-within-the-framework (blue's reading) explicit in the prose rather than dependent on a reviewer parsing "Unlike claims about emergent spacetime or consciousness" as the qualifier. It also names the implementation-gap dependency the 3211 preamble already binds the conjecture to.

A fourth pressed edit on 3221 is smaller but worth naming. The four-domain sentences are in indicative mood ("Scientific communities establish terminology...", "Neural systems...may wire themselves..."), while the closing self-tag is conditional ("This principle might apply ... or it may only be a pretty way to describe something more ephemeral"). The hedge is deferred to the end of the paragraph, allowing the four domain-specific sentences to read as assertions about social systems, neural systems, market economies, and scientific communities. Shifting those four sentences into the subjunctive mood ("Scientific communities might be found to establish...", "Neural systems might wire themselves...") places the hedge in-line with the assertions it qualifies, rather than rescuing them after the fact. This is the minimal edit, not a restructuring.

## Defense

The original red opening identified five locations where the prose calibration trails the structural calibration. Blue's defense, properly read, concedes three of these (BERTology citation, necessity-vs-convention lexical defect, superlative overstatement) and pushes back only on the structural reading. I accept the structural reading; the concession is on what the bracket discharges versus what remains to be edited.

The 3217 creolization claim ("we might observe rapid curvature reduction as the language acquires consistent structure") remains under-defended. Blue invokes [Bickerton1984] and [McWhorter2001] to support a curvature-reduction reading "as one hypothesis," and notes the two conditional verbs ("may provide an example," "we might observe"). The verb hedge is correct. What remains unanswered is operationalization: the Wilson observable at 3211 is "an index-space invariant of the connection tensor rather than a property of the data flow" for autoregressive attention, which the manuscript itself flags. No connection field $\delta_{ij}$ has been estimated from a creole/pidgin corpus, and the framework does not currently specify how one would be. The minimal repair is to add a clause acknowledging that the creolization test "requires implementing the Regime II edge-relaxed cocycle of Section \ref{sec:discrete_regime_ii} and estimating $\delta_{ij}$ from longitudinal corpus data — a research program rather than an immediate experiment." Without this, the 3217 sentence reads as a near-term prediction; with it, it reads as a long-horizon target.

The 3221 generalization paragraph carries the strongest self-tag in the subsection ("it may only be a pretty way to describe something more ephemeral"). Blue is right that this is the appropriate epistemic register. The defense reduces to the in-line mood shift named above: indicative-to-subjunctive on the four domain sentences, leaving the closing tag intact. This is editorial, not structural.

On the falsifiability statement at 3223, blue cites [Popper1959 §6] for the formal definition of "potentially falsifiable" and notes that the closing clause names observables (field strength tensors, creole-vs-pidgin curvature differences, scientific terminology curvature reduction) and a refutation condition (failure to correlate with linguistic efficiency, communicative success, or evolutionary fitness). I grant the Popperian formal compliance. The pressed edit on the superlative is not about falsifiability per se; it is about the absolute-vs-comparative reading of "strongest." With the tightening above, both readings agree: this is the most testable prediction the framework makes, conditional on Regime II being implemented.

The remaining attack surface is the **gap between the verb-level hedging that blue documents (correctly) and the noun-phrase-level assertions that the verb hedges do not reach**. "Necessity rather than convention" is a noun-phrase dichotomy; "would then be" does not soften the dichotomy itself, only the truth-value of the disjunction. Subjunctive verbs over indicative noun-phrases is a common over-claiming pattern in speculative prose; the four pressed edits above close it at every load-bearing site.

Net verdict the evidence supports: REMAND for four specific edits (BERTology citation patch at 3219; necessity-vs-convention lexical tightening at 3219; superlative tightening at 3223; creolization operationalization clause at 3217; optional mood shift at 3221). The structural calibration survives; the prose calibration needs four sentences rewritten.
