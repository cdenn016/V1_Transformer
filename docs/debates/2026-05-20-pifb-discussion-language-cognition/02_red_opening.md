# Red Opening — pifb-discussion-language-cognition

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Language and Cognition subsection openly registers the gauge-curvature conjecture as conditional on a Regime II extension that is not implemented (preamble at 3211), labels the curvature claim a "potentially falsifiable conjecture" rather than a theorem (3213), names a concrete experimental probe (the Wilson observable $W_{ijk}$) with explicit caveats limiting its dynamical content to bidirectional rather than causal-DAG attention (3211, 876), and closes by denying that the framework constitutes a unification of physical forces with cognition or language (3225) — so the hedges discharge the substantial empirical and unification claims being made.

## Position

The subsection is **not** correctly calibrated. Four load-bearing assertions inside the hedged scope are themselves not hedged, are not cited, or are contradicted by the framework's own implementation status. The Regime II preamble at 3211 protects the *conjecture*; it does not protect the four downstream sentences in 3217–3223 that make positive, citation-free assertions against an established empirical literature (BERTology, sociolinguistics, linguistic-evolution efficiency theory). The "strongest testable prediction" superlative at 3223 is internally inconsistent with the Regime I implementation status the manuscript itself acknowledges at 3211, 3215, and 880.

## Evidence

**E1. The syntax-from-attention claim at 3219 is written as prospective when the empirical result already exists.** The manuscript writes "thresholding this coupling matrix $A_{ij} = \mathbb{I}[\beta_{ij} > \theta]$ *may yield* dependency graphs that closely match syntactic parse trees" (`Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3219`, emphasis added). Clark, Khandelwal, Levy, and Manning (2019) — already in the project's `references.bib` as `clark2019does` per the evidence pack — empirically demonstrated that BERT attention heads "attend to the direct objects of verbs, determiners of nouns, objects of prepositions, and coreferent mentions with remarkably high accuracy" (Clark et al. 2019, BlackboxNLP, ACL Anthology W19-4828, abstract). Hewitt and Manning (2019, NAACL N19-1419) showed that "squared L2 distance encodes the distance between words in the parse tree" in BERT representations — i.e., the parse-tree-from-attention correspondence has been measured, not just conjectured. The prospective "may yield" framing without citing this literature treats a known empirical finding as a future hypothesis. The claim further generalizes from the empirical result that *certain heads track certain syntactic relations* to the much stronger thesis that *thresholded attention reconstructs the dependency graph as a whole* — which is not what Clark et al. demonstrated and is not supported by their data.

**E2. "Grammar is an information-theoretic necessity rather than cultural convention" at 3219 is contradicted by the canon.** McWhorter (2001, *Linguistic Typology* 5(2-3), 125-166) argues that creole grammars are systematically simpler than older grammars because of "break in transmission" during pidginization — a contingent sociohistorical mechanism, not an information-theoretic optimum. DeGraff (2001, *Linguistic Typology*) and the more recent Mufwene-Nakayasu line dispute the simplicity claim but situate the dispute squarely within transmission and contact dynamics, not curvature minimization. Gibson, Futrell, Piantadosi, Dautriche, Mahowald, Bergen, and Levy (2019, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* 23(5), 389-407) — the canonical recent review of how "efficiency shapes human language" — frames efficiency as one shaping pressure among many (cognitive constraints, learnability, social transmission), explicitly not as a *necessity* that rules out cultural convention. The "necessity rather than convention" disjunction is a false dichotomy: the canon supports "necessity *and* convention act jointly." Either the framing softens to "information-theoretic pressures shape grammar alongside cultural transmission" or it cites a primary source that actually claims necessity over convention. The current text cites neither.

**E3. The "strongest testable prediction" superlative at 3223 is internally inconsistent with the framework's own implementation status.** The manuscript states at 3211: "In Regime~I the curvature $F_{\mu\nu}$ vanishes identically by the Maurer-Cartan identity (Lemma thm:vanishing_holonomy), so a curvature-minimization principle has no content under the present implementation." At 3215 it repeats: "Under the Regime~I parameterization $A = U^{-1}dU$ used in the present implementation, $F_{\mu\nu}$ is identically zero and 'curvature minimization' is automatic." At line 880 the manuscript admits explicitly: "In the Regime~I implementation realized in this manuscript, the connection is pure-gauge and curvature vanishes identically by Lemma~\ref{thm:vanishing_holonomy}; the holonomy diagnostics above are degenerate in that limit" (`Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:880`). A prediction whose probe is "degenerate in that limit" under the implemented framework is not a *current* testable prediction; it is a prospective prediction conditional on a future Regime II implementation. Calling it "the strongest testable prediction of our framework" is a superlative on a prediction that is not testable now in any operationalized sense and has no quantitative target value attached even in principle.

**E4. The creolization probe at 3217 has no operationalization.** The text says "Creolization may provide an example: when pidgins develop into creoles with systematic grammar, we might observe rapid curvature reduction." Operationalizing this requires (i) the Regime II extension (per 3211); (ii) a learned connection field $\delta_{ij}$ trained on natural-language corpora (per 878); and (iii) the bidirectional-attention condition where the Wilson observable acquires "direct dynamical content" rather than being "an index-space invariant of the connection tensor rather than a property of the data flow" (per 3211 and 876). Historical creole-formation corpora (Hawaiian Creole English, Haitian Creole, Tok Pisin) are text, not connection fields learned by a Regime II transformer. The manuscript provides no method for extracting $\delta_{ij}$ from a historical corpus, and no quantitative prediction of how much curvature reduction would count as "rapid" or as confirmation. This is not a falsifiable test in Popper's sense; it is a metaphorical association.

**E5. The 3221 generalization to social systems, scientific communities, neural systems, and market economies is metaphor extension without primary-source backing.** The paragraph asserts that "Scientific communities establish terminology, results, and notation that flatten communication gauge structure", that "Neural systems meanwhile, may wire themselves to minimize curvature in neural information flow, potentially explaining observed patterns of cortical connectivity", and that "market economies develop institutions and contracts that reduce transaction curvature." None of these four assertions carries a citation to the literature on scientific terminology evolution (Kuhn 1962, Galison 1997), cortical wiring economy (Bullmore-Sporns 2012 on brain network cost-efficiency), or institutional economics (Coase 1937, Williamson 1985 on transaction-cost economics). The closing self-tag — "This principle might apply to any emergent informational system or it may only be a pretty way to describe something more ephemeral" — is honest about the speculative status of the principle as a whole, but it does not cover the four preceding sentences that read as confident predictions, not as candidates that "may only be a pretty way to describe something more ephemeral." The hedge at the end of the paragraph does not retroactively label the four assertion sentences as metaphor.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if:

1. **R1 falsifies**: If Clark et al. (2019) and Hewitt-Manning (2019) do *not* in fact establish that attention encodes syntactic structure empirically, I concede R1. (Verified above against ACL Anthology W19-4828 and N19-1419 — they do.)

2. **R2 falsifies**: If a primary source in the linguistic-evolution canon (Hawkins 2004, Gibson et al. 2019, Piantadosi 2014, Christiansen-Chater 2008, Kirby 2017) actually defends grammar as an information-theoretic *necessity* that overrides cultural convention, I concede R2. The Gibson et al. (2019) framing is "efficiency shapes language," not "efficiency necessitates language" — necessity-vs-convention is not the canonical disjunction.

3. **R3 falsifies**: If the Regime II extension is in fact already implemented in the codebase or in the manuscript's experimental results with a Wilson observable computed on natural-language data, I concede R3. The 3211 preamble, 3215 statement, and 880 explicit admission make this unlikely; pointing me to a Regime II implementation that produces a numerical Wilson value from a corpus would close it.

4. **R4 falsifies**: If the manuscript or its supplementary material contains an operationalization mapping historical creole corpora to a $\delta_{ij}$ connection field with a quantitative curvature-reduction prediction, I concede R4. Absent that, the creolization claim is a metaphor.

5. **R5 falsifies**: If the four domain-extension sentences at 3221 are supported by primary sources (cortical wiring, transaction-cost economics, scientific terminology evolution) that the manuscript cites, I concede R5. The paragraph as it stands cites none.

The strongest single attack is R3 plus R1: R3 because the "strongest testable prediction" framing is contradicted by the manuscript's own admission at 880 that Regime I holonomy diagnostics are "degenerate in that limit"; R1 because the prospective framing of the BERTology result treats years of empirical work as a future hypothesis. Both can be addressed editorially — R1 with a one-citation patch and qualification of the "thresholding may yield parse trees" sentence relative to Clark et al. and Hewitt-Manning, R3 with replacing "the strongest testable prediction" with "a prospective testable prediction conditional on the Regime II extension." But until those edits land, the subsection is miscalibrated, not correctly calibrated.
