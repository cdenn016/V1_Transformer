The QBism sub-claim survives.

I also grant blue's substantive Wheeler concession — blue agrees the 3187 paragraph needs an inline hedge cross-referencing the speculative subsection at 3230, and this is the load-bearing point. ## Core attack

The Wheeler defect at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3187` is the load-bearing weakness and blue's proposed remedy under-corrects it. The 3187 paragraph reads: "Wheeler's 'law without law' thesis acquires concrete form within the framework: dynamical laws are not posited but arise as stationarity conditions of the variational functional on a principal bundle, and gauge invariance is then a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom — agents that agree on reality must be related by elements of the structure group." Two declarative verbs do work here: "acquires" and "is." The subsection that develops this thesis, `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus` at 3228-3306, opens at line 3230 with the explicit self-identification "this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation. The thesis below, that the gauge structure observed in physics is a property of human cognitive architecture rather than of the noumenal substrate, may be unfalsifiable." A reader encountering 3187 in isolation, without yet reaching 3230, receives the gauge-invariance-as-consensus thesis in indicative mood with no signal that the manuscript itself disclaims derivation status. Blue's proposed hedge — "acquires a candidate concrete form (developed and qualified in Section sec:gauge_invariance_consensus)" — repairs the noun phrase "concrete form" but leaves the second clause "gauge invariance is then a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom" untouched. That second clause is the exact thesis 3230 marks as metaphysical interpretation. The minimum honest repair requires modal weakening on both clauses, not just the first. A sufficient form is: "Wheeler's 'law without law' thesis would acquire concrete form within the framework if the consensus reading of gauge invariance proposed in Section sec:gauge_invariance_consensus is granted: dynamical laws would arise as stationarity conditions of the variational functional on a principal bundle, and gauge invariance would be a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom — agents that agree on reality being related by elements of the structure group. Section sec:gauge_invariance_consensus develops this as an interpretive thesis rather than a derivation." This carries the conditional through the whole paragraph and discharges the 3230 self-disclaimer.

## Defense

The Kant and Rovelli sub-attacks survive blue's defense as small editorial fixes, and the load-bearing strike is Wheeler with a stronger hedge than blue offers.

On Kant at 3179-3180. Blue defends "sensuous intuition" via the Kemp Smith 1929 translation lineage. The Kemp Smith lineage does use "sensible intuition" and the SEP entry does use "forms of our sensible intuition of objects." What blue does not produce is a direct quotation of Kemp Smith using the exact phrase "sensuous intuition" — the SEP language blue cites is "sensible," not "sensuous." The two words differ in contemporary philosophical English: "sensible" means "of the senses" (the technical Kantian sense), while "sensuous" connotes aesthetic-sensual experience and is largely archaic in this technical use. The Guyer-Wood 1998 Cambridge Edition, which is the current canonical English translation in active Kant scholarship, uses "forms of intuition" with "sensible" reserved for the faculty. The substantive philosophical content blue identifies — that space and time are not things in themselves but cognitive forms organizing experience (A26/B42, A33/B49-50) — is correct and the manuscript reports it correctly. The defect is purely editorial: the manuscript should replace "forms of 'sensuous intuition'" with "forms of intuition" (the Guyer-Wood phrasing, A19/B33) or "forms of sensible intuition" (the SEP/Kemp-Smith phrasing). Either fixes the slight terminological drift while preserving the philosophical content blue defends.

On Rovelli at 3182-3183. Blue defends the "extends...compatible with" pair as internally calibrated on two axes (extension along the model-fiber axis: mass, geometry, temporal flow; non-extension at the level of physical-law observer-dependence). The dual framing is coherent on the substance but the verbs are in surface tension for a reader who does not perform blue's axis decomposition. "Extends this further" at 3182 invites the reading that the present framework supersedes RQM along some axis; "compatible with the relational reading" at 3183 invites the reading that the framework sits alongside RQM without superseding it. The two are reconcilable through blue's axis decomposition, but the manuscript does not perform that decomposition for the reader. The textbook hedge for this case is to replace "extends this further with a mathematical model" with "develops a related but distinct mathematical model" — that preserves the substantive claim blue defends (mass, geometry, temporal flow are agent-frame-dependent in the present framework where they are not in standard RQM) while removing the surface tension with the 3183 "compatible with" hedge. This is an editorial tightening; the RQM sub-claim survives in substance.

On Wheeler at 3187 — the load-bearing strike. Blue's concession that an inline hedge is needed is the correct adjudication direction; the disagreement is calibration of the hedge. Blue's proposed phrasing repairs "acquires concrete form" to "acquires a candidate concrete form (developed and qualified in...)" but leaves the predicative clauses "dynamical laws are not posited but arise as stationarity conditions" and "gauge invariance is then a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom" in indicative mood. The 3230 self-disclaimer is about the second of these clauses, not the first: 3230 says "the gauge structure observed in physics is a property of human cognitive architecture rather than of the noumenal substrate, may be unfalsifiable." That is the proposition 3187 states as established. A hedge on the noun phrase does not discharge the disclaimer on the predicate. The minimum honest repair is modal weakening of the predicates ("would arise," "would be a consequence") plus a closing sentence pointing to 3230 as the interpretive development, or equivalently a "subject to the interpretive thesis developed in Section sec:gauge_invariance_consensus" conditional opening the paragraph. Either form discharges the obligation; blue's proposed form does not.

The appropriate judge verdict is REMAND with the targeted edits: Kant phrasing replaced with Guyer-Wood or Kemp-Smith canonical form, Rovelli "extends" softened to "develops a related but distinct," QBism citation pinned to a Fuchs 2017 §V or DeBrota-Fuchs-Stacey 2024 page-level reference, and Wheeler 3187 modally weakened on both noun and predicate clauses with a cross-reference to sec:gauge_invariance_consensus. The substantive characterizations all survive; the wording does not.
