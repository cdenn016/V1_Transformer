## Canon excerpts (teams should expand via WebFetch / literature-review)

### Kant 1781 Critique of Pure Reason canon

- **Kant, I. (1781/1787)**, *Critique of Pure Reason*, A19/B33–A48/B66 ("Transcendental Aesthetic" §1–§3). Kant's standard terms: *Anschauungsformen* (forms of intuition) for space and time; *sinnliche Anschauung* (sensible intuition / sensuous intuition) is a distinct term used for the faculty by which the forms operate. The phrase "forms of sensuous intuition" appears in some translations (e.g., Norman Kemp Smith 1929) but the canonical short form is "forms of intuition." Teams should verify which translation the manuscript implicitly uses.
- **Allison, H. E. (2004)**, *Kant's Transcendental Idealism: An Interpretation and Defense*, Yale University Press. Standard secondary source. Especially §5–§6 on the "Transcendental Aesthetic" and the forms-of-intuition doctrine.
- **Guyer, P. (2006)**, *Kant*, Routledge.

### Rovelli RQM canon

- **Rovelli, C. (1996)**, "Relational quantum mechanics," *International Journal of Theoretical Physics* 35, 1637–1678. The canonical RQM paper. Teams should verify the precise claim being attributed — RQM claims observer-relative facts about quantum systems, not just states.
- **Smerlak, M. and Rovelli, C. (2007)**, "Relational EPR," *Foundations of Physics* 37, 427–445. Updates RQM.
- **Adlam, E. and Rovelli, C. (2022)**, "Information is physical: cross-perspective links in relational quantum mechanics," *Quantum Studies: Mathematics and Foundations*. The "cross-perspective links" paper the manuscript cites at 3185.
- **Brukner, Č. (2018)**, "A no-go theorem for observer-independent facts," *Entropy* 20(5), 350. The other cross-perspective-links cite at 3185.

### QBism canon (load-bearing for the "QBism resists multi-agent consensus extensions" claim)

- **Fuchs, C. A., Mermin, N. D., Schack, R. (2014)**, "An introduction to QBism with an application to the locality of quantum mechanics," *American Journal of Physics* 82, 749. (The `Fuchs2014` cite.)
- **Fuchs, C. A. (2017)**, "On participatory realism," in *Information & Interaction*, Springer, 113–134. (The `Fuchs2017` cite.) Critical primary source for the "QBism resists multi-agent consensus" claim — teams should verify whether Fuchs explicitly resists this.
- **DeBrota, J. B. and Fuchs, C. A. (2020)**, "Negativity bounds for Weyl–Heisenberg quasiprobability representations," *Foundations of Physics* 50, 1133–1153. (Background.)
- **Stacey, B. C. (2019)**, "Ideas Abandoned en Route to QBism," arXiv:1911.07386. Useful secondary discussion of the consensus question.

### Wheeler "law without law" canon

- **Wheeler, J. A. (1989/1990)**, "Information, Physics, Quantum: The Search for Links," in *Complexity, Entropy, and the Physics of Information*, W. H. Zurek, ed. (also Wheeler 1990 in *Sakharov Memorial Lectures*). The "law without law" thesis. Standard summary: "All things physical are information-theoretic in origin and this is a participatory universe."
- **Penrose, R. and Wheeler, J. A.** correspondence in *Quantum Theory and Measurement* (1983) — relevant background.

## What this evidence does NOT settle

1. Whether "forms of 'sensuous intuition'" at 3180 is a translation choice or a misremembered phrase. Standard contemporary translations (Guyer-Wood 1998) use "forms of intuition"; the Kemp Smith 1929 translation uses "forms of sensible intuition" or "forms of sensibility." "Sensuous intuition" is acceptable but unusual.
2. Whether Fuchs et al. have actually "been explicit that QBism resists multi-agent consensus extensions" or whether this is a manuscript inference from Fuchs's personalist framing. The 3185 claim is strong; primary-source verification is needed.
3. Whether the Wheeler paragraph at 3187 anticipates a derivation the manuscript later admits has not been done. The "gauge invariance is then a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom" claim is exactly the speculative thesis advanced in sec:gauge_invariance_as_consensus (lines 3230-3306), which is explicitly marked "this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation" at 3232. The 3187 paragraph reads the speculative claim as established.
4. Whether the framework's "extends this further" framing for RQM at 3182 is justified. The manuscript explicitly admits at 3183 "The model is compatible with the relational reading; it does not establish that physical laws themselves are observer-dependent" — but then asserts at 3182 the framework "extends" RQM. "Extends" implies the framework goes beyond RQM in a substantive way; "compatible with" implies it sits alongside. The two framings are in mild tension.
5. Whether other participatory-universe traditions should be added beyond the four named. The constructivism debate already settled the constructivism additions (declined). Other possibilities (e.g., participatory realism literature beyond Fuchs 2017, Tegmark mathematical universe, Penrose conformal cyclic cosmology) are admissible but the manuscript's selection of four named comparisons is editorial choice.

Teams should verify points 1-3 against primary sources. Points 4-5 are editorial judgment calls.
