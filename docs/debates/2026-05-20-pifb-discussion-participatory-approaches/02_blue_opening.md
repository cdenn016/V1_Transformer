# Blue Opening — pifb-discussion-participatory-approaches

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Kant phrasing "sensuous intuition" diverges from the dominant contemporary translation, the QBism characterization is a manuscript inference dressed as a Fuchs quotation unless tied to a specific page, the "extends" framing for Rovelli at 3182 is in tension with the "compatible with" hedge at 3183, and the Wheeler paragraph at 3187 states the gauge-invariance-as-consensus thesis in declarative form while the very subsection that develops it (3230) opens "this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation," so 3187 over-claims relative to what the manuscript actually proves.

## Position

Three of the four characterizations in `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3176-3187` are defensible against primary-source review: the Kant statement is consistent with the Kemp Smith translation lineage and with Stanford Encyclopedia usage, the Rovelli statement is the canonical one-line summary of Rovelli 1996 and the "extends/compatible with" pair is internally calibrated, and the QBism statement is directly supported by Fuchs's published personalist commitments and the surrounding QBism literature. The Wheeler paragraph at 3187 needs an inline hedge cross-referencing `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus` because that subsection self-identifies as metaphysical interpretation rather than derivation; the rest stands.

## Evidence

**Kant 3179-3180 — "forms of 'sensuous intuition'"**

- The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on Kant's Transcendental Idealism uses the phrase "forms of our sensible intuition of objects" verbatim for Kant's characterization of space and time. The terminological family ("sensible intuition" / "sensuous intuition") is standard, not idiosyncratic. [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, "Kant's Transcendental Idealism"]
- The Kemp Smith 1929 translation, the dominant English Kant translation for most of the twentieth century and still in active scholarly use, employs "forms of sensible intuition" and "sensuous intuition" interchangeably for *sinnliche Anschauung*. The Guyer-Wood 1998 Cambridge translation prefers "forms of intuition" simpliciter, but does not displace "sensuous intuition" as an acceptable rendering. [Kant 1781, A19/B33–A48/B66 "Transcendental Aesthetic" §1–§3; Kemp Smith 1929]
- The substantive content the manuscript attributes to Kant — that space and time are not "things in themselves" but cognitive forms organizing experience — is exactly the Transcendental Aesthetic thesis at A26/B42 (space is "nothing but the form of all appearances of outer sense") and A33/B49–50 (time is "nothing but the subjective condition under which all intuitions can take place in us"). The translation choice is editorial; the philosophical content is correct.

**Rovelli 3182-3183 — "quantum states are relative to observers" and "extends this further"**

- Rovelli 1996 abstract: "the notion of observer-independent state of a system (or observer-independent values of physical quantities)" is rejected; quantum mechanics "describes only the information that systems have about each other." [Rovelli 1996, *International Journal of Theoretical Physics* 35, 1637–1678, §1]
- The manuscript's one-line summary "Rovelli argues quantum states are relative to observers" is the canonical paraphrase of this thesis; no honest reading of Rovelli 1996 §1 contradicts it.
- The "extends this further" framing at 3182 is qualified at 3183 by "The model is compatible with the relational reading; it does not establish that physical laws themselves are observer-dependent." That qualifier is the load-bearing calibration. Read together, the paragraph claims (i) extension along the model-fiber axis (the framework makes mass, geometry, and temporal flow agent-frame-dependent, not just states), and (ii) explicit non-extension at the level of physical-law observer-dependence. The dual framing is internally consistent: there is an extension on one axis, no extension on another, and the manuscript names both.

**QBism 3185 — "QBism resists multi-agent consensus extensions"**

- The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy entry on QBism states: "Quantum mechanics is a single user theory, and any coincidence among states assigned by different users is just that — coincidence." This is the strongest primary-source-level confirmation that QBism rejects shared agent-external quantum states. [SEP, "Quantum-Bayesian and Pragmatist Views of Quantum Theory"]
- Fuchs, Mermin, Schack 2014 grounds QBism in personalist Bayesianism: probabilities are an agent's personal degrees of belief, the Born Rule is a normative consistency condition on a single agent's beliefs, and "QBism takes the very form of quantum theory seriously as a personalist tool." [Fuchs, Mermin, Schack 2014, *American Journal of Physics* 82, 749]
- Fuchs 2017 "On Participatory Realism" develops the participatory-realist reading of QBism explicitly as a single-agent-perspectival theory; the paper's central move is that "reality is more than any third-person perspective can capture" — i.e., precisely the rejection of the agent-external shared state the manuscript characterizes at 3185. [Fuchs 2017, in *Information & Interaction*, Springer, 113–134]
- The DeBrota-Fuchs-Stacey 2024 "When will two agents agree on a quantum measurement outcome? Intersubjective agreement in QBism" abstract states: "According to QBism, nothing in the quantum formalism implies either that the quantum state assignments of two agents or their respective measurement outcomes need to be mutually consistent." This is contemporary Fuchs-camp confirmation that QBism positively denies the necessity of inter-agent agreement on states. [DeBrota, Fuchs, Stacey 2024, arXiv:2312.07728]
- The 3185 paragraph's exact phrasing — "the probabilities live with the individual agent and there is no agent-external shared state to which separate agents are converging" — is consistent with all four of the above citations. The manuscript's claim that the framework "adopts QBism's personalist starting point...but then adds a structure QBism rejects" (variational consensus on a gauge bundle) is honest: variational consensus dynamics on a shared structural scaffold are exactly what the QBism literature has positioned itself against.

**Wheeler 3187 — "law without law...gauge invariance...consequence of consensus rather than an axiom"**

- Wheeler 1989/1990 "Information, Physics, Quantum": "all things physical are information-theoretic in origin and this is a participatory universe." [Wheeler 1990, in *Complexity, Entropy, and the Physics of Information*, ed. W. H. Zurek]
- The manuscript's framing at 3187 — that the framework gives Wheeler's slogan "concrete form" by making dynamical laws stationarity conditions of a variational functional on a principal bundle — is the substantive claim the manuscript's later subsection `sec:gauge_invariance_consensus` develops.
- The "concrete form" claim is calibrated where it lands: 3187 says the *form* the framework takes is Wheeler-shaped, not that the framework has empirically established the participatory thesis. This is consistent with the Wheeler-1990 literature, which itself is a programmatic position, not a derivation.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if:

1. **Kant translation falsifier.** If primary-source review of the Kemp Smith 1929 translation does *not* contain the phrase "sensuous intuition" or close variants for *sinnliche Anschauung*, then the 3180 phrasing is an idiosyncratic invention, not a translation choice. In that case the strongest defense available is to substitute the Guyer-Wood 1998 phrasing "forms of intuition" or the SEP phrasing "forms of sensible intuition," and the Kant sub-claim fails to its strict form.

2. **QBism falsifier.** If primary-source review of Fuchs 2014, Fuchs 2017, or the DeBrota-Fuchs-Stacey 2024 paper shows that QBism *does* admit some agent-external shared structure or *requires* inter-agent agreement on states, then the 3185 characterization is wrong and must be retracted. The evidence above weighs strongly the other way, but the manuscript does not cite a specific Fuchs page; if challenged on a page-level reading, the manuscript needs to add a `§V` or specific page citation to Fuchs 2017 or a quoted line from Fuchs-Mermin-Schack 2014.

3. **Rovelli falsifier.** If "extends this further" is read by a strict reader as claiming an extension Rovelli 1996 does not support — specifically, if a reader interprets 3182 as claiming the framework derives Rovelli's results from a more fundamental position — then the paragraph needs to weaken "extends this further with a mathematical model" to "is compatible with relational quantum mechanics and adds, on a separate axis, agent-frame-dependent models." The current 3183 hedge already says the latter; the tension is between "extends" at 3182 and "compatible with" at 3183, and a strict reader could ask for the two framings to be unified.

4. **Wheeler falsifier — the strongest red attack.** The Wheeler paragraph at 3187 reads "dynamical laws are not posited but arise as stationarity conditions of the variational functional on a principal bundle, and gauge invariance is then a consequence of consensus rather than an axiom." The subsection that develops this thesis, `Gauge Invariance as Cognitive Consensus Requirement` at 3228, opens (line 3230) with: *"this section advances a metaphysical interpretation, not a derivation. The thesis below, that the gauge structure observed in physics is a property of human cognitive architecture rather than of the noumenal substrate, may be unfalsifiable."* The 3187 paragraph states the conclusion of a subsection that self-identifies as non-derivative. If the judge treats 3187 as anticipating a derivation the manuscript has not produced, the paragraph needs an inline hedge — e.g., "acquires a candidate concrete form (developed and qualified in Section~\ref{sec:gauge_invariance_consensus})" — to cross-reference the speculative qualification. I grant this is the strongest single red attack on the subsection, and the appropriate fix is an inline hedge at 3187, not a removal.

## Honest calibration

The blue position holds on Kant, RQM, and QBism subject to the falsification conditions above. On Wheeler at 3187, the strongest honest defense is that the substantive content is the manuscript's own programmatic claim, developed in a later subsection that explicitly labels itself as metaphysical interpretation; the appropriate remedy is a one-clause inline hedge at 3187, not a substantive retraction. A judge ruling REMAND with a targeted hedge instruction at 3187 would be consistent with the evidence; a ruling RED_WINS broad would over-read the defect.
