# Red Opening — rg-construction-meta-agent

## Steelman (opposing position)

The manuscript performs an honest two-part decomposition that exhibits real discipline: an exact pushforward of the Gibbs measure that is rigorous by elementary measure theory (Theorems 1-2), a gauge-covariance result on a compact group with stated hypotheses on weight invariance (Theorem 3), a Laplace integration of internal modes with a quantitative anharmonic remainder, a Schur-complement effective metric standard in singular perturbation theory, and a labeled separation between "exact" and "approximate" steps with the closure residual defined modulo additive constants. The construction collects the right named objects (Gibbs pushforward, Karcher mean, M-projection barycenter, Schur complement, Laplace approximation, MDL retention), labels Proposition 1 as a proposition rather than a theorem precisely because its constants are not pinned down, and registers the noncompact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ obstruction explicitly at line 4580. Under the canon-licensed reading of "well-motivated by the literature" that the prior verdict at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/04_verdict.md` established — labeling-with-justification rather than derivation-from — each of the four sub-claims is defensible on the manuscript's own discipline.

## Position

The integrated claim fails because the rigorous backbone is hollow at the load-bearing junctions. Three structural cracks land:

1. Theorem 4's "Gaussian closure with local-potential correction" delivers strict closure only in a regime ($A_{\xi\xi}$ independent of $Y$) that no nontrivial Gaussian belief network with parent-dependent precisions can occupy; in the general regime the theorem produces a $Y$-dependent log-determinant scalar potential that is outside the multi-agent KL functional class, so the projection $\Pi_{\mathfrak{M}}$ at line 4498 is back into a smaller class than the renormalized free energy actually lies in.
2. The rigorous Theorems 1-5 are proved on $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$ (manuscript line 4435) while the framework's natural gauge group is $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ (manuscript line 587, line 2100). The construction is rigorous only outside the framework's natural setting; the manuscript admits at line 4580 that "the noncompact case requires a gauge slice or regulator that the present manuscript does not provide."
3. Proposition 1's "Controlled approximate closure" (line 4541 paragraph heading) controls nothing in the technical sense: the constants $C_1, \ldots, C_6$, the closure norm $\|\cdot\|_{\mathcal{B}}$, and the regularity class of the parent ansatz are explicitly unspecified (line 4554). A bound with unspecified constants and unspecified norm is a structural template, not a bound.

These three cracks jointly defeat sub-claim B (closure is approximate-but-controlled) and sub-claim A (rigorous on the natural gauge group). Sub-claim C inherits sub-claim B's residual $\varepsilon_I$ as an unbounded input to Theorem 5's Lipschitz hypothesis. Sub-claim D's reference appropriation is a smaller but real defect: the manuscript cites Wilson1971 and Cardy1996 for the structural form of an RG step, but delivers Kadanoff-style block aggregation rather than the Wilson-Cardy coarse-grain-and-rescale step.

## Evidence

### Sub-claim A — Rigorous backbone misses the natural gauge group

Manuscript line 4435: "The rigorous theorem-level statements are proved on a compact gauge group $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$ with a bi-invariant Riemannian metric." The Karcher mean construction at lines 4441-4444 cites `[karcher1977riemannian]` for local uniqueness on convex normal balls; this is the compact bi-invariant setting [Karcher1977].

Manuscript line 587 (framework section): the natural gauge group of the framework is $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$. Manuscript line 2100 confirms: "For non-compact $G$ (such as $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$, where the Killing form on $\mathfrak{gl}(K)$ is indefinite) no bi-invariant Riemannian metric exists, and~\eqref{eq:meta_agent_frame_barycenter} must be replaced by either a left-invariant alternative or a polar-decomposition / SPD-restricted construction; both substitutes break gauge symmetry partially."

Manuscript line 4580 summary: "The construction is rigorous on a compact gauge group; the noncompact case requires a gauge slice or regulator that the present manuscript does not provide."

Canon `external_canon_math.md §2`, the Lie algebra exponential entry, records that "for $\mathrm{GL}^+(K, \mathbb{R})$ the exponential map is not surjective" and that two-exponential parameterization of $\Omega_{ij} = \exp(\phi_i)\exp(-\phi_j)$ is structurally subtle. Cencov uniqueness `[Cencov1972]` on the Fisher metric is also the compact-statistical-manifold result; on noncompact groups the bi-invariant structure that underwrites Theorem 3's Karcher-equivariance step does not exist [KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §1.5].

The manuscript's body subsection at line 4368 records the same gap: "for $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ a gauge slice or Radon-Nikodym correction is required because the Lebesgue density transforms by $|\det h|^{\dim}$ and the action is not unimodular." The Radon-Nikodym factor $|\det h|^{\dim}$ is precisely the modular function whose nontriviality breaks the pushforward-invariance argument of Theorem 1; the manuscript registers this and then proves Theorems 1-3 in the setting where the modular function is trivial.

The claim's wording — "rigorous on a compact gauge group; the noncompact case requires a gauge slice or regulator that the present manuscript does not provide" — is candid, but the claim asserts that the construction "provides a meaningful theoretical backbone for the threshold-based detector of Section~\ref{sec:meta_agent_threshold}." The detector operates on the simulator, which uses $\mathrm{SO}(N)$ in the experiments but is framed against the manuscript's general $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ formalism. The rigorous backbone, by the manuscript's own admission, does not cover the formalism's natural gauge group; it covers a special case.

### Sub-claim B — Theorem 4 closure is essentially empty; Proposition 1 controls nothing

**B1 (Theorem 4).** The theorem statement at lines 4507-4517 says: under conditions (i)-(v), the exact renormalized free energy equals
$$
\mathcal{F}_{s+1}^{\mathrm{exact}}(Y) = \mathcal{F}_0 + \tfrac{1}{2}(Y - Y_0)^\top A_{\mathrm{eff}}(Y - Y_0) + \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}(Y) + \mathrm{const},
$$
with the proof's closure clause at line 4521: "the class closes when $A_{\xi\xi}$ is independent of $Y$ (the log-determinant is then an additive constant); when $A_{\xi\xi}$ depends smoothly on $Y$, the log-determinant contributes a smooth local parent potential."

The constrained internal Hessian $A_{\xi\xi}$ is the block of the parent quadratic form (line 4503) restricted to the internal subspace $\sum_i w_i^I \xi_i = 0$. For Gaussian children $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \Sigma_i)$ in the multi-agent KL functional class of the manuscript (e.g., lines 2143-2150, free energy at scale $s+1$), the second-derivative blocks of the KL terms $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{ij}q_j)$ at the saddle point involve the precision matrices $\Sigma_i^{-1}, \Sigma_j^{-1}$. Canon `external_canon_math.md §1` gives the closed-form Gaussian KL whose Hessian in the natural parameters is the Fisher information $F(q_i)$, which depends on $\Sigma_i$. The internal Hessian $H_I^\perp \approx L_I \otimes F(q_I)$ at line 4380 of the manuscript *itself* states the parent-Fisher dependence; the manuscript's own form makes $A_{\xi\xi}$ depend on $Y = (\mu_I, \Sigma_I, \ldots)$ through $F(q_I)$.

Reference `[BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §3]` on Gaussian variational families confirms that the Fisher information of a Gaussian family is parameter-dependent: $F(\mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma))$ depends on $\Sigma$. The "$A_{\xi\xi}$ independent of $Y$" condition therefore requires $F(q_I)$ to be constant across the parent neighborhood $\mathcal{N}_I$, which in a Gaussian belief network with parent-dependent precision is generically false.

Consequence: in the general regime, Theorem 4 produces $\mathcal{F}_{s+1}^{\mathrm{exact}}(Y)$ as a Gaussian quadratic form *plus* a $Y$-dependent log-determinant scalar potential. The multi-agent functional class $\mathfrak{M}$ at line 4498 is sums of KL terms (the form at lines 2143-2150 of the manuscript). A $Y$-dependent log-determinant of the constrained Hessian is not a sum of Gaussian KLs; it is a different functional. Strict closure into $\mathfrak{M}$ obtains only in the special case the proof identifies as the closure case, which is the case the framework does not occupy.

The projection $\Pi_{\mathfrak{M}}$ at line 4498 then projects out the log-determinant potential, but the closure residual $\varepsilon_I$ at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_closure} (line 4372) absorbs this. The manuscript's claim that Theorem 4 establishes "Gaussian closure with local-potential correction" (the theorem name) overstates what the proof delivers: it establishes that the renormalized free energy is in *a larger class* (Gaussian-KL plus log-det potential), with strict closure into $\mathfrak{M}$ only in the trivial sub-case. The sub-claim's phrasing — "strict Gaussian-KL closure when $A_{\xi\xi}$ is $Y$-independent is a valid mathematical statement" — is true as a statement, but the closure result advertised in the theorem header is mathematically void in the operational regime.

**B2 (Proposition 1).** Lines 4547-4551 state the schematic bound
$$
\varepsilon_{s+1} \le C_1\sum_I (V_I^{(q)} + V_I^{(p)})^{3/2} + C_2\sum_{I,J}\mathcal{H}_{IJ} + C_3\sum_I \delta_I^{\mathrm{marg}} + C_4\sum_I m_I^{-1}\|A_{Y\xi}^{(I)}\|^2 + C_5\sum_I \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{anh}}^{(I)} + C_6\sum_I \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{nonG}}^{(I)}.
$$
Line 4554 then states: "This is stated as a proposition rather than a theorem because the constants $C_k$, the closure norm $\|\cdot\|_{\mathcal{B}}$, and the regularity class of the parent ansatz are not pinned down here, and tightness is not established; a theorem-grade statement would require specifying each."

A bound of the form $\varepsilon \le \sum_k C_k X_k$ with unspecified $C_k$ and unspecified ambient norm on $\varepsilon$ does not control $\varepsilon$ in any operational sense: for any value of $\varepsilon$, suitable $C_k$ make the inequality vacuous. The paragraph heading at line 4541 — "Controlled approximate closure" — does not match the proposition's content; the closure is uncontrolled by the manuscript's own admission. Standard saddle-point / Laplace-method texts `[Wong2001 §II.1]` and `[BenderOrszag1999]` deliver explicit, computable constants for Laplace-approximation error bounds in concrete cases; Proposition 1 declines to do this.

The sub-claim B wording asserts that "Proposition 1's schematic residual bound is honestly labeled as a proposition (not a theorem) because constants and closure norm are not pinned down." The labeling is accurate, but the labeling does not make the bound mathematically meaningful. Honest labeling of a vacuous bound does not promote that bound to a "meaningful theoretical backbone." A backbone needs bones; an unbounded $\varepsilon_I$ is no backbone.

**B3 (Karcher mean and holonomy obstruction).** Line 4530's renormalized inter-cluster transport $\Omega_{IJ}^R$ is a weighted Karcher mean of transports $\Theta_{ij}^{IJ}$, with residual $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ at line 4534 named as "one of the principal closure-error terms." For Karcher mean uniqueness on a compact Lie group, the inputs must lie within a convex normal ball of radius below half the convexity radius `[Karcher1977]`, `[Afsari2011]`. The manuscript does not bound the spread of $\Theta_{ij}^{IJ}$ across the cluster pair — the prior debate's verdict at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/04_verdict.md` Action item 2 names this as a separate verification still to be performed. The holonomy obstruction $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ is for a generic nontrivial $G$-bundle *the* obstruction to a consistent renormalized transport [Nakahara2003 §10.3 on holonomy], and the manuscript provides no mechanism by which it can be made small in any worked example.

### Sub-claim C — Theorem 5's Lipschitz bound is asserted, not derived

Line 4561 states the hypothesis verbatim: "Suppose, for candidate cluster $I$, the parent approximation satisfies the local Lipschitz bound $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{parent}}^*(I) \le \mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{micro}}^*(I) - A_I + L_q V_I^{(q)} + L_p V_I^{(p)} + \varepsilon_I$." The proof at lines 4569-4571 is correct conditional on this bound: $\Delta_I > 0$ follows by substitution under Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_app_detector_retention} (line 4563).

The Lipschitz constants $L_q, L_p$ are not bounded anywhere in the section; the saving term $A_I > 0$ is not derived; the bound itself is hypothesized for "candidate cluster $I$" without conditions on when it holds. The bound has the form of a first-order Taylor expansion of the parent free energy as a function of belief and model dispersion around the consensus configuration, but no derivation establishes either the existence of such an expansion or quantitative values of $L_q, L_p$.

Theorem 5's content reduces to: "if a Lipschitz bound holds with sufficiently small Lipschitz constants and sufficiently large $A_I$, and the detector fires, then retention gain is positive." This is conditional on hypotheses that the manuscript does not establish in any worked example. The closure residual $\varepsilon_I$ on the right-hand side is itself the uncontrolled object from B2; the theorem then bounds $\Delta_I$ in terms of an uncontrolled quantity plus uncontrolled Lipschitz constants. Useful as a conditional template; not as a delivered retention guarantee.

### Sub-claim D — Wilson1971/Cardy1996 reference appropriation

Wilson1971 [Wilson1971] develops the RG step as a recursion on couplings $g \mapsto g'$ generated by a $\beta$-function $dg/dl = \beta(g)$, with the recursion *content* of the construction being the explicit functional form of $\beta$. Cardy1996 §3 [Cardy1996] develops the RG step as a sequence of three operations: (1) coarse-grain (block aggregation), (2) rescale (length-scale dilation $a \to ba$), (3) renormalize (rescale the fields to restore canonical normalization). Without step (2) — the rescaling — the construction is `[Kadanoff1966]` block-spin aggregation, not full Wilsonian RG.

Manuscript lines 4356-4419 deliver (1) coarse-graining via $\mathcal{R}_s$ and (3) renormalization via $\beta_{IJ}^R, \kappa_{IJ}^R, \Omega_{IJ}^R$. No rescaling step is exhibited; the parent variable $Y$ lives on the same continuum $\mathcal{C}$ as the children, on a coarser grid $\Lambda_{s+1} \subset \Lambda_s$ at line 4438. The lack of a length-scale rescaling is consistent with the manuscript's own admission at lines 2137 and 4577 that "we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance beyond the parametric form." The Wilson-Cardy citation is therefore appropriated for the coarse-grain-and-renormalize portion that is genuinely Kadanoff-style block aggregation.

The prior verdict at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/04_verdict.md` licensed the "RG-inspired" framing under the canon-licensed reading of "well-motivated by the literature." This narrower defect — citing Wilson1971/Cardy1996 for the *structural form* when the actual structural form is Kadanoff1966 — survives that licensing. The sub-claim D wording asks whether the references are "correctly used as templates for the structural form"; the structural form delivered is not Wilson-Cardy's, it is Kadanoff's. The reference should be `[Kadanoff1966]`.

## Falsification conditions

The integrated claim survives if any of the following holds:

1. **Sub-claim B1 survives** if Theorem 4's "log-determinant local potential" $V(Y) = \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det' A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ can be shown to lie inside the multi-agent KL functional class $\mathfrak{M}$ as a sum of KL terms in $(q_I, p_I)$ — either via an explicit rewriting in any nontrivial Gaussian regime, or by demonstrating that the $Y$-independent special case is generically occupied by the simulator's regime. Concession: if blue shows the simulator's $A_{\xi\xi}$ at the parent-formation regime is empirically $Y$-independent (e.g., near a Gaussian fixed point), or that the log-determinant absorbs into the standard self-coupling $\alpha \mathrm{KL}(q_I \| p_I)$ as a precision rescaling, B1 falls. I cannot rule this out without primary-source evidence on the simulator's empirical regime; blue is welcome to supply it.

2. **Sub-claim A survives** if the manuscript exhibits a worked $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ gauge slice or Radon-Nikodym correction elsewhere (not in this section, but in the framework section or supplementary appendix) that recovers Theorems 1-3 for the noncompact natural gauge group. Concession: I have not exhaustively searched the supplementary appendices; if such a slice exists, A falls back to the steelmanned form.

3. **Sub-claim B2 survives** if Proposition 1 is rephrased as a structural template (which the manuscript text at line 4554 already does), and the integrated claim is read as licensing only the structural template and not a quantitative bound. The sub-claim's wording — "Proposition 1's schematic residual bound is honestly labeled as a proposition (not a theorem) because constants and closure norm are not pinned down" — is consistent with this weakening, but then the paragraph heading "Controlled approximate closure" at line 4541 is misleading and should be revised to "Structural template for an approximate closure bound."

4. **Sub-claim C survives** if the Lipschitz bound at line 4561 is either derived elsewhere (e.g., from a smooth dependence of the parent free energy on dispersion under conditions (i)-(v)) or labeled as a hypothesis that the simulator empirically satisfies. The current presentation states it as a hypothesis without verification; if blue shows the hypothesis is generic under conditions (i)-(v), C falls.

5. **Sub-claim D survives** if "Wilson1971/Cardy1996" is read as a generic pointer to "the RG literature" rather than to the specific Wilson-Cardy structural form (recursion + rescaling). The narrower attack — that the structural form is Kadanoff's, not Wilson-Cardy's — would then reduce to an editorial recommendation to also cite `[Kadanoff1966]`. Concession: this is the weakest of my attacks; under the prior verdict's licensing it survives as an editorial point.

If, after blue's defense, any one of B1, A, or B2 stands, the integrated claim's assertion that the construction provides a "meaningful theoretical backbone" fails — a backbone requires the load-bearing junctions to deliver content beyond honest labeling. The pushforward-step rigor (Theorems 1-2) and the framing discipline (the labeled separation between exact and approximate) are real and uncontested; the load-bearing claim that this constitutes a backbone for the detector requires the closure step to do more than name its uncontrolled residuals.
