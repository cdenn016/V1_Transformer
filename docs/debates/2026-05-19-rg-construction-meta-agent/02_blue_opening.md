# Blue Opening — rg-construction-meta-agent

## Steelman (opposing position)

The section borrows the name "renormalization group" while delivering only (a) a textbook change-of-variables identity for pushforward measures dressed up as Theorems 1-2, (b) a Theorem 4 whose strict-closure clause is vacuous in any nonlinear empirical regime because $A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ genuinely depends on $Y$, (c) a Proposition 1 whose schematic bound is unfalsifiable because its constants $C_1,\ldots,C_6$ and its closure norm $\|\cdot\|_\mathcal{B}$ are explicitly left unpinned, and (d) a Theorem 5 whose load-bearing Lipschitz inequality at line 4561 is asserted as a hypothesis rather than derived — so the "rigorous RG construction" reduces to a chain of named-after-Wilson-Cardy structural identities plus conditional theorems whose hypotheses are not verified for the manuscript's own simulator.

## Position

The integrated claim survives because the manuscript itself enforces the same scope discipline the steelman demands. The pushforward step is labeled exact and structural; the closure step is labeled approximate and conditional; the strict Gaussian-KL closure clause is labeled a special case while the substantive content is the augmented-class form with the local potential $V(Y)=\tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det'A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$; Proposition 1 is labeled proposition-grade with constants and norm explicitly unpinned; Theorem 5 is labeled conditional on the local Lipschitz bound at line 4561; the RG framing is labeled "RG-inspired" with finite-size scaling registered as future work. Under this labeling discipline the four sub-claims hold:

- **A.** Theorems 1 (line 4458), 2 (line 4467), and 3 (line 4488) are textbook-correct on a compact gauge group with bi-invariant Haar measure, exactly as stated.
- **B.** Theorem 4's substantive content (the Schur complement plus the local-potential correction) is the standard adiabatic-elimination Laplace result, and Proposition 1's schematic decomposition into dispersion / holonomy / marginal / anharmonic / non-Gaussian / Schur-coupling residuals is qualitatively correct as a structural taxonomy of closure-error sources.
- **C.** Theorem 5 is a conditional theorem of the standard "suppose $L$-Lipschitz, then ..." form ubiquitous in variational inference and statistical learning; the conditional character is plainly stated, and the proof at lines 4569-4571 is a clean three-line consequence of the assumed inequality.
- **D.** The Wilson-Cardy citations at line 4354 are used as templates for the coarse-grain / pushforward / renormalize structure, with the universality claims they would entail explicitly registered as future work at line 4577 and the pre-existing disclaimer at line 2137.

## Evidence

**Sub-claim A — exact pushforward and gauge covariance.**

The pushforward identity at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_exact_free_energy} (line 4358) is the Radon-Nikodym derivative of $(\mathcal{R}_s)_*(e^{-\mathcal{F}_s/\tau}d\nu_s)$ with respect to $(\mathcal{R}_s)_*\nu_s$. This is the standard pushforward of Gibbs measures under measurable maps, exactly the content of [Bogachev2007 Vol. I §3.6-3.7] for pushforward measures and [Georgii2011 Ch. 1] for Gibbs measures under coarse-graining. Theorem 1's proof at lines 4463-4465 is the canonical change-of-variables argument: $\rho_{s+1}(X_{s+1}) = \rho_s(\mathcal{R}_s^{-1}(X_{s+1})) = \rho_s(X_s) = Z_s$. Theorem 2's proof at lines 4472-4481 invokes only associativity of preimages: $(\mathcal{R}_{s+1})_*(\mathcal{R}_s)_*\mathbb{P}_s(A) = \mathbb{P}_s((\mathcal{R}_{s+1}\circ\mathcal{R}_s)^{-1}(A)) = (\mathcal{R}_{s+1}\circ\mathcal{R}_s)_*\mathbb{P}_s(A)$. Both proofs are short because they should be short — the structural content does not exceed what change-of-variables delivers, and the manuscript names this honestly at line 4362: "this identity is structural; it does not yet claim that $\mathcal{F}^{\mathrm{exact}}_{s+1}$ has multi-agent form."

Theorem 3 (line 4488) is the load-bearing geometric content. The proof at lines 4493-4495 invokes three independent textbook facts: (i) bi-invariance of the Riemannian metric on a compact $G$ gives $d_G(hU,hU_i) = d_G(U,U_i)$, so $U_I \mapsto hU_I$ minimizes the transformed Karcher objective; this is [Karcher1977], with local uniqueness on convex normal balls of radius below the injectivity radius. (ii) Forward-KL is invariant under common pushforward, $\mathrm{KL}(h_\#a \| h_\#b) = \mathrm{KL}(a\|b)$, which is the change-of-variables identity for relative entropy [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]. (iii) Gaussian moment-matching uniqueness transfers $q_I \mapsto h_\#q_I$. Each step is independently standard; the composition is gauge covariance of $\mathcal{R}_s$ under the base-local action.

The noncompact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat at line 4368 ("a gauge slice or Radon-Nikodym correction is required because the Lebesgue density transforms by $|\det h|^{\dim}$ and the action is not unimodular") and at line 4435 ("the noncompact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ case requires a gauge slice or Radon-Nikodym correction, as flagged in the body") is correctly identified: noncompact gauge groups carry no two-sided invariant Haar measure, and the standard remedy is gauge fixing via Faddeev-Popov ghosts [BlauThompson1991, KobayashiNomizu1963 Vol. I §III]. The two distinct gauge actions (a) and (b) at lines 4364-4368 are correctly distinguished — action (a) leaves $\Omega_{ij} = U_i U_j^{-1}$ invariant, action (b) conjugates $\Omega_{ij} \mapsto h\Omega_{ij}h^{-1}$ tensorially — which is the same distinction between global rigid gauge transformations and base-local gauge transformations made in [Nakahara2003 §10.3].

**Sub-claim B — closure ansatz, Schur, adiabatic elimination.**

The Schur-complement effective metric at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_schur} (line 4410) is the textbook block-matrix elimination from [HornJohnson2013 §0.8.5, Zhang2005]. The Laplace integration at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_laplace} (line 4395) producing the log-det correction $\tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det'H_I^\perp(Y_I)$ and the anharmonic remainder of order $O(\tau^2\|H^{-1}\|^3\|T_3\|^2 + \tau^2\|H^{-1}\|^2\|T_4\|)$ is the standard saddle-point asymptotic with quartic and squared-cubic remainders [Wong2001 §II.1, BenderOrszag1999 §6.6].

Theorem 4 (line 4507) is best read by its split: a Gaussian-closure-with-local-potential statement that is non-trivial, plus a strict-Gaussian-KL-closure special case. The substantive content is the local-potential clause — the exact renormalized free energy lies in the Gaussian quadratic KL class augmented by $V(Y) := \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det'A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$. The proof at lines 4520-4522 is the standard Gaussian integration of internal modes producing a Schur-complement quadratic plus the log-determinant of the constrained internal Hessian; under smooth $Y$-dependence the log-determinant is a smooth scalar potential. This part is mathematically clean.

The constrained internal Hessian $H_I^\perp \approx L_I \otimes F(q_I)$ at line 4380 (Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_internal_hessian}), the weighted Rayleigh quotient $\lambda_{I,w}$ at line 4389 (Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_constrained_gap}), and the normal-hyperbolicity / slow-manifold reduction at line 4574 are the standard structure of geometric singular perturbation theory [Fenichel1979, Jones1995, Kuehn2015 §3.4]. The construction breaks down at near-critical / phase-transition-like points where $\lambda_{I,w} \to 0$, which is the correct behavior for any adiabatic-elimination scheme: criticality is a singular limit, not a regime where the elimination is supposed to work. Hypothesis (e) at line 4409 ("no near-critical soft mode within $\mathcal{N}_I$") flags this explicitly.

Proposition 1 (line 4544) is labeled a proposition rather than a theorem because the constants $C_1,\ldots,C_6$, the closure norm $\|\cdot\|_\mathcal{B}$, and the regularity class are not pinned down (manuscript explicitly states this at line 4554). What survives that labeling is the structural decomposition: closure residual splits into belief-dispersion $V_I^{(q)}$, model-dispersion $V_I^{(p)}$, holonomy spread $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ (line 4534), marginal mismatch $\delta_I^{\mathrm{marg}}$ (line 4539), Schur-coupling $m_I^{-1}\|A_{Y\xi}^{(I)}\|^2$, anharmonic remainder $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{anh}}$, and non-Gaussian cumulant residual $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{nonG}}$. Each term has been independently derived in lines 4378-4419; the proposition collects them. The honest labeling — Proposition, not Theorem — is the right form of mathematical hygiene, not a hedge.

The renormalized inter-cluster transport $\Omega_{IJ}^R$ at line 4530 is a weighted Karcher mean of the transported edges $\Theta_{ij}^{IJ} = \Omega_{Ii}\Omega_{ij}\Omega_{jJ}$, well-defined under the same convex-normal-ball condition [Karcher1977, Afsari2011] as the parent frame. The holonomy obstruction $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ at line 4534 measuring how much pairwise microscopic transport fails to descend to a single parent-parent transport is the natural curvature analogue [Nakahara2003 §10.4, KobayashiNomizu1963 Vol. I §II.4] for the discrete coarse-graining setting; the manuscript names it as a closure-error term, which is correct.

**Sub-claim C — retention and detector.**

The Wilsonian relevance criterion at line 4422 (eigendirections of $DR_{\theta^*}$ classified by multiplier magnitude) is the canonical Wilson-Fisher RG flow classification [Wilson1971, wilson1975renormalization, Cardy1996 §3]. The MDL/BIC retention criterion at line 4422-4424 with $\Delta_I = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{micro}}(I) - \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{parent}}(I)$ and complexity penalty $C(I) \in \{\text{BIC}, \text{Bayesian evidence prior}, \text{variational KL}\}$ is the standard model-selection penalty [Schwarz1978, Rissanen1978, Grunwald2007]. The manuscript's statement at line 4427 that the two criteria "coincide only in favorable regimes" is correct: relevance and minimum description length pull in genuinely different directions in general, and conflating them is a documented error in the literature on RG-flow model selection. The identification of the free-energy improvement criterion of Section `sec:meta_agent_variational` as the $\Delta_I > 0$ special case is the structural mapping the manuscript needs.

Theorem 5 (line 4559) is conditional. The hypothesis at line 4561 is a local Lipschitz bound:
$$\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{parent}}^*(I) \le \mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{micro}}^*(I) - A_I + L_q V_I^{(q)} + L_p V_I^{(p)} + \varepsilon_I.$$
This is asserted as a hypothesis, not derived from conditions (i)-(v) of the body subsection. The proof at lines 4569-4571 is then a three-line consequence: detector $\Gamma_I = P_I C_q(I) C_p(I)$ with $C_q(I) = \exp[-V_I^{(q)}/\tau_q]$ and $C_p(I) = \exp[-V_I^{(p)}/\tau_p]$ gives $V_I^{(q)}/\tau_q + V_I^{(p)}/\tau_p < \log(1/\Gamma_{\min})$ when $\Gamma_I > \Gamma_{\min}$ and $P_I \le 1$; substituting into the Lipschitz bound yields $\Delta_I > 0$ under Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_app_detector_retention}. The conditional form is identical to countless theorems in variational inference and statistical learning of the form "suppose $L$ is $\beta$-Lipschitz, then ..." [BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017 §3.3]; the hypothesis is plausible in the high-coherence regime where the parent variational free-energy is bounded in second-derivative norm on a compact neighborhood, but it is not derived here, and the theorem is therefore conditional on that hypothesis.

**Sub-claim D — RG framing as a whole.**

The manuscript's pre-existing disclaimer at line 2137 ("the present construction is RG-inspired rather than a literal RG analysis: we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance beyond the parametric form") and the finite-size scaling paragraph at line 4576-4577 ("we register this as a future-work program rather than as a delivered result, and the body subsection accordingly speaks of 'reorganization event' or 'phase-transition-like event' rather than 'phase transition' in the statistical-mechanical sense") establish the calibration explicitly. The Wilson-Cardy citations at line 4354 are used for the structural template of coarse-grain-then-renormalize, which is the content of [Cardy1996 §3] in its discrete form (Kadanoff block-spin) and [Wilson1971] in its continuum form. No claim of $\beta$-function, fixed point, or critical exponent is made; the universality program is registered as future work with finite-size scaling observables specified at line 4577.

## Falsification conditions

The integrated claim does not survive if any of the following can be established:

**For A:** the Karcher mean fails to be locally unique on the convex normal ball of $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$ under the bi-invariant metric, or the forward-KL change-of-variables identity $\mathrm{KL}(h_\#a \| h_\#b) = \mathrm{KL}(a\|b)$ fails for diffeomorphic $h$. Neither holds: Karcher uniqueness on convex normal balls is [Karcher1977]; the KL change-of-variables identity is textbook [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]. If instead the gauge covariance theorem (Theorem 3) tacitly required surjectivity of the exponential map on a noncompact group, the noncompact-caveat acknowledgment would be insufficient — but the theorem is stated and proved only for compact $G$, so this falsifier is foreclosed by the proof's own scope.

**For B:** the schematic decomposition of Proposition 1 double-counts a residual source — for example, if the marginal-mismatch $\delta_I^{\mathrm{marg}}$ at line 4539 is structurally identical to a contribution already absorbed in the holonomy term $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ at line 4534 (it is not: marginal mismatch is a scalar weight-vs-row-marginal discrepancy in the convex weighting, holonomy spread is a Riemannian distance between transported edges and the parent transport in $G$). Or: the Schur-complement Laplace integration produces a log-det correction that is *not* smooth in $Y$ in any open neighborhood of the slow manifold (it is smooth wherever $A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ is bounded away from singularity, which is hypothesis (e)). Or: Theorem 4's strict-closure clause is misread as a substantive operating regime claim rather than a special case — this would falsify the *interpretation* of Theorem 4 but not the proven content, since the substantive content is the augmented-class form.

**For C:** no Lipschitz constants $L_q, L_p < \infty$ can exist in any open neighborhood of the parent state for any choice of $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{parent}}$ and $\mathcal{F}_{\mathrm{micro}}$. This would require the parent variational free-energy to have unbounded directional derivatives with respect to belief or model dispersion in every neighborhood, which contradicts the bounded-Hessian conditions of the high-coherence regime where Theorem 4's expansion is valid. Or: the detector $\Gamma_I = P_I C_q(I) C_p(I)$ at line 4557 fails to upper-bound $V_I^{(q)}/\tau_q + V_I^{(p)}/\tau_p$ via the bounded-exponential identity (it does: $\Gamma_I \le \exp[-(V_I^{(q)}/\tau_q + V_I^{(p)}/\tau_p)]$, so $\Gamma_I > \Gamma_{\min}$ implies the bound at line 4570).

**For D:** the manuscript at any point claims a $\beta$-function, locates a fixed point, exhibits a critical exponent, or asserts universality without finite-size-scaling evidence (it does not — line 2137 disclaims this, line 4577 registers it as future work, and the body subsection consistently uses "reorganization event" / "phase-transition-like event" terminology). If the Wilson-Cardy citations at line 4354 were used in a context that required the cited content to deliver a $\beta$-function (the section does not require this — only the structural coarse-grain-renormalize template).

The integrated claim is honestly defensible. The strongest concession the blue team must register pre-emptively is that Theorem 5's Lipschitz hypothesis is asserted rather than derived and that Theorem 4's strict-closure special case is likely restrictive in the manuscript's full nonlinear simulator regime. The manuscript's own labeling discipline already registers both: Theorem 5 explicitly begins "Suppose ... the parent approximation satisfies the local Lipschitz bound ..." and Theorem 4 explicitly splits the substantive augmented-class content from the strict-special-case content. The claim under debate asserts that the construction is "mathematically correct and well-motivated" under the stated hypotheses with correct labeling of theorem-grade versus proposition-grade content and exact-structural versus approximate-conditional content. That is what the manuscript delivers.
