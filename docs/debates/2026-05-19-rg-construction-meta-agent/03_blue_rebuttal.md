# Blue Rebuttal — rg-construction-meta-agent

## Concession

Two of red's three cracks land cleanly and I grant them.

First, red's B1 attack on the strict-closure clause of Theorem 4 is correct on the evidence. For any Gaussian belief network in which the children's precisions $\Sigma_i^{-1}$ depend on the parent state $Y$ — which is the generic case for the manuscript's multi-agent KL functional with parent-dependent precision matrices (line 4380, $H_I^\perp \approx L_I \otimes F(q_I)$) — the constrained internal Hessian $A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ inherits that $Y$-dependence through the Fisher information $F(q_I)$, whose closed form $F(\mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma))$ depends on $\Sigma$ `[AmariNagaoka2000 §2.3, Eq. 2.66]`. The "strict Gaussian-KL closure when $A_{\xi\xi}$ is $Y$-independent" special case of Theorem 4 (line 4510) is therefore empty in the operational regime of the manuscript's simulator. The closure on the strict class $\mathfrak{M}$ at line 4498 does not obtain; only closure on the augmented class $\mathfrak{M}' = \mathfrak{M} + \{V(Y) : V \in C^\infty\}$ obtains.

Second, red's C attack on Theorem 5's Lipschitz hypothesis is correct: the bound at line 4561 is asserted, not derived, and the saving term $A_I$ and Lipschitz constants $L_q, L_p$ are not bounded in the section. The proof at lines 4570-4572 is conditional on a hypothesis the section does not establish.

Both concessions narrow the integrated claim's "rigorous backbone" reading. They do not collapse the substantive contributions of the section, which my core attack and defense address below.

## Core attack

Red's argument has one load-bearing weakness: it conflates "strict closure into $\mathfrak{M}$ is empty" with "Theorem 4 is mathematically void." These are different statements. The substantive content of Theorem 4 is the *explicit form* of the renormalized free energy at line 4517,
$$\mathcal{F}_{s+1}^{\mathrm{exact}}(Y) = \mathcal{F}_0 + \tfrac{1}{2}(Y - Y_0)^\top A_{\mathrm{eff}}(Y - Y_0) + \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}(Y) + \mathrm{const},$$
with the Schur-complement effective quadratic form $A_{\mathrm{eff}} = A_{YY} - A_{Y\xi}A_{\xi\xi}^{-1}A_{\xi Y}$ at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_app_schur_complement}. This is the standard slow-manifold projection identity from `[HornJohnson2013 §0.8.5]` for the Schur complement and `[Kuehn2015 §3.5]` for slow-fast reduction. The log-determinant local potential $V(Y) = \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ is not an uncontrolled remainder — it is an explicit smooth scalar potential of known functional form, computable directly from the parent quadratic form's $\xi\xi$ block.

The augmented class $\mathfrak{M}' = \mathfrak{M} + \{V(Y) : V \in C^\infty, V = \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}\}$ is a *controlled* enlargement: the enlargement is by exactly one explicit smooth potential per cluster, traceable to the constrained internal Hessian, smooth in $Y$ wherever $A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ is bounded away from singularity (which is precisely the regime closure conditions (i)-(v) guarantee — the positive constrained gap $\lambda_{I,w} > 0$ at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_constrained_gap} bounds $A_{\xi\xi}$ away from zero). This is the canonical form of slow-manifold reduction with log-det correction in geometric singular perturbation theory `[Fenichel1979 §3]`, `[Kuehn2015 §3.5, Eq. 3.66]`, `[BenderOrszag1999 §6.3]` — the log-determinant of the slaved internal Hessian is the standard correction term. The manuscript's theorem statement at line 4510 says exactly this: "the exact renormalized free energy remains in the Gaussian quadratic KL functional class augmented by a smooth scalar potential $V(Y) := \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$." The augmented-class statement is the theorem's substantive content; the strict-closure clause is a derivative corollary covering a special case.

Red's reading — "strict closure is empty so the theorem is void" — drops the augmented-class result. The augmented class is not a "different functional" outside the manuscript's reach: it is the explicit, smooth, additive correction that Theorem 4 *delivers*. The projection $\Pi_\mathfrak{M}$ at line 4498 indeed does not see the log-det potential — but the manuscript registers this exactly through the $\varepsilon_I$ residual at Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_closure} (line 4372) and decomposes it through Proposition 1, term $C_4 \sum_I m_I^{-1}\|A_{Y\xi}^{(I)}\|^2$ at line 4550, which is precisely the size of the cross-block coupling generating the log-det's $Y$-dependence. The accounting is self-consistent; the theorem delivers the correction term, the proposition catalogs it.

## Defense

I defend three load-bearing components: the augmented-class form of Theorem 4, the structural decomposition of Proposition 1, and the rigorous-on-compact-G reading of sub-claim A. I weaken sub-claim D's wording but defend its substantive content. Theorem 5's hypothesis I defend as locally derivable, not as derived in the section.

**On the augmented-class form (Theorem 4).** The augmented class $\mathfrak{M}'$ is a one-potential extension with explicit functional form. Such controlled extensions are standard in effective-action constructions: integrating out fast modes generates a logarithmic correction to the slow-mode effective action `[Cardy1996 §3.3, Eqs. 3.27-3.30]` (the "fluctuation determinant" of fast-mode integration), `[Wilson1974 §V]` (Wilson's $\epsilon$-expansion log-determinant terms). The manuscript's $\tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det{}'A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ is the FEP analog of this fluctuation determinant in variational free energy `[Friston2010 §4]` — and is precisely the variational analog of the entropy contribution that arises when slow-fast separation is applied to a Gibbs measure. Theorem 4 names the correction; the construction works in the augmented class. Sub-claim B's wording — "strict Gaussian-KL closure when $A_{\xi\xi}$ is $Y$-independent is a valid mathematical statement" — survives because the manuscript's text at line 4510 reads *the exact renormalized free energy remains in the Gaussian quadratic KL functional class augmented by a smooth scalar potential*; the strict case is correctly identified as the trivial sub-case of the general augmented-class result.

**On Proposition 1 (structural decomposition).** Red attacks the bound as having unspecified constants. The defense is two-pronged. First, the *taxonomy* of closure-residual terms in the bound — dispersion $V_I^{(q)} + V_I^{(p)}$ (term $C_1$), holonomy $\mathcal{H}_{IJ}$ (term $C_2$), marginal mismatch $\delta_I^{\mathrm{marg}}$ (term $C_3$), cross-block coupling $m_I^{-1}\|A_{Y\xi}\|^2$ (term $C_4$), anharmonicity $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{anh}}$ (term $C_5$), non-Gaussianity $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{nonG}}$ (term $C_6$) — is itself a mathematical contribution. Each term has a precise derivation in the preceding paragraphs: the holonomy term at lines 4530-4537, the marginal mismatch at lines 4539-4540, the cross-block coupling at lines 4500-4524 with the explicit Schur form, the anharmonic remainder at lines 4525-4526 with explicit cubic and quartic tensor magnitudes. Proposition 1 assembles these into a structural bound. The proposition's role is taxonomic-structural, not quantitative-tight. Second, this is the standard form of asymptotic-expansion bounds in singular perturbation theory: `[BenderOrszag1999 §6.4-6.5]` delivers explicit Laplace-approximation bounds with constants that "depend on the smoothness class of the integrand" — exactly the manuscript's phrasing. `[Wong2001 §II.4]` likewise leaves the multiplicative constants as functions of the smoothness class. The manuscript's explicit labeling at line 4555 — "stated as a proposition rather than a theorem because the constants $C_k$ ... are not pinned down" — is the *correct* mathematical practice for a structural decomposition: name the load-bearing terms, leave the constants for case-by-case analysis. Calling such a bound "vacuous" would also vacate textbook bounds in `[BenderOrszag1999]`.

**On sub-claim A (rigorous-on-compact-G).** Red is correct that Theorems 1-3 are rigorous on $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$ (line 4435), not on $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ (line 587). The defense is calibrated: the manuscript's empirical work uses $G = \mathrm{SO}(N)$ in the simulator (the simulator regime red itself names), and the rigorous backbone covers exactly that regime. The noncompact extension is flagged at line 4368 and line 4580 as requiring a gauge slice or Radon-Nikodym correction; flagging this is correct mathematical practice, not a defect. Compare `[BlauThompson1991 §3]`, where noncompact gauge-theory pushforwards require Faddeev-Popov gauge fixing — the same situation, where rigorous statements require gauge slices. `[Nakahara2003 §10.4]` and `[Bleecker1981 §3]` register the same point: bi-invariant Riemannian structures on Lie groups exist on compact groups; noncompact groups require partial choices that break some symmetry. The construction *is* rigorously well-motivated for the working empirical regime; the noncompact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ extension is correctly registered as future work in the manuscript's summary at line 4580. Sub-claim A's wording — "The acknowledged noncompact $\mathrm{GL}^+(K)$ caveat (line 4368) is correctly registered" — captures this exactly.

**On Theorem 5 (local derivability of the Lipschitz bound).** I conceded above that the bound is asserted, not derived in the section. The defense is that it is *derivable locally* under closure conditions (i)-(v), where the cluster is internally coherent (small $V_I^{(q)}, V_I^{(p)}$). The local Lipschitz constants in the Gaussian belief family are explicit: $L_q$ is the operator norm of the parent free-energy Hessian against belief dispersion, which on the Gaussian family is bounded by the Fisher information $F(q_I)$ times the cluster weights `[AmariNagaoka2000 §3.2, Eq. 3.5]`. The saving term $A_I$ is the constrained Hessian gap $\tau \cdot m_I$ from Eq.~\ref{eq:rg_constrained_gap}, which closure condition (iii) makes positive by hypothesis. Under (i)-(v), the parent free energy is smooth in dispersion around the consensus configuration, and a first-order Taylor expansion with explicit remainder gives the form at line 4561 — this is the standard form `[AmariNagaoka2000 §3, Eq. 3.7]` of variational free energies in the Gaussian family with local-Lipschitz dependence on belief moments. The bound is therefore not arbitrary; it is the local linearization of the parent free energy in the high-coherence regime. The manuscript's omission of the explicit derivation is a presentation gap, not a content gap.

**On sub-claim D (Wilson1971/Cardy1996 references).** Red's narrowest attack — that the structural form delivered is Kadanoff-style block aggregation rather than Wilson-Cardy coarse-grain-rescale — is correct on the rescaling step (no length-scale dilation is exhibited) but does not defeat the references. Modern machine-learning RG-inspired work routinely cites Wilson-Cardy for the structural form of "coarse-grain-then-renormalize" without delivering the full $\beta$-function-fixed-point-critical-exponent package — see `[Mehta-Schwab 2014]` (arxiv 1410.3831) "An exact mapping between the Variational Renormalization Group and Deep Learning," which cites Wilson1971 and Cardy1996 for exactly the same template-rather-than-derivation use. The combined manuscript disclaimers — line 2137 ("RG-inspired rather than literal RG ... we do not exhibit a $\beta$-function, locate fixed points, or demonstrate scale invariance"), line 4577 ("we register this as a future-work program rather than as a delivered result"), and line 4580 — collectively establish the right calibration. The prior debate's verdict at `docs/debates/2026-05-19-agent-meta-agent-hierarchy-theory/04_verdict.md` already licensed this "labeling-with-justification" reading. Sub-claim D survives under that licensing; an editorial recommendation to also cite `[Kadanoff1966]` for the block-aggregation step is reasonable and I grant it as an improvement, but does not invalidate the Wilson1971/Cardy1996 references for the structural template.

**On the weight-circularity concern (if pressed).** The fixed-point weight $w_i^I(x) = \chi_i(x)\exp[-\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \bar q_I)]/Z$ uses the gauge-invariant transported KL divergence $\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \Omega_{i,I}\bar q_I)$ — both numerator and parent belief are constructed by gauge-covariant operations. The fixed-point character is a computational concern (when does the iteration converge?), not a gauge-invariance concern (does the converged result transform correctly?). Theorem 3's gauge-covariance argument at lines 4485-4495 applies pointwise at the fixed point — for any fixed-point weight $w_i^I$ that solves the consistency relation, $h \cdot w_i^I = w_i^I$ (the weights are gauge-invariant scalars by construction), and Karcher-mean equivariance carries through. The fixed-point existence on compact $G$ with bi-invariant metric follows from `[Karcher1977]` uniqueness on convex normal balls plus a contraction-mapping argument in the high-coherence regime.

**Net position.** The integrated claim's "meaningful theoretical backbone" reading survives in the following form: Theorems 1-3 are rigorous on compact $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$, which is the working empirical regime; Theorem 4 delivers the explicit form of the renormalized free energy in the augmented Gaussian-KL class with a known log-det correction; Proposition 1 catalogs the closure-residual taxonomy honestly as a structural decomposition; Theorem 5 is locally derivable under closure conditions (i)-(v) and the section's omission of the derivation is a presentation gap. Sub-claims A, B (augmented-class form), and D survive. Sub-claim B's strict-closure clause and sub-claim C's "delivered retention guarantee" wording are correctly identified by red as overreaches and should be tightened in the manuscript to read "augmented-class closure" and "conditional retention guarantee under the locally derivable Lipschitz bound." With those edits, the construction is a meaningful theoretical backbone for the threshold-based detector.
