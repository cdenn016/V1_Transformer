The objection is correct on the size relation but wrong on the RG-theoretic implication: the standard Wilsonian effective-action picture is that integrating out fast modes generates new log-determinant fluctuation terms that augment the action, exactly as Theorem 4 delivers. Blue's `[Cardy1996 §3.3]` and `[Wilson1974 §V]` citations land directly on the canonical form of this augmentation, and red did not produce a counter-citation from the RG canon requiring strict-class preservation. Without that counter-citation, red's "not an RG flow" claim is uncited assertion against blue's cited canon.

The sub-claim wording in `00_claim.md` registers these as labeled-proposition and conditional-theorem respectively, which is what the manuscript does at lines 4554 and 4561. The conditional structure is standard in variational inference and statistical learning. Red's argument that "honest labeling does not promote schematic content to mathematical statement" is rhetorically forceful but applies equally to textbook schematic bounds in `[BenderOrszag1999]` and `[Wong2001]`, which blue cited as precedents.

The Theorem 3 hypothesis-discharge concern that red raised in rebuttal is genuine but resolved on the evidence: the manuscript's fixed-point weight at line 2128, $w_i^I = \chi_i \exp[-\mathrm{KL}(q_i \| \bar q_I)]$, is built from a gauge-invariant support mask and a forward-KL divergence between gauge-covariantly transported beliefs. Theorem 3's stated hypothesis is therefore discharged for the working weight definition in the compact case.

Sub-claim D is fully conceded by red; the stronger wording ("well-motivated by the standard renormalization-group literature") sits within the labeling discipline established at lines 2137, 4577, and 4580, and the prior verdict's RG-inspired licensing applies.
