## Core attack

Red's escalation from "editorial gap" to "topples the compound claim" relies on three moves, each of which fails on the evidence.

**Move 1 — load-bearingness inflation.** Red argues at `02_red_opening.md:25` that the empirical-Bayes claim at `GL(K)_attention.tex:944` ("Both hyper-parameters may be learned via empirical Bayes by introducing two scalar parameters") "presupposes that `b₀, c₀` have a hyper-parameter interpretation" and that "without the Gamma identification, `b₀` and `c₀` are unmotivated constants." This conflates a sufficient condition with a necessary one. Empirical Bayes is a procedure for marginal-likelihood maximization or method-of-moments estimation of hyperparameters of a prior; it does not require the prior to be conjugate, nor even to be named [Murphy2012 §5.6 "Empirical Bayes"; Casella1985 "An Introduction to Empirical Bayes Data Analysis"]. The manuscript's two-scalar-parameter EB existence claim at line 944 stands whether or not `R(α)` is named as `−log Gamma(c₀ + 1, b₀)`. The Gamma identification SIMPLIFIES the EB story to the standard Gamma-EB procedure; it does not GROUND it. Red's claim that the empirical-Bayes assertion is "without the standard machinery that makes the formula make sense" mistakes a stylistic preference (name the conjugate prior) for a derivational dependency.

**Move 2 — equivalence of editorial gap with structural failure.** Red invokes the compound-claim adjudication rule at `00_claim.md:36` as if it were a uniform RED_WINS rule for any sub-claim failure. The rule actually reads: "If one or two sub-claims fail editorially but the others survive structurally, the judge should issue RED_WINS with the falling sub-claims identified, **and the action items should be scoped accordingly**." The scoping clause is load-bearing. The §4–§5 series verdicts that red implicitly cites as precedent (`00_claim.md:11`: "softmax-β stationarity verdict applied at §3.4; canonical-F-vs-surrogate verdict applied at §3.5 line 874") forced equation-level rewrites — the softmax-β verdict added the attention-entropy term `τ β log(β/π)` to the canonical functional form to restore stationarity, and the canonical-F-vs-surrogate verdict added the gap formula `−τ⁻¹ Cov_β(KL, ∇KL)` to reconcile autograd and reduced-F gradients. Those edits altered the manuscript's mathematical content. The remedy red proposes for Sub-claim D, by red's own statement at `02_red_opening.md:39`, is to "add the Gamma-hyperprior identification at §3.7 with `[Bishop2006 §2.3.6]` or equivalent citation, and re-state the closed-form `α* = c₀/(b₀ + KL)` as the MAP estimator under that hyperprior." This is a single-citation editorial fix and a one-paragraph reframing. It does not change any equation. It does not change any gradient. It does not change any implementation path. Calling this "structurally load-bearing" (red, `02_red_opening.md:25`) overstates the scope of the proposed fix by red's own admission.

**Move 3 — selective application of the "No bullshit" rule.** Red cites `CLAUDE.md §Communication Style` at `02_red_opening.md:21`: "If a correspondence is interpretive rather than mathematically exact, say so explicitly." But that rule cuts in BOTH directions, and red applies it asymmetrically. The §3.7 manuscript framing IS interpretive ("a natural choice") about a regularizer choice; red's complaint is that the underlying Gamma correspondence is exact and SHOULD be named. Granted as an editorial improvement. The opposite case — claimed-exact mathematical content that fails — is what the §4–§5 verdicts addressed. Sub-claim D is in the first category, and red does not exhibit a mathematical error in the §3.7 derivation. Red's evidence at `02_red_opening.md:19` confirms this: "Setting `∂F/∂α = KL + b₀ − c₀/α = 0` yields `α* = c₀/(b₀ + KL)`. This is precisely the posterior-mode estimate of α under a `Gamma(c₀ + 1, b₀)` prior." Red admits the derivation IS exact and matches the standard Bayesian machinery — it just is not labeled as such. That is editorial, not structural.

## Defense

The §3.7 construction at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:902–962` is mathematically correct, Bayesian-interpretable, and faithfully implemented downstream. The remaining defense rests on four citations.

First, the regularizer choice is mathematically grounded whether or not the manuscript names it. The identity `R(α) = b₀α − c₀ log α = −log Gamma(α; c₀ + 1, b₀) + const` is verified by direct computation against `Gamma(α; k, b) ∝ α^{k−1} exp(−bα)` [Bishop2006 §2.3.6, Eq. 2.146; Murphy2012 §2.4.4 "The gamma distribution"]. The match is exact, and red has confirmed it. Manuscript line 914's "natural choice" framing is therefore CONSISTENT with — not in conflict with — the Gamma-conjugate-prior identification.

Second, the manuscript at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:942` provides the Bayesian interpretation in everything but the explicit Gamma name: "when an agent is close to its prior (`D_KL(q_i ‖ p_i) ≈ 0`), `α_i ≈ c₀/b₀` takes a large value ... such that the agent trusts its prior and resists displacement by the alignment term. Conversely, when an agent is far from its prior (large `D_KL`), `α_i → 0`, the prior's influence is reduced." This is the standard Bayesian shrinkage-toward-prior reading of MAP estimation under a Gamma precision prior, glossed in natural language. The framing at line 944 ("The ratio `c₀/b₀` sets the maximum precision (the value when beliefs match the prior exactly)") gives the standard `(shape − 1)/rate` mode-of-Gamma interpretation in transparent terms. The Bayesian interpretation is delivered; the formal citation is missing.

Third, Sub-claim D is downstream-coupled to the canonical-F-vs-surrogate verdict (already adjudicated 2026-05-19), and that coupling depends on the closed-form being algebraically correct — not on it being NAMED Gamma-MAP. The product-rule chain at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:957` (Eq. eq:alpha_product_rule, `∂(α* KL)/∂θ = (α*)² (b₀/c₀) ∂KL/∂θ`) was the load-bearing piece of that earlier verdict, and the implementation pointer at line 962 (`transformer/core/vfe_gradients.py`) wires up the corrected form. Whether `R(α)` is labeled "natural choice" or "negative log-density of Gamma(c₀ + 1, b₀)" does not change a single character of `∂α*/∂θ`, of `∂(α*·KL)/∂θ`, or of the implementation. Red's load-bearingness claim cannot reach the downstream consequences.

Fourth, the remaining sub-claims A, B, C, E, F all survive primary-source pressure. Red explicitly concedes this at `02_red_opening.md:11`: "Sub-claims A, B, C, E, and F survive primary-source pressure under the available evidence."
