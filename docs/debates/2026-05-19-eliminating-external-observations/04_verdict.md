# Verdict — eliminating-external-observations

## Outcome

RED_WINS (on the compound claim, at the current text)

## Decisive evidence

`Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:1394` (unconditional subsection title "Eliminating External Observations: A Self-Contained Framework") and `:1447` (unconditional parsimony sentence "no external reality providing special inputs"), read against `:1409` ("subject to the same information-geometric dynamics as all other agents" — conceded by blue at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:5` as stronger than the construction supports), `:1428` ($p_{e_k} = q_{e_k}$ set by hand — no cross-scale shadow per the definitional requirement at `:540`–`:546`), `:1442` (cross-entropy substitution required for full variational equivalence in the covariance sector), and `:1484` (`sec:symmetry_breaking`: environmental agents "play the role of an external source field analogous to a Zeeman term"). The bookend pair at 1394 and 1447 advertises self-containment; the licensing apparatus that the body and the cross-referenced section invoke explicitly names env agents as an external source field. The two cannot both be true on the current text.

## Reasoning

C1 (the explicit algebra at lines 1431–1445) is uncontested. Both teams' sympy verifications agree with the manuscript on the closed-form Gaussian KL ([BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017]; `external_canon_math.md` §1), the mean gradient $\Lambda_o(\mu_i - c_k)$, the covariance discrepancy $-\tfrac12 \Sigma_i^{-1}$, and the cross-entropy identity $-\mathbb{E}_{q_i}[\log q_{e_k}] = \mathrm{KL}(q_i\|q_{e_k}) + H(q_i)$ ([CoverThomas2006 §2.5]). On the algebra-only sub-claim the section is correct.

C2 (motivational adequacy) is where the section fails on the current text. Three distinct defects survive the rebuttal exchange.

The first is the agent-definition deficit. The manuscript's own agent definition at `:617`–`:626` and the cross-scale shadow at `:540`–`:546` make $p_i = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$ definitional. The construction at `:1426`–`:1431` supplies only $q_{e_k}$ and an implicit $\phi_{e_k}$ via $\Omega_{i,e_k} = I$; $s_{e_k}$, $r_{e_k}$, the scale index $s$, and the meta-agent index $I$ are not supplied, and $p_{e_k} = q_{e_k}$ is set by stipulation. Blue's defense routes this through the bottom-scale boundary clause at `:548`, but inspection of that line shows the bottom-scale clause specifies only that "cross-scale propagation acts only downward from above" and the immediately following sentence states that the multi-scale shadow relation "becomes operative only in the participatory dynamics of Section sec:participatory." Line 548 does not license setting $p = q$ by hand for a bottom-scale agent; it licenses the absence of propagation from below. The two are distinct. Blue's appeal to line 548 fails to deliver the construction the agent definition requires. Blue concedes the substance of this at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:5`.

The second is the cross-entropy / coupling-form question. Blue's rebuttal at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:13`–`23` correctly observes that the canonical functional at `:1252`–`:1263` already contains a cross-entropy slot at term 5 (line 1260), not a uniform KL form. That is direct primary-source evidence against red's "homogeneous KL coupling" framing in the opening. Red's rebuttal at `03_red_rebuttal.md:31`–`35` accepts the existence of a heterogeneous functional and reformulates the attack: the cross-entropy substitution at `:1442` is identification with the existing observation-term slot at `:1260`, which is itself the standard FEP observation operator ([Friston2010 §3; ParrPezzuloFriston2022 §4.3]; `external_canon_inference.md` §2). Identifying the env-agent KL with the standard observation operator is not elimination of the observation term — it is recovery of the standard observation term under a renamed wrapper. The "self-contained framework" framing collapses into a renaming of the same external observation channel. Blue's defense closes the homogeneity attack but in doing so concedes the substantive point: full variational equivalence reduces to the standard observation operator the section claims to have eliminated.

The third is the bookend / body tension. The proposition at `:1415`–`:1423`, the body sentences at `:1398`, `:1423`, `:1445`, and `:1447` are conditional. The subsection title at `:1394` and the parsimony sentence at `:1447` are not. Blue concedes this at `02_blue_opening.md:72` and again at `03_blue_rebuttal.md:37`–`45`. The cross-reference at `:1431` to `sec:symmetry_breaking` makes the externalism explicit: at `:1484` env agents enter "with fixed gauge frames" playing "the role of an external source field analogous to a Zeeman term in a ferromagnet." Standard statistical mechanics treats the Zeeman field as the canonical external source ([Kittel2005 §12; Goldenfeld1992 §5.6]). The very mechanism the section invokes to license its gauge-fixing names env agents as external sources; the title and closing sentence claim the framework is self-contained. The bookends overclaim relative to the body and the cross-referenced licensing section.

A separate observation on the user's framing: environmental agents have $q_{e_k} = p_{e_k}$ and $\beta_{i,e_k} = 1$ fixed at construction, with no fast-channel or slow-channel dynamics of their own. The receiving agent updates in response to $e_k$; $e_k$ does not update in response to the receiving agent. That structure is unidirectional broadcast from a fixed source, which is the formal structure of observation, not communication between agents. The user's intended reading ("observations are then communication between agents potentially across various scales") is not delivered by the construction. Blue concedes this at `02_blue_opening.md:82` and `03_blue_rebuttal.md:5`.

The verdict applies to the compound claim of correctness AND motivation at the current text. On the algebra-only sub-claim, C1 holds. On the motivation sub-claim, C2 fails on three independent grounds. Per the methodology, the compound fails when either sub-claim fails. The defects are repairable by targeted revisions to specific lines (see Action); under those revisions the verdict on the repaired text would flip to BLUE_WINS, because the math is right and the body's conditional language is calibrated.

The forbidden split is avoided: blue has not produced an equation-level cross-scale construction (no $s' \neq s$ index, no shadow invocation in the env-agent construction), has not produced text that licenses $p_{e_k} = q_{e_k}$ at the bottom boundary (line 548 covers a different case), and has conceded the bookend overclaim and the line-1409 overstatement. Red has cited the manuscript's own definition, the manuscript's own symmetry-breaking section's externalism language, and the standard FEP literature for the observation operator. The evidence weighs to red on the compound claim.
