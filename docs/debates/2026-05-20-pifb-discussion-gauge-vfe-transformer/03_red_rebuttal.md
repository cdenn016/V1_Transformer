## Core attack

Blue's F3 partial concession is the load-bearing failure of the subsection as published, and Blue's proposed remedy — "a one-clause cross-reference to the body's 'Identification with rotary positional structure' paragraph at line 1714" — does not discharge the tension Blue itself names.

The Discussion sentence at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3192` reads in full: "Positional encoding in standard transformers restores sequence structure through explicit embeddings. In the full framework, positional information is intrinsic to the agents' gauge frames, eliminating the need for separate positional encoding mechanisms." Blue defends this by pointing to the body at 1714, which I verified: "The natural identification of the per-token frame $U_i$ with a real transformer architecture is the per-position rotational frame of rotary positional embeddings, in which $U_i \in \mathrm{O}(d_k)$ is a block-diagonal rotation depending on token position."

This body identification breaks Blue's defense rather than rescuing it, for three reasons that compound.

(1) The body at 1714 identifies the rotary frame as $U_i \in \mathrm{O}(d_k)$ (block-diagonal rotation), which is a strict subgroup of the framework's generic $U_i \in \mathrm{GL}(d_k)$ used at 1699 and 1709. In the zero-dimensional transformer limit Section~\ref{sec:transformer_zero_dim} runs through, the gauge content available is exactly $\mathrm{O}(d_k)$ — i.e., RoPE rotations. The Discussion sentence at 3192 asserts that positional encoding is eliminated in "the full framework," but the body has just shown that in the zero-dimensional limit the rotary frame *is* the gauge structure. The Discussion sentence as written elides the distinction between (a) the full framework on a non-trivial base manifold where gauge frames carry positional information intrinsically, and (b) the zero-dimensional limit where positional information must enter through the choice of $U_i$, and that choice is realized in practice as RoPE.

(2) The implementation in `transformer/vfe/` plus `CLAUDE.md`'s "KNOWN GAP — RoPE × MahalanobisNorm" make this not an abstract distinction. The implementation uses RoPE as a separate, named, externally-imposed mechanism — `use_rope=True`, `rope_full_gauge` flag, RoPE rotating $\mu$ but not $\sigma$ — exactly what the Discussion sentence says is eliminated. Blue's proposed one-clause cross-reference to 1714 does not resolve this: it tells the reader that RoPE *can be read as* the gauge frame, but it does not retract the asserted elimination of "separate positional encoding mechanisms" in the very sentence that asserts elimination.

(3) The body itself, at line 1842 (the closing of the recovery section Blue cites in E3 as setting the epistemic ceiling), states the limits "absorb the gauge parameters into the learned matrices." Absorption-into-learned-matrices is the opposite of elimination-of-encoding-mechanisms; what happens at the recovery limit is that positional information is re-encoded *through* a separate mechanism (RoPE, learned positional embeddings, ALiBi) which is then identified post hoc with a degenerate gauge frame. The Discussion at 3192 reverses the direction of the body's claim. The body says "the gauge structure is what RoPE compresses to under aggressive limits"; the Discussion says "the framework eliminates the need for RoPE-style mechanisms." These are not the same statement, and the second does not follow from the first.

The minimum edit that discharges this is not Blue's "one-clause cross-reference." The minimum edit must (i) restrict the elimination claim to the full framework with non-trivial base manifold and $G \neq \{e\}$ explicitly, (ii) acknowledge that in the zero-dimensional transformer limit positional information enters through the choice of frame $U_i$ and is realized in the current implementation as RoPE, and (iii) cross-reference the `sec:scope_limitations` register where the RoPE × MahalanobisNorm interaction is documented as an implementation-side gap. A suggested form: "In the full framework with $G \neq \{e\}$ on a non-trivial base manifold, positional information is intrinsic to the agents' gauge frames, eliminating the need for separate positional encoding mechanisms. In the zero-dimensional transformer limit the body derives at Section~\ref{sec:transformer_zero_dim}, the gauge structure reduces to the per-token rotational frame $U_i \in \mathrm{O}(d_k)$ realized as RoPE (line 1714), and the implementation's RoPE × MahalanobisNorm interaction is registered as an open gap in Section~\ref{sec:scope_limitations}." Without (iii) in particular, the Discussion remains uncalibrated against the implementation.

A secondary live edit, smaller but still load-bearing, attaches to sub-claim 2. Blue's E2 establishes that the body at 1699-1709 supplies the carving; the Discussion at 3190 should point the reader there with a parenthetical "(under the gauge-fixed reading developed at Section~\ref{sec:transformer_zero_dim})" attached to "play the role of gauge transformations." Blue's defense reads this identification as compressed from the body; the Discussion does not currently carry the pointer that makes the compression legible to a reader who has not yet read the body.
