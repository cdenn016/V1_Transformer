# Blue Rebuttal — reduction-to-standard-transformer

## Concession

I grant red's falsifier #2 in part: standard `LayerNorm(x) = γ ⊙ (x − μ)/√(σ² + ε) + β` of [BaKirosHinton2016] with per-feature trainable `γ ∈ ℝ^{d_k}` and `β ∈ ℝ^{d_k}` does **not** make `‖μ_j‖²` exactly token-independent. A direct numerical check confirms this. With `d=6` and independent Gaussian draws of `x_1, x_2, γ, β`, the post-LN squared norms differ at order one: `‖y_1‖² = 13.31`, `‖y_2‖² = 12.36`. Only the degenerate sub-case `γ = c · 1, β = 0` (RMSNorm-like, with `‖z_j‖² = d_k` exactly) delivers literal token-independent norms (verified: `‖z_1‖² = 5.99999`, `‖z_2‖² = 5.99998`, to machine precision modulo `ε`).

The manuscript at line 1213 writes `−(1/(2σ²))‖μ_j‖² ≈ const`, with `≈` not `=`. Under modern transformer LN this approximation is `O(1)` in absolute scale, not exact. I do not defend the contrary.

This concession affects Route 2 (the `Σ → 0`, constant-gauge, dot-product route §5.2.2) and does not affect Route 1 (untied carving §5.2.1). Whether the headline claim survives therefore depends on which route the word "EXACTLY" attaches to. The remainder of this rebuttal argues that the claim survives on Route 1 and on the LayerNorm-as-gauge-quotient reading of Route 2.

## Core attack

Red's case rests on attacking the constant-gauge dot-product route (§5.2.2) and treating it as if it were the only reduction the claim asserts. The manuscript exhibits **two reduction routes**, and red's opening engages with only one of them.

The opposing-route problem is decisive. `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1022` opens §5.2 by stating: "Standard transformer attention is recovered as a special case along **either of two equivalent routes**. The first route (§5.2.1) retains `Ω_ij = U_i U_j^{-1}` non-trivially throughout and exposes untied query-key projections `Q_i = U_i^{-1} μ_i`, `K_j = U_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j` **directly from the transported KL**." The second route (§5.2.2) is the one red attacks. Red's Citation 1 (`approximate cancellation` at line 1198), Citation 2 (`full limit need not be taken` at line 1252), and Citation 4 (`absorb the gauge transport Ω into W_V` at line 1311) are all inside §5.2.2. None of them appear in §5.2.1.

Route 1 has no `‖μ_j‖²` j-bias step at all. The exact decomposition at `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1126` (`\eqref{eq:full_kl_general}`) is verified symbolically to machine precision against direct Gaussian KL (line 1133). The j-only structural terms `r_j = μ_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j`, `log det Σ_j`, `log|det U_j|` are absorbed into the **softmax prior** `log π_{ij}` (line 1154), not cancelled by an external concentration argument. Standard attention already accommodates additive j-only logit biases — that is exactly what causal masking and ALiBi inject (`external_canon_transformers.md §6`); the manuscript's recovery of these as non-uniform `π_j` is also disclosed at §4.7 lines 776–836. Red's claim that the `‖μ_j‖²` cancellation is an **approximation external to the gauge framework** misses that Route 1 absorbs the analogous j-only terms into the same `log π_{ij}` slot that standard attention reserves for additive biases. There is no approximation in this step. It is identity.

Red's Citation 2 — that "the full limit need not be taken" at line 1252 makes the reduction a change of variables rather than a limit — does not bite the headline either. A change of variables that yields a target identity is precisely an exact identification. The claim's phrasing "degenerate-covariance, infinite-precision-prior limit" is loose phrasing for the `σ → 0` regime; the manuscript itself shows that the relevant object is the combination `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}`, which is held finite under reparameterization. The map from `{σ, Ω}` parameters to `{W_Q, W_K}` parameters via `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T} = W_Q W_K^T` is a literal bijection on the image (any `M ∈ GL(d_k)` is some `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}` for some σ-Ω pair, via SVD — line 1241). The resulting boxed identity at line 1286 is a **theorem on the reparametrized parameters**, not a limit taken in any asymptotic sense. Red's appeal to [Murphy2012] "infinite-precision prior" as `Σ^{-1} → ∞I` is a vocabulary objection: the analytic limit `σ → 0` is ruled out (line 1036), but the algebraic absorption replaces it with an exact identity on the absorbed-parameter manifold. The choice of words "infinite-precision-prior limit" in the claim is imprecise; the underlying mathematics it points to is exact.

Red's Citation 3 (rectangular projection at lines 1243–1250) does not show the reduction is non-exact. It shows the reduction is **to the GL(d_head) sub-factor of the standard transformer's per-head computation**, not to the rank-deficient ambient `d_model × d_model` kernel. This is a structural disclosure about *what part of the standard transformer the gauge framework matches*, not an admission of an approximation. The Vaswani forward pass `Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` (`external_canon_transformers.md §1`) operates on `Q, K ∈ ℝ^{N × d_k}` after the rectangular projection has already collapsed the ambient `d_model` space down to `d_head` (= `d_k`). The bilinear that determines the attention weights is exactly `M_h ∈ GL(d_head)`. The manuscript's identification `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T} ↔ M_h` is therefore an identification between the gauge bilinear and the **invertible bilinear the standard transformer actually uses on the head-space**. The thin-SVD lift `W_Q = U_Q A_Q`, `W_K = U_K A_K` is not "post-hoc"; it is the standard parameterization of any rank-r matrix as the product of an isometric embedding and an invertible head-space map. Red's framing — that lifting the head-space identification through isometric embeddings makes the reduction "to a sub-form" — confuses the invertible-bilinear computation that determines β with the isometric embedding that determines which subspace of `d_model` carries that bilinear. The claim concerns the attention operation, which the manuscript recovers exactly on the head subspace it acts on.

Red's Citation 5 — the manuscript's own "set of limiting cases" framing at line 992 and "deliberately aggressive" disclosure at line 1337 — does not say what red needs it to say. "Discards a specific geometric degree of freedom" means *the gauge framework contains structure that standard attention forgets*, not that the limit produces an approximation of standard attention. Forgetting is exact; an approximation is not. A surjective projection from a larger space onto a smaller space is well-defined and produces every element of the smaller space exactly. The manuscript at line 1339 then explicitly says the standard transformer is "the three-limit simplification" of the gauge framework — a categorical, not approximate, identification.

## Defense

**Route 1 carries the claim without invoking `‖μ_j‖²` cancellation.** Under arbitrary SPD covariances `Σ_i` (no isotropy assumption), `Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1126` writes the exact transported Gaussian KL as
```
D_KL(q_i ‖ Ω_{ij} q_j) = ½[log det Σ_j − log det Σ_i + 2 log|det U_i| − 2 log|det U_j|
                        − d_k + tr(H_j P_i) + x_i^T H_j x_i − 2 x_i^T k_j + r_j]
```
with `x_i = U_i^{-1} μ_i`, `H_j = U_j^T Σ_j^{-1} U_j`, `k_j = U_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j`, `r_j = μ_j^T Σ_j^{-1} μ_j`. The cross term `−2 x_i^T k_j = −2 Q_i^T K_j` is the only `(i,j)`-coupling through the means, and it is exactly the dot-product score `Q_i^T K_j` of [Vaswani2017]. The j-only terms `r_j, log det Σ_j, log|det U_j|` go into the standard softmax prior bias slot. The covariance-coupling terms `tr(H_j P_i)` and `x_i^T H_j x_i` vanish exactly under the closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` (line 1155), yielding `Q_i = C^{-1/2} U_i^{-1} μ_i`, `K_j = C^{-1/2} U_j^{-1} μ_j` and standard attention with shared bilinear `C^{-1}`. The closure is a single algebraic constraint on the covariance field; under it, Route 1 produces the Vaswani forward pass with no concentration argument and no LN approximation. The decomposition itself is verified symbolically to machine precision (line 1133).

**The LayerNorm-as-gauge-quotient reading delivers exact cancellation on Route 2.** The manuscript at lines 1218–1225 gives the algebraic reading of LN: `gl(K) = sl(K) ⊕ ℝ`, and LN "effectively quotients out the positive scaling degree of freedom from the embedding space, projecting representations onto a sub-manifold of constant norm." Under the RMSNorm-like degenerate LN (`γ = c · 1`, `β = 0`) — which the numerical check confirms produces `‖z_j‖² = d_k` to machine precision — the j-bias `−(1/(2σ²))‖μ_j‖²` is **literally constant in j**, and softmax shift-invariance gives exact cancellation. This is not an approximation; it is the standard softmax identity `softmax(x + c · 1) = softmax(x)`. Red's falsifier #2 demands that the standard `γ, β` LN deliver exact constancy. Granted, it does not. But the gauge-theoretic reading is that the *relevant* LN — the one that makes the gauge quotient exact — is the constant-norm projection, and modern transformers approximate this via the full LN with learned `γ, β`. The reduction is exact under the constant-norm quotient (which the framework derives from `gl(K) = sl(K) ⊕ ℝ`); modern LN is the engineering approximation to that geometric projection. The direction of approximation runs from standard transformer LN *toward* the gauge identity, not from the gauge identity *toward* standard attention.

**The value-aggregation step is exact under constant gauge, which is part of the claim.** The claim explicitly invokes "the flat-bundle … limit". A flat connection with single global frame is the constant-gauge specialization `Ω_{ij} = Ω` (line 1115). Under this specialization, line 1296's `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_{ij} Ω_{ij} μ_j = Ω · Σ_j β_{ij} μ_j` pulls `Ω` out of the sum and absorbs it into `W_V`, yielding `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_{ij} V_j` with `V_j = W_V^T μ_j` exactly. Red's Citation 4 frames this as a "further restriction beyond flat-bundle framing" — it is not. The flat connection with no holonomy on a single-point base space (`Attention/GL(K)_attention.tex:1026`: "a single-point base space `C`") *is* the constant-gauge case. There is no separate restriction. The claim names the flat-bundle limit; the manuscript performs that limit; the value aggregation is exact.

**The τ = √d_k step is exact under the variance argument the standard transformer itself uses.** The manuscript at lines 1273–1281 reproduces the dimensional-variance argument of [Vaswani2017 §3.2.1] verbatim: dot products with unit-variance components have variance `d_k`, so dividing by `√d_k` restores unit variance. This is the standard's own justification for `τ = √d_k`. The user's broader framework `τ = κ √K` with learnable κ (line 855) corresponds to `κ = 1`, as the manuscript explicitly states. Recovering `τ = √d_k` exactly at `κ = 1` is an identity, not an approximation.

**Falsification conditions for my position (carried forward).** The claim is *not* defensible if (a) Route 1's covariance-closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T` introduces a hidden non-equivariance — it does not; it is a covariance-field choice that respects the gauge action by construction (line 1158: this closure holds automatically for RoPE-style `U_i ∈ O(d_k)`); (b) the `M_h ∈ GL(d_head)` identification fails to determine the bilinear computation that the standard transformer's per-head attention performs — it does not fail; `M_h = A_Q A_K^T` is the invertible head-space factor of any factorization of `W_Q W_K^T`; (c) the softmax-prior absorption `log π_{ij}` is not algebraically equivalent to standard additive logit biases — they are equivalent by `softmax(x + log π) ∝ π · softmax_input(x)` (`external_canon_transformers.md §6`).

The honest verdict the manuscript supports: standard attention is recovered exactly along Route 1 under the covariance closure `Σ_j = U_j C U_j^T`, and exactly along Route 2 under the constant-norm gauge quotient (which standard LN approximates). The word "EXACTLY" in the claim survives on Route 1; on Route 2 it survives under the constant-norm reading of LN. Red's case fastens on the modern-LN approximation in Route 2 and ignores Route 1 entirely.
