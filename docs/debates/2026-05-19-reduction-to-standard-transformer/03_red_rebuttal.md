# Red Rebuttal — reduction-to-standard-transformer

## Concession

I grant blue's points (1)-(2) and (5)-(7) from §Evidence: the closed-form Gaussian KL at line 1043 is the textbook identity (`[BleiKuckelbirgJordan2017; KingmaWelling2014 App. B]`, simplified by `tr((σ²ΩΩ^T)^{-1} σ² I) = tr((ΩΩ^T)^{-1})` and `log|σ²ΩΩ^T|/|σ²I| = log det ΩΩ^T`); the Mahalanobis identity at line 1049 is exact for any invertible Ω; softmax shift-invariance at lines 1184-1188 is a textbook fact; the SVD factorisation existence at line 1241 is constructive; and the constant-gauge pullout of Ω from `μ̂_i = Σ_j β_{ij} Ω_{ij} μ_j` (line 1296 → line 1316) is an exact pull when, and only when, the constant-gauge specialisation is assumed.

Blue's sympy demonstrations of steps (i) (KL form) and (ii) (Mahalanobis identity) verify what blue says they verify. I am not contesting those algebraic identities.

## Core attack

Blue's defense collapses on a single move: the conversion at §Evidence (3)/(9) and §Pre-empting / falsifier (3) of "approximate under concentration" (the manuscript's own word at `GL(K)_attention.tex:1198`) into "**exact** under standard layer normalization." This is the load-bearing step on which blue's "exact reduction" rests — and it is false against the textual record and against the canonical definition of LayerNorm.

The manuscript itself, at line 1198, writes the unhedged sentence: "Two well-trodden mechanisms ensure its **approximate** cancellation," and at line 1213 writes `−(1/2σ²)||μ_j||² ≈ (constant in j)`, using the binary relation `≈`, not `=`. Line 1271 repeats: `s̃_ij = Q_i K_j^T + O(1)`, where "`O(1)` represents the approximately constant key bias (controlled by layer normalization)." Line 1289 then states the boxed softmax holds "**up to the key-norm bias term that cancels under the layer normalization or high-dimensional concentration conditions**." The manuscript itself records the cancellation as approximate even under LN; this is not a rhetorical hedge.

Blue's claim that LN makes `||μ_j||²` "bitwise constant across j" `(02_blue_opening.md §Pre-empting)` is wrong as a definition of LayerNorm. Per `external_canon_transformers.md §4`, the canonical LayerNorm is
```
LayerNorm(x)_i = γ_i · (x_i - μ) / √(σ² + ε) + β_i,
```
with **learnable per-feature affine** `γ_i, β_i` `[BaKirosHinton2016]`. Pre-affine, the projection onto the centred unit-norm sphere makes `||x − (1/d)1·1^T x||² = d` (constant), but the post-affine output `γ ⊙ x_norm + β` does *not* have constant norm across rows in general: for two tokens with normalised vectors `x̂_j` and `x̂_k`, `||γ ⊙ x̂_j + β||² − ||γ ⊙ x̂_k + β||² = (γ ⊙ (x̂_j + x̂_k) + 2β)^T (γ ⊙ (x̂_j − x̂_k))`, which is not zero unless `γ` is constant and `β = 0`. Vaswani 2017 LN does not constrain `γ` to be constant.

This kills the "exact under standard LN" precondition that blue moved the approximation into. Blue's falsifier (3) anticipated exactly this: "If red constructs an LN that does not enforce `||μ_j||² = const` across tokens (e.g., per-token LN with learnable affine that varies across tokens, which is *not* the standard LN), then line 1213 ceases to hold and the cancellation reverts to approximate." Standard `[BaKirosHinton2016]` LN has *per-feature* learnable `γ, β`; these break the cross-token norm equality once the input passes through the affine. The standard LN is the LN with affine — that is the one published, the one in PyTorch, and the one Vaswani 2017 uses. Blue therefore needs an LN variant that is not the standard, which is the falsification condition blue itself wrote.

Blue's fallback — the concentration-of-measure argument at line 1204 (`||μ_j||² = d_k σ_0² ± O(σ_0² √d_k)`) — is `O(1/√d_k)` approximate and exact only in the `d_k → ∞` limit. The headline claim asks that "every intermediate step [be] mathematically valid" (00_claim.md, line 11). A step whose validity requires a limit `d_k → ∞` that is *not* part of the stated three limits (isotropic Σ, flat Ω, σ→0 absorbed) is an additional, undisclosed limit. Blue's "exact under stated limits" framing therefore secretly requires a fourth limit (`d_k → ∞` or `LN-with-γ-suppressed`) that the manuscript does not name.

A secondary load-bearing weakness: blue's defense of the rectangular-projection caveat `(02_blue_opening.md §6, falsifier 5)` argues "I argue these are the same thing — Vaswani 2017's multi-head construction *is* a stack of rank-`d_head` head factors." This is a substitution of the headline. The headline (`00_claim.md` line 11) is "standard scaled dot-product transformer attention, as defined in Vaswani et al. (2017) §3.2.1." Vaswani §3.2.1 is the single-head scaled-dot-product attention `softmax(QK^T/√d_k) V` with `Q, K ∈ ℝ^{N × d_k}` from learned `W_Q, W_K ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_k}` (`external_canon_transformers.md §1`, lines 13-23). The ambient-space logit kernel for that construction is `W_Q W_K^T ∈ ℝ^{d_model × d_model}`, of rank ≤ d_k = d_head — not in `GL(d_model)`. The manuscript itself records at line 1243 that "neither is individually invertible in the d_model-dimensional space" and that "the ambient-space logit kernel ... is not an element of GL(d_model)." The gauge-theoretic identification is then explicitly displaced to the *invertible head-space factor* `M_h^a := A_Q^a (A_K^a)^T ∈ GL(d_head)` (line 1246) — a strict sub-object obtained via thin SVD. The reduction therefore lands not at "Vaswani §3.2.1 attention" but at the invertible factor of its low-rank kernel. Pitfall 10 of `external_canon_transformers.md` flags exactly this: "The user's GL(K) gauge transformer has *no* learned QKV projections — attention emerges from the KL on gauge-transported beliefs. Standard transformers have learned QKV. When comparing, ensure the comparison is apples-to-apples." Identifying `σ^{-2} Ω^{-T}` with `M_h^a` and not with `W_Q W_K^T` is not apples-to-apples.

Blue's sympy verifications at §Evidence (1)-(3) do not rescue this. They verify the algebraic identities at lines 1043 and 1049, and the softmax shift-invariance at lines 1184-1188. They do not verify (a) the key-norm cancellation at line 1198, (b) the `||μ_j||² = const` LN claim at line 1213, or (c) that the head-space factor `M_h^a` is equivalent to the standard `W_Q W_K^T`. A passing sympy test for the algebraic steps is evidence that those algebraic steps are correct; it is not evidence that the chain composing them produces an exact reduction to Vaswani §3.2.1.

## Defense

My opening's position survives blue's argument because blue concedes the load-bearing approximation in advance (`02_blue_opening.md §Pre-empting`, first paragraph): "I concede the wording: the cancellation is *approximate in absence of layer normalisation*." Blue then attempts to relocate the approximation into the precondition (LN), but the canonical LN definition (`external_canon_transformers.md §4` line 69, citing `[BaKirosHinton2016]`) does not enforce cross-token norm equality after the affine, so the relocation fails.

The honest reading of `GL(K)_attention.tex` §5.2.2 supports my opening's position. The manuscript itself frames the section at line 992 with the verb "emerges as a set of limiting cases" — not "is mathematically identical to." Line 1337 records that "The limits are deliberately aggressive. They collapse the statistical manifold into a basic Euclidean space and absorb the gauge parameters into the learned matrices. Each limit discards a specific geometric degree of freedom and each can be independently relaxed." This is the manuscript's own characterisation of the reduction as a controlled approximation under aggressive limits, not as an exact identity.

The headline claim (`00_claim.md` line 11) demands two things simultaneously: (i) "recovered exactly (not approximately)" and (ii) "every intermediate step mathematically valid." Step 9 in `01_evidence.md` (line 33) — the key-norm bias cancellation at lines 1196-1225 — is documented by the manuscript itself with the words "approximately cancels." Step 11 (`01_evidence.md` line 35) — the rectangular-projection caveat at lines 1243-1250 — is documented by the manuscript itself as a displacement of the identification onto the invertible head-space factor `M_h^a`, not onto the standard Vaswani kernel. Either step alone falsifies "every intermediate step mathematically valid as an exact reduction to Vaswani 2017 §3.2.1."

The defensible claim — and the one the manuscript actually makes at line 992 and line 1337 — is "exact under aggressive limits, with one approximate cancellation conditioned on a fourth precondition (LN-without-affine, concentration-of-measure, or `d_k → ∞`), reducing to the invertible head-space factor of Vaswani §3.2.1." That is a substantively different claim from the headline. The headline asks for exactness; the manuscript and the algebra together deliver exact-under-aggressive-limits-and-an-approximation. Those are not the same statement, and on the headline's wording, the reduction is approximate, not exact.
