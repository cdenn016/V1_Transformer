# Blue Opening — pifb-spec-1-pullback

## Steelman (opposing position)

A speculative-extensions section that takes a real-valued, gauge-non-invariant quadratic form `κ(A_μ, A_ν)` and identifies it as a "metric on the noumenal substrate" — while citing Wheeler, Lloyd, Rovelli, Page–Wootters, Connes–Rovelli, Jacobson, Van Raamsdonk in a single sentence — is doing more interpretive work than the disclaimers can carry, and the structural-tier identification of "perceived space" with `G_i^{(s)}` reads as a philosophical assertion about Friston/Hoffman that those authors do not in fact make.

## Position

Under the binding operational reading of "rock-solid" (math correct, canon correctly invoked, disclaimers complete and honest, no speculative claim dressed as derivation), lines 2584–2772 pass. Each load-bearing mathematical object matches a canonical form in `[AmariNagaoka2000]`, `[Nakahara2003 Ch. 10]`, or `[Amari1998]`. Each speculative inference (bit-counting time, Planck-time link, structural-tier identification of perceived space, within-species consensus) is labeled as proposal, outlook, or toy model — not as derivation. The single most dangerous failure mode for a section like this — burying gauge non-invariance under a Yang-Mills escape hatch — is explicitly refused at line 2723 with the correct mathematical reason (`F_{μν} = 0` in Regime I, so `tr(FF)` supplies no escape).

## Evidence

**1. Fisher–Rao on the Gaussian fiber (2673) matches canon verbatim.** The manuscript writes
`g_B(δq, δq) = δμᵀ Σ⁻¹ δμ + ½ tr(Σ⁻¹ δΣ Σ⁻¹ δΣ)`.
This is the standard closed-form Fisher–Rao metric for the Gaussian family, identical to `[AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]` and to the Gaussian-KL second-order expansion in `external_canon_math.md §1` ("KL is the second-order expansion of the Fisher metric"). No reparameterization sleight-of-hand; the score-function identity at 2713 (`g_B(∇_μ q, ∇_ν q) = E_q[(∇_μ log q)(∇_ν log q)]`) is the canonical definition `g_{ij}(θ) = E[∂_i log p · ∂_j log p]` from `[AmariNagaoka2000 Ch. 2]`, `external_canon_math.md §1 (Fisher information metric)`.

**2. Natural-gradient forms (2617) match `[Amari1998]`.** The expressions `~∇_μ F = Σ (∇_μ F)` and `~∇_Σ F = 2 Σ (∇_Σ F) Σ` are the canonical Gaussian natural gradients (inverse Fisher applied to Euclidean gradient). The `2Σ(·)Σ` sandwich keeps the update in `S⁺_K` automatically — a property derived in `[Amari1998 §4]` and reproduced in `external_canon_math.md §1 (Natural gradient)`: `~∇ L = G⁻¹ ∇L`. The group-level retraction `U^{t+1} = U^t exp(-η ~∇_φ F)` at Eq. `gauge_group_retraction` is the standard exponential-map retraction on a matrix Lie group (`[AbsilMahonySepulchre2008]`, `[Bonnabel2013]`), preserving `U_i ∈ G` exactly at every step.

**3. Horizontal–vertical decomposition (2682–2685) is the canonical principal-connection split.** The manuscript writes `T_{(c,q)} E_q = H_{(c,q)} ⊕ V_{(c,q)}` with `V ≅ T_q B` and `H` the horizontal lift of `T_c C` specified by the connection one-form `A^{(i)} = U_i⁻¹ dU_i`. This is the textbook decomposition in `[Nakahara2003 §10.1]` (principal-bundle connection) and `[KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.1–§II.5]`, transferred to the associated bundle via `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`. The bundle metric in Eq. `bundle_metric` is the standard "horizontal-vertical sum": horizontal block `κ(A_μ, A_ν)` (a Lie-algebra Gram form pulled back through the section) plus vertical block `g_B` (Fisher–Rao on the fiber). Both blocks are pulled back through the section `σ_i^{(q)}` to land on `C`. This is a genuine fiber-respecting metric on `E_q` in the sense of `[KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §IV]` (Riemannian metric on a fiber bundle = base metric ⊕ fiber metric in a chosen connection split), not an `L²` pullback of densities — the manuscript explicitly says so at 2680.

**4. The piecewise `+tr/−tr` convention at 2697–2700 is mathematically correct.** For `X, Y ∈ so(K)` (real skew-symmetric), `tr(XY) ≤ 0`, so `−tr(XY)` is positive semidefinite (positive definite on the simple compact part). This is the standard inner product on the compact real form (`[Helgason, Differential Geometry, Lie Groups, and Symmetric Spaces, Ch. III §6]`; equivalent to the Frobenius `⟨X,Y⟩_F = tr(Xᵀ Y) = −tr(XY)` for skew-symmetric `X, Y`). The Killing-form proportionality `B(X,Y) = (K−2) tr(XY)` for `so(K)`, `K > 2` is the standard formula (`[Knapp, Lie Groups Beyond an Introduction, Ch. II §1]`); restricted to skew-symmetric matrices this is a negative scalar multiple of the positive-definite Frobenius form, consistent with the Killing form being negative-definite on the compact real form. For non-compact `gl(K, C)`, the bare `+tr(AB)` gives an indefinite form, matching the worked signature example. The manuscript at 2720 explicitly addresses the trap: "for skew-symmetric `A^{(i)} ∈ so(N)` one has `tr(A²) ≤ 0`, so a bare `+tr(A^{(i)}_μ A^{(i)}_ν)` would produce a negative-semidefinite horizontal contribution and break the Riemannian character of the body construction." This is the exact failure mode a sloppy author would hide; the manuscript declares it.

**5. Gauge non-invariance is disclosed with the correct Maurer–Cartan transformation law (2722).** Under `U_i → U_i g(c)`, `A^{(i)} = U_i⁻¹ dU_i → g⁻¹ A^{(i)} g + g⁻¹ dg`. This is the textbook gauge-transformation law for a connection one-form on a principal bundle (`[Nakahara2003 §10.4]`, `[KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.1]`). The manuscript then explicitly refuses the Yang-Mills escape: in Regime I the connection is pure-gauge (`A = U⁻¹ dU`), so the curvature `F = dA + ½[A,A]` satisfies the Maurer–Cartan structure equation `dA = −½[A,A]` and hence `F ≡ 0`, making `tr(FF)` identically zero. This is mathematically correct (`[KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §II.5]`, Maurer–Cartan structure equations). The manuscript routes invariance to the consensus construction (forward-referenced) and labels the bundle metric as "metric pulled back within an agent's gauge fixing" at 2723. This is the honest move — declare the non-invariance, refuse the spurious escape hatch, route the invariance claim elsewhere where it can be argued or fail honestly.

**6. The BCH error term at 2649 is correctly stated.** The chart-coordinate update `dφ/dt = −η ~∇_φ F + O(‖η ~∇‖²)` follows from the Lie-group BCH formula `log(exp(A) exp(B)) = A + B + ½[A,B] + (1/12)([A,[A,B]] − [B,[A,B]]) + ...` (`[Hall, Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations, Ch. 5]`; `external_canon_math.md §2 (Lie algebra exponential)`). The leading BCH correction is order-two in the step size and vanishes when `[A,B] = 0`. The manuscript's claim that the truncation is "exact in the abelian sector and a first-order approximation otherwise" is what BCH gives.

**8. The Wheeler / Lloyd / Rovelli / Page–Wootters / Connes–Rovelli citation cluster (2592) is correctly used.** Wheeler 1990 "Information, Physics, Quantum" does propose information as ontologically primary; the "time prevents everything from happening at once" phrase is widely associated with Wheeler though it antedates him in folk physics, and the manuscript hedges with "popular formulation ... associated with Wheeler." Lloyd 2002 "Computational capacity of the universe" does bound the universe's evolution by its computational capacity. Rovelli's relational interpretation makes time relational, not background. Page–Wootters 1983 (Phys. Rev. D 27, 2885) does recover time from clock-subsystem entanglement. Connes–Rovelli 1994 does identify modular flow as intrinsic time. Jacobson 1995 derives Einstein equations from horizon thermodynamics. Van Raamsdonk 2010 connects entanglement and spatial connectivity. The manuscript groups these as "broad neighborhood" — not as supporting the construction, but as parallel proposals. The closing sentence at 2592 explicitly differentiates the manuscript's construction from each: "The construction here differs from each of these in placing the temporal parameter on the agent's own belief trajectory rather than on a clock subsystem entangled with the rest of the universe, on an external observer's modular flow, or on a horizon thermodynamic state." This is the correct discipline.

**9. The structural-tier proposal at 2749 is framed as proposal, not derivation, and the supporting citations track the actual literature.** Friston 2017 ("Active inference: a process theory" and subsequent hierarchical formulations) distinguishes Bayesian parameter learning (slow, structural) from Bayesian belief updating (fast, state-level) — the manuscript's `G^{(s)}` vs `G^{(q)}` distinction is exactly the slow-parameter vs fast-belief decomposition `[FristonEtAl2017]`, `external_canon_inference.md §3 (Hierarchical / nested formulations)`. The identification "perceived space ≈ structural tier rather than epistemic tier" is an interpretive proposal that aligns with what these authors do say, framed as a proposal at 2749 ("We propose that...").

## Falsification conditions

This blue position is wrong if any of the following hold:

1. **Killing-form proportionality is wrong.** If the formula `B(X,Y) = (K−2) tr(XY)` for `so(K)`, `K > 2` is off by a sign or scalar that the manuscript hides, or if the "proportional to `−tr(XY)`" claim does not survive the sign of `(K−2)` for `K > 2` (the proportionality factor is negative, which the manuscript glosses with "proportional to"). A red strike lands if Helgason III §6 or Knapp II §1 gives a different formula.

2. **Bundle metric `g_E` (2688) fails to be fiber-respecting.** If the horizontal–vertical sum is not block-orthogonal under the canonical connection split, the "fiber-respecting metric on E_q" label is wrong and the construction collapses to the `L²` pullback the manuscript explicitly says it is not. Red can falsify by exhibiting a tangent vector `X ∈ T_{(c,q)} E_q` whose horizontal and vertical components fail to be `g_E`-orthogonal in the canonical split of `[Nakahara2003 §10.3]`.

3. **Citation misrepresentation in the 2592 cluster.** If Connes–Rovelli 1994 does not in fact identify modular flow as intrinsic time, or if Page–Wootters 1983 does not in fact recover time from clock-subsystem entanglement, or if Jacobson 1995 does not derive Einstein equations from horizon thermodynamics — the citation cluster is then bad. A red strike lands on direct fetch of any of these papers showing the claim attached to them in 2592 is not what they say. (Empirically checkable via WebFetch on the arXiv versions of these papers; Page–Wootters and Connes–Rovelli are pre-arXiv but their abstracts and standard summaries are well-documented.)

4. **Structural-tier identification overreaches Friston/Hoffman.** If Friston 2017 does *not* place "structural learning" at the parameter level (i.e., if Friston's "structural" terminology means something else in the cited paper), the citation at 2751 fails. If Hoffman 2019's interface-theory thesis does not in fact locate the interface at a slow-timescale evolved structure (vs. moment-to-moment perception), the "sharpens this further" claim at 2751 fails. (Empirically checkable: WebFetch Hoffman 2019 abstract / FristonEtAl2017 abstract.)

5. **The BCH error term `O(‖η ~∇‖²)` is wrong by an order.** If the leading BCH correction is order three rather than order two for the specific update structure at 2647–2649, the truncation-order claim fails. (Empirically checkable via sympy expansion of `log(exp(φ) exp(−η ~∇))` around `η = 0`.)

6. **The pure-gauge `F = 0` claim at 2723 fails in Regime I.** If under Regime I the connection is not in fact pure-gauge, or if a pure-gauge connection has nonzero curvature (contradicting Maurer–Cartan), the refusal of the Yang-Mills escape is unsound. (Standard mathematics: pure-gauge `A = U⁻¹ dU` gives `F = dA + ½[A,A] = 0` by the structure equation, so this is hard to falsify, but if Regime I is defined differently in `sec:connection_forms` than the manuscript implies, the local refusal is malformed.)

7. **A speculative claim is dressed as derivation somewhere I missed.** If a single sentence between 2584 and 2772 asserts as derived a claim that is only speculative — for example, if the within-species-consensus invariance is presented as proved rather than conditional on a regulator — the operational-reading criterion (4) fails. (I read the section closely; the candidate sentences I identified all carry their disclaimers. Red can falsify by exhibiting a specific sentence.)

Item 7 is the hardest for blue: the section is long and an attacker who quotes a single load-bearing sentence as overstated, where the disclaimer is several lines away or in a different subsection, can land a strike under the operational reading. The defense rests on the explicit framing at 2582 and the subsection-level disclaimers being read as governing their respective subsections.

Items 3, 4, and 5 are empirically checkable now. The strongest red attack is likely on the citation cluster at 2592 or on the structural-tier identification at 2749–2751 — both of those depend on what the cited authors actually say, which is the source of truth and not the manuscript itself.
