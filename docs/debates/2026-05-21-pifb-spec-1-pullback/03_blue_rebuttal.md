# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-spec-1-pullback

## Concession

I concede the **BCH order estimate at PIFB:2649 is sub-canonical** as written, and I concede the **Hoffman 2019 attribution at PIFB:2751 is over-attributed**. Both strikes land.

**On the BCH order (Strike 1).** Red's sympy verification is correct on its own terms: with `phi, g in so(3)` of unit Frobenius norm, the leading BCH correction `(1/2)[phi, g]` has Frobenius norm `sqrt(2)/2`, scaling as `Theta(||phi|| · ||g||)` in each argument separately. Substituting `g -> eta · ~∇_phi F`, the leading correction is `O(eta · ||phi|| · ||~∇||)`. At fixed `phi` of generic O(1) size, this is **first order in `eta · ~∇`, not second order**. The manuscript line `O(||eta_phi ~∇_phi F||^2)` at PIFB:2649 is therefore wrong as stated, and it is **internally inconsistent** with the qualifier "exact in the abelian sector" at PIFB:2651 — a commutator-based correction is linear in its commutator arguments, not quadratic, so the two clauses cannot simultaneously hold under the natural reading.

The correct canonical form, derivable from the standard `dexp` formula [Hall *Lie Groups, Lie Algebras, and Representations* Ch. 5, ext. canon `external_canon_math.md` §2 "Lie algebra exponential"], is

```
dphi/dt = dexp_phi^{-1}(-eta ~∇) = -eta ~∇ + (1/2)[phi, eta ~∇] - (1/12)[phi,[phi, eta ~∇]] + ...
```

The discrete additive update `phi^{t+1} = phi^t - eta ~∇` is the truncation that **drops** the `(1/2)[phi, eta ~∇]` term; the discrepancy between the additive chart step and the exact log of `U^{t+1} = exp(phi^t) exp(-eta ~∇)` is `O(eta · ||phi|| · ||~∇||)` in the non-abelian sector, vanishing identically in the abelian sector. The manuscript needs either (i) the order replaced with `O(eta · ||phi|| · ||~∇||)` (first-order, but suppressed when `||phi||` is small), or (ii) the explicit clarification that this is the discrepancy between the additive chart update and the group-level exact retraction Eq. (eq:gauge_group_retraction), not a property of the canonical update itself.

What does survive: the **group-level retraction `U^{t+1} = U^t exp(-eta · ~∇_phi F)` at Eq. (eq:gauge_group_retraction)** is exact at the group level — there is no error in `eta · ~∇` at all there. The error lives in the choice of `phi(t+1) = phi(t) - eta · ~∇` as the additive chart approximation. The canonical theoretical update is the group-level form, with chart truncation as an implementation specialization. This is also what PIFB:2651 says in its final sentence. So the underlying content is canonical; the order estimate in Eq. 2649 mislabels which approximation is being made.

**On the Hoffman 2019 attribution (Strike 2).** Direct check of Hoffman's *The Case Against Reality* (W. W. Norton, 2019; reviewed at Publishers Weekly and Springer's *Biological Theory*) confirms that the book's load-bearing technical content is the **Fitness-Beats-Truth (FBT) theorem** of Prakash, Stephens, Hoffman, Singh, & Fields — an evolutionary-decision-theoretic result that organisms whose perceptions track fitness payoffs strictly dominate those that estimate true world states. The book's framing is the desktop-icon metaphor: perception as a species-level interface optimized by natural selection, not a faithful depiction of mind-independent reality. **Hoffman 2019 is silent on the architectural distinction between slow-timescale generative-model parameter learning and moment-to-moment posterior state inference** — it does not adjudicate whether the interface is carried by `s_i` or by `q_i` in a hierarchical-Bayesian decomposition, because the FBT argument is not framed in those Bayesian-architectural terms. The Stevens Handbook synthesis (Hoffman, "Interface Theory of Perception," https://sites.socsci.uci.edu/~ddhoff/Hoffman-Stevens-Handbook.pdf), reviewed for slow/fast-state language, contains no such distinction either.

The clause at PIFB:2751 "the carrier of that interface is precisely the slow-timescale generative model rather than the moment-to-moment posterior" is therefore the manuscript's own three-tier identification, not a Hoffman claim. The wrong-domain charge sticks under the `external_canon_inference.md` §6 pitfall ("specific architectural implementations require explicit generative-model specification, not invocation of a top-level principle that does not articulate them"). The fix is to reword:

> "The Interface-Theory reading [Hoffman2019] frames perceived space as an evolved species-specific interface optimized for fitness rather than a faithful depiction of an agent-independent geometry. Within the present framework's hierarchical decomposition, we identify the carrier of that interface with the slow-timescale generative model rather than the moment-to-moment posterior; this identification is internal to the framework, not derivable from Hoffman 2019."

The same applies to the [Friston2017] citation in the next sentence: hierarchical active inference does distinguish state-level inference from parameter-level learning (this is canonical [Friston2017]), but the *identification of perceived spatial structure with `s_i` specifically* is again an internal framework move, not a Friston conclusion. The Friston citation is closer to canonical than the Hoffman one, but the manuscript currently phrases both attributions as if the authors had drawn the structural conclusion themselves.

## Core attack

Red's third strike — **"the bundle metric Eq. (eq:bundle_metric) is a metric on the trivialization, not on the abstract bundle E_q"** — overreaches. Red conflates two distinct objects:

1. **The horizontal distribution `H ⊂ TP`** is intrinsic to the principal bundle once a connection is chosen, in the precise sense that `H` is right-equivariant under the G-action on P and is independent of which local trivialization is used to *express* it [Kobayashi & Nomizu Vol. I §II.3-II.5; Wikipedia "Connection (principal bundle)" §"Connection 1-form" confirms: "the underlying horizontal distribution remains intrinsic to the principal bundle structure — the geometric object survives the transformation"].

2. **The connection one-form `A = U_i^{-1} dU_i ∈ Ω^1(C; g)`** is the **pullback of the principal connection by the section `U_i`**. As a section-pulled-back object, it transforms inhomogeneously under gauge change `U_i → U_i g(c)`: `A → g^{-1} A g + g^{-1} dg` [Kobayashi & Nomizu Vol. I §II.5 Prop. 5.4; Nakahara §10.3]. This is the *standard* gauge-potential transformation law — it is not a defect of the construction, it is the definition of the connection's section-dependence.

The bundle metric `g_E` of Eq. (eq:bundle_metric) is well-defined as a metric on `E_q` **once both a connection and a base-manifold metric are fixed**, by the standard Riemannian-submersion construction [Kobayashi & Nomizu Vol. I §III; standard fact: "Given a Riemannian metric on a base space, there exists a unique Riemannian metric on a principal bundle such that the bundle projection map is a Riemannian submersion with totally geodesic fibers, and the horizontal distribution is the orthogonal complement of the vertical distribution"]. The manuscript's construction differs from the textbook Riemannian-submersion setup in that it **derives** the base-metric component `g_C^tw = kappa(A_mu, A_nu)` from the connection itself, rather than taking a pre-existing base metric as input. This is a coherent geometric construction — it produces a bona-fide (0,2)-tensor on `E_q`, with vertical block `g_B` (Fisher-Rao on the fiber) and horizontal block `g_C^tw` (frame-twist on the base, pulled along the section to live on `E_q` via the horizontal distribution). The bundle metric **does** live on `E_q`; what red conflates is "lives on `E_q`" with "is gauge-invariant on `E_q`."

The gauge-non-invariance disclosure at PIFB:2722-2723 is therefore not a confession that the construction defines a metric only on the trivialization — it is a disclosure that **the bundle metric on `E_q` itself depends on the choice of section `U_i`**, in the same sense that the connection one-form `A` depends on the section. Different sections yield different metrics on the same `E_q`. The manuscript is explicit about this at line 2723: "different gauge fixings of the same agent yield different horizontal contributions, which is consistent with the framework's treatment of distinct gauge frames as generating distinct pulled-back geometries." This is the correct geometric statement; red rebrands it as a fatal flaw, but it is the manuscript's own claim, properly disclosed.

The genuinely sharp version of red's attack would be: "If `g_E` depends on `U_i`, then the pulled-back `G_i^{(q)}` on `C` is `U_i`-dependent, and the section is *also* `U_i`-dependent, so the construction has two coupled `U_i`-dependencies and cannot deliver an observer-independent geometry on `C`." But the manuscript anticipates exactly this: the "consensus metric / Haar-averaged construction of Section sec:consensus_metric" at PIFB:2723 is the explicit mechanism intended to integrate out the `U_i`-dependence. Whether that consensus construction succeeds is a separate sub-debate (debate `pifb-spec-2-consensus`, partitioned out by the user). Within the scope of `pifb-spec-1`, the bundle metric is well-defined on `E_q` modulo section choice, and the manuscript discloses the section dependence explicitly.

## Defense

The strongest single defense of the section as publication-ready is that **the three honest moves the manuscript actually makes are canonically supported**:

1. **The Fisher-Rao fiber metric at Eq. 2673** — `g_B(δq, δq) = δμᵀ Σ⁻¹ δμ + (1/2) tr(Σ⁻¹ δΣ Σ⁻¹ δΣ)` — is the canonical Gaussian Fisher-Rao form [Amari & Nagaoka 2000 §2.2; Cencov 1972 uniqueness theorem; `external_canon_math.md` §1 "Fisher information metric"]. The pullback along a smooth section is the standard construction of an induced metric on the base; in this sense the score-function expression at Eq. 2713 is the canonical pullback of `g_B` along `σ_i^{(q)}` and earns the name "Fisher-Rao pullback" in exactly the sense Amari & Nagaoka use it for parameterized families [AmariNagaoka2000 §3.5].

2. **The group-level natural-gradient retraction at Eq. (eq:gauge_group_retraction)** — `U_i^{t+1} = U_i^t exp(-eta_phi · ~∇_{phi_i} F)` — is the canonical Lie-group natural-gradient update [Amari 1998 §4 for the natural-gradient definition `~∇F = G⁻¹ ∇F`; standard Lie-group retraction via right-invariant exponential, see e.g. Absil, Mahony, Sepulchre *Optimization Algorithms on Matrix Manifolds* Ch. 4]. The piece red attacks is the chart-coordinate truncation order, which I have already conceded is mislabeled; the canonical update itself is sound.

3. **The honest disclaimers are present and accurate** at PIFB:2611 (Fisher arc length is Riemannian, not Lorentzian, and runs opposite to proper time); PIFB:2662 ("we emphasize this is a toy model demonstration ... the foundation remains incomplete"); PIFB:2722-2723 (gauge non-invariance of the horizontal block, F = 0 in Regime I so `tr(FF)` cannot rescue gauge invariance, routed to consensus metric); PIFB:2779 ("we do not claim that variational free-energy minimization on the real Gaussian fiber selects the imaginary assignment; the example demonstrates structural compatibility, not derivation"). The section consistently fences off speculative content from derived content, in the manner Wheeler 1990's own "it from bit" essay invites for exploratory programs [Wheeler 1990 in *Complexity, Entropy, and the Physics of Information*].

What red has established, taken together with my concessions:

- **Two local edits are required.** PIFB:2649 needs the order estimate corrected from `O(||eta · ~∇||^2)` to either `O(eta · ||phi|| · ||~∇||)` with the abelian-sector remark moved to qualify that prefactor, or rephrased to make clear the comparison is between the additive chart step and the exact group-level retraction. PIFB:2751 needs the Hoffman 2019 attribution rewritten so that the slow-tier/fast-tier identification is presented as an internal framework move rather than as a Hoffman conclusion (and the analogous mild rewording for the Friston 2017 citation). Both are line-edit-scale fixes.

- **The structural attack on the bundle metric does not land.** The bundle metric is well-defined on `E_q` modulo the section choice that defines it, which is the standard situation for any section-pulled-back gauge-potential construction; the gauge non-invariance is correctly disclosed and routed to the consensus construction in the next debate.

**Falsification conditions for this defense.** This rebuttal fails if (i) Hoffman 2019 in fact contains a slow-timescale-generative-model identification of the perceptual interface that I missed (a direct page citation would defeat the concession on Strike 2 and let the manuscript stand); (ii) the canonical Riemannian-submersion construction in Kobayashi & Nomizu Vol. I §III requires a pre-existing gauge-invariant base metric and explicitly rules out connection-derived horizontal blocks as bona-fide bundle metrics (a direct reading from Kobayashi & Nomizu that contradicts the "intrinsic horizontal distribution + section-dependent gauge potential" decomposition would re-elevate red's third strike from "overreach" to "lands"); (iii) the additive chart step `phi^{t+1} = phi^t - eta ~∇` is what the manuscript intends as the canonical theoretical update rather than the implementation specialization of the canonical group-level retraction (if the manuscript is *not* committing to the group-level retraction as the canonical form, then the BCH critique elevates from "line edit" to "the wrong update is being canonized"). Items (i) and (ii) require red to produce primary-source counter-evidence I have not seen; item (iii) is a reading of PIFB:2651's final sentence, which is explicit that "the canonical theoretical update is the group-level form Eq. (gauge_natural_gradient)-(gauge_group_retraction), with the chart-coordinate truncation serving as the implementation specialization" — i.e., the manuscript does in fact canonize the group form.

**Verdict-relevant summary.** The section is publication-ready conditional on two line-edits (BCH order at 2649; Hoffman attribution at 2751). It is **not** publication-ready as currently written, because both items are mathematically/citation-wise wrong. The section is **not** structurally broken — the bundle-metric construction is geometrically sound, the disclaimers are honest, and the canonical updates are canonical. Under the strict reading of the claim ("publication-ready and rock-solid"), red carries it on the two local strikes. Under a charitable reading (publication-ready after stipulated edits), the verdict is closer to BLUE_WINS_AFTER_EDITS — which is not on the verdict menu. The honest call: **RED_WINS on the strict reading, with the action being a two-line edit list that is straightforward and does not require structural rewrite.**
