## Reasoning

The claim under evaluation is binary: the four subsections at PIFB:2584-2772 are "publication-ready and rock-solid." The operational reading (binding on both sides and the judge) requires the math to be correct, citations to be correctly invoked, disclaimers to be complete and honest, and no speculative claim dressed as derivation. Two of the four criteria fail in load-bearing ways. Criterion (1) fails at PIFB:2649: the order estimate on the BCH correction is wrong by a full order in the gradient, sympy-verified by red and conceded by blue. Criterion (2) fails at PIFB:2751: the Hoffman 2019 citation attaches a slow-tier vs fast-tier architectural identification to an author whose work does not make that distinction, primary-source-verified by red and conceded by blue. Either failure alone is sufficient for a red strike per the operational reading; both fire.

Red's third strike — that the bundle metric is defined only on the trivialization, not on the abstract bundle `E_q` — does not land. The remaining question is whether two line-edit-scale defects are sufficient to falsify "publication-ready and rock-solid." Under the operational reading the answer is yes: "rock-solid" does not permit a known math error and a known citation misattribution, even local ones. Blue's own rebuttal frames the honest call as "RED_WINS on the strict reading, with the action being a two-line edit list." That is the call.
