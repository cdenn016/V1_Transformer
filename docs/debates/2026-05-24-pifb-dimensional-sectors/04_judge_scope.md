## False dichotomies / equivocations detected

1. **The decisive equivocation — "well-defined" (weak vs. strong) on the single phrase "by eigenvalue magnitude."** The claim says "the spectral decomposition ... into observable / subthreshold informational / internal sectors *by eigenvalue magnitude* is well-defined." Two parses both fit the words and have opposite truth values:
   - **Weak (Blue):** for any threshold pair the partition exists and reconstructs $G_i$ (orthogonal complete eigenprojectors). True, trivial, theorem-backed — and would not need a debate.
   - **Strong (Red):** the named sectors are *features of the geometry* — eigenvalue magnitudes meaningful enough (reparameterization-invariant, gap-stable) to identify "observable / subthreshold / internal" as real structure.
   Blue's rebuttal concedes the strong reading outright: "the section should be read as defining sectors only up to a choice of base chart" and "well-defined relative to a chosen base chart and threshold pair." Under the strong reading that concession *is* the claim collapsing; under the weak reading the claim is trivially true. The qualifier "by eigenvalue magnitude" makes magnitudes do the labeling work (tilts strong); the verb "well-defined" tilts weak. The sentence does not force either. Red wins the strong reading, Blue wins the weak — neither carries the claim *as written* because the claim is two claims.

2. **"Mathematically sound" conflates set-partition-well-definedness with spectral-decomposition-soundness.** This is the same equivocation viewed from the canon side. Both sides actually agree on every individual fact: partition exists for any threshold (Blue), magnitudes are chart-dependent and gaps are unexhibited (Red, conceded by Blue). They disagree only on which fact "mathematically sound" denotes. That is a frame disagreement, not a mathematical one — Blue states this verbatim ("This is a frame error, not a mathematical one").

3. **No false dichotomy on instantiation.** Red's Vector 2 (vacuous at $\dim\mathcal{C}=0$) and Blue's :3021-gate reply are not arguing the same proposition — "not realized in the transformer limit" vs. "not a well-defined construction." Blue's distinction is correct and Red's rebuttal effectively absorbed it (Vector 2 reframed, not pressed). This is resolved, not REMAND-worthy.

## My verdict reasoning

The claim is not well-formed: its central proposition, "the spectral decomposition into observable/subthreshold/internal sectors by eigenvalue magnitude is well-defined," carries two non-equivalent readings whose truth values diverge, and the debate split exactly along that seam — Red attacked the strong reading and won it (chart-dependence conceded by Blue, gaps unexhibited, ordering postulated not derived), Blue defended the weak reading and won it (partition exists and reconstructs $G_i$ for any threshold). Both are correct about their own proposition; neither carries the claim *as written* because the sentence is two claims fused. This is the canonical REMAND signature: same sentence, two propositions, both sides arguing past each other on a frame they each silently fix differently. Compounding it, the surviving Red strike exposes an in-span/out-of-span manuscript inconsistency — :3057–3062 says the hierarchies "satisfy" the ordering (declarative) while :3190 lists that same hierarchy under "What is postulated" — which "mathematically sound" cannot resolve and which Blue's "We define" defense does not reach. The bookkeeping (:3019/:3045/:3073), the arithmetic ($296{,}064$), and the (3+1) speculative label are conceded by both sides and need no sub-debate. OUT_OF_SCOPE is wrong because the claim is answerable once decomposed — the failure is equivocation and internal inconsistency, not unfalsifiability. RED_WINS / BLUE_WINS are both wrong because each is true only under one unforced parse. REMAND, with two crisp sub-claims, is the highest-value decomposition.

## Decisive evidence

The frame-breaking observation: the claim says sectors are well-defined "**by eigenvalue magnitude**," yet Blue's rebuttal concedes "the section should be read as defining sectors only up to a choice of base chart" (coordinate-dependence of magnitudes under $\tilde G=J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, both sides agreeing per Čencov-is-tensor-not-pullback-eigenvalue [Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2]). A predicate "well-defined by eigenvalue magnitude" whose magnitudes both sides agree are chart-dependent is well-defined only in the weak (per-chart, per-threshold) sense — which is trivially true and not what "mathematically sound" is naturally read to assert. The single sentence thus resolves to TRUE under one parse and FALSE under the other; that is the equivocation, and it is the tie-breaker that forces REMAND over either side winning.

Corroborating in-span/out-of-span inconsistency (verified in manuscript): :3057 "The dimensional and eigenvalue hierarchies **satisfy** ..." vs. :3190 "**What is postulated:** (i) the eigenvalue hierarchy itself."
