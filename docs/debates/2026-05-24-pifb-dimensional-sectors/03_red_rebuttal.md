## Core attack

Blue wins those points by narrowing the claim below what the debated span asserts, and the narrowed claim is not the claim. The claim under debate (00_claim.md) is that the §2994–3074 treatment "is mathematically sound," including that "the spectral decomposition ... into observable / subthreshold informational / internal sectors by eigenvalue magnitude is well-defined." Blue's evidence establishes only that the threshold partition is *set-theoretically* well-defined "as a definition for every threshold choice" (02_blue_opening.md, numerical-analyst paragraph). That is true and beside the point. The section asserts three things inside the span that "well-defined-for-any-threshold" does not cover, and the manuscript's own downstream concession at :3190 confirms each is imposed rather than carried by the mathematics.

First, the magnitude-ordering is not a coordinate-free predicate, and Blue never engaged this. The sectors are defined by absolute thresholds on raw eigenvalues, $\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}=\{\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\text{obs}}\}$ (:3026), $\mathcal D_{\text{subthresh}}$ at :3034, $\mathcal D_{\text{internal}}$ at :3042. Under a reparameterization of the base $c\mapsto\psi(c)$ with Jacobian $J$, the pullback transforms as a $(0,2)$-tensor, $\tilde G=J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, so its eigenvalues rescale by the squared singular values of $J$. "$\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\text{obs}}$" is therefore a statement about the chosen coordinates on $\mathcal C$, not about the direction $e_a$. Blue's opening cites Čencov to establish that the Fisher metric is PSD — but Čencov-invariance is invariance of the metric *tensor* under sufficient statistics, not invariance of the *numerical eigenvalues* of a pullback under arbitrary base reparameterization [Čencov1972; AmariNagaoka2000, Ch. 2] (info-geometer). The only coordinate-free spectral content is rank, eigenvalue *ratios*, and *gaps*; the section defines its sectors by absolute thresholds and asserts the magnitude hierarchy $\lambda_{\text{obs}}\gg\lambda_{\text{subthresh}}\gg\lambda_{\text{internal}}$ (:3062) in absolute terms (:3062), neither of which is invariant. A "sector" whose membership changes under a smooth base reparameterization is not a feature of the geometry.

Second, even granting fixed coordinates, a partition into three *separated* clusters is meaningful only across genuine spectral gaps, and the section exhibits none. Blue's "well-defined as a definition for every threshold choice" is exactly the move the Davis–Kahan $\sin\theta$ theorem refutes for cluster separation: the invariant subspace spanned by a cluster is stable, and the cluster meaningfully separable, only when an eigengap $\delta$ separates it from the rest of the spectrum, with subspace perturbation bounded by $\|E\|/\delta$, diverging as $\delta\to0$ [Davis–Kahan 1970, *SIAM J. Numer. Anal.* 7(1):1–46; Stewart–Sun 1990, Ch. V]. Weyl's inequality $|\lambda_k(A+E)-\lambda_k(A)|\le\|E\|$ [Bhatia 1997, Ch. III] means a cut placed inside a dense region of the spectrum is not perturbation-stable. Eq. :3062 asserts two large gaps; the section computes no spectrum and proves no gap theorem, so the three-tier separation is asserted, not produced (numerical-analyst). Set-theoretic partition always succeeds and always returns three sets; that the operation succeeds is not evidence the sets are spectral clusters.

Third — and this converts the first two from objections into a decided RED on the soundness claim — the manuscript itself concedes the load-bearing content is postulated, at :3190: "the eigenvalue hierarchy itself, including the choice of three large eigenvalues in $V_{\text{obs}}$" is listed under "What is postulated." But the in-span Eqs. :3057–3058 ($|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{subthresh}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{internal}}|$) and :3061–3062 ($\lambda_{\text{obs}}\gg\lambda_{\text{subthresh}}\gg\lambda_{\text{internal}}\approx0$) are written *unconditionally* — as properties the spectrum "satisfies," not as posits and not gated by the :3021 "when $\dim(\mathcal C)$ is large enough" clause (that clause governs the *existence* of three sectors, not the *ordering* within them). The downstream honesty at :3190 does not rescue the in-span text; it confirms that the in-span text states as a spectral property what the framework can only assume. An assertion that the spectrum satisfies a hierarchy the framework concedes it has merely postulated is not "mathematically sound" — it is an unproven claim formatted as a derived one. This is the ad-hoc move in its exact form: a result fixed by a free auxiliary (here two thresholds plus an assumed eigenvalue profile) forbids nothing, since for any spectrum and any target cardinalities two cut points realizing them exist [Popper1959, §§19–20] (philosophy-of-science).

## Defense

Blue's three concessions do not contain the damage to the in-span soundness claim, because two of them name failures that live *inside* the debated span. Concession #2 grants that identifying the observable sector with "the (1+3) dimensions we perceive as spacetime" (:3065) is interpretive and, taken as an explanation, "would import the explanandum." That import is not a downstream event Blue can wall off — :3065 is at the center of §2994–3074, the span the claim calls sound. Perceived $(1+3)$ spacetime is precisely what a "dimensional structure and observable sectors" account is meant to explain; naming the high-flux sector "the perceived spacetime" (:3029) and then re-posing $|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|=4$ as a puzzle for neural/sensory/evolutionary mechanisms (:3069) reverses the explanatory direction the deductive-nomological schema requires — the explanandum must be a consequence of the explanans, not assumed by it and then re-posed [Hempel–Oppenheim 1948, *Philosophy of Science* 15(2):135–175] (philosophy-of-science). Blue's subjunctive-mood defense ("may suppose") softens the assertion's tone, not its logical role. Concession #3 grants that non-vacuity requires $\dim(\mathcal C)$ large; the manuscript's only computationally validated construction is the transformer limit at $\dim(\mathcal C)=0$ (:482, :1001, :1613), where $G_i$ is the empty $0\times0$ tensor with no spectrum, and its one concrete geometric example is $\mathcal C=\mathbb R^2$ (:2803), which cannot fill even $|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|=4$, let alone three nested sectors [Lee2013, Ch. 13: a pullback is a genuine metric iff the section is an immersion, degenerate otherwise] (geometer). So the three-sector separation has no witness anywhere in the framework's realized constructions; it is a decomposition of a spectrum the framework never produces with enough eigenvalues to decompose.

On the signature, Blue's externalization defense ("the temporal sign is correctly attributed to an external, separately-flagged postulate") is itself a concession that the $(1+3)$ structure is not carried by this section's mathematics. The :3029 wording "the 4-dimensional sector admits a $(1+3)$ Lorentzian decomposition" is still load-bearing inside the span: the spectral decomposition admits no such thing. A PSD pullback has $\lambda_a\ge0$ and, by Sylvester's law of inertia, zero negative eigenvalues invariant under any real change of base coordinates [Nakahara 2003, §7.1–7.2; Horn–Johnson 2013, Thm 4.5.8] (gauge-theorist). The 4-dimensional subspace the spectrum admits is Euclidean; the Lorentzian sign is inserted entirely from the signature-section postulates, which carry Debate 2's REMAND. Blue's own opening agrees the sign is imported — which is exactly why "admits a Lorentzian decomposition" at :3029 overreads the in-span mathematics. Blue has correctly defended the bookkeeping, the arithmetic, and the speculative label; it has not defended the three claims that make the section's title — soundness of a *decomposition into observable / subthreshold / internal sectors by eigenvalue magnitude* — more than set-theoretic, and the manuscript's own :3190 postulate list testifies against it.
