# Blue Rebuttal — pifb-dimensional-sectors

Synthesis of the five-lens panel (geometer, info-geometer, numerical-analyst, gauge-theorist, philosophy-of-science). Every memo is cited below; none is discounted. Source-of-truth precedence: the manuscript span `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2994–3074` is the claim under evaluation; the defense is grounded in external canon, and concedes wherever canon contradicts.

## Concession

I grant two of Red's three vectors as genuine strikes, and sharpen the concession beyond the opening rather than rehearsing it.

First, the threshold-freeness and the hierarchy-as-posit. The two cutoffs $\Lambda_{\rm obs},\Lambda_{\rm subthresh}$ (:3026, :3034, :3042) are not fixed by the dynamics, an intrinsic scale, or a gap criterion, and the size and magnitude orderings at :3057–3062 do not follow from the spectral decomposition — they are a modeling posit. This is the numerical-analyst's own concession and it stands: a stable three-cluster partition of a spectrum requires demonstrated eigengaps, and the perturbation of a cluster's invariant subspace is bounded by $\|E\|/\delta$, which diverges as the gap $\delta\to0$ [Davis & Kahan 1970, *SIAM J. Numer. Anal.* 7(1):1–46; Bhatia 1997, *Matrix Analysis*, Ch. III, Weyl's inequality]. The section exhibits no spectrum and no gap argument, so it does not establish that the cuts fall in gaps. The hierarchy is asserted, not derived.

Second — and this absorbs Red's sharpest sub-point cleanly — I concede the coordinate-dependence of the thresholds. Čencov invariance is invariance of the Fisher *tensor* under sufficient statistics, not invariance of the numerical *eigenvalue magnitudes* of a pullback under base reparameterization [Cencov 1972; Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2]. Under $c\mapsto\psi(c)$ with Jacobian $J$ the pullback transforms as $\tilde G=J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, rescaling eigenvalues by the squared singular values of $J$, so an absolute cut "$\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\rm obs}$" names a coordinate, not an intrinsic direction. The only reparameterization-invariant spectral content is rank and eigenvalue ratios, and the section defines its sectors by absolute magnitude, not by ratios. Red is right on this and the section should be read as defining sectors only up to a choice of base chart.

These concessions tighten what is defended. They do not touch the claim, for the reason in the next section.

## Core attack

Red's load-bearing move — Vector 2, "the decomposition is vacuous in the framework's only realized construction, $\dim\mathcal C=0$ for transformers" — attacks a proposition the claim does not assert. This is a frame error, not a mathematical one, and it is the decisive flaw in Red's opening.

The claim (00_claim.md, L11) is that the treatment is *mathematically sound* and *correctly labeled speculative*. It does not claim the three sectors are non-trivially instantiated in any current run. Red's Vector 2 establishes that the spectrum is empty where the transformer construction computes — granted, at $n=0$ the pullback is the empty $0\times0$ tensor — but "the decomposition is not realized in the transformer limit" and "the decomposition is not a well-defined construction" are different propositions. The first is true and unclaimed; the second is what the claim asserts and is false.

The manuscript itself draws this line, and Red quotes the very sentence that draws it. At :3021 the section gates the decomposition: the spectrum "naturally decomposes into three distinct sectors *when $\dim(\mathcal C)$ is large enough to accommodate them*." Red reads this gating as an admission of vacuousness; it is the opposite — it is the honest scope marker that makes the construction well-formed. A pullback $\sigma^* g$ is a Riemannian (positive-definite) metric iff $\sigma$ is an immersion, and a well-defined though degenerate bilinear form otherwise, with the $n=0$ case the trivial empty form [Lee 2013, *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* (2nd ed.), Ch. 11, 13]. The construction "spectral decomposition of $\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B}$, partitioned by eigenvalue magnitude" is mathematically sound as written for every $n$; whether a particular run instantiates $n\ge5$ so that three sectors can be exhibited is a separate empirical question the section explicitly brackets at :3021. Red's falsification condition 2 ("exhibit a construction with $\dim\mathcal C\ge5$ and an immersion section") demands a witness for a claim — non-trivial instantiation — that no one is making. The geometer's pullback theorem and the section's own :3021 gate jointly defeat the vacuousness charge against the *soundness* claim.

## Defense

The "honestly labeled" half of the claim survives a test the opening could not yet run: the downstream-leakage check. Philosophy-of-science flagged in the opening that the one live risk to "honestly labeled" was the supposition at :3065 ("we may suppose that the observable sector corresponds to the $(1+3)$ dimensions we perceive as spacetime") leaking into later sections as established. I ran the grep. It does not leak; every downstream use re-hedges.

At :3192 the manuscript states in plain text: "We do not claim to have derived physical spacetime, the Lorentz signature, or the dimensionality count from the framework; the construction shows that the framework is compatible with such a reading under the postulates listed above." At :3188–3190 the eigenvalue hierarchy and "the choice of three large eigenvalues" are listed under "What is postulated," closing with "None of (i)–(iv) is derived from variational free-energy minimization." At :2858 the $(1+3)$ split "is fixed by the choice of a single imaginary frame direction rather than derived from free-energy dynamics," and "the construction does not currently distinguish 1+3 from 2+2 or other indefinite splits on dynamical grounds." At :3598–3600 the signature work is filed under open questions and closes "it does not show that the framework derives it." Four independent downstream references to `sec:observable_sectors`, four re-hedges, zero assertions of the supposition as established. The falsification condition for "honestly labeled" — downstream leakage — was checked and is unmet within the manuscript.

This bears directly on Red's Vector 3. Red is correct that a PSD pullback cannot be Lorentzian: signature is invariant under real congruence and a form with $\lambda_a\ge0$ has zero negative eigenvalues [Sylvester's law of inertia, Horn & Johnson 2013, Thm 4.5.8; Nakahara 2003, §7.1–7.2]. But the section does not claim its spectral decomposition delivers the minus sign. At :3029 it states the temporal sign "is not intrinsic to the positive semi-definite spectral decomposition ... it is imported from the postulate set of Section [signature_resolution] and inherits the open-problem status of that section." This is the correct division of labor: the spectral decomposition delivers a 4-dimensional Euclidean ($\lambda_a\ge0$) sector; the indefinite sign is attributed to an external, separately-flagged, REMANDed postulate, not smuggled in. The gauge-theorist's Sylvester argument is exactly the fact the manuscript itself invokes (:2858, :3596 cite Sylvester to explain why non-compactness alone does not flip the signature). Red's Vector 3 names a gap the section has already named; it does not show the section conceals it.

The rigorous core stands on theorems, not modeling choices. The base-versus-fiber bookkeeping is exact: $G_i(c)=\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B}$ is a tensor on $T_c\mathcal C$, an $n\times n$ object with $n=\dim\mathcal C$, $\operatorname{rank}(\sigma^* g)\le\operatorname{rank}(d\sigma_c)\le\dim\mathcal C$, and PSD-preserving because $(\sigma^* g)_c(u,u)=g(d\sigma_c u,d\sigma_c u)\ge0$ [Lee 2013, Ch. 11, 13] — matching :3019 verbatim. The "vast majority of dimensions invisible" rhetoric is correctly relocated to the image of $d\sigma_i$ inside the fiber tangent space, a subspace of dimension at most $\dim\mathcal C$ [Nakahara 2003, Ch. 10; Kobayashi & Nomizu Vol. I, §III], and :3073 names the two senses and forbids conflating them — which Red grants. The fiber dimension $\dim(\mathcal B)=K+K(K+1)/2=K(K+3)/2$ is the standard Gaussian statistical-manifold count, $g_{\mathcal B}$ is the Fisher–Rao metric, PSD as a score-Gram matrix [Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2; Cencov 1972], and the arithmetic checks: $768+\tfrac{768\cdot769}{2}=296{,}064$. The spectral theorem for a symmetric PSD operator gives a real orthonormal eigenbasis with $\lambda_a\ge0$ and orthogonal complete eigenprojectors, so the three-sector sum at :3050–3052 reconstructs $G_i$ exactly for any threshold pair [doCarmo 1992; Bhatia, Jain & Lim 2019]. Red grants the bookkeeping, the arithmetic, and the honest empirical disclaimer. Those grants, plus the :3021 gate and the downstream-hedge evidence, are the claim.

## Where this defense fails

To keep the defense honest rather than sycophantic, the conditions under which it loses:

1. **If the claim is read as asserting non-trivial instantiation.** The defense holds only because the claim is "sound construction," not "three sectors realized in a current run." If a judge reads the claim as the latter, Red wins outright on Vector 2: the only computed construction has $\dim\mathcal C=0$ and no witness with $\dim\mathcal C\ge5$ and an immersion section is exhibited anywhere. The discriminating line is :3021's gate. The manuscript would be improved by stating where, if anywhere, $\dim\mathcal C$ is large enough — but absence of that statement does not make the construction unsound, only un-instantiated.

2. **If the supposition leaks downstream as established.** Checked within the manuscript; unmet (:3192, :3188–3190, :2858, :3598–3600 all re-hedge). If a downstream section outside the searched file asserts the $(1+3)$ identification without the hedge, the "honestly labeled" claim fails there and a consistency fix is owed.

3. **If "sound" requires the thresholds to be principled and reparameterization-invariant.** Conceded that they are neither. If the judge holds that a decomposition defined by coordinate-dependent absolute cutoffs is for that reason not "well-defined," the well-definedness claim narrows to "well-defined relative to a chosen base chart and threshold pair" — which is what the construction actually delivers, and weaker than an unqualified reading of the claim.
