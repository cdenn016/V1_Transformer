# Red Opening — pifb-dimensional-sectors

## Steelman (opposing position)

The §"Dimensional Structure and Observable Sectors" treatment is the most careful speculative passage in the manuscript: the base-versus-fiber bookkeeping is correct (the induced metric $G_i(c)=\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B}$ is a tensor on $T_c\mathcal C$ with at most $n=\dim\mathcal C$ eigenvalues, and the "vast majority of dimensions invisible" claim is properly about the $\dim(\mathcal B)-n$ unsampled fiber directions of $d\sigma_i$, not eigen-directions of $G_i$ — :3019, :3073), the Gaussian dimension count $\dim(\mathcal B)=K(K+3)/2=296{,}064$ for $K=768$ is right (:3013–3017), the spectral facts ($G_i$ symmetric PSD, $\lambda_a\ge0$) are elementary, and the $(3+1)$ subsection is honestly labeled "(Speculative)" with "no quantitative predictions ... that could be tested experimentally" (:3071), with the temporal sign openly flagged as imported rather than intrinsic (:3029).

## Position

Three of those concessions are genuine and I grant them up front: the base/fiber disambiguation is mathematically exact, the $K(K+3)/2$ arithmetic checks ($768+\tfrac{768\cdot769}{2}=296{,}064$ — confirmed against [Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2] for the Gaussian parameter count), and the $(3+1)$ subsection's empirical disclaimer is honest. But the claim under debate is that the *three-sector decomposition is mathematically sound and well-defined*, and that survives only if "well-defined" is read so weakly that it carries no content. My position is falsifiable and threefold: (a) the partition is structure *imposed* by two free dials, not structure *found* in any spectrum; (b) in the framework's only computationally realized construction the spectrum is empty, so the decomposition is vacuous where the framework actually runs; and (c) the $(1+3)$ Lorentzian identification is carried entirely by an imported, unresolved postulate, not by the spectral mathematics of this section. The honest "(Speculative)" label defends against the charge "you claimed a prediction"; it does not repair any of the three.

### Vector 1 — the three sectors are imposed by free thresholds, not found in a spectrum

The observable, subthreshold, and internal sectors are defined by two thresholds $\Lambda_{\rm obs},\Lambda_{\rm subthresh}$ (:3026, :3034, :3042) that the manuscript never fixes by anything. They are free parameters, and the reported "structure" is their image. Two free cut points partition any monotone non-negative spectrum $\lambda_1\ge\cdots\ge\lambda_n\ge0$ into three sets of *any* prescribed cardinalities summing to $n$; so the size hierarchy $|\mathcal D_{\rm obs}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\rm sub}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\rm int}|$ asserted at :3057 forbids nothing — it is automatically satisfiable by dial-turning. A construction whose output is set by an adjustable auxiliary tuned to give the desired answer is the textbook ad-hoc move and has no empirical content [Popper, *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*, §§19–20: a hypothesis must forbid something].

The numerical and information-geometric canon say the thresholds cannot be made principled *as written*. First, a two-threshold partition into three meaningfully separable clusters is well-defined only when there are genuine eigengaps: the Davis–Kahan $\sin\theta$ theorem bounds the perturbation of a cluster's invariant subspace by $\|E\|/\delta$, which diverges as the gap $\delta\to0$, and Weyl's inequality lets eigenvalues in a dense region cross any interior cut under arbitrarily small perturbation [Davis & Kahan 1970, *SIAM J. Numer. Anal.* 7(1); Bhatia 1997, *Matrix Analysis*, Ch. III]. The manuscript asserts the gaps ($\lambda_{\rm obs}\gg\lambda_{\rm sub}\gg\lambda_{\rm int}\approx0$, :3061) but exhibits no spectrum and no gap argument, so the cuts sit in an unknown, possibly gapless, profile and the resulting sectors are not perturbation-stable. Second, even the magnitudes the thresholds compare against are coordinate-dependent: under a base reparameterization $c\mapsto\psi(c)$ with Jacobian $J$ the pullback transforms as $\tilde G=J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, rescaling eigenvalues by the squared singular values of $J$. Čencov-invariance is invariance of the Fisher *tensor* under sufficient statistics, not invariance of pullback eigenvalue *magnitudes* [Čencov 1972; Amari & Nagaoka 2000, Ch. 2], so an absolute threshold "$\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\rm obs}$" names a coordinate, not an intrinsic direction. The only coordinate-free spectral content is rank and eigenvalue ratios, neither of which the section uses to define the sectors.

### Vector 2 — the decomposition is vacuous in the framework's only realized construction

The bookkeeping I conceded is exactly what sinks the construction in practice. A pullback of a (0,2)-tensor has rank $\le\dim\mathcal C$, and is a genuine (full-rank) Riemannian metric iff $\sigma$ is an immersion, degenerate otherwise [Lee, *Introduction to Smooth Manifolds* (2nd ed.), Ch. 11, 13]. The three-sector decomposition presumes a spectrum with enough eigenvalues to fill three sectors; the manuscript itself gates it on "when $\dim(\mathcal C)$ is large enough to accommodate them" (:3021). But the framework's only computationally instantiated construction is transformers, where the manuscript states $\dim\mathcal C=0$ (the bundle-figure caption :482, and the section title "Transformer Architectures as the Zero-Dimensional Limit," :1604). At $n=0$, $G_i(c)$ is the empty $0\times0$ tensor: zero eigenvalues, no observable sector, no subthreshold sector, no internal sector, no spectrum to partition. The decomposition is not merely speculative there — it is empty, because its carrier $T_c\mathcal C$ is the zero vector space. The manuscript demonstrates no construction in which $n$ is large enough to exhibit even two sectors. "Well-defined" for a decomposition of an empty spectrum holds only in the trivial sense that $\varnothing$ partitions into three copies of $\varnothing$; the substantive claim — that a generic induced metric *separates* into three sectors of vastly different size — has no witness anywhere the framework computes.

### Vector 3 — the $(1+3)$ signature is carried by an imported, unresolved postulate, not by this section

A PSD pullback has $\lambda_a\ge0$ (:3005). A Lorentzian $(1+3)$ metric is by definition indefinite, with at least one negative eigenvalue, and signature is a basis-independent invariant under real congruence [Nakahara 2003, §7.1–7.2; Sylvester's law of inertia, Horn & Johnson 2013, Thm 4.5.8]. A PSD form cannot be made Lorentzian by any real change of base coordinates. So when :3029 says the 4-dimensional sector "admits a $(1\,\text{temporal}+3\,\text{spatial})$ Lorentzian decomposition," the word "admits" conceals the gap: the spectral decomposition admits only a 4-dimensional *Euclidean* (all-$\lambda_a\ge0$) subspace; the minus sign is inserted by hand from the signature-section postulates (:3029 names them — imaginary frame component, single non-compact generator, $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ convention). The manuscript concedes this ("not intrinsic to the positive semi-definite spectral decomposition ... imported ... inherits the open-problem status of that section," :3029), and that section carries Debate 2's signature REMAND. The load-bearing element of the $(1+3)$ identification therefore rests on an unresolved postulate, and the claim that this section soundly delivers a Lorentzian observable sector overreaches what :3029 itself admits.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following is shown.

1. **Thresholds principled (defeats Vector 1).** Blue exhibits a determination of $\Lambda_{\rm obs},\Lambda_{\rm subthresh}$ fixed by the dynamics, an intrinsic scale, or a gap criterion — recast as reparameterization-invariant quantities (eigenvalue ratios or relative gaps, not absolute magnitudes) — together with an actual or gap-theorem-argued spectrum exhibiting two Davis–Kahan-stable eigengaps where the cuts fall, so the three-sector partition and its size hierarchy *follow* rather than being imposed.

2. **A non-degenerate witness (defeats Vector 2).** Blue exhibits a construction in the framework — transformer or multi-agent — with $\dim\mathcal C\ge5$ and a section $\sigma$ that is an immersion, in which the induced spectrum actually separates into the three sectors, rather than the only realized construction having $\dim\mathcal C=0$ and hence an empty spectrum.

3. **Signature derived, not imported (defeats Vector 3).** Blue shows the $(1+3)$ indefinite signature follows from the framework's own construction of $G_i$ without importing the temporal sign from a separate postulate set, OR that the signature section's REMAND has been lifted to a resolved derivation this section can cite as established. As written, :3029 concedes both that the sign is imported and that it inherits open-problem status.

A demonstration that the bookkeeping is correct (granted) or that the speculative label is honest (granted) does not meet any of these three — those concessions are compatible with the construction being content-free where it matters.
