# Blue Opening — pifb-dimensional-sectors

Synthesized from a five-lens panel (philosophy-of-science, geometer, info-geometer, numerical-analyst, gauge-theorist; see `02_blue_panel_choice.md` and `02_blue_lens_memos.md`). The claim under defense is narrow and the defense is built from external canon, not from the manuscript citing itself.

## Steelman (opposing position)

The section dresses an arbitrary eigenvalue-threshold partition as physical structure and then identifies the large-eigenvalue sector with "perceived spacetime" — importing the very explanandum a theory of emergent spacetime owes — while the sector-size hierarchy $|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{subthresh}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{internal}}|$ is tuned, not derived, and the "we may suppose ... $(1+3)$" line at :3065 reads as an assertion that arrives before any speculative label.

## Position

The treatment at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2994–3074` is mathematically sound and, on the $(3+1)$ identification, honestly labeled. Specifically: (1) the base-vs-fiber bookkeeping at :3019/:3045/:3073 is a correct statement of pullback algebra — $G_i(c)=\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B}$ is an $n\times n$ PSD tensor on $T_c\mathcal C$ with $n=\dim\mathcal C$ and rank $\le n$, and the "invisible" rhetoric is correctly relocated to the image of $d\sigma_i$ in the fiber; (2) the Gaussian fiber dimension $\dim(\mathcal B)=K(K+3)/2$ and the value $296{,}064$ at $K=768$ are exact; (3) the spectral decomposition and three-sector partition are well-defined for any threshold pair; (4) the temporal sign is correctly attributed to an external, separately-flagged postulate, not to the spectral decomposition; and (5) the $(3+1)$ structure is honestly self-labeled "(Speculative)" with an explicit disclaimer of testable predictions. The defense does **not** extend to the sector-size hierarchy being derived (it is a posit) nor to the spacetime identification being non-interpretive (it is interpretive); these are conceded below and the claim is stated to exclude them.

## Evidence

**Base-vs-fiber bookkeeping is correct pullback algebra (geometer, gauge-theorist).** For a smooth section $\sigma_i:\mathcal C\to\mathcal B$, the pullback of the $(0,2)$-tensor $g_{\mathcal B}$ is $(\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B})_c(u,v)=g_{\mathcal B}\big(d\sigma_i\,u,\,d\sigma_i\,v\big)$ for $u,v\in T_c\mathcal C$ [Lee2013, Ch. 11]. Three consequences are exactly what :3019 asserts: it is an $n\times n$ object on $T_c\mathcal C$ with $n=\dim\mathcal C$; its rank is $\le\operatorname{rank}(d\sigma_i)\le\dim\mathcal C$ (in coordinates $\sigma_i^* g=(d\sigma_i)^\top g\,(d\sigma_i)$, and $\operatorname{rank}(A^\top M A)\le\operatorname{rank}(A)$); and it is PSD when $g_{\mathcal B}$ is, since $g_{\mathcal B}(d\sigma_i u, d\sigma_i u)\ge0$ [Lee2013, Ch. 13]. The differential $d\sigma_i:T_c\mathcal C\to T_{q_i}\mathcal B$ has image of dimension $\le\dim\mathcal C$, and the pullback sees only $g_{\mathcal B}$ restricted to that image; fiber directions outside $\operatorname{im}(d\sigma_i)$ never enter the pullback [Nakahara2003, Ch. 10; KobayashiNomizu Vol. I §III]. So the manuscript's relocation of "vast majority of dimensions invisible" to the unsampled fiber directions (:3045, :3073), kept explicitly distinct from the base-direction subthreshold eigenvalues, is the correct disambiguation — the precise distinction a critic would otherwise demand is already made in the text.

**The fiber dimension and the PSD source are standard (info-geometer).** The $K$-variate Gaussian family is a statistical manifold of dimension $K+K(K+1)/2=K(K+3)/2$ [AmariNagaoka2000, Ch. 2]. The arithmetic is exact: $768+\tfrac{768\cdot769}{2}=768+295{,}296=296{,}064=\tfrac{768\cdot771}{2}$ — both forms of Eq. (:3014) agree (verified). The metric $g_{\mathcal B}$ being pulled back is the Fisher–Rao metric, PSD as a Gram matrix of score functions and the canonical metric on a statistical manifold [Cencov1972]. The $\lambda_a\ge0$ claim at :3005 therefore rests on a score-Gram fact, not an assumption, and combines with the pullback PSD-preservation above.

**The spectral decomposition and three-sector partition are well-defined (numerical-analyst).** A real symmetric PSD $G_i$ has a real orthonormal eigenbasis with $\lambda_a\ge0$ [doCarmo1992; BhatiaJainLim2019]. For any cutoffs $\Lambda_{\text{subthresh}}\le\Lambda_{\text{obs}}$, the partition at :3026/:3034/:3042 is a disjoint Borel partition of the spectrum, and the three-term sum (:3050–3052) reconstructs $G_i$ exactly because the eigenprojectors are orthogonal and complete. The construction is well-defined as a definition for every threshold choice; :3023–3042 introduce the sectors with "We define," consistent with reading them as a partition rather than a derived hierarchy.

**The temporal sign is correctly externalized (philosophy-of-science).** :3029 states the temporal sign "is not intrinsic to the positive semi-definite spectral decomposition" and "is imported from the postulate set of Section [signature_resolution] and inherits the open-problem status of that section." A PSD spectrum yields $\lambda_a\ge0$ only; Lorentzian signature requires an external indefinite-form postulate. Attributing the sign to a separately-flagged, open postulate is the correct division of labor and inherits Debate 2's signature REMAND honestly rather than smuggling its conclusion.

**The $(3+1)$ structure is honestly self-labeled (philosophy-of-science).** The subsection header at :3067 is "Hypothesized $(3+1)$ Structure (Speculative)"; the text enumerates three candidate mechanisms as alternatives rather than asserting one; and :3071 states "it makes no quantitative predictions about measured spacetime properties that could be tested experimentally." On the demarcation standard [Popper1959], a passage that labels its central physical identification speculative and disclaims testable predictions is flagging an unfalsifiable conjecture as such — the honest move. The failure mode this lens polices is the reverse (speculation dressed as result); the labeling here runs the opposite direction.

## Concessions (load-bearing — the claim is stated to exclude these)

1. **The sector-size and eigenvalue hierarchies (:3058, :3062) are a modeling posit, not a theorem.** With two free thresholds, nothing in the spectral decomposition forces $|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{subthresh}}|\ll|\mathcal D_{\text{internal}}|$ or $\lambda_{\text{obs}}\gg\lambda_{\text{subthresh}}\gg\lambda_{\text{internal}}$. The defense covers well-definedness of the partition, not derivation of the ordering. The section presents the sectors as definitions (:3023 "We define") and hedges the sizes ("may comprise a large number ... depending on the complexity," :3037), which keeps the posit honest, but it is a posit.
2. **Identifying the observable sector with perceived spacetime (:3065) is interpretive.** "We may suppose that the observable sector corresponds to the $(1+3)$ dimensions we perceive" is a reading, not a derivation; taken as an explanation of spacetime it would import the explanandum. The subjunctive mood ("may suppose," "we conjecture," "we hypothesize") and the immediately-following "(Speculative)" subsection cushion it, but it is interpretive and is conceded as such.
3. **Non-vacuity depends on $\dim(\mathcal C)$ being large.** The decomposition is non-trivial only when $\dim\mathcal C$ is large enough to host three sectors; the section brackets this explicitly ("when $\dim(\mathcal C)$ is large enough to accommodate them," :3021). Whether the framework's actual base manifold has such a dimension in any given construction is a separate scope question, distinct from the correctness of the bookkeeping, and the defense does not assert non-vacuity for any particular construction.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following hold.

1. **Pullback algebra fails.** If $\sigma_i^* g_{\mathcal B}$ could have rank $>\dim\mathcal C$, or fail to be PSD when $g_{\mathcal B}$ is PSD. Settled against this by [Lee2013, Ch. 11/13] (rank bound and PSD-preservation are theorems). Unmet.
2. **The dimension count is wrong.** If $\dim(\mathcal B)\ne K(K+3)/2$, or $K=768\not\Rightarrow296{,}064$, or $g_{\mathcal B}$ were indefinite (which would unseat $\lambda_a\ge0$). The Gaussian count is standard [AmariNagaoka2000, Ch. 2], the arithmetic checks, and the Fisher metric is PSD [Cencov1972]. Unmet.
3. **The spectral partition is ill-defined.** If a symmetric PSD $G_i$ could lack a real orthonormal eigenbasis or non-negative eigenvalues, or if the threshold partition failed to reconstruct $G_i$. Settled against by the spectral theorem [doCarmo1992]. Unmet.
4. **The $(3+1)$ structure is asserted, not hypothesized.** If $|\mathcal D_{\text{obs}}|=4$ were stated as established, or a testable quantitative prediction were claimed. The header is "(Speculative)" (:3067) and :3071 disclaims testable predictions. Unmet within :2994–3074.
5. **The supposition leaks downstream as established.** If the :3065 spacetime identification, or the underived hierarchy, is used in a *later* section as if proven, the "honestly labeled" claim fails for the manuscript as a whole. This is the one live risk; it lies outside the :2994–3074 span and is flagged for the judges to check against the sections that follow.

The claim, as narrowed by the three concessions, survives every falsification condition that lies inside the debated span. The mathematical core (pullback bookkeeping, dimension count, spectral partition, externalized temporal sign) is correct, and the $(3+1)$ identification is honestly self-labeled speculative. The residual exposure is entirely about downstream usage and about the hierarchy being a posit — both conceded, neither fatal to the narrowed claim.
