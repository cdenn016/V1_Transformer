## Decisive evidence
[Cencov1972] + [AmariNagaoka2000, Ch. 2] — both verified entries in `external_bibliography.md`. Fisher invariance is invariance of the metric *tensor* under sufficient statistics, NOT invariance of the *numerical eigenvalues* of a pullback under base reparameterization. Under a base chart change $c\mapsto\psi(c)$ with Jacobian $J$ the pullback transforms as $\tilde G=J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, so eigenvalues rescale by the squared singular values of $J$. The sectors are defined at :3026/:3034/:3042 by absolute thresholds on raw eigenvalue *magnitudes* ($\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\rm obs}$, etc.), and the in-span hierarchy at :3061–3062 is stated in absolute magnitudes. An absolute eigenvalue threshold therefore names a coordinate chart, not an intrinsic geometric direction; the only reparameterization-invariant spectral content (rank, eigenvalue ratios, relative gaps) is not what the section uses to define the sectors. Blue conceded this verbatim.

## Reasoning
The rubric for RED_WINS is met: Red's weighted total on the contested proposition exceeds Blue's, and Red holds at least one verified external-canon citation that contradicts the claim as written. [Cencov1972] and [AmariNagaoka2000 Ch.2] establish that pullback eigenvalue magnitudes are coordinate-dependent, so a partition defined by absolute thresholds $\lambda_a>\Lambda_{\rm obs}$ (:3026/:3034/:3042) names a base chart rather than an intrinsic geometric structure — directly contradicting the claim that "the spectral decomposition ... into ... sectors by eigenvalue magnitude is well-defined." Blue conceded this in its rebuttal ("Red is right on this") and conceded that the :3057–3062 hierarchy is a posit, not a theorem, with no exhibited eigengaps [Davis–Kahan; Weyl]. The manuscript convicts its own in-span text: :3055 introduces the hierarchy with "satisfy" and states :3057–3058 and :3061–3062 unconditionally as spectral properties, while the boxed summary at :3188 writes "under an *assumed* eigenvalue hierarchy" and :3190 lists "the eigenvalue hierarchy itself, including the choice of three large eigenvalues in $V_{\rm obs}$" under "What is postulated," closing "None of (i)–(iv) is derived." An unconditional in-span assertion of a property external canon shows to be chart-dependent, and which the manuscript itself elsewhere concedes is postulated, is not "mathematically sound" as written — it is a posit formatted as a derived spectral property. Blue's verified canon (Lee, Amari arithmetic, doCarmo) is correct but supports only the parts Red grants (bookkeeping, dimension count, set-theoretic partition); it does not reach the contested magnitude-soundness claim. The signature line at :3029 ("the 4-dimensional sector admits a $(1+3)$ Lorentzian decomposition") independently overreads a PSD spectrum [Nakahara2003 §7.1–7.2; Sylvester], inheriting Debate 2's REMAND, though the same sentence's self-flag mitigates it. Canon-strict verdict is rendered on the text as written; whether the chief treats this as a wording/disclosure REMAND rather than a substantive defeat is the chief's and scope judge's call, not this judge's — my mandate is uncompromising external-canon adherence, and the in-span text fails it.
