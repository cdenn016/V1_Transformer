# Red Opening — rock-example-meta-agent

## Steelman (opposing position)

The rock section is illustrative pedagogy within the framework's already-stated pan-agentic commitment; the inertia / consensus / back-action intuitions follow from natural-gradient flow on $\mathcal{F}$ with $\Sigma_{\text{rock}} \approx \epsilon I$, the cross-observer convergence is a transitive coupling argument under the framework's transport law, the back-action vanishing is the $O(\epsilon)$ scaling of $\dot\mu_{\text{rock}} \propto \Sigma_{\text{rock}}$, and the section's looser language about photons / proper time / phenomenal geometry is invited by the framework's commitment to treat every system at every scale as an agent.

## Position

The compound claim fails on C2 (appropriateness of framing): the rock section at `Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:3837-3851` contains three in-manuscript contradictions with the framework's own scope and hedge statements. The section asserts in plain language exactly the extensions the manuscript explicitly disavows elsewhere — the quantum extension, the relativistic proper-time identification, and the precision-equals-physical-mass identification — without flagging the disavowal. C1 (derivability of inertia / consensus / back-action from natural-gradient flow) is largely defensible and red does not contest it; the failure is in framing, where the section reproduces the pre-hedge versions of claims the manuscript has elsewhere qualified.

## Evidence

**1. Quantum-extension contradiction.** Line 3849 reads: "For microscopic quantum particles with broad wavefunctions (low Fisher information, high uncertainty), the perturbation can be significant: $\Delta q_{\text{particle}}$ may be large, corresponding to quantum measurement back-action. The classical-quantum distinction emerges from the magnitude of Fisher information rather than from qualitatively different dynamics." This is a substantive extension of the framework to quantum-mechanical scenarios. The same manuscript at line 2024 (immediately following the hedged §`sec:mass`) states: "We do not extend this analogy to quantum-mechanical scenarios: spatial delocalization of a quantum particle does not imply lower inertial mass in standard quantum mechanics, and the framework's effective mass is a configuration-space stiffness that we are not equating with physical inertial mass here." The scope-and-limitations section at line 146 states the same prohibition at the framework level: "**No quantum extension.** A rigorous quantum version of the framework does not currently exist; the structural parallels with QBism and other relational interpretations are suggestive rather than derived." Line 3166 (also in this manuscript) reinforces it: "The analogy is suggestive only. The framework contains no quantum-mechanical formalism — no Hilbert space, no Born rule, no superposition states — so it cannot derive the measurement problem's resolution." Line 3849 asserts the very extension that lines 146, 2024, and 3166 disavow. Beyond the in-manuscript inconsistency, the claim itself fails on its face against standard quantum mechanics, where measurement back-action is a statement about the Born-rule projector and continuous monitoring [Wiseman & Milburn 2009, *Quantum Measurement and Control* Ch. 1-3], not a statement about Fisher information of a Gaussian belief.

**2. Proper-time contradiction.** Line 3845 reads: "The rock's proper time $\tau_{\text{rock}}$ advances slowly because information updates occur infrequently - the system is near equilibrium. This formalizes the intuition that massive objects have inertia and resist changes in state." The phrase "proper time" carries a specific relativistic meaning, and the gloss "advances slowly... near equilibrium... massive objects" is the gravitational-redshift / slow-clock intuition from general relativity. Line 3070 of the same manuscript (in the Pan-Agentic Ontology subsection) directly disowns this identification: "Time is plural in the same sense: each agent's Fisher arc length $\tau_i$ (Section~\ref{sec:fisher_arc_length}) is its own clock. Relativistic proper time is not reproduced under the present compact-group construction --- Fisher arc length is Riemannian and positive-definite while proper time is Lorentzian, and the two run in opposite directions with motion. Whether any quantity here reproduces relativistic proper time requires the indefinite-metric extension of Section~\ref{sec:signature_resolution} and remains open." Line 2532 in §`sec:fisher_arc_length` reinforces this: "We do not identify this quantity with relativistic proper time: as discussed in detail in Section~\ref{sec:fisher_arc_length}, Fisher arc length and special-relativistic proper time have opposite character (positive-definite Riemannian versus indefinite Lorentzian) and run in opposite directions with motion. The connection to physical time dilation, if there is one, requires the indefinite-metric extension of Section~\ref{sec:signature_resolution} and is not established here." The rock section uses the exact phrase the framework explicitly disowns at two other locations, and pairs it with the slow-clock gloss that evokes physical time dilation.

**3. Mass-identification contradiction.** Line 3849 contains the parenthetical "(high mass)" attached to "the rock's large Fisher information," and line 3845 says the Fisher-information argument "formalizes the intuition that massive objects have inertia." The hedged §`sec:mass` at line 2015 says explicitly: "Throughout this subsection, the identification of the Hessian sector $[M_{\mu\mu}]$ with an 'effective mass' is interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass... the effective mass we obtain has dimensions inherited from $\Sigma^{-1}$ (dimensionless on the framework's representational space) rather than the kilogram dimension of physical mass." Line 2024 reinforces: "the framework's effective mass is a configuration-space stiffness that we are not equating with physical inertial mass here." The rock section's "(high mass)" parenthetical equates Fisher information with mass without the §`sec:mass` qualifier — it is the pre-hedge version of a claim the manuscript has elsewhere committed to qualifying.

**4. Photon-as-agent label without construction (subsidiary).** Line 3847 calls photons "agents" that "function as information carriers." The agent definition at §`sec:agent_definition` line 613 requires every agent $\mathcal{A}^i$ to have primitive belief $q_i$, primitive generative model $s_i$, derived prior $p_i = \Omega_{i,I}[q_I^{(s+1)}]$, derived hyper-prior $r_i$, and gauge frame $\phi_i \in \mathfrak{g}$, with a defined support domain $\mathcal{U}_i \subseteq \mathcal{C}$ and an enveloping meta-agent index $I$. The framework's pan-agentic commitment at §`sec:cognitive_first` line 119 ("an electron is a scale-0 agent, a molecule a higher-scale meta-agent") authorizes the ontology for matter constituents but does not specify what the photon's $q_\gamma, s_\gamma, p_\gamma, r_\gamma, \phi_\gamma$ are, what scale a photon inhabits, or what meta-agent $I$ envelops a photon-in-flight. Photons are quantum bosons in standard QED — quantum field excitations carrying polarization and four-momentum, with no classical $(q, p, s, r, \phi)$ structure available without the quantum extension that line 146 says does not exist. The "photon agent" terminology is a label, not a construction; the manuscript would need either to identify the photon's five-tuple explicitly or to flag the label as informal usage.

**5. "Sufficient coherence" without naming the criterion (subsidiary).** Line 3843 says: "When these atomic agents achieve sufficient coherence (small KL divergences between transported beliefs), they form a scale-1 meta-agent — the rock itself." The framework provides three distinct meta-agent formation criteria: the variational free-energy improvement criterion at §`sec:meta_agent_variational` line 2063 (`eq:meta_agent_FE_criterion`), the threshold detector at §`sec:meta_agent_threshold` (the implementation-side surrogate), and the information-bottleneck refinement at Eq. `eq:meta_agent_IB` (a research direction). Line 140 of scope-and-limitations notes the variational rule "is not itself evaluated continuously in the simulation; whether the discrete detector and the continuous variational rule produce the same hierarchical organization is open." The rock section asserts meta-agent formation without naming which criterion is satisfied for a real crystalline lattice at finite temperature (where pairwise KL between thermally fluctuating atoms is small but nonzero). This is a soft strike on its own — illustrative sections need not formalize every claim — but it compounds the framing issues above.

- [Amari1998 §4]: natural gradient $\tilde\nabla_\mu = \Sigma \nabla_\mu$ — the inertia-from-precision result (C1.1 of the evidence pack) is canonical and red does not contest it.

The framework's claim that "the classical-quantum distinction emerges from the magnitude of Fisher information" is not a statement of standard QM but a within-framework reading the manuscript should label as such.

## Falsification conditions

This position (that C2 fails) is wrong if any of the following hold:

(i) Line 3849's "classical-quantum distinction emerges from the magnitude of Fisher information" is *not* the kind of quantum extension that line 146 ("No quantum extension") and line 2024 ("We do not extend this analogy to quantum-mechanical scenarios") disavow — i.e., blue can show that asserting "$\Delta q_{\text{particle}}$ may be large, corresponding to quantum measurement back-action" is consistent with "the framework contains no quantum-mechanical formalism — no Hilbert space, no Born rule, no superposition states" (line 3166).

(ii) Line 3845's "proper time $\tau_{\text{rock}}$ advances slowly because information updates occur infrequently" is *not* the kind of physical proper-time identification that line 3070 ("Relativistic proper time is not reproduced under the present compact-group construction") and line 2532 ("We do not identify this quantity with relativistic proper time") disown — i.e., blue can show the rock section's "proper time" is a different object from the manuscript's "Fisher arc length $\tau_i$" object, and the slow-clock gloss does not invoke relativistic time dilation.

(iii) Line 3849's "(high mass)" parenthetical is *not* the kind of mass identification that line 2015 ("interpretive within the framework rather than a derivation of physical inertial mass") and line 2024 ("the framework's effective mass is... not... physical inertial mass here") flag as needing qualification — i.e., blue can show the parenthetical is read by every plausible reader as a within-framework stiffness shorthand rather than a Fisher-info-equals-physical-mass identification.

If blue concedes (i)–(iii) and proposes specific edits hedging the rock section to match the §`sec:mass` and §`sec:scope_limitations` qualifications, the framing failure collapses to a localized cleanup; the section's C1 derivability content (inertia, consensus, back-action) survives.
