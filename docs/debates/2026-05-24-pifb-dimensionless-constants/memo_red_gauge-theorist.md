# Memo — debate-expert-gauge-theorist (red, opening)

## Steelman of the claim
By naming "comparison structure" alongside "statistical manifolds" and "Cencov scale fixings"
as preconditions (:3133), the manuscript correctly identifies that an α-derivation requires a
rule for comparing belief geometries across agents/fibers — i.e., a connection / transport rule
— and presents this as a thing to be "specified," signaling awareness that the gauge structure
matters.

## Falsification of the claim
"Comparison structure" is the gauge-theoretic crux, and naming it as a precondition makes the
test less symmetric, not more. In this framework the comparison between agents is the transport
Ω_ij = exp(φ_i)exp(−φ_j) acting by the sandwich Σ → ΩΣΩ^T, with the gauge group GL+(K) and a
chosen irrep decomposition. A gauge connection is not unique: the framework admits a flat
default and an optional non-flat (MLP-mode) transport, different irrep dimensions, and a
choice of group (GL(K,R) vs the GL(K,C) invoked for the Lorentzian-signature pathway at :3109).
Each choice is a different "comparison structure," and a dimensionless number extracted from a
holonomy or a coupling ratio depends on which connection and which irrep you pick. There is no
canonical, physics-fixed connection here that the geometry forces; the connection is precisely
the gauge freedom. So "specify the comparison structure" is not a constraint that pins α — it is
an instruction to make a choice, and the space of choices is large enough to reach many targets.
Worse, :3109 already concedes that the temporal generator in the GL(K,C) signature pathway is
"acknowledged as imposed by hand," which is an explicit admission that a load-bearing piece of
the comparison/signature structure is chosen by fiat rather than derived. Gauge freedom that is
fixed by hand is the textbook mechanism by which a post-hoc match is engineered: pick the gauge
that makes the number come out, then declare the construction "fully developed." This is the same
deferral the philosophy lens identifies, now with a concrete generator: the connection/gauge
choice is the auxiliary hypothesis in the protective belt.

## External primary-source citation
Nakahara, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* (2nd ed., 2003), Ch. 10–11 [Nakahara2003]: a
connection on a principal bundle is additional structure, not determined by the base manifold;
parallel transport (and hence holonomy) depends on the chosen connection. Distinct connections
give distinct holonomy groups and distinct transported quantities. Therefore any dimensionless
invariant built from transport/holonomy is connection-dependent unless the connection is fixed by
an independent principle — which "specify the comparison structure" (:3133) does not provide.

## Falsification condition for THIS memo
This attack fails if the manuscript fixes the connection/comparison structure by a stated
invariance or uniqueness principle (e.g., the unique flat connection compatible with a stated
symmetry) such that the α-relevant holonomy/coupling ratio is connection-independent, with the
gauge group and irrep also fixed in advance. The :3133 text treats all three (manifold,
comparison structure, Cencov scale) as items to be "specified" later, and :3109 admits at least
one generator is "imposed by hand," so the gauge freedom remains open.
