# Memo — debate-expert-numerical-analyst (red, opening)

## Steelman of the claim
A dimensionless target is the right kind of thing to predict — no unit-conversion gap, no kg↔nat
problem — so "derive α and m_e/m_p from information geometry" is at least a well-posed numerical
target, and pinning a pure number to many digits would be a strong success signal.

## Falsification of the claim
Two numerical facts break the symmetric-test claim from the success side, which the philosophy
lens cannot supply alone.

First, α ≈ 1/137 is not a fixed pure number. It is a running coupling: α ≈ 1/137.036 at low
energy and "at the scale of the Z boson, about 90 GeV, one instead measures an effective
α ≈ 1/127" (Wikipedia "Fine-structure constant," standard QED running). "Deriving 1/137" is
therefore not even a target until a renormalization scale is chosen, and that scale choice is an
extra free input the :3131 schema ("ratios of coupling strengths or Fisher information scales")
does not fix. A program that "derives" 1/137 has implicitly chosen the low-energy scale post hoc;
the same machinery aimed at 1/127 would claim the Z scale. This is a textbook degree of freedom
that makes any single-target "success" non-diagnostic.

Second, there is a direct historical instance of exactly the failure mode the manuscript claims
to escape. Eddington (1929) "conjectured that the reciprocal of the fine-structure constant was
not approximately but precisely the integer 137" and tried to derive it from first principles;
"by the 1940s experimental values for 1/α deviated sufficiently from 137 to refute Eddington's
arguments" (Wikipedia "Fine-structure constant"). Wyler (1969) produced a closed-form match to
±1.5 ppm that Adler (1972) dismissed as "a number in search of a theory," noting whether the
agreement "has a basis in physics, or is purely fortuitous, remains completely open." "No
numerological explanation has ever been accepted by the physics community." This is the
empirical record: pure-structure derivations of α that hit the target to several digits have
repeatedly been judged non-confirmatory. So the manuscript's claimed symmetry is broken on BOTH
ends — failure is deferred to "not fully developed" (philosophy lens), and success, even to ppm,
historically fails to confirm any particular metaphysics (numerical/historical record). A test
whose success cannot confirm and whose failure cannot refute is not symmetric; it is empty.

## Falsification condition for THIS memo
This attack fails if the program specifies, in advance, both the renormalization scale at which
its derived α is to be compared AND a target precision with error bars, and then a constant-free
derivation hits that target within stated error — i.e., a genuine pre-registered point prediction
rather than a post-hoc match to "≈1/137." The :3131 text fixes neither scale nor precision.
