## Core attack

The claim under evaluation asserts the criterion has a "**GENUINELY SYMMETRIC** success/failure
criterion" (00_claim.md). Blue's own opening defeats that word. Blue concedes "the criterion
names no party who certifies 'fully developed,' so its symmetric trigger is **aspirational**
until the three preconditions are concretely instantiated" and that "the SUFFICIENCY of the
symmetric criterion does not [survive]; the manuscript should not be read as having delivered an
operational test, only a self-binding commitment to build one" (02_blue_opening.md, Concession).
A criterion that is aspirational, not yet operational, and whose failure-side trigger ("fully
developed construction") has no arbiter is — by blue's own characterization — *aspirationally*
symmetric, not *genuinely* symmetric. The two words are not synonyms. "Genuinely symmetric"
asserts the test is symmetric now; "aspirational until preconditions instantiated" asserts it
is not symmetric now and might become so. The claim overstates exactly the modality blue concedes.

This is not a quibble; it is Popper's advance-specification requirement applied to the failure
side. Popper demands that "criteria of refutation have to be laid down beforehand: it must be
agreed which observable situations, if actually observed, mean that the theory is refuted" (SEP
"Karl Popper", reporting *Logic of Scientific Discovery* §20). The success side of the :3133
criterion meets this: derive α and the test corroborates. The failure side does not: failure
counts against the interpretation only "under a fully developed information-geometric
construction," and what makes a construction "fully developed" is not laid down beforehand — it
is, on blue's concession, left to a party the manuscript never names. A test whose refuting
condition is specified in advance on the success side but not on the failure side is asymmetric
in precisely Popper's sense. "Genuinely symmetric" is the wrong word for it.

Blue's load-bearing rescue is the Lakatosian-positive-heuristic reframe (Vector 1): the three
named preconditions plus the anti-deferral clause are "a path toward a refutable variant," not a
protective belt. That reframe fails on Lakatos's own criterion. A positive heuristic earns
progressive standing only when the programme "predicts novel, hitherto unexpected facts" and
"some novel predictions are corroborated" (SEP "Imre Lakatos", reporting FMSRP:34); a programme
degenerates when it names commitments but delivers no corroborated novel prediction and instead
"explained away unpredicted 'observations' after the event." The manuscript names three
preconditions (manifolds, comparison structure, Cencov scale fixings) and a target (α, m_e/m_p),
but it supplies no *novel* prediction — α and m_e/m_p are century-old measured constants, not
hitherto-unexpected facts — and no corroboration. Naming a development path is the *form* of a
positive heuristic; Lakatos requires the *substance* of a corroborated novel prediction, which
:3129–:3133 does not provide. So blue's reframe does not convert the asymmetry into a virtue; it
relabels an as-yet-empty commitment with the vocabulary of a progressive one.

The "three preconditions are a checklist, not pure rhetoric" defense (blue Vector 1, numerical-
analyst fold-in) collapses under two of red's own lenses, because each checklist item is itself
unbounded. Čencov's theorem fixes the Fisher–Rao metric as the unique invariant under sufficient
statistics *only up to a positive multiplicative constant* (Chentsov's theorem, citing Čencov
1972/1981; Ay–Jost–Lê–Schwachhöfer arXiv:1207.6736, "uniquely up to a constant"). "Cencov scale
fixings are all specified" is therefore not a box the geometry lets you tick — it is a free
scalar the program must supply by fiat, and a cross-sector ratio for α carries one such free
constant per manifold. The "comparison structure" item is worse: a connection on a principal
bundle is independent additional structure, not fixed by the base manifold, and parallel
transport and holonomy are connection-dependent (Nakahara, *Geometry, Topology and Physics* 2nd
ed., Ch. 10–11). Any dimensionless invariant built from the transport Ω is connection-dependent
unless an external principle fixes the connection, which :3133 does not provide — it instructs
the program to "specify" the comparison structure, i.e., to *choose* one. A checklist whose every
entry gates further unbounded choices does not bound deferral; it relocates it. Blue's
"finite, decidable objects" reading mistakes *named* for *bounded*.

## Defense

Red's opening position was that the :3133 criterion is asymmetric and the symmetry claim
overstated. Blue's concession converts this from a contested charge into a near-agreement on the
operative word: blue grants the trigger is aspirational and the criterion not yet operational.
The residual disagreement is narrow and resolvable by wording. The honest statement of the
manuscript's position — the one blue has functionally endorsed — is "**aspirationally** symmetric,
pending concrete specification of the three preconditions *and* of a completion criterion (and an
arbiter) for declaring the construction fully developed." That is not the claim as written. The
claim as written says "GENUINELY SYMMETRIC."

Blue is right that this is incompleteness, not dishonesty, in the manuscript's drafting intent —
red does not allege the authors meant to deceive. But the debate evaluates the *claim*, not the
authors' intent, and the claim's word "genuinely" asserts a property blue concedes the criterion
lacks. The appropriate outcome is REMAND with a wording fix: replace "genuinely symmetric" with
"aspirationally symmetric, pending instantiation of three preconditions whose own completion
criteria are themselves open," and supply at :3133 either an external invariance principle that
fixes the Čencov constant and the connection (so the α-route is constant-free and connection-
independent [Chentsov 1972/1981; Nakahara2003 Ch. 10–11]) or a dated, independently-checkable
novel-prediction milestone that discharges Lakatos's progressive criterion (FMSRP:34). Until one
of those is in the text, "genuinely symmetric" is the overstatement, and the manuscript's own
:3109 reinterpretation latitude ("any measurement can be reinterpreted as labeling information
geometry") supplies exactly the route by which a missed α is relabeled rather than counted —
which is the asymmetry the word "genuinely" papers over.
