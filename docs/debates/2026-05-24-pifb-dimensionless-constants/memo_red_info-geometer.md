# Memo — debate-expert-info-geometer (red, opening)

## Steelman of the claim
Naming "Cencov scale fixings" as a precondition (:3133) shows the authors know the Fisher metric
is the canonical invariant on a statistical manifold and that a scale must be pinned; this is the
right vocabulary and signals a serious, not hand-wavy, target.

## Falsification of the claim
The invocation of "Cencov scale fixings" actually undercuts the symmetric-test claim. Cencov's
theorem fixes the Fisher–Rao metric as the unique metric invariant under sufficient statistics /
Markov morphisms ONLY UP TO A POSITIVE MULTIPLICATIVE CONSTANT. The constant is not determined by
the theorem; it is a free choice. So "Cencov scale fixings are all specified" (:3133) is not a
fact the geometry hands you — it is an external input the program must supply by fiat. A
dimensionless target like α = 1/137 is a pure number, and the proposed route is "ratios of
coupling strengths or Fisher information scales" (:3131). A ratio of Fisher scales cancels the
overall Cencov constant only if the SAME constant is used in numerator and denominator on the
SAME manifold; the moment two different statistical manifolds (or two different irrep sectors)
are compared — which "comparison structure" (:3133) explicitly contemplates — each carries its
own undetermined Cencov constant, and their ratio is tunable. That tunability is precisely the
free structure that lets a determined investigator hit any small target post hoc. The "comparison
structure" is therefore not a constraint that makes the test symmetric; it is the degree of
freedom that makes failure deferrable and success uninformative.

This is consistent with the manuscript's own honest disclosure at :3119 that the mass↔Fisher
identification "is a definitional consequence of that identification rather than an empirical
dispersion law, and no operationally independent measurement is reported." The same definitional/
free-constant character infects the α route: a ratio of Fisher scales is whatever the chosen
manifold-and-constant package makes it, with no operationally independent pin.

## External primary-source citation
Chentsov (Čencov) theorem: "The Fisher information metric is, up to rescaling, the unique
Riemannian metric on a statistical manifold that is invariant under sufficient statistics"
(Wikipedia "Chentsov's theorem", citing Čencov 1972/1981; corroborated by Ay & Jost et al.,
"Information geometry and sufficient statistics," arXiv:1207.6736: the Fisher metric and
Amari–Chentsov tensor are characterized "uniquely (up to a constant)" by invariance). The
"up to a constant" is the load-bearing clause. [Cencov1972] / [AmariNagaoka2000].

## Falsification condition for THIS memo
This attack fails if the manuscript exhibits a derivation in which the Cencov constant cancels
identically (so the α-ratio is constant-independent) AND the comparison structure between the
two manifolds is itself fixed by an invariance principle rather than chosen — i.e., if the route
to α has zero free real parameters after the canonical Fisher metric is imposed. The text gives
no such derivation; "ratios of coupling strengths or Fisher information scales" (:3131) is a
schema with open scale choices, not a constant-free invariant.
