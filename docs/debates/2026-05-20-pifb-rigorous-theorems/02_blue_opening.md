# Blue Opening — pifb-rigorous-theorems

## Steelman (opposing position)

The strongest red attack is that Theorem `thm:rg_exact_closure` calls its construction "augmented-class closure" while the augmenting potential $V(Y) = \tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det' A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ is generated by the parent Fisher information and re-enters the next-scale block Hessian, so the "class" under iteration is not actually closed in a fixed-point sense — what is iterable is the *form* of the augmentation, not the parameter space at a fixed scale; the manuscript conflates "iterable" with "closed."

## Position

The Rigorous Theorem-Level Statements appendix at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:4592-4767` is mathematically correct and adequately scoped: every theorem-grade statement follows from the standard external literature (Bogachev measure-theoretic pushforward, Afsari/Karcher Riemannian center of mass, Wilson-Cardy effective-action, Wong-Bender-Orszag Laplace expansion, Cover-Thomas Pinsker, Amari Fisher-information Lipschitz bound) under the explicit hypotheses each theorem registers, and the appendix discharges its body-level invocation honestly by separating theorem-grade content from proposition-grade content and from registered future-work claims.

## Evidence

**E1. Pushforward and semigroup (Theorems `thm:rg_pushforward`, `thm:rg_semigroup`) are textbook measure theory.** The proofs at `:4623-4625` and `:4632-4641` are the standard change-of-variables / pre-image composition arguments. Bogachev 2007 *Measure Theory* §3.6 gives the change-of-variables identity $\int A \, d\mu_* = \int A \circ T \, d\mu$ for any measurable $T$ and bounded measurable $A$; Folland 1999 §2.6 gives the pre-image composition $(T \circ S)^{-1}(A) = S^{-1}(T^{-1}(A))$. The proofs are line-for-line the textbook arguments. No hidden assumption beyond measurability.

**E2. Gauge covariance (Theorem `thm:rg_covariance`) follows from three standard ingredients explicitly assumed.** The proof at `:4653-4655` uses (a) bi-invariance of the Riemannian metric on $G = \mathrm{SO}(K)$ — Kobayashi-Nomizu Vol. I §4.2 establishes that every compact Lie group admits a bi-invariant metric, and that $d_G(hU, hV) = d_G(U, V)$ is the defining property; (b) KL invariance under common invertible pushforward — Cover-Thomas 2006 Thm 2.4.1 (data-processing for KL), or equivalently the direct change-of-variables identity for the Radon-Nikodym derivative under an invertible measurable transformation; (c) forward-KL barycenter uniqueness in the exponential family — moment matching makes the M-projection a strictly convex minimization on the natural parameter space of the Gaussian family. Combining these gives $\mathcal{R}_s(h \cdot X_s) = h \cdot \mathcal{R}_s(X_s)$ by direct substitution. The hypothesis "weights $w_i^I$ are gauge-invariant functions of gauge-invariant quantities" at `:4650` is the only nontrivial closure hypothesis on the framework; the manuscript states it explicitly.

**E3. Augmented-class closure (Theorem `thm:rg_exact_closure`) is the canonical Wilsonian effective-action correction at the Gaussian saddle, and the appendix explicitly admits the strict-closure case is empty in the operational regime.** The Schur complement $A_{\mathrm{eff}} = A_{YY} - A_{Y\xi}A_{\xi\xi}^{-1}A_{\xi Y}$ at `:4671` is the standard result of completing the square in the Gaussian fluctuation integral — Bishop 2006 *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* §2.3.2 Eq. 2.81 gives this exact form for marginalizing one block of a joint Gaussian. The fluctuation-determinant log-det $\tfrac{\tau}{2}\log\det' A_{\xi\xi}(Y)$ at `:4676` is the Wilsonian effective-action correction — Polchinski 1984 *Nucl. Phys. B* 231 §2 and Zinn-Justin 2002 §10.2-10.4 give exactly this term as the one-loop fluctuation correction when fast modes are integrated out, and the form is preserved under iteration because each subsequent integration produces another log-det that adds to the local potential. The Remark at `:4684-4686` explicitly registers that strict Gaussian-KL closure ($A_{\xi\xi}$ independent of $Y$) is "empty in the operational regime of this manuscript" because $A_{\xi\xi}$ inherits $Y$-dependence through the parent Fisher information. This is honest scoping: the theorem's substantive content is the augmented form, not strict closure.

**E4. The C_5 Wick coefficients are verified by direct sympy computation.** Run on $S(x) = \tfrac{1}{2}m x^2 + \tfrac{g_3}{6}x^3 + \tfrac{g_4}{24}x^4$ with the Gibbs weight $e^{-S/\tau}$:

So $-\tau \log(Z/Z_0) = \tfrac{g_4 \tau^2}{8 m^2} - \tfrac{5 g_3^2 \tau^2}{24 m^3} + O(\tau^3)$.
