## Canon excerpts (teams should expand via WebFetch / literature-review)

### Information geometry / Fisher-Rao canon

- **Amari, S., Nagaoka, H. (2000)**, *Methods of Information Geometry*, AMS — §2.2, §3.2: Fisher-Rao metric on the Gaussian family is positive-definite; the statistical manifold has Riemannian (not Lorentzian) structure under standard conditions.
- **Pistone, G., Sempi, C. (1995)**, "An infinite-dimensional geometric structure on the space of all the probability measures equivalent to a given one," *Annals of Statistics* 23(5), 1543-1561 — extension of Fisher-Rao to non-parametric distributions; remains Riemannian.

### General relativity / gauge theory canon

- **Wald, R. M. (1984)**, *General Relativity*, University of Chicago Press — §3.1: spacetime is a 4-manifold equipped with a Lorentzian metric of signature $(-,+,+,+)$; §4.3: Einstein field equations $G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G T_{\mu\nu}$ relate Ricci curvature to stress-energy.
- **Misner, C. W., Thorne, K. S., Wheeler, J. A. (1973)**, *Gravitation*, Freeman — §16: equivalence principle in mathematical form; §17: stress-energy as the source of curvature.
- **Nakahara, M. (2003)**, *Geometry, Topology and Physics*, 2nd ed., IoP — §7.10: connection forms and curvature on principal bundles; §7.11: difference between Yang-Mills curvature and Riemann curvature.
- **Baez, J. and Muniain, J. (1994)**, *Gauge Fields, Knots and Gravity*, World Scientific — Part II: gravity as a gauge theory of diffeomorphisms (Diff(M) is NOT GL(K); the gauge group structure differs at root from Yang-Mills theories).

### Emergent gravity proposals (relevant precedents for "speculative gravity from information")

- **Verlinde, E. (2011)**, "On the origin of gravitational and inertia," arXiv:1001.0785, *JHEP*. The canonical "emergent gravity" proposal — derives Newton's second law and the Einstein equations from holographic / entropic arguments. Hedged but substantive. Provides a precedent for the manuscript's epistemic move.
- **Padmanabhan, T. (2010)**, "Thermodynamical aspects of gravity: new insights," *Reports on Progress in Physics* 73(4), 046901. Derives Einstein equations from horizon thermodynamics. Provides another emergent-gravity precedent.
- **Jacobson, T. (1995)**, "Thermodynamics of spacetime: the Einstein equation of state," *Physical Review Letters* 75(7), 1260. The seminal derivation of the Einstein equation from a thermodynamic identity (Clausius relation applied to a local Rindler horizon).
- **Caticha, A. (2015)**, *Entropic Inference and the Foundations of Physics*, AIP Conference Proceedings — explicit information-geometric route toward physical dynamics; remains short of deriving GR.
- **Caticha, A. (2021)**, "The entropic dynamics of relativistic quantum fields," *Foundations of Physics* — extends the entropic-dynamics approach; relevant comparison for the manuscript's framing.

### Dimensional analysis canon (relevant to the R∝Σ^{-1} ansatz)

- **Bridgman, P. W. (1922)**, *Dimensional Analysis*, Yale University Press. The standard form of dimensional analysis. The Riemann tensor has dimensions $[\text{length}]^{-2}$; if $\Sigma_{s}$ has dimensions of (representation space variance), then $\Sigma_s^{-1}$ has dimensions of inverse representation-space variance. Equating these requires a dimensional bridge.

## What this evidence does NOT settle

1. Whether the manuscript's self-applied "(Speculative)" title label is sufficient as a global qualifier on the subsection, or whether specific load-bearing equations (R∝Σ^{-1}; the structural pullback formula at 3105) need stronger inline hedging despite the surrounding paragraphs being well-hedged.
2. Whether the "honest cost" framing at 3114 of the inertia-gravity precision decoupling actually preserves any substantive prediction, or whether the decoupling silently neutralizes the original mass-analogy motivation while pretending to be a refinement.
3. Whether comparison to canonical emergent-gravity proposals (Verlinde, Padmanabhan, Jacobson) is helpful — those proposals derive *more* than the manuscript does (Verlinde derives Newton's law; Jacobson derives Einstein's equation; the manuscript stops at "the curvature tensors have not been computed"). If the manuscript is "speculative" in a weaker sense than even Verlinde/Padmanabhan/Jacobson, the speculative label may not be the right label.
4. Whether the absence of a derived Einstein-equation analogue (manuscript explicitly registers this at 3121) is consistent with the section's central claim that the framework provides a "candidate" gravitational reading — if no field-equation analogue exists, what is being predicted?
5. Whether the Lorentzian-signature mechanism via the GL(K,C) imaginary-frame postulate (sec:worked_signature) is structurally analogous to Wick rotation in physics (a standard if non-trivial move) or to ad-hoc reverse-engineering (the structural argument needs to be primary-source verified).
6. Whether the gauge-group mismatch between GL(K) (the framework) and Diff(M) (general relativity per Baez-Muniain Part II) is a fatal obstruction to ever recovering general relativity as a limit, or whether it can be bridged by an appropriate construction the manuscript hints at but does not develop.

Teams are required to address points 1-3 with primary-source citations; points 4-6 are technical correctness checks.
