# Red Opening — pifb-discussion-pullback-gravity

## Steelman (opposing position)

The Pullback Gravity subsection's title-level "(Speculative)" qualifier plus the in-section disclaimers at 3112 ("ansatz, not a derived consequence"), 3114 ("honest cost"), 3121 ("no analog of the Einstein field equations has been derived"), and 3123 ("an existence toy model rather than a derivation") together calibrate the epistemic load of the section to match what is actually being proposed — a structural pathway whose dynamical content is open — and therefore constitute appropriate hedging in the tradition of Verlinde 2011, Padmanabhan 2010, and Jacobson 1995, all of which mix derivation and conjecture under similar labels.

## Position

The hedges are not correctly calibrated. Two specific defects survive the surrounding paragraphs and cannot be repaired by a "(Speculative)" title.

First, the "honest cost" framing at line 3114 of the inertia/gravity precision decoupling is not registered honestly: sec:mass at lines 1844 to 2024 builds and uses the $\Sigma_p^{-1}$-as-effective-mass identification as a structural premise (boxed at Eq. 2017–2021 in the manuscript form `M_i = \bar{\Lambda}_{p_i} + ...`, and applied at line 2024 in the "rocks are certain, thus exhibit large second-variation rigidity" interpretive sentence). Nothing in sec:mass announces that its identification of perceived stiffness/mass with $\Sigma_p^{-1}$ will be moved off that tensor in the Discussion. The gravity subsection's in-place disclaimer at 3114 does not back-propagate to amend or retract anything upstream, and there is no forward reference in sec:mass to the structural-tier decoupling of sec:gravity_pullback. The "honest cost" is honest within one section but invisible from the section that paid the cost. Calibrated hedging would require an explicit amendment of sec:mass.

Second, the appeal to emergent-gravity precedents (Verlinde 2011, Padmanabhan 2010, Jacobson 1995) used by the claim's defenders cuts the opposite way once the primary sources are checked. Verlinde 2011 [arXiv:1001.0785] frames its result as "Newton's law of gravitation is shown to arise naturally and unavoidably" and as "directly leads to the Einstein equations" — derivation language, not speculation language. Jacobson 1995 [PRL 75:1260, "Thermodynamics of spacetime"] derives the Einstein equation from $\delta Q = T\,dS$ at a local Rindler horizon. The manuscript at line 3121 acknowledges in plain words that "the induced curvature tensors in the pullback metrics have not been computed, no analog of the Einstein field equations has been derived, and the identification of the scalar mass $m_{\text{eff},i}$ with a gravitational coupling has not been carried out beyond dimensional matching." A section that has not derived Newton's law, has not computed any curvature tensor, and has not produced an Einstein-equation analogue is not "speculative" in the same sense as Verlinde or Jacobson; the latter exhibit derivations whose foundational premises are open, while the manuscript exhibits a structural ansatz whose mathematical content is itself not produced. The "(Speculative)" label imports the social meaning of the term from the Verlinde/Jacobson tradition while paying none of the structural cost those papers paid.

If blue establishes that the appropriate precedent class is Caticha-style entropic dynamics and that those papers also stop at structural ansatz, R3 weakens substantially.
