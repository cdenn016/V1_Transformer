## Position

The Conclusion at line 3526 is miscalibrated against the manuscript's own worked-example bookkeeping in one specific, verifiable way: the parenthetical enumeration "(designated temporal direction, imaginary temporal frame component, real-part projection)" misstates both the count and the membership of the worked-example postulate set as the worked example itself records that set. This is not a defect in the limitations register, the unfalsifiability acknowledgement, the transformer-recovery framing, or the "suggests but does not establish" hedge --- those are calibrated and stand. It is a count-and-membership mismatch internal to a single parenthetical.

The mismatch has two parts. First, the postulate count. The subsection title at `Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2742` reads "Worked Example: $\mathrm{GL}(2, \mathbb{C})$ Gauge Frames and Lorentzian Signature Under \textbf{Two} Postulates," and the worked example's own three-features summary at line 2781 enumerates exactly two postulates verbatim: "the assignment of an imaginary component along $\tau$ in Eq.~\eqref{eq:complex_gauge_frame} is a postulate, and the projection to the real part of $G_{\mu\nu}$ is a further postulate." The Abstract-adjacent "Worked example only" statement at line 142 likewise reads "an imaginary temporal gauge component plus a real-part projection as postulates" --- two-count. The Conclusion at 3526 says "three postulates," which matches the parallel passage at line 3472 ("three further postulates ... (i) one base direction is designated temporal, (ii) ... multiplied by $i$, (iii) ... discarded by a real-part projection") but does not match the worked-example section's title, the worked-example summary, or the abstract-adjacent caveat.

Second, the postulate membership. The worked-example introductory paragraph at line 2747 states explicitly: "The choice of generator and the choice of bilinear form on $\mathfrak{g}$ are therefore part of the postulate set that drives the result." Line 2826 makes the same point and is more specific: obtaining the indefinite metric "requires the same postulates as the worked example of Section~\ref{sec:worked_signature}: an imaginary temporal component of the gauge frame ..., a real-part projection of the resulting complex frame-twist form, and the choice of $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ rather than $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ as the bilinear form on $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$." Three-count, with the third postulate being the bilinear-form sign convention. The Conclusion's parenthetical drops that postulate and substitutes "designated temporal direction" in the slot --- a substitution that is doubly tendentious because (a) the designation of a temporal direction is the assignment that gets multiplied by $i$ in the next item ("imaginary temporal frame component") and so is partially redundant with it, and (b) the sign-of-trace convention is the postulate that the cross-section editorial verdict at commit d1857aa6 (RED_WINS narrow on spec-ext-signature-collective, see `docs/debates/2026-05-20-pifb-spec-ext-signature-collective/04_verdict.md`) and the worked-example body text both treat as load-bearing for the construction's ability to produce a Lorentzian split in the first place.

The standard external position: the bilinear form on the Lie algebra (the trace form $\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ or its negative) is part of the data of a Lie-algebra metric; on compact real Lie algebras the Killing form is negative-definite and $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ is the conventional positive-definite inner product (Helgason, *Differential Geometry, Lie Groups, and Symmetric Spaces*, 1978, Ch. III §6, the Killing-form sign theorem), while on non-compact and on complex Lie algebras the trace form is indefinite. The worked example's signature result hinges on which sign convention is chosen --- this is line 2747's explicit acknowledgement. A Conclusion that enumerates the postulate set for that result without listing the bilinear-form convention is enumerating the wrong set.

## Falsification conditions

This position is wrong if any of the following holds:

1. The worked-example subsection title at line 2742 reads "Three Postulates" rather than "Two Postulates." (It reads "Two Postulates." Falsified.)

2. The worked-example summary at line 2781 enumerates three postulates rather than two. (It enumerates two: imaginary frame component, and real-part projection. Falsified.)

3. The bilinear-form sign convention at line 2747 / line 2826 is not actually load-bearing for the signature result --- i.e., the result holds independently of whether one uses $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ or $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ on $\mathfrak{gl}(K, \mathbb{C})$. The manuscript at line 2762 explicitly computes $G_{\tau\tau} = i^2 (\partial_\tau \psi_\tau)^2 \mathrm{tr}(T^2) = -2 (\partial_\tau \psi_\tau)^2$ using $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ with $\mathrm{tr}(T^2) = 2 > 0$; under $-\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ on the same $T$, the sign would flip and the metric would not be Lorentzian. The sign convention is load-bearing. Falsified.

4. The Conclusion at 3526 is reading the postulate set off a different convention than the worked-example body --- e.g., absorbing the bilinear-form choice into the generator choice. Even if one accepts this absorption, the Conclusion's enumeration still does not match either the two-count of the worked-example body or the three-count of line 3472, and the substitution of "designated temporal direction" for the bilinear-form convention is not derivable from any of the four canonical enumerations.

The fix is one editorial sentence: either reduce the Conclusion's parenthetical to the two-count form the worked-example title and summary use ("imaginary temporal frame component, real-part projection"), or expand to the four-count form that includes both the temporal designation and the bilinear-form convention ("designated temporal direction, imaginary temporal frame component, real-part projection, and the $+\mathrm{tr}(AB)$ bilinear-form convention"). The remaining ten lines of the Conclusion stand as written.
