# Meta-agent time as "a difference which makes a difference"

**Date:** 2026-05-24
**Scope:** Theory investigation for PIFB §"Time as Information Flow" (Attention/Participatory_it_from_bit.tex:2584). Not a manuscript edit; a proposal for how to formalize a scale-dependent information clock. Editorial placement is left to the author.

## The question

The current construction (PIFB:2588) defines a per-agent information clock by
$$\Delta\tau_i = \mathrm{KL}(q_i^{\text{new}} \| q_i^{\text{old}}) / (1\text{ bit}),$$
with a single universal quantum of "1 bit" for every agent at every hierarchical scale. The intuition under examination is Bateson's: a tick should be *a difference which makes a difference*, and a large meta-agent should require a large informational change before it registers a substantive change of state. Two things must be settled. First, what is the rigorous content of "a difference which makes a difference" applied to a belief update. Second, whether the clock unit renormalizes across the meta-agent hierarchy of §"Bottom-Up Emergence and Renormalization Group Structure".

## A notation hazard to settle first

The manuscript already overloads $\tau$: the information clock (PIFB:2588), the attention/Gibbs softmax temperature ($\tau = \kappa\sqrt{K}$ in the companion transformer paper), and the dispersion temperatures $\tau_q, \tau_s$ of the consensus detector (PIFB:2180). Any scale-dependent clock proposal should use a distinct symbol. Below the information clock is written $\mathcal{T}_i$, reserving $\tau$ for temperatures.

## What the just-noticeable difference actually is: the Fisher-information ball

Bateson and Weber–Fechner supply the concept; the formal content is Cramér–Rao distinguishability. Two beliefs $q(\theta)$ and $q(\theta+\delta\theta)$ are statistically distinguishable from a single observation when
$$\delta\theta^\top \mathcal{I}(\theta)\delta\theta \gtrsim 1,$$
where $\mathcal{I}$ is the Fisher information. The locus $\delta\theta^\top \mathcal{I}\delta\theta = 1$ is the just-noticeable-difference ellipsoid: belief changes inside it are below resolution, changes that cross it are noticeable. This makes the Fisher arc-length construction at PIFB:2603 (sec:fisher_arc_length) the geometric carrier of the just-noticeable difference rather than an aside.

The relation between the manuscript's two clocks must be stated exactly, because it is a square and not an identity. The bit-counting clock of PIFB:2588 accumulates KL, while the arc-length clock of PIFB:2603 accumulates Fisher–Rao length $ds = \sqrt{g_{\mathcal{B}}(\dot q, \dot q)}d\tau$. For a Gaussian belief the Fisher metric on the mean sector is the precision $\Lambda = \Sigma^{-1}$, so for a pure mean step at fixed covariance, verified symbolically,
$$\mathrm{KL}(q^{\text{new}}\|q^{\text{old}}) = \tfrac12 \delta\mu^\top \Lambda \delta\mu = \tfrac12 ds^2,$$
recovering the leading term of PIFB:2674. The bit-clock counts squared Fisher length; the arc-length clock counts Fisher length. One unit of Fisher length, $ds = 1$, which is the just-noticeable-difference threshold, is therefore $\tfrac12$ bit of KL, not one bit. The two clocks run at different rates over the same trajectory, and the meta-agent slowdown below carries a square-root between them. The manuscript currently presents both definitions; the choice between them is consequential and is taken up explicitly in the renormalization section.

## The bit is an RG-invariant unit; the slowdown lives in the dynamics

A trap must be avoided. KL divergence is dimensionless and invariant under reparametrization of the belief coordinates, so the bit is invariant under the coarse-graining map as a *unit*. The slowdown a large meta-agent exhibits cannot therefore be charged to a rescaling of the ruler. It must live in the rate at which bits are produced. Two readings of the original intuition follow, and they are not interchangeable.

Reading A treats the clock unit as universal ($1$ bit for every scale) and lets the meta-clock run slow because the meta-belief trajectory is short in Fisher length. This is what the manuscript already asserts qualitatively at PIFB:2244–2253: barycenter averaging removes high-frequency constituent drift, consensus barriers resist rapid updates, and the stiffness $[M_{\mu\mu}]_{II}$ grows with cluster size. Under Reading A the bit does not renormalize at all; the dynamics carry the entire scale dependence.

Reading B introduces a scale-adapted clock $\hat{\mathcal{T}}_I = \mathcal{T}_I / Z_I$, chosen so each scale's natural clock runs at order one. Under Reading B the unit rescales by $Z_I$ across scales. This is a legitimate change of clock, analogous to comoving versus physical time in cosmology, but it is a choice of units, not a forced renormalization of a dimensionless quantity. The physically meaningful, unit-independent content shared by both readings is the dimensionless bit-production ratio
$$Z_I := \frac{\mathbb{E}[\Delta \mathcal{T}_I]}{\mathbb{E}[\Delta \mathcal{T}_i]},$$
the number of meta-ticks produced per microscopic tick. Everything substantive is a statement about $Z_I$.

## The decisive computation: meta-agent time is a coherence filter

The computation here is carried in the KL-bit convention of PIFB:2588, which is the manuscript's headline definition and the one the original "one bit per tick" question is posed in; the Fisher-arc-length reading is recovered by a square root and is reported alongside. Model constituent beliefs as Gaussians $q_i = \mathcal{N}(\mu_i, \Sigma)$ with precision $\Lambda = \Sigma^{-1}$, undergoing per-step mean increments $\Delta\mu_i$. The covariance sector contributes nothing here because the steps are taken at fixed $\Sigma$, which is the same consensus-regime simplification under which PIFB:2198 already drops the dispersion term; reinstating covariance drift adds the $\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}$ term of PIFB:2674 without changing the scaling argument. The meta-agent mean is the barycenter $\mu_I = \tfrac1N\sum_i \mu_i$ (PIFB:2192), so $\Delta\mu_I = \tfrac1N\sum_i \Delta\mu_i$. Expected bit production per step is $\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}(\Lambda_I\mathrm{Cov}(\Delta\mu_I))$ at the meta level and $\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}(\Lambda\mathrm{Cov}(\Delta\mu_i))$ per constituent. Monte-Carlo confirmation (200k–400k steps, $K=2$, $N=16$) gives the following, matched against closed-form predictions.

| covariance convention | constituent increments | $Z_I$ (KL-bit) | predicted | $Z_I$ (Fisher length) | predicted |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| average, $\Lambda_I = \Lambda$ (the PIFB:2195 implementation) | incoherent (iid) | 0.0626 | $1/N = 0.0625$ | 0.250 | $1/\sqrt N = 0.250$ |
| average, $\Lambda_I = \Lambda$ | coherent (common mode) | 1.003 | $1$ | $1$ | $1$ |
| product-of-experts, $\Lambda_I = N\Lambda$ | incoherent | 1.001 | $1$ | $1$ | $1$ |
| product-of-experts, $\Lambda_I = N\Lambda$ | coherent | 16.04 | $N$ | $\sqrt N$ | $\sqrt N$ |

The $N=1$ case returns $Z = 1$ exactly, recovering PIFB:2588. The per-sample identity $\mathrm{KL} = \tfrac12 ds^2$ is exact to machine precision in the same run, so the Fisher-length column is the square root of the KL-bit column and not an independent measurement.

Decompose the constituent increment into a coherent common mode and an idiosyncratic part, $\Delta\mu_i = c + \xi_i$ with $\xi_i$ iid of covariance $v_\xi$. Then $\mathrm{Cov}(\Delta\mu_I) = \mathrm{Cov}(c) + v_\xi/N$, so the meta-clock rate is $\tfrac12\mathrm{tr}\big(\Lambda_I[\mathrm{Cov}(c) + v_\xi/N]\big)$. As $N$ grows the idiosyncratic term is suppressed as $1/N$ and only the coherent mode survives. This is the precise formalization of Bateson's phrase. A difference makes a difference to the meta-agent exactly to the extent that it is coherent across constituents and therefore moves the consensus barycenter; idiosyncratic constituent fluctuation, however large at a single constituent, cancels in the average and produces no meta-tick. The meta-agent does not carry a larger bit. It counts only coherent bits, and because most microscopic activity is incoherent, registering one meta-tick requires order $N$ bits of microscopic activity. The original intuition is correct in this aggregate-information sense, with the operative quantity being the microscopic information needed per meta-tick rather than a redefined quantum.

One caveat sharpens an existing implementation decision. The direction of the effect depends on the covariance-aggregation convention. The averaging convention $\Lambda_I \approx \Lambda$ used in the reference implementation (PIFB:2195, with the dispersion term dropped per PIFB:2198) gives the slowdown $Z_I \in [1/N, 1]$ that the timescale hierarchy at PIFB:2244 requires. The precision-pooling product-of-experts convention $\Lambda_I = N\Lambda$, which PIFB:2198 explicitly declines to adopt, would make the meta-agent *more* sensitive (a smaller just-noticeable difference) and would erase or reverse the slowdown. The manuscript's covariance-aggregation choice and its timescale-hierarchy claim are therefore the same decision viewed twice, which is a nontrivial internal consistency check worth stating in the text.

## What "renormalization of time" means here, made precise

The bit-production ratio is the natural candidate for a per-scale rescaling, $Z^{(s)} = Z_I$ for the typical cluster at scale $s$, and the scale-adapted clock $\hat{\mathcal{T}}^{(s+1)} = \hat{\mathcal{T}}^{(s)}/Z^{(s)}$ is the analogue of choosing comoving units at each scale. The numerical value the reader will quote depends on which of the manuscript's two clocks is canonical, and the difference is not cosmetic: under the bit-counting clock of PIFB:2588 a cluster of $N$ incoherent constituents slows by $Z^{(s)} = 1/N$, while under the Fisher-arc-length clock of PIFB:2603 the same cluster slows by $1/\sqrt N$. The manuscript should declare one clock primary before stating a slowdown law, since the two give different exponents for the same hierarchy. Three further points govern whether this deserves to be called a renormalization in the Wilsonian sense the manuscript invokes at PIFB:2209.

First, $Z_I$ is set by the coherent fraction of microscopic information, which is governed by the spectrum of the consensus coupling $\beta_{ij}$ already present in the free energy. The barycenter is the zero mode of the coupling Laplacian, and incoherent activity projects onto that single mode with weight $1/N$. This is derived in the next section, where the same Laplacian also fixes the cross-scale information flow $\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1}$ of PIFB:2233 and the cluster-formation timescale.

Second, the slowdown should not be advertised as a dynamical critical exponent. In statistical physics $z$ is defined at a critical point through $\tau \sim \xi^z$. The $1/N$ here is the law of large numbers acting on the barycenter, not a critical exponent. Promoting $Z^{(s)}$ to a genuine exponent requires the RG fixed point that PIFB:2227 already flags as open: an analytic $\beta$-function from a closed-form pushforward of the Gibbs measure, linearization about a fixed point, and identification of relevant operators. Until that is in hand, $Z^{(s)}$ should be called the bit-production slowdown, and the statement "time renormalizes" should read "the scale-adapted clock unit rescales by the bit-production ratio $Z^{(s)}$, whose promotion to a universal dynamical exponent is the open fixed-point problem of PIFB:2227."

Third, the dynamical and kinematic accounts coincide. The stiffness mechanism at PIFB:2251 (growing $[M_{\mu\mu}]_{II}$) is the Hessian of the free energy, and in the Laplace or saddle-point approximation of $F$ at the consensus point, the same approximation invoked through Beal §2.2.2 at PIFB:2190, the Hessian on the mean sector equals the precision that appears in the Fisher metric. The kinematic coherence filter computed above and the dynamical stiffness barrier of PIFB:2251 are the same phenomenon read through covariance versus Hessian, which removes any worry that the manuscript is offering two unrelated explanations of the same slowing.

## The slowdown from the consensus-coupling spectrum

The bit-production ratio is not a free parameter; it is fixed by the same belief-coupling weights $\beta_{ij}$ that already appear in the free energy, through the graph Laplacian they define. In the Gaussian mean sector at consensus, where the transport operators are locally trivial and the coupling KL reduces to a quadratic form, the belief-coupling term of $\mathcal{F}$ is
$$\mathcal{F}_{\text{couple}} = \tfrac12 \sum_{ij} \beta_{ij}(\mu_i - \mu_j)^\top \Lambda(\mu_i - \mu_j) = \mu^\top (L \otimes \Lambda)\mu,$$
where $L$ is the weighted graph Laplacian with off-diagonal entries $L_{ij} = -\beta_{ij}$ and diagonal $L_{ii} = \sum_j \beta_{ij}$, and $\mu$ stacks the constituent means. The natural-gradient belief flow of PIFB:2623, preconditioned by the Fisher metric $\Sigma = \Lambda^{-1}$, then reads $\dot\mu = -2\eta_\mu (L \otimes I)\mu + \text{(injected information)}$, which is linear consensus dynamics on the coupling graph. The eigendecomposition of $L$ assigns three distinct roles to its spectrum, each verified numerically.

The zero mode carries the barycenter. Because $L\mathbf{1} = 0$, the eigenvector of eigenvalue zero is the constant vector $\mathbf{1}/\sqrt N$, which is exactly the direction the meta-agent barycenter $\mu_I = \tfrac1N\sum_i\mu_i$ reads off. This mode is undamped by the consensus coupling alone, which is what the free-diffusion check (a) simulates; in the full free energy the parent-shadow term $\mathrm{KL}(q_I\|p_I)$ adds a restoring force that bounds $\mu_I$ over long times, but $Z_I$ is a per-step injection ratio read from the projection geometry and is unaffected. Incoherent microscopic activity, modeled as per-constituent information injection that is independent across constituents, has its power spread uniformly over all $N$ eigenmodes, so the projection onto the single zero mode collects a fraction $1/N$. This is the spectral origin of $Z_I = 1/N$, confirmed at $0.0837$ against $1/12$. Coherent activity, a common-mode injection shared across constituents, lies entirely along the zero mode and projects with full weight, giving $Z_I = 1$. The general value $Z_I = (\text{coherent fraction}) + O(1/N)$ is the zero-mode share of the injected information power.

The spectral gap sets the formation timescale. The nonzero eigenvalues $\lambda_a$ damp their modes at rate $2\eta_\mu \lambda_a$, so the slowest disagreement relaxes at the algebraic connectivity (Fiedler value) $\lambda_2$. A candidate cluster reaches consensus, and the threshold detector of PIFB:2175 triggers, on the timescale $1/(2\eta_\mu \lambda_2)$. This is the dynamical content of the RG-closure condition at PIFB:2716: a cluster is closed when its internal Laplacian gap dominates its coupling to the exterior, which is the same regime in which dropping the dispersion term (PIFB:2161, PIFB:2198) is justified.

The nonzero modes carry the cross-scale information flow. The dispersion of constituents about the barycenter is the energy in the nonzero eigenmodes,
$$\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1} = \sum_{i} \tfrac12(\mu_i - \mu_I)^\top \Lambda(\mu_i - \mu_I) = \tfrac12 \sum_{a\ge 2} \|\hat\mu_a\|_\Lambda^2,$$
matched to machine precision, and at the stochastic steady state this equals $\sum_{a\ge 2} D/(4\eta_\mu \lambda_a)$ for injection rate $D$, confirmed at $1.009$ against the predicted $1.008$. So the injected information power partitions cleanly across the spectrum: the zero-mode share advances the meta-clock, and the nonzero-mode share is the standing disagreement that PIFB:2233 already names. The bit-production ratio $Z_I$ and the cross-scale flow $\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1}$ are therefore complementary readouts of one Laplacian, the part of microscopic information the meta-agent ticks on and the part it averages away. This is the quantitative form of "a difference makes a difference only if it is coherent."

## Constraints the proposal satisfies

The construction reduces to PIFB:2588 at $N=1$, confirmed numerically. It reproduces the hierarchy $\mathcal{T}^{(s+1)} > \mathcal{T}^{(s)}$ of PIFB:2248 whenever microscopic activity carries an incoherent component, since then $Z_I < 1$. It is gauge-invariant because every KL is evaluated within a fixed frame after gauge-covariant transport, so the meta-clock does not depend on which gauge the meta-agent's Karcher-mean frame (PIFB:2164) selects, inheriting the frame-independence the barycenter already enforces at PIFB:2200.

## Proposed formalization for the manuscript

Rename the clock to $\mathcal{T}_i$ to break the $\tau$ collision. Keep the bit-counting definition of PIFB:2588 as primary, $\Delta\mathcal{T}_i = \mathrm{KL}(q_i^{\text{new}}\|q_i^{\text{old}})/(1\text{ bit})$, since it is the manuscript's headline and the one the question is posed in, and state the exact relation $\mathrm{KL} = \tfrac12 ds^2$ to the Fisher arc length of PIFB:2603, so that the just-noticeable-difference threshold $\delta\theta^\top\mathcal{I}\delta\theta = 1$ is $\tfrac12$ bit rather than one bit. Keep the bit universal across scales, which is forced by reparametrization-invariance of KL. Introduce the bit-production ratio $Z_I = \mathbb{E}[\Delta\mathcal{T}_I]/\mathbb{E}[\Delta\mathcal{T}_i]$ as the unit-independent carrier of scale dependence, give the coherent-or-idiosyncratic decomposition showing $Z_I = (\text{coherent fraction}) + O(1/N)$, and state the two boundary values $Z_I \to 1$ (coherent) and $Z_I \to 1/N$ (incoherent) with the closed forms above, noting that the Fisher-arc-length clock would report the square roots $1$ and $1/\sqrt N$. Offer the scale-adapted clock $\hat{\mathcal{T}}^{(s+1)} = \hat{\mathcal{T}}^{(s)}/Z^{(s)}$ as an optional comoving-time choice, explicitly not a renormalization of the dimensionless bit. Tie $Z^{(s)}$ to the consensus-coupling Laplacian spectrum and to $\mathcal{I}_{s\to s+1}$ (PIFB:2233), note the Hessian/precision identity linking this to the stiffness mechanism at PIFB:2251, and flag the promotion of $Z^{(s)}$ to a universal exponent as the same open RG-fixed-point problem already recorded at PIFB:2227.
